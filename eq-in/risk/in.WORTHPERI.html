Analysis of Worth Peripherals Limited (WORTHPERI)

Worth Peripherals Limited (CIN: L67120MP1996PLC010808), a manufacturer of corrugated boxes (single operating segment), reported strong H1 FY26 results (ended Sep 30, 2025). Standalone revenue grew 8.3% YoY to ₹10,555 lakhs (PAT +30.5% to ₹833 lakhs, EPS ₹5.29). Consolidated revenue +12.6% YoY to ₹15,369 lakhs (PAT +32.9% to ₹989 lakhs attributable to owners ₹800 lakhs, EPS ₹5.08). Balance sheet remains debt-light (standalone borrowings <₹8 lakhs), with equity up to ₹18,202 lakhs. Operating cash flows strong (standalone ₹853 lakhs, consolidated ₹1,324 lakhs). Recent AGM approved FY25 financials, ₹1/share dividend, and director re-designations.

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Robust Topline & Bottomline Growth: H1 revenue +8-13% YoY (standalone/consolidated); PAT margins expanded (standalone ~7.5%, consolidated ~6.4%), driven by operational efficiencies, other income (₹477/₹248 lakhs), and subsidies (₹230 lakhs received).
  • Debt-Free Balance Sheet: Near-zero borrowings (standalone ₹8 lakhs current), high equity (94% of assets), ample liquidity (bank balances ₹3,652/₹3,852 lakhs).
  • Strong Cash Generation: Operating CF covers capex/investments; consolidated net investing CF positive due to bank balance adjustments.
  • Expansion Momentum: Capex visible (standalone PPE ₹5,348 lakhs; consolidated CWIP ₹1,791 lakhs, PPE ₹8,279 lakhs); capital subsidies support growth.
  • Subsidiary Synergies: Yash Packers & Worth Wellness contribute ~27% to consolidated revenue; NCI PAT share +73% YoY.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Margin Pressures: Cost of materials ~73% of revenue; inventory changes added expense (H1 consolidated +₹31 lakhs). Employee/other expenses up 15-20% YoY.
  • Working Capital Strain: Standalone inventories +1.5%, receivables stable but high (₹2,547 lakhs); consolidated receivables down but still elevated (₹3,934 lakhs).
  • Subsidiary Drag: Standalone investing outflow ₹3,885 lakhs in subs/joint ventures; may pressure RoE if returns lag.
  • Higher Finance Costs: Consolidated up 114% YoY to ₹89 lakhs (borrowings ₹1,805 lakhs group-level).

Growth Prospects

  • High: 15-20% revenue CAGR feasible via capex (new plants/CWIP), packaging demand from FMCG/e-commerce, and capacity expansion. PAT growth outpacing revenue (30%+ YoY trend) supports EPS trajectory (FY26E ~₹10+). Single-segment focus aids scalability; subsidies/tax credits (deferred tax credits) boost. Market cap potential if execution sustains (current implied low multiples based on EPS).
