Analysis of Liberty Shoes Ltd. (LIBERTSHOE / 526596)

Liberty Shoes Ltd., a footwear manufacturer (single operating segment), reported solid revenue growth in H1 FY26 (Apr-Sep 2025) amid stable operations, but faced margin pressures. Key metrics: H1 Revenue ₹347 Cr (+6% YoY), Net Profit ₹5.3 Cr (-5% YoY); Total Assets ₹511 Cr (+3% YoY). AGM resolutions (Sep 2025) passed overwhelmingly, approving director remuneration hikes and secretarial auditor appointment, signaling governance continuity.

Tailwinds

  • Revenue Momentum: H1 revenue up 6% YoY to ₹347 Cr; Q2 up 1.4% YoY. Consistent top-line growth (FY25 full-year ₹676 Cr) supports market share gains in competitive footwear sector.
  • Operational Cash Flow Strength: Net operating cash flow ₹27 Cr in H1 FY26 (vs. ₹19 Cr H1 FY25), driven by EBITDA adjustments and working capital efficiency in payables.
  • Shareholder Confidence: 99.99% approval for key AGM items (e.g., director pay hikes for Shammi Bansal, Adish Gupta, Anupam Bansal), with promoter voting at ~83%. Low dissent (0.0007%).
  • Asset Base Expansion: PPE up 7% to ₹98 Cr; inventories support sales scaling. Equity base stable at ₹228 Cr.

Headwinds

  • Margin Compression: H1 PBT ₹7.6 Cr (-11% YoY) despite revenue growth; Q2 profit ₹2 Cr (up from ₹0.9 Cr but sequentially down 41% from Q1). Higher employee costs (+16% YoY), finance costs (+23%), and depreciation (+19%) squeezed EBITDA margins (~5-6%).
  • Working Capital Strain: Inventories +8% YoY (₹188 Cr); receivables +4% (₹120 Cr), tying up cash. Cash & equivalents down to ₹1.3 Cr.
  • Cost Pressures: Material costs elevated; other expenses up. Exceptional losses (₹0.6 Cr H1) persist.

Growth Prospects

  • Capex & Expansion: Ongoing investments (CWIP ₹4 Cr; H1 capex ₹14 Cr outflow) in PPE/ROU assets signal capacity build-up for footwear demand (projected India market CAGR 10-12% to 2030).
  • Revenue Trajectory: Full FY26 revenue could exceed FY25 if Q3/Q4 sustain H1 growth; single-segment focus aids efficiency.
  • Balance Sheet Resilience: Equity ~45% of assets; ROE ~9-10% (FY25). Positive FCF from ops supports debt servicing.
  • Outlook: Potential 8-10% revenue growth FY26e if margins stabilize; AGM approvals enable management continuity.

Overall Summary: Liberty Shoes shows moderate growth potential (revenue tailwind, capex) but faces near-term headwinds from cost inflation and working capital drag, risking profitability. Bull case: 10%+ revenue growth FY26 with margin recovery. Bear case: Stagnant profits if debt/costs escalate. Recommendation: Neutral; monitor Q3 results for festive sales and debt trends.
