Plaza Wires Limited (PLAZACABLE) Analysis

Company Overview: Plaza Wires Limited is engaged in manufacturing and trading of electrical wires, cables, and allied products. Listed on NSE (PLAZACABLE) and BSE (544003). FY25 H1 (Apr-Sep 2025) results show robust top-line growth amid capacity expansion, but with working capital pressures. All AGM resolutions (Sep 30, 2025) passed with ~99% approval (promoter-heavy voting).

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Strong Revenue & Profit Momentum: H1 revenue at ₹1,401 Mn (+47% YoY from ₹951 Mn); Q2 at ₹747 Mn (+56% YoY from ₹478 Mn). PAT H1 at ₹16 Mn (+90% YoY). Margins stable despite volume growth, aided by operational leverage.
  • Capacity Expansion: CWIP at ₹33 Mn (new); PPE at ₹480 Mn (stable post-depreciation). Ongoing capex (₹7 Mn PPE + ₹33 Mn CWIP in H1) signals scaling for future demand.
  • Sector Tailwinds: Wires/cables demand boosted by India’s infra push (housing, electrification, renewables), PLI schemes, and real estate recovery. Exclusive focus (per Ind AS 108) on high-growth segment.
  • Healthy Balance Sheet: Equity up 1% to ₹1,239 Mn; RoE implied ~2.6% H1 annualized (improving). Low non-current debt (₹13 Mn).

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Working Capital Strain: Inventories up 20% to ₹444 Mn (H1 cash burn ₹74 Mn); receivables stable at ~₹613 Mn. Op. cash flow negative at -₹34 Mn (vs +₹90 Mn YoY), driving current borrowings up 41% to ₹324 Mn.
  • Commodity Exposure: 88% of costs in materials (₹1,159 Mn H1); vulnerable to copper/aluminum price spikes (not explicitly hedged in notes).
  • Liquidity Crunch: Cash at ₹0.8 Mn (up marginally); reliant on short-term debt for growth funding.

Growth Prospects

  • High Visibility: Revenue trajectory (FY25 H1 >64% of FY24 full-year ₹2,182 Mn) suggests 40-50% FY25 growth if sustained. EPS H1 ₹0.36 (annualized ~₹0.72 vs FY24 ₹0.66).
  • Expansion Leverage: Capex ramp-up positions for market share gains in ₹50,000+ Cr wires/cables market (CAGR 12-15% per industry trends).
  • Outlook: 20-30% CAGR feasible over 2-3 years with infra capex (₹11 Lakh Cr govt. budget) and export potential. Debt-funded growth viable if margins hold >2-3% PBT.

Key Risks

GST Litigation ₹11.51 Cr demand (₹5.75 Cr tax + penalty) from Shimla GST (Feb 2025); appealed (Apr 2025). Auditor flags uncertainty. High: 10-15% of equity; cash outflow if lost. Strong merits per mgmt./experts; no provision yet.
Liquidity/Debt Negative op. cash flow; borrowings up 47% to ₹337 Mn total. Interest cost ₹14 Mn H1. Medium: Debt/Equity ~0.27x but rising; cash low. Revenue growth to fund WC; monitor DSCR.
Commodity Volatility Copper prices up ~20% CY25; 80%+ cost tied to inputs. High: Margin compression (PBT margin 1.6% H1). Pass-through pricing; inventory hedging.

Summary Verdict: Positive Outlook with Cautious Stance. Explosive H1 growth (47% revenue, 90% PAT) and capex signal multi-year potential (target ₹3,000+ Mn FY26 revenue), but GST overhang, WC drag, and commodity risks cap near-term upside. Buy on dips if GST appeal resolves favorably; monitor Q3 for cash flow inflection. Valuation context: FY24 P/E ~20-25x implied (mkt. cap ~₹1,200 Cr at ₹275/share est.). Promoter alignment strong (100% voting support).
