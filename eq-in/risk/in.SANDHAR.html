Sandhar Technologies Limited (SANDHAR) Analysis

Overview: Sandhar is an auto ancillary manufacturer focusing on locks, mirrors, sheet metal, die-casting (ADC), and emerging EV components. Q2/H1 FY26 showed robust revenue growth (consolidated +29% QoQ / +24% YoY H1) driven by India ops (+33% revenue), but margins dipped due to integration costs. Strong H2 outlook amid festive demand. Stock listed on BSE/NSE.

Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts)

  • Robust Domestic Demand: India revenue +33% YoY; 2W segment (+7.4% industry growth) key driver. Order book strong for Q3/Q4 due to extended festive season.
  • Diversified Portfolio & Expansion: Sheet metal/die-casting scaling (Sundaram-Clayton acquisition added INR198 cr H1 revenue); Vision Systems doubled revenues. JVs (5 nos.) all PAT-positive (+68.57 cr revenue at 50% share).
  • EV Entry: Commercial invoicing of battery chargers/motor controllers; H1 revenue INR6.94 cr, FY26 target INR15 cr. Pilot DC-DC converters gaining traction.
  • Customer Gains: HMSI share up to 4.2% (from 3.7%); new projects across sheet metal/castings/locks/mirrors.
  • Margin Normalization: Adjusted India EBITDA 10.44% (vs. 10.16% prior); overseas losses halved QoQ, breakeven eyed in H2 via cost cuts/new orders.
  • ROCE Improvement: India at 16.09% annualized (vs. 15.5% prior); target 18% pre-tax.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Margin Pressure: H1 EBITDA margin down to 8.5% (from 9.7%) due to new projects (Sundaram + Pune/South plants: -INR11.21 cr drag, ~8% EBITDA hit). Assembly business declined (spokes wheels shifting to alloys).
  • Overseas Drag: Revenue flat/slight up QoQ but EBITDA -12%; Europe volatile (Trump admin, industry slowdown). Power costs in Romania noted (though disputed).
  • Industry Softness: PV degrowth (-1.5%); cabins/fabrication subdued due to CE emission norms (inventory overhang).
  • Working Capital Strain: Receivables/inventories up; debt at INR858 cr (working capital heavy).

Growth Prospects

  • FY26 Guidance: 15%+ core growth + INR400 cr from Sundaram; Q3/Q4 strong. H1 revenue INR2,360 cr (24% YoY); aim double-digit EBITDA (~10-10.5%).
  • Medium-Term (2-3 Yrs):
    • Die-casting/sheet metal to INR1,000 cr+ each; smart locks/mirrors scaling (12-14% margins).
    • Overseas recovery to 12-13% margins via Romania shift/new customers.
    • EV ramp-up; JVs strategic for tech (sensors/antennas).
    • Consolidated revenue run-rate INR1,200 cr/quarter sustainable; EBITDA target 11% (+50 bps/year).
  • Capex: INR300 cr FY26 (mostly completion); post-FY26 maintenance-level (INR40-50 cr/project). QIP approved (INR500 cr backup for M&A).
  • ROE/ROCE: 18% pre-tax target; capacity utilization to rise with ramps.

Key Risks

Overseas Losses persist (Europe demand weak, forex translation hits); power/raw material volatility. Cost optimization, new RFQs, financial re-engineering.

Investment Summary: Strong growth trajectory (15-20% CAGR feasible) with EV/JV tailwinds offsetting near-term margin headwinds. H2 turnaround critical for 10%+ EBITDA FY26. Risks tilted to execution/overseas, but normalized metrics reassuring. Positive AGM outcomes (all resolutions passed, incl. borrowing/QIP hikes). Monitor Q3 results for overseas/EV progress. Overall: Bullish with caution on margins/debt.
