Zensar Technologies Limited (ZENSARTECH) - Q2 FY26 Analysis

Zensar delivered steady performance with revenue of $162.8M (+4.2% YoY reported, +3.4% CC; +0.5% QoQ), EBITDA margin of 15.4% (+20 bps QoQ despite salary hikes), and PAT margin of 12.8%. Key positives include AI traction (28% of bookings AI-influenced), strong BFSI/HLS growth, and margin resilience. TMT remains a drag. Below is a structured summary of headwinds, tailwinds, growth prospects, and key risks based on earnings materials.

Headwinds (Challenges Impacting Performance)

  • TMT Vertical Weakness: Sharp QoQ decline of 9.9% (YoY -6.7%), now ~20% of revenue (down from 27% in FY24). Driven by secular shift to CapEx (GPUs/data centers), OpEx cuts, and layoffs in tech clients. Management views it as ongoing stress, not client-specific.
  • Soft Deal Intake: Order book $158.7M (book-to-bill H1: 1.02x), but Q2 wins softer due to timing (e.g., US uncertainties, summer slowdown in Europe). Lowest book-to-bill in 11 quarters (0.97x).
  • Macro Pressures: US policy shifts (tariffs, “Liberation Day”), vendor consolidation, and Q3 furloughs expected industry-wide. US revenue -1.9% QoQ.
  • Muted MCS: Flat QoQ/YOY growth, limiting overall momentum.

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Vertical Strength: BFSI (43.6% revenue) +5.6% QoQ/+11% YoY; HLS +3.9% QoQ/+11.3% YoY. Reducing TMT exposure improves portfolio resilience.
  • Margin Discipline: Gross margin 31.0% (+50 bps QoQ), EBITDA stable mid-teens despite ~$3.2M salary hike impact. Benefits from +50 bps utilization (84.8%), better offshore mix (54.2%), and cost controls.
  • AI Momentum: ZenseAI platform launch; 28% bookings AI-influenced (up from 21% prior); 5,000+ employees AI-trained. Key wins in fraud detection, modernization (e.g., 25-70% efficiency gains).
  • Operational Health: Attrition stable at 9.8% (<10% for 3Q); cash $293M (post-dividend); DSO 75 days (up slightly due to FX); low H1B exposure (<3%).

Growth Prospects (Outlook and Catalysts)

  • Pipeline Strength: “One of the highest qualified pipelines” with large deals in BFSI/HLS/MCS/Europe/Africa. Management “very positive” outside TMT; expects conversion in coming quarters.
  • AI as Differentiator: Agentic AI platform resonating; scaling from pilots to production. Awards (Avasant Challenger, Everest PEAK) boost credibility.
  • Strategic Moves: Brazil entity approval for expansion; geographic diversification (Europe +5.2% QoQ, Africa +6.9% QoQ).
  • Volume Leverage: Offshore growth > headline revenue; utilization headroom. H1 book-to-bill 1.02x supports steady momentum; mid-teens margins guided.
  • Overall: No formal guidance, but FY26 double-digit aspiration recalibrated to industry ~4-6% (per NASSCOM). Organic focus; top-quartile growth history.

Key Risks

  • TMT Prolonged Downturn: If CapEx shift persists, further erosion despite de-risking (could drag growth if other verticals don’t accelerate).
  • Macro/Execution Risks: US tariffs/visas (impacts Nov 2026), furloughs, deal delays. High DSO (75 days) signals collections pressure.
  • Client Concentration: Top-10 clients ~40.5% revenue; any consolidation hits.
  • Currency/Inflation: Rupee depreciation aided margins (partial FX gain), but volatility a threat. Wage hikes absorbed but recurring.
  • Competition/Talent: AI investments ongoing; underinvestment risk if margins prioritized (management denies).
  • Geopolitical: Evolving US policies; low H1B mitigates but not eliminates visa risks.

Summary Verdict: Resilient quarter amid TMT headwinds, with AI/vertical diversification as key growth levers. Margins and cash position strong; watch Q3 furloughs and TMT for inflection. Positive bias if pipeline converts. (Stock traded at ~INR 763 as of Sep 30, 2025; market cap ~INR 17,319 Cr.)
