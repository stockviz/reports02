Zensar Technologies Limited (ZENSARTECH) Analysis: Q2 FY26 (Sep 30, 2025)

Zensar delivered steady Q2 FY26 results amid a challenging macro environment: revenue of $162.8M (+4.2% YoY reported / +3.4% CC; +0.5% QoQ), EBITDA margin 15.4% (+20bps QoQ despite salary hikes), PAT margin 12.8%, utilization 84.8% (+50bps QoQ), and attrition stable at 9.8%. Order book was $158.7M (H1 book-to-bill 1.02x). AI focus intensified with ZenseAI launch and 28% AI-influenced bookings (up from 21% in Q1). TMT weakness offset BFSI/HLS strength; exposure to TMT fell to 20% (from 27% in FY24). Cash at $293M (no debt), DSO at 75 days (+3 QoQ).

Headwinds
  • TMT Sector Drag: -9.9% QoQ / -6.7% YoY; secular shift to CapEx (GPUs/data centers), OpEx rationalization, and layoffs. US-heavy (66.6% revenue), contributing to US -1.9% QoQ.
  • Macro Pressures: US policy uncertainty (tariffs, visas – though H1B <3%), Q3 furloughs expected (industry-wide, less TMT impact due to lower exposure).
  • Deal Timing: Soft Q2 bookings (book-to-bill 0.97x); seasonality (Europe summer, low renewals), some delays due to “Liberation Day” uncertainty.
  • Operational: DSO up to 75 days (exchange impact); flat CC QoQ revenue; MCS soft (-0.6% QoQ CC).
Tailwinds
  • Vertical Strength: BFSI (43.6% rev, +5.6% QoQ / +11% YoY), HLS (+3.9% QoQ / +11.3% YoY); service lines like Cloud (+14.1% YoY), Data (+9.2% YoY).
  • Geographic Diversification: Europe +5.2% QoQ, Africa +6.9% QoQ / +9.8% YoY (11.9% rev share).
  • Margin Discipline: Absorbed ~2% EBITDA hit from hikes/bonuses via utilization (+50bps), offshore mix (54.2%), cost controls, forex gains. Gross margin 31.0% (+50bps QoQ).
  • AI Momentum: 5k+ employees AI-trained; ZenseAI traction (agents for legacy mod, fraud, etc.); multiple wins (e.g., 25% GTM acceleration, 40% workload reduction).
  • Balance Sheet: $293M cash post-dividend; healthy client metrics (171 active clients, top-10 40.5% rev).
Growth Prospects
  • Pipeline Strength: “Highest qualified pipeline in history” incl. large deals in BFSI/HLS/MCS/Europe/Africa; timing issue expected to resolve.
  • AI-Driven Upside: 28% bookings AI-led; platform adoption moving POC-to-production; industry resonance (e.g., agentic AI for APIs, 70% cost savings).
  • Strategic Moves: TMT de-risking (20% rev); Brazil entity approval; service line shift (68% rev, +150bps YoY).
  • FY26 Outlook: Management “positive except TMT”; mid-teens margins guided; volume growth via offshore/utilization. Potential double-digit if TMT stabilizes/deals convert (peers grew despite headwinds).
  • Longer-Term: Top-quartile growth aspiration (ranked #4 last year organically); ESG leadership (CRISIL 71/100).
Key Risks
Risk Category Details Mitigation
Sector-Specific Prolonged TMT weakness (CapEx shift, AI productivity proving via layoffs). Exposure down to 20%; grow others faster.
Macro/External US tariffs/visas, global slowdown, Q3 furloughs. Low H1B; geographic diversification (Europe/Africa up).
Execution Deal slippage, higher DSO/attrition. Strong pipeline; stable attrition <10%; cash buffer.
Operational Wage inflation, forex volatility. Margins resilient; hedges ($80M outstanding).
Competition Vendor consolidation, peers with inorganic growth. AI differentiation (ZenseAI), organic #4 ranking.

Overall Outlook: Resilient performance with AI tailwinds offsetting TMT/macro headwinds. Growth hinges on pipeline conversion and non-TMT verticals (projected 4-6% industry growth per NASSCOM). Positive for FY26 if AI scales; monitor TMT and Q3 furloughs. Buy/Hold for AI exposure in mid-tier IT; target upside from $700-800/share range (current ~INR 763).
