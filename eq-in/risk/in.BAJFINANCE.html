Bajaj Finance Limited (BAJFINANCE) Analysis: Headwinds, Tailwinds, Growth Prospects, and Key Risks

Bajaj Finance (BFL) reported robust Q2 FY26 results (ended Sep 2025), with consolidated AUM at ₹4.62 lakh Cr (+24% YoY), new loans at 12.17 Mn (+26% YoY), and PAT at ₹4,948 Cr (+23% YoY). Standalone AUM grew 23% to ₹3.38 lakh Cr, with PAT (ex-exceptional) up 24% YoY. Subsidiaries BHFL (AUM +24%, PAT +18%) and BFinsec (AUM +40%, PAT +27%) performed strongly. Below is a structured summary based on the Q2 investor presentation, financial results, and disclosures.

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Volume & Franchise Momentum: Record festive disbursements (7.4 Mn loans, +26% YoY; 6.3 Mn consumer loans, +29% value). Customer additions at 4.13 Mn (total franchise 110.64 Mn, +20% YoY). Cross-sell franchise at 71.32 Mn.
  • Diversified Growth: AUM mix balanced (Mortgages 31%, Urban B2C 21%, MSME 11%). Rural (+24%), Gold loans (+85%), MFI (+217%) scaling fast. Deposits at ₹69,766 Cr (+5% YoY), aiding funding mix (18% of borrowings).
  • Operational Efficiency: Opex/NTI improved to 32.6% (consolidated). Cost of funds at 7.52% (-27 bps QoQ). AI-driven “FINAL” initiative (123 use cases, 80 live by Feb’26) promising productivity gains in 12-18 months.
  • Subsidiary Strength: BHFL’s pristine quality (GNPA 0.26%, ROA 2.3%). BFinsec’s retail/HNI focus adding 94K customers.
  • Ratings & Capital: AAA/Stable ratings; CRAR 21.23% (Tier-1 20.54%). S&P/Moody’s upgrades signal resilience.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Credit Stress: Annualized credit cost at 2.05% (elevated vs. 2.13% YoY but up QoQ). GNPA 1.24% (+18 bps YoY), NNPA 0.60% (+14 bps). High stress in captive 2W/3W (GNPA 12.08%) and MSME (2.47%; volumes cut 25%).
  • Consumer Leverage: Multiple loans a concern; vintage performance weak except non-MSME. Stage 3 up ₹1,168 Cr QoQ.
  • Funding Pressures: Deposits reliance reduced for cost optimization; borrowings mix shifted (Money markets 52%). CoF guidance 7.55-7.60% FY26.
  • Opex & Attrition: Employee count 67,461 (+1,933 QoQ); attrition 17.8%. Competitive intensity in housing (BHFL AUM growth slowed).
  • Macro: Potential slowdown in consumer sentiment post-festive; regulatory overlays on unsecured lending.

Growth Prospects

  • AUM/Profit Guidance: 25-27% AUM, 23-24% profit growth FY26+. Target 16-17 Mn customer adds; ROA 4.3-4.7%, ROE 19-21%.
  • Strategic Levers: Cross-sell (PPC rising), tech (AI bots: 85% resolutions, 442 voice agents), rural hub-spoke (2,500 towns). BHFL to hit min public shareholding (recent 2% stake sale at ₹1,588 Cr).
  • Product Expansion: Premiumization in consumer (TVs/ACs), Gold/MFI ramp-up, LAS (₹29.5k Cr). Digital: 78 Mn app users, UPI 48 Mn handles.
  • Subsidiary Upside: BHFL (AUM ₹1.27 lakh Cr) eyes 24%+ growth; BFinsec HNI/retail scaling (1.15 Mn customers).
  • Medium-Term: FY26 AUM ~₹5.2-5.3 lakh Cr; FINAL to drive leverage via 200 Mn customer vision.

Key Risks

Risk Category Description Mitigation
Credit Quality Rising NPAs (esp. unsecured/MSME/2W); consumer debt traps. GNPA could hit 1.4% upper guidance. Vintage improvements; 25% MSME cut; Stage 2 down ₹1,007 Cr; PCR 52%.
Regulatory RBI norms on unsecured lending, MPS requirements (BHFL stake dilution ongoing). Strong compliance; diversified book; AAA ratings.
Liquidity/Funding Rate hikes, deposit slowdown (growth halved YoY). LCR ~290% but volatile. ₹16k Cr liquidity buffer; ECB/deposits mix; conservative leverage (4.7x).
Macro/Economic Slowing consumption, inflation, elections. Festive boost temporary? Diversification (31% mortgages); rural/MSME focus.
Operational Attrition (17.8%), cyber/tech risks amid AI rollout. FINAL (80 use cases live); 242k distribution points.
Concentration Promoter holding 54.66%; BHFL 19% AUM contribution. Public float increasing; balanced segments.

Overall Outlook: Strong tailwinds from franchise/tech sustain 25%+ AUM growth, but credit headwinds cap near-term margins (ROA steady at 4.5%). FY26 on track; monitor NPAs and unsecured stress. Valuation supportive at historical multiples given resilient ROE.
