Analysis of Puravankara Limited (PURVA) – Headwinds, Tailwinds, Growth Prospects, and Key Risks

Overview: Puravankara Limited (NSE/BSE: PURVA), a mid-cap real estate developer with a 50-year legacy, focuses on residential (luxury to mid-income) and emerging commercial assets across South and West India. Q2/H1 FY26 (ended Sep 30, 2025) showed resilient pre-sales/collections amid a loss-making P&L due to IndAS revenue timing. Land bank ~32 msf developable (Group interest ~28.6 msf), ongoing ~34 msf. Strong macro tailwinds support growth, but execution delays and legal overhangs pose risks.

Tailwinds (Positive Factors)
  • Robust Demand & Pricing Power: Pre-sales Rs 1,322 Cr Q2 (+4% YoY), Rs 2,445 Cr H1 (+4% YoY) – 100% sustenance-driven, signaling brand strength. Avg. realization Rs 8,814/sqft Q2 (+7% YoY), Rs 8,891/sqft H1 (+8% YoY). 81% sales <Rs 2 Cr/unit; South (B’luru) dominant but West up to 27% H1 mix.
  • Collections Momentum: Rs 1,047 Cr Q2 (+8% YoY), Rs 1,904 Cr H1 (+1% YoY). Cash conversion healthy (~78% of pre-sales).
  • Pipeline & Expansion: H1 additions 6.36 msf (GDV Rs 9,100 Cr); H2 launches 15.46 msf (Rs ~10,000 Cr GDV). Land bank runway for 5+ years. West diversification (Mumbai redev ~4.4 msf developable; Pune closures imminent). Commercial annuity: Zentech (IKEA lease 1.2L sqm), Aerocity OC Jan’26.
  • Macro Support: India GDP +7.8% Q1 FY26; RBI repo cut 100bps to 5.5%; residential demand strong (1-2 Cr segment +5-17% prices). Office leasing +2% YoY.
  • Balance Sheet Discipline: Net debt stable Rs 2,894 Cr (debt/sqft residential Rs 859, -31% 3-yr); cost of debt 11.32% (-QoQ). Projected surplus Rs 15,568 Cr (>5x net debt coverage). HDFC platform Rs 700 Cr deployed (Rs 110 Cr repaid).
Headwinds (Challenges)
  • P&L Pressure: Q2 revenue Rs 663 Cr (+27% YoY) but EBITDA margin 18% (vs 28% YoY); PAT loss Rs 42 Cr (vs Rs 20 Cr). H1 loss Rs 111 Cr due to IndAS timing, high finance costs (Rs 329 Cr H1, +27% YoY), and inventory build-up (Rs 11,329 Cr, +6% QoQ).
  • Launch Delays: Regulatory hurdles (B’luru e-khata/GBA transition, Mumbai BMC/approvals) pushed Q3 launches to Q4/H1 FY27 (e.g., Andheri Jan’26, Bellandur touch-and-go).
  • Project-Specific Issues: High unsold inventory (Thane Panorama 73%, Zentech 93% sold but slow). Collections growth muted H1 (+1%).
  • Regional Concentration: ~65% ongoing/planned in South (B’luru heavy); non-B’luru sales dipped to 17% H1 (vs 44% FY25).
Growth Prospects
  • Near-Term (FY26/H1 FY27): H2 launches could drive pre-sales Rs 3,000-4,000 Cr (30-50% absorption). Sustenance ~Rs 2,600 Cr. Collections to accelerate with construction ramp-up/deliveries (4.3 msf planned FY26, 1.3 msf H1 done).
  • Medium-Term (FY27-28): Pipeline GDV Rs 20,000+ Cr; West share to rise (Mumbai/Pune redev/JVs). Commercial surplus Rs 2,008 Cr + annuity by FY30. Total developable 82 msf (incl. settlements).
  • Long-Term: Pan-India (9 cities); plotted/commercial diversification. Surplus cash flows to delever (debt to zero in 4 yrs if no new adds). Target: Multi-year pre-sales CAGR 20-25% on launches/execution.
  • Catalysts: IKEA lease validates commercial; HDFC Rs 450 Cr undrawn; PE inflows (office/resi land).
Key Risks
Risk Category Description Mitigants
Execution/Regulatory Approval delays (e-khata, MoEF, BMC); possession bottlenecks (e-khata registrations). Strong track record (93 projects delivered); diversified pipeline.
Financial/Leverage Debt Rs 3,599 Cr gross; interest sensitivity (repo cuts help but high cost 11.32%). Surplus coverage 5x; payable-when-able NCDs (Rs 771 Cr off-balance).
Legal/Title Ongoing suits (Rs 62 Cr deposits/advances, IT search Rs 23 Cr tax demand, Benami notice on 43.5 acres). No provisions. Mgmt confident; writs filed (Karnataka HC stalled Benami).
Market/Cyclical Demand slowdown (high inventory Thane); competition in B’luru/Mumbai. Pricing discipline; 51% sales <Rs 1 Cr/unit.
Concentration B’luru ~42% ongoing; South 65%. West ramp-up (25% H1 sales).
Other Forex (Sri Lanka ops minor); construction cost inflation. Fixed-price contracts; contingencies in costs.

Valuation Context: Stock down 34% YTD (vs Nifty Realty -22%, Sensex -5%). Trades at ~0.3x FY26F sales; P/B ~0.7x. Growth re-rating on launches/debt reduction.

Summary: Moderate Buy/Bullish Medium-Term. Tailwinds from pipeline, collections, and macro outweigh Q2 loss (non-cash IndAS). Growth hinges on H2 launches (watch approvals). Risks (legal/delays) cap upside near-term; target 20-30% returns in 12-18 months on execution. Monitor Q3 pre-sales/debt metrics.
