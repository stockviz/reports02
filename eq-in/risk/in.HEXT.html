Analysis of Hexaware Technologies Limited (HEXT) - Q3CY25 Overview

Hexaware Technologies Limited (NSE: HEXT, BSE: 544362), a global IT services provider focused on AI-led digital transformation, reported solid Q3CY25 results (ended Sep 30, 2025) amid a challenging macro environment. Revenue grew 5.5% YoY (USD terms) to USD 394.8 Mn, with EBITDA margins expanding 154 bps YoY to 17.5%. Key strategic moves include the USD 66 Mn acquisition of CyberSolve (IAM specialist) and plans to merge subsidiaries for group simplification. Below is a structured analysis of headwinds, tailwinds, growth prospects, and key risks, derived directly from the provided filings (investor presentations, financial results, board outcomes, ESOP updates, and press releases).

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)
  • Revenue Momentum & Operational Efficiency: +3.3% QoQ / +5.5% YoY USD growth (CC: +3.4% QoQ / +5.2% YoY); INR revenue +11.1% YoY. EBITDA +15.6% YoY (absolute), with 30 bps QoQ expansion driven by cost controls and utilization at 83.8%.
  • Margin Resilience: Reported EBITDA outlook for CY25 at 17.1-17.2%; LTM cash conversion at 79.6%; strong balance sheet with INR 20,201 Mn cash (USD 228 Mn).
  • Deal Pipeline & Wins: Largest-ever pipeline; key Q3 wins include large consolidations (e.g., top-3 Canadian bank, German financial services), GCC opportunities, and expansions in digital ITO/ServiceNow. TTM client metrics stable (Top 5: 26% revenue).
  • Strategic Acquisitions: CyberSolve (USD 25.8 Mn CY24 revenue, 230+ specialists) bolsters IAM/cybersecurity entry into USD 100 Bn market (15.3% CAGR to 2033). Prior deals (e.g., SMC Squared) enhance GCC/AI capabilities.
  • Vertical/Geography Strength: FS (+12.2% YoY), H&I (+9.4%), M&C (+8.0%) leading; Americas (76% revenue) +8.4% YoY.
  • Talent & Incentives: Headcount at 33,590 (+1,054 LTM net add); low IT attrition (11.4%); 2 Mn ESOP shares allotted at INR 382.5, signaling retention focus.
  • ESG/Corporate Actions: Strong awards (e.g., EcoVadis Gold); subsidiary mergers to streamline structure; investor meets signal engagement.
Headwinds (Challenges)
  • Profitability Dip QoQ: PAT -2.6% QoQ (INR 3,699 Mn); EPS -2.6% QoQ due to higher tax (43.1% QoQ), D&A (+18.2% QoQ), and finance costs (+24.4% QoQ).
  • Vertical/Geography Weakness: HTPS -8.6% QoQ / -10.1% YoY (budget cuts/project closures); T&T -9.8% QoQ; Europe/APAC soft (-0.7% / -8.4% YoY).
  • Macro/External Pressures: US govt shutdown/visa restrictions delaying ramps; improving but cautious demand in M&C.
  • Operational Metrics: DSO up to 73 days (billed: 37); BPS revenue -1% QoQ; one-off costs in prior quarters (e.g., Q2 provisions) normalized but highlight volatility.
  • Pipeline Timing: Expected Q4 decline post-decisions; short-term ramps delayed to Jan 2026.
Growth Prospects
  • High-Confidence Drivers (Medium-Term):
    • Deal Momentum: 2 major consolidations won; more Q4 decisions; Legacy Mod/POC progress. Banking/FS to lead QoQ growth.
    • Strategic Bets: CyberSolve integration for cross-sell (90% US revenue); AI/cybersecurity/GCC focus aligns with market tailwinds (IAM growth).
    • Vertical Expansion: FS/H&I/Banking acceleration; M&C recovery signs; new “Technology, Products & Platforms” vertical.
    • Geographic/Revenue Mix: Offshore IT rising (48.8%); IT services 84% of revenue.
  • Quantitative Outlook: CY25 revenue implied ~+6-8% USD growth (based on 9M trends); EBITDA stable at 17%+; EPS +22% YoY trajectory.
  • Longer-Term: AI platforms, 30+ Fortune 500 clients, global presence (30+ countries); net-zero/ESG commitments enhance appeal.
