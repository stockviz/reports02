Mahindra Logistics Limited (MAHLOG) Analysis

Current Context (Q2/H1 FY26 ended Sep 2025):
MLL reported strong revenue growth (Consolidated: H1 revenue ₹3,310 Cr, +12.5% YoY; Standalone: ₹2,713 Cr, +13.4% YoY) driven by Supply Chain Management (SCM, 95% of revenue). However, consolidated net loss widened to ₹17.8 Cr (vs. ₹17.5 Cr YoY) due to SCM segment losses (H1: -₹17.3 Cr). Rights issue raised ₹749 Cr (Aug 2025), enabling debt repayment (Standalone borrowings: ₹0 from ₹150 Cr; Consolidated NC borrowings down 92% to ₹34 Cr). Balance sheet strengthened (Equity up 157% to ₹1,165 Cr consolidated). Investments in subs (e.g., ₹264 Cr in MESPL) for working capital. Recent L’Oréal partnership highlights ESG focus.

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Revenue Momentum & Market Expansion: Robust top-line growth (Q2 consol +10.8% YoY), fueled by SCM (e.g., Rivigo integration via MESPL, PAN-India network >19k pincodes) and Enterprise Mobility. Partnerships like L’Oréal (women-operated warehouse in Indore) boost capabilities in FMCG/e-commerce.
  • Balance Sheet Deleveraging: Rights issue proceeds repaid debt, reduced finance costs (H1 consol ₹44 Cr vs. FY25 ₹81 Cr annualized). Cash from ops improved standalone (₹5 Cr H1).
  • Asset-Light, Tech-Driven Model: In-house WMS for real-time visibility; investments in subs (₹50 Cr approved for MESPL) support scalability without heavy capex.
  • ESG & Investor Engagement: Initiatives like all-women warehouse enhance brand; frequent analyst meets signal interest.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Profitability Pressure: SCM segment chronically loss-making (-₹6.9 Cr Q2; high opex ₹1,446 Cr Q2, dep. ₹136 Cr H1, ECL ₹14 Cr H1). Consolidated EBITDA negative; Enterprise Mobility profitable but small (5% revenue).
  • Margin Squeeze: Operating expenses up 13% YoY (fuel/labor inflation?); high lease liabilities (₹603 Cr consol).
  • Cash Flow Volatility: Consol ops cash used ₹36 Cr H1 (high receivables ₹712 Cr, tax outflows); heavy capex ₹75 Cr H1.

Growth Prospects

  • High (Medium-Term): 10-15% revenue CAGR potential from logistics boom (e-commerce, auto/FMCG recovery). SCM dominance + mobility (e.g., Rivigo B2B express) positions for market share gains. Rights issue funds for corporate purposes/capex; sub expansions (MESPL turnover ₹364 Cr FY25). FY26 guidance implied via results: sustained growth post-debt reduction.
  • Catalysts: Tech differentiation, regional hubs (e.g., Indore for Central India), M&A history (Rivigo acquisition).

Overall Summary: MLL is at an inflection point—strong revenue tailwinds and deleveraged balance sheet support growth prospects (target: EBITDA positivity FY26+), but SCM profitability headwinds persist as key drag (consol losses). Risks tilted operational; monitor Q3 results for margin traction. Investment Stance: Accumulate on dips for growth recovery (post-rights issue liquidity). CMP context needed for valuation, but FY25 EV/EBITDA ~15x peers; forward multiples attractive if losses narrow.
