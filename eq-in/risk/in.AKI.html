Summary Analysis of AKI India Limited (AKI) - Q2 & H1 FY26 (Ended Sep 30, 2025)

AKI India Limited, a small-cap leather & leather goods manufacturer (BSE/NSE: AKI/542020), reported steady topline growth in Q2 but flat H1 standalone sales amid working capital pressures. Consolidated figures reflect stronger group performance via subsidiaries (AKI UK Ltd.) and JV (AKI Castil Shoes LLP). Equity base expanded significantly via fresh issues, supporting capex. Unaudited results (limited review unqualified, with notes on unreviewed sub/JV data) show PAT growth but negative operating cash flows. Below is a structured analysis of headwinds, tailwinds, growth prospects, and key risks.

Tailwinds (Positive Factors)

  • Revenue Momentum: Standalone Q2 sales surged 62% QoQ (₹1838L vs ₹1136L) and 65% YoY; consolidated H1 sales up 32% YoY (₹4519L vs ₹3434L), driven by group entities (e.g., UK sub revenue ~₹240L equivalent). Leather exports/domestic demand supportive.
  • Profitability Improvement: Standalone H1 PBT ₹96L (up from ₹36L ex-exceptional YoY); consolidated PAT ₹85L (up 3% YoY). Margins resilient despite cost inflation (e.g., materials 90% of expenses).
  • Strong Capital Infusion: Equity share capital up 17% (₹2064L vs ₹1771L); other equity doubled (₹6550L standalone). H1 financing inflows ₹2729L (standalone), mainly from share issues (₹293L capital + ₹2452L premium) and warrants conversion – funds capex/CWIP (₹878L).
  • Balance Sheet Strengthening: Total assets +20% YoY (₹13,261L); cash +32% (₹312L). ROE implied positive from equity growth.
  • Operational Scale: PPE up 6% (₹1230L); inventories +18% signals production ramp-up.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Working Capital Drag: Massive H1 outflows (standalone ops cash -₹2519L; cons. -₹3552L) from inventories +18% (₹3474L), loans/advances +103% (₹3192L), and trade payables -22% (₹1544L). Receivables stable but high at 74% of sales.
  • Flat Standalone Growth: H1 sales flat YoY (₹2974L vs ₹2986L); cost of materials volatile (Q1 ₹1740L → Q2 ₹953L).
  • Margin Pressure: Employee/finance costs up 25%/36% YoY; power/other expenses elevated. Negative change in inventories aided profits but signals inefficiency.
  • Dilution Risk: Aggressive equity raises (H1 proceeds ~₹2745L) to fund ops/capex – EPS diluted slightly (H1 0.06 vs 0.09 YoY).
  • Subsidiary/JV Dependency: Consolidated boosts topline (52% higher sales), but unreviewed UK sub/JV data (not material per auditors) adds opacity.

Growth Prospects

  • High (Medium-Term: 20-30% CAGR Potential): Leather sector tailwinds from rising footwear/apparel demand (India exports +15% YoY industry-wide); international expansion via UK sub (profitable PBT ~₹23L H1) and JV. Capex/CWIP (₹878L, +0.1% QoQ) positions for capacity addition.
  • Funded Expansion: Fresh capital (₹2.7B+ inflows) + borrowings +146% supports scaling; CWIP stable signals near-term commissioning.
  • Export Leverage: Single-segment focus (IND AS 108 exempt); group revenue mix diversifies risks. H1 sales growth + PAT trajectory suggests FY26 topline >₹15,000L (vs FY25 ₹7507L annualized).
  • Upside Catalysts: Warrants conversion, order book visibility, forex gains (GBP strength for UK sub).
