Latent View Analytics Limited (LATENTVIEW) Analysis

Company Overview: Latent View Analytics Limited (NSE/BSE: LATENTVIEW) is a global data analytics, AI, and data engineering firm serving Fortune 500 clients in Technology, Financial Services, CPG/Retail, Healthcare, and Industrials. It reported strong Q2FY26 results (ended Sep 30, 2025): consolidated revenue ₹2,575 Mn (+23.2% YoY, +9.1% QoQ; $29.6 Mn), Adjusted EBITDA ₹580 Mn (22.5% margin), PAT ₹457 Mn (16.6% margin). H1FY26 revenue ₹4,936 Mn (+27% YoY). 11th straight quarter of growth, with 76% revenue from >5-year clients and 2x revenue since FY22 listing.

Tailwinds (Positive Factors)

  • Robust Revenue Momentum: Consistent double-digit growth (QoQ 9.1%, YoY 23.2%; USD QoQ 7.2%, YoY 18.8%). Key verticals shining: Financial Services (+94% YoY), CPG/Retail (+23% YoY). Added 4 new logos; 4.2% YoY growth from existing clients.
  • Margin Resilience: Adjusted EBITDA margin stable at 22.5% (flat YoY/QoQ), driven by operational efficiency despite investments. Historical CAGR 19% with improving profitability (FY25 EBITDA 23.7%).
  • Strategic Positioning: AI-led (GenAI via RAISE framework, Agentic AI); Databricks partnership scaling data engineering. Nearshoring expansion (Canada, Mexico, LATAM) enhances client proximity/delivery. Global footprint (1,650+ employees across NA, LATAM, APAC, EU/UK).
  • Client Stickiness & Recognition: 60% revenue from top 5 clients (stable), 85% from top 20. Partnerships (Databricks, NVIDIA, Microsoft); awards (Forrester Leader in multiple quadrants, Great Place to Work).
  • Capital Strength: Strong balance sheet (cash ₹1,272 Mn, investments ₹8,920 Mn); low debt.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Margin Pressures: Reported EBITDA dipped QoQ due to higher visa/marketing costs, nearshoring utilization fluctuations (+1.5% forex impact, -0.4% nearshoring). PAT QoQ -9.6% from one-offs.
  • Client Dependency: High concentration (top 5: 60%, top 10: 74%) exposes to key client risks. Revenue mix: USA 57%, Financials/CPG dominant.
  • Investment Drag: Heavy capex on AI R&D, talent upskilling, GCC expansion, and nearshoring could pressure short-term margins.
  • Macro/Operational: Potential forex volatility (USD strength aided Rupee growth); rising employee costs (up 17% YoY); garbled attrition data hints at IT sector pressures.

Growth Prospects

  • High Potential (Medium-Term 3 Years): AI/GenAI focus to drive scalable solutions; Databricks for data engineering; expand Marketing Analytics, nearshore (LATAM growth post-Decision Point acquisition synergies), APAC GCC. Target: Deepen “diamond” accounts, add logos in high-growth verticals.
  • Quantifiable Outlook: FY25 revenue $111 Mn (+29% YoY); H1FY26 $57 Mn trajectory suggests FY26 >$120 Mn. 19% historical CAGR sustainable via 76% repeat revenue + AI tailwinds.
  • Catalysts: New wins (e.g., sales forecasting, Databricks pipelines); events/roadshows; advisory council additions. Post-Decision Point (1-year synergies realized in CPG/Retail).

Overall Summary: Strong Buy/Bullish Outlook. Tailwinds dominate with proven growth engine (11 quarters running), AI differentiation, and stable margins amid investments. FY26 growth >20% likely, targeting $120-130 Mn revenue. Headwinds (concentration, costs) manageable short-term; risks tilted operational/macro but mitigated by balance sheet/strategy. Key monitor: Q3 utilization, AI deal wins. Trading at reasonable multiples given 19% CAGR track record. (Analysis based solely on provided docs; current market price/context not considered.)
