Summary Analysis for Latent View Analytics Limited (LATENTVIEW)

Tailwinds (Positive Momentum): - Robust Financial Performance: Q2FY26 revenue grew 23.2% YoY (₹2,575 Mn) and 9.1% QoQ, marking 11 consecutive quarters of growth. Adjusted EBITDA margin stable at 22.5% (up 0.4% QoQ). PAT at ₹457 Mn (+14.6% YoY). Strong USD revenue growth (7.2% QoQ, 18.8% YoY), driven by Financial Services (+94% YoY) and Consumer/Retail (+23% YoY). - Client Stickiness & Expansion: 76% revenue from >5-year clients; 4.2% YoY growth from existing base. Added 4 new logos. Top clients contribute reliably (Top 5: 60%). - Strategic Positioning: AI/GenAI focus (RAISE framework, Agentic AI leadership per Forrester/AIM). Key partnerships (Databricks, NVIDIA, Microsoft). Decision Point acquisition synergies evident in CPG/Retail. Global footprint (1,650+ employees across NA, LATAM, APAC, EU/UK) with nearshoring gains (Canada/Mexico/LATAM). - Recognition & Talent: Multiple awards (Forrester Leader, Great Place to Work). ESOP allotments signal employee alignment (share capital up to ₹20.69 Cr).

Headwinds (Challenges): - Margin Pressures: EBITDA margin dip from utilization fluctuations, nearshoring ramp-up, and higher visa/marketing costs (offset by operational rigor). - Operational Scaling: Investments in AI CoE, Databricks, and GCCs may pressure short-term margins. Attrition trends (historical LTM ~20-25%) and talent upskilling needs in competitive AI market. - Geographic/Revenue Concentration: 57% revenue from USA; APAC/LATAM growth nascent but forex-exposed (e.g., unrealized FX gains noted). - Macro Sensitivity: Potential slowdown in US/enterprise spending could impact high-concentration clients.

Growth Prospects: - High Visibility: Historical 19% CAGR since FY22 (revenue 2.02x from FY22). H1FY26 already at ~32% YoY pace. Targets: Deepen “diamond” accounts, scale GenAI/nearshore (LATAM/Canada), expand APAC GCCs, and marketing analytics. - AI/Data Engineering Tailwinds: Databricks traction, Agentic AI leadership, and client wins (e.g., sales forecasting, data pipelines) position for enterprise AI adoption. Nearshoring to boost efficiency/client proximity. - Pipeline Strength: 4 new logos Q2; focus on Fortune 500 (40+ clients). LTM revenue distribution shows scalable buckets (>₹500 Mn clients: 4). - FY26 Outlook: Sustained double-digit growth expected, with R&D investments yielding differentiated AI solutions.

Key Risks: - Client Concentration: Top 10 clients = 74% revenue; loss of key accounts (e.g., Top 5) could materially impact growth. - Execution Risks: Delays in AI/nearshore scaling, integration of acquisitions (Decision Point), or partnership outcomes (Databricks). - External Factors: US economic slowdown, rupee appreciation (USD-heavy), geopolitical/FX volatility. Regulatory (SEBI compliance, tax regime shifts). - Internal Risks: Talent attrition/wars in AI space; margin erosion from inflation/escalations. No UPSI in analyst meets, but competitive intelligence shared. - Market/Competition: Intense rivalry in data analytics/AI from global players; dependency on tech partnerships.

Overall Outlook: Strong tailwinds from AI momentum and execution drive positive growth trajectory (target 20%+ FY26 revenue growth). Headwinds manageable via operational discipline, but client concentration remains a watch item. Bullish on medium-term prospects in GenAI/data engineering.
