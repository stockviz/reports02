Analysis of Loyal Textile Mills Ltd (LOYALTEX)

Overview: Loyal Textile Mills Ltd, a textile manufacturer (yarn, fabrics, technical clothing) with plants in Tamil Nadu and Andhra Pradesh, reported Q2/H1 FY26 (ended Sep 30, 2025) unaudited results showing revenue decline but narrowing losses. Key actions include COO resignation, asset monetization (e.g., windmills, plants), unit sales approvals (AP, Sivaganga), and operational realignment. Balance sheet shows deleveraging (borrowings down 20% YoY to ₹33,094 lakhs) via asset sales and working capital efficiency. Management emphasizes turnaround via high-value segments, with positive JV contribution (Gruppo P&P Loyal Spa).

Headwinds (Challenges Pressuring Performance)

  • Revenue Contraction: Q2 revenue fell 42% YoY to ₹10,654 lakhs (H1: 37% drop to ₹24,197 lakhs), driven by exit from low-margin operations and export disruptions. Volumes appear weak amid inventory rationalization (WIP changes +₹3,531 lakhs in Q2).
  • Persistent Losses: Q2 standalone PAT loss ₹966 lakhs (improved from ₹4,858 lakhs YoY); H1 ₹3,479 lakhs (vs. ₹6,760 lakhs). Consolidated similar. EBITDA reportedly turned positive in Q2 per notes, but pre-exceptional PBT remains negative.
  • Export Tariff Impact: ₹10.22 Cr provision on export inventory due to “recent tariff-related developments on textile imports from India,” signaling US/EU policy risks eroding realizations.
  • Leadership Transition: COO RM Shanmugam resigned (personal reasons); potential short-term operational disruption.
  • High Fixed Costs: Employee expenses (₹5,088 lakhs H1), finance costs (₹1,847 lakhs), and depreciation persist despite scale-down.

Tailwinds (Supportive Factors)

  • Narrowing Losses & Efficiency Gains: Losses halved YoY; Q2 EBITDA positive (per notes). Cost optimization and high-value focus (technical textiles/protective garments) showing traction.
  • Deleveraging & Liquidity Boost: Borrowings reduced ₹8,236 lakhs H1 via repayments; cash from ops ₹6,820 lakhs, investing ₹4,995 lakhs (asset sales ₹4,539 lakhs). Cash equivalents up to ₹1,840 lakhs (from ₹23 lakhs).
  • Asset Monetization Progress: PPE down 33% to ₹17,110 lakhs; held-for-sale up to ₹7,163 lakhs. Exceptional gain ₹10.72 Cr from windmill/plant sales. AGM approved sale of AP/Sivaganga units as “undertakings.”
  • JV Support: Share of profit ₹1,211 lakhs H1 (vs. parent losses), lifting consolidated metrics.
  • Tax Relief: Deferred tax credit ₹972 lakhs H1 aids bottom-line.

Growth Prospects (Opportunities Ahead)

  • Turnaround Strategy: Exiting low-margin ops, consolidating production; focus on high-value technical textiles (strong order book). Management targets “operational profitability” in near term/coming year.
  • Monetization Upside: Ongoing asset sales to fund efficiency/capex; potential ₹10-15 Cr+ gains (based on H1 trends).
  • Market Tailwinds: Domestic textile demand (e.g., protective garments); alternate export markets/pricing to mitigate tariffs. Integrated certifications (ISO) aid premium positioning.
  • EBITDA Trajectory: Positive Q2 EBITDA signals inflection; H1 revenue mix shift could stabilize at ₹12-15k/Q if volumes recover.
  • Valuation Potential: Narrowing losses, debt reduction could re-rate stock amid textile cycle upturn (if cotton prices stabilize).

Key Risks

Operational Plant closures (AP/Sivaganga) risk supply disruptions; COO exit. Single-segment exposure (textiles). Multi-plant footprint; leadership continuity assumed.

Summary: LOYALTEX faces acute headwinds from revenue collapse and export tariffs but shows turnaround signs via 50% loss reduction, debt paydown (20% YoY), and ₹10+ Cr asset gains. Tailwinds from efficiency, JV profits, and strategic sales support deleveraging. Growth hinges on technical textiles execution and order conversion, targeting profitability FY26/27. Investment Stance: High-risk turnaround play; monitor Q3 revenue stabilization and unit sale proceeds. Key metric: H2 EBITDA positivity. Risks (execution, tariffs) outweigh near-term upsides unless macro textile sentiment improves. (Data as of Nov 11, 2025 filings.)
