Summary Analysis for Rhetan TMT Limited (NSE: RHETAN, BSE: 543590)

Rhetan TMT Limited, a Gujarat-based TMT bars manufacturer (single segment), recently listed on NSE mainboard on Sep 26, 2025 post-IPO. FY25 was strong (revenue ₹3,716L, PAT ₹495L), but H1 FY26 shows revenue collapse offset by high “other income,” leading to robust reported PAT (H1 PAT ₹363L). Balance sheet expanded via debt/equity, but cash flows deteriorated. AGM resolutions (Sep 25, 2025) passed unanimously, approving key RPTs with related entities.

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Recent Listing & Capital Infusion: IPO proceeds likely boosted equity (stable at ₹7,969L) and funded non-current borrowings surge (₹2,567L vs ₹748L YoY). CWIP up 17% signals capex/expansion potential.
  • Profitability Resilience: Q2/H1 PAT margins ~60%/37% despite revenue drop, driven by ₹313L other income (Q2: ₹245L spike, possibly interest/dividends from investments/loans). EPS steady at ₹0.05 (H1).
  • Shareholder Approval: 100% pass rate on 8 AGM resolutions (73% voter turnout overall; 100% favor in ordinary resolutions). RPTs with 5 entities (e.g., Ashoka Metcast) approved by public shareholders (promoters recused).
  • Steel Sector Backdrop: Infra push (govt schemes like PMAY, highways) supports TMT demand; Ind AS adoption smooth.

Headwinds (Negative Pressures)

  • Revenue Collapse: H1 revenue ₹979L (-60% YoY from ₹2,429L); Q2 ₹476L (-4% QoQ). Materials consumed down, but inventory build-up (+7% to ₹4,259L) and negative inventory change (-₹190L) indicate weak sales/demand.
  • Working Capital Strain: Other current assets ballooned ₹1,794L (likely advances), trade receivables +3% (₹2,346L), driving negative CFO (-₹359L). Total expenses controlled, but ops cash burn persists.
  • Sequential Weakness: Q1 revenue ₹504L → Q2 ₹476L; other expenses volatile (Q1 ₹165L vs Q2 ₹20L).
  • Low Operating Leverage: Core ops profit reliant on non-ops income; FY25 revenue was 10x higher, highlighting cyclical vulnerability.

Growth Prospects

  • Moderate (Infrastructure-Led Recovery): Steel demand projected 7-9% CAGR (FY26-28) on housing/infra capex (₹11L cr govt budget). Capacity expansion (PPE ₹1,603L, CWIP ₹176L) could scale output if demand rebounds.
  • IPO Liquidity: Listing enables growth funding; promoter holding ~62% stable.
  • Upside Catalysts: Normalize revenue to FY25 levels → PAT could double. Other equity up 25% YoY supports reinvestment. Potential for debt optimization post-listing.
  • Base Case: H2 recovery to FY25 run-rate → FY26 revenue ~₹2,500-3,000L, PAT ₹600-800L (EPS ₹0.08-0.10), assuming steel prices stabilize.
