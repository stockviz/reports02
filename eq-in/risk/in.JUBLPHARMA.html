Analysis of Jubilant Pharmova Limited (JUBLPHARMA)

Jubilant Pharmova (JUBLPHARMA) is a diversified pharma player with key segments in Radiopharma, Allergy Immunotherapy, CDMO Sterile Injectables, CRDMO (Drug Discovery + API), Generics, and Proprietary Novel Drugs. Q2 FY26 results show robust YoY growth: Revenue +12% (₹1,966 Cr), EBITDA +13% (17.8% margin), Normalized PAT +21% (6.3% margin). H1 FY26: Revenue +11%, Normalized PAT +32%. Company is on track for Vision 2030 (2x revenue to ₹13,500 Cr by FY30, 23-25% EBITDA margins, zero net debt, high-teens RoCE). Below is a structured analysis based on the provided filings.

Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts)

  • US Tariffs & Onshoring Demand: 82% US revenue; CDMO Sterile Injectables benefits as innovators shift from Europe/China (e.g., strong RFPs for Spokane Line 3). Goods from US/Canada (89% of US sales) largely exempt. Biosecure Act favors India CRDMO.
  • Radiopharma Strength: Ruby-Fill® install base +24% YoY; leadership in SPECT (MAA, DTPA); MIBG Phase 2 dosing complete (launch FY27); 9 new PET/SPECT products (TAM $550 Mn, FY27-29).
  • Capacity Expansions: Spokane Line 3 launched (tech transfer revenue started; full utilization in 3 yrs, peak $160-180 Mn with Lines 3+4); PET radiopharmacies to 9 sites ($50 Mn capex, FY28); CRDMO FTE to 4,000 (FY30).
  • Segmental Momentum: Allergy Immunotherapy margins at 39% (+1,210 bps YoY); CRDMO large-pharma scaling (revenue share 5x); API margins +560 bps to 15%; Generics H1 margins +460 bps to 8%.
  • Financial Discipline: Reduced finance costs; Net Debt/EBITDA at 1.5x (range-bound); RoCE 12% annualized (H1 FY26).

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Competition: SPECT radiopharmacy margins ~1% (high intensity); DTPA generics entry eroding share.
  • Pricing/Operational Pressures: API/Generics pricing headwinds; Montreal shutdown (Q2 impact, restart Q3 FY26; OAI upgrades).
  • Margin Volatility: Product mix shifts (e.g., Radiopharma 44% vs 48%); Generics quarterly variability (though annualizing positively).
  • Capex/Debt Load: Heavy investments (Spokane $300 Mn+, CRDMO $150 Mn); Net Debt up to ₹1,897 Cr (from ₹1,348 Cr); Long-term capex creditors ₹626 Cr.
  • Macro: Forex volatility (91% USD revenue); US drug shortages but execution risks.

Growth Prospects

  • Near-Term (FY26-27): Mid-teens revenue growth; EBITDA margins 17-18%; CDMO Line 3 commercialization (FY27); 6-8 Generics launches/year; PET revenue ramp; MIBG approval.
  • Medium-Term (FY28-30): 2x revenue via expansions (Spokane Line 4, PET sites); CRDMO large-pharma/Biologics (ADCs/mAbs); Allergy international push; Generics non-US scaling (3-4 key markets).
  • Long-Term (Vision 2030): Radiopharma >2x (50%+ margins); CDMO doubling; CRDMO tripling; Zero debt via cash flows. TTM Revenue ₹7,618 Cr (vs FY24 ₹6,703 Cr); on-track trajectory.
  • Pipeline: 85+ CRDMO programs; Novel Drugs (JBI-802 Phase II, JBI-778 Phase I; data CY26, potential licensing).

Summary & Outlook

Bullish with Strong Execution Track Record: Tailwinds from tariffs, expansions, and Radiopharma/CDMO momentum dominate (driving 11-12% H1 growth). Headwinds (competition, temporary shutdowns) are manageable, with improving margins across segments. Growth prospects align with Vision 2030 (already 14% revenue progress from FY24). Key monitor: Debt trajectory and regulatory milestones. Rating Potential: Accumulate/Buy on dips; Target upside 20-30% from current levels (~₹800-900/share implied by growth). Risks tilted toward execution but offset by diversified portfolio (46% Radiopharma, 20% CDMO-SI) and 82% US exposure in high-barrier niches.
