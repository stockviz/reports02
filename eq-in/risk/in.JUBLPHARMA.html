Analysis of Jubilant Pharmova Limited (JUBLPHARMA)

Company Overview: Jubilant Pharmova is a diversified pharma company with key segments in Radiopharma (46% of H1 FY26 revenue), CDMO Sterile Injectables (20%), CRDMO (16%), Allergy Immunotherapy (10%), Generics (9%), and Proprietary Novel Drugs. 93% revenues in USD, 82% from US market. H1 FY26: Revenue ₹3,867 Cr (+11% YoY), EBITDA ₹653 Cr (+13%, margin 16.8%), Normalized PAT ₹227 Cr (+32%). On track for Vision 2030 (2x revenue to ₹13,500 Cr by FY30, EBITDA margin 23-25%, zero net debt, high-teens RoCE). Net Debt/EBITDA at 1.5x (up from 1.1x due to capex).

Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts)

  • US Tariffs & Onshoring: Minimal direct exposure (72% US-origin goods/services tariff-exempt; Canada/India exports also largely exempt). Boost for CDMO Sterile Injectables (Line 3 RFP traction; full utilization in 3 years vs. prior 4; peak revenue potential USD 160-180 Mn from Lines 3+4).
  • Radiopharma Strength: Ruby-Fill® install base +24% YoY; leadership in SPECT (MAA, DTPA); PET expansion (3→9 sites, USD 50 Mn invest, RoCE >20%). US radiopharma market CAGR 20%+.
  • High-Margin Segments: Allergy Immunotherapy margins at 39% (+1,210 bps YoY); CRDMO scaling large pharma clients (revenue share 5x in 1 year).
  • Biosecure Act Tailwind: Favors India CRDMO (drug discovery/API); capacity expansion to 4,000 FTEs (capex USD 150 Mn, RoCE >20%).
  • Financial Momentum: Strong H1 growth across segments; EBITDA margin expansion; cash from ops supports capex; dividend ₹5/share proposed.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Competition: SPECT radiopharmacy margins weak (1%) due to intensity; generics entry in DTPA impacting Radiopharma revenue.
  • Regulatory/Operational Hiccups: Montreal facility shutdown (OAI status; restart Q3 FY26); FDA approvals critical for CDMO Line 3 commercial batches (FY27).
  • Pricing Pressure: Ongoing in Generics/API (industry-wide); H1 Generics revenue flat YoY.
  • Debt Rise: Net Debt up to ₹1,897 Cr (1.5x EBITDA) from capex; range-bound but monitorable.
  • Macro Sensitivity: US pharma growth driven by niche products (GLP-1s); tariff/Biosecure uncertainties could indirectly affect outsourcing.

Growth Prospects

  • Short-Term (FY26): Revenue growth acceleration (H1 +11%; Q2 +12%); EBITDA/PAT margins to improve (Allergy/CRDMO scaling, CDMO Line 3 tech transfer revenue started). Generics profitability sustained (H1 margin 8%, +460 bps).
  • Medium-Term (FY27-29): Radiopharma new launches (9 products, TAM USD 550 Mn); CDMO Lines 3/4 full ramp-up; CRDMO biologics/ADCs via Pierre Fabre; Generics 6-8 launches/year.
  • Long-Term (Vision 2030): 2x revenue via capex (Spokane USD 300 Mn, CRDMO USD 150 Mn); zero debt; high-teens RoCE. Novel Drugs: Phase II JBI-802 data CY26 (TAM USD 3.3 Bn US); potential licensing.
  • Pipeline: Strong in PET/SPECT/Therapeutics; US radiopharma USD 20 Bn market; CDMO sterile fill TAM USD 20 Bn by FY27.

Overall Outlook: Positive. Strong H1 momentum, tariff tailwinds, and execution on high-ROCE capex position JUBLPHARMA for 10-15%+ CAGR to FY30. Risks manageable via diversification and track record. Stock likely to re-rate on sustained margins/debt reduction. Monitor Q3 CDMO progress and debt metrics.
