Analysis of Gandhar Oil Refinery (India) Ltd (GANDHAR) - Q2 & H1 FY26

Gandhar Oil, India’s largest white oil producer by revenue (top-5 globally), focuses on high-margin PHPO (Personal care, Healthcare, Performance Oils ~49% revenue), lubricants (~28%), PIO (Process & Insulating Oils ~10%), and channel sales (~13%). FY26 H1 showed resilient sequential growth amid global softness, with revenue flat YoY but EBITDA/PAT up, driven by domestic demand and cost discipline. Key metrics: H1 volumes +9% YoY (261k KL), EBITDA margin improved, debt negligible, cash ~₹70-80 Cr.

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Robust Domestic Demand: Strong manufacturing, pharma/personal care, and power sector (DISCOMs/transformers) offset global weakness. PHPO (USP/FDA-compliant) dominates (47-49% revenue), fueled by consumer/healthcare growth.
  • Margin Expansion: Q2 gross spread ₹8,662/KL (up QoQ); EBITDA +43% QoQ via better realizations, product mix, lower finance costs (non-LC suppliers, SOFR <5%), and opex discipline. Auto lubes/transformer oils offer highest margins.
  • Operational Efficiency: High utilization (Taloja 95%, Silvassa 85-90%); JIT inventory minimizes crude volatility losses. Price pass-through with marquee clients (P&G, Unilever, etc.).
  • Strategic Wins: BHEL transformer oil contract (₹24.6 Cr over 2 yrs); Silvassa capex to add 19k KL auto lubes/annum (high-margin).
  • Healthy Balance Sheet: Current ratio strong, D/E negligible; ₹70-80 Cr cash for flexibility/inorganic pursuits.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Global Pressures: Export markets hit by consumption softness, geopolitics (Red Sea stabilizing but freight elevated), logistics delays → WC days up to 85 (inventory 58, receivables 68).
  • Margin Squeeze History: Post-IPO (4-5 qtrs), revenues held but margins pressured (industry-wide); Q2 recovery but EBITDA margins still industry-standard (~6%).
  • Capacity Ramp: UAE (Texol) at 70-72% utilization; full ramp needs 1.5-2 yrs.
  • Input Volatility: Base oil prices lag crude by 45-60 days (~45-50% correlation); partial hedging via pass-through/index-linked pricing.
