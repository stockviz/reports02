Thomas Cook (India) Ltd. (THOMASCOOK) Analysis

Performance Snapshot (Q2 & H1 FY26, ended Sep 30, 2025): Consolidated revenue grew 9% YoY to ₹44,818 Mn (Q2: +3% to ₹20,738 Mn), driven by Travel & Related Services (+12%). PBT stable at ₹2,211 Mn (+1% H1 YoY) despite headwinds. Strong balance sheet: Cash ₹23.9 Bn (up from ₹20.7 Bn FY25), gross debt ₹2.7 Bn, ROE ~12%. EBIT mix: Travel 52%, Financials 33%, Leisure 13%, DEI 2%.

Headwinds (Short-term Pressures)
  • Geopolitical Disruptions: Middle East tensions subdued inbound; India-Pakistan advisories hit DMS (Nepal protests); USA/SE Asia spillovers muted bookings. DEI revenue -6% YoY due to Middle East home markets.
  • Weather & Seasonality: Monsoon floods/landslides in North India (hills ~21% resorts impacted); Q2 soft period for Leisure (jungle closures, fewer auspicious dates).
  • Margin Compression: Travel EBIT -16% Q2 (3.9% margin vs 4.9% prior); Leisure EBIT -44% Q2 (new resorts setup costs, occupancy ramp-up); overall segment EBIT -3% H1.
  • Forex Slowdown: RBI LRS education outflows -22% YoY (Apr-Aug 2025); though retail +13% via holidays/digital.
  • External Factors: Protests (Nepal), off-peak Middle East inbound.
Tailwinds (Positive Momentum)
  • Revenue Resilience: Travel B2B +12% H1 (DMS intl +16%); Forex retail +13% Q2 (digital: app +25%, WhatsApp +108%); Sterling resorts +13% revenue, 11% REVPAR growth.
  • Financial Strength: Net cash position robust (₹23.9 Bn); low leverage (interest coverage 5.1x); awards for Treasury (Adam Smith Asia 2025).
  • Digital/Operational Wins: TC Pay app (Ghar Baithe Forex, Google Pay integration); Blinkit forex delivery; corporate self-booking adoption 59%.
  • Expansion Execution: Sterling added 7 resorts Q2 (total 69, +117% rooms); Nature Trails revenue +14% Q2.
  • Leadership Stability: New Chairman (Gopalakrishnan Soundarajan); ESOP grants/amendments for talent retention.
Growth Prospects (Medium-Term Opportunities)
  • Diversified Segments: Travel (79% revenue) poised for festive/winter rebound (Europe Summer 2026 launch: 60+ tours, early-bird discounts); Forex education/holidays recovery; Leisure H2 momentum (fewer weather risks, +30% auspicious dates); DEI 9 new partnerships.
  • Strategic Initiatives: JV addendum/investment ₹27 Cr; MoUs (Queensland Tourism, Disney Cruises); spiritual/festival/Van tours; China portfolio expansion.
  • Digital & B2B Scale: Corporate Travel non-air +20%; MICE 200+ events; DMS Asia +18% YoY.
  • Macro Tailwinds: Govt push (GST 2.0, tourism infra); rising discretionary spends; Fairfax backing (64% stake).
  • Talent/Expansion: ESOP pool hikes (e.g., ESOP 2024-EXECOM to 65.7L options); Sterling pipeline for 94 resorts.
  • Outlook: MD commentary: H2 growth from pent-up demand; FY26 revenue potential >₹82 Bn FY25 base.
Key Risks
Risk Category Description Mitigation
Geopolitical/External Escalating tensions (Middle East, regional conflicts) disrupting 20-30% inbound/outbound. Diversified DMS (94% intl); digital forex buffers.
Economic/Cyclical Discretionary spend sensitivity; LRS curbs on forex outflows. Strong cash (20% ROE enabler); 16 brands/900 touchpoints.
Operational New resort ramp-up (occupancy/costs); execution in expansions/JV. Proven track record (23rd profitable quarter); low debt.
Regulatory/Compliance SEBI ESOP approvals; FEMA forex limits; Listing Regs. Proactive disclosures; Postal Ballot for amendments.
Dilution/Financial ESOP increase (dilution ~10-12% if fully exercised). Long vesting (3-7 yrs); performance-linked.
Competition Intense in travel/forex (MakeMyTrip, others). Niche (MICE, DMS global); omnichannel (B2C/B2B).

Overall View: Resilient performer amid macro headwinds (geopolitics/weather), with tailwinds from cash fortress, digital traction, and diversification fueling H2/FY26 growth (target: sustain 10%+ revenue CAGR). Risks tilted external but mitigated by balance sheet strength. Buy/Hold for long-term compounding (est. FY26 PBT ₹4-5 Bn). Monitor geopolitics/Q3 execution.
