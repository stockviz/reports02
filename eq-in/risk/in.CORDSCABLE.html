Analysis of Cords Cable Industries Ltd. (CORDSCABLE)

Company Overview: Cords Cable Industries Ltd. is a manufacturer of cables with multiple production units in Bhiwadi, Rajasthan. FY25 revenue: ₹794 Cr (PAT: ₹14.7 Cr). H1 FY26 (ended Sep 30, 2025) shows strong growth: Revenue ₹451 Cr (+27% YoY), PAT ₹7.4 Cr (+35% YoY). Single-segment operations (cables). Market cap implied via scrip codes (NSE: CORDSCABLE, BSE: 532941).

Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts)

  • Robust Financial Performance: Q2 FY26 revenue ₹226 Cr (+23% YoY), H1 annualized ~₹90 Cr (track to exceed FY25). PAT margins stable ~1.6-2%. Operating cash flow strong at ₹2.67 Cr (H1 FY26), driven by efficient working capital (despite inventory build).
  • Balance Sheet Strength: Equity up to ₹189 Cr (from ₹182 Cr FY25-end). Total assets ₹434 Cr (+3%). Borrowings reduced (net -₹788K H1), leverage easing. Positive free cash flow post-capex.
  • Operational Scale: 16+ units in industrial hubs signal capacity expansion. Inventory up 31% YoY (₹96 Cr) indicates production ramp-up for demand.
  • Governance & Shareholder Actions: AGM (Sep 29, 2025) approved MD reappointment (Naveen Sawhney, high salary + commission), new WTD (Pawan Kumar Maheswari), Independent Director (Praveen Verma), dividend (₹1/share, ₹1.29 Cr payout), cost/statutory auditors. Dividend yield ~1-2% attractive.
  • Regulatory Relief: GST penalty slashed 99% (₹1.05 Cr → ₹50K) on MD/ex-WTD; no material financial/ops impact claimed.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Past Tax Demand: ITC disallowance (₹1.76 Cr deposited under protest for FY19-21). Company appealing rectification order; potential cash outflow/liability if lost.
  • Governance Red Flags: AGM rejected salary hikes (₹50K/month each) for promoter relatives (Varun/Gaurav Sawhney, VPs Marketing/Finance) due to related-party abstentions (promoters held 52% stake). Signals shareholder pushback on family perks.
  • High Costs: Finance costs ₹13.6 Cr H1 (up YoY), ~3% of revenue. Employee/other expenses rising with scale.
  • Working Capital Strain: Trade receivables steady ~₹200 Cr (45-50 days DSO). Inventory surge could pressure if sales slow.

Growth Prospects

  • High Visibility: H1 growth (revenue +27%, EBITDA implied ~₹10 Cr) on strong order book/execution. Cable demand from infra/power sectors (India capex cycle tailwind). Annualized FY26 revenue potential ₹90+ Cr (+13% YoY).
  • Expansion Leverage: Multi-unit setup (all in Bhiwadi) enables scale. Positive cash flow supports capex (₹528K H1, PPE stable ₹75 Cr).
  • Profitability Upside: Margins resilient (despite costs); tax efficiency (deferred tax credits). Dividend policy sustained.
  • Outlook: 15-20% revenue CAGR feasible if infra spend continues. EPS H1 ₹5.71 (FY25 ₹11.25); potential FY26 EPS ₹13+.
