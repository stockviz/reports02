Finolex Cables Limited (FINCABLES) Analysis: Q2/H1 FY26 (Sep 2025)

Finolex Cables reported modest revenue growth (+5% YoY Q2; +9% H1) amid segment-specific challenges, but delivered strong profitability (EBITDA +26% YoY Q2; PAT +35% YoY standalone Q2) through pricing discipline and cost control. Electrical cables drove core performance, while communication cables lagged. Balance sheet remains robust (net WC ~33 days, low debt). No formal guidance provided. Below is a structured summary of headwinds, tailwinds, growth prospects, and key risks.

Headwinds (Challenges Impacting Performance)

  • Demand Moderation in Key Segments: Prolonged monsoon depressed agricultural wires (~10-12% of revenue) and fans; real estate slowdown led to flat building wires and fewer new launches.
  • Muted Communication Cables: Volumes flat across lines; no meaningful BharatNet orders (lost prior tenders); low fiber prices depressed revenues; protracted contract negotiations delayed catch-up.
  • Lagging Peer Growth: Modest topline (+5-9%) vs. double-digit peer growth; smaller cables base (~Rs200-250 Cr vs. INR25k Cr market) limits scale.
  • Commodity Volatility: Copper prices dipped early Q2 then surged (Sep pricing action of 3-3.5%); ongoing fluctuations pressure margins.
  • FMEG Headwinds: Monsoon hit fans; persistent LED price erosion in lighting (now stabilizing).
  • WC Expansion: H1 net WC days at 33 (vs. 22 prior; inventory 65 days due to disruptions).

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Margin Expansion: EBITDA margins improved via pricing actions and efficiency; electrical cables EBIT ~11%; ahead-of-peers WC management.
  • Cables Volume Surge: +60% YoY in power cables (industrial/solar/auto); diversified portfolio resilient.
  • Strong Balance Sheet: Nil debt (ex-lease), high cash/investments; Capex funded internally (Rs100 Cr H1).
  • Brand & Retail Strength: Premium positioning protects against new entrants (e.g., UltraTech); ad spend up (Rs20 Cr Q2) for safety/longevity messaging.
  • Capex Progress: Preform plant trials imminent (margin-accretive via import substitution); fiber draw completion by Mar26 to lift comm margins to 8-10%.
  • Profitability Beat: PAT +21-35% YoY despite modest revenue; other income supportive.

Growth Prospects

Segment Key Opportunities Outlook
Electrical Cables (~85% revenue) Infra/industrial demand; solar/auto ramp-up; capacity ~70% utilized (potential >Rs6,500 Cr peak, adjusted higher post-expansions). Strong; double-digit vol. growth in cables to offset wires flatness.
Communication Cables (~8%) BharatNet Phase-3 retenders (Sep); 7 state tenders (next 2-4 months); private (Bharti ~60% capacity booked); post-capex margins to mid-teens. Recovery H2FY26+; follow-up with tender winners lacking capacity.
FMEG/Others (~7%) New launches (fans/switches/water heaters); conduits gaining traction; target Rs500 Cr by FY28. Steady; in-house mfg. post-volume scale-up.
Overall Real estate/infra revival (1-2Q lag); housing implicit demand; diversified portfolio (wires/cables/FMEG). H1 momentum into FY26; no guidance but “modest” growth implied; peers’ strength highlights market potential.

Key Risks

Risk Category Details Mitigation
Competition Big entrants (UltraTech Q3 start; others announced); project discounting erodes premiums. Brand entrenchment; selective bidding (“not at any cost”).
Cyclical/Macro Real estate/infra slowdown; monsoon/agri variability; govt tender lumpiness (BSNL losses). Diversification (industrial/solar/private telecom).
Commodity/Execution Copper volatility; capex delays (preform/fiber trials). Pricing agility; strong WC buffers.
Regulatory/Other Minor GST demand (Rs1.87L, appealable; negligible impact); no major litigation updates. Robust compliance; low exposure.
Strategic No guidance; FMEG missing targets historically. Capex focus; committed to FMEG scale.

Overall Outlook: Resilient amid headwinds, with tailwinds from margins/capex positioning for H2 acceleration. Modest topline but superior profitability sustains ROE; monitor competition and tenders. Stock trading at premium valuations justified by balance sheet/brand.
