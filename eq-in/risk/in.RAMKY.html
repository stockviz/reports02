Summary Analysis of Ramky Infrastructure Limited (RAMKY)

Ramky Infrastructure Limited (NSE: RAMKY, BSE: 533262), a leading EPC player in water/wastewater, industrial infrastructure, urban solutions, and buildings, reported robust Q2 FY26 (Sep 2025) results with consolidated revenue of ₹472 Cr (up QoQ), EBITDA margins ~30% (Q2 standalone), and PAT of ₹76 Cr (H1). Order book stands at ~₹9,200 Cr (2.5-3x FY26 revenue guidance of ₹2,400 Cr), bolstered by a new ₹2,085 Cr HAM water project. The company exited restructuring in Jul 2025, with nil term debt and strong cash flows. However, legacy subsidiary issues persist. Below is a structured analysis of headwinds, tailwinds, growth prospects, and key risks.

Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts)

  • Strong Order Visibility & Execution Momentum: ₹9,200 Cr order book (Industrial: ₹4,000 Cr; Water/Waste: ₹2,700 Cr; Buildings: ₹2,000 Cr; Others: ₹500 Cr), with ~44% O&M (high-margin, annuity-like). H1 revenue ramp-up expected in H2 FY26; FY27/FY28 guided by key projects (e.g., Mallanna Sagar water scheme).
  • High-Margin Mix & Cost Discipline: EBITDA margins 21-30% (peer-leading), driven by O&M/HAM (vs. pure EPC). Q2 standalone EBITDA ₹117 Cr on ₹445 Cr revenue (~26%). Other income sustainable at 75-90% of Q2 levels (interest from group HAM lending, scrap sales).
  • Debt Reduction & Balance Sheet Strength: Nil term debt; consolidated debt ~₹163 Cr (H1). Positive cash flows (₹1,621 Mn operating CF H1 FY26); equity growth supports funding.
  • Government Tailwinds: Focus on water (24x7 supply, AMRUT/Jal Jeevan), industrial corridors/parks aligns with expertise (e.g., role-model pharma park in AP). Recent ₹2,085 Cr HAM award from HMWSSB.
  • Asset Monetization: Progress on Visakha Pharmacity sale (due diligence ongoing, closure by Mar 2026); Srinagar Banihal receivable discounting (~₹X Cr expected by Mar 2026).

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Revenue Volatility: H1 FY26 revenue declined YoY (standalone ₹800 Cr executed vs. FY26 guidance ₹2,400 Cr) due to HAM project transitions (engineering phase delays). Q2 margins dipped ex-other income (17.5% vs. 24% YoY).
  • Subsidiary Drag: Legacy toll assets (Hospet Chitradurga terminated; Sehore Kosmi on liquidation basis) and Srinagar Banihal (NHAI deductions ₹2,523 Mn under arbitration) weigh on consolidated results. Unreviewed subsidiaries contribute ~₹33 Bn assets but limited transparency.
  • Related Party Exposure: Recent postal ballot approval for ₹150 Cr ICD to Numen Growth Partners (related) and ₹800 Cr corporate guarantee to Srinagar Banihal—adds balance sheet risk amid arbitration uncertainties.
  • Project Funding Pressures: New HAM Capex ₹1,600 Cr (40% client-funded, 30:70 debt:equity); financial closure by Jan 2026. Rising input costs/execution delays could impact.

Growth Prospects

  • Order Inflows: ₹2,000 Cr+ L1 positions (Q3/Q4 FY26); $1 Bn (~₹8,300 Cr) bid pipeline (water/waste, industrial parks) over next 12 months. Target ₹12,000 Cr order book by FY27 end (2.5x revenue visibility).
  • Segment Expansion: | Segment | Pipeline/Opportunities | |———|————————| | Water/Wastewater | HAM priority; ZLD/desal bids; 24x7 schemes (multi-state). | | Industrial | Parks in Maharashtra (life sciences), Bihar/Telangana/Himachal; govt corridors. | | International | ME/Africa (IWP/IPP water projects, industrial parks) in next 2 quarters. |
  • Revenue/EBITDA Outlook: FY26 revenue ₹2,400 Cr (H2 ramp); EBITDA ₹500 Cr (~21%). FY27+ growth from O&M annuity, new HAM execution.
  • Strategic Shifts: New leadership (CEO Sunil Nair Sep 2025) prioritizing EPC diversification, supply chain, international; branding refresh; dividend intent.

Key Risks

Risk Category Details Mitigation
Execution/Project Large HAM/EPC delays, cost overruns (e.g., ₹2,085 Cr project Capex). Selective bidding but pipeline intensity high. In-house execution (70%+), de-risked bids, 36-month visibility.
Legal/Arbitration Srinagar Banihal (₹2,523 Mn NHAI claims); Sehore/Hospet resolutions pending. Ramky City (240 acres ED-attached). Advanced discussions; NOC applied; arbitration optimistic.
Funding/Liquidity HAM equity needs; related party ICD/CG (₹950 Cr total). Cash flows strong; innovative funding (non-bank); 40% client payments.
Regulatory/External Govt tender slowdowns, input inflation, geopolitical (intl expansion). ESG/compliance in water/industrial. Selective HAM focus; intl negotiated deals.
Financial Reporting Auditor emphasis on subsidiaries; unreviewed entities (~₹33 Bn assets). Management confident; standard reclassification post-restructuring.

Overall Outlook: Positive with Moderate Risks. Tailwinds from order book, margins, and policy support outweigh headwinds. Growth hinges on H2 execution and new wins (high probability per mgmt). Risks concentrated in legacy subsidiaries—monitor arbitrations. Target FY26 PBT/EPS upside if inflows materialize; stock could re-rate on debt-free status and intl traction. Investors: Favor for 20-25% revenue CAGR potential.
