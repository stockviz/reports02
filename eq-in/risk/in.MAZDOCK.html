MAZDOCK (Mazagon Dock Shipbuilders Ltd.) Analysis: Q2/H1 FY26

Summary Overview:
MAZDOCK reported solid Q2 standalone revenue growth (+6% YoY to ₹2,929 Cr) and PAT surge (+27% YoY to ₹715 Cr), driven by project completions (e.g., P17A frigates). H1 revenue grew 9% YoY to ₹5,555 Cr, but PAT dipped 8% due to prior provisions (₹1,000 Cr for onerous contracts). Order book stands at ₹27,415 Cr (visibility ~2-3 years). Board approved ₹6/share interim dividend (record date Nov 4, 2025). Management guides FY26 revenue at ~₹12,500 Cr (+~10% YoY), with acceleration post new orders. Navratna PSU with strong defense execution track record (delivered 6 Scorpene subs, 7 destroyers ahead of schedule).

Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts)

  • Robust Order Pipeline & Visibility: Massive Navy tenders (~₹2-3 lakh Cr potential): P75 follow-on subs (immediate execution), P75I (contract by FY26-end), LPD (₹35-40k Cr with Swan MoU), MCMV (₹40k Cr), P17B frigates (RFP soon), next-gen destroyers (₹70-80k Cr). Short-term: Commercial aggregated demand (112 vessels), ONGC offshore. Order book to exceed ₹1 lakh Cr by FY27.
  • High Margins & Efficiency: 15%+ EBITDA margins expected on familiar projects (frigates/destroyers/subs); Q2 op. margin ~24%. Zero debt, consistent profitability (20+ years), strong cash reserves (₹4,318 Cr cash equiv., ₹8,673 Cr bank balances).
  • Strategic Expansions: Colombo Dockyard acquisition (~₹450 Cr outflow, ramp to ₹1,500 Cr revenue); Tuticorin greenfield shipyard (₹5k Cr phase 1, dual-use for VLCCs/naval); diversification to commercial/offshore (ONGC ₹7k Cr orders) to derisk from Navy (80-90% exposure).
  • Govt. Support & Incentives: Maritime Amritkaal Vision; Navratna status; MoUs (Swan, Guidance TN, Naval Group for exports). Dividend policy signals shareholder focus.
  • Execution Strength: Capacity for 11 subs + 10 warships; ahead-of-schedule deliveries boost credibility.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Profit Volatility from Provisions: H1 PAT down due to ₹1,000 Cr prior provisions (Coast Guard/MPV); Q1 provisions dragged half-year comparison.
  • Cash Flow Pressure: Negative H1 operating cash flow (₹-329 Cr) from Navy flexi account drawdown and working capital swings (inventories down but liabilities reduced sharply).
  • Near-Term Revenue Dip: Major projects (P15B destroyers complete); reliance on lower-margin Coast Guard/ONGC until new orders. Subcontract costs elevated (offshore turnkey).
  • Execution Bottlenecks: Monsoon delays (ONGC); warranty periods post-deliveries reduce revenue temporarily.

Growth Prospects

  • High Double-Digit Revenue CAGR: FY26 ~₹12,500 Cr; FY27 +5% base + submarine ramp-up. Long-term: Commercial shipbuilding (VLCCs, MR tankers via Tuticorin); exports (Asian subs via Naval Group MoU).
  • EBITDA/Margin Expansion: 15-25% on subs/frigates; derisk via mix (defense 70-80%, offshore/commercial 20-30% by FY32).
  • Order Inflow Acceleration: P75I/LPD/P17B RFPs imminent; DAC approvals (₹79k Cr) favor incumbents like MAZDOCK.
  • Capex-Led Capacity: ₹500 Cr FY26 (floating dock); ₹2k Cr Nhava/South Yard; ₹5k Cr Tuticorin phase-1 (1-1.5M DWT capacity). Colombo ramp-up adds 50% revenue in 2 years.
  • Valuation Upside: Strong ROE (~30%), dividend yield (~1-2%), order book multiple re-rating on inflows.

Key Risks

Risk Category Description Mitigation
Order Delay MoD/Navy sanctions pending (P75 subs at sanction stage; LPD RFP 4-5 months away). Strong track record; negotiations advanced (P75I rounds complete).
Cost Overruns Fixed-price contracts (ONGC PRP); provisions history. Familiar projects; indigenization efficiencies.
Customer Concentration 80-90% Navy-dependent. Diversification (ONGC, exports, commercial).
Execution/Capex Greenfield delays (clearances); submarine S-curve revenue (9-yr cycle, shorten to 7-yr). Phased capex; partnerships (Swan, Korean/Japanese).
External Geopolitical (sub exports); competition (L&T, Cochin for LPD); monsoon/global supply chains. Govt. priority; MSRA registration for repairs.

Investment Thesis: Strong buy on defense mega-cycle + diversification. Near-term steady (12.5k Cr rev), multi-year explosion post-orders. Monitor Q3 order inflows & cash flow normalization. Target order book >₹50k Cr by FY26-end for re-rating.
