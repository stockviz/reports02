Wendt (India) Limited (WENDT) - Q2/H1 FY26 Analysis

Overview: Wendt (India) Ltd, part of the Murugappa Group, manufactures superabrasives, grinding machines, and precision components. Q2 FY26 (ended Sep’25) showed flat-to-modest revenue growth but sharp PAT declines (standalone -57% YoY, consolidated -75% YoY) due to segment mix, amortization, and subsidiary losses. H1 revenue grew mildly (standalone +2%, consolidated +4% YoY), but PAT fell ~47-65% YoY. Balance sheet remains robust with net cash position and low debt. Figures in Rs lakhs unless noted.

Headwinds (Challenges Pressuring Performance)

  • Profit Erosion: Standalone Q2 PAT at 455 (vs 1,050 YoY); H1 at 950 (vs 1,795). Consolidated worse: Q2 PAT 270 (vs ~1,069); H1 648 (vs 1,837). Driven by lower high-margin machine sales mix, higher amortization (Wendt brand), and elevated expenses (employee costs +11% YoY Q2 standalone, depreciation +57% YoY).
  • Export Weakness: Standalone exports down 5% YoY to 1,064 (Q2), hit by geopolitics (Indonesia, UK, Eastern Europe slowdowns).
  • Loss-Making Segments/Subsidiaries: Machines & Accessories loss widened to -131 (Q2 standalone, vs +31 YoY). Subsidiaries (Wendt Thailand/Germany) posted Q2 consol loss of 185; H1 277, dragging group margins.
  • Margin Pressure: Standalone EBITDA margin ~12% (Q2, inferred from PBT); down from higher prior periods due to cost inflation (materials, other expenses up mildly).

Tailwinds (Supportive Factors)

  • Revenue Resilience: Standalone Q2 sales flat at 4,986 (domestic stable at 3,922); H1 +2% to 9,635. Consolidated Q2 +2% to 5,617; H1 +4% to 10,780. Super Abrasives (75% revenue) steady at 3,744 (Q2 standalone).
  • Rock-Solid Balance Sheet: Standalone equity 22,507 (up from 21,975 FY25); reserves 22,307. Total assets stable ~26k; net debt zero (borrowings repaid). Consolidated equity 24,849. High liquidity (investments ~4.5k-5k).
  • Cash Generation: Standalone operating cash flow strong at 1,457 (H1); despite dividend payout (400) and capex (731). Consolidated similar.
  • Group Backing: Murugappa Group’s resources aid stability; active investments (e.g., 546 in subsidiary).

Growth Prospects

  • Core Segment Strength: Super Abrasives (core ~75% revenue) robust (Q2 segment result 648; H1 1,303), with assets up 6% QoQ. Precision Products steady. Potential for market share gains in abrasives/grinding tools.
  • Capex Momentum: PPE up 15% YoY to 7,178 (standalone); intangibles 3,329; CWIP ongoing. Signals expansion in high-growth areas (e.g., machines recovery).
  • Export Recovery Potential: Geopolitical headwinds temporary; historical export reliance (~20%) could rebound with global manufacturing upcycle.
  • Subsidiary Upside: Investments in overseas units (Thailand/Germany) for grinding tech; could yield synergies despite current losses.
  • EPS Trajectory: H1 EPS 47.50 (standalone); FY25 was 191.46. Revenue growth + capex could drive FY26 PAT rebound to 3,500-4,000 if margins stabilize at 18-20%.
  • Valuation Upside: Low equity base (20mn shares); strong ROE (~17% FY25). Murugappa peer multiples suggest rerating potential.

Investment Summary: Short-term headwinds from profits/mix weigh on sentiment, but tailwinds from balance sheet strength and core segment resilience support stability. Growth prospects hinge on export/machines recovery and subsidiary turnaround; FY26 could see 5-10% revenue growth with margin repair to 18%+. Hold/Buy on dips for long-term investors; monitor Q3 exports and Machines P&L. Risks skewed to macro but mitigated by finances. (Data from unaudited results; limited review by PW clean.)
