Analysis of Arihant Capital Markets Ltd (ARIHANTCAP)

Arihant Capital Markets Ltd (ACML), a diversified financial services player (broking, financing, wealth management via subsidiaries), operates in a competitive Indian market. Key insights from Q2/H1 FY26 results (ended Sep 30, 2025), warrant conversion (Nov 22, 2025), board outcomes, and 33rd AGM (Sep 27, 2025) highlight resilience amid moderation. Below is a structured summary of headwinds, tailwinds, growth prospects, and key risks.

Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts)

  • Strong Capital Infusion & Promoter Confidence: Recent conversion of 50L warrants into equity shares (Nov 22, 2025) raised ~₹25.7 Cr (at ₹68.50/warrant, 75% tranche received). Promoter group (Ashok Kumar Jain HUF & Arpit Jain HUF) allotted 100%, increasing their stake subtly (post-allotment ~2.28% each). Prior preferential issues (5L shares in Q2 FY26) boosted equity capital to ₹104.6 Cr (10.46 Cr shares, up from ₹104.1 Cr). Overwhelming AGM approvals (99.99%+ in favor for key resolutions) signal alignment.
  • Robust Profitability: H1 FY26 consolidated net profit ₹25.8 Cr (EPS ₹2.48 basic; down YoY but stable QoQ). Broking segment (90%+ revenue) resilient at ₹106 Cr revenue. Margins intact (~24% PBT margin).
  • Asset Growth: Consolidated assets up 23% YoY to ₹965 Cr (loans +88% to ₹302 Cr; securities for trade +44%). Bank balances swelled (₹405 Cr), supporting liquidity.
  • Diversification: Subsidiaries (e.g., Arihant Futures, Financial Services) contribute; financing segment growing (revenue +12% YoY).

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Revenue Moderation: H1 revenue down 28% YoY to ₹108 Cr (Q2 ₹57 Cr vs. ₹76 Cr YoY). Broking fees/commission -30% YoY; interest income flat amid competition. Net FVC gains halved.
  • Cash Flow Pressure: Operating cash flow deeply negative (-₹104 Cr) due to aggressive lending (loans outflow ₹141 Cr) & working capital buildup (securities/trade receivables). Relies on financing inflows (borrowings +₹115 Cr).
  • Cost Pressures: Finance costs doubled YoY to ₹10 Cr (borrowings up 160% to ₹187 Cr). Employee/admin expenses up 15-20%.
  • Cyclical Exposure: 90% revenue from broking (volatile with market sentiment); H1 dip reflects softer equity volumes post-2024 rally.

Growth Prospects

  • High (Medium-Term: 15-25% Revenue CAGR Potential):
    • Capital Raising Momentum: Fresh equity/warrants fund lending/broking expansion. Post-conversion equity base supports leverage.
    • Restructuring Play: Board-approved Composite Scheme of Arrangement (Aug 26, 2025) for subsidiaries (AFSL demerger, AEFSL/AIBSL/AMMWML mergers) to streamline ops, unlock value (pending NCLT/SEBI nods).
    • Lending Ramp-Up: Loans portfolio exploding (+88% YoY); financing revenue +12% signals NBFC-like growth.
    • Subsidiary Synergies: Consolidated fold-in of 100% subs (e.g., IFSC unit); associate (Electrum, 27%) adds depth. Sold non-core insurance broking (gain ₹3.3 Cr exceptional).
    • Market Tailwinds: Bullish Indian equities/bonds could boost broking (BSE/NSE member); digital push via website/app.
  • Near-Term Catalysts: Q3 results (Dec 2025); scheme approvals; dividend payout (approved at AGM).

Overall Outlook: Positive with Cautious Optimism. Tailwinds from capital/debt efficiency and restructuring outweigh headwinds; profitability shields downside. Target 20%+ PAT growth FY26 if markets hold. Monitor Q3 cash flows and scheme progress. Valuation likely attractive (low EPS multiple implied).
