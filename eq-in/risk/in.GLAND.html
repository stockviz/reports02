Gland Pharma Limited (GLAND) Analysis: Q2FY26 Results Overview

Executive Summary:
Gland Pharma reported solid Q2FY26 results with consolidated revenue up 6% YoY to ₹14,869 Mn and PAT up 12% YoY to ₹1,837 Mn, driven by US growth (10% YoY) and margin expansion. H1FY26 showed even stronger trends: revenue +7% YoY, PAT +30% YoY. Base business (Gland) grew steadily with superior margins (EBITDA 35%), while Cenexi (French CDMO sub) posted 21% YoY revenue growth despite EBITDA losses from planned shutdowns. Tailwinds include robust US pipeline (7 new launches, 378 ANDAs cumulative), R&D ramp-up, and capacity expansions (e.g., GLP-1). Headwinds are Cenexi losses and softness in India/RoW. Growth prospects are strong in H2FY26 via launches, Cenexi recovery, and complex injectables/CDMO. Key risks: regulatory delays, forex volatility, and Cenexi execution. Overall, positive momentum with healthy balance sheet (net cash ~₹24 Bn, ROCE ~8-11%).

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • US Market Strength: 53% of H1FY26 revenue (₹15,722 Mn, +4% YoY); Q2 up 10% YoY. 7 new launches (e.g., Daptomycin-RTU, Sumatriptan), 6 ANDAs filed/5 approved (cumulative: 378 filed, 329 approved). Base business uptake in Enoxaparin.
  • Margin Expansion: Consolidated gross margin 63% (+400 bps YoY), EBITDA margin 21-23%, Adj. EBITDA margin 23-24% (+200-370 bps YoY). Base business EBITDA margin stable at 35%, PAT margin 28%.
  • R&D & Pipeline Momentum: Q2 R&D ₹614 Mn (5.8% of revenue, +34% QoQ). In-house complex injectables (6 launched, 3 approvals pending); 15 co-dev products (FY28 launch); RTU bags (20 filed, 14 approved, $659 Mn US opportunity); GLP-1 capacity scaling to 140 Mn units.
  • Cenexi Recovery Signs: Revenue +21% YoY to ₹4,102 Mn; gross margin 67%. Upgrades at Fontenay (GMP renewed to CY26, new ampoule line); ramp-up in vaccines/ophthalmics at Hérouville; lyophilizer quals at other sites.
  • Financial Health: Net cash ₹24.5 Bn (up QoQ), CFO ₹5.9 Bn (H1), low debt (borrowings <2% of assets), CCC improved to 163 days. Net worth ₹95.6 Bn (+4% YoY).

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Cenexi Profitability Drag: EBITDA loss €6 Mn (-15% margin) due to planned shutdowns; H1 loss €5 Mn. Contributes to consolidated EBITDA QoQ dip (-15%).
  • Regional Softness: India -24% YoY (₹665 Mn); Other Core Markets (CAN/AUS/NZ) -5% YoY/-34% QoQ; RoW -3% YoY (tech-transfer/CMO down 53% H1). Europe base business phasing issues (-14% QoQ).
  • QoQ Volatility: Consolidated revenue -1% QoQ; some markets like Europe/Other Core down sharply.
  • Higher Costs: Employee expenses up (₹3.85 Bn), depreciation +13% YoY (capex ₹1.97 Bn H1), ESOP expenses (₹140 Mn Q2).
  • Forex Headwinds: Other comprehensive loss ₹2.9 Bn H1 from translation of foreign ops (e.g., Cenexi € revenue).

Growth Prospects

  • Short-Term (H2FY26): Management expects “stronger momentum” from new US launches, Cenexi recovery (post-upgrades), and base business uptake. US to remain core (pipeline filings ongoing); Europe +22% H1 YoY.
  • Medium-Term (FY27-28): 49 pending US ANDAs; 15 co-dev products launch FY28; RTU/GLP-1 expansions target high-value segments ($659 Mn US RTU). Cenexi CDMO ramp-up (vaccines, lyophilizers by CY25 end).
  • Long-Term: Complex injectables as “central pillar”; global CDMO expansion; B2B model in 60 countries. H1 PAT growth (30%) signals scalable profitability; capex focus on high-end modalities.
  • Quantitative Outlook: Consensus implies 10-15% revenue CAGR FY26-28, supported by 35% base EBITDA margins and pipeline.
