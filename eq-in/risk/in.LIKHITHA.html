Analysis of Likhitha Infrastructure Limited (LIKHITHA)

Likhitha Infrastructure Limited (CIN: L45205TG1998PLC029911) is a specialized player in oil & gas pipeline laying and allied infrastructure (single reportable segment). It operates primarily in India with subsidiaries in India (CPM-Likhitha Consortium) and Saudi Arabia (Likhitha HAK Arabia Contracting). FY25 was strong (revenue ₹52,009 Cr consolidated, PAT ₹6,943 Cr standalone), but H1 FY26 showed moderation (consolidated revenue down 13% YoY to ₹22,464 Cr; standalone PBT down 26% YoY to ₹3,433 Cr). Balance sheet remains robust (net cash ~₹7,300 Cr standalone; equity ~₹39,906 Cr; negligible debt). Recent JV approval (65% stake) in UAE signals international push. AGM resolutions passed unanimously.

Tailwinds (Positive Factors)

  • Robust Financial Position: Debt-free (borrowings <₹20 Cr); cash & equivalents ₹7,310 Cr (standalone); current ratio >8x. Supports capex/growth without leverage.
  • International Expansion Momentum: 65% JV in Abu Dhabi (with Sait Specialized Engineering LLC) for UAE/oil-gas exporting markets; existing Saudi sub. ODI via cash, no equity dilution.
  • High Margins Retained: EBITDA margins ~25-30% despite YoY dip; EPS H1 FY26 ₹6.45 (standalone).
  • Energy Sector Tailwinds: Gas pipeline demand from India’s energy transition; Middle East oil/gas infra opportunities.
  • Governance: Clean auditor reviews (limited review unqualified); promoter voting alignment.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Revenue & Profit Pressure: H1 FY26 revenue -10% YoY (standalone); Q2 -19% YoY/-18% QoQ. PBT/PAT down 26% YoY due to higher contract costs (up 20% YoY) and inventory buildup.
  • Working Capital Drag: Negative operating cash flow (-₹1,058 Cr standalone) from inventory surge (+10% to ₹17,300 Cr) and other assets (+115% YoY). Receivables stable but collections key.
  • Domestic Slowdown: Project execution delays/contract phasing evident in WIP changes (-₹1,566 Cr).
  • Subsidiary Drag: Saudi sub reported losses (₹1.76 Cr H1); unaudited/unreviewed.
