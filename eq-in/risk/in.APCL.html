Anjani Portland Cement Limited (APCL) - Analysis Summary

Overview: APCL is a mid-sized cement manufacturer (1.16 MnTPA capacity in Telangana) listed on BSE/NSE (Scrip: 518091/APCL). FY25 saw revenue decline (standalone: -22% to ₹373 Cr; consolidated: to ₹430 Cr) and widening losses (standalone PAT: -₹35 Cr; consolidated: -₹81 Cr) amid weak demand. Key recent development: Board approved sale of 48% stake (~₹254 Cr) in loss-making subsidiary Bhavya Cements Pvt Ltd (BCPL) to parent Chettinad Cement Corp Pvt Ltd (CCCPL) via postal ballot (pending approval).

Headwinds (Key Pressures)

  • Weak Demand & Pricing: Cement sales volume down 10% YoY; production utilization at 61% (vs. 73% FY24). Factors: surplus supply, elections, erratic weather, sand shortages, competition. Realizations fell, leading to EBITDA loss (-₹3 Cr standalone).
  • Financial Strain: Debt-equity ratio rose to 1.03 (standalone)/2.17 (consolidated); interest costs up 2% YoY. Negative ROE (-9%), NPM (-9%), high gearing limits capex.
  • Subsidiary Drag: BCPL (99% owned, 1.2 MnTPA) incurred ₹37 Cr loss FY25; drags consolidated metrics. Continuous losses since FY23.
  • Operational Inefficiencies: Power/fuel costs at 31% of revenue (up from prior); freight up 2% YoY. No dividend for 5+ years.

Tailwinds (Supportive Factors)

  • Deleveraging Initiative: Sale of 48% BCPL stake to CCCPL at ₹40/share (premium to fair value) for ₹254 Cr. Aims to settle ₹360 Cr ICD debt (7% interest), cut interest burden (~₹25 Cr/year), boost liquidity/profitability. Post-sale, APCL retains 51% control.
  • Parent Support: CCCPL (75% owner) provides ICDs, raw materials (₹46 Cr purchase FY25), logistics. RPT approvals sought up to ₹1,000 Cr for FY26.
  • Efficiency Gains: Power cost stable at ₹7.4/unit; alternative fuels at 13%; green initiatives (ISO 14001/45001). Voluntary CSR spend (₹15-22 lakhs).
  • Market Position: Proximity to markets aids logistics; traded cement buffers production dips.

Growth Prospects (Medium-Term Outlook)

  • Industry Tailwinds: India cement demand +7-8% FY26 (infra/housing push, govt capex). Capacity to hit 600 MnTPA; APCL could benefit from regional recovery.
  • Strategic Moves: BCPL stake sale + potential amalgamation (approved Jan’24, pending) to streamline ops/consolidate 2.36 MnTPA. Deleveraging could free cash for capex/marketing.
  • Operational Upside: Utilization to 75% feasible with demand revival; alt fuels/raw materials (13-14% substitution) cut costs. Parent integration may optimize supply chain.
  • Base Case: FY26 revenue +10-15% if demand rebounds; EBITDA breakeven possible post-debt reduction. Long-term: Infra boom supports 5-10% CAGR.
