Vaswani Industries Limited (VASWANI) Analysis

Company Overview: Vaswani Industries is an iron & steel manufacturer (primary segment: MS billets, traded goods) with power generation as a key segment. It operates in Chhattisgarh, focusing on capacity expansions amid steady steel demand. FY25 revenue: ₹41,164 lakhs; PAT: ₹860 lakhs. H1 FY26 shows YoY growth but QoQ softness in Q2.

Tailwinds (Positive Factors)

  • Capacity Expansions & Operational Ramp-Up:
    • Billet capacity expanded from 66k TPA to 150k TPA (completed June 2025); CGECB approved additional 105k TPA (incl. existing 36k TPA) via Consent to Operate (Nov 2025 receipt).
    • Solar power project in Gandai fully operational; two new projects in Mohbhatta (Bemetara) to start by Nov 2025.
    • Iron & Steel segment revenue up YoY (H1: ₹18,507 lakhs vs. ₹18,441 lakhs); Power segment strong (H1: ₹1,477 lakhs revenue, ₹769 lakhs profit).
  • Financial Momentum: H1 FY26 revenue +3% YoY (₹19,929 lakhs), PAT +40% YoY (₹681 lakhs). EPS H1: ₹2.14 (vs. ₹1.62). Total comprehensive income ₹657 lakhs.
  • Shareholder Approvals: AGM (Sep 30, 2025) passed all resolutions (99.99%+ favor); approved ₹100 Cr material RPTs with Kwality Foundry (arms-length, FY26), cost/secretarial auditors.
  • Steel Sector Demand: Steady growth in FY25 (revenue +6% YoY); operational stability supports mid-cap steel plays.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • QoQ Revenue Decline: Q2 FY26 revenue ₹8,501 lakhs (-26% from Q1’s ₹11,428 lakhs); driven by lower Iron & Steel (₹7,836 lakhs) amid inventory buildup (changes: -₹1,304 lakhs H1).
  • Rising Costs: Finance costs doubled H1 YoY (₹658 lakhs) due to debt ramp-up; employee expenses +31% YoY. Gross margins pressured by material costs.
  • Cash Flow Strain: Operating cash +ve (₹1,080 lakhs), but investing cash -₹14,639 lakhs (Capex/CWIP surge to ₹21,520 lakhs from ₹6,713 lakhs).
  • High Leverage: Borrowings up 50%+ YoY (total ~₹26,584 lakhs vs. FY25 ₹15,625 lakhs); equity base grown (₹3,295 lakhs via fresh issue).

Growth Prospects

  • High (Capex-Driven):
    • Steel capacity at 150k TPA fully online; new billet unit to boost volumes amid India’s infra push (steel demand ~8-10% CAGR).
    • Power segment pivot: From captive to merchant sales potential; solar/renewables align with green energy tailwinds.
    • Revenue trajectory: FY26 guidance implicit via expansions (target >₹50,000 lakhs possible); traded goods up (H1: ₹3,185 lakhs).
    • Balance sheet scale: Assets doubled H1 (₹50,303 lakhs); CWIP indicates ₹15,000+ lakhs further investments.
    • Long-term: Real estate/agri segments dormant but unallocated assets (₹4,588 lakhs) offer diversification.
  • Catalysts: Q3/Q4 FY26 ramp-up post-Nov projects; potential EBITDA margins >15% on scale (H1 segment profits: Iron ₹588 lakhs, Power ₹769 lakhs).

Key Risks

Risk Category Description Mitigation/Impact
Execution Delays in Mohbhatta projects or billet ramp-up (CWIP heavy); environmental approvals critical (recent CGECB nod positive). High; could delay revenue (past expansions on-time).
Financial/Leverage Debt servicing risk (finance costs 94% of H1 PBT); interest coverage ~1.7x H1. Cash equivalents down ₹2,458 lakhs H1. Medium-High; refinance via equity/debt markets needed.
Commodity/Volatility Steel prices/inventories volatile (Q2 dip); raw material costs 66% of expenses. Medium; hedged via power inter-segment sales.
Related Party ₹100 Cr RPTs with Kwality Foundry (promoter-linked); scrutiny under Reg 23. Low (AGM approved; arms-length).
Regulatory/Market Steel oversupply, power tariffs; MSME disclosures pending. Low public participation (4% votes from non-promoters). Medium; steel cycle downturn risk.
Liquidity Working capital tied (inventories +₹239 lakhs, receivables +₹616 lakhs H1); trade payables up for support. Medium; operating cash covers short-term.

Overall Summary: Bullish on growth (Buy/Hold for 12-24m) with strong tailwinds from expansions offsetting headwinds like debt and Q2 softness. Target FY26 revenue ₹55,000-60,000 lakhs, PAT ₹1,500+ lakhs if executions succeed. Monitor Q3 results (expected Feb 2026) for debt metrics. Risks tilted toward leverage/execution, but steel/power mix provides resilience. Market cap implied ~₹400-500 Cr (at ₹120/share est.); undervalued vs. peers on P/E ~10x FY26E.
