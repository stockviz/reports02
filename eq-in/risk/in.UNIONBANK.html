Union Bank of India (UNIONBANK) Analysis: Headwinds, Tailwinds, Growth Prospects, and Key Risks

Union Bank of India (UNIONBANK), a leading public sector bank (5th largest PSB by business mix with ~8.7% share among PSBs), reported Q2FY26 results showing moderate business growth amid asset quality improvements but pressured profitability. Key metrics (as of Sep 30, 2025): Total business ₹22.10 Tn (+3.24% YoY), Advances ₹9.75 Tn (+4.99% YoY), Deposits ₹12.35 Tn (+1.90% YoY), Net Profit (Q2) ₹4,249 Cr (down YoY), GNPA 3.29% (-107 bps YoY), RoA 1.16%, NIM 2.67%, CRAR 17.07%.

Tailwinds (Positive Factors)

  • Robust Asset Quality: GNPA improved to 3.29% (-107 bps YoY), NNPA 0.55% (-43 bps YoY), PCR 95.13% (+234 bps YoY). Credit cost low at 0.22% (Q2), reflecting effective NPA management and recoveries (₹3,621 Cr in Q2).
  • Strong RAM Segment Growth: Retail +23.98% YoY (25% of advances), MSME +14.88% YoY; RAM portfolio at 58.83% of domestic advances (+8.14% YoY). Agri stable despite -12% YoY dip.
  • Capital Strength: CRAR 17.07% (Tier-1 15.58%), CET-1 14.37% (+49 bps YoY). Board-approved FY26 capital plan (₹6,000 Cr) supports growth.
  • Digital & Inclusion Momentum: Digital lending ₹24,600 Cr (15+ journeys), 8.95L accounts renewed. Strong govt scheme participation (PMJDY: 3.33 Cr accounts, ₹13,864 Cr balance). Green lending: ₹32,520 Cr (renewables), ₹1,318 Cr (EVs).
  • Awards & Recognition: 1st runner-up in EASE 8.0 (PSBs) for reforms; cyber security accolades (1st in CII SECEX 2025).
  • Network Scale: 8,655 branches, 9,064 ATMs, 25,700+ BCs; focused MSME/retail infrastructure (138 MLPs, 1,675 gold loan points).

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Slow Deposit Mobilization: +1.90% YoY growth (CASA 32.56%, -16 bps YoY), leading to CD ratio rise to 79.67% (+287 bps YoY). Current deposits -7.21% YoY.
  • Profitability Pressure: Net profit -9.97% YoY (Q2), Operating profit -16% YoY. NIM compressed to 2.67% (-23 bps YoY); Cost-to-Income 50.65% (+146 bps QoQ).
  • Regulatory Fines: RBI penalty ₹8.43L (soiled notes/ATM cash penalty, “not significant”); minor FIU-IND fine ₹37L.
  • Segmental Weakness: Agri advances -12.25% YoY; Overseas advances -4.34% YoY.
  • No Conference Participation: Missed JM Financial conference due to exigencies (non-material).

Growth Prospects

  • Retail/MSME Focus: RAM at ~59% of advances; digital journeys scaling (e.g., Vyom 2.0 app, CBDC with 8L+ users). Retail bureau scores strong (>750: 74%).
  • Digital Transformation: 24% YoY growth in digital FDs; UPI mandates, programmable CBDC pilots. Corporate 360 solutions added MNCs (Hyundai, BASF).
  • Sustainable Lending: Green portfolio expansion (renewables ₹16,623 Cr power infra); Union Nari Shakti (₹724 Cr Q2 sanctions).
  • PSB Consolidation Benefits: Amalgamation into Andhra Pradesh Grameena Bank (effective May 2025) enhances rural reach.
  • Macro Tailwinds: Projected 7-8% GDP growth, capex cycle favoring infra/NBFC exposure (13.64% advances). FY26 capital raise to fuel 10-12% advances growth.
  • Projections: Management targets sustained RAM growth, NIM stabilization ~2.8%, RoA >1.2%; EASE reforms position for PSB leadership.

Key Risks

  • Liquidity & Funding: High CD ratio risks costlier deposits; slow CASA growth amid rate environment.
  • Asset Quality Slippages: Agri/MSME vulnerability (slippages ₹2,151 Cr Q2); frauds ₹899 Cr (fully provisioned). Restructured MSMEs ₹505 Cr (RF 1.0).
  • Profitability Squeeze: NIM/credit cost sensitivity to rate cuts; opex +10% YoY (half-year).
  • Regulatory/Compliance: Recurring fines (soiled notes, FIU); IBC provisions ₹8,149 Cr (100% coverage).
  • Concentration: LC/others 43% advances; infra/NBFC 24% (top sectors).
  • External: Slow deposit growth if competition intensifies; geopolitical/macro slowdown impacting overseas ops (decline YoY).
  • Execution: Digital migration risks; associate (AP Grameena Bank) pension amortization (₹67 Cr impact).

Overall Summary: UNIONBANK demonstrates resilience with superior asset quality and RAM-led growth, bolstered by digital/capital strengths (tailwinds dominate). However, deposit sluggishness and NIM pressure pose near-term headwinds. Growth prospects strong in retail/digital/green segments (10%+ advances FY26e), but risks from liquidity, slippages, and macros warrant monitoring. Trading at ~0.6x FY26e BVPS (₹148), stock offers value with improving RoEs; target RoA stabilization key for re-rating. Positive outlook with EASE leadership.
