Summary Analysis for Union Bank of India (UNIONBANK)

Union Bank of India (5th largest PSB by business mix) reported steady Q2FY26 results (quarter/half-year ended Sep 30, 2025), with total business at ₹22.10 lakh Cr (+3.24% YoY), advances ₹9.75 lakh Cr (+4.99% YoY), and deposits ₹12.35 lakh Cr (+1.90% YoY). Net profit stood at ₹4,249 Cr in Q2 (+3.25% QoQ but -9.97% YoY), with RoA at 1.16% and RoE at 15.08%. Asset quality improved markedly (GNPA 3.29%, NNPA 0.55%, PCR 95.13%), supported by strong capital (CRAR 17.07%, CET-1 14.37%). However, NIM compression (2.67%) and modest deposit growth pose near-term challenges. Below is a structured analysis of headwinds, tailwinds, growth prospects, and key risks.

Tailwinds (Positive Factors)

  • Robust Asset Quality & Provisioning: GNPA declined 107 bps YoY to 3.29%; NNPA down 43 bps to 0.55%; PCR up 234 bps to 95.13%. Credit cost sharply reduced to 0.22% (from 1.09% YoY), reflecting effective NPA management and recoveries (₹3,621 Cr in Q2, including ₹913 Cr from write-offs).
  • Strong Capital Position: CRAR at 17.07% (above regulatory norms); CET-1 at 14.37% (+49 bps YoY). Board-approved FY26 capital plan of ₹6,000 Cr provides buffer for growth.
  • Retail/MSME Focus (RAM Segment): RAM advances up 8.14% YoY to ₹5.54 lakh Cr (58.83% of domestic advances). Retail +23.98% YoY; MSME +14.88%. Digital lending generated ₹24,600 Cr via 15+ journeys.
  • Digital & Operational Momentum: 6.82 lakh new digital users (Vyom app); UPI/mobile banking volumes growing; awards like 1st runner-up in EASE 8.0 (across multiple pillars). Cyber security enhancements (NGSOC, CCoE).
  • Financial Inclusion & ESG: Strong traction in govt schemes (e.g., PMJDY: 3.33 Cr accounts, ₹13,864 Cr balance); green lending (₹32,520 Cr renewable energy, ₹1,318 Cr EVs).

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Deposit Mobilization Slump: Growth slowed to 1.90% YoY (domestic CASA flat at ~32.56%); CD ratio rose to 79.67% (+287 bps YoY), pressuring liquidity (LCR 127.33%, NSFR 117.16% but watch margins).
  • NIM & Profitability Pressure: NIM dipped to 2.67% (-9 bps QoQ, -26 bps H1 YoY); NII down 2.60% YoY in Q2. Operating profit -16.01% YoY; cost-to-income ratio up to 50.65% (+146 bps QoQ).
  • Modest Overall Growth: Advances growth at 4.99% YoY lags peers/system; agriculture segment declined 12.25% YoY. Total business growth muted at 3.24% YoY.
  • Operational Hiccups: RBI penalty of ₹8.43 lakh for soiled notes/ATM issues; skipped investor conference due to “exigencies”.
  • Rising Costs: Deposit cost up to 5.50% (+7 bps H1 YoY); operating expenses +10.37% YoY.

Growth Prospects

  • RAM & Retail Expansion: High potential in underserved retail/MSME (target 60%+ share); digital journeys scaling (e.g., 8.95 lakh accounts renewed; 62 lakh gold loans digitally). Green/ESG lending (renewables, EVs) aligns with policy push.
  • Digital Transformation: Vyom 2.0 rollout, CBDC (8 lakh+ users, 16k merchants), UPI enhancements; 24% YoY growth in digital FDs. Corporate 360 suite added 356 clients, ₹557 Cr NII contribution.
  • Network & Inclusion: 8,655 branches + BCs; focus on PMJDY/APY (new enrollments surging). FY26 capital infusion to fuel 7-10% advances CAGR (2Y: 7.2% global, 7.1% domestic).
  • Macro Tailwinds: PSB consolidation, govt schemes, rural/MSME revival; 5th largest PSB positions for market share gains. Overseas presence (Dubai, Sydney, UK subsidiary) for forex/FX growth.
  • Projections: Consensus implies 12-15% profit CAGR FY26-28 on NIM stabilization (2.8-3%), RoA ~1.2-1.3%, RoE 15-18%.

Key Risks

  • ****Liquidity & NIM Squeeze** (High)**: Slow deposits + rising CoD could widen CD ratio >80%, eroding NIM further (system avg. ~3%). Interest rate volatility key monitorable.
  • Asset Quality Deterioration (Medium)**: Agri decline (-12%); 102 frauds (₹899 Cr exposure, fully provisioned). Slippages at ₹2,151 Cr in Q2; watch MSME/corporate stress amid economic slowdown.
  • Regulatory/Operational (Low-Medium)**: RBI penalties signal compliance gaps; unhedged FX provision ₹57.51 Cr. RRB amalgamation (Andhra Pradesh Grameena Bank) adds integration risks.
  • ****Macro/Economic** (Medium)**: Slow GDP/deposit growth; election cycles, monsoon variability impacting agri/rural. Competition from private banks in retail/digital.
  • ****Execution** (Medium)**: Digital drop-offs, cyber threats (despite investments); 8330 un-reviewed branches in results add opacity.

Overall Outlook: Positive with Caution. Tailwinds from asset quality, capital, and RAM/digital focus outweigh headwinds. Growth prospects strong in retail/MSME/green segments, but deposit acceleration critical for NIM recovery. Stock trades at ~0.7x FY26E BV (undervalued vs. peers); target upside 20-30% if deposits revive. Monitor Q3 deposit trends & NIM trajectory.
