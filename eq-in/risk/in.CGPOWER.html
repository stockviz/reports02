CG Power and Industrial Solutions Limited (CGPOWER) Analysis

Overall Summary: CG Power delivered robust H1 FY26 performance with consolidated revenue up ~25% YoY to ₹5,801 Cr and PAT up ~20% to ₹551 Cr, driven by Power Systems (35% YoY growth) and emerging Semiconductors segment. Recent ₹3,000 Cr QIP, greenfield Switchgear capex (₹748 Cr), and semiconductor push (with ₹4,901 Cr govt incentives) signal strong growth momentum amid India’s power infra boom. Tailwinds dominate, but semi losses and execution risks warrant monitoring. Stock outlook positive for medium-term, supported by capacity expansion and policy tailwinds.

Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts)

  • Strong Financial Momentum: H1 revenue +25% YoY (standalone +21%); EBITDA margins stable ~25-30%. PAT growth aided by operational leverage, low finance costs (debt near-zero post-QIP).
  • QIP Infusion: ₹3,000 Cr raised (Sep 2025) bolsters balance sheet (cash + investments ~₹6,000 Cr standalone); funds growth/capex without dilution pain (EPS intact at ₹3.58 consolidated).
  • Segment Strength: Power Systems revenue doubled YoY; Industrial Systems steady. Unallocable gains from treasury ops.
  • Policy/Govt Support: Semiconductor OSAT eligible for ₹3,501 Cr central + ₹1,400 Cr state grants (recognized ₹475 Cr in Q2); aligns with India Semiconductor Mission.
  • Acquisitions Synergies: RF Components (Renesas) and G.G. Tronics integrated; semis now a reportable segment (₹236 Cr H1 revenue).

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Semiconductor Losses: New segment reported ₹30 Cr loss H1 FY26 (Q2: -₹22 Cr); high capex (CWIP ₹455 Cr consolidated) and ramp-up costs.
  • Working Capital Strain: Inventories +23% (₹1,424 Cr), receivables +10%; ops cash flow dipped despite profits (H1 standalone -₹60 Cr).
  • Subsidiary Setback: G.G. Tronics’ Loco Kavach order cancelled due to RDSO/ISA delays (12-month timeline missed).
  • Capex Intensity: ₹748 Cr Switchgear plant + semi investments (~₹7,584 Cr total project) to strain near-term FCF.
