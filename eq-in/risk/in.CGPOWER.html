CG Power and Industrial Solutions Limited (CGPOWER) Analysis

Overview: CG Power reported robust Q2/H1 FY26 results (ended Sep 30, 2025) with standalone revenue at ₹5,293 Cr (+21% YoY) and PAT at ₹594 Cr (+31% YoY); consolidated revenue ₹5,801 Cr (+25% YoY), PAT ₹555 Cr (+20% YoY attributable to owners). Key drivers include Power Systems (strong growth) and Industrial Systems. Recent QIP raised ₹3,000 Cr; board approved ₹748 Cr greenfield switchgear plant and promoter reclassification. Subsidiary developments mixed (semis progress, railways order cancel).

Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts)

  • Financial Strength: Exceptional revenue/PAT growth (17-25% YoY Q2), high margins (EBITDA ~15-18% implied), net cash position post-QIP (standalone cash ₹46 Cr, cons. ₹190 Cr; total assets up 56% YoY). Equity dilution minimal (shares up ~3%).
  • Strategic Expansions: New 35-acre switchgear plant to double capacity (MV/EHV focus, completion in 33 months); funded internally/debt. Semiconductor tailwinds via OSAT (₹7,584 Cr project, ₹4,901 Cr govt aid approved) and RF acquisition from Renesas.
  • Segment Momentum: Power Systems revenue +48% YoY Q2 (₹1,254 Cr); Industrial +0.2% but stable. Emerging Semiconductors segment (₹237 Cr H1 revenue).
  • Policy Support: India Semiconductor Mission grants (₹475 Cr recognized Q2); power infra boom aids switchgear/motors.
  • Corporate Actions: Promoter group reclassification (to public, 0% holding) improves free float; office shift enhances efficiency.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Loss-Making Segments: Semiconductors deep loss (₹30 Cr H1; ₹22 Cr Q2), dragging group profitability (despite overall growth).
  • Order Setbacks: Subsidiary G.G. Tronics’ Loco Kavach order (from CLW) cancelled due to RDSO/ISA delays; impacts railways revenue visibility.
  • Working Capital Pressure: H1 cash from ops ₹723 Cr standalone but inventories up ₹164 Cr, receivables ₹133 Cr; cons. ops cash ₹481 Cr amid high growth.
  • Capex Intensity: ₹751 Cr H1 capex (PPE/intangibles); ongoing OSAT/RF investments strain near-term FCF.

Growth Prospects

  • High (3-5 Yr Horizon): Switchgear doubling targets domestic/export demand (power infra, EVs); semis OSAT/RF could scale to multi-GW capacity with govt backing (FY25-29 grants). Consolidated revenue trajectory: FY25 ₹9,909 Cr → FY26E 20-25%+ growth possible on order book/infra capex.
  • Railways/Exports: GGT’s Uniform Braking Algorithm positions for future tenders (bulk procurement eligible post-approvals); global ops (Sweden, US) aid Industrial Systems.
  • QIP Utilization: Funds parked in FDs/mutual funds; earmarked for capex/M&A (e.g., semis, RF synergies).
  • Projections: H2 FY26 could accelerate on plant ramp-up; ROE >20% sustainable with 15-20% revenue CAGR.
