Summary Analysis: Tata Motors Passenger Vehicles Limited (TMPV) - Q2 FY26 (Sep 30, 2025)

TMPV (post-demerger of CV business) operates two key segments: Tata Passenger Vehicles (India PV/EV) and Jaguar Land Rover (JLR). Consolidated Q2 revenue: ₹72.3K Cr (-13.5% YoY); EBITDA: -0.1% (-1120 bps); PBT (bei): -₹5.5K Cr. H1 FCF (auto): -₹18K Cr; net auto debt: ₹20.1K Cr (from net cash FY25-end). Demerger gain: ₹82.6K Cr (notional, no net worth impact). Domestic PV resilient; JLR hit by cyber incident.

Headwinds (Key Challenges)

  • JLR Cyber Incident: Production halt in Sep (full month lost); Q2 wholesales 66K (-24% YoY), revenue £4.9B (-24%), EBIT -8.6% (-1370 bps), FCF -£791M. Q3 impacted (~50K unit loss total); exceptional costs £238M (cyber £196M + redundancies). Revised FY26 guidance: EBIT 0-2%, FCF outflow £2.2-2.5B.
  • Macro/External Pressures: US tariffs (Trump): £74M Q2 hit (cum. H1 £328M); structural risk. China luxury tax + weak demand: VME up to 6.9%. Global luxury slowdown, geopolitics, supply chain shocks (e.g., Nexperia chips).
  • Operational Costs: High warranty, VME (elevated globally), commodities/FX adverse. ICE PV margins muted (6.4% EBITDA, -2% YoY).
  • Financial Strain: H1 net debt up; JLR liquidity £6.6B but stressed (new facilities added).

Tailwinds (Key Positives)

  • Domestic PV Rebound: Post-GST 2.0 cuts (up to 10% price drop), volumes +10% YoY (144K units); revenue +15% (₹13.5K Cr); mkt share 12.8% (13.7-14% festive). Record Sep/Oct offtakes (60K+/61K); inventories <30 days. EV: 24K units (+59% YoY, 42% share); CNG strong. PLI: ₹125 Cr Q2 accrual (+₹350 Cr FY25 cash received).
  • Segment Health: India PV net cash; EV EBITDA 4.2% (+920 bps YoY). JLR plants at capacity post-Oct 8 restart; strong brands (Range Rover Top 100).
  • Structural: Demerger clean (focus on PV); H1 FCF positive ₹1.6K Cr in domestic; liquidity backstops (e.g., £1.5B UKEF loan).

Growth Prospects

  • Domestic PV/EV (High Confidence): H2 double-digit industry growth (FY26 ~5% overall). Launches: Sierra (Nov), Harrier/Safari petrol, Sierra.ev; refreshes (Nexon.ev ADAS). EV penetration 17% → mainstreaming via charging (Tata.ev mega chargers); PLI expansion (Nexon/Harrier qualify Q3/Q4). Target: ICE double-digit EBITDA; EV profitability via scale/PLI.
  • JLR Recovery: Q4 normalization (strong seasonal); pipeline refill; tariff mitigation (UK 10%, EU 15%); new EVs (Range Rover Electric/Jaguar relaunch). Overseas focus, China resilience (lean stock).
  • Overall: H2 stronger (JLR ramp-up + domestic momentum); international (South Africa entry); capex sustained (~₹1.3K Cr Q2 domestic) for product offensive. Long-term: 10% EBIT aspirational (review FY27).
