Tata Motors Passenger Vehicles Limited (TMPV) Analysis

Overview: TMPV (post-demerger from Tata Motors CV business, effective Oct 1, 2025) focuses on Passenger Vehicles (PV + EV in India) and Jaguar Land Rover (JLR). Q2 FY26 results were weak (consolidated revenue -13.5% YoY to ₹72.3K Cr; EBITDA -0.1%; PBT bei -₹5.5K Cr) due to JLR’s cyber incident (Sep production halt). India PV showed resilience (+15.6% revenue). H1 FCF negative ₹18K Cr; net auto debt ₹20K Cr. Nov 2025 sales: 59K units (+25.6% YoY). Revised JLR FY26 guidance: EBIT 0-2%; FCF outflow £2.2-2.5bn. Demerger notional gain ₹83K Cr (no P&L impact).

Headwinds (Challenges Pressuring Performance)
  • JLR Cyber Incident: Full Sep shutdown; ~50K unit loss (20K in Q2, rest Q3). Exceptional costs ₹2.6K Cr; Q3/Q4 pipeline refill delayed. Recovery ongoing but H1 EBIT -1.4%.
  • Weak Global Demand: Luxury segment shrinking in China (luxury tax); Europe struggling; US stable but absorbing limited capacity. VME elevated (6.9% from 4.1%); industry overcapacity.
  • Tariffs/Taxes: US Trump tariffs (£328mn H1 hit; structural); China luxury tax. Raw material costs up (Euro FX adverse).
  • India PV: Commodity headwinds (~1% YoY hit); pricing pressure (ICE EBITDA 6.4%, -2% YoY); high discounts/VME post-festive.
  • Macro/Supply Chain: Geopolitics, regulatory shocks (CAFE norms, E20 fuel), Nexperia chip risks, warranty spend high despite quality gains.
  • Debt/Liquidity: Net debt up to ₹20K Cr; JLR FCF negative £1.5bn H1.
Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts)
  • India PV Resurgence: GST 2.0 cuts spurred demand (Sep/Oct record 60K+ offtakes); festive sales 1L units (+33% YoY); inventories <30 days. Market share 12.8-14%; EV/CNG 45% mix aids CAFE compliance.
  • EV Momentum: India EV offtakes 24K (+59% YoY); 42% share; PLI ₹125 Cr Q2 (full FY25 received). Harrier.ev/Nexon.ev strong; Sierra.ev upcoming.
  • JLR Recovery: Production restarted Oct 8 at capacity; systems cleansed. Missions: 25% less off-road vehicles, cost cuts (500 redundancies), tariff mitigation.
  • Brand/Product Strength: Range Rover top global brand; Punch 6L milestone; new launches (Sierra Nov 2025, Harrier/Safari petrol).
  • Liquidity: JLR £6.6bn (incl. new £3.5bn facilities); India PV net cash.
  • Policy: GST cuts, PLI incentives.
Growth Prospects
  • India PV: H2 double-digit industry growth (FY26 ~5%); TMPV outperformance via launches (Sierra, EV refreshes), EV penetration to 17%+, international (South Africa entry). Margins to double-digits via leverage, PLI, price hikes Q4.
  • EV Leadership: 42% share; rapid interventions; charging infra expansion. PLI expansion (Nexon/Harrier Q3-Q4).
  • JLR: Q4 normalization; new EV Jaguar/Range Rover Electric; China resilience; H2 EBIT recovery. FY27 update post-Q3.
  • Overall: Consolidated H2 strength; EV/CNG mix; product offensive. Long-term: 10% EBIT target (review FY27).
