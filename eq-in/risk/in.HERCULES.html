Analysis of Hercules Investments Limited (HERCULES)

Hercules Investments Limited (formerly Hercules Hoists Limited) is a non-banking investment holding company (unregistered NBFC Category C/C) focused on managing a portfolio of investments primarily in Bajaj Group companies. Post-demerger (effective Sep 2025, with restatements), it has no operating revenue, deriving income solely from dividends, interest, and investment gains. Key financials for H1 FY26 (ended Sep 30, 2025, unaudited): - Total Income: ₹361.73 lakhs (vs. ₹314.95 lakhs H1 FY25). - PBT/Net Profit: ₹528.65/₹519.00 lakhs (up from ₹292.97/₹285.46 lakhs). - Balance Sheet: Assets ₹95,359 lakhs (dominated by ₹93,590 lakhs non-current investments); Equity ₹86,220 lakhs. - Cash Flow: Negative operating cash (-₹175 lakhs post-tax), offset by investing inflows (₹30 lakhs from dividends). - EPS (H1): ₹1.62 (basic/diluted, FV ₹1). - Key Event: Board approved voluntary delisting from NSE (effective Nov 14, 2025) without shareholder exit offer, retaining BSE listing.

Tailwinds (Positive Factors)

  • Strong Investment Portfolio: ₹93,590 lakhs (~98% of assets) in Bajaj Group equities (FVOCI), benefiting from group’s robust performance (e.g., Bajaj Finance, Bajaj Finserv). Dividend income (₹530 lakhs H1 FY26) provides stable cash flow.
  • Profitability Momentum: H1 PBT up 80% YoY, driven by higher other income (₹550 lakhs Q2 FY26). Net profit margins near 100% due to low expenses (₹37 lakhs H1).
  • Capital Efficiency: High ROE potential (implied >12% annualized); low debt (no borrowings); cash hoard supports dividends/distributions.
  • Bajaj Group Ecosystem: Tailwinds from promoter-aligned investments, potential for special dividends or capital returns.
  • Delisting Benefits: Reduced compliance costs (SEBI LODR), lower listing fees; focuses on long-term holding without quarterly pressure.

Headwinds (Negative Factors)

  • No Operational Revenue: 100% reliant on investment income; Q2 FY26 revenue ₹550 lakhs but volatile (e.g., prior losses like Q1 FY26 ₹-0.77 lakhs).
  • Declining Cash Position: Cash fell to ₹39 lakhs from ₹79 lakhs; negative operating cash flow signals liquidity strain despite dividend inflows.
  • Delisting Impact: Loss of NSE liquidity (higher volume exchange); potential share price pressure, reduced discoverability for retail investors.
  • OCI Volatility: Massive OCI swings (e.g., ₹16,647 lakhs Q2 gain but historical losses); total comprehensive income can flip negative (e.g., Q2 FY26 ₹-8,829 lakhs).
  • Restatement Adjustments: Demerger-related tweaks (₹72 lakhs to reserves) indicate ongoing integration challenges.

Growth Prospects

  • Moderate (Dependent on Markets): Growth tied to Bajaj portfolio appreciation/dividends (5-10% CAGR potential if group grows 15-20% as historical). No new capex; prospects hinge on:
    • Dividend payouts from investees (yield ~5-6% on portfolio).
    • Potential capital monetization (e.g., stake sales) or promoter infusions.
    • Post-delisting flexibility for buybacks/special dividends (recent cash flows support ₹300-500 lakhs payouts).
  • Upside Scenarios: Bajaj Group expansion (EV, finance); NAV growth (current BVPS ~₹269 vs. market price TBD); 10-15% EPS CAGR if markets stable.
  • Base Case: Flat-to-5% revenue growth; focus on yield preservation over expansion.

Summary

Bull Case: Attractive Bajaj proxy (high dividend yield, low costs); delisting unlocks value via distributions (target 10-15% returns).
Bear Case: Delisting + volatility erodes liquidity/premium (5-10% downside).
Overall: Stable hold for Bajaj believers (Tailwinds > Headwinds); limited growth (portfolio yield-focused). Monitor delisting approval, Q3 dividend flows, and Bajaj group earnings. Trading at ~0.3-0.4x BV (est.); buy on dips if liquidity holds. Recommendation: Neutral-Hold for long-term investors.
