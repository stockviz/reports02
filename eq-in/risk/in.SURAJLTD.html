Summary Analysis for Suraj Limited (SURAJLTD)

Suraj Limited, a manufacturer and exporter of stainless/alloy/carbon steel seamless pipes, tubes, fittings, and flanges, reported unaudited H1 FY26 results (ended Sep 30, 2025). Standalone revenue declined 9.8% YoY to ₹10,053 lakhs (from ₹11,139 lakhs), with PAT down 76% to ₹210 lakhs (EPS ₹1.14). Consolidated figures (incl. associate Suraj Enterprise Pvt Ltd) show revenue flat YoY at ₹10,053 lakhs but PAT down 71% to ₹357 lakhs (EPS ₹1.95), aided by ₹148 lakhs associate profit share. Balance sheet strengthened with borrowings cut 31% to ₹4,389 lakhs; operating cash flow surged to ₹2,372 lakhs (from negative ₹894 lakhs YoY). FY25 full-year baseline: revenue ₹23,374 lakhs, PAT ₹1,171 lakhs standalone.

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Debt Reduction & Liquidity Boost: Current borrowings dropped sharply by ₹2,019 lakhs YoY, driving finance costs stable at ₹205 lakhs despite operations. Strong OCF (₹2,372 lakhs) reflects improved working capital (receivables down 23% to ₹2,542 lakhs, payables up). Cash conversion supports deleveraging.
  • Operational Efficiency: Positive inventory swing normalized (₹20 lakhs vs. -₹850 lakhs YoY); asset sales (₹220 lakhs proceeds) offset capex (₹374 lakhs). Other equity grew 2% to ₹11,427 lakhs standalone.
  • Associate Contribution: Added ₹148 lakhs PAT share in H1, stabilizing consolidated profitability (EPS up QoQ).
  • Cost Discipline: Employee/depreciation expenses controlled; no exceptional items.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Revenue Pressure: Down 9.8% YoY in H1 (Q2 flat QoQ but -11% YoY); likely weak demand or pricing in stainless steel exports/infra segments.
  • Margin Squeeze: Gross margins hit by high material costs (Q2 ₹3,933 lakhs vs. Q1 ₹2,409 lakhs); other expenses up 6% YoY (₹1,863 lakhs). PBT down 73% YoY to ₹337 lakhs standalone.
  • Seasonal/Inventory Volatility: Q2 PAT halved QoQ (₹55 lakhs vs. ₹155 lakhs); large inventory changes distorted prior periods.
  • Asset Base Shrinkage: Total assets down 8% to ₹20,204 lakhs (PPE depreciated net of minor capex).

Growth Prospects

  • Moderate Recovery Potential: Steel demand tailwinds from India’s infra push (pipelines, oil&gas), exports (co.’s focus). H1 OCF funds capex/debt paydown, enabling 10-15% FY26 revenue growth if Q3 normalizes (FY25 revenue grew ~20% implied).
  • Balance Sheet Flexibility: Net debt/equity improving (borrowings <33% of equity); frees cash for expansion. Single-segment focus (Ind AS 108 compliant) on high-margin seamless products positions for niche growth.
  • Associate Leverage: Could add 10-20% PAT uplift if sustained.
  • Outlook: FY26 PAT could rebound to ₹1,200-1,400 lakhs standalone if revenue stabilizes at FY25 levels and costs moderate (steel prices easing?).

Key Risks

Risk Category Description Mitigation/Impact
Commodity Volatility Steel raw material prices spiked (63% of Q2 expenses); inventory fluctuations swing P&L (±₹932 lakhs in Q2). Hedging absent; high impact on margins (FY25 materials 71% of expenses).
Demand/Cyclical Weak H1 revenue signals export/infra slowdown; 100% single segment exposure. Macro headwinds (global steel glut, China exports); no diversification.
Liquidity/Debt Still ₹4,389 lakhs borrowings; interest coverage ~1.6x (PBIT/interest). Strong OCF buffers, but rising rates could pinch.

Overall Verdict: Short-term headwinds from revenue weakness and costs dominate (Hold/Neutral), but tailwinds from deleveraging and cash flow signal improving fundamentals. Growth hinges on steel cycle recovery; monitor Q3 for inflection. Target P/E ~10-12x FY26 EPS for valuation. Data as of Nov 10, 2025 filing.
