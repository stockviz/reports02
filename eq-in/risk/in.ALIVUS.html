Analysis of Alivus Life Sciences Limited (ALIVUS, Scrip: 543322)

Alivus Life Sciences (formerly Glenmark Life Sciences) is a leading Indian API manufacturer focused on high-value, non-commoditized molecules in chronic therapies (e.g., CVS, CNS, diabetes). It serves regulated markets (US, Europe, Japan) and emerging markets, with growing CDMO exposure. Q2/H1 FY26 results (ended Sep 30, 2025) show resilient growth amid challenges. Below is a structured summary based on the provided disclosures (financial results, investor presentation, press release, regulatory filings, AGM outcomes).

Tailwinds (Positive Factors)

  • Robust Financial Performance & Margins: Q2 revenue +16% YoY (₹5,880 Mn), EBITDA +36% YoY (33% margin, +480 bps YoY), PAT +37% YoY (22.1% margin). H1 revenue +9% YoY, EBITDA margins at 31.5% (+340 bps). Gross margins expanded to 57.7% on better product mix and new launches. Debt-free balance sheet with ₹6,526 Mn cash (incl. short-term investments) and strong FCF (₹1,477 Mn in H1).
  • Non-GPL Business Momentum: 40% YoY growth (₹4,443 Mn, 76% of revenue), driven by demand in LATAM, Japan, Europe, ROW, and India (ex-GPL). Chronic portfolio at 69% of revenue.
  • Segmental Strength: Generic API +15% YoY; CDMO +16% YoY, with H2 ramp-up from new projects.
  • R&D & Pipeline: 586 DMFs/CEPs filed; HP API grid (26 products, $66 Bn TAM); iron complexes ($2.7 Bn TAM). Recent filings signal execution.
  • Manufacturing Quality: 4 facilities (1,424 KL capacity) with recent USFDA approvals (Ankleshwar Jan 2025, Dahej May 2025); global regulator inspections (PMDA, EDQM).
  • Investor Engagement: Scheduled one-on-one meetings (Dec 2025) indicate interest; AGM resolutions passed overwhelmingly.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • GPL Dependency & De-Growth: GPL (likely Glenmark Pharma Ltd.) at 24% revenue, -24% YoY (₹1,437 Mn) due to customer inventory rationalization; QoQ -6%.
  • QoQ Revenue Dip: -2% in Q2 due to GPL weakness, offset by non-GPL.
  • Regulatory/Operational Incident: Flash fire at Ankleshwar Unit 6 (Sep 30, 2025) led to GPCB penalty (₹50 Lks interim EDC, ₹29 Lks deposit + ₹10 Lks BG). No material financial/operational impact claimed; appeal planned.
  • Capex Intensity: Ongoing expansions (Solapur Phase 1: 200 KL; Dahej/Ankleshwar brownfield) amid rising inventories (₹7,225 Mn) and trade receivables (₹10,028 Mn).
  • Margin Pressure Outlook: Sustaining ~30% EBITDA without PLI benefits requires continued efficiencies.

Growth Prospects

  • H2 FY26 Outlook: Management confident of “stronger performance” via GPL recovery, CDMO ramp-up, non-GPL continuity. Guided EBITDA ~30%.
  • Capacity Expansion: Total reactor capacity to grow 89% to 2,690 KL by FY28 (Solapur greenfield Phases 1-2; brownfield adds). Calibrated per demand.
  • Strategic Levers: | Lever | Details | Potential Impact | |——-|———|—————–| | CDMO | New projects; pipeline discussions | High growth in H2+ | | Complex APIs | HP APIs (10 validated), oncology, iron complexes | $68 Bn+ TAM | | Generic API | New launches, regulated market focus (81% revenue), 2nd-sourcing | Steady 15%+ YoY | | Efficiencies | Flow chemistry, backward integration, debottlenecking | Margin sustenance |
  • Geographic/Therapeutic Mix: Regulated markets 81%; chronic 69%. Diversified (170 molecules, 100+ customers).
  • Long-Term: ROICE ~29%, FATR 2.4x; strong cash supports calibrated capex/greenfield R&D.

Overall Summary: Alivus demonstrates strong fundamentals with margin resilience and non-GPL tailwinds offsetting GPL headwinds. Growth prospects are solid (H2 acceleration, capacity/demand alignment), supported by ₹6.5 Bn cash war chest. Key monitorables: GPL recovery, capex execution, GPCB resolution. Valuation likely supported by 30%+ margins and API/CDMO leverage; low debt enhances resilience. Positive AGM turnout reflects shareholder confidence.
