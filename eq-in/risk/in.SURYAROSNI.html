Surya Roshni Limited (SURYAROSNI) Analysis

Surya Roshni Limited operates in two key segments: Steel Pipes & Strips (78% of Q2 FY26 revenue) and Lighting & Consumer Durables (FMEG) (22%). Q2 FY26 showed strong recovery: consolidated revenue +21% YoY to ₹1,845 Cr, EBITDA +69% to ₹141 Cr (7.6% margin), PAT +117% to ₹74 Cr. Steel drove growth with record Q2 volumes; Lighting grew steadily on festive demand. Company is zero-debt with ₹246 Cr net cash surplus (Sep 2025), ROCE 16.5%, ROE 11.9%, and healthy order book (₹875 Cr). Interim dividend of ₹2.5/share declared.

Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts)
  • Steel Segment Momentum: Exports +45% YoY (Europe/Canada pre-CBAM/quotas); value-added products (API/Spiral +86% YoY) at higher EBITDA/ton (₹9,300 vs. GI ₹6,500). Order book ₹750 Cr (Oil & Gas, Water, Exports). Capacity utilization ~80%.
  • Lighting/FMEG Resilience: Double-digit volume growth in LEDs (lamps +37%, battens +36%, streetlights +104%); Professional Lighting +25% YoY (order book ₹125-150 Cr). Festive/winter demand tailwinds; new launches (wires targeting ₹150 Cr FY26, digital water heaters, fans).
  • Financial Strength: Zero debt, cash surplus, NWC cycle 63 days. Backward integration (PLI for LED components), solar capex for cost savings.
  • Macro Support: Govt. schemes (Jal Jeevan, Housing for All, CGD); GST cuts boosting rural/infra demand.
Headwinds (Challenges)
  • Seasonal/External Pressures: Extended monsoon delayed GI pipe demand (agri/infra); steel price drop caused ₹500/ton inventory loss (July).
  • Pricing/Competition: LED pricing pressure (industry-wide); competitive intensity in fans/appliances ahead of BLDC transition (Jan 2026, 10-12% price hikes planned).
  • Regulatory/Minor Issues: BSE/NSE fines (₹2.8L + GST each) for delayed Independent Director appointment; compliance delays noted.
  • Volume Flatness: Steel volumes historically stuck ~10L tons despite capex; H1 FY26 flat YoY.

Key Risks
  1. Commodity Volatility: Steel prices (HR Coil) – inventory losses; forex exposure in exports.
  2. Demand Execution: Monsoon/GST fund delays in agri/infra; PSU tender slowdowns.
  3. Competition/Margins: LED price erosion; FMEG intensity (fans BLDC shift).
  4. Capex/Execution: Greenfield delays; ₹500 Cr+ investments need demand to fill capacity.
  5. Regulatory/Compliance: SEBI fines; board composition issues.
  6. Macro: Rural slowdown, global trade barriers (e.g., US confusion).

Overall Outlook: Positive with steel exports and lighting stability as anchors. FY26 growth moderating but profitable (EBITDA margins 7-9%). Long-term upside from expansions/value-add (target 1.5-1.75M tons sales, 55-60% high-margin mix). Monitor steel cycles and rural revival. Recommendation: Accumulate on dips for 15-20% upside in 12-18 months.
