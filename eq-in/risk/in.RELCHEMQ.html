Analysis of Reliance Chemotex Industries Ltd. (RELCHEMQ)

Company Overview: Reliance Chemotex Industries Ltd. (CIN: L40102RJ1977PLC001994) is a single-segment yarn manufacturer based in Udaipur, Rajasthan. It focuses on yarn production with operations tied to textiles. Listed on BSE (503162) and NSE (RELCHEMQ). Key metrics from H1 FY26 (ended Sep 30, 2025): Revenue ₹191.89 Cr (+4.2% YoY), Net Profit ₹2.72 Cr (+35% YoY), EPS ₹3.60. Balance sheet stable at ~₹478 Cr assets; debt-heavy with ~₹252 Cr borrowings. Equity base ~₹7.55 Cr shares outstanding.

Tailwinds (Positive Factors)

  • Revenue & Profit Momentum: H1 FY26 revenue grew to ₹191.89 Cr from ₹184.17 Cr YoY, driven by operational efficiency (Q2 revenue ₹99.52 Cr, up ~8.7% YoY). Net profit surged 35% to ₹2.72 Cr, with PBT at ₹2.02 Cr (vs. ₹0.51 Cr YoY). Strong operating cash flow (₹23.02 Cr) supports working capital.
  • Cost Management & Cash Generation: Raw material costs stable (~49% of revenue), employee expenses controlled. Positive operating cash flow (₹23 Cr H1) amid inventory build-up indicates demand resilience.
  • Corporate Governance & Shareholder Approval: Overwhelming AGM support (67.77% voter turnout, 100%+ approval on key resolutions). Dividend of ₹0.50/share declared (yield ~0.7% at current levels).
  • Promoter Reclassification: Nominal promoter (Nand Gopal Khaitan, 100 shares/0.00%) seeking shift to public category (approved by board Nov 14; application filed Nov 18). Confirms no control/special rights; enhances free float perception, potential liquidity boost.
  • Focused Operations: No subsidiaries/associates; pure-play yarn segment with stable assets (PPE ₹268 Cr).

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • High Debt Burden: Borrowings ~₹252 Cr (53% of assets), with finance costs at ₹12.37 Cr H1 (stable YoY but ~6% of revenue). Net debt reduction (LT -₹2.63 Cr, ST -₹7.69 Cr H1) ongoing but slow.
  • Margin Pressure: Thin net margins (~1.4% H1); power & fuel spiked (Q2 ₹11.34 Cr vs. ₹6.34 Cr YoY, ~11% of Q2 revenue). EBITDA margins implied low (~5-6%).
  • Liquidity Squeeze: Cash & equivalents dropped to ₹0.15 Cr (from ₹0.58 Cr); other bank balances ₹11.32 Cr but tied to deposits. Negative net cash flow H1 (-₹0.43 Cr).
  • Cyclical Exposure: Yarn/textile sector vulnerable to raw material volatility (e.g., cotton) and muted Q2 other income (₹0.36 Cr vs. ₹3.75 Cr YoY).
  • Minor Director Transition: Khaitan (professional director, no control) re-appointed at AGM but now reclassifying; negligible shareholding impact.

Growth Prospects

  • Moderate Organic Growth (10-15% CAGR Potential): Textile/yarn demand tied to apparel exports/domestic consumption; H1 trend suggests FY26 revenue could hit ₹75-80 Cr (vs. FY25 ₹358 Cr). Efficiency gains (e.g., inventory optimization) and capex (CWIP ₹0.81 Cr) support capacity utilization.
  • Debt Optimization: Continued repayments could lower interest (~₹25 Cr FY25), freeing ~₹1-2 Cr annual profit. Positive FCF trajectory aids deleveraging.
  • Market Expansion: Export potential in yarns; stable customer base. Reclassification may attract institutional interest (current promoter holding ~67.7% per AGM).
  • Upside Catalysts: Power cost stabilization, volume growth in festive/winter seasons. No major capex drag; ROE ~4-5% improvable to 8-10% with margins.
