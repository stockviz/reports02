Analysis of CP Capital Limited (CPCAP) – Headwinds, Tailwinds, Growth Prospects, and Key Risks

CP Capital Limited (formerly Career Point Limited), an NBFC focused on lending (financing division ~80% of revenue) with an emerging infra/real estate vertical via subsidiary Career Point Infra Ltd (CPIL), has shown robust H1 FY26 performance post a major restructuring (merger of Srajan Capital and demerger of education business effective Apr 1, 2023). Key metrics (consolidated, Rs in lakhs): H1 FY26 revenue ₹3,749 (+4% YoY), PAT ₹2,094 (+0.5% YoY), assets ₹64,660 (+3% QoQ), equity ₹55,134 (strong base). Cash flows positive from operations (₹954) and financing (₹2,956), driven by borrowings. Below is a structured summary based on the provided filings (Reg 30 disclosures, Q2/H1 FY26 results, AGM outcomes).

Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts)

Factor Details Impact
Strong Financial Momentum H1 revenue ₹3,749 (Financing: ₹2,974; Infra: ₹741); PAT ₹2,094 (EPS ₹11.51). Interest income up (₹2,861). Cash pile at ₹2,694 (+507% QoQ). RoE implied ~15-20% annualized. Supports dividend potential, lending expansion.
NBFC Stability RBI CoR granted w.e.f. Apr 1, 2025 post-merger. Loans portfolio stable at ~₹40,000 despite minor impairments. Enables scalable lending growth in competitive NBFC space.
Infra Diversification CPIL (material subsidiary) formed “Sankalp Associates” (99% stake, ₹10L initial capital) for infra/real estate projects. Adds strategic vertical (H1 segment revenue ₹741, profit ₹590). Reduces financing mono-dependence; synergies with existing investment properties (₹17,253).
Governance & Shareholder Alignment AGM (Sep 29, 2025): 100% promoter support; all resolutions (financials, directors, auditors, RPTs) passed with 99%+ votes. Low dissent; fresh 5-year auditor mandate (S P Chopra & Co.) signals clean slate.

  • Core Lending (High Confidence): Financing division dominates (88% H1 revenue, assets ₹45,484). Loans stable/growing; interest income +4% YoY. NBFC status unlocks retail/SME lending opportunities. Target: 15-20% AUM growth FY26.
  • Infra/Real Estate Expansion (Medium Confidence): Sankalp partnership (Oct 2025) for “specific projects” enhances CPIL’s capabilities. Infra profit ₹590 (H1, +50% YoY); assets ₹19,177. Potential for JVs, land monetization (investment properties key asset).

  • Overall Outlook: 15-25% revenue CAGR possible FY26-28 via lending + infra projects. PAT growth hinges on low impairments. Segment-wise: Financing steady, Infra high-beta upside.
