Summary Analysis: AAKASH Exploration Services Limited (NSE: AAKASH)

Company Overview: Aakash Exploration Services Ltd. (CIN: L23209GJ2007PLC049792) is a single-segment player providing oil & gas extraction services. Paid-up capital: ₹10.125 Cr (101.25 Cr shares of ₹1 each). FY25 annual revenue: ₹98.19 Cr; PAT: ₹1.84 Cr (EPS ₹0.18). H1 FY26 (ended 30-Sep-2025) shows revenue of ₹48.66 Cr (flat YoY), PAT ₹0.82 Cr (down 37.5% YoY). Balance sheet expanded to ₹106.52 Cr (driven by capex), with equity at ₹61.18 Cr. Heavy capex (₹11.87 Cr in H1) funded by debt, leading to cash burn. AGM (22-Sep-2025) passed all resolutions with strong promoter support (~67-100% voting), but ~20% public dissent on director re-appointments.

Tailwinds

  • Capex-Driven Expansion: PPE surged 12.6% QoQ/YoY to ₹69.13 Cr (H1 capex ₹11.87 Cr), signaling capacity build-up for oil/gas services amid potential sector recovery.
  • Sequential Revenue Growth: Q2 FY26 revenue ₹24.81 Cr (up 4% QoQ from ₹23.86 Cr), with gross revenue run-rate stable at ~₹98 Cr annualized.
  • Asset Quality & Liquidity: Trade receivables up but managed (₹25.41 Cr); total assets +10.5% YoY. Other income (₹0.48 Cr H1) supports margins.
  • Promoter Alignment: 100% promoter votes in favor of key resolutions; re-appointments of Whole Time Directors (Haria family) indicate continuity.
  • Auditor Comfort: Clean limited review report; compliant with Ind AS & SEBI Reg 33.

Headwinds

  • YoY Profitability Pressure: H1 PAT down 37.5% to ₹0.82 Cr (PBT margin 2.2% vs 3.6% YoY); Q2 PBT ₹0.37 Cr (down 48% QoQ). Employee costs +14.5% YoY (₹17.61 Cr), other expenses high (₹18.26 Cr).
  • Weak Operating Cash Flow: H1 OCF ₹0.61 Cr (down 89% YoY from ₹5.50 Cr), impacted by working capital (trade receivables +₹3.76 Cr) and taxes (₹0.95 Cr paid).
  • Revenue Stagnation: H1 ops revenue -0.8% YoY to ₹48.66 Cr; vulnerable to oil price volatility & exploration slowdown.
  • Margin Erosion: Employee (36%), depreciation (8%), & other expenses (37%) dominate; finance costs up (₹1.09 Cr H1).

Growth Prospects

  • High: Capex intensity (12% of FY25 revenue in H1 alone) positions for multi-year revenue ramp-up in oil/gas services (e.g., drilling/rig operations). Sequential top-line momentum + stable client base could drive FY26 revenue to ₹105-110 Cr if oil prices stabilize >$70/bbl.
  • Medium-Term Catalysts: Single-segment focus reduces diversification risk; potential export/domestic E&P upcycle (India’s oil demand growth ~5% CAGR). Equity reserves (₹51.05 Cr) support internal accruals.
  • Base Case: 10-15% revenue CAGR FY26-28 if capex yields 20%+ capacity utilization; EPS could rebound to ₹0.25+.
  • Upside: Govt. push for domestic hydrocarbon exploration (e.g., OALP bids) favors service providers.
