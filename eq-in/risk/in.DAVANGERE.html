Davangere Sugar Company Limited (DAVANGERE / 543267) Analysis

Overview: DSCL is a Karnataka-based integrated sugar, ethanol, and cogeneration player with 4,750 TCD crushing, 65 KLPD distillery (expanding), and 24 MW power. Post-rights issue (share capital up ~52% to ₹14,300L), focus is on ethanol-led growth amid India’s biofuel push. H1FY26 (ended Sep’25) showed revenue of ~₹7,330L (distillery ~92%), PBT ₹506L (EPS ₹0.14 half-year), but sugar losses and high debt persist. All AGM resolutions (expansions, director pay, MoA changes) passed with 99%+ approval.

Tailwinds (Positive Momentum)

  • Ethanol Policy Support: Aligned with Ethanol Blending Program (EBP); distillery revenue dominates (Q2: ₹4,403L/₹4,819L ops, segment PBT ₹2,174L H1). Expansion to 85 KLPD (FY26) then 110 KLPD positions for year-round ops via grains (rice/maize trading).
  • Rights Issue Capital Infusion: ₹4,891L proceeds + ₹10,028L premium boosted equity (₹50,294L total vs. ₹34,982L prior), reduced working capital borrowings (down ₹8,720L), cash up to ₹1,197L.
  • Diversification & Efficiency: CO₂ plant (35 TPD), grain trading, mechanized harvesting/transport subsidies target 5L MT cane crush. Zero-waste model (fertilizers, power) enhances margins.
  • Strong Promoter Backing: Overwhelming AGM votes (99%+); leadership emphasizes farmer ties and energy transition.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Sugar Segment Drag: Chronic losses (H1 PBT -₹161L; inventories ₹15,719L vs. ₹14,453L). Q2 changes in FG WIP +₹975L inflated expenses.
  • High Debt & Costs: Finance costs Q2 ₹944L (H1 ₹1,606L); total borrowings ~₹20k L (NC ₹6,359L + C ₹13,617L). Operating CF negative (H1 -₹2,740L) due to WC (inventories -₹1,266L, debtors -₹831L).
  • Seasonality: Sugar/cane cyclical; Q2 revenue spike but co-gen/aviation weak (co-gen H1 loss ₹194L).
  • Low Public Float Participation: AGM e-voting ~42% turnout; public non-insts <1% shares voted in some items.

Growth Prospects

  • High (Ethanol-Driven): Capacity ramps + grains/CO₂ to unlock ₹ revenue streams; distillery scaled to ~2x (H1 already ₹6,757L rev). Cane targets + farmer incentives for supply stability.
  • Medium-Term Catalysts: Post-rights execution (FY26 CO₂/grain ops), biofuel demand (India’s 20% blending by 2025). Integrated ops could lift ROE (equity base stronger).
  • Projections: If ethanol sustained @ H1 margins (~32% seg), FY26 rev >₹25k L possible (prior FY ₹21,676L); EPS upside to ₹0.50-0.70 annualized.

Summary Verdict: Moderate Buy/Bullish Tilt. Tailwinds from ethanol expansion and rights-funded growth outweigh sugar headwinds; distillery profitability (70%+ of seg PBT) drives prospects amid biofuel boom. Risks center on debt/execution, but low valuations (post-rights equity dilution) offer upside. Monitor Q3 debt reduction and ethanol ramp. Target: 20-30% upside if expansions deliver; watch sugar recovery and policy. (Data as of Nov’25 filings; market context: cyclical agri stock).
