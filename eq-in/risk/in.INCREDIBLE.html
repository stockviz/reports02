Analysis of Incredible Industries Limited (INCREDIBLE / Scrip: 538365 BSE, INCREDIBLE NSE)

Company Overview: Incredible Industries Ltd. (CIN: L27100WB1979PLC032200) is engaged in a single segment of Iron & Steel Products. FY25 revenue: ~Rs. 756 Cr; PAT: ~Rs. 12.5 Cr. H1 FY26 (ended 30.09.2025) shows revenue growth to Rs. 432 Cr (+18.6% YoY) and PAT Rs. 5.2 Cr (+43.6% YoY). Equity shares: 4.68 Cr (FV Rs. 10), market cap ~Rs. 150-200 Cr (indicative). Promoter holding ~75%. Recent actions: Divested minor windmill asset (Rs. 1.5 Cr), board changes, capex in backward integration, AGM resolutions passed unanimously.

Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts)

  • Strong Financial Momentum: H1 FY26 revenue up 18.6% YoY to Rs. 432 Cr; EBITDA margins stable (~5-6%); PAT growth 43.6% YoY. Operating cash flow positive at Rs. 3.53 Cr (H1), supported by efficient working capital management in H1.
  • Debt Reduction: Non-current borrowings at zero; current borrowings down to Rs. 15 Cr. Finance costs halved YoY (Rs. 1.11 Cr H1 FY26 vs. Rs. 1.93 Cr H1 FY25). Lease liabilities managed.
  • High Promoter Confidence: ~75% holding; 100% approval on all 8 AGM resolutions (financials, director re-appointments, RPTs, debentures, auditors). Re-appointment of CMD Rama Shankar Gupta.
  • Asset Monetization: Sold non-core windmill (K-342) for Rs. 1.5 Cr (0.06% revenue, 0.89% net worth contributor), infusing liquidity without material impact.
  • Governance: Unaudited results reviewed/limited reviewed; no subsidiaries; ISO-certified.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Liquidity Strain: Cash & equivalents low at Rs. 0.14 Cr (down sharply); heavy reliance on fixed deposits (Rs. 15.75 Cr) and working capital loans. Net cash burn in investing/financing activities.
  • Working Capital Intensity: High inventories (Rs. 42 Cr), trade receivables (Rs. 38.5 Cr, ~90 days implied), other current assets (Rs. 46.5 Cr) – total current assets Rs. 143 Cr vs. current liabilities Rs. 43.5 Cr, but ties up capital.
  • Margin Pressures: Raw material costs ~88% of revenue; changes in inventories volatile (positive Rs. 19 Cr H1). Other expenses elevated.
  • Board Transitions: Resignation of Non-Exec Director Niket Agarwal (personal reasons); new appointee Raj Prakash Verma (CS-qualified, corporate advisory experience) – potential short-term continuity risk.
  • Macro Steel Sector: Potential input cost volatility (e.g., iron ore, coal); competition from larger players.

Growth Prospects

  • Backward Integration Capex: Rs. 12.7 Cr CWIP for 256,000 MTPA Steel Melting Shop (SMS), rolling mill upgrade/automation, and hot billet charging to eliminate reheating fuel costs and boost yield. Expected to enhance capacity, margins (yield improvement), and competitiveness in steel products.
  • Volume-Led Growth: H1 revenue growth indicates demand recovery; single-segment focus allows operational leverage.
  • Fundraising Flexibility: AGM approval for private placement of Non-Convertible Debentures (NCDs) – could fund capex without equity dilution.
  • Profitability Trajectory: EPS H1 *1.13 (annualized ~2.26); FY25 EPS 2.70. Potential 15-20% revenue CAGR if capex executes on time (FY27+).
  • RPT Approvals: Material related party transactions greenlit for FY27, ensuring supply chain stability.

Overall Summary: Incredible is a micro-cap steel player showing turnaround momentum (revenue/PAT growth, debt deleveraging) with strong promoter backing. Key growth driver is SMS backward integration for cost efficiencies, positioning for 15-20% CAGR. Near-term headwinds from liquidity/working capital persist, but tailwinds outweigh. Recommendation: Positive watchlist for execution; monitor Q3 FY26 results and capex progress. Risks moderate; upside from steel cycle recovery.
