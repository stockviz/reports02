Analysis of Shreyans Industries Limited (SHREYANIND)

Shreyans Industries Limited is a paper manufacturing company focused on a single segment: “Writing and Printing Paper.” The analysis is based on the provided disclosures: (1) CCI investigation notice (Nov 12, 2025), and (2) unaudited Q2/H1 FY26 financial results (ended Sep 30, 2025), including P&L, balance sheet, cash flows, and auditor review. Financials show robust historical growth but some recent cash flow pressures. All figures in Rs lakhs unless stated.

Tailwinds (Positive Factors)

  • Strong Revenue & Profitability Growth: Revenue from operations grew ~2x from FY22 (implied ~26k annualized) to FY25 (61,677), with Q Sep’24 at 26,803 (down slightly from Q Sep’23’s 29,593 but still elevated). H1 FY26 revenue robust at ~42k+ (inferred from trends). PBT margins healthy (11%+ in recent quarters), with net profit at 2,304 (Q Sep’24) and EPS 16.67 (basic, not annualized). Total comprehensive income up YoY.
  • Asset-Light, Investment-Driven Income: High current investments (25,140 as of Sep’25, up from 23,638 Mar’25) generate significant “other income” (1,655 Q Sep’24, ~40% from fair value gains). Other equity strong at 42,520 (Sep’25), supporting EPS of ~36.60 annualized FY25.
  • Healthy Balance Sheet: Total assets grew 4% QoQ to 64,065. Equity base solid (43,902 or ~68% of assets). Low non-current debt (792 borrowings). Inventory and receivables managed (inventories up 8% QoQ but controlled).
  • Operational Efficiency: Cost controls evident in employee expenses stable (~7-8% of revenue), depreciation steady. ISO certifications signal quality focus.
  • Regulatory Compliance Stated: Full cooperation with authorities; unmodified auditor review.

Headwinds (Negative Factors)

  • CCI Investigation: Officials raided Delhi office (Nov 11, 2025) for an unspecified probe under competition laws. Could signal anti-competitive practices (e.g., pricing/cartel in paper sector). No details on scope/impact, but creates overhang; material updates promised.
  • Margin Pressures: High variable costs – power/fuel (20%+ of expenses, 5,524 Q Sep’24), materials (47%). Negative inventory changes inflate costs. PBT dipped slightly QoQ in some periods.
  • Cash Flow Weakness: H1 FY26 operating cash flow negative at -637 (vs. -745 H1 FY25), due to working capital outflows (trade receivables up, payables down). Investing cash positive from investment sales (+921), but capex heavy (-2,280 PPE/CWIP).
  • Cyclical Exposure: Paper sector sensitive to raw material (pulp/wood) volatility, energy prices. Revenue dipped in Q Sep’22 (13,247).
  • Single-Segment Reliance: 100% “Writing & Printing Paper” – vulnerable to digitalization/substitutes.

Growth Prospects

  • Moderate to High (Sector-Tailored): Paper demand steady in India (education, packaging shift from printing paper). Revenue CAGR ~20% (FY22-25); if sustained, FY26 revenue could hit 70k+ (12-15% growth). Investment portfolio (25k+ liquid assets) provides non-operating buffer for acquisitions/capex (CWIP at 1,089, up 9% QoQ).
  • Expansion Potential: Capex ongoing (PPE stable at 23k+ but CWIP rising). Dividend payouts consistent (recent ~873 H1 FY25), signaling confidence. Equity reserves support buybacks/debt reduction.
  • Upside Catalysts: Resolution of CCI probe positively; favorable pulp prices; export growth (if any). EPS trajectory (16.67 quarterly → 36.60 FY) supports 15-20% annualized returns if margins hold.
  • Base Case: 10-15% revenue growth FY26, driven by volume recovery post any FY25 slowdown.

Summary

Bull Case: SHREYANIND is a growth stock with doubled revenue, fat margins (10%+ PBT), and a fortress balance sheet (68% equity-funded, liquid investments). Tailwinds from efficiency and non-ops income outweigh sector cyclicality; growth prospects solid at 10-15% if CCI resolves cleanly. Target multiples: 8-10x FY26 EPS (~300-400/share potential).

Bear Case: CCI probe is a red flag (fresh Nov’25 raid), signaling governance risks in oligopolistic paper sector. Cash burn + cost pressures cap upside; digital disruption looms long-term.

Overall Outlook: Neutral-Positive (Hold/Buy on dips). Fundamentals strong (ROE ~12% implied), but monitor CCI updates. Stock likely trades at 0.7-1x book (~300-400); near-term volatility from probe. FY26 EPS est. 35-40; revenue 65-70k. Investors: Favor for income/dividends over pure growth.
