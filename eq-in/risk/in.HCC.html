Analysis of Hindustan Construction Company Ltd. (HCC) - Q2 FY26 Overview

HCC, a veteran infrastructure player with a ~100-year legacy in highways, hydro, nuclear, metro, and tunneling projects, reported Q2 FY26 results amid ongoing deleveraging and order wins. Standalone revenue fell 20% YoY to ₹958 Cr (PAT ₹37 Cr), while consolidated revenue dropped 32% to ₹961 Cr (PAT ₹48 Cr), impacted by project phasing, divestments (e.g., Steiner AG), and seasonality. Orderbook stands at a robust ₹13,152 Cr (up from ₹11,852 Cr in FY25), with a ₹57,000 Cr bid pipeline. A ₹1,000 Cr Rights Issue is approved to aid debt reduction (total debt ~₹3,050 Cr as of Oct 2025).

Headwinds (Challenges Pressuring Performance)

  • Revenue and Margin Pressure: Q2 standalone revenue down 20% YoY; EBITDA margin compressed to 16.1% (vs. 17.8%). Consolidated revenue decline sharper at 32% due to divestment of Steiner AG and lumpy project execution.
  • High Debt Burden: Finance costs remain elevated (standalone ₹112 Cr in Q2); total debt ₹3,050 Cr despite ₹339 Cr prepayments YTD FY26. Debt/Equity at 0.71x (standalone), but cash flow from ops negative (₹82 Cr outflow H1).
  • Receivables Delays: ~₹511 Cr (unbilled WIP + trade receivables) tied to closed projects under arbitration/litigation; management confident but adds uncertainty.
  • Auditor Qualifications: Modified review reports flag recoverability of ₹1,159 Cr investment in subsidiary HCC Infra (net worth eroded) and ₹189 Cr net DTA (history of losses questions future profitability assumptions).
  • Seasonality and Project Phasing: Infra business inherently lumpy; Q2 impacted by monsoon and execution ramps.

Tailwinds (Supportive Factors)

  • Robust Orderbook and Diversification: ₹13,152 Cr backlog (68% hydro/nuclear/buildings, 22% transport, balanced geographically across 10 states). Provides ~2-3 years visibility.
  • Strong Order Inflows: Secured ₹2,770 Cr (Patna Metro packages + Hindalco smelter); L1 for ₹840 Cr. Bids under eval: ₹29,581 Cr (hydro-dominant).
  • Execution Momentum: Key milestones – Mumbai Metro Line 3 inaugurated (PM Modi); Tehri PSP Unit 7 grid-synced; 7.5/12.1 km TBM tunneling at Vishnugad Pipalkoti; Indore Metro/Bhivpuri PSP piling/excavation advancing.
  • Deleveraging Progress: ₹339 Cr prepayments FY26; ₹450 Cr more expected Q3. HCC CG on Prolific Resolution debt cut to 20% (97% lender approval). Awards ₹1,800 Cr available.
  • Capital Infusion: ₹1,000-1,100 Cr Rights Issue on track (Q3 completion) to further cut debt.
  • Macro Support: India’s infra boom (hydro, metro, pumped storage) aligns with HCC’s expertise (26% India’s hydro, 60% nuclear capacity built historically).

Growth Prospects

  • High Bid Pipeline: ₹57,000 Cr opportunity (hydro ₹19,888 Cr, transport ₹2,940 Cr, water ₹6,753 Cr); potential to double orderbook in 12-18 months.
  • Sector Tailwinds: Govt push for hydro/PSP (Tehri, Bhivpuri), urban metro (Patna, Indore), and private capex (Hindalco ₹204 Cr win signals diversification beyond govt ~90% orders).
  • Balance Sheet Strengthening: Rights Issue + cash accruals could reduce debt to <₹2,500 Cr, lowering finance costs (already down 16% YoY) and boosting margins to 18-20%.
  • EBITDA/PAT Trajectory: H1 FY26 EBITDA margins stable ~15-16%; full-year guidance intact at ₹2,000-2,200 Cr revenue, with order execution ramp-up in H2.
  • Long-Term: EPC focus on high-margin hydro/tunneling; potential private sector expansion (aluminium smelters, process plants).

Summary

Bull Case: HCC is at an inflection – robust ₹13k Cr orderbook + ₹57k Cr pipeline positions it for 15-20% revenue CAGR FY26-28, aided by Rights Issue deleveraging (debt/equity <0.5x target) and infra tailwinds. Margins could rebound to 18%+ with execution ramps; PAT growth to ₹200+ Cr FY26 possible.
Bear Case: Persistent receivables delays, debt drag, and auditor flags could cap upside if bids convert slowly or cash burn continues.
Overall Outlook: Positive with Caution. Tailwinds (orders, deleveraging) outweigh headwinds; stock likely re-rates on Rights Issue execution and H2 inflows. Target multiples: 0.3-0.4x orderbook (current ~0.2x). Monitor Q3 debt metrics and arbitration wins. FY26 EV/EBITDA ~6-7x appears undervalued vs. peers (L&T ~25x).
