TATACHEM (Tata Chemicals Limited) Analysis - Q2/H1 FY26

Overall Performance Snapshot (Q2 FY26 ended Sep 30, 2025): - Consolidated: Revenue ₹3,877 Cr (-3% YoY), EBITDA ₹537 Cr (-13% YoY), PAT ₹219 Cr (-18% YoY pre-exceptional/NCI). H1: Revenue ₹7,596 Cr (-2% YoY), EBITDA flat at ₹1,186 Cr, PAT ₹535 Cr (+21% YoY). - Standalone (India-focused): Strong growth with Revenue ₹1,204 Cr (+19% YoY), EBITDA ₹240 Cr (+67% YoY), PAT ₹178 Cr (+80% YoY). H1: Revenue +15%, EBITDA +35%, PAT +37%. - Key drivers: Volume growth in India offset by global pricing weakness; one-offs (UK provisioning ₹65 Cr, US WIP ₹5M). Net debt ₹5,583 Cr (up due to forex/inventories).

Headwinds (Challenges Pressuring Margins/Performance)
  • Global Soda Ash Oversupply: High inventories (China ~1.65M tons), operating rates elevated. Prices weakened further; Chinese spot prices down 56-58% since Q2FY23. SE Asia/Northeast Asia hit hard by Chinese exports.
  • Pricing Pressure in Key Markets:
    • India: Imports (avg. $232-236/ton) despite MIP extension; anti-dumping duty (ADD) recommended ($17-100/ton) but pending MoF approval.
    • US: Export prices dropping; Q2 EBITDA depressed by WIP drawdown (~₹55 Cr normalized run-rate).
    • UK/Kenya: Lower realizations/volumes; Lostock closure complete but one-offs persist.
  • Geopolitical/Macro: Tariff uncertainties (e.g., PV glass in Malaysia/Vietnam), slow economic recovery into 2026, China capacity shifts delaying synthetic closures.
  • Operational: Kenya shipment delays (resolving H2), currency depreciation (GBP impacting UK savings), higher net debt.
Tailwinds (Supportive Factors)
  • India Strength: Higher volumes across Soda Ash (+30kt YoY), Salt (+50kt), Bicarb (+14kt), FOS (858kt vs. 675kt). Cost efficiencies drove standalone EBITDA margins up.
  • Litigation Wins:
    • Kenya Court of Appeal (Oct 24, 2025): Ruled land rates demand (₹783 Cr) “arbitrary/illegal”; removes contingent liability (Supreme Court appeal possible within 14 days).
    • Kenya tax settlement: ₹16 Cr paid/closed.
  • UK Turnaround: Reconfiguration done; focus on pharma-grade salt/bicarb. Expect EBITDA positive Q3, steady Q4 (volumes depressed by 4kt in Q2).
  • Policy Support: India ADD recommendation; salutary effect on imports already visible.
  • Cost Discipline: Fixed costs controlled; power/fuel savings from UK closure.
Growth Prospects (Medium/Long-Term Opportunities)
  • India Expansion (Capex ~₹1,000 Cr FY26):
    • Soda Ash: +15% phase-1, then +35% (total +50%, incl. 150kt quick/350kt phased).
    • Bi-carb/Silica: +42kt Cuddalore, +60kt Mithapur.
    • FOS: New 5kt L55/P95 commissioned; ramp to 80% util. then +5kt.
    • Funded by ₹1,500 Cr NCD private placement (approved Nov 1, 2025).
  • Global Tailwinds: Medium-term Soda Ash demand from solar PV/EV; China policy targeting 50:50 natural:synthetic mix (new 5-6M tons natural by 2029-30, but synthetic rationalization key).
  • Portfolio Shift: UK non-cyclical focus; Rallis EBITDA margin 18%; sustainability (e.g., flue gas bicarb demand).
  • EBITDA Savings Target: ₹600 Cr FY26 revised to ~75% (₹450 Cr) due to forex/pricing; still resilient.

Concise Summary: TATACHEM faces near-term headwinds from Soda Ash oversupply/pricing pressure (global/India imports), offset by strong India volumes, cost controls, and litigation wins (Kenya ₹783 Cr relief). Tailwinds include ADD prospects and UK pivot. Growth anchored in India capex (Soda Ash +50%, specialties), targeting H2FY26 recovery. Investment Thesis: Resilient standalone (20% NPM); monitor ADD/pricing stabilization. FY26 EBITDA savings ~₹450 Cr achievable; long-term positive on EV/solar. Risks tilted to commodities; diversify via specialties. Rating Outlook: Neutral-Positive (volume-led recovery H2).
