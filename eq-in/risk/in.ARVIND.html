Analysis of Arvind Limited (ARVIND) - Q2 FY26 Overview

Headwinds (Challenges Pressuring Performance)

  • US Tariffs and Trade Uncertainty: Direct US revenue exposure ~₹500 Cr (21% of topline). Q2 impact: ₹23 Cr on EBITDA; H2 expected ₹25-30 Cr/quarter. Partially offset by volumes/cost actions, but restricts margin expansion (e.g., EBITDA margin +41 bps YoY despite ~140 bps potential without tariffs). No customer loss, but ongoing unpredictability in US business.
  • Margin Pressure: EBITDA margins improved modestly (11.0% Q2, 10.2% H1), but below pre-tariff trajectory (~12%). Textile margins dipped slightly to 10.0% due to tariff absorption.
  • Regulatory/Compliance: Fines of ₹3.45 Lakhs (ex-GST) each from BSE/NSE for delayed board composition (Reg 17(1) non-compliance); resolved via ID appointment effective Sep 8, 2025. No material financial impact.
  • Macro Volatility: Global disruptions (geopolitics, trade wars) affecting supply chains; prior Ethiopia asset phase-out and Santej strike (resolved).

Tailwinds (Supportive Factors Driving Resilience)

  • Strong Volume Growth: Textiles led with Garments +17% YoY (10.7 Mn pcs record), Denim +16% (15 Mn mtr), Woven +8% (35 Mn mtr, 100% utilization). Full near-term order book.
  • Revenue & Profitability Momentum: Q2 revenue +8% (record ₹2,371 Cr), EBITDA +13%, PAT +70%; H1 PAT +52%. Segment-wise: Textiles revenue +10.4%, AMD +14.9%.
  • Operational Efficiency: Vertical integration, new customers/geographies, cost optimizations. ROCE +50 bps to 14.4% (17.2% normalized). Net debt stable at ₹1,270 Cr.
  • Strategic Moves: AMD demerger to wholly-owned subsidiary (AAML) completed (Aug 2025), unlocking focus. Sustainability edge (supercritical CO2 dyeing: -76% water; SBTi net-zero by FY50; H&M/Circ Inc. partnerships).
  • Shareholder Approvals: Postal ballot (Nov 2025) passed special resolutions for ID appointment and promoter ED remunerations (92-95% approval).

Growth Prospects

  • Core Segments: AMD targeting 18-20% revenue growth (defense resumption, industrials/composites resurgence). Garments mid-teens volume growth via capacity utilization/verticalization. Textiles: Healthy Q3 order book, H2 seasonality.
  • Diversification: Non-US expansion, balanced portfolio (fabrics/garments). Q3 macro tailwinds: Strong US consumption (Fed cuts), India GST/tax relief, extended wedding season.
  • Capex & Expansion: FY26 capex ₹400-450 Cr (₹220 Cr spent H1); selective deferrals for liquidity.
  • Long-Term: Integrated supply chain resilience; ESG leadership for premium clients. Run-rate ROCE trajectory supports scaling.

Key Risks

  • Tariff Escalation: Further US policy changes could amplify EBITDA hit (20-25% revenue exposure); mitigation via pricing/sourcing ongoing but untested.
  • Demand Volatility: Global uncertainty (US slowdown, geopolitics) risks order delays; heavy US reliance despite diversification.
  • Execution Risks: Capex absorption, working capital (inventories/receivables up), debt servicing (interest ₹41 Cr Q2).
  • Regulatory/External: Forex fluctuations (cash flow hedges loss ₹23 Cr Q2), labor issues (past strikes), competition in textiles/AMD.
  • Governance: Promoter remuneration approvals faced ~8-17% institutional dissent; ongoing board compliance scrutiny.

Summary

Arvind delivered robust Q2/H1 FY26 results (revenue +8-9%, EBITDA +13%, PAT +52-70%) amid tariff headwinds, powered by record textile volumes and AMD rebound—showcasing operational resilience and strategic agility. Tailwinds from diversification, sustainability, and order visibility support mid-teens segment growth, with FY26 capex driving capacity. However, US tariffs (~₹25-30 Cr/quarter drag) cap margins, while trade/geopolitical risks loom large. Overall Outlook: Positive (Buy/Hold bias for long-term players)—strong fundamentals offset near-term pressures; monitor Q3 tariff mitigation and US demand. ROCE trajectory and debt stability bolster balance sheet health. (Data as of Nov 2025 filings.)
