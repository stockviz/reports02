Devyani International Limited (DEVYANI) - Q2 FY26 Analysis Summary

Devyani International Limited (DIL), India’s largest Yum Brands franchisee (KFC, Pizza Hut), reported consolidated revenue of INR 1,377 Cr (+12.6% YoY) driven by store expansion (2,184 stores, +39 NNUs), but faced margin pressure with Reported EBITDA at INR 194 Cr (14.1% margin, down YoY) due to Sky Gate consolidation, promotions, and delivery costs. KFC India grew 5.3% YoY (ADS INR 89k), Pizza Hut flat, International +14%. Demand remains muted amid weak consumption, but expansion and diversification provide buffers. Below is a structured analysis of headwinds, tailwinds, growth prospects, and key risks.

Headwinds (Challenges Impacting Performance)

  • Weak Demand & SSSG Pressure: Moderately negative SSSG (KFC impacted by Shraavana/Navaratri overlap, unseasonal rains in East/Pujo; Pizza Hut -4%). Oct/Nov trends still muted; consumers value-conscious, relying on promotions/low-price items (e.g., Chana Burger at INR 69).
  • Margin Compression: Brand contribution down to 11.7% (ex-Sky Gate: 12.4%); gross margins stable at 67.8% but hit by aggregator/delivery costs (off-premise mix: KFC 46%, PH 55%), promotions, and Sky Gate drag (70bps EBITDA impact).
  • Seasonal/External Factors: Festive disruptions (rains, civil unrest in Nepal), channel shift hurting dine-in profitability.
  • International Softness: Thailand negative SSSG despite revenue growth; optimizing expansion amid high store density (~1,150 KFC stores).

Tailwinds (Supportive Factors)

  • Robust Store Expansion: 39 NNUs (KFC India +30); total India stores at 1,802. Demonstrated execution capability.
  • International Resilience: +14% YoY revenue (INR 450 Cr), 16.7% brand margins (Thailand-led); Nepal/Thailand additions ongoing.
  • Policy Benefits: GST 2.0 minor input cost savings passed to consumers; no output GST relief.
  • Innovation & Diversification: Strong menu response (KFC grilled chicken/LTOs, PH Ultimate Cheese Crust); Tealive test launch (6 stores, positive feedback); Sky Gate (BBK/Goila) momentum post-Dussehra.
  • Cost Efficiencies: Gross margin tweaks (input costs, operations) sustainable QoQ; Pizza Hut near breakeven.

Growth Prospects (Medium-Term Opportunities)

  • Aggressive Expansion: FY26 KFC India 100-110 stores; Franchise/Own brands ~100 stores (low capex INR 50 Cr); total ~250-300 NNUs possible.
  • Sky Gate Turnaround: Breakeven brand contribution by Mar 2026; airport/food court tests for BBK.
  • Portfolio Diversification: Tealive/NY Fries/Sanook scaling post-tests; Own brands (Vaango/BBK) steady; International optimization (Thailand 20-21 stores FY26).
  • QSR Tailwinds: India’s consumption recovery (GST 2.0 boost), organized player shift; Yum protection on Pizza Hut agreements amid global rationalization.
  • Guidance: H2 festive strength (Q3 big quarter); margin stabilization as promotions optimize, Sky Gate improves.
