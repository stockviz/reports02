Analysis of Indo Amines Limited (INDOAMIN)

Overview: Indo Amines Limited, a chemical manufacturing company (single segment), reported solid H1 FY26 (ended Sep 2025) results with revenue growth of ~3% YoY to ₹538 Cr and PAT growth of ~35% to ₹46 Cr (standalone). Q2 showed mild sequential softness, but profitability remains robust. The 32nd AGM (Sep 24, 2025) saw unanimous approvals (99.99% in favor across resolutions), including ESOP plan, signaling strong governance and promoter confidence (92.56% promoter voting participation).

Tailwinds (Positive Factors)

  • Profitability Momentum: H1 PBT up 30% YoY to ₹60 Cr; PAT up 35% to ₹46 Cr; EPS doubled YoY to ₹6.33. Q1 boosted by ₹595 lakh gain from leasehold land transfer (other income ₹203 Cr H1 vs ₹126 Cr H1 FY25).
  • Revenue Stability: H1 revenue +3% YoY; consistent quarterly ops revenue ~₹267-271 Cr.
  • Financial Health: Net worth up 25% YoY to ₹3,575 Cr; healthy coverage ratios (ISCR 7.28x, DSCR 1.31x); stable debt metrics (D/E 0.85x).
  • Shareholder Confidence: 10% dividend declared (₹0.50/share); all 11 AGM resolutions (incl. director re-appointments, ESOP) passed overwhelmingly with near-zero dissent.
  • Talent Retention: Approval of ESOP 2025 plan (extendable to subsidiaries) aids employee incentives.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Revenue Softness: Q2 revenue -1% QoQ and flat YoY; ops margins compressed to 15% H1 (vs higher inventory turns in FY25).
  • Cost Pressures: Employee expenses +14% H1 YoY; finance costs +13%; depreciation +22% (capex signal?).
  • One-Time Boost Fading: Q2 other income halved post-land gain; core ops profitability may moderate.
  • Operational Disruptions: Lingering impact of Jun 2024 fire at Dombivli plant (₹260 Cr inventory + ₹336 Cr PPE loss, booked as insurance receivables).

Growth Prospects

  • Moderate Organic Growth: Chemical mfg focus; H1 revenue trajectory suggests FY26 ops revenue ~₹1,050-1,100 Cr (in-line with FY25). Profitability edge from cost efficiencies could drive 15-20% PAT CAGR if margins hold.
  • Strategic Flexibility: ESOP approval enables expansion incentives; land monetization (₹600 Cr deal) provides liquidity for capex/debt reduction.
  • Promoter Backing: 58% promoter holding with 100% AGM support; potential for inorganic moves (subsidiaries eligible for ESOP).
  • Outlook: Steady demand in chemicals; net worth growth supports scaling. Target FY26 PAT ₹120-140 Cr if insurance recoveries materialize and no major disruptions.

Key Risks

  • Insurance Dependency: ₹597 Cr fire losses (FY25) as receivables; delays/non-recovery could hit balance sheet/cash flows.
  • Liquidity/Debt: Current ratio stable at 1.28x but LT debt/working capital 0.80x; DSCR dipped to 1.31x (monitor EBITDA stability).
  • Regulatory/External: Pending Social Security Code notification may raise employee costs; single-segment exposure to chemical cycles/volatility.
  • Margin Sustainability: Inventory turnover halved YoY (8.11x); bad debts nil but AR turnover moderate (3.84x); one-off gains mask core pressures.
  • Market: Debtor turnover slowdown; total debt/assets steady at 37% but rising finance costs signal interest rate sensitivity.

Summary Verdict: Positive Bias (Buy/Hold). Tailwinds from profit growth, dividend, and governance outweigh Q2 softness. Growth anchored in core chemicals with ESOP upside; risks mitigated by insurance/strong net worth. Monitor Q3 for fire recovery and organic momentum. FY26 EV/EBITDA ~8-10x appears fair.
