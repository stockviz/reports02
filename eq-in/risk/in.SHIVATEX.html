Analysis of Shiva Texyarn Limited (SHIVATEX) - Q2 & H1 FY26 (Ended 30.09.2025)

Overall Performance Summary
Shiva Texyarn Limited, a textile manufacturer (single reportable segment under Ind AS 108), reported robust H1 FY26 results (consolidated basis). Revenue grew 16% YoY to ₹18,124 lakhs, driven by sequential strength in Q2 (₹10,505 lakhs, +38% QoQ). PAT surged ~101% YoY to ₹827 lakhs (EPS ₹6.38, up 101% YoY), aided by operating leverage, inventory efficiencies, and associate contributions (L.K. Distributors Pvt Ltd). Balance sheet stable with equity at ₹14,316 lakhs; inventories down 19% YoY but receivables up 53%. Operating cash flow strong at ₹1,313 lakhs. Unmodified auditor review; board approved on 05.11.2025.

Tailwinds (Positive Factors)

  • Revenue Momentum: H1 growth of 16% YoY; Q2 +10% YoY/+38% QoQ signals demand recovery in textiles (possibly exports/domestic apparel). Other income up (₹117 lakhs H1).
  • Margin Expansion: PBT margins improved to 5.5% (H1) from 3.6% YoY; total expenses as % of revenue declined to 95% from 96.7%, driven by lower inventory changes (+₹90 lakhs vs. -₹736 lakhs YoY) and cost controls.
  • Working Capital Efficiency: Inventories reduced 19% to ₹5,944 lakhs (from ₹7,382 lakhs FY25-end), freeing cash. Op. CF positive ₹1,313 lakhs (flat YoY but resilient).
  • Profitability Leverage: PAT doubled YoY; associate added ~₹52 lakhs share of profit (H1). Reserves up 6% to ₹13,020 lakhs.
  • Debt Moderation: Long-term borrowings down 18% to ₹2,202 lakhs; disciplined capex (₹320 lakhs outflow).

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Rising Receivables: Trade receivables ballooned 53% to ₹6,206 lakhs (from ₹4,045 lakhs FY25-end), signaling potential credit extension/delayed collections; impacted WC changes (-₹2,161 lakhs).
  • High Leverage & Finance Costs: Total borrowings ~₹9,807 lakhs (32% of assets); finance costs up 12% YoY to ₹626 lakhs (3.5% of revenue), pressuring margins amid high interest rates.
  • Cost Pressures: Employee expenses +20% YoY (₹3,241 lakhs); other expenses elevated. Volatile inventory changes (₹1,634 lakhs build-up in Q2).
  • Short-term Debt Uptick: Current borrowings +5% to ₹7,605 lakhs, reflecting working capital needs.
  • Tax Volatility: Prior period taxes/MAT in prior years; current H1 tax ₹215 lakhs.

Growth Prospects

  • High: Strong H1 momentum positions FY26 for 15-20% revenue growth if textile demand sustains (India’s textile exports +PLI schemes tailwinds; capacity utilization likely improving).
  • Capex Continuity: PPE stable at ₹16,037 lakhs; ongoing investments (CWIP ₹16 lakhs) for modernization/efficiency.
  • Associate Synergies: L.K. Distributors boosted consolidated PAT by ~25%; potential for deeper integration.
  • Cash Generation: Op. CF covers capex/dividends (₹78 lakhs paid); net debt reduction possible if receivables normalize.
  • EPS Trajectory: H1 EPS ₹6.38 (64% of FY25 full-year); on track for double-digit FY26 EPS if trends hold.
