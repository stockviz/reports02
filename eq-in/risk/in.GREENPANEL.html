Analysis of Greenpanel Industries Limited (GREENPANEL) - Q2/H1 FY26 Overview

Greenpanel Industries, India’s largest wood panel manufacturer (primarily MDF and plywood), reported a strong operational turnaround in Q2 FY26 despite reported losses driven by one-offs. Consolidated revenue grew 17% YoY to ₹389 cr in Q2 (H1: ₹697 cr, flat YoY), led by MDF domestic volumes (+31% YoY). Operating EBITDA improved to 10.2% (₹40 cr) in Q2 excl. FX/one-offs, but reported PAT was negative (Q2: -₹6 cr; H1: -₹41 cr) due to ₹40 cr unrealized FX loss on Euro ECB and ₹20 cr higher dep/int from new AP plant. Net debt fell to ₹173 cr amid WC optimization (CCC: 30 days). Guidance: High-teens domestic MDF volume growth; EBITDA margins high single/low double-digit for FY26.

Tailwinds (Positive Factors)

  • Volume Momentum: MDF domestic volumes surged 31% YoY/+27% QoQ to 113k CBM (Q2), driven by sales re-energization (Bali dealer event, MITR 2.0 app with 13k users, 13k trade engagements), new products (Thin MDF, FRMDF, HDWR), and digital/ground campaigns across 7k+ outlets.
  • Operational Efficiencies: Cost of production down 5.5% QoQ via raw material optimization (half from RM mix), power/fuel savings; gross margins stable at 48%.
  • Favorable Industry Dynamics: MDF imports negligible (<1k CBM/month vs. 20k prior; BIS norms tightening, QC on furniture incoming). Timber costs softening; no major organized capex; demand ~2.8 mn CBM vs. 4.2 mn capacity.
  • Balance Sheet Strength: Net debt reduced ₹60 cr QoQ (₹71 cr adj. for FX); zero WC line utilization; A+ ratings reaffirmed. New AP plant (40% util, fungible across lines) unlocks scale.
  • Strategic Initiatives: Value-added mix at 44%; OEM/export opportunistic for EPCG benefits (₹11 cr taken H1, ₹40 cr balance).

Headwinds (Negative Factors)

  • Realization Pressure: MDF blended down 4% YoY (₹28k/CBM; half from price realignment to peers/customers, half mix shift to thin/OEM from new plant).
  • Plywood Weakness: Volumes -5% YoY (despite +19% QoQ); margins low at 4.6%.
  • One-Off Impacts: Reported EBITDA dragged by ₹12 cr Q2 FX MTM loss (H1: ₹40 cr on ₹340 cr Euro ECB); ₹20 cr incremental dep/int; ₹4.8 cr voluntary GST deposit (ineligible ITC disallowance).
  • Input Costs: Chemicals elevated (methanol/formaldehyde; expected moderation Q4 FY26).
  • Subsidies Deferred: ₹81 cr AP plant subsidies (power/green/land) not recognized pending clarity (₹19 cr partial receipt post-quarter).

Growth Prospects

  • Volume/Leverage Upside: MDF capacity 891k CBM (util ~53% Q2); target high-teens domestic growth FY26 (from FY25 base ~438k CBM total). AP plant ramp to 60%+ exit FY26; fungible lines optimize thin/thick production.
  • Market Tailwinds: Domestic MDF demand growing (furniture/real estate green shoots); unorganized can’t match value-add; imports muted; BIS/QC enforcement favors organized players.
  • Margin Expansion: Operating EBITDA guidance 9-12%; high-teens possible with volumes, value-mix (>44%), cost levers (further power/fuel/procurement), softening timber/chemicals.
  • Revenue Potential: FY26 topline could exceed FY25 if exports/OEM fill gaps; new products/digital push to sustain 20%+ dealer/carpenter engagement.
  • Capex Discipline: Steady-state maintenance ₹20-30 cr; comfortable liquidity supports organic growth.

Key Risks

  • Forex Volatility: High Euro exposure (₹340 cr ECB) amid EUR-INR spikes (15-16% rise); further depreciation could add ₹10-15 cr/qtr MTM losses (speculative reversal underway).
  • Pricing/Competition: No near-term hikes expected; unorganized capex or supply glut could pressure realizations (already aligned with peers).
  • Input/Execution Risks: Chemical/timber volatility; new plant ramp-up delays (currently 40%); seasonal WC spikes (rainy/winter timber shortages).
  • Regulatory/Contingencies: GST probe (₹4.8 cr paid; potential escalation); deferred subsidies (₹81 cr); stricter BIS if imports adapt.
  • Demand/Macro: Weak plywood; real estate slowdown; geopolitical export hits (Middle East).

Summary: Greenpanel’s strategic pivot (sales activation + cost discipline) delivered robust MDF volumes and 10%+ core margins, positioning it for FY26 recovery amid favorable industry undersupply and import curbs. However, FX/distortions masked underlying strength (reported losses), with risks centered on currency/margins. Outlook positive: High-teens growth achievable at 10%+ EBITDA if macros hold; stock likely to re-rate on operational leverage. Monitor Q3 volumes/FX for confirmation.
