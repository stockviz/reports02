Analysis of Chambal Fertilisers and Chemicals Limited (CHAMBLFERT)

Overview: Chambal Fertilisers reported robust H1 FY26 results (ended Sep 30, 2025), driven by explosive growth in traded P&K fertilisers (volumes ~2x YoY) and CPC/SN segments (29-37% YoY growth). Standalone revenue up 31% to ₹12,110 Cr, PAT up 18% to ₹1,240 Cr. Urea faced headwinds from plant downtime, but overall margins held firm (EBITDA flat YoY). TAN project nears completion, poised for Q4 ramp-up. Balance sheet strong (cash ~₹700 Cr, equity up to ₹9,656 Cr standalone). Interim dividend of ₹5/share (50%) declared.

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • P&K Fertilisers Boom: Volumes doubled to ~11L MT (H1), revenue up sharply due to timely NBS policy extension, advantage-disadvantage mechanism (₹50-52k/ton subsidy on DAP), and strategic sourcing. Management expects Q3 momentum continuation.
  • CPC/SN & Agri-Inputs Momentum: 32% revenue/33% contribution growth (H1); biologicals +80% YoY. 22 new products launched (incl. seeds, bio-nano phosphorus). Portfolio now 76 CPC products; farmer outreach (1,820 meetings, 38k soil samples) and digital app boosting traction.
  • TAN Project Execution: 87% complete (₹1,052 Cr spent); trial runs Dec’25, sales mid-Feb’26. Capacity 240k MT/year; captive ammonia advantage yields “healthy 4-5 digit margins.” Quick breakeven expected; provisions for LDAN expansion.
  • Subsidy & Cash Flow Discipline: Timely receipts (₹6,347 Cr H1); ₹953 Cr de-escalation recovery (balance sheet only). Net cash from ops ₹347 Cr (standalone H1).
  • JV & Efficiency: IMACID margins improved; phosphoric acid expansion by Dec’26. Energy norms impact low double-digits/quarter.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Urea Volume Pressure: Q2 production/sales down 3-4% YoY (8.81L/9.34L MT) due to Gadepan-3 prilling stoppage (~17-18k MT loss) + unseasonal rains. H1 shortfall ~60-70k MT; G2 turnaround in Feb’26.
  • Working Capital Strain: Receivables up to ₹2,260 Cr from P&K surge; inventories/traded goods capex jumped 10x YoY (temporary, to normalize by Dec’25).
  • Margin Compression in P&K: Procurement costs rose (DAP $650→$850); limited pass-through to farmers. Gross margins volatile.
  • Operational Hiccups: Recent plant issues (syngas compressor in prior Q); reliance on 3 plants for makeup.

Growth Prospects

  • Short-Term (FY26-27): TAN revenue from Q4 FY26 (full utilization H2 FY27); P&K volumes to sustain 1.1M+ MT annually; CPC/SN to 28%+ growth trajectory. Urea recovery via G1/G3 ramp-up.
  • Medium-Term: Urea demand-supply gap widening (90L MT now →120L MT by 2030; 3-4% CAGR). Policy tailwind possible for expansion (Gadepan-4 feasible). Seeds/biologicals as high-margin adjacencies; “seed-to-harvest” ecosystem.
  • Capex Pipeline: ₹2-3k Cr annual cash gen supports allied agri-inputs (e.g., LDAN, energy efficiency). 6-7yr payback threshold; IRR attractive for TAN.
  • Outlook: Rabi prospects strong (8% excess monsoon rainfall); ammonium sulphate under subsidy adds portfolio.

Risks policy-tied but manageable (low debt, cash-rich). Bullish on agri-input diversification; monitor GST appeals and Rabi execution. Target multiples: 15-18x FY27 EPS (est. ₹55-60). Rating: Buy (long-term compounding story).
