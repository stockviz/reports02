Chambal Fertilisers and Chemicals Limited (CHAMBLFERT) Analysis

Overall Summary: Chambal reported robust H1 FY26 results with 31% YoY revenue growth to ₹12,110 Cr (standalone) and 18% PAT growth to ₹1,240 Cr, driven by P&K fertilisers (volumes ~2x YoY), CPC/SN (29% growth), and timely urea subsidies. EBITDA margins held steady at ~15% despite lower urea volumes from plant stoppages. Balance sheet strong (net cash ~₹700 Cr), with TAN project at 87% completion. Tailwinds from policy support and diversification outweigh headwinds like input costs and GST penalties (being contested). Growth prospects strong in value-added agri-inputs and TAN; key risks tied to policy/execution.

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Policy Support & Volumes: NBS policy enabled P&K volumes to ~11 LT (vs. 5.5 LT last year), with ~₹50k/ton subsidy. Urea subsidies timely (₹6,347 Cr received in H1).
  • Diversification Success: CPC/SN revenue +29% to ₹374 Cr (Q2), contribution +37%; biologicals +80% YoY. 22 new products launched in H1, including seeds and bio-nano phosphorus.
  • TAN Project Momentum: 240k tons capacity on track (Jan 2026 start); captive ammonia gives cost edge; healthy 4-5 digit margins expected at full utilization.
  • Favourable Macros: Good monsoon (+8% rainfall), strong Rabi prospects; IMACID JV margins improved.
  • Financial Health: PAT growth 20% (Q2), cash reserves ₹700 Cr; receivables to normalize post-Q3.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Urea Volume Pressure: Q2 sales 9.34 LT (-3% YoY) due to Gadepan-3 stoppage (material handling issue) and unseasonal rains; H1 shortfall ~60-70k tons.
  • Margin Compression in P&K: Procurement costs up (DAP +$200/ton sympathy rise); working capital cycle inflated receivables to ₹2,260 Cr and inventories.
  • GST Penalties: ₹526 Cr (Patna, subsidy on fertilisers) + ₹15.6 Cr (Udaipur, ITC disallowance); frivolous per mgmt., but appeals ongoing; no immediate impact claimed.
  • Capex Drag: TAN spend ₹1,052 Cr; routine energy efficiency/maintenance capex.

Growth Prospects

  • Near-Term (FY26-27): TAN revenue from Q4 FY26, full ramp-up H2 FY27 (20k tons/month); P&K momentum into Q3 (Rabi covered); CPC/SN to sustain 25-30% growth via new geos (Maharashtra) and digital outreach (app with 80-90k users, target 3-5L).
  • Medium-Term: Urea demand-supply gap (90 LT shortfall now, 120 LT by 2030); potential Gadepan-4 if policy announced. Portfolio expansion (LDAN, seeds, bio-stimulants); payback-focused capex (6-7 yrs IRR).
  • Long-Term: “Seed-to-harvest” ecosystem; ammonium sulphate opportunity; IMACID phosphoric acid expansion (Dec 2026).

Key Risks

Operational Plant turnarounds (G2 Feb 2026); TAN commissioning delays/quality issues. Proven track record; competent team.

Investment Outlook: Positive; trading at reasonable multiples with diversification reducing urea reliance (from ~80% to ~50% revenue). Monitor TAN execution and policy for urea/TAN upside. Target: Accumulate on dips for 15-20% FY27 EPS CAGR.
