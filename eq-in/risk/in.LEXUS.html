Analysis of Lexus Granito (India) Limited (LEXUS)

Company Overview: Lexus Granito (India) Ltd. (CIN: L26914GJ2008PLC053838) is a Gujarat-based manufacturer of vitrified and wall tiles, located in Morbi (a major tile hub). It is listed on NSE (Symbol: LEXUS, ISIN: INE500X01013). Recent filings cover Q2/H1 FY26 financials (ended 30.09.2025), board changes, warrant conversions, and 17th AGM outcomes (30.09.2025). FY25 was loss-making (PAT loss ₹583 lakhs on ₹7,525 lakhs revenue), but H1 FY26 shows turnaround.

Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts)

  • Revenue Momentum: Strong sequential growth in Q2 FY26 (₹2,210 lakhs vs. Q1 ₹1,804 lakhs, +23%) and YoY H1 improvement (₹4,014 lakhs vs. ₹3,515 lakhs prior H1, +14%). Indicates demand recovery in domestic tile market.
  • Profitability Turnaround: H1 FY26 PAT ₹99 lakhs (vs. ₹564 lakhs loss prior H1); Q2 PBT ₹29 lakhs (vs. Q2 FY25 loss ₹199 lakhs). Total comprehensive income positive at ₹103 lakhs. EPS improved to ₹0.49 (basic).
  • Balance Sheet Strengthening: Other Equity improved from -₹1,583 lakhs (FY25) to -₹406 lakhs (H1 FY26). Inventories stable (~₹6,492 lakhs); trade receivables managed. Positive operating cash flow ₹215 lakhs (vs. outflow ₹550 lakhs prior H1).
  • Debt Reduction: Long-term borrowings down 24% (₹3,120 lakhs vs. ₹4,099 lakhs FY25); repaid ₹979 lakhs in H1. Total liabilities reduced.
  • Capital Infusion: Allotted 45L+ warrants earlier (raised ₹1,074 lakhs upfront); converted 3L warrants (18.11.2025, raised ₹102 lakhs more). Paid-up capital up to ₹2,049 lakhs (20.49 Cr shares). Remaining 92L warrants provide potential ₹4,000+ Cr influx.
  • Governance: AGM resolutions (financials adoption, director re-appointment, secretarial auditor, designation change for Mrs. Dimple Detroja to Non-Exec Director) passed unanimously (100% favor, 58.24L votes, ~29% participation). Limited review by auditors (Keyur Shah & Associates) clean.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Persistent Losses Legacy: Negative Other Equity (-₹406 lakhs) reflects accumulated FY25 losses. Thin margins: H1 PBT margin ~2.2%; high costs (depreciation ₹333 lakhs, finance ₹174 lakhs, other expenses ₹5,286 lakhs dominate).
  • High Debt & Interest Burden: Total borrowings ~₹5,371 lakhs (45% of assets); finance costs ₹174 lakhs in H1 (4.3% of revenue). Repayment pressure evident (net financing outflow ₹226 lakhs).
  • Cost Pressures: Other expenses spiked (Q2 ₹1,512 lakhs); employee costs steady but inventory changes volatile (H1 +₹752 lakhs WIP/finished goods impact).
  • Low Liquidity: Cash equivalents low (₹11 lakhs); reliant on operations/working capital borrowings (net reduction ₹156 lakhs).
  • Low Shareholder Engagement: AGM voting ~29% of shares; promoter holding ~55%, public ~45%, but low public participation signals limited retail interest.

Growth Prospects

  • Industry Tailwinds: Indian ceramics/tile sector benefits from real estate boom (housing, infra via PMAY, RERA), urbanization, and exports (company notes export debtors). Morbi cluster advantages (low costs, scale).
  • Operational Recovery: Revenue trajectory suggests capacity utilization improving (post-FY25 slump). Potential from warrant funds for capex/debt paydown (minimal FY26 capex ₹15 lakhs so far).
  • Expansion Potential: Primarily domestic (India segment dominant); scope for exports (noted overseas assets minor). HR focus (Mrs. Detroja’s role) and regulatory approvals could aid scaling.
  • Capital Raising: Full warrant conversion (within 18 months from Apr 2025) could infuse ~₹4,000 lakhs, deleveraging balance sheet and funding growth (3-5x revenue potential in 2-3 years if sustained).
  • Outlook: H1 FY26 inflection point; FY26 revenue could hit ₹8,000-9,000 lakhs (20-25% YoY growth) if Q3/Q4 mirrors H1, with PAT breakeven/positive.

Key Risks

  • Debt/Liquidity Risk (High): High leverage (debt/equity ~3.3x); default risk if cash flows weaken (no current defaults noted). Interest coverage thin (~0.5x in H1).
  • Cyclical/Industry Risk (Medium-High): Tiles tied to construction (sensitive to interest rates, monsoons, realty slowdown). Competition intense in Morbi (price wars, raw material volatility e.g., gas/fuel).
  • Governance/Promoter Risk (Medium): Family-dominated board (MD Anilkumar Detroja, wife/sister-in-law roles); potential conflicts despite clean disclosures. No RPTs detailed in filings.
  • Execution/Operational Risk (Medium): Inventory pile-up (₹6,492 lakhs, 80% of current assets); receivables ~₹777/735 lakhs non-current/current. MSME dues identified but unpaid.
  • Market/Regulatory Risk (Low-Medium): Small-cap volatility; NSE-only listing. Compliance good (SEBI filings timely), but warrant dilution (if fully converted, promoter stake may dilute further).
  • Macro Risks: Inflation, rupee depreciation (export exposure), or slowdown in capex cycle.

Summary: Lexus Granito shows early signs of recovery (revenue/profit turnaround, debt reduction, cash positivity) post-FY25 losses, driven by operational efficiencies and warrant funding—a strong tailwind for deleveraging/growth. Prospects bright in growing tile sector (target FY26 PAT ₹200-300 lakhs), but headwinds from debt burden and thin margins persist. Investment Stance: Speculative buy for risk-tolerant investors eyeing turnaround; monitor Q3 results/debt metrics. Key watch: Warrant conversions, interest coverage >2x, revenue >₹2,500 lakhs/Q.
