Summary Analysis for PPAP Automotive Limited (as of Q2/H1 FY26, ended Sep 30, 2025)

PPAP Automotive, a manufacturer of automotive sealing systems, interior/exterior plastics, and diversified into aftermarket, tools, industrial products, and EV batteries, reported subdued H1 FY26 results amid auto sector weakness. Revenue fell 5.2% YoY to ₹253.6 Cr, EBITDA dropped 21.9% to ₹22 Cr (8.7% margin), and PAT swung to a ₹2.3 Cr loss (driven by battery losses and low utilization). Revised FY26 guidance: Revenue ₹575-600 Cr (+4-8% YoY), EBITDA ₹60-65 Cr (10-11% margin), PAT ₹10-12 Cr. Strong order book (₹4,171 Cr lifetime) provides visibility. Below is a structured analysis:

Headwinds (Challenges Pressuring Performance)

  • Subdued Auto Demand & OEM Delays: PV sales down 1.5% YoY in Q2 FY26; key models (Honda, MG, Tata Curvv) saw volume cuts. SOP shifts delayed ramp-ups, leading to 65% parts utilization (vs. expected higher).
  • Battery Division Drag (Avinya): Operated at 5% utilization; ₹2.1 Cr H1 loss due to delayed marquee customer approvals (now Q3). Needs ₹15 Cr quarterly sales for breakeven.
  • Fixed Cost Under-Absorption: Lower volumes hurt EBITDA margins (down from 10.5% YoY); H1 inventory build-up signals weak offtake.
  • Revised Guidance: Down from earlier ₹600-660 Cr revenue/₹75-80 Cr EBITDA due to H1 softness and battery pivot delays.
  • Inventory & Debt Pressure: Inventories up ~25% QoQ to ₹76.7 Cr; working capital strained (net WC changes +₹1.8 Cr), borrowings rose to ₹178 Cr.

Tailwinds (Positive Supports)

  • Robust Order Book: H1 wins ₹707 Cr (incl. ₹460 Cr Tata order for plastics/rubber over 3-5 yrs); total lifetime ₹4,171 Cr (3-5 yr lifecycle). EV orders ₹27 Cr.
  • H2 Ramp-Ups: New launches (Tata Sierra Nov’25, Maruti e-Vitara Q3, Renault Duster Q4) to boost utilization to 75-80%. Commenced supplies for Tata Altroz, Maruti Victoris, Vinfast VF6.
  • Diversification Gains: | Segment | H1 Revenue | Growth/Margin | Outlook | |———|————|—————|———| | Aftermarket (Elpis) | ₹7.5 Cr (5%) | +37% YoY; 7-8% EBITDA | 10% of FY26 revenue; 1,269 SKUs, 133 distributors. | Tools (Meraki) | ₹5 Cr (3%) | 85% util.; 16-17% EBITDA | +20% FY26; 138-mold book (₹30 Cr); independent co. from Q4. | Industrial Products | ₹2 Cr | Multifold growth | Export traction.
  • Margin Resilience: Gross margins improved to 45.4% (vs. 43.5% YoY) via in-house tools, better mix, stable RM (PVC localized vs. anti-dumping).
  • Shareholder Returns: Interim dividend ₹1/share (record date Nov 19); AGM resolutions passed overwhelmingly.

Growth Prospects (Medium-Term Opportunities, 3-5 Yrs)

  • Auto Recovery: H2 festive/rural demand, GST 2.0 easing; industry growth via exports (PV +23% YoY). PPAP’s content-per-vehicle rise in Maruti/Tata/Renault.
  • Diversified Revenue: Non-auto (aftermarket/tools/industrial/battery) to drive >market growth; target 12-14% EBITDA margins.
  • EV/Exports Push: 300+ SKUs under dev.; US/GCC exports starting; battery pivot to storage (trials done, Q3 sales expected).
  • Capex Efficiency: Sweat existing assets; tool capacity to 150 molds. FY26 top-line implies H2 ₹322-347 Cr (vs. H1 ₹254 Cr).
  • Order Execution: ₹621 Cr Q2 wins; lifetime book supports steady ramps.

Overall Outlook: Short-term cautious (H1 loss, revised guidance) but H2 inflection via orders/ramp-ups positions FY26 turnaround. Long-term positive on diversification (non-auto ~10-15% mix), EV exposure, and industry tailwinds. Stock trades at ~0.8x FY26E sales; monitor battery progress and auto volumes. Valuation attractive if guidance met.
