Summary Analysis of Ujjivan Small Finance Bank (UJJIVANSFB)

Ujjivan SFB reported resilient Q2 FY26 (Sep 2025) performance amid microfinance sector stress, with sequential improvements in PAT (+18% QoQ to ₹122 Cr), NII (+8% QoQ to ₹922 Cr), and asset quality (GNPA stable at 2.5%). Loan book grew 14% YoY to ₹34,588 Cr, driven by secured diversification (47% share, +55% YoY), while deposits rose 15% YoY to ₹39,211 Cr (CASA 28%). However, YoY profitability dipped due to elevated credit costs (2.8%) and NIM compression (7.9%). FY26 guidance signals 20% advances growth with controlled credit costs (2.3-2.4%). Long-term FY30 vision targets ₹1L Cr loans (65-70% secured), RoA 1.8-2%. Overall Outlook: Positive on diversification and deposit strength; cautious on microfinance risks but improving trajectory supports 10-12% RoE in FY26.

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Deposit Franchise Momentum: Deposits +15% YoY; CASA +22% YoY (28% ratio); Retail TD+CASA at 71%. CoF improved 30bps QoQ to 7.3%, aiding NIM stability.
  • Secured Lending Shift: Secured book ₹16,173 Cr (+53% YoY, 47% share vs 35% YoY). Strong growth in Housing (+51%), MSME (+69%), Vehicle/Gold/Agri (hyper-growth). Higher yields (e.g., MSME 10.5%) and better quality (low NPAs in new books).
  • Disbursement Surge: Record ₹7,932 Cr (+48% YoY, +22% QoQ); H1 FY26 +36% YoY. Micro banking collections at 99.5% (Bucket-X).
  • Digital & Infra Scale: 99L customers, 766 branches (26 states), robust IT (Finacle CBS, analytics). Digital channels (UPI, apps) drive low-cost growth.
  • Capital Buffer: CAR 21.4% (Tier-1 19.9%); floating provisions ₹181 Cr.
  • ESG & Ratings: Strong ESG score (80/100, “Outstanding”); AA- stable rating.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Profitability Pressure YoY: PAT -48% YoY (₹122 Cr); NII -2% YoY; NIM down 130bps to 7.9% due to CoF rise and yield moderation (micro shift to secured).
  • Microfinance Stress: GL -12% YoY (38% book share); higher PAR (5.4% GL) in states like Karnataka/TN. Credit cost elevated at 0.7% QoQ (2.8% annualized).
  • High Operating Costs: Cost/Income 66%; Opex/Avg Assets 6.3%. Personnel costs +19% YoY.
  • Liquidity Absorption: CD ratio 88% (up from 86%); excess liquidity impacted margins.
  • Slippage & Provisions: ₹278 Cr slippage (down QoQ); write-offs ₹211 Cr; PCR 73%.

Growth Prospects

  • Short-Term (FY26 Guidance): Advances ~20% (to ~₹41,500 Cr); Secured ~35%; CD ~88%; CASA ~27%; RoA 1.2-1.4%, RoE 10-12%. Focus: Secured ramp-up, CASA via ASBA/MF/Forex.
  • Medium-Term (FY27-30): 20-25% CAGR to ₹1L Cr advances (65-70% secured); NIM 6-7%; Cost/Income <55%; RoA 1.8-2%, RoE 16-18%. Enablers: 400+ branches, digital (DBU application), analytics for cross-sell.
  • Strategic: Universal Bank license applied (Feb 2025); product suite (Housing/MSME new launches); pan-India (South/East strong).
  • Projections: H2 FY26 could see PAT recovery if credit costs ease to 2.3%; 15-20% EPS CAGR feasible on diversification.
