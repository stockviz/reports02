Analysis of Ujjivan Small Finance Bank (UJJIVANSFB): Headwinds, Tailwinds, Growth Prospects, and Key Risks

Ujjivan Small Finance Bank (USFB), focused on underserved retail/microfinance segments, reported Q2 FY26 (Sep 2025) results showing resilient QoQ growth amid YoY profitability pressures. Gross loan book reached ₹34,588 Cr (+14% YoY), deposits ₹39,211 Cr (+15.1% YoY), with secured lending at 47% share (up from 35% YoY). PAT was ₹122 Cr (+18.2% QoQ but -47.6% YoY), RoA 1.0%, RoE 7.7%. Below is a structured summary based on the provided filings (business highlights, investor presentation, financial results, press release).

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Robust Deposit Franchise & Liquidity: Deposits grew 15.1% YoY to ₹39,211 Cr; CASA +22.1% YoY to ₹10,783 Cr (27.5% ratio, up from 25.9%). Retail deposits (CASA + Retail TD) at 71% of total. Cost of funds improved to 7.3% (-30 bps QoQ), supporting NIM stability at 7.9%.
  • Disbursement Momentum & Portfolio Diversification: Record Q2 disbursements ₹7,932 Cr (+47.6% YoY, +21.3% QoQ); H1 at ₹14,471 Cr (+35.8% YoY). Secured book surged 53% YoY to ₹16,173 Cr (47% share vs. 35% YoY), driven by Housing (+51% YoY), MSME (+69%), Gold (+565%), Vehicle (+150%). Microbanking stable at 54% of book.
  • Improving Asset Quality Trends: Bucket-X collections at 99.5% (Microbanking); SMA at 1.99% (lowest since Q1FY25). PCR at 73%; write-offs ₹211 Cr in Q2. GNPA stable at 2.46% (slight dip QoQ).
  • Operational Scale: 766 branches (26 states/334 districts), 98.8L customers (+8% YoY), digital push (4.2Mn digital base, +23% YoY). Strong CRAR 21.4% (Tier-1 19.9%).
  • ESG & Awards: Top RBI Data Quality score (93.9); ESG rating “Outstanding” (80/100 by ICRA).

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Profitability Compression YoY: PAT -47.6% YoY to ₹122 Cr; NII -2.3% YoY to ₹922 Cr (despite +7.7% QoQ). PPoP -14.3% YoY to ₹395 Cr. Credit cost elevated at ~2.8% of avg. GLB (slippage ₹278 Cr).
  • Asset Quality Stress: PAR 4.45% (down QoQ but high); Microbanking PAR 5.4% (GL). Group loans contracted 12% YoY; regional stress (e.g., Karnataka/TN PAR >7%).
  • Margin/NIM Pressure: Yield moderation (secured mix shift); NIM 7.9% (-130 bps YoY). Opex/Avg. Assets 6.3%; Cost/Income 66% (high vs. peers).
  • Macro/Competition: Slow microfinance growth; high overlap lending in GL (51% unique borrowers). Elevated credit costs from legacy stress.
  • H1 YoY Declines: Overall PAT -57.9% YoY to ₹225 Cr; RoA/RoE at 0.9%/7.2%.

Growth Prospects

  • Near-Term (FY26 Guidance): Advances ~20% CAGR; Secured ~35%; Deposits in-line (CD ratio ~88%); CASA ~27%; NIM stable; Credit cost 2.3-2.4% of GLB; Cost/Income ~67%; RoA 1.2-1.4%; RoE 10-12%. Focus: Secured ramp-up (Housing/MSME/Gold/Vehicle/Agri), branch +400, digital (WhatsApp/DBU).
  • Long-Term (FY30 Vision): Advances ₹1L Cr (20-25% CAGR); Secured 65-70%; CASA ~35%; NIM 6-7%; Credit cost 1-1.5%; Cost/Income ~55%; Opex/Avg. Assets <5%; RoA 1.8-2%; RoE 16-18%. Universal Bank application filed (Feb 2025).
  • Catalysts: Secured diversification (low-NPA new products: Gold NPA 0%, Vehicle 2%); Analytics-driven underwriting; Retail liability push (MF/Forex/ASBA); 766+ branches scaling.

Overall Summary: USFB shows strong execution on diversification (secured tailwind) and liquidity, with QoQ momentum (disbursements +21%, PAT +18%). Headwinds from YoY profitability dip and micro-stress persist, but guidance signals recovery (20% advances growth). Prospects bright long-term via scale/product mix, but risks tied to asset quality/execution in competitive SFB space. Stock trades at ~1x FY26E BVPS; monitor Q3 collections/NIM. Positive for patient investors betting on secured pivot.
