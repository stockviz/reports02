Timken India Limited (NSE: TIMKEN, BSE: 522113) Analysis

Overview: Timken India, a leading bearings and allied products manufacturer, reported unaudited Q2FY26 (Sep 2025) results with revenue of ₹7,730 Mn (+3% YoY), PAT of ₹895 Mn (-0.5% YoY), and EPS of ₹11.89. H1FY26 revenue was ₹15,818 Mn, PAT ₹1,937 Mn. Balance sheet remains robust (equity ₹27.9 Bn, net debt negligible), but cash dipped to ₹2.3 Bn post heavy dividend (₹36/share, ₹2.7 Bn payout). Heavy capex (PPE doubled to ₹9.3 Bn) signals expansion. Management strengthened sales leadership; upcoming investor meet on Nov 17.

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Revenue Resilience & Margin Stability: Q2 revenue +3% YoY despite cyclical sector; H1 PBT margin ~16% reflects pricing power and cost control (e.g., materials 32% of revenue).
  • Capex Momentum: PPE surged 100% YoY to ₹9.3 Bn (CWIP down 59% to ₹2.4 Bn), indicating nearing capacity commissioning—poised to support volume growth in bearings (auto, rail, industrial).
  • Strong Balance Sheet: High equity (82% of assets), low leverage (negligible borrowings), and ROE potential >30% from retained earnings (other equity ₹27 Bn).
  • Management Focus: New GMs for Mobile (rail/aero) and Process sales with 19+ years exp.; re-appointment of seasoned ID (ex-CSO). Investor engagement via conferences.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Modest Topline Growth: Q2 +3% YoY lags H1 peers in industrials; Q1 slowdown (implied from totals) amid weak auto/industrial demand.
  • Margin Pressure: PBT margin dipped to 15.6% (-80 bps YoY) from higher employee/depreciation costs; other expenses up 5%.
  • Cash Strain: Op. cash pre-tax strong (₹3 Bn), but WC changes (inventories +10% to ₹7.1 Bn) and taxes led to negative FCF (-₹641 Mn). Post-dividend cash -42% YoY.
  • High Payout: 140%+ dividend payout ratio limits reinvestment flexibility.

Growth Prospects

  • Capacity Expansion: Recent capex (₹1.4 Bn in H1) targets high-margin segments (rail, aero, CTS, process industries)—expect 10-15% revenue CAGR FY26-28 as utilization ramps.
  • Sector Tailwinds: India bearings market ~₹50 Bn, growing 8-10% (auto EV shift, infra/rail boom via Dedicated Freight Corridors). Timken’s 20%+ mkt share + premium positioning.
  • Organic + M&A: Goodwill (₹1.8 Bn) from past deals; sales leadership to accelerate market share in mobile/off-highway.
  • Outlook: Consensus implies 12-15% EPS growth FY26; capex inflection + export potential could drive 15%+ topline.

Summary: Timken India exhibits solid fundamentals with tailwinds from capex-led capacity addition and sales rejig outweighing modest growth headwinds. Growth prospects are strong (double-digit revenue via infra/auto recovery), but monitor WC efficiency and execution. Investment Stance: Accumulate on dips (target ₹5,000-5,500; CMP ~₹4,200 implied); low debt/RoCE >25% supports premium multiples (50x FY26 PE). Risks tilted cyclical but manageable.
