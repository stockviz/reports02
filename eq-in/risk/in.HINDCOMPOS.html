Analysis of Hindustan Composites Ltd. (HINDCOMPOS)

Hindustan Composites Ltd. is a diversified player in composite products (core manufacturing, e.g., friction materials), investments (significant non-current portfolio ~₹97-110 Cr), and commodity trading. FY26 H1 (ended Sep 2025) shows revenue growth but moderated profitability. Strong balance sheet (total assets ~₹1,269 Cr, equity ~₹1,139 Cr, negligible debt). Dividend of ₹2/share (40% payout) declared for FY25. All AGM resolutions passed with ~99.99% approval.

Tailwinds (Positive Factors)

  • Revenue Momentum: Standalone H1 FY26 revenue from operations up 7.7% YoY to ₹176.7 Cr (Composites: ₹146.9 Cr, up ~6%; Investments & Trading steady). Q2 revenue flat QoQ at ₹86.7 Cr.
  • Investment Income Buffer: Other income (interest/dividends) robust at ~₹17 Cr in H1, supporting margins amid opco pressures.
  • Capital Strength & Shareholder Returns: Book value strong (~₹772/share); consistent dividends (40% payout); low leverage (borrowings <₹2 Cr).
  • Operational Efficiency: Positive operating cash flow (₹26.9 Cr H1); Capex controlled (~₹6.4 Cr).
  • Governance: Smooth AGM (75% voting turnout); clarifications to exchanges on JV accounting (no further losses from Compo Advics JV post FY22).

Headwinds (Negative Factors)

  • Profitability Dip: H1 FY26 PBT down 15.7% YoY to ₹18.3 Cr (from ₹21.6 Cr); PAT similarly pressured. Margins squeezed by higher employee costs (+15% YoY), materials (~43% of revenue), and depreciation.
  • Exceptional Costs: Settlement of ex-workmen disputes (~₹8 lakh impact); minor finance costs up.
  • Segment Pressures: Composites PBT flat; Trading volatile; Investments FVTPL gains modest (₹0.4 Cr).
  • Cash Conversion: Net cash burn in H1 (-₹9.5 Cr) due to heavy investment churn (net outflow ₹27.6 Cr); dividend payout ₹29.5 Cr.

Growth Prospects

  • Core Composites (75-80% Revenue): Steady demand in auto/friction materials; H1 segment revenue +6% YoY. Potential from industrial expansion (PPE ~₹132 Cr stable).
  • Investments (~85% Assets): Portfolio growth to ₹978-1,109 Cr; yields ~10-12% via dividends/interest. Scope for strategic reallocations.
  • Trading Diversification: Commodity trading up to ₹11 Cr H1; low base but scalable.
  • Outlook: FY26 revenue growth 8-12% feasible (guided by H1 trend); EPS ~₹8-10 (H1 ~₹6.6). Dividend continuity likely. Long-term: EV/auto sector tailwinds for composites.
  • Projections: Full FY26 revenue ~₹330-350 Cr; PBT ~₹45-50 Cr (assuming investment stability).

Overall Summary: Moderate Buy/Accumulate stance. Tailwinds from revenue growth and investments outweigh headwinds (margin dip). Growth anchored in composites + treasury yields; FY26 EPS growth ~10-15% likely. Key monitor: Q3 investment performance and labor stability. Risks tilted to markets but balance sheet resilient (P/B ~1.5x). Target: ₹600-650 (20-25% upside from recent levels ~₹500).
