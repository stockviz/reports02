Analysis of Shiva Mills Limited (SHIVAMILLS) – H1 FY2026 (Ended 30.09.2025)

Shiva Mills Limited, a textile manufacturing company (primarily cotton yarn spinning, based on CIN L17111TZ), reported unaudited H1 FY2026 results showing revenue contraction but narrowing losses, driven by aggressive working capital management and debt reduction. Key metrics: Revenue ₹6,800 lakhs (↓26% YoY), Net Loss ₹79 lakhs (↓50% YoY), Operating Cash Flow ₹568 lakhs (↑1,327% YoY). Balance sheet strengthened with receivables down 50% and borrowings reduced 36%. Below is a structured analysis of headwinds, tailwinds, growth prospects, and key risks.

Headwinds (Challenges Pressuring Performance)

  • Sharp Revenue Decline: H1 revenue fell 26% YoY (₹6,800L vs. ₹9,229L) and 11% QoQ in Q2, with Q2 at ₹3,044L (↓37% YoY). Indicates weak demand, pricing pressure, or volume drop in textiles amid global slowdowns (e.g., cotton price volatility, export weakness).
  • Persistent Operating Losses: Pre-tax loss of ₹94L in H1 (vs. ₹187L YoY), with negative EBITDA implied (~₹248L loss before adjustments). High cost of materials (74% of revenue) and other expenses remain sticky.
  • Inventory Build-Up: Inventories up 16% to ₹2,943L (from ₹2,548L at FY25 end), risking obsolescence or tying up cash if demand doesn’t recover.
  • Macro Textile Sector Pressures: Single-segment exposure to textiles vulnerable to raw material inflation (cotton), competition from unorganized players, and muted domestic/export demand.

Tailwinds (Positive Momentum)

  • Narrowing Losses: Net loss halved YoY to ₹79L; Q2 loss ₹16L (vs. ₹72L YoY). EPS improved to -₹0.91 (H1) from -₹1.82. Deferred tax credits (₹15L) and lower finance costs (↓54% to ₹29L) aided this.
  • Strong Working Capital Discipline: Trade receivables slashed 50% to ₹1,348L (cash inflow ₹1,344L), driving robust operating cash flow of ₹568L (vs. ₹40L YoY). Trade payables also down, signaling better supplier terms.
  • Debt Reduction: Short-term borrowings cut 36% to ₹636L (repayment ₹353L), lowering leverage (current ratio improves). Finance costs already trending down.
  • Asset Quality: PPE steady at ₹5,807L; positive OCI from employee benefits. Auditor’s clean limited review adds credibility.

Growth Prospects

  • Short-Term Stabilization: Cash generation supports capex (₹230L outflow in H1) for capacity modernization. Debt paydown could unlock cheaper funding, boosting margins if revenue stabilizes.
  • Medium-Term Recovery: Textile sector tailwinds possible from PLI scheme benefits, rising domestic demand (e.g., apparel/garment exports rebound post-China+1 shift), and cotton cycle upturn. Inventory build may position for H2 demand pickup.
  • Balance Sheet Resilience: Equity base solid (₹9,164L, 85% of assets); low net debt post-repayment enables expansion. Potential for dividend resumption if FY26 turns profitable (FY25 annual loss ₹381L).
  • Upside Catalysts: Website (shivamills.com) suggests focus on value-added yarns; any export recovery or cost efficiencies could drive 10-15% revenue growth in FY26.

Key Risks

  • Revenue/Demand Risk (High): Continued decline could erode equity (reserves down 1% to ₹8,300L). Textile cyclicality amplifies this.
  • Liquidity/Working Capital Risk (Medium): Cash & equivalents low at ₹19L; further inventory/receivables spikes could strain despite positive ops cash.
  • Operational Risks (Medium): High depreciation (₹342L, 5% of revenue) signals aging assets; capex continuity needed. Employee costs up 17% QoQ.
  • External Risks (Medium): Commodity volatility (materials 74% of costs), forex (if export-focused), regulatory changes in textiles, and competition. No exceptional items but “Other Adjustment” ₹387L in prior cash flow raises minor red flags.
  • Profitability Risk: Zero current tax but deferred tax liability ₹145L; sustained losses could trigger impairment tests.

Overall Summary

Shiva Mills shows early signs of turnaround amid textile headwinds, with revenue weakness offset by superior cash flow and deleveraging. H1 performance reflects defensive cost management rather than growth, positioning the stock for re-rating if demand recovers (target multiples: 0.8-1x sales for loss-making textiles). Bull Case: H2 revenue rebound → breakeven FY26 (stock upside 20-30%). Bear Case: Prolonged slowdown → further equity erosion (downside 15-20%). Recommendation: Hold/Accumulate on dips for patient investors; monitor Q3 revenue for demand cues. Market cap implied ~₹800-900Cr (based on 8.64Cr shares @₹10 FV); trading at deep value post-losses.
