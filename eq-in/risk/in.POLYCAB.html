Polycab India Limited (POLYCAB) Analysis

Based on Q2 & H1 FY26 Financial Results (ended Sep 30, 2025), Board Outcomes, Press Release, and Disclosures

Polycab delivered record H1 performance with consolidated revenue up 21% YoY to ₹1,24 Bn, EBITDA up 55% to ₹18.8 Bn (15.2% margin), and PAT up 53% to ₹12.9 Bn (10.4% margin). Q2 saw revenue +18% YoY to ₹64.8 Bn, EBITDA +62%, and PAT +56%. Wires & Cables (W&C) drove ~85-90% of growth (26% YoY in H1), with FMEG at 16% and EPC declining 19% YoY. Balance sheet remains strong (net cash ₹29.4 Bn), supporting capex (₹7.5 Bn in H1).

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Robust W&C Demand: 21-26% YoY growth from govt infra (e.g., housing/electrification), private capex recovery, and broad-based channels (distribution + institutional). International ops up 25% YoY (6% of revenue), aided by exports to 84 countries.
  • Margin Expansion: W&C EBIT margins +270 bps YoY to 15.1% in Q2 via operating leverage, favorable mix, and pricing power amid commodity inflation. Overall EBITDA margins +130 bps QoQ.
  • FMEG Momentum: 14-16% growth despite headwinds; solar category scaling rapidly (poised to be largest FMEG sub-segment) due to govt rooftop schemes.
  • Financial Strength: Net cash position up to ₹29.4 Bn (+21% YoY); strong operating cash flow (₹17.9 Bn in H1). Low debt (borrowings <1% of assets).
  • Strategic Initiatives: “Project Spring” for growth; ongoing capex (CWIP ₹8.6 Bn); amalgamation with subsidiary Uniglobus for integration.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • EPC Weakness: Revenue -19% YoY (₹7.5 Bn in H1) due to project delays/selectivity; though EBIT +11% YoY from margins.
  • FMEG Headwinds: Prolonged monsoon, elevated channel inventory hit fans; higher ad/promotional spends pressured short-term profitability.
  • Commodity Inflation: Rising input costs (e.g., copper implied in material costs up sharply); changes in inventories reflect inventory build-up.
  • Leadership Transition: CFO resignation (Oct 27, 2025); interim appointment (Niyant Maru, 9-month term) ensures continuity but signals short-term flux.
  • Segment Concentration: W&C ~87% of revenue; any slowdown could amplify impact.

Growth Prospects

  • High Visibility in Core: Infra boom (govt spending) and real estate recovery to sustain W&C at 20%+ CAGR; international to contribute 7-10% of sales.
  • FMEG Scale-Up: 15-20% growth potential; solar/policy tailwinds could drive mix shift. Aim: Profitable expansion via distribution (4,300 dealers, 200K outlets).
  • EPC Recovery: Selective bidding for high-margin projects; EBIT growth outpacing revenue.
  • Capex-Led Expansion: 27 plants; H1 capex signals capacity addition for wires/cables/FMEG. Amalgamation to streamline ops from Apr 1, 2025 (post-approvals).
  • Outlook: FY26 revenue >₹260 Bn implied; PAT margins 11-12%. Earnings call (Oct 17) and investor meets (Dec) to elaborate.

Key Risks

  • Commodity Volatility: Copper/prices sensitive (48% of costs); unhedged exposure could squeeze margins if deflation hits.
  • Execution/Competition: FMEG scaling amid rivals (Havells, etc.); EPC delays from tenders/contractor issues.
  • Macro/Regulatory: Infra slowdown, monsoon variability, or delayed amalgamation approvals (NCLT/ROC).
  • Inventory/Working Capital: Inventories +48% YoY (₹54 Bn); trade receivables up, straining WC if demand softens.
  • Governance: Interim CFO; over-reliance on promoters (Jaisinghani family).
  • Valuation: Stock trades at ~50x FY26 EPS (post-results); growth slowdown could trigger de-rating.

Summary: Polycab’s momentum is strong (tailwinds > headwinds), with W&C as growth engine and improving diversification. Prospects bright for 20%+ revenue CAGR FY26-27, but monitor EPC/FMEG execution and commodities. Buy/Hold for growth investors; risks tilted to cyclical/margin pressures.
