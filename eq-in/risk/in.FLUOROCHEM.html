Analysis of Gujarat Fluorochemicals Limited (FLUOROCHEM) - Q2 FY26 Update

Gujarat Fluorochemicals Limited (GFL), part of the INOXGFL Group, is a leading Indian producer of fluorochemicals, fluoropolymers, bulk chemicals, and emerging battery materials (via GFCL EV subsidiary). Q2 FY26 (ended Sep 2025) showed resilient performance amid global headwinds, with chemical segment EBITDA margins expanding to 32% (+608 bps YoY). Consolidated revenue was Rs 1,210 Cr (+2% YoY), EBITDA Rs 364 Cr (+23% YoY, 30% margin), and PAT Rs 179 Cr (+49% YoY). H1 FY26 revenue grew 5% YoY to Rs 2,491 Cr. Battery business remains pre-revenue but progressing toward Q4 FY26 sales.

Headwinds (Challenges Pressuring Near-Term Performance)

  • US Tariffs and Trade Disruptions: Higher tariffs on fluoropolymers (new grades) and R125 refrigerant impacted Q2 sales (-4% QoQ in fluoropolymers). Customers delayed decisions expecting tariff reversals; US was a high-margin market pre-tariffs.
  • Refrigerant Weakness: R-22 sales down due to quota cuts and seasonality (-15% YoY fluorochemicals revenue). R125 hit by tariffs/market shifts.
  • Battery Pre-Commercial Stage: EV segment EBITDA loss of Rs 17 Cr (Q2), with expenses on capex/depreciation. Revenue minimal (Rs 3 Cr Q2); reliant on customer qualifications (6-12 months).
  • Working Capital Strain: Days rose to ~182 (from 120 in FY22) due to export depots (US/Europe) and EV sample production.
  • Macro Uncertainties: Global supply chain ripples from multi-country tariffs; slower H1 fluoropolymer recovery vs. initial 25% FY26 guidance.

Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts Supporting Resilience/Margins)

  • Margin Expansion: Chemical EBITDA +26% YoY to Rs 381 Cr (32% margin, +608 bps) driven by better mix (high-value fluoropolymers for semicon/aerospace), cost optimizations (power), and stable specialty chemicals.
  • LiPF6 Price Surge: Battery salt prices doubled ($10/kg to $17/kg), boosting outlook as non-China integrated producer. Domestic/strategic edge vs. China imports.
  • Domestic Protections: DGTR-recommended anti-dumping duty (ADD) on PTFE imports (~7kt annual India imports); GFL positioned to capture 50-60% with spare capacity and wide grades.
  • R32 Ramp-Up: Post-fire recovery on track; 20kt capacity by FY26-end (high utilization expected due to demand gap), potential to 30kt quota entitlement.
  • Funding Secured: EV capex (Rs 1,200 Cr FY26, ~Rs 1,500 Cr FY27) fully funded (Rs 1,000 Cr equity at Rs 25k Cr valuation + debt/sovereign funds); no immediate dilution.

Growth Prospects (High-Potential Drivers for FY27+)

  • Battery Materials Leadership: GFCL EV targeting global non-China EV/BESS (LFP/NMC agnostic; ~50% LFP cell cost coverage via LiPF6, binders, CAM, electrolytes). LFP CAM commissioned (samples soon), binders CY26 H2, LiPF6 sales Q4 FY26. Export focus (US eligible under IRA/O3B; non-PFE compliant). FY27 EBIT breakeven; FY28 major scale-up (ex-China LiB demand ~2 TWh by CY30). Rs 6,000 Cr capex over 4-5 years for multi-GW wallet share.
  • Fluoropolymers Recovery: Adjusted 25% FY26 growth intact (alternate markets: Japan/Korea/Europe semicon). Legacy US player exit aids share gains.
  • Refrigerants/Bulk: R32 high utilization; chloromethanes price/volume gains.
  • Overall Outlook: Surging demand from EV/BESS, data centers/AI, renewables, 5G/semicon. H2 pickup expected; long-term EBITDA margins to improve further.

Investment Thesis Summary: GFL’s chemical core is margin-accretive (32% EBITDA), providing cash flow for high-growth battery bet (non-China edge amid LiPF6 surge). Near-term tariff/R-22 drag offset by H2 catalysts (R32, PTFE ADD, LiPF6 sales). Long-term structurally positive (EV/BESS tailwinds), but execution risks in battery ramp. Neutral-positive outlook; monitor Q3 for tariff adjustments and qualifications.
