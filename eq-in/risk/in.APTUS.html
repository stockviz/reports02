Analysis of Aptus Value Housing Finance India Limited (APTUS)

Aptus Value Housing Finance India Limited (APTUS) is a housing finance company (HFC) focused on affordable housing and small business loans for self-employed, low/middle-income groups (LIG) in semi-urban/rural Tier-2/3/4 areas. It operates 321 branches across 6 states, with a granular portfolio (avg. ticket ₹8-9L, 79% self-employed customers, 75% LIG). Q2/H1 FY26 results (ended Sep 30, 2025) show resilient growth amid rising delinquencies. Below is a structured analysis based on the provided documents (investor presentation, financial results, board outcomes, press release, etc.).

Tailwinds (Positive Factors)

  • Robust Growth Momentum: AUM at ₹11,767 Cr (+22% YoY, +4% QoQ); Disbursements ₹963 Cr Q2 (+24% QoQ, +3% YoY). H1 disbursements ₹1,738 Cr (+8% YoY). Customer base at 1.72L (+18% YoY).
  • Superior Profitability: H1 PAT ₹446 Cr (+26% YoY); Q2 PAT ₹227 Cr (+24% YoY). RoA 7.9% / RoE 20.0% (industry-leading, sustainable due to diversified products). Spreads at 8.9% (+18 bps QoQ), NIM 13.4%, Opex stable at 2.7% of AUM.
  • Capital Strength & Liquidity: Net worth ₹4,657 Cr (+16% YoY); CRAR 69.2%. Comfortable liquidity (₹1,738 Cr incl. unavailed sanctions). Rating upgrade to AA(Stable) by ICRA/CARE supports lower CoF (8.4%, -26 bps QoQ).
  • Operational Efficiency: >90% digital agreements, >95% digital collections. Fully in-house model (321 branches, 3,717 employees). Tech-led underwriting (90% approvals in 72 hrs), low LTV (~40%).
  • Shareholder Returns: Interim dividend ₹2/share (100% payout, record date Nov 7, 2025). Promoter reclassification (WestBridge exit) enhances liquidity/free float.
  • Strategic Positioning: Diversified mix (HL 42%, Quasi-HL 35%, SBL 23%); rural focus (83%); contiguous expansion (Maharashtra/Odisha +244% AUM YoY).

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Asset Quality Pressure: GNPA 1.55% (+30 bps YoY, +6 bps QoQ); NNPA 1.17% (+23 bps YoY). 30+ DPD at 6.34% (improved QoQ but elevated). ECL provisions up (1.03% coverage).
  • Cost of Funds Stability but Macro Sensitivity: Yield stable at 17.3% but spreads compressed slightly (-7 bps YoY). Self-employed/LIG exposure vulnerable to economic slowdown/inflation.
  • Opex Discipline Tested by Scale: Flat at 2.7% but branch expansion (20 new in Q2) could pressure if growth slows.
  • Geographic Concentration: ~90% AUM in South India; new markets (Odisha/Maharashtra) nascent (0.7% of AUM).

  • Medium-Term Target: ₹25,000 Cr AUM (implies ~20-25% CAGR from current ₹11,767 Cr). MD guidance: Sustain 25%+ growth via HL/SBL focus (ticket ₹8-20L), contiguous expansion (289 branches >1-yr vintage).
