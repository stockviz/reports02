Analysis of Aptus Value Housing Finance India Limited (APTUS) - Q2/H1 FY26

Aptus Value Housing Finance India Ltd., a leading affordable housing finance player focused on self-employed low/middle-income groups (LIG/MIG) in Tier 2-4 cities, reported robust profitability amid steady growth. AUM reached ₹11,767 Cr (+22% YoY), driven by 321 branches (+30 YoY) and 1.72L customers (+18% YoY). PAT H1 FY26 at ₹446 Cr (+26% YoY), with RoA/RoE at 7.9%/20.0% (industry-leading). Key metrics: Yield 17.3%, Spread 8.9%, GNPA 1.55% (up 30 bps YoY), CRAR 69.2%. Board approved ₹2 interim dividend (100% payout) and promoter reclassification (Westbridge exit).

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Sustained Profitability & Efficiency: NIM 13.4%, Opex stable at 2.7% (best-in-class), driving 27% YoY operating profit growth to ₹312 Cr (Q2). RoA/RoE resilient across cycles due to diversified products (HL 47%, Quasi-HL 25%, SBL/NHBL 28%) and granular book (avg. ticket ₹9L, LTV ~40%).
  • Network & Digital Scale: 321 branches (289 >1-yr vintage), 3,717 employees (+22% YoY). >90% digital agreements, >95% collections via UPI/e-NACH; tech-led underwriting (100+ data points, 72-hr approvals).
  • Funding Strength: Diverse mix (NHB 30%, Banks 58%, NCDs 12%); avg. tenure 80+ months; rating upgrade to ICRA/CARE AA(Stable). Low short-term CP exposure.
  • Shareholder Value: ₹2 dividend; ESOP allotments; promoter reclassification enhances liquidity/institutional participation (FIIs/FPIs ~36%).
  • Macro Tailwind: Underserved LIG segment (75% portfolio), rising homeownership demand in semi-urban India.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Disbursement Moderation: H1 disbursements ₹1,738 Cr (+8% YoY, subdued vs. AUM growth); Q2 QoQ +24% but seasonal.
  • Asset Quality Pressure: GNPA 1.55% (+30 bps YoY, +6 bps QoQ); Stage 3 at 1.5% (PCR 25%). 30+ DPD improved to 6.34% via collections, but early delinquency up in new markets.
  • Spread Dynamics: Yield -7 bps YoY (17.3%); CoB -18 bps (8.4%); NIM flat YoY. Rising rates could compress further.
  • Opex Discipline Tested: Flat at 2.7%, but branch expansion (20 new in Q2) may pressure short-term.

Growth Prospects

  • Medium-Term Vision: ₹25,000 Cr AUM (~20% CAGR from FY26); target 25%+ sustained growth via HL/SBL focus (ticket ₹8-20L).
  • Geographic Expansion: Contiguous scaling in Maharashtra/Odisha (+244% AUM YoY); deepen TN/AP/TS/KA (86% AUM).
  • Product & Tech Leverage: In-house model (0% DSA), ML analytics, account aggregators for higher-value segments. Vintage maturity: >3-yr branches at ₹49.5 Cr avg. AUM/branch.
  • Capital/Rating Upside: Networth ₹4,657 Cr (+16% YoY), CRAR 69.2%; AA rating aids cheaper funding.
  • H1 Momentum: Q2 disbursements +24% QoQ; collections >99%; liquidity ₹1,738 Cr (incl. undrawn lines).

Key Risks

  • Asset Quality (High): Delinquency rise in new regions; ECL ₹117 Cr (1.03% coverage). Vulnerable to rural/semi-urban slowdown (79% self-employed, 75% LIG).
  • Interest Rate/Spread Risk (Medium): CoB volatility; 82% floating assets vs. 74% floating liabilities exposes to rate hikes.
  • Execution in New Markets (Medium): Maharashtra/Odisha scaling risks initial credit costs; <1-yr branches at ₹2.9 Cr AUM/branch.
  • Regulatory/Competition (Low-Medium): RBI/NHB norms (e.g., LTV caps); rising HFC competition.
  • Liquidity/Macro (Low): Strong position (₹563 Cr cash), but monsoon/election cycles impact low-income borrowers.
  • Mitigants: Conservative underwriting (PCR 25% Stage 3), 100% in-house sourcing, zero willful defaulters.

Overall Outlook: Strong tailwinds from profitability/tech/expansion outweigh headwinds; growth on track for 20-25% AUM CAGR. Monitor asset quality quarterly. Recommendation: Positive; hold/buy on dips for long-term compounding (sustainable RoE 20%).
