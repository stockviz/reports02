UFLEX Limited (BSE: 500148, NSE: UFLEX) - Q2 & H1 FY26 Analysis

Performance Snapshot (Consolidated, Unaudited): - Q2 FY26: Revenue flat YoY at ₹38,610 Mn (slight -1.6% QoQ); Normalized EBITDA -12.4% YoY at ₹3,895 Mn (10.1% margin, -140 bps YoY); PAT ₹269 Mn (vs loss of ₹646 Mn YoY). - H1 FY26: Revenue +3.2% YoY at ₹77,828 Mn; Normalized EBITDA -5.9% YoY at ₹8,593 Mn (11.0% margin, -110 bps YoY); PAT ₹849 Mn (vs loss of ₹1,631 Mn YoY). - Sales volume: Q2 -3.7% YoY (161,161 MT); H1 +2.0% YoY (331,665 MT). Packaging films ~77% of volume. - Net debt: ₹77,533 Mn (4.51x annualized normalized EBITDA, up from 3.60x in Mar’25). - Capex: ₹4,897 Mn in Q2 (focus: Egypt aseptic, Mexico WPP, Noida recycling).

UFlex, India’s largest flexible packaging firm with global ops (9 countries, 1.3 Mn+ MTPA capacity), showed resilience amid headwinds but moderated FY26 guidance (5% revenue growth, EBITDA ₹1,800-1,850 Cr). Below is a structured analysis.

Headwinds (Short-term Pressures)
  • Trade/Tariff Disruptions: US 25% reciprocal + 25% oil tariffs hit India exports (-18% BOPET, -22% BOPP YoY); China/SE Asia diversion to Europe/North America pressured realizations/volumes (Egypt -20% YoY).
  • Domestic Demand Weakness: GST transition caused destocking/delays; prolonged monsoon/cooler summer hurt seasonal FMCG (beverages/juices); BOPET/BOPP imports surged 120%/100% YoY.
  • Global Macro: Elevated inflation/food prices (US CPI 2.9%, EU 2.5%); geopolitical tensions; Nigeria devaluation.
  • Operational: Seasonality in aseptic (Q2 dip to 99% util. vs 136% prior); Europe/MEA volumes down (imports dumping).
  • Financial: Debt up (gross ₹90,896 Mn); forex/derivative losses (₹293 Mn adj. in Q2); high leverage (4.51x).
Tailwinds (Supportive Factors)
  • Policy Boost: GST rationalization/tax cuts to spur consumption; EPR deferral (FY26 shortfall carry-forward 3 yrs) favors UFlex’s recycling lead (372 Mn PET bottles, 4,940 MT MLP recycled in H1).
  • Domestic Strength: India packaging films util. +260 bps YoY (79.7%); vPET chips +89% YoY; BOPP margins 30% (fire at competitor aided).
  • Diversification: Mexico/USMCA shields US exports; global footprint (56% intl. revenue) mitigates tariffs.
  • Sustainability Edge: Asclepius (100% rPET films), Project Plastic Fix; single-pellet PET chips (70:30 virgin:recycled) for EPR compliance.
  • Innovation: 20+ new products (e.g., barrier BOPP/CPP films, scratch-off inks, laser-engraved cylinders); awards (IFCA Star, FPA Showcase).
Growth Prospects (Medium-Long Term)
  • Capacity Ramp: 4 projects (India aseptic/recycling, Egypt aseptic, Mexico WPP) to add ₹3,000 Cr revenue/₹500 Cr EBITDA at optimal util. (Q1 FY27 timeline).
  • Aseptic/Packaging: H1 volumes +5.5% YoY; Sanand expansion to 12 Bn packs; pet food (Mexico) high-growth.
  • Recycling Boom: EPR mandates (10-30% recycled content FY26+); UFlex’s 43k MTPA rPET/31k MTPA rMLP capacity positions it as leader.
  • India Tailwinds: 6.8% GDP FY26; rural FMCG > urban; urbanization/polymer per capita < peers (15 kg vs US 112 kg).
  • Guidance: FY26: 5% revenue (+ from H1), EBITDA ₹1,800-1,850 Cr; FY27: 10% revenue growth.
  • Dharwad BOPP: New 54k TPA line (₹715 Cr capex, FY28) to capture South India demand post-competitor fire.

Projected Incremental Revenue (at optimal util.): ₹3,000 Cr | EBITDA Margin: ~17%
