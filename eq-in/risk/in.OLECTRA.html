Analysis of Olectra Greentech Limited (OLECTRA)

Olectra Greentech Limited is a leading Indian manufacturer of electric vehicles (primarily e-buses and e-tippers) and composite insulators, with a strong focus on the e-mobility sector. Key highlights include a robust order book of 9,818 vehicles, 3,200+ EVs on roads (48+ Cr clean km driven), and a new greenfield EV plant (150 acres, initial capacity 5,000 units/year, scalable to 10,000). Q2/H1 FY26 results (ended Sep 30, 2025) show revenue growth (25%/20% YoY consolidated), driven by insulators (83% YoY Q2 growth) offsetting softer EV margins. Market cap: ~INR 127 Bn. Recent board actions include appointing a new MD (Mahesh Babu Subramanian, ex-SWITCH Mobility CEO) at INR 10 Cr p.a., signaling aggressive expansion.

Below is a structured analysis of tailwinds, headwinds, growth prospects, and key risks, based on the provided filings (investor presentation, financials, board outcomes, postal ballot).

Tailwinds (Supportive Factors)

  • Policy & Market Momentum in EVs: Strong govt push (e.g., FAME-II, state tenders for e-bus fleets). Olectra has deep ties with STUs (pan-India deliveries to 20+ cities like Pune, Mumbai, Bangalore). 3,000+ e-buses on roads validate reliability.
  • Robust Order Book & Execution Track Record: 9,818 e-buses/tippers (FY25 orders: 8,823 buses). Historical delivery ramp-up (e.g., 1,000 buses in FY25). New wins in e-tippers (first delivery) and private segments.
  • Diversified Revenue Streams: Insulators division shining (Q2 revenue INR 7,537 lakhs, +83% YoY; EBITDA margin 35%). EV stable at 88-89% of revenue. Historical revenue CAGR 45% (FY22-25: INR 1.09L Cr to 1.80L Cr).
  • Capacity Expansion: Greenfield plant partially operational; entry into inter-city/staff/private transport. Product portfolio: 135+ variants (V-7m to CX-12m buses, tippers).
  • Strong Financials & Leadership: Q2/H1 FY26 revenue/EBITDA/PBT up 25%/9%/6% YoY. ROCE ~19%. New MD brings 30+ yrs EV experience (ex-Mahindra Electric CEO), with tech edge (BYD tie-up).
  • Market Leadership: Largest e-bus maker in India; 3,200+ vehicles deployed.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Margin Pressure in Core EV Business: EV EBITDA margin dipped (Q2: 15.3% → 11.4%; H1: 14.9% → 11.8%) despite 20%/15% revenue growth. Likely due to higher input costs, inventory build-up (inventories +41% YoY to INR 40,939 lakhs), and pricing pressures from competition.
  • Working Capital Strain: H1 FY26 operating cash flow negative (INR -3,097 lakhs) vs. positive prior years. Trade receivables high (INR 67,340 lakhs); inventories up sharply amid capex.
  • Capex Intensity: Heavy investments (H1 capex INR 8,823 lakhs; CWIP INR 20,653 lakhs). Total assets up 15% YoY to INR 2.36L Cr, funded partly by borrowings (non-current up to INR 21,672 lakhs).
  • Execution Delays: Large order book implies ramp-up needs; past reliance on STU tenders exposes to tender cycles/delays.
  • High MD Remuneration: New MD’s INR 10 Cr p.a. (fixed INR 8 Cr + 25% variable) is elevated (~5-6% of H1 PAT), amid potential profit inadequacy (requires Schedule V approval).

Growth Prospects

  • High-Volume EV Execution: Order book visibility for 2-3 years; FY26 guidance implies strong deliveries (H1: 580 EVs sold vs. 541 YoY). New segments: e-tippers, staff/private transport, inter-state.
  • Capacity & Product Ramp: New plant to 5-10k units/year; 135+ variants position for diversified demand (e.g., CX-Coach for tourism).
  • Insulators Tailwind: Export/state-of-art greenfield potential; margins >35% could drive overall profitability.
  • Market Opportunity: India e-bus market ~10x growth by 2030 (govt targets 30% EV penetration). Olectra’s 20-25% share + pan-India presence = multi-year revenue doubling potential.
  • FY26 Outlook: Consensus from presentation: Revenue >INR 2L Cr possible (FY25: 1.80L Cr); EBITDA margins to stabilize/improve with scale.
  • Strategic Moves: New MD’s EV expertise; R&D emphasis; adjacent EVs (tippers) and geographies.

Projected Growth: 20-30% revenue CAGR over FY26-28, driven by 70% EV + 30% insulators; PAT margins to recover to 8-10%.

Overall Risk Profile: Medium. Execution and margins are near-term watches, but order book de-risks revenue.

Summary

Bull Case (High Conviction): Olectra is well-positioned in India’s EV bus boom, with unmatched order book, capacity ramp, and insulator kicker. Expect 25%+ FY26 growth, margins rebound to 12-15%, stock re-rating to 30-40x FY27 EPS (current ~25x FY26 est.). Tailwinds dominate.

Bear Case: Margin compression persists, capex drains cash, tender delays hit execution → flat FY26 growth, de-rating.

Recommendation: Positive. Buy/Hold for long-term EV exposure. Monitor Q3 execution, cash flows, and postal ballot outcome (Dec 25, 2025). Target: 20-30% upside from current levels, assuming INR 1,200-1,500/share (mcap implies ~INR 1,200).
