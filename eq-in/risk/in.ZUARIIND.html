Zuari Industries Limited (ZUARIIND) Analysis

Zuari Industries Ltd (ZIL) is an apex holding company in the Adventz Group with operations in Sugar-Power-Ethanol (SPE), real estate, engineering services, financial services, and strategic investments in fertilizers/rail. Q2 FY26 (Sep 2025) showed resilient standalone EBITDA growth amid seasonality, boosted consolidated PAT by associate gains. Strategic investments valued ~₹4,680 Cr (10% down QoQ). Debt reduction via Dubai realty inflows (~₹800 Cr expected Q1 FY27) is key focus. Below is a structured analysis based on earnings call, financials, investor presentation, and disclosures.

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)
  • Operational Efficiency in Core SPE (Sugar-Power-Ethanol):
    • Sugar realizations up 5.7% YoY (₹4,033/qt); ethanol production +44% QoQ (311 operating days, highest ever); power output doubled H1 YoY.
    • Earliest-ever crushing start (Oct 26, 2025); EBITDA margins improved to 6.9% (Q2).
  • Real Estate Momentum (Zuari Infraworld): Asset-light DM model with GDV ₹2,900 Cr; St. Regis Dubai 86% complete (handovers Mar 2026, ~₹800 Cr inflow); Mysore Phase 4 95% done; new DM mandates (Gangotri/Texmaco Rail: ~₹4,000 Cr GDV, 7% revenue share).
  • Bioethanol Expansion: ZEBPL (180 KLPD JV) 95% complete (Nov 2025 commissioning); plan to scale to 1,000 KLPD; 100% tender allocation in grain-based ethanol.
  • Subsidiaries/JVs Growth: Simon India orders ₹144 Cr (fertilizer focus, AI/digital push); Zuari Finserv AUM +48% YoY (₹560 Cr); Insurance Brokers EBITDA +93% YoY.
  • Financial Discipline: Borrowing cost down 13 bps YoY (10.26%); finance cost -₹3 Cr Q2/-₹6.3 Cr H1 YoY; BBB- rating stable.
  • Associate Boost: ZACL gains from MCFL-PPL merger (~₹817 Cr exceptional profit share).
Headwinds (Challenges)
  • Seasonal/Quota Pressure in Sugar: Q2 sales -12% YoY (3.7L qtl vs 4.2L); lower govt quota; sugarcane price hike to ₹400/qt (UP) pressures margins (expected 10-12% EBITDA sustainable, but surplus sugar may cap prices).
  • Furniture Division Drag: Operations closed; ongoing ~₹10L/month maintenance on 17-acre land; impairments recognized.
  • Investment Volatility: Strategic portfolio down 10% QoQ to ₹4,680 Cr (Texmaco -22%, Chambal -10%).
  • High Debt/Interest: Gross external debt ₹1,863 Cr; consolidated finance cost ₹61.5 Cr Q2 (despite reductions).
  • Subsidiary Inconsistencies: Real estate EBITDA negative Q2; some JVs (e.g., ZIntL) with losses.
