Summary Analysis: Shivalik Bimetal Controls Limited (SBCL)

SBCL is India’s leading integrated manufacturer of precision components like thermostatic bimetals, shunt resistors, and electrical contacts, serving EVs, smart meters, switchgear, and energy storage. It exports 56% of revenue to 38 countries, with a tech moat (EBW, diffusion bonding, 77 bimetal grades) and debt-free balance sheet (net cash ₹68 Cr FY25). H1 FY26 showed resilient growth: revenue +8.5% YoY to ₹274 Cr, EBITDA +25% to ₹63 Cr (23% margin, +306 bps), PAT +26% to ₹47 Cr (17.3% margin). Shunts (~50% mix) and bimetals drove performance amid margin expansion from cost control and richer mix.

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Margin Expansion & Operational Efficiency: Gross margins up 296 bps to 45.8% (H1 FY26) via better mix, pricing power, and in-house EBW (halves capex vs. imports). EBITDA margins at 23%, ROCE >25%.
  • Domestic Demand Surge: Smart meters (250M rollout, 15% CAGR to $763M by 2031), switchgear exports, and PLI localization benefits.
  • Debt-Free Balance Sheet: Self-funded growth, ₹68 Cr net cash (FY25), supports opportunistic capex (₹15-20 Cr FY26-27 for ₹1,600 Cr capacity).
  • Institutional Backing: 20% MF/AIF holding, long-only funds, broker endorsements; promoter reclassification (EGM approved 99.9%) signals cleaner governance.
  • ESG Alignment: Hydro/solar power, low Scope-2 emissions, attracting green funds.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Export Softness: North America EV slowdown (Americas shunts -17% YoY Q2); Europe/Asia mixed. FY25 revenue flat at ₹437 Cr despite domestic support.
  • Working Capital Strain: Inventory days +8 to 199, net WC days +13 to 245 (H1 FY26), tying up cash amid volume growth.
  • Raw Material Volatility: Fluctuations impacted margins; higher employee/other expenses (+17-20% YoY).
  • Macro Headwinds: China+1 shifts aid but euro-peers face inflation/lead times; NA EV delays persist.

Growth Prospects

  • Multi-Decade TAM Expansion: EV shunts (3x ICE, $3-4B by 2029, 32% CAGR); smart meters/energy storage/data centers (15% CAGR to $45B by 2035); electrification tailwinds.
  • Capacity Leverage: Existing assets support >₹1,300-1,600 Cr (vs. FY25 ₹437 Cr); shunts/bimetals 20-21% 5-yr CAGR.
  • Strategic Initiatives: Forward integration (PCBA pilot FY26), Italy WOS for EU access, inorganic acquisitions, backward integration (raw materials). Order visibility strong through FY30+.
  • Export Acceleration: 56% share rising; Asia/EU recovery + China diversification.

Key Risks

  • Commodity/Geopolitical Exposure: Raw material price swings (copper/manganin); 56% exports vulnerable to trade wars, FX volatility.
  • Execution Risks: WC bloat could pressure cash flows (op cash ₹19 Cr H1 FY26 vs. FY25 ₹93 Cr); capex delays in EBW/PCBA.
  • Competition/Moat Erosion: Global players in shunts/bimetals; 24-month customer requalification but design-ins lock-in (15+ yrs).
  • Regulatory/Concentration: Promoter changes (33% holding post-reclass); segment concentration (shunts/bimetals 85% revenue).
  • Macro Sensitivity: EV/smart meter policy delays (e.g., IRA/Fit-55); cyclical industrials/auto demand.

Overall Outlook: Strong tailwinds from electrification and moats outweigh headwinds; FY26 guidance implies 15-20% revenue growth with 22-24% margins. Net positive with self-funded scalability, but monitor WC and exports. Valuation supported by 21% 5-yr revenue CAGR, 32% PAT CAGR.
