Analysis of Power & Instrumentation (Gujarat) Limited (PIGL)

PIGL is a 50-year-old electrical EPC contractor specializing in power T&D, substations, airports (35+ projects), renewables, and infrastructure. FY25 revenue: ₹169 Cr (standalone), PAT ₹11.8 Cr. H1 FY26 consolidated revenue: ₹112 Cr (+37% YoY), PAT ₹7.3 Cr. Strong order wins (₹134 Cr recent), PECL subsidiary acquisition (51% stake), and sector tailwinds drive momentum. Stock trades at ~₹158 (MC ₹278 Cr as of Nov 2025).

Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts)

  • Robust Order Pipeline & Execution: Secured ₹134 Cr orders (e.g., ₹103 Cr RDSS from Ajmer Discom, ₹21 Cr ATS Techno factory, ₹9.5 Cr solar). Arbitration win: ₹4.1 Cr +18% interest. Order book supports 12-15 month visibility; 300+ projects delivered.
  • Financial Momentum: Revenue doubled FY25; H1 FY26 EBITDA ₹11.5 Cr (10% margin). Dividend ₹0.20/share declared/paid.
  • Strategic Acquisition: 35.8% stake in PECL (total 51%, Sep 2025) for backward integration (panels, busducts, substations). PECL’s CPRI approval for 11kV/3000A busduct enhances credibility.
  • Sector Boom: Aviation (220 airports by 2030, ₹25K Cr AAI capex), renewables (450 GW target, solar EPC focus), RDSS schemes favor EPC players.
  • Client Strength: Govt/PSUs (AAI, discoms, ISRO, IOCL), repeat orders; pan-India footprint (8 offices).

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Working Capital Strain: High inventories (₹48-71 Cr), receivables (₹60 Cr+), negative CFO (₹-40 Cr FY25). Trade payables volatile.
  • Debt & Costs: Borrowings ₹18 Cr+; interest ~₹1.5 Cr H1 FY26. Employee/other expenses rising.
  • Margin Pressure: EBITDA dipped to 10% H1 FY26 (from 12% FY25) due to material costs (93% of revenue).
  • Low Institutional Participation: Public institutions hold ~0%; promoter voting abstained on some resolutions (e.g., RPTs).
  • Inventory Opacity: Auditors flag management-certified valuation (technical nature, multi-location).

Growth Prospects

  • High Visibility: 50% YoY revenue target FY26; aim for ₹300-350 Cr orders. PECL synergies to boost margins via in-house manufacturing.
  • Diversification: Solar EPC/BESS entry; renewables/aviation infra (18-20% YoY airport growth). Total addressable market: ₹221 Bn renewables investment by 2030.
  • Backward Integration: PECL (₹31 Cr book orders) adds busducts/panels; positions for data centers/industrials.
  • Scalability: 100+ staff, “A” class licenses in 7 states; mainboard listing (May 2023) aids funding.
  • Projections: Sustained 30-50% CAGR feasible with order execution; ROE ~15-18% (FY25: 15%).
