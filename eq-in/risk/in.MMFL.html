MM Forgings Limited (MMFL) - Q2/H1 FY26 Analysis

MMFL, a leading Indian forging company focused on automotive components (primarily CVs at ~80-90% of sales), reported a challenging H1 FY26 amid global headwinds. Consolidated revenue declined 4.5% YoY to ₹758 Cr (EBITDA down 13% to ₹142 Cr, margin 18.7% vs 20.5%; PAT down 42% to ₹36 Cr). Volumes fell (sales 37k tons vs 40k; EBITDA/ton ₹38k vs ₹40.5k), driven by US export weakness, but domestic sales held steady at 61.5%. Management guided FY26 revenue near PY levels (~₹1,500 Cr), with H2 at ₹750-800 Cr, and FY27 as a “breakout year” on new capacity.

Headwinds
  • US Export Slowdown: North America share halved to 9% (from 16%) due to Class VIII truck inventory buildup and demand weakness; Q2 was the “worst.” Preponed supplies earlier led to destocking; recovery expected from Nov ’25/Jun ’26.
  • Tariff Pressures: 50% US tariffs (customer-borne so far) create uncertainty; post-Mar ’26, could trigger resourcing to local/US/Asia suppliers.
  • Domestic CV Shift: Preference for tractor-trailers over multi-axle trucks reduced front axle demand, stagnating addressable market despite flat TIV.
  • Margin Compression: Employee costs and power/fuel inflation; operating EBITDA margin at 17.3% (ex-other income).
  • High Debt & Capex Drag: Net debt ~₹850-855 Cr (peak levels; gross debt ₹1,062 Cr); WC at ₹330 Cr. Interest up to ₹39 Cr (H1), though optimizations underway.
Tailwinds
  • Domestic Stability & Europe Gains: Domestic at 61.5% (₹437 Cr standalone, near flat YoY); Europe up to 21% with new clients.
  • Cost Discipline: Inventory compression to cut WC by ₹50-75 Cr; debt swap to foreign currency loans saving ~₹12 Cr p.a. interest (target run-rate ₹50-60 Cr).
  • Product Mix: Machined sales at 54% (vs 52% PY); sales/ton up to ₹1.93 lakh on higher value-add.
  • Subsidiary Improvements: DVS EBITDA up to ₹4.6 Cr (from ₹1.3 Cr); merger pending NCLT approval.
  • EV Progress: Abhinava Rizel near first order (₹20-30 Cr annual potential); magnet-less motor designs (2% more efficient) bypassing China supply issues.
Growth Prospects
  • Capacity Ramp: New 16,500-ton press (world’s largest) commissioning by Mar/Apr ’26; adds ₹300 Cr revenue (FY27 Q2 start, 10-20% higher margins via heavy forgings). Total capacity ~140k tons; utilization to 80-90k tons (FY27).
  • Order Book & Outlook: ₹300 Cr book (₹200 Cr FY27 realization); potential to add ₹500-700 Cr revenue from existing expansions, targeting >₹2,000 Cr (from ₹1,500 Cr base). FY27/FY28 “breakout” with sales rebound.
  • Diversification: Off-highway/engineering (10%); EV motors/controllers; heavy forgings (HF division >6k-ton press).
  • Capex Moderation: H2 FY26: ₹70 Cr; FY27: ₹100-120 Cr (focus on productive assets). Free cash to build balance sheet for M&A/organic jumps.
  • Macro Recovery: US Class VIII rebound expected; India positive; South America “bustling.”
Key Risks
Risk Category Description Mitigation
Demand/Export Prolonged US weakness/tariffs eroding 8-16% sales; CV dependence (90%). Europe/domestic focus; no major share loss; resourcing unlikely short-term due to capacity gaps elsewhere.
Financial Peak debt (net debt/EBITDA ~6x); forex/interest volatility. No new debt; accruals to strengthen BS; interest savings; outlier equity infusion considered.
Execution New press/EV delays; customer project slips. Strong order book; in-house motor design expertise.
Macro/External Commodity inflation (material ~42% sales); geopolitics; NPA rise in retail CE finance. Cost controls; 75% rupee debt conversion.
Operational Lower volumes/utilization; machining capex needed for off-highway. Inventory/WC optimization; FY27 volumes >80k tons targeted.

Overall Outlook: Near-term defensive (FY26 flat), with FY27 inflection on capacity/EBITDA leverage (20%+ margins possible). Resilient fundamentals (IATF-certified; global competitiveness) offset cyclical auto exposure. Balanced buy/hold for long-term investors; monitor US tariffs and debt trajectory. Valuation context: Trades at ~15-20x FY27 EPS potential (implied ₹2,000 Cr rev, 20% EBITDA).
