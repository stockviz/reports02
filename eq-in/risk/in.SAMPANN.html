Analysis of Sampann Utpadan India Limited (SAMPANN, BSE: 534598, NSE: SAMPANN)

Sampann Utpadan India Limited (formerly S.E. Power Ltd.) is a Gujarat-based listed company (CIN: L40106GJ2010PLC091880) primarily operating in two segments: Reclaimed Rubber (core revenue driver) and Non-Conventional Energy (wind/solar power, loss-making). The company has transitioned from pure power generation to diversified operations, with recent financials (Q2 FY26 ending 30/09/2025) showing revenue growth but persistent losses, high debt, and negative cash flows. Key documents highlight governance changes, Q2 results (unmodified audit review), and strong AGM approvals (27/09/2025). Below is a structured analysis of headwinds, tailwinds, growth prospects, and key risks.

Tailwinds (Positive Factors)

  • Strong Shareholder Confidence: 15th AGM resolutions passed with ~99-100% approval (73% voter turnout). Key approvals include director re-appointments (e.g., Sanjeetkumar Rath, Anant Kumar, Ashok Jolly), remuneration hikes for MD Sachin Agarwal and related party Anjanayae Agarwal, and secretarial auditor appointment. Promoters voted fully in favor where eligible.
  • Revenue Momentum in Core Segment: Reclaimed Rubber generated ~₹3,464 Lakhs (Q2 FY26 standalone), up from prior periods, indicating operational scale-up in sustainable/recycling business.
  • Capital Infusion: Allotment of ~50-82 lakh equity shares on warrant conversion (noted in Q2 and AGM), boosting share capital (~₹4,582 Lakhs) and reducing warrant liabilities.
  • Clean Audit Opinion: Unmodified limited review reports for Q2 FY26 standalone/consolidated results by D. Tayal & Jain, affirming compliance with Ind AS 34.
  • Diversification Benefits: Shift to Reclaimed Rubber (high-margin potential amid green economy push) offsets weak energy segment.

Headwinds (Negative Factors)

  • Governance Concerns: Sudden resignation of Independent Director/Chairman Pramod Agarwal (07/11/2025, DIN: 08862101) citing personal/professional reasons (confirmed no material issues). He ceases committee roles, potentially weakening board independence amid related-party approvals.
  • Persistent Losses & Weak Financials: Negative reserves (~₹-1,087 Lakhs standalone), accumulated losses in energy segment (e.g., ₹-83 Lakhs Q2), and total profit before tax volatile (₹240 Lakhs Q2 but historical swings).
  • High Leverage & Liquidity Strain: Long-term borrowings ~₹7,486 Lakhs; short-term ~₹1,050 Lakhs. Operating cash flow deeply negative (₹-924 Lakhs half-year), with net cash burn and low balances (₹5 Lakhs).
  • Segment Imbalance: Non-Conventional Energy contributes negligible revenue (~₹11 Lakhs) but consistent losses (~₹-83 Lakhs half-year), dragging overall profitability.
  • Low Institutional Participation: No votes from public institutions at AGM; public non-institutions at ~69% turnout.

Growth Prospects

  • Moderate to High in Reclaimed Rubber: Core segment shows scaling (revenue ~₹6,753 Lakhs half-year), aligned with India’s rubber recycling push (sustainable materials demand). Capital employed positive (~₹2,911 Lakhs), with potential for EBITDA margins if costs controlled.
  • Renewable Energy Upside: Small base (~1% revenue) but tailwinds from India’s green energy targets (wind/solar assets ~₹7,218 Lakhs PPE). Policy support (e.g., PLI schemes) could revive if execution improves.
  • Balance Sheet Strengthening: Warrant conversions and equity proceeds (~₹278 Cr investing inflow) support capex (e.g., ₹15 Cr fixed assets). Consolidated subsidiary (Shubham Electrochem) adds minor diversification.
  • Near-Term Catalysts: Q2 revenue growth (6,753 Lakhs half-year vs. prior), sensitivity analysis confirms asset recoverability. FY26 guidance implicit in MD remuneration hike signals expansion confidence.
  • Overall Outlook: 15-25% revenue CAGR feasible if debt reduced and energy turned around; market cap potential in small-cap renewables/recycling space.
