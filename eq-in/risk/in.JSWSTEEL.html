JSW Steel Limited (JSWSTEEL) Analysis: Headwinds, Tailwinds, Growth Prospects & Key Risks

Summary Overview:
JSW Steel delivered robust Q2 FY26 (Jul-Sep 2025) results with record consolidated crude steel production (7.90 MT, +17% YoY), sales (7.34 MT, +20% YoY), revenue (₹45,152 Cr, +14% YoY), and Adj. EBITDA (₹7,849 Cr, +39% YoY, 17.4% margin), driven by volume growth and cost efficiencies. H1 FY26 capex at ₹6,535 Cr signals aggressive expansion. Oct 2025 production rose 9% YoY to 24.95 LT despite maintenance. Domestic demand remains strong (+8.9% YoY steel consumption), but global headwinds persist. Net debt/EBITDA improved to 2.97x. Supreme Court upheld BPSL resolution, boosting sentiment. Stock benefits from capacity ramp-up and raw material security, but faces pricing pressures.

Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts)

  • Volume & Operational Strength: Record production/sales; Indian ops at 92% util. ex-maintenance. Retail sales +26% YoY; exports +89% YoY.
  • Cost Efficiencies: Lower iron ore, coking coal, and power costs drove EBITDA surge (+39% YoY).
  • Raw Material Security: Stake in M Res NSW raised to 83.33%, lifting effective interest in Illawarra coal to 30% (offtake rights +10% YoY).
  • Domestic Demand: India steel consumption +8.9% YoY; infra/construction robust; RBI GDP forecast 6.8% FY26.
  • Legal Wins: Supreme Court upheld BPSL resolution (Sep 2025), unlocking value from turnaround (BPSL PAT ₹166 Cr in Q2).
  • Sustainability Edge: Green hydrogen electrolyser commissioned; 885 MW renewables online; aligns with net-zero goals.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Pricing Pressure: Realizations down YoY; Adj. EBITDA/ton -9% QoQ (₹10,701).
  • Global/Export Risks: China exports +18% YoY; India net importer; US/EU tariffs/geopolitics weigh on 2026 outlook.
  • QoQ Dip: EBITDA -6% QoQ due to maintenance (Dolvi/Vijayanagar); US ops losses (Ohio EBITDA loss US$1.08 Mn).
  • Macro Caution: Elevated tariffs, policy uncertainty; rural demand uneven despite good monsoon.

Growth Prospects

  • Capacity Expansion: Domestic 34.2 MTPA → 43.4 MTPA in 3 years (Vijayanagar HSM fully ramped; Dolvi +5 MTPA by FY27; new 1 MTPA EAF at Kadapa by FY29).
  • Value-Added/Downstream: New CRM (0.6 MTPA Khopoli), CGL (0.4 MTPA Vijayanagar), CRNO (0.55 MTPA), CRGO expansion (+188 KT Nashik/Vijayanagar); section mills at Raigarh/Salem.
  • Capex Momentum: FY26 guidance ₹20,000 Cr; H1 spend ₹6,535 Cr. Slurry pipeline (Odisha) by FY27.
  • Strategic Moves: US ops consolidation; merger of 3 subs (Amba River Coke, etc.); Oct production signals Q3 rebound (BF3 restart Feb 2026).
  • EBITDA Outlook: Volumes/costs to support 18-20% margins; analyst meets (Dec 2) to highlight.

Key Risks

  • Commodity Volatility: Steel prices/raw mats (coking coal) sensitive to China dumping/tariffs.
  • Execution Delays: Capex-intensive expansions (₹20K Cr FY26); maintenance impacts util.
  • Debt/Leverage: Net debt ₹79,153 Cr (0.93x equity); refinancing risks amid rate cuts.
  • Geopolitical/Trade: US tariffs, Middle East peace fragile; export reliance (10% sales).
  • Regulatory/Legal: Mining royalties/premiums high (₹3,309 Cr H1); forex on borrowings.
  • Competition: China overcapacity; domestic imports.

Investment Outlook: Strong tailwinds from India growth/expansions outweigh headwinds; monitor Q3 volumes/pricing. Positive for long-term (capacity +26%), but near-term volatile due to global macros. Analyst presentation (Oct’25 link) and Dec meets key for updates.
