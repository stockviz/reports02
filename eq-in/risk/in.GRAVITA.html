Gravita India Ltd. (GRAVITA) Analysis: Headwinds, Tailwinds, Growth Prospects, and Key Risks

Gravita India Ltd. is a leading recycler of Lead (~80% revenue), Aluminium, Plastics, Rubber, and Turnkey Solutions, with global operations across 13+ facilities. FY26 H1 results show resilient growth (consolidated revenue +13% YoY to ₹2,076 Cr, PAT +36% to ₹190 Cr), driven by 4% volume growth, 10% EBITDA rise, and 25% ROIC. Vision 2029 targets top-5 global recycler status by 2028 via 25%+ volume CAGR, 7 LTPA capacity, and diversification.

Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts)

  • Robust Execution & Financial Discipline: Consistent 9-10% EBITDA margins, 23% 5-yr revenue CAGR, 57% PAT CAGR. Back-to-back hedging stabilizes margins amid volatility; 47% value-added products boost profitability.
  • Favorable Industry Shift: Battery Waste Management Rules (BWMR), EPR, GST drive formalization (informal lead recycling share down from 80% to 25% by FY26E), increasing scrap availability for organized players like Gravita.
  • ESG & Policy Support: Recycling aligns with green energy transition (e.g., EV batteries); 30%+ RE power target, ILA-accredited plants enhance appeal to OEMs (e.g., Exide, Amara Raja).
  • Diversified Supply Chain: 1,900+ global touchpoints, 33 owned yards, pan-India/30-country customer base reduce logistics costs.
  • Strong Partnerships & Barriers: OEM approvals, import licenses, tech know-how deter new entrants; tie-ups with Tata, Panasonic, etc., yield 60k MT orderbook.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Commodity Volatility: Metal price swings (mitigated but not eliminated by hedging); Q2 forex/metal adjustments noted.
  • Margin Pressure: Rising costs (employee, finance) amid expansions; remuneration hikes for CMD/Directors (e.g., CMD from ₹54L to ₹56L/month) signal cost inflation.
  • Working Capital Intensity: Inventory/receivables up (H1FY26: inventories +21% QoQ), cash from ops negative at -₹91 Cr standalone.
  • Subsidiary Streamlining: Voluntary closure of inactive South Africa unit (0% revenue impact but signals cleanup needs).
  • Execution in New Markets: Scaling Africa/Asia facilities amid logistics/geopolitical risks.

Growth Prospects (High Potential)

  • Capacity Ramp-Up: Current 3.4 Lac MT to 7 LTPA by FY28 (Lead/Alu/Plastic/Rubber); new verticals (Li-ion, Steel, Paper) target 30%+ non-lead revenue, 50%+ value-added.
  • Geographic Expansion: 40% overseas capacity; Mundra port facility, Ghana/Sri Lanka plants unlock proximity to scrap/markets.
  • Diversification & Innovation: Turnkey projects (70+ executed globally), rubber/plastic recycling, MCX empanelment; 25%+ volume CAGR via VISION 2029 (35% profitability growth, 25%+ ROIC).
  • Sustainability Edge: 13.7% green energy, Scope 3 reporting; net-zero by FY50 positions for ESG funds/investors.
  • Projections: H1FY26 PAT +36% YoY; analysts meetings (Dec 2025) signal positive outlook; QIP proceeds (₹1,000 Cr) fuel capex.

Key Risks (Moderate-High, Mostly Mitigated)

Risk Category Description Mitigation
Commodity/ Forex Price swings, currency exposure (38% forex OCI gain H1FY26). Back-to-back hedging since 2016; core inventory hedged since 2019.
Regulatory/Legal Customs dispute (₹70 Cr demand, under appeal); EPR/BWMR changes. Legal opinions favorable; no material provision.
Execution/Operational Capex delays (₹58 Cr H1FY26), capacity utilization 63%. Phased expansions, strong mgmt (avg 29 yrs exp).
Concentration Lead-dependent (90% revenue); single-supplier risks. Diversification to 30% non-lead by 2029.

Overall Outlook: Positive (Buy/Hold). Tailwinds from sustainability megatrends and execution outweigh headwinds. Growth prospects strong (20-25% CAGR feasible), but monitor capex returns and commodity cycles. Risks well-hedged; maintain 25% ROIC trajectory for outperformance. Market cap ~₹15,000-20,000 Cr implied valuation at 50-60x FY26 EPS.
