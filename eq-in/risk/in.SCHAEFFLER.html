Schaeffler India Limited (SCHAEFFLER) - Q3 CY2025 Analysis

Schaeffler India Limited, a leading player in automotive and industrial motion technology (bearings, clutches, etc.), reported robust Q3 CY2025 results amid a stable Indian economy (GDP ~6.4%, IIP ~4%). Standalone revenue grew 13.9% YoY to ₹2,360 Cr, with EBITDA margin expanding to 20.2% (breaching 20% for the first time). 9M revenue up 12.7% YoY, PAT margin at 12.9%. Key drivers: Automotive Technologies (28.8% YoY growth), localization at 79%, and new wins. Below is a structured analysis of headwinds, tailwinds, growth prospects, and key risks, based on earnings call transcript, financials, investor presentation, and disclosures.

Tailwinds (Positive Factors)
  • Strong Financial Momentum: Double-digit YoY revenue growth sustained (Q3: 13.9%, 9M: 12.7%); EBITDA margins improved to 20.2% (vs. 18.5% YoY) due to volume leverage, fixed-cost absorption, and gross margin gains (+₹148 Cr). PAT up 24% YoY; record free cash flow (₹223 Cr Q3, ₹747 Cr 9M).
  • Automotive Sector Strength: 35% of revenue; Automotive Technologies up 28.8% YoY (18.7% 9M). Benefits from 2W/tractor growth (6-13% YoY), GST reforms (late Sep’25 rollout expected to boost PV/CV demand in Q4), and OEM capacity ramps.
  • Localization & Cost Efficiency: 79% localization (up from prior quarters, targeting 80%); supports margins and Atmanirbhar Bharat. Automotive localization at 85-90% vs. industrial ~60%.
  • Customer Recognition & Wins: Awards from Escorts Kubota/TAFE for quality/zero defects. New business in clutches, tensioners, slewing rings, bearings; e-axle lifetime order book ~€300M.
  • Cash & Capex Discipline: Strong FCF funds 4% sales capex (localization focus); Savli plant at 90% utilization.
  • Industrial Stability: Cement/steel up 9-10% YoY (BIS 38% of revenue); overall IIP steady.
Headwinds (Challenges)
  • Segment Imbalances: BIS (38% revenue) declined 1.3% YoY (4.1% 9M) due to project/tender timing (e.g., wind energy, railways); QoQ flatness dragged overall growth.
  • QoQ Declines: Exports down 4.5% QoQ (low base helped YoY 37%); Vehicle Lifetime Solutions down 8.1% QoQ.
  • Subsidiary Drag: KRSV (e-commerce, acquired 2023) posted -14.7% EBITDA (₹75 Cr revenue Q3); breakeven targeted for 2027 amid expansion costs.
  • Working Capital Pressure: Up to 19% of sales (vs. 19.8% YoY) due to inventory build for anticipated GST-driven demand.
  • Traded Goods Mix: Slight QoQ rise (16.6% to 19.8% of sales), though YoY localization improved; lower margins vs. manufactured goods.
Growth Prospects
  • Double-Digit Trajectory: Management committed to sustained double-digit growth; domestic (85% revenue) up 10.5% YoY, with Q4 upside from GST clarity, festive/PV recovery (OEMs “bullish”).
  • EV & Emerging Plays: E-axle ramp-up (customer-aligned, NDA-bound); new products like large spherical roller bearings (Savli) target steel/cement/mining.
  • Capacity Leverage: Post heavy capex (prior years), focus on utilization (e.g., Sheffield UK clutch line relocation to Hosur: 80% domestic). 2026 capex >2025; 2027+ pickup.
  • Aftermarket/Distribution: Vehicle Lifetime (12% revenue) testing innovations (lube vending); industrial distribution growing via digital tools.
  • Exports Recovery: Intercompany up 27.5% 9M (low base); US order book intact despite tariffs.
  • Overall Outlook: 9M PAT margin 12.9%; no formal guidance, but “sustain YTD performance.” Consolidated revenue ₹2,435 Cr (19.1% EBITDA).
Key Risks
  • Industrial Volatility: Project/tender-based (wind, railways) timing mismatches; private capex stable but not accelerating (IIP ~4-5%). | Sector Dependency | Automotive (47% incl. VLS): GST delay caused PV sales dip (-1-1.5%); EV single-customer risk.
  • External Shocks: US/EU tariffs/geopolitics (exports 15%); low external growth (3-4%) limits intercompany upside.
  • Counterfeit Threat: Ongoing raids (Delhi Nov’25); erodes brand/trust (mitigated via OriginCheck app).
  • Margin Pressure: KRSV losses; raw material/inflation (CPI 1.7% but services up); competition in BIS.
  • Execution: Capex delays, localization targets, EV ramp (not even over lifetime). Auditor transition (Price Waterhouse from 2026 AGM).
  • Macro: Delayed monsoons, inflation rebound, or slower infra push could hit tractors/BIS.
Summary

Schaeffler India is firing on most cylinders with strong tailwinds from automotive recovery, margin expansion (20%+ EBITDA), and localization driving 12-14% growth. Headwinds are contained (BIS timing, sub losses), but growth prospects shine in EV ramps, GST boost, and capacity sweat (double-digit FY26 potential). Key risks center on industrial lumpiness, exports, and counterfeits—manageable with robust FCF (net debt-free). Overall positive outlook: Buy/Hold for growth investors; trading at premium valuations justified by quality metrics (ROCE ~25-30% implied). Monitor Q4 for GST traction and BIS tenders.
