Siemens Limited (SIEMENS EQ / BSE: 500550) Analysis

Date Context: Analysis based on Q4 FY2025 (Jul-Sep 2025) and 12M results (Oct 2024-Sep 2025, continuing ops post-Energy demerger), plus GST SCN quashing (Nov 2025). Standalone revenue: ₹46.3B (Q4, +16% YoY); ₹155B (12M, +8% YoY). Consolidated: ₹51.7B (Q4, +16%); ₹174B (12M, +8%). PAT (consol, continuing): ₹485Cr (Q4, -7% YoY, adjusted flat ex-prior gain); EPS ₹13.63.

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Robust Execution & Growth Momentum: Q4 revenue +16% YoY (consol), driven by Smart Infrastructure (+20% YoY to ₹27B) and Mobility (+29% to ₹11B). Order backlog +6% at ₹42k Cr signals visibility. New orders +11% YoY at ₹4.8k Cr.
  • Energy Demerger Benefits: Completed Mar 2025 (appointed date Mar 1); transferred ₹38k Cr net assets (incl. ₹25k Cr cash) to Siemens Energy India Ltd. Sharp balance sheet cleanup: Total assets down to ₹188B (standalone), equity ₹130B; cash ₹17B. Focus on high-margin continuing ops (Smart Infra, Mobility, Digital Industries).
  • Regulatory Win: Gujarat HC quashed ₹35 Cr GST SCN (Nov 2025) on Halol property lease transfer—removes liability, interest/penalty risk (~₹35-50 Cr potential hit).
  • Macro Support: Govt infra capex intact; festive consumption uptick from tax cuts/GST reforms. Order book growth in Mobility (rail/infra) and Smart Infra (grids/buildings).
  • Financial Health: Net cash position strong (₹17B standalone cash); low debt (finance costs <₹50 Cr/Q). RoCE implied healthy post-demerger.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Segment Weakness: Digital Industries flat/slight dip (₹10.7B Q4; 12M -7% YoY) due to low prior backlog penetration and muted private capex. Low Voltage Motors volatile.
  • Profit Pressure: Q4 PAT flat ex-prior ₹69 Cr property gain; margins squeezed by demerger costs (₹201 Cr Q4), higher employee/direct costs. Operating cash flow weak at ₹1B (12M standalone) due to working capital (receivables/inventories up).
  • Transitional FY Complexity: Shift to Apr-Mar (18M FY26: Oct’24-Mar’26); 6 quarters reporting adds noise. Auditors flag scheme accounting deviation (effective vs. appointed date).
  • Post-Demerger Scale: Continuing ops revenue down ~60% YoY in Q4 (ex-Energy ₹20B prior); backlog/profits adjusting.

Growth Prospects

  • High (Medium-Term: 12-24M):
    • Core Segments: Smart Infra/Mobility ~70% revenue; +20-30% YoY potential from infra push (railways, smart cities, grids). Digital Industries recovery with private capex revival.
    • Macro Tailwinds: Govt stimulus (tax relief, infra budget) to boost consumption/private investment. Festive uptick signals demand pickup.
    • Strategic: Digital/sustainability focus (Ind AS compliant); order backlog supports 12-18M revenue visibility. Targets: 10-15% CAGR revenue (consensus implied).
    • 12M Outlook: FY26 (18M) growth 15-20% possible; full FY27 normalized.
  • Catalysts: Private capex inflection, export orders, Siemens global synergies.

Summary

Siemens shines post-Energy demerger with cleaner, growth-focused portfolio (Smart Infra/Mobility drivers); Q4 delivers 16% revenue beat amid private capex weakness. Tailwinds (backlog, infra spend, GST relief) outweigh headwinds (DI softness, WC drag). Growth outlook strong (15%+ FY26E) if private capex revives; risks tilted macro/execution. Buy/Hold for infra/digital exposure; target upside 15-20% in 12M (backlog-led). Neutral near-term volatility from FY transition.
