Siemens Limited (SIEMENS EQ / 500550) Analysis

Period Analyzed: Q4 FY2025 (Jul-Sep 2025, unaudited) and 12M ended Sep 30, 2025 (transitional context; prior FY Oct 2023-Sep 2024). Consolidated continuing operations post-Energy demerger (effective Mar 1, 2025). Key metrics: Revenue +16% YoY (₹5,171 Cr), Orders +10.5% (₹4,800 Cr), Backlog +6% (₹42,253 Cr), PAT -7.1% YoY (₹485 Cr, adjusted for prior property sale gain).

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Robust Order Book & Execution: Backlog at record ₹42,253 Cr (+6% YoY) signals strong revenue visibility. Q4 revenue growth led by Smart Infrastructure (+20% segment revenue) and Mobility (+29%), reflecting infra/mobility demand.
  • Govt Capex Momentum: Continued public spending on infrastructure; policy boosts (e.g., income tax cuts, GST reforms) driving festive consumption uptick, aiding industrial recovery.
  • Demerger Benefits: Energy business demerged to Siemens Energy India Ltd (SEIL), streamlining to high-growth segments (Smart Infra, Mobility, Digital Industries). Reduced balance sheet bloat (total assets down ~20% post-demerger to ₹188 Bn standalone).
  • Legal Win: Gujarat HC quashed ₹34.83 Cr GST SCN (Nov 26, 2025), avoiding interest/penalty; enhances cash flow confidence.
  • Financial Health: Strong liquidity (cash ₹16.7 Bn standalone), low debt (finance costs <₹50 Mn/Q), positive CFO (₹1 Bn standalone 12M). Dividend payout sustained.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Profit Pressure: PAT dip due to prior-year one-off property gain (₹69 Cr); EBITDA margins stable but demerger costs (₹201 Mn in Q4) and higher employee/other expenses weighed in.
  • Segment Disparity: Digital Industries flat/soft (-ve backlog carryover, muted private capex); Low Voltage Motors weak. Private sector slowdown caps overall growth.
  • Transitional FY Complexity: Shift to Apr-Mar FY (18M transitional to Mar 2026) creates non-comparable periods; procedural approvals pending, potential regulatory hiccups.
  • Working Capital Drag: Receivables/inventories up, leading to negative WC change (₹14.6 Bn consolidated), muting CFO growth vs. prior year.

Growth Prospects

  • High Visibility: Backlog supports 12-18M revenue growth (book-to-bill ~0.93x); Smart Infra/Mobility (60%+ revenue) poised for 15-20% CAGR on infra/rail/urbanization tailwinds.
  • Macro Recovery: Cautious optimism on private capex pickup (post-festive uptick); digital transformation/smart city push favors core segments. 12M revenue +8% YoY (continuing ops); full FY26 (post-transitional) could see 10-15% growth.
  • Strategic Focus: Post-demerger purity play on industry/infra/mobility; share buybacks/dividends signal capital return. EPS (continuing) ₹47.4 (12M), tracking 10%+ ROE.
  • Upside Catalysts: Govt budgets, export recovery, sustainability tech demand.

Overall Summary: Siemens exhibits resilient growth (revenue/orders up double-digits) amid demerger transition, with strong backlog/infra tailwinds offsetting private capex headwinds. PAT normalization post one-offs; prospects favor 10-15% revenue CAGR FY26+, driven by execution. Buy/Hold bias for long-term infra play; watch private capex revival. Risks tilted regulatory/transitional but low severity. (Data as of Nov 14/27, 2025 filings.)
