SJVN Limited (SJVN-EQ) Analysis: Headwinds, Tailwinds, Growth Prospects, and Key Risks

SJVN Limited, a Navratna CPSE focused on hydro, thermal, and renewables (capacity: ~3,146 MW operational; ~5,091 MW under construction), reported flat Q2/H1 FY26 results amid commissioning milestones but execution challenges. Below is a structured summary based on the Q2 FY26 earnings call transcript, financial results (standalone/consolidated for H1 FY26 ended Sep 30, 2025), board outcomes, AGM minutes, and other disclosures.

Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts)

  • Capacity Additions & Revenue Ramp-Up:
    • Buxar Thermal Unit-1 (660 MW) achieved full-load trial; COD imminent (within days); Unit-2 targeted Jan-Feb FY26. Annual generation: ~9,827 MU at ~₹5.4/unit (85% to Bihar).
    • Bikaner Solar: 680 MW COD phased; +320 MW by Dec 2025 → total capacity ~3,500 MW.
    • Upcoming: 650 MW solar (Jamui 75 MW, Assam 120 MW) in FY26; 1.5 GW solar in FY27.
  • Pipeline Strength: 14 solar (2,213 MW), 4 hydro (1,558 MW) under construction. New Nepal hydro (Lower Arun 680 MW, Upper Karnali 900 MW) tenders soon; PPA signed for Arun-III (900 MW).
  • Renewables Momentum: REIA status – tenders 19.8 GW, LOA 13.74 GW, PSA/PPA 3.74 GW (+3.75 GW soon). PM Surya Ghar (rooftop solar in 3 states; 21 MW awarded). Foundation laid for 200 MW Gujarat solar (₹867 Cr; COD Dec 2026; ₹2.88/unit).
  • Capex Execution: H1 FY26 capex ₹3,600 Cr (vs. FY26 target ₹7,500 Cr); FY27 guidance ₹8,000 Cr. Funds via ROE monetization (₹1,000 Cr from Nathpa Jhakri).
  • Operational: H1 FY26 generation +6.3% YoY (9,442 MU). Incentive income ₹104 Cr (H1).
  • Recognition: SCOPE HR Award; strong governance (new CFO, Secretarial Auditor).

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Revenue Pressure Despite Capacity: Q2 revenue flat (~₹1,000 Cr YoY) due to hydro floods (Nathpa Jhakri/Rampur; ₹50-60 Cr PAF loss), end of 80IA tax benefit (₹52 Cr hit), +finance cost/depreciation (Q2: ₹269 Cr/₹115 Cr vs. prior ₹166 Cr/₹116 Cr). H1 PAT ₹631 Cr (-21% YoY).
  • High Costs: Consolidated depreciation +26% YoY (₹331 Cr H1), finance cost +50% (₹531 Cr). Operating margin dipped to 61% (H1).
  • Debt Burden: Consolidated D/E 2.03 (standalone 0.70); total debt ₹29.5k Cr. ISCR 4.19 (H1); cash from ops strong (₹1,049 Cr) but capex heavy.
  • Execution Delays: RE PPA/PSA stalled (9 GW LOA pending) on grid connectivity/land issues (Rajasthan/Gujarat). Arunachal hydro (5 GW, e.g., Etalin 3,097 MW) – clearances/land near-complete but FY27 start.
  • Disputes: Himachal hydro (Luhri/Sunni/Dhaulasidh; ₹3,465 Cr capex) – GoHP renegotiation/takeover threat (court stay). Devasari on MoP hold (₹251 Cr spent).

Growth Prospects

  • Near-Term (FY26-27): Buxar full commissioning → ₹~5,000 Cr annual revenue add. Solar ramp (2 GW+). Total op. capacity >5 GW by FY27-end.
  • Medium-Term (FY27-30): Hydro CODs (Arun-III, Sunni, Luhri); Nepal projects (1.6 GW; attractive tariffs). REIA pipeline → 10 GW PSA soon (wind/BESS/pump storage tenders).
  • Strategic: RE focus aligns with 500 GW RE target (2030). Diversification (thermal/solar/Nepal). Capex FY26-28: ₹23k+ Cr → revenue/EBITDA growth 15-20% CAGR (est., post-Buxar).
  • Valuation Upside: PAT margins ~28-35%; ROE improvement post-CODs/debt shift from CWIP. Dividend yield ~2-3% (FY25: ₹1.46/share).

Key Risks

Risk Category Description Mitigation/Status
Execution Hydro delays (silt/floods, land/clearances); RE grid connectivity (10 GW stalled). Phased CODs on track; parallel tendering.
Financial High debt (D/E 2+); interest rate sensitivity; low ISCR if delays. Buxar cash flows to service; ROE monetization.
Regulatory CERC tariff provisional (FY24-29); Himachal disputes (court pending). Provisional billing; active resolution.
Operational Hydro hydrology (floods impacted Q2); Nepal forex/political. Diversified portfolio (RE/thermal buffer).
Market Low RE tariffs (₹2.6-2.88/unit); PPA delays/rebidding risk. Govt. push (REIA); long-term PSAs.
Others Project holds (Devasari); capex slippage. MoP/GoUK engagement.

Overall Outlook: SJVN is at an inflection with Buxar/solar CODs driving H2 FY26 recovery (revenue +20-30% est.). Tailwinds from RE/govt. mandates outweigh headwinds, but debt/execution risks cap upside. Stock likely range-bound near ₹100-130 until Q3 results/CODs confirm trajectory. Positive for long-term RE/hydro play. (Analysis as of Nov 2025 disclosures; not investment advice.)
