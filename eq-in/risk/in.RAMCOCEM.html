Summary Analysis for RAMCOCEM (The Ramco Cements Limited)

Financial Snapshot (Standalone, H1 FY26 ended Sep 30, 2025): - Revenue: ₹4,305 Cr (+4% YoY); Q2: ₹2,235 Cr (+10% YoY). - EBITDA: ₹394 Cr in Q2 (+22% YoY); ₹866/ton blended. - PAT: ₹160 Cr (+163% YoY); EPS: ₹6.78. - Net Debt: ₹4,591 Cr (stable YoY); Debt/Equity: 0.61x. - Capacity Utilization: 71% (down YoY due to expansions). - Auditors: Unmodified limited review opinion.

The company shows resilient profitability amid flat volumes, driven by pricing, cost efficiencies, and diversification. Below is a structured analysis of headwinds, tailwinds, growth prospects, and key risks.

Headwinds (Challenges Pressuring Performance)

  • Flat Cement Volumes: Q2 volumes steady at 4.4 MT YoY, impacted by early monsoon/excess rainfall and pre-GST rationalization slowdown. Overall sales volume up only 1% YoY.
  • Rising Input Costs: Raw material costs +10% YoY (₹903 to ₹995/ton), driven by TN’s Mineral Bearing Land Tax (MBT) at ₹160/ton limestone (₹40 Cr Q2 impact; unique to TN, under representation to govt.).
  • Lower Capacity Utilization: Down to 71% from 75% YoY due to 1.3 MTPA new capacities (de-bottlenecking) not yet fully ramped.
  • High Capex Strain: ₹601 Cr spent in H1 (FY26 est. ₹1,200 Cr), leading to slight net debt rise; depreciation up 8% YoY.

Tailwinds (Supportive Factors Boosting Momentum)

  • Strong Pricing & EBITDA Growth: Cement prices improved; EBITDA/ton up 20% to ₹866. Operating margin: 18% (vs. 16% YoY).
  • Cost Efficiencies: Power & fuel down 8% to ₹1,029/ton; green energy share up to 48% (from 39%) via record wind generation (100% owned). Blended fuel: $122/ton (vs. $130 YoY).
  • Diversification Success: Construction chemicals volume +72% YoY (0.86 to 1.48 LT); H1 revenue ₹165 Cr (FY25 full-year: ₹210 Cr).
  • Lower Borrowing Costs: Interest expense down 7% YoY to ₹111 Cr (Q2); blended cost 7.18% (vs. 7.96% YoY) due to repo rate cuts (100 bps since Feb 2025).
  • Asset Monetization: ₹501 Cr realized from non-core assets (50% of ₹1,000 Cr target); supports deleveraging.

Growth Prospects (High-Potential Opportunities)

  • Capacity Expansion: Targeting 30 MTPA by Mar-26 via Q4FY26 grinding unit (Kolimigundla Line-2) + de-bottlenecking (nominal capex).
  • Construction Chemicals Ramp-Up: New “Hard Worker” brand (20 products, incl. water-saving Eco Plaster); FY25 revenue ₹210 Cr → ₹2,000 Cr in 4-5 years using existing facilities + dealer network.
  • Green/Sustainable Initiatives: WHRS commissioning (10 MW RR Nagar; 15 MW Kolimigundla FY27); Karnataka greenfield (57% mining land acquired).
  • Windfarms Integration: Amalgamation of 100% subsidiary Ramco Windfarms (appointed date Apr 1, 2026) to consolidate assets, cut compliance costs, and carry forward losses.
  • Cash Flow Strength: Operating cash flow ₹646 Cr in H1 (up from ₹445 Cr YoY); supports internal funding.
