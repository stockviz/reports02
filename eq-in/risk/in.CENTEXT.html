Analysis of Century Extrusions Limited (CENTEXT)

Century Extrusions Limited (CIN: L27203WB1988PLC043705), engaged in manufacturing aluminium extruded products and transmission & distribution (T&D) line hardware, released unaudited Q2/H1FY26 results (ended Sep 30, 2025) on Nov 14, 2025. Key highlights: H1 revenue ~₹20,881 lacs (flat YoY), PAT ₹512 lacs (up ~8% YoY implied), EPS ₹0.65. Balance sheet expanded to ₹18,130 lacs (vs ₹17,083 lacs FY25-end), driven by capex and working capital. Cash flow positive from operations (₹833 lacs) but net cash down ₹288 lacs due to investments.

Tailwinds (Positive Factors)

  • Stable Revenue & Profitability: Q2 revenue ₹10,466 lacs (near-flat YoY vs ₹10,650 lacs); H1 PAT margins ~2.5% with sequential quarterly growth (Q2 PAT ₹276 lacs vs Q1 ₹236 lacs). PBT ₹671 lacs (up ~4% YoY).
  • Balance Sheet Strength: Equity up 6% to ₹8,796 lacs; reserves grew ₹499 lacs. Low non-current debt (₹75 lacs).
  • Operational Cash Generation: Strong CFO ₹833 lacs (up from ₹778 lacs YoY), supporting capex without excessive reliance on external funding.
  • Expansion Momentum: CWIP surged to ₹1,205 lacs (vs ₹426 lacs), PPE stable at ₹2,573 lacs; signals capacity addition in core segments.
  • Compliance & Governance: Clean limited review by auditors (Alps & Co.); routine Reg 74(5) certificate confirms demat compliance.

Headwinds (Negative Factors)

  • Revenue Stagnation: Flat YoY growth amid high base; no material other income visible (possible reporting glitch in P&L table).
  • Margin Pressure: High material costs (86-87% of revenue); finance costs up (H1 ₹569 lacs vs implied lower prior); EBITDA margin ~5-6% strained by depr (₹233 lacs) and forex/fair value adjustments.
  • Working Capital Drag: Inventories ballooned 24% to ₹5,009 lacs; short-term borrowings up 6% to ₹5,018 lacs. Trade receivables down but still ₹2,903 lacs (~1 month sales).
  • Cash Outflow: Net cash decline ₹288 lacs; capex ₹867 lacs outpacing ops cash in investing activities.

Growth Prospects

  • Sector Tailwinds: Aluminium extrusions benefits from India’s infra/realty boom (housing, EVs); T&D hardware rides power capex (₹7+ lakh cr govt. push). Non-reportable segments but diversified.
  • Capex Cycle: ₹779 lacs CWIP increase positions for volume ramp-up; potential 10-15% revenue growth in H2FY26 if execution smooth.
  • Profit Leverage: Ind AS 115 adoption added ₹69 lacs profit; cost controls (employee/other expenses flat) could lift margins to 4-5% with scale.
  • Market Position: Listed on BSE/NSE; steady EPS (₹0.35/Q2); ROE ~12% annualized supports re-rating if growth accelerates.

Key Risks

  • Commodity Volatility: Aluminium prices sensitive to global cues (LME); raw material costs >85% of sales exposes to input inflation.
  • Liquidity & Debt: Working capital loans rising; interest coverage ~2.2x (manageable but tight if sales dip).
  • Execution/Operational: High inventories signal demand softness or stocking; capex delays could impair returns.
  • Macro/Economic: Infra slowdown, competition from unorganized players, or monsoon impacts on T&D.
  • Regulatory: SEBI compliance routine but forex/debt covenants unmentioned; no exceptional items but watch for impairments.

Overall Summary: CENTEXT shows resilience with flat-topline but improving bottomline and capex-driven growth setup in high-potential sectors (aluminium/T&D). Tailwinds from ops cash and equity base outweigh headwinds like stagnant sales and WC strain. Growth prospects solid (10-20% FY26 upside if capex yields), but risks from commodities/WC warrant monitoring. Neutral-positive outlook; suitable for tactical longs on dips, target P/B ~1.2x (current implied ~1x). Full FY25/peer comps needed for deeper valuation.
