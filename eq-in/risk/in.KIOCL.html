KIOCL Limited Analysis (Based on Q2/H1 FY26 Results & AGM Filings)

KIOCL, a Govt of India PSU in iron ore pelletization & pig iron, reported H1 FY26 revenue growth but continued losses (narrowed YoY). Core plants underutilized; revenue skewed to O&M contracts. Strong liquidity (₹69Cr cash/bank balances) offsets negative cash flows. Balance sheet stable at ~₹2,208Cr assets.

Headwinds (Key Challenges)

  • Persistent Losses & Margin Pressure: H1 FY26 pre-tax loss ₹55.6Cr (vs. ₹118Cr YoY); Q2 loss ₹17.2Cr. High opex (power ₹130Cr, employees ₹71Cr) amid low core revenue.
  • Weak Core Operations: Pellet revenue crashed to ₹14.7Cr (vs. ₹162Cr YoY); Pig Iron negligible. Segment losses: Pellet ₹99Cr, Pig Iron ₹7.6Cr.
  • Cash Flow Strain: Operating cash outflow ₹16.6Cr; total cash equivalents dropped 99% to ₹3.2Cr (invested in term deposits/MFs).
  • Governance Issues: No Independent Directors/Audit Committee (auditor emphasis); delays in govt nominations.
  • High CWIP/Capex Drag: ₹174Cr CWIP; intangible assets ₹540Cr (likely land/mining rights) yielding no revenue yet.

Tailwinds (Positive Factors)

  • Revenue Momentum: Ops revenue +43% YoY to ₹233Cr (Q2 ₹142Cr). O&M Kaliapani contract drove ₹219Cr (profitable net ₹23Cr).
  • Loss Improvement: PAT loss halved YoY; treasury income strong at ₹29Cr (H1).
  • Robust Liquidity: Bank balances ₹687Cr; negligible debt (only leases ₹18Cr).
  • PSU Support: Govt backing evident in AGM (100% resolutions passed); stable equity base ₹608Cr.
  • Cost Discipline: ECL write-back, inventory reduction aided working capital.

Growth Prospects

  • O&M Expansion: Kaliapani ramp-up (Q2 ₹139Cr vs. FY25 full ₹107Cr); potential for more service contracts in mining/steel sector.
  • Plant Revival: Pellet capacity utilization could rebound (FY25: ₹484Cr); Pig Iron revival amid infra/steel demand (India’s steel prod. growth ~8% CAGR).
  • Capex Monetization: CWIP/intangibles signal Bolani/V.V. Puram projects; govt push for self-reliance in pellets.
  • Treasury Leverage: ₹690Cr liquid assets yield steady income; FY26 revenue could hit ₹100-120Cr+ if plants operationalize.
  • Sector Tailwinds: Iron ore/pellet demand from steel majors (e.g., NMDC integration); export potential.

Key Risks

Financial Negative FCF (H1 outflow ₹136Cr); liquidity erosion if losses persist. High deposits; no borrowings.

Overall Outlook: Short-term defensive (loss-making, cash burn); medium-term positive if core plants revive (target FY26 revenue ₹100Cr+, breakeven). Hold for PSU revival theme; monitor Q3 ops ramp-up. Valuation: Trading at ~1x book (equity ₹166Cr); undervalued if growth materializes.
