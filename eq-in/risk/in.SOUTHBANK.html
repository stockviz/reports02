Analysis of South Indian Bank Ltd. (SOUTHBANK) – Q2 FY26 (Sep 2025) Update

South Indian Bank (SIB) reported a record quarterly net profit of ₹351 Cr (+8% YoY), driven by robust deposit growth, asset quality turnaround, and non-interest income surge (+26% YoY). Gross advances grew 9% YoY to ₹92,286 Cr, with diversification into retail/MSME (60% of book). However, NIM compressed to 2.80% amid rising borrowings. Balance sheet remains strong (CRAR 17.70%, RoA 1.02%, RoE 13.11%). Below is a structured analysis based on the filings (investor presentation, financial results, ESOP, conference disclosure, press release).

Tailwinds (Positive Momentum)

  • Asset Quality Turnaround: GNPA down 147 bps YoY to 2.93%; NNPA down 75 bps to 0.56%. PCR (excl. write-offs) at 81.29% (+1,005 bps YoY); incl. write-offs at 90.25% (+952 bps). 86% of current GNPA from “old book” (pre-2020 churned 83% of loans). Slippages low at 0.21% (vs. 0.36% YoY).
  • Deposit Franchise Strength: Deposits +10% YoY to ₹115,635 Cr; retail deposits +11% to ₹112,625 Cr. CASA stable at 31.86%; NRI deposits +9% to ₹33,195 Cr (low-cost focus). Cost of deposits stable at 5.41%.
  • Diversified & Granular Growth: Retail/MSME advances ~60% of book; housing (+25%), gold (+13%), vehicle (+25%) loans surging. A+ rated corporates up to 67% of corporate book. Avg. MSME ticket ~₹52 lakh (granular).
  • Operational Efficiency: Branch value added +31% YoY (indexed); digital transactions 98.4% of total (+ve trend). Cost-to-income 59.6% (stable). Multiple STP digital platforms live (e.g., GST Power, Gold Xpress).
  • Capital & Liquidity: CRAR 17.70% (well above 11.5% regulatory); low RWA density (40.49%). ESOP grant (2.27 Cr options) aligns incentives without major dilution.
  • Non-Interest Income Boost: +26% YoY to ₹516 Cr (treasury/forex +21%, core fees +9%).

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Margin Pressure: NIM down 44 bps YoY to 2.80% (Q2FY26 vs. 3.24%); yield on advances dipped to 8.56%. Higher borrowings (₹6,648 Cr, +155% YoY) signal liquidity reliance amid advance-deposit mismatch.
  • Provision Volatility: Q2 provisions low (₹63 Cr, -43% YoY), but H1 at ₹303 Cr (+36% YoY). Potential for uptick if slippages rise.
  • Moderate Advance Growth: +9% YoY lags deposits (+10%); corporate shift ongoing but legacy issues persist (e.g., write-offs of ₹546 Cr in FY25).
  • Geographic Concentration: ~38% MSME/Kerala exposure; diversification to pan-India (948 branches) progressing but early-stage.
  • Operating Costs: +6% YoY; employee costs stable but outsourcing/digital investments weigh on efficiency.

Growth Prospects (High Potential)

  • Retail/MSME Ramp-Up: Target sustained 10-15% growth via co-lending (e.g., Axis MF, Moneyview), sector lending, and new products (Gold Xpress, GST Power up to ₹5 Cr). Retail disbursements strong; housing/gold momentum to continue.
  • Digital/Non-Branch Scale: 10+ new STP LOS live (e.g., LAP Power, Edu Power); UPI-GST integration, NRI onboarding via SWIFTe. Digital share ~98%; partnerships (e.g., Muthoot Fincorp) to boost non-branch (target 20-30% mix).
  • Efficiency Gains: Branch productivity +31-69% (region-wise); headcount optimization (9,242 employees). Vision 2025: 6Cs (Capital, CASA, Cost-income, Competency, Customer, Compliance).
  • Profitability Trajectory: RoE >13%; NIM stabilization via low-cost NRI/CASA. FY26 guidance implicit: 12-15% advance/deposit growth, sub-3% GNPA.
  • Capital Flexibility: Strong CRAR supports 15-20% RoE; ESOP for talent retention.

Projected Metrics (Inferred): Advances ~₹2.1-2.2 Tn by FY27; NIM 3%+; RoA 1.1-1.2%; EPS ₹10+ (from FY25 ₹4.98).

Key Risks

  • Asset Quality Reversal: Economic slowdown/MSME stress could elevate slippages (monitor Ever 30+ at 6 MOB ~0.56%). Legacy “old book” (15% GNPA contrib.) runoff critical.
  • NIM/Rate Risk: Persistent compression if deposit costs rise (5.41%) or MCLR lags; unhedged forex exposure provisioning.
  • Liquidity/Concentration: Borrowings surge (285% QoQ); Kerala/South exposure (38-39%); NRI reliance (29% deposits).
  • Regulatory/Competition: RBI norms (e.g., Basel III NSFR/LCR), ESOP dilution (~1-2% equity), co-lending caps. Intense private bank competition in retail/digital.
  • Execution/Operational: Digital rollout risks (cybersecurity via new CIO); branch migration (948 touchpoints).
  • Macro: Inflation, monsoon impact on agri/MSME (17% book); geopolitical effects on NRI flows.

Summary

Bullish Outlook with Quality Focus: SIB’s transformation shines – record profits, pristine asset quality (best-in-class PCR/NNPA), and retail pivot position it for 12-15% CAGR in advances/profits. Tailwinds from deposits/digital outweigh NIM headwinds; CRAR buffers growth. Buy/Accumulate for 20-25% upside (target P/B 1.0-1.1x FY27 BV ₹45+). Risks mitigated by strong mgmt (avg. 30+ yrs exp.) and 83% loan churn since 2020. Monitor Q3 NIM/slippage for confirmation. (Ratings: Tailwinds 8/10, Growth 8/10, Risks 6/10).
