Yatharth Hospital & Trauma Care Services Ltd (YATHARTH) Analysis

Overview: Yatharth Hospitals, a North India-focused super-specialty chain (NCR-heavy), reported record Q2 FY26 results (Sep 2025 quarter): Revenue +28% YoY to ₹2,794 Cr, EBITDA +18% YoY to ₹645 Cr (adj. +35% to ₹737 Cr, 26.7% margin), PAT +33% YoY to ₹413 Cr. Operational beds at 2,305 (ex-Agra), occupancy 66%, ARPOB ₹32k (+4% YoY). Debt-free with ₹3.7 Bn net cash. Recent expansions (Delhi/Model Town: 300 beds; Faridabad Sec-20: 400 beds) and Agra acquisition (250 beds, EBITDA+ from Q4). Strong governance (new BDO auditor, CRISIL A/Stable rating).

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Robust Demand & Execution: New hospitals drove 110% YoY revenue growth; mature ones +19%. Greater Faridabad (1-yr old) at 10% group revenue. Occupancy stable at 66% despite +700 beds.
  • Expansion Momentum: Agra integration (Q4 FY26, EBITDA accretive); brownfield (Greater Noida/Noida Ext.); targeting 3,000+ beds by FY28 (potentially double in 3-4 yrs via ₹1,500 Cr capex over 4.5 yrs).
  • ARPOB/Mix Improvement: +4-19% YoY across segments; super-specialties at 18-56% revenue (Noida Ext: ₹40.8k ARPOB). CGHS revision: +1.5-2.5% revenue uplift FY26/FY27.
  • Financial Strength: Net cash ₹3.7 Bn funds growth; ROCE 19%; debtor days improving (target further reduction).
  • Governance/Visibility: Assets unfrozen (tax issue resolving); MSCI Small Cap inclusion; clinical feats (robotic surgeries, AI stroke tech); MVT push (offices in Baghdad/Tashkent, Jewar Airport tie-up).
  • MVT/International: 45-55% ARPOB premium; 5-6 info centers FY26.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Margin Pressure from Ramps: New Delhi/Faridabad dragged EBITDA by 3.3% (initial losses); FY26 margins ~24% (H1 at 24%).
  • Working Capital: Receivables up (debtor days stretched); OCF/EBITDA at 58% (adj. 78% post salary policy change).
  • Cost Inflation: Employee/doctor costs (21.5% revenue); ALOS decline (4.8 to 4.1 days) from tech but ARPOB growth muted in some units.
  • Govt Payor Mix: ~37% (CGHS/Ayushman); Jhansi drag on ARPOB (half group avg).

Growth Prospects

  • High Visibility: FY26 revenue >30% (conservative); ARPOB +8-10% annually to ₹40k by FY28. H1 FY26 +25% sets strong base.
  • Capacity Leverage: 1,000 beds added FY26 (3 new sites); ramps in 15-17 months to breakeven (30-35% occupancy). Agra quick ramp (existing super-specialties).
  • Strategic Focus: Super-specialties (oncology/transplants up); NCR metro bias (cutoff ARPOB >₹35k); MVT/international ramp (H1 FY27 contribution).
  • Catalysts: CGHS benefits; GST cut on consumables (+0.3-0.5% EBITDA); brownfield (Noida Ext/Gr. Noida +15-17 months).
  • Long-Term: Double beds in 3-4 yrs; sustained 25-30% revenue CAGR feasible with cash accruals.

Key Risks

Risk Category Description Mitigation
Operational New hospital ramps (15-17 mo to breakeven); occupancy/ARPOB delays (Delhi at 13-14%). Proven track record; 30-35% occupancy breakeven.
Financial Margin dilution (new beds); capex execution (₹1,500 Cr); WC stretch. Debt-free; internal accruals; debtor improvement.
Regulatory Ongoing tax issue (resolution by FY26-end); govt payor risks (37% mix). Assets unfrozen; cooperating with authorities.
Competitive/Market NCR saturation; doctor retention (21.5% costs). Super-specialty focus; clinical excellence (JCI/NABH).
External Reimbursement changes; MVT execution; economic slowdown. Diversified payors; international upside.

Overall Outlook: Strong tailwinds from expansions/governance outweigh headwinds. High growth (30%+ FY26) with improving margins post-ramps. Buy/hold for long-term compounding; monitor new hospital traction and tax closure. Target multiple: 25-30x FY27E EV/EBITDA.
