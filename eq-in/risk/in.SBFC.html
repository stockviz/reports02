SBFC Finance Limited (NSE/BSE: SBFC) Analysis

Overview: SBFC is a secured MSME-focused NBFC (82% AUM in MSME loans ₹5-30L ticket size, 100% secured by property/gold). Q2 FY26 (Sep’25) results show resilient growth amid sector stress: AUM ₹9,938 Cr (+29% YoY, +6% QoQ), PAT ₹109 Cr (+30% YoY), RoA 4.56%, RoE 14.09%. Guidance intact at 5-7% QoQ AUM growth despite credit tightening.

Tailwinds

  • Improving Margins & Efficiency: Spreads at 9.05% (+68 bps YoY), yield 18.01%, borrowing cost down 36 bps YoY to 8.96% (repo rate cuts aiding transmission). OPEX/AAUM at 4.40% (-112 bps since IPO), operating leverage from branch maturation (60% branches >3 yrs at ₹60-65 Cr AUM/branch).
  • Strong Fundamentals: Capital adequacy 34%, TNW ₹3,174 Cr, low leverage (D/E <2x, room to 3-4x). 100% secured book (LTV ~42-46%), PCR 46% (>40% target). Co-origination (15% disbursals, 19% AUM) with ICICI validates quality.
  • Pan-India Scale: 220 branches (16 states/2 UTs), adding 20-25 branches/Yr + gold loans (185 branches → +15 soon). Avg ticket up to ₹10.2L.
  • Macro Support: MSME TAM ₹4 Tn (+24% CAGR, doubling ~every 3 yrs); low inflation/volatility favors stable rates/growth.

Headwinds

  • Credit Stress: GNPA stable at 2.77% but 1+DPD elevated/rising (intensity calming QoQ); credit cost 1.29% (may +10-15 bps). Legacy issues in <₹7L tickets (stopped), Karnataka (11-12% exposure, ordinance/political hit post-Jan’25).
  • Slowing Disbursals: Tightened underwriting (CIBIL >700 now 88% AUM/96% new; rejection >60%) led to -6% QoQ disbursals (₹815 Cr). Volumes prioritized over growth.
  • OPEX Run-Rate: Employee costs up 10% YoY despite 30% revenue growth; moderating but investments cap near-term compression.

Growth Prospects

  • AUM Trajectory: 5-7% QoQ (25-30% FY26) via higher tickets (₹7-30L), branch sweat (capacity for ₹3,200 Cr annual disbursals at 20%+ YoY), gold/LAP ramp-up. Hit ₹10,000 Cr milestone.
  • Profitability Upside: Leverage to 3x → higher RoE (guided 15%). Tailwinds (cheaper incremental borrowing, OPEX leverage) offset modest credit cost rise; PAT doubled since listing.
  • Medium-Term: MSME market to ₹8 Tn in 4 yrs; distribution expansion in underserved states; no new products needed. ESOP pool (1.21 Cr options) to aid talent retention.

Key Risks

Risk Category Details Mitigants
Credit/Asset Quality Elevated slippages (small tickets, Karnataka); over-levered customers; longer small-ticket recoveries (LGD <50%, but 2x SARFAESI timeline). Tight screens (no <₹7L, CIBIL 700+); 100% secured; in-house collections (99% digital).
Regulatory/Execution RBI scrutiny spillover (unsecured/MFI); NBFC SARFAESI limits (>₹20L); rejection-led growth miss. Conservative growth; co-origination validation; AA- rating.
Macro/External Political (e.g., Karnataka ordinance), climate risks; rate volatility/customer leverage from unsecured boom. Diversified geography (no state >15%); essential services focus.
Operational Key resignations (Head-Internal Audit/Credit); ESOP dilution (~10% of equity). Quick replacements; strong mgmt (cycle-tested).
Liquidity ALM matched (90% floating); diversified borrowings (banks 60%+). Surplus in short-term buckets; strong CAR.

Overall Outlook: Resilient performer in stressed MSME space (growth + profitability amid peers’ slowdown). Tailwinds from efficiency/margins outweigh near-term credit headwinds; 25-30% FY26 growth achievable with risk controls. Monitor Karnataka/1+DPD quarterly. Buy/Hold for long-term MSME play (target RoE 15%+).
