Summary Analysis: Nila Spaces Limited (NILASPACES) - Q2 & H1 FY26 Financials

Nila Spaces Limited, a real estate developer focused on construction and development of buildings (single operating segment), reported robust standalone revenue growth of 32% YoY to ₹8,302L and PBT growth of 94% to ₹1,775L in H1 FY26, with consolidated figures showing 30% revenue growth and 81% PAT growth to ₹1,132L (attributable to owners). Key positives include strong execution momentum and cash build-up, but rising debt and inventory levels signal leverage risks amid real estate sector dynamics.

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Strong Operational Momentum: Revenue from operations grew 32% YoY standalone (₹8,302L vs. ₹6,302L), driven by project expenses (up 72% YoY) and positive changes in inventories, indicating healthy project progression. Q-o-Q stability (Q2 ₹4,218L vs. Q1 ₹4,083L).
  • Profitability Surge: Standalone PAT up 95% YoY to ₹1,256L (EPS ₹0.32 vs. ₹0.16); consolidated PAT up 81%. Other income doubled YoY, boosted by interest/ investments.
  • Liquidity Improvement: Standalone cash & equivalents jumped to ₹3,579L (+282% QoQ), supported by ₹6,232L financing inflows and positive operating CF (₹539L). Consolidated cash at ₹4,333L.
  • Asset Growth: Total assets up 25% YoY standalone (to ₹36,233L), with investments (e.g., mutual funds ₹2,511L) and inventories signaling project pipeline execution.
  • Sector Support: Indian real estate tailwinds (urbanization, housing demand, govt. schemes like PMAY) favor developers like Nila Spaces.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Rising Leverage: Non-current borrowings surged to ₹7,106L standalone (from ₹3L) and ₹8,367L consolidated (from ₹1,264L), driving finance costs up 53% YoY to ₹1,052L standalone. Debt/equity ratio deteriorated sharply.
  • Inventory Pile-Up: Standalone inventories +15% to ₹11,863L; consolidated +14% to ₹22,544L, tying up capital and signaling execution delays or slower sales.
  • Cash Flow Pressure (Consolidated): Operating CF negative at -₹283L (improved from -₹2,672L YoY but reliant on working capital swings like -₹3,051L drop in other current liabilities).
  • Margin Variability: Gross margins appear pressured by higher project costs (standalone cost of materials +72% YoY); other expenses up 62%.
  • Subsidiary Drag: Non-controlling interest losses (₹14L in H1) from subsidiary (Nila Urban Living) slightly offset group profits.

Growth Prospects

  • High (Medium-Term): Inventory build-up (₹22.5B consolidated) positions company for revenue recognition in H2 FY26/FY27 as projects near completion. Revenue trajectory (FY25: ₹13.6B → H1 FY26: ₹8.3B annualized ~₹16.6B) suggests 20-25% FY26 growth potential.
  • Expansion Signals: Capex on PPE (₹693L consolidated outflow) and new borrowings indicate project scaling. Single-segment focus on real estate aids efficiency.
  • Upside Catalysts: Improving cash position enables debt servicing; potential for stake sales or JV monetization (e.g., associate Megacity Cinemall nil contribution). Sector growth (India RE expected 10-15% CAGR) supports 15-20% revenue CAGR over 2-3 years if execution holds.
  • Base Case: PAT could double FY26 vs. FY25 if debt costs stabilize and sales accelerate.
