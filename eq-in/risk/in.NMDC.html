NMDC Limited (NSE: NMDC) Analysis

Overview: NMDC, India’s largest iron ore producer (Govt-owned ~61%), reported stellar H1 FY26 (Apr-Sep 2025) results with record production/sales driven by operational ramp-up in Chhattisgarh/Karnataka mines. Iron ore remains ~95% of revenue; avg realization ₹5,170/T (H1, +2% YoY). Latest prices (w.e.f. Nov 15, 2025): Lump ₹5,600/T, Fines ₹4,750/T (FOR). PAT ₹3,663 Cr (+13% YoY); ROE implied strong at ~12-15% annualized.

Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts)

  • Operational Excellence: Record Q2/H1 production (102/222 LT, +23%/27% YoY) & sales (107/222 LT, +10%/12% YoY); beat prior peaks (e.g., H1 sales prior best 205 LT). Oct’25 prod/sales: 4.27/3.89 MT (+5%/+3% YoY).
  • Financial Strength: Revenue +27% YoY (₹12,895 Cr); EBITDA margin 40%; PAT +13% (₹3,663 Cr). Cash-rich (bank balances ₹10,360 Cr); low debt (borrowings ~₹3,134 Cr current).
  • Realization Support: +2% YoY avg price; pellet fines job work (13.7 LT H1) adds diversification.
  • Stable Ownership: Govt (60.8%), LIC (5.3%); AGM resolutions passed overwhelmingly (e.g., dividends, directors).
  • Steel Demand: India steel capex (e.g., NSL/SAIL tie-ups) aids offtake.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Cost Pressures: OpEx +57% YoY (H1); royalty/levies +29% (₹4,857 Cr). Q2 realization dipped 7% QoQ to ₹4,973/T.
  • Receivables Drag: ₹3,831 Cr from RINL (stressed, revival package ₹11,440 Cr approved); NSL dues ₹5,944 Cr (incl. demerger trust). ECL provisions taken (₹106 Cr).
  • Contingent Liabilities: Karnataka tax bill (₹14,749 Cr retrospective, pending Presidential assent); RTP penalty (₹1,621 Cr); compensation demand (₹1,623 Cr) – all sub-judice.
  • Subsidiary Exposure: KVSL land advance ₹640 Cr stalled; Legacy Iron Ore (AUS) exploration-stage.

Growth Prospects

  • Volume-Led Expansion: Targeting sustained 20-25% YoY growth; H1 already best-ever. New clearances (e.g., Deposit-13 extension to Sep’26) + subs/JVs (₹1,303 Cr invested) unlock capacity.
  • Diversification: Pellets/other minerals +2% revenue share; ICVL coal, Legacy magnetite JV with Hancock.
  • Capex Momentum: CWIP ₹5,309 Cr (+41% YoY); rail/infra JVs (BRPL).
  • Outlook: FY26 guidance implicit strong (Q2 best-ever PAT ₹1,694 Cr +33% YoY); steel demand +7-8% aids pricing. Potential 15-20% PAT CAGR if volumes hit 50+ LT annually.
