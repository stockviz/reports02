Analysis of Kothari Petrochemicals Limited (KOTHARIPET)

Kothari Petrochemicals Ltd., a single-segment manufacturer of Poly Iso Butylene (PIB), reported solid YoY financial performance for H1 FY26 (ended Sep 30, 2025), with revenue up 3.6% to ₹303 Cr and net profit up 0.8% to ₹36 Cr (EPS ₹6.12). Balance sheet strengthened (equity up 9.7% to ₹336 Cr), driven by retained earnings and treasury investments, though cash dipped due to capex and dividends. Operating cash flow robust at ₹48 Cr (vs ₹31 Cr YoY). Here’s a breakdown:

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Revenue & Profit Momentum: H1 revenue +3.6% YoY; PBT +12.8% to ₹47 Cr (margins expanded to 15.6% from 14.3%). Q2 profit held steady despite QoQ revenue dip.
  • Operational Efficiency: Strong cost control (e.g., utilities/stores down 20% YoY H1); EBITDA-like profit before exc/tax at ₹48 Cr (up YoY). Exceptional write-off minor (₹0.26 Cr).
  • Balance Sheet Strength: Equity surged 9.7% on profits; investments ballooned to ₹8.1 Cr (+130% non-current), signaling prudent cash deployment. Net debt near-zero (low finance costs ₹0.4 Cr H1).
  • Cash Generation: Op. cash flow +58% YoY to ₹48 Cr, funding capex/dividends internally.
  • Segment Focus: Monopoly-like position in PIB (adhesives, lubricants demand tied to industrials/auto recovery).

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • QoQ Weakness: Q2 revenue -11% QoQ (₹143 Cr vs ₹161 Cr); materials cost -10% but inventories built up (+3%).
  • Cash Outflow: Net cash -₹3 Cr H1 (vs +₹12 Cr YoY); investing drag from ₹40 Cr net investment buys + capex (CWIP +135% to ₹4.6 Cr).
  • Margin Pressures: Employee costs +17% YoY; other expenses flat but high at 8% of revenue.
  • Receivables Cycle: Trade receivables down 19% QoQ (good), but still ₹42 Cr (3-4 months sales).
  • Dividend Payout: ₹5.9 Cr paid, reducing liquidity (cash -24% to ₹10.5 Cr).

Growth Prospects

  • High: Strong industrials tailwind (PIB demand from tyres, sealants); FY25 full-year revenue ₹577 Cr sets base for 10-15% growth if Q3/Q4 sustains H1 trajectory.
  • Capex Ramp: ₹5 Cr plant/equip spend + CWIP signals capacity expansion (PPE stable at ₹190 Cr).
  • Treasury Yield: Investment gains (₹1.2 Cr H1) + rental income (₹1.2 Cr) boost non-op income (down YoY but scalable).
  • ROE Potential: 11% H1 ROE (annualized ~22%); low leverage enables buybacks/reinvestment.
  • Outlook: 12-18 month growth to ₹650-700 Cr revenue if petrochemical cycle holds (India chem mkt +8-10% CAGR).
