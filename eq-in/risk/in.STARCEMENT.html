Analysis of Star Cement Limited (STARCEMENT) – Headwinds, Tailwinds, Growth Prospects, and Key Risks

Star Cement, a leading NE India cement player (26% market share), reported robust Q2/H1 FY26 results amid expansions. Below is a structured summary based on earnings transcripts, financials, investor presentation, and announcements (as of Nov 2025). FY26 guidance implies sustained EBITDA/ton of ₹1500-1550, with volumes targeting industry growth.

Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts)

  • Strong Operational Metrics: Q2 volumes +20% YoY (10.73 LT cement), EBITDA/ton ₹1650 (+66% YoY), fuel cost at industry-low ₹1.25/kcal (80% FSA-linked, sustainable 5-6 months). H1 EBITDA ₹424 Cr (+97% YoY).
  • Robust NE Demand: 8-9% growth (above national avg.), infra-led (hydro/dams in Arunachal). Non-NE sales +45% YoY (3.09 LT).
  • Policy Incentives: Bihar’s 300% SGST refund (₹400-500/ton benefit); NE incentives intact (₹180-190 Cr FY26 est., despite GST cut lag).
  • Cost Efficiencies: TSR 21.5%, green energy 30.9% (target 55% FY27), WHRS 25% power mix.
  • Distribution Strength: 2,000+ dealers, 13,300 retailers; premium sales 13.1% (up QoQ).

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Incentive Erosion: GST cut (28%→18%) reduces SGST reimbursement by ₹130-150/ton; Q2 booking ₹56 Cr, but quantum stretched over 15 years.
  • Pricing Pressure: NE prices stable (~₹493/ton post-Q2), but Bihar/WB down ₹5-7 QoQ; competition intensifying.
  • Logistics Costs: Lead distance 230 km (up QoQ), rail coeff. 9% (up); non-trade mix (20%) drags efficiency.
  • Shutdown Impact: Q2 EBITDA/ton down ₹100 due to annual maintenance (recurring).
  • Demand Deferral: Tepid Sep volumes ahead of GST cut; Q4 election risks in key states.
