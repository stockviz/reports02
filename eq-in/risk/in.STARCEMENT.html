Star Cement Limited (STARCEMENT) Analysis

Overview: Star Cement, a leading cement producer in Northeast India (26% market share), reported robust Q2 FY26 results (ended Sep 2025): cement/clinker sales up 20% YoY to 11.73 LT, revenue ₹811 Cr (+26%), EBITDA ₹194 Cr (+99%, ₹1,650/ton), PAT ₹71 Cr. Half-year PAT ₹159 Cr. Company is aggressively expanding capacities amid strong NE demand (8-9% growth), low costs, and incentives, with shareholders approving ₹1,500 Cr fundraising.

Tailwinds (Positive Factors)

  • Strong Regional Dominance & Demand: 71% sales from NE (7.64 LT in Q2, +2% YoY); robust infra (dams, hydro projects). Non-NE sales +45% YoY (3.09 LT). October demand healthy.
  • Operational Efficiency: Fuel cost at ₹1.25/kcal (lowest in industry, 80% FSA; sustainable 5-6 months). TSR 21.5%, green energy 30.9% (WHRS 25%). Lead distance 230 km, rail mix 9%.
  • Incentives & Premiumization: Q2 incentive ₹56 Cr (FY26 est. ₹180-190 Cr); Bihar’s 300% SGST benefit (₹400-500/ton margin boost). Premium sales 13.1% of trade (up from 10.6%).
  • Diversification: AAC blocks ramping (60% util., ₹13-14 Cr Q2 rev; FY26 ₹50-60 Cr, FY27 ₹80-90 Cr). New construction chemicals launched.
  • Fundraising Approved: Postal ballot passed for ₹1,500 Cr equity raise (QIP/preferential) to fund expansions.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • GST Rate Cut Impact: Incentives down ₹130-150/ton due to SGST reduction (28% to 18%); accruals stretched over 15 years (time lag, not quantum loss).
  • Demand Volatility: Sep sales tepid due to GST anticipation; Q4 FY26 risk from state elections.
  • Logistics & Mix: Non-trade 20% (up), lead distance up to 231 km; rail coeff. 9.6%.
  • Shutdown Costs: Q2 EBITDA/ton down ₹100 due to annual maintenance (₹13-14 Cr).

Growth Prospects

  • Near-Term (FY26): Silchar 2 MTPA commissioning by Jan 2026 → total capacity 9.7 MTPA cement (6.1 MTPA clinker). FY26 CAPEX ₹720 Cr (Silchar + 42 MW solar). Volume +12% FY27. EBITDA/ton guidance ₹1,500-1,550.
  • Medium-Term (FY27-30): Bihar 2 MTPA grinding (₹500 Cr CAPEX, FY27 priority, clinker via rail from Lumshnong/Silchar). Rajasthan entry: Nimbol (3 MTPA clinker + 4 MTPA grinding, ₹2,400-2,500 Cr), Barwala 2-2.5 MTPA, Jaisalmer reserves. Umrangso clinker + Jorhat grinding (~FY28-29). Target 18-20 MTPA by FY30.
  • Long-Term: Forward integration (AAC, chemicals), ESG (55% green energy FY27), 84% blended cement. ROE sales growth via Siliguri ramp-up.

Key Risks

  • Competition: Bihar (JK Cement, pricing tailspin); NE (Ambuja greenfield in 2-3 years, Dalmia ramp-up). 18% NE imports could rise.
  • Execution Delays: Greenfield expansions (land in Bihar/Rajasthan, permissions for Umrangso). CAPEX inflation (Rajasthan ₹2,400-2,500 Cr for 4 MTPA).
  • Cost Volatility: Fuel post 5-6 months (stock at ₹1.25-1.3/kcal); power/logistics.
  • Macro/Regulatory: Election-led Q4 slowdown; incentive policy changes; monsoon/infra delays.
  • Financial: Debt up with expansions (FY26 borrowings +₹10-15k Cr); forex on imports.
  • Other: Terrain challenges in NE/Rajasthan; overcapacity if demand lags (Bihar growing but competitive).

Summary: Star Cement is well-positioned for 2x capacity growth (9.7 → 18-20 MTPA by FY30) driven by NE leadership, low costs, and strategic expansions (Bihar/Rajasthan). Tailwinds from demand/incentives outweigh headwinds like GST cuts/competition. FY26 EBITDA could exceed guidance if demand sustains. Buy/Accumulate for long-term; monitor execution and fuel prices. Target upside 20-30% from expansions. (Valuation: EV/EBITDA ~10-12x FY26 est., peers 12-15x).
