Analysis of Andhra Paper Limited (ANDHRAPAP)

Overview: Andhra Paper Limited, a manufacturer of pulp, paper, and paperboards (single reportable segment under Ind AS 108), released unaudited H1 FY26 results (Apr-Sep 2025) showing flat revenue (₹755 Cr, +1% YoY), sharp PAT decline to ₹10 Mn (vs. ₹694 Cr YoY) due to Q2 loss (₹20 Cr) from a brief worker strike (Jul 6-12) and planned outage (Jul 13-26). Balance sheet remains strong with ₹65 Cr current investments and debt reduction. Operating cash flow robust at ₹64 Cr. Auditor (MSKA & Associates) issued unmodified limited review. Postal ballot approved two new Independent Directors.

Headwinds (Challenges Pressuring Performance)

  • Operational Disruptions: Q2 hit by 1-day strike and 14-day outage, causing revenue drop (₹362 Cr vs. ₹433 Cr YoY) and EBITDA loss; exposes vulnerability to labor issues in single-plant ops (Rajahmundry).
  • Cost Pressures: Expenses up 11% YoY to ₹791 Cr (materials +11%, depreciation +26% from capex); power/fuel flat but high at ₹62 Cr; margins compressed (PBT 0.2% vs. 12.5% YoY).
  • Revenue Stagnation: H1 sales flat despite inventory drawdown; Q2 sequential decline from Q1 signals weak demand or pricing in paper segment.
  • Seasonal/Quarterly Volatility: Heavy Q2 losses mask H1 breakeven PAT; prior-year H1 was boosted by strong Q2.

Tailwinds (Supportive Factors)

  • Strong Liquidity & Cash Generation: ₹64 Cr op. cash flow (up 13% YoY); net debt down ~8% to ₹253 Cr; ₹65 Cr current investments + ₹7 Cr cash provide buffer for capex/dividends.
  • Capex Momentum: CWIP up 157% to ₹76 Cr (PPE stable at ₹1,017 Cr); signals capacity expansion amid India’s paper demand growth.
  • Investment Income: ₹37 Cr (down YoY but key offset); total OCI positive ₹40 Cr.
  • Governance: Clean audit; new Independent Directors (Ramesh Aggarwal, Deepak Jalan) approved via 99.99% postal ballot vote; NIL physical share re-lodgement per SEBI.

Growth Prospects

  • Industry Tailwinds: India’s paper mkt. growing ~8-10% CAGR (packaging/writing demand); co.’s ISO certifications position it for market share gains.
  • Capex-Driven Expansion: Ongoing investments (₹119 Cr H1 outflow) likely to boost production post-outages; historical ROCE ~15-20% supports scalability.
  • Treasury Efficiency: Active investment churning (₹73 Cr sales vs. ₹61 Cr buys) yields steady non-op. income; potential for debt-free ops if cash flows sustain.

Key Risks

  • Labor & Operational: Recurring strikes/outages (recent 15-day impact); single-location concentration heightens downtime risk.
  • Input Cost Volatility: Exposure to wood pulp, power/fuel (16% of expenses); forex on imports adds unrealized losses.
  • Demand & Competition: Flat revenue amid capex; cyclical paper sector vulnerable to economic slowdowns/print media decline.
  • Execution: High capex needs sustained cash flows; dividend payout (₹20/shr prior) could strain if profits volatile.

Summary: ANDHRAPAP faces near-term headwinds from ops disruptions and costs, leading to H1 profit plunge, but strong balance sheet (Net Worth ₹1,926 Cr), cash flows, and capex signal resilience. Growth hinges on capex fruition and industry upcycle; undervalued if disruptions prove one-off (EV/EBITDA ~4-5x historical). Investment Stance: Hold/Buy on dips for long-term; monitor Q3 for outage normalization. Risks tilted operational; tailwinds financial. (Data as of Nov 2025 filings.)
