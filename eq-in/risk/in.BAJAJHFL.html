Bajaj Housing Finance Limited (BAJAJHFL) - Q2/H1 FY26 Analysis

Overview: BAJAJHFL reported robust H1 FY26 performance with AUM at ₹1,26,749 Cr (+24% YoY), PAT at ₹1,226 Cr (+19% YoY), ROA at 2.3%, and pristine asset quality (GNPA 0.26%, NNPA 0.12%). Disbursements grew 32% YoY to ~₹16,000 Cr in Q2 amid competition. FY26 guidance: AUM 21-23%, ROA 2.0-2.2%, ROE 11-12%. Portfolio diversified (HL 55%, LRD 21.5%, DF 11.8%). Strong balance sheet (CAR 26%, liquidity surplus ₹2,265 Cr).

Headwinds

  • Intense Competition: Aggressive pricing from PSU banks in prime home loans (industry leader in segment) driving elevated attrition (21-22% annualized in HL, vs. historical norms). Industry HL disbursement growth muted at 6-7% YoY.
  • Margin Pressure: Yield down 60 bps YoY to 9.3%; NIM flat QoQ at 4% but guided -15-20 bps FY26 decline due to rate cuts (portfolio attrition + expected Dec cut). Lower assignment/derecognition income (H1: -60% YoY) caps NTI growth.
  • AUM Growth Moderation: FY26 guided lower (21-23%) due to high repayments; HL share dipped to 55% (-70 bps QoQ).
  • Opex/Scale: Opex/NTI at 19.6% (Q2), guided 20-21%; medium-term 14-16% aspiration needs 3-4 years.

Tailwinds

  • Operational Resilience: Disbursements +32% YoY despite headwinds; AUM +24% YoY, loan assets +26%.
  • Funding Efficiency: CoF down 50 bps YoY/34 bps QoQ to 7.4%; diversified mix (54% money market, 37% banks, 9% NHB). AAA ratings enable competitive borrowing.
  • Asset Quality: GNPA improved 4 bps QoQ to 0.26%; credit cost 18 bps (normalized ~14 bps ex-overlay); PCR ~56%. Stage-1 assets at 99.4%.
  • Efficiency & Digital: Opex/NTI improved 90 bps YoY; 94% e-agreements, 93% online onboarding. 220 branches across 176 locations.
  • Capital Strength: CAR 26% (Tier-1 25.6%); net worth ₹21,170 Cr; gearing 5.5x (target 7-8x).

Growth Prospects

  • Short-term (FY26): 21-23% AUM via disbursement focus (HL 19%, LAP 29%, LRD 35%, DF 25% YoY); near-prime/affordable SBU scaling (₹250 Cr/month, 139 locations) to boost HL yields (+1.25-1.5% premium).
  • Medium-term (2-3 years): Revert to higher AUM growth post-rate stabilization; LRD/DF expansion (low-risk, scale potential; no internal cap beyond regulatory 40% non-HL); gearing to 7.5x; opex/NTI to 14-16%; ROE normalization.
  • Strategic Enablers: Full mortgage suite; micro-market deepening; digital leverage; Sambhav HL for underserved segments. Mortgage penetration low (11% GDP); long-term industry AUM CAGR ~12-13% sustainable.

Key Risks

Investment Summary: BAJAJHFL demonstrates resilience in a competitive, low-rate environment with superior quality and efficiency. Headwinds are cyclical (competition/rates), but tailwinds from diversification/digital scale position it for medium-term acceleration. Risks well-managed; attractive for quality-focused investors (ROE build-up post-FY26). Monitor attrition and rate trajectory.
