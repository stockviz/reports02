Summary Analysis: Bajaj Housing Finance Limited (BAJAJHFL)

BAJAJHFL, a leading Housing Finance Company (HFC) and subsidiary of Bajaj Finance Ltd., reported robust H1 FY26 (ended Sep 30, 2025) results with AUM at ₹1,26,749 Cr (+24% YoY), loan book at ₹1,13,059 Cr (+26% YoY), NII +34% YoY, and PAT +19% YoY at ₹1,226 Cr. Asset quality remains pristine (GNPA 0.26%, NNPA 0.12%, PCR 56%), CRAR at 26.12% (well above 15% regulatory), and LCR at 176%. Promoter Bajaj Finance holds 88.7% stake and plans to sell up to 2% (166.6 Mn shares) by Feb 2026 to meet minimum public shareholding norms. Below is a structured analysis of tailwinds, headwinds, growth prospects, and key risks.

Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts)

  • Strong Fundamentals & Execution: Explosive AUM/loan growth (24-26% YoY) driven by housing demand, low NPAs, and NIM expansion (implied ~8-9%). Operating efficiency improved (opex/NTI at 19.6% vs 20.5% YoY). High ratings (AAA/Stable long-term, A1+ short-term) ensure low-cost funding.
  • Parentage & Ecosystem: Backed by Bajaj Finance (strong balance sheet, distribution network). Related-party synergies (e.g., servicing fees, ESOP recharge) enhance cost efficiencies. RPTs are arm’s-length and approved.
  • Liquidity & Capital Buffer: LCR 176% (>100% regulatory), security cover 1.002x on book value for NCDs. Proceeds from NCDs/CP utilized as stated (on-balance sheet deployment).
  • Market Positioning: Focus on salaried/affluent segments in housing finance (low-risk portfolio). Digital capabilities and low provisions support scalability.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Promoter Dilution Pressure: High promoter stake (88.7%) requires ongoing sales to cap at 75% (min 25% public holding). Announced 2% sale (Dec 2025-Feb 2026) could create short-term supply overhang, pressuring stock price/liquidity.
  • Rising Costs & Provisions: Finance costs +10-12% YoY amid potential rate hikes; provisions jumped to ₹50 Cr in Q2 (from ₹5 Cr, incl. overlay). Employee/tech expenses up 16-21% YoY.
  • Macro Sensitivity: HFC sector vulnerable to interest rate cycles, slowing housing demand (e.g., if RBI tightens), and competitive funding (deposits at ₹48 Cr remain nascent).
  • Regulatory Scrutiny: NHB/RBI norms on co-lending, assignment (disclosed volumes: ₹326 Cr assigned), and resolution (₹202 Cr standard post-resolution, low slippage).

Growth Prospects (High Potential)

  • Sector Tailwinds: India’s housing finance market growing ~15-20% CAGR (urbanization, PMAY, affordable housing). BAJAJHFL’s AUM trajectory (FY25: ₹1.15L Cr) positions it for 20-25%+ growth, targeting ₹1.5-2L Cr AUM by FY27.
  • Strategic levers: Loan book expansion via co-lending/assignments (e.g., ₹689 Cr acquired in Q2), digital underwriting, and cross-sell (insurance commissions ₹25 Cr). NIM/profitability scalable with 23% NPM.
  • Capital Access: Strong CRAR supports inorganic growth; equity dilution aids compliance while unlocking value. Analyst meets signal institutional interest.
  • Upside Scenario: Sustained 20%+ AUM growth, NIM >8%, ROA 2.3-2.5% → PAT CAGR 25%+ to FY27.
