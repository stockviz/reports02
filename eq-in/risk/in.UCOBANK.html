UCO Bank (UCOBANK) Analysis: Q2 FY26 (Sep 2025) Review

UCO Bank delivered a steady quarter with Net Profit of ₹620 Cr (+3% YoY) and Operating Profit of ₹1,613 Cr (+12.64% YoY), driven by robust RAM (Retail/Agri/MSME) growth (22.87% YoY, 65.23% of advances) and improving asset quality (GNPA 2.56%/-62 bps YoY; NNPA 0.43%/-30 bps YoY; PCR 96.99%). Business grew 13.23% YoY (deposits +10.85%, advances +16.56%), CASA stable at 38.11%, NIM resilient at 2.90% global (excl. one-time), and CAR strong at 17.89%. Guidance largely met/exceeded, with digital initiatives gaining traction. However, H1 credit growth slowed due to corporate de-growth.

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Asset Quality Turnaround: GNPA/NNPA at multi-quarter lows; recovery/upgradation ₹792 Cr (H1 ₹1,548 Cr vs. FY guidance ₹2,200-2,700 Cr); slippages 0.26% (annualized 1.05%, within 1-1.25% guidance).
  • RAM Focus: High-yield segments (retail +25.4%, MSME +23.8%, agri +17.28%) driving advances; CD ratio up to 75.47% (within 75-77% guidance).
  • Operational Efficiency: Cost-to-income down to 52.79%; business/employee ₹25 Cr (+14% YoY); fee income +10% QoQ; excess provisions ~₹1,000 Cr (incl. COVID ₹530 Cr, ECL ₹462 Cr).
  • Digital Momentum: Mobile users 57L (from 14L in FY23); WhatsApp banking 15L users/46 services; digital business ₹10,554 Cr; tab banking 57% accounts.
  • Capital Strength: CAR 17.89% (Tier-1 15.90%), supporting growth without dilution.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Credit Growth Slowdown: H1 advances need acceleration (corporate de-growth ₹4,000 Cr due to PSU/IBPC yield pressure); overall FY credit target 12-14% at risk if pricing issues persist.
  • NIM Pressure: Revised FY guidance 2.8-2.9% global (from 3-3.1%) post-RBI repo cuts; CoD down to 4.73% but treasury income halved QoQ (₹104 Cr).
  • Income Volatility: Non-interest income down QoQ (treasury/write-off recovery dip); NII growth 10.08% but dependent on rate environment.
  • Seasonal Slippages: Agri/KCC ₹238 Cr (repeat of prior year); SMA (>₹1 Cr) 0.77% of advances.
  • Restructured Book: Total ₹2,784 Cr (0.65-1.2% of advances), though declining.

Growth Prospects

  • Balanced Portfolio Expansion: RAM guidance 61-63% (already 65%); sanction pipeline ₹17-18k Cr; new segments (renewables, data centers, battery swap, smart metering, green financing ~₹3,000 Cr outstanding).
  • Fee & Cross-Sell: Mutual fund tie-ups, IPO/M&A financing (via Singapore/HK/GIFT City), co-lending (₹2,100 Cr exposure, scaling post-RBI guidelines), insurance/car loans.
  • Digital/IT Push: ₹1,000 Cr FY spend (₹270 Cr done, ₹300 Cr pipeline); new features (video KYC, RPA, omni-channel, API, CBDC); 150 branch additions; AI/ML grievance redressal.
  • Profitability Ramp: DTA ₹5,258 Cr utilizable in 2 years (2027-28) with sustained ₹2,800-3,000 Cr FY profit; ROA 0.71%.
  • Recovery Leverage: WO book ₹24-25k Cr; NCLT pipeline for more ₹100+ Cr recoveries.

Key Risks

  • ECL Transition (2028 rollout): Forward provisioning underway (₹462 Cr on SMA>₹1 Cr); potential P&L hit if shortfalls (though baseline covered; excess provisions buffer).
  • Rate Sensitivity: Further repo cuts could compress NIM/yield on advances (domestic 8.39%); treasury gains opportunistic.
  • Corporate/Competition: Pricing wars in low-yield PSU/corporate; need to rebuild book without margin erosion.
  • Sectoral Stress: Agri slippages (seasonal); MSME restructuring ₹504 Cr (slip risk monitored).
  • Operational/Regulatory: Cyber/fraud (mitigated via RBI integrations/AI); DTA reversal tied to profits; penalty ₹0.09 Cr H1 from RBI.
  • Macro: Slow capex cycle; unhedged FX exposure ₹0.29 Cr (fully provided).

Overall Outlook: Positive with tailwinds from asset quality/digital/RAM outweighing headwinds. FY26 growth intact if corporate rebounds and rates stabilize. Stock supportive on improving ROA/efficiency; watch ECL/slippage trajectory. Target metrics: GNPA <2.5%, NNPA <0.35%, NIM ~2.9%.
