UCO Bank (NSE: UCOBANK) - Q2 & H1 FY26 Analysis

UCO Bank reported a steady quarter with Net Profit of ₹620 Cr (+3% YoY), Operating Profit of ₹1,613 Cr (+12.6% YoY), and robust business growth of 13.2% YoY (deposits +10.9%, advances +16.6%). Asset quality improved markedly (GNPA 2.56%, NNPA 0.43%, PCR 97%), driven by recoveries (H1: ₹1,548 Cr). RAM advances hit 65% of portfolio with 23% growth. NIM stable at 2.9% global (ex-one-off). Balance sheet strong: CAR 17.9%, CD ratio 75.5%, CASA 38.1%. Guidance largely on track despite repo cuts.

Tailwinds (Positive Momentum)

  • Superior Asset Quality: GNPA down 62 bps YoY to 2.56%; NNPA at 0.43%; PCR at 96.99% (tangible ex-TWO: 83.7%). Slippages controlled at 1.05% annualized; restructured book down to 1.2% of advances.
  • RAM Portfolio Strength: Retail/Agri/MSME grew 23% YoY (Retail +25%, MSME +24%); now 65% of advances (vs. guidance 61-63%) – higher yields supporting NIM (domestic yield 8.39%).
  • Stable Liability Franchise: CASA steady at 38.1%; deposits +10.9%; CoD down 15 bps to 4.73%, aiding CoF decline to 4.57%.
  • Profitability Discipline: Cost-to-income improved to 52.8%; business/employee up 14% to ₹25 Cr; fee income +10% QoQ.
  • Digital/Customer Traction: Mobile users 57L (+300% from FY23); WhatsApp banking 15L users (46 services); digital business ₹10,554 Cr; tab banking 57% of accounts – boosting efficiency/stickiness.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Corporate De-growth: -₹4,000 Cr QoQ drag (esp. PSU/IBPC due to yield pricing); H1 credit growth lags FY target (needs aggressive H2 push).
  • NIM Pressure: Guidance revised to 2.8-2.9% global (from 3-3.1%) post-RBI repo cuts; Treasury income halved QoQ (₹104 Cr); global NIM sensitive to rates.
  • Variable Recoveries: WO recoveries down QoQ; total H1 at 70% of FY guidance – lumpy (e.g., NCLT ₹104 Cr).
  • Provisions Drag: Credit cost 0.72%; ECL provisioning ₹462 Cr on SMA (>₹1 Cr: ₹1,790 Cr or 0.77%); total excess provisions ₹1,000 Cr.
  • Sectoral Stress: Agri slippages ₹238 Cr (KCC seasonal); SMA overall ~₹5-6,000 Cr.

Growth Prospects

  • Credit Pipeline: ₹17-18k Cr sanctions ready (renewables, data centers, battery/smart metering); H2 acceleration via co-lending (current ₹2,100 Cr, expanding post-RBI norms), M&A/IPO financing, GIFT City branch.
  • Fee & Cross-Sell: Mutual fund tie-ups (direct ARN for higher commissions); capital markets cell; green financing (₹3,000 Cr sanctioned).
  • Digital/Expansion: ₹1,000 Cr IT capex (₹800-900 Cr FY26); 150 new branches; omni-channel, Video KYC, API treasury by Dec’25 – targeting higher CASA/fee.
  • Guidance Upside: FY targets met/exceeded in most areas (credit 16.6% vs. 12-14%; GNPA <2.5%; slippage 1-1.25%; recovery ₹2,200-2,700 Cr).
  • Capital Flexibility: CAR 17.9% supports growth; potential dividend post-DTA utilization (by FY28).
