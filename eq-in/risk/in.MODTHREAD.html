Analysis of Modern Threads (India) Limited (MODTHREAD)

Company Overview: Modern Threads (India) Limited is a textile manufacturer focused on woollens and yarn divisions, listed on BSE (500282) and NSE (MODTHREAD). FY2026 H1 results (ended Sep 30, 2025) show robust revenue growth amid operational improvements, but persistent audit qualifications highlight legacy liabilities. The 44th AGM (Sep 30, 2025) saw unanimous approval of all resolutions, indicating strong shareholder alignment.

Tailwinds (Positive Factors)
  • Revenue Momentum: H1 FY26 revenue from operations surged 22.6% YoY to ₹14,754 lakhs (Q2: ₹7,767 lakhs, up 25% YoY), driven by higher sales volumes in textiles. Total income up 22.3% YoY.
  • Profitability Turnaround: Net profit jumped 422% YoY to ₹263 lakhs (Q2: ₹262 lakhs vs. ₹32 lakhs last year). PBT up 255% to ₹374 lakhs. EPS improved to ₹0.76 (H1) from ₹0.14.
  • Operational Efficiency: Gross margins implicitly strong (cost of materials ~60% of revenue); employee costs and other expenses controlled. Positive operating cash flow of ₹461 lakhs (up from ₹311 lakhs YoY).
  • Balance Sheet Strength: Equity base stable at ₹13,544 lakhs (up 2%); inventories managed down slightly. Investments in mutual funds (₹2,917 lakhs current) provide liquidity buffer.
  • Strategic Moves: Heavy capex (CWIP up 133% to ₹1,170 lakhs; PPE ₹4,378 lakhs); new wholly-owned UK subsidiary (Modern Woollens UK Ltd., incorporated Nov 2024) signals international expansion potential.
  • Governance: Overwhelming AGM approvals (100% favor for key resolutions like MD reappointment).
Headwinds (Negative Factors)
  • Audit Qualifications (Recurring):
    • No provision for cumulative redeemable preference shares dividend/interest: ₹18 lakhs (H1 FY26) + massive ₹1,066 lakhs cumulative (process of settlement ongoing).
    • Trade receivables (₹4,057 lakhs) and payables (₹3,334 lakhs) unconfirmed, risking adjustments.
  • Cash Constraints: Cash & equivalents down to ₹52 lakhs (from ₹87 lakhs at FY25 end); net cash burn in investing activities (₹761 lakhs) due to capex.
  • Legacy Issues: ₹1,450 lakhs share application money refundable post-SICA repeal (2016). No deferred tax asset recognition (₹177 lakhs) due to uncertain future profits.
  • Thin Margins: Finance costs low (₹98 lakhs H1) but rising borrowings (current borrowings up 17% to ₹2,466 lakhs). Net worth impact from qualifications: Adjusted profit down to ₹245 lakhs.
Growth Prospects
  • High: Strong H1 momentum (revenue +23% QoQ in Q2) suggests demand recovery in textiles. Capex focus (₹801 lakhs PPE purchases H1) positions for capacity expansion in woollens/yarn.
  • Medium-Term Catalysts:
    • UK subsidiary could enable exports/Europe entry (yet to commence ops).
    • Single-segment textiles but scalable with improving working capital (inventories down 0.2%, receivables stable).
    • Potential 20-25% FY26 revenue growth if trends sustain; profitability could normalize post-preference settlement.
  • Outlook: Bullish if industry tailwinds (e.g., domestic demand, PLI scheme benefits) persist; FY25 full-year profit was ₹337 lakhs, H1 already at 78%.
Key Risks

Financial/Liability Cumulative preference dividend (₹1,066 lakhs) settlement; unconfirmed balances. Profit erosion (adjusted H1 PAT -7%); balance sheet strain. Ongoing negotiations; low finance costs indicate manageable debt.
Liquidity Low cash (₹52 lakhs); high CWIP/capex. Funding gaps for expansion/refunds. Positive ops cash flow; mutual fund investments.
Operational Textile cyclicality; raw material (cotton) volatility; unconfirmed AR/AP. Margin pressure; bad debts. Revenue growth indicates demand resilience.
Regulatory/Tax No DTA recognition; 115BAA tax regime; SICA legacy refunds. Higher effective tax (30% H1); cash outflows. Tax planning; historical compliance.
Market/External Competition from imports (China/Bangladesh); forex for UK ops. Revenue slowdown. Domestic focus; capex for competitiveness.

Overall Summary: Positive outlook with caution. Tailwinds from revenue/profit growth and expansion outweigh headwinds, but legacy liabilities (esp. preference shares) pose material risks—watch for Q3 settlement updates. Stock could rerate on sustained growth (target 20%+ topline CAGR), but qualifications cap multiples. Recommendation: Accumulate on dips for long-term textile play; monitor debt and audit resolutions.
