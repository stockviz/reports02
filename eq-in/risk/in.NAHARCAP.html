Analysis of NAHARCAP (Nahar Capital and Financial Services Ltd.)

NAHARCAP is a listed NBFC focused on investments (securities, financial assets) and real estate (rental/investment properties). Standalone performance shows moderation, but consolidated results (including associates Nahar Spinning Mills Ltd. and Nahar Poly Films Ltd.) remain robust. FY26 H1 standalone net profit declined 21% YoY to ₹1988 lakhs (EPS ₹11.87), driven by lower dividend/rental income and fair value gains. Consolidated net profit rose 44% YoY to ₹3186 lakhs (EPS ₹19.03). Assets grew 7% to ₹99,289 lakhs standalone (investments ~90%), supported by unrealized gains. Dividend of ₹1.50/share (30% yield) declared and approved at AGM. Promoter holding ~72%; all resolutions passed with 99.96-99.98% approval.

Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts)

  • Robust Investment Portfolio: Investments surged 7% QoQ to ₹89,271 lakhs standalone (₹1,72,530 lakhs consolidated), driving 75% of income via interest (₹681 lakhs), dividends (₹851 lakhs standalone), and fair value changes. OCI gains (₹4,234 lakhs H1) boosted comprehensive income.
  • Associate Leverage: Share of associates’ profits jumped to ₹2,107 lakhs (H1 FY26 vs. ₹323 lakhs prior), lifting consolidated PBT 29% YoY to ₹4,408 lakhs. Textile associates provide diversification.
  • Strong Balance Sheet & Shareholder Returns: Reserves at ₹95,306 lakhs (standalone); equity base stable at ₹837 lakhs. Dividend payout signals confidence; healthy promoter voting (99.99% in favor).
  • Segment Resilience: Investment/financial segment contributes 95% revenue (₹3,133 lakhs H1) with 93% of assets; real estate steady (₹147 lakhs revenue).
  • Low Debt: Borrowings minimal (₹596 lakhs); finance costs low at 1-2% of income.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Standalone Profit Pressure: Total income down 17% YoY (₹3,280 lakhs); dividend income volatile (down post-Q1 spike); rental income halved (₹147 lakhs vs. ₹345 lakhs).
  • Declining Operational Metrics: PBT down 23% YoY; employee/other expenses up 18% (₹595 lakhs). Fees/commission negligible.
  • Negative Cash Flows: Operating cash flow -₹4,832 lakhs (H1), driven by ₹5,808 lakhs net investment outflows; reliant on investing activities for liquidity.
  • Real Estate Slowdown: Rental revenue halved; CWIP up to ₹2,165 lakhs but no near-term monetization visible.
  • Macro Sensitivity: Q2 fair value losses in OCI (-₹308 lakhs) highlight volatility.

Growth Prospects

  • High (Investments-Driven): Portfolio expansion (7% QoQ growth) positions for market rallies; dividend/interest income could rebound with equity recovery. Potential NBFC lending ramp-up (loans at ₹1,699 lakhs).
  • Medium (Associates & Real Estate): Textile associates’ turnaround (e.g., spinning mills) offers 20-30% consolidated EPS upside if sustained. Real estate (₹7,395 lakhs assets) has CWIP potential for future rentals/sales.
  • Catalysts: Dividend yield ~2-3%; buybacks/expansion possible with ₹96,000+ lakhs reserves. NBFC sector tailwinds from rate cuts; segment revenue grew 5% in investments despite dips.
  • Outlook: 15-20% EPS growth FY26 if markets stable; consolidated assets could hit ₹1.1-1.2 lakh cr. by FY27.
