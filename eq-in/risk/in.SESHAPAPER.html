Analysis of Seshasayee Paper and Boards Limited (SESHAPAPER)

Seshasayee Paper and Boards Limited (CIN: L21012TZ1960PLC000364), a paper manufacturing company, reported unaudited H1 FY26 (ended Sep 30, 2025) results showing revenue decline amid cost pressures but maintained profitability on a strong balance sheet. Standalone revenue fell 11% YoY to ₹731.58 Cr, PBT down 44% to ₹47.14 Cr, and PAT down 45% to ₹34.86 Cr (EPS ₹5.79). Consolidated figures are marginally better (PAT ₹37.84 Cr). Declines driven by softer demand and inventory build-up, offset by other income and cost controls. Key highlights include asset acquisition for expansion and renewable energy investments.

Tailwinds (Positive Factors)

  • Strong Balance Sheet: Equity base robust at ₹1,936 Cr (standalone), low debt (current borrowings ₹77 Cr), and reserves at ₹1,924 Cr (up from ₹1,900 Cr in FY25). Cash equivalents rose to ₹49.52 Cr.
  • Renewable Energy Partnership: ₹26 Cr investment (26.1% stake) in Navia One Power SPV for 52.8 MWp solar + 9 MW wind capacity, exclusively supplying power. Potential to cut high power/fuel costs (₹49 Cr in Q2, 14% of expenses) long-term.
  • High Other Income: ₹27.69 Cr in H1 (from interest/dividends), cushioning core weakness.
  • Clean Audit: Unqualified limited review reports; single paper segment simplifies operations.

Headwinds (Negative Factors)

  • Revenue Pressure: H1 revenue down 11% YoY (₹731 Cr vs ₹821 Cr); Q2 down 13% QoQ and 13% YoY due to lower volumes/pricing in paper segment.
  • Margin Squeeze: Gross margins impacted by raw material costs (₹554 Cr, 76% of expenses) and power/fuel (up YoY relatively). PBT margins at 6.4% (vs 10.3% H1 FY25).
  • Cash Flow Strain: Negative operating cash flow (-₹73 Cr) from ₹115 Cr inventory pile-up and working capital changes; reliant on investing activities (term deposit maturities).
  • OCI Volatility: Sharp Q2 swing (-₹42 Cr standalone) from equity investment fair value losses, dragging total comprehensive income to -₹24 Cr.

Growth Prospects

  • Capacity Expansion via Acquisition: NCLT-approved purchase of Servalakshmi Paper assets (May 2023); revival/refurbishment underway despite pending NCLAT appeals. Could add meaningful paper production capacity.
  • Cost Optimization: Renewable power supply from SPV (operationalizing soon) to reduce ₹100-240 Cr annual power expenses; aligns with green energy trends in industry.
  • Sector Tailwinds: Paper demand steady (packaging/writing); potential recovery if raw material prices stabilize. FY25 full-year revenue base of ₹1,754 Cr provides scale.
  • Moderate Outlook: H1 PAT covers ~33% of FY25; annualizing suggests FY26 PAT ~₹70-100 Cr if demand rebounds, supported by ₹135 Cr investing cash inflow.

Key Risks

  • Legal/Execution Risk: NCLAT appeals on Servalakshmi assets could delay/derail revival (capex ₹28 Cr in H1 on PPE).
  • Cyclical Sector Exposure: 100% paper segment vulnerable to raw material (pulp/wood) volatility, demand softness (e.g., post-monsoon inventory glut), and competition.
  • Cash Conversion: Persistent negative OpCF (-₹73 Cr H1) from inventory/debtor cycles; dividend payout (₹16 Cr) adds pressure.
  • Concentration & External: Associate (Ponni Sugars) contribution variable; power costs remain 14% of expenses until SPV online. No exceptional items, but forex/investment volatility in OCI.
  • Macro: Rising finance costs (₹5.5 Cr H1, up sharply); interest rate sensitivity on borrowings.

Overall Summary: Near-term headwinds from demand weakness and cash burn temper performance (stock may face pressure), but tailwinds from renewables and asset revival offer 10-20% upside in 12-18 months if executed well. Conservative FY26 revenue growth of 5-10% assumed; monitor Q3 for inventory unwind and SPV progress. Low debt and liquidity provide downside protection. Recommendation: Hold/Buy on dips for long-term investors focused on industrial recovery. (Analysis based solely on provided Q/H1 FY26 results; cross-check with sector peers/market data.)
