1. Executive Summary

Pashupati Cotspin Limited, a Gujarat-based player in the cotton ginning and spinning sector, has completed its migration from the SME platform (EMERGE) of NSE to the Main Board of BSE and NSE with effect from July 17, 2025. As a result, the company has transitioned to Indian Accounting Standards (Ind AS) effective April 1, 2025. This transition involves significant accounting changes, particularly under Ind AS 116 (Leases) and Ind AS 109 (Financial Instruments).

The unaudited financial results for 2H FY2026 show a sharp improvement in profitability, especially when compared to the prior year. However, due to the reclassification and restatement under Ind AS, historical comparability is limited.

The market remains volatile for cotton-based businesses, but operational efficiency, improved tax treatment, and strong cash flow from operations point to positive momentum.
