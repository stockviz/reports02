Summary Analysis for MUNJALAU (Munjal Auto Industries Limited)

Context: Q2 & H1 FY26 (ended Sep 30, 2025) unaudited results show consolidated revenue growth (implied ~₹1,07,510 lakhs from segments vs prior trends), but moderated profitability due to working capital pressures and one-time tax charge. Standalone PBT down 59% YoY to ₹1,313 lakhs; consolidated up 33% to ₹4,103 lakhs. Total assets up 5% consolidated to ₹1.30 Cr lakhs. Auto Components segment volume-led but margin-challenged; Composites subsidiary (Indutch) strong performer.

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Strong Revenue Momentum: Consolidated revenue robust at ~₹58k lakhs in Q2FY26 (Auto: ₹37k lakhs + Composites: ₹21k lakhs), H1 ~₹1.08 Cr lakhs, driven by 26% QoQ Auto growth and steady Composites (~8% QoQ). Ties to India’s auto sector recovery (2W/3W OEMs like Hero MotoCorp).
  • Composites Segment Star: Subsidiary contributes ~38% revenue, 89% of segment profits (Q2: ₹1,992 lakhs). High-margin diversification (vs Auto’s ₹240 lakhs Q2 profit).
  • Tax Efficiency: Shift to Sec 115BAA (22% + cess = 25.17% effective) saves future taxes; one-time ₹409 lakhs charge already absorbed.
  • Asset Base Expansion: PPE up 6% standalone, inventories/receivables signal volume growth. Equity up 5% to ₹4.50 Cr lakhs consolidated.
  • Auditor Comfort: Clean limited review reports; no qualifications.

Headwinds (Negative Pressures)

  • Margin Squeeze in Core Auto: Q2 segment profit just ₹240 lakhs (down vs prior), implying EBITDA pressure from input costs/materials (60-70% of expenses typically). Standalone PBT halves YoY.
  • Working Capital Drag: Trade receivables +27% to ₹3.79 Cr lakhs cons., inventories +12% to ₹2.59 Cr lakhs → Ops cash flow -₹1,796 lakhs (vs -₹464 lakhs prior H1). Cycle time stretched.
  • Cash Burn: Net cash down ~₹589 lakhs; low liquidity (cash equiv. ₹54 lakhs cons.). Dividend payout (₹1,000 lakhs) adds strain.
  • Leverage Uptick: Current borrowings +53% to ₹1.80 Cr lakhs cons., driven by WC term loans.

Growth Prospects

  • High (Medium-Term): Auto Components (core, 62% revenue) benefits from India EV/2W boom (e.g., Hero exposure); H1 revenue +ve momentum. Composites (high-growth, 38% revenue) expanding (revenue +9% YoY implied), assets ₹5.81 Cr lakhs. Total revenue CAGR potential 15-20% if auto volumes sustain.
  • Capex Support: ₹638 lakhs standalone CWIP + ₹2.73 Cr cons.; investing activities -₹786 lakhs cons. (PPE buys).
  • Diversification: 2 segments reduce auto cyclicality; subsidiary adds ₹1,813 lakhs PAT H1.
  • Outlook: FY26 revenue >₹2.5 Cr lakhs possible if Q3 margins recover; ROE ~18-20% potential post-tax optimization.

Key Risks

  • Liquidity/Execution (High): Persistent negative ops cash flow (-₹1.8 Cr lakhs H1); receivables collection risk (90+ days?). Could force more debt (interest ₹1.08 Cr lakhs H1).
  • Cyclical Auto Exposure (Medium-High): 62% revenue from volatile OEMs; Q2 Auto margins <1% imply cost/volumes sensitivity.
  • Subsidiary Leverage (Medium): Composites assets ₹5.81 Cr lakhs but liabs ₹5.16 Cr lakhs (high gearing); 100% reliance on 1 sub.
  • One-Offs/Macro (Medium): Tax charge impacts PAT; forex/inflation in materials (unrealized FX noted). Regulatory (SEBI LODR compliance fine).
  • Overall Risk Profile: Moderate; improving balance sheet (D/E ~0.7x cons.) but monitor WC quarterly.

Investment Takeaway: Positive growth story with Composites tailwind offsetting Auto headwinds, but near-term cash/liquidity watch needed. Buy on dips if auto volumes confirm; target P/B 1.5-2x (current implied fair). Data limited (no full P&L); await Q3 for confirmation.
