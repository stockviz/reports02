Analysis of Radhika Jeweltech Ltd (BSE: 540125, RADHIKAJWE)

Radhika Jeweltech Ltd, a jewelry manufacturing and trading company (gold, diamond, platinum), reported strong profitability growth for H1 FY26 (ended Sep 30, 2025), with PAT up 55% YoY to ₹3,661.84 lakhs despite modest 3% revenue growth to ₹23,219.18 lakhs. This reflects margin expansion (PBT margins ~21% vs. 16% YoY) driven by efficient inventory management, lower finance costs, and controlled expenses. Balance sheet remains healthy with equity at ₹35,927 lakhs (81% of assets), robust operating cash flow (₹2,509 lakhs), but elevated inventories signal potential risks. Auditor’s review is unqualified.

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Margin Expansion & Profitability Surge: Q2 PAT ₹1,841 lakhs (+74% YoY); H1 PAT margins improved to 16% from 11%. Operating profit up 39% YoY to ₹4,929 lakhs, aided by positive inventory changes (-₹1,453 lakhs in H1) and 32% drop in finance costs (₹131 lakhs).
  • Revenue Momentum in Q2: Q2 revenue ₹13,305 lakhs (+1% YoY, +34% QoQ), indicating seasonal strength and operational scaling.
  • Strong Liquidity & Cash Generation: Operating cash flow ₹2,509 lakhs (vs. ₹487 lakhs YoY); cash & equivalents up 64% to ₹901 lakhs. Low debt-to-equity (~0.12x).
  • Asset Quality: Investments rose to ₹2,030 lakhs (current); inventories at ₹38,598 lakhs support future sales in a growing organized jewelry market (India’s ~₹6 lakh crore sector, 10-15% CAGR).
  • Cost Discipline: Employee/other expenses stable; depreciation steady.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Modest Topline Growth: H1 revenue +3% YoY (Q2 flat YoY), lagging sector peers amid potentially softening discretionary demand or gold price headwinds.
  • Working Capital Strain: Inventories +9% QoQ (+₹3,079 lakhs cash outflow); trade payables ballooned 5.5x to ₹3,292 lakhs (from FY25 end), signaling aggressive supplier credit reliance.
  • Elevated Tax Burden: H1 tax ₹1,267 lakhs (26% effective rate), including ₹13 lakhs prior-year adjustment, denting net profits.
  • Investing Cash Burn: ₹2,118 lakhs outflow (investments ₹2,500 lakhs net of sales), temporarily pressuring cash despite sales proceeds.

Growth Prospects

  • High (Short-Term: Strong Execution; Medium-Term: Sector Tailwinds):
    • PAT trajectory suggests FY26 PAT could exceed FY25’s ₹6,012 lakhs (annualized H1 ~₹7,324 lakhs, +22% potential).
    • Organized jewelry shift (GST, branding) favors listed players; rising gold prices (MCap ~₹600-700 Cr implied at current levels) boost realizations.
    • Inventory buildup positions for festive/wedding season (Q3-Q4 typically 50%+ of sales).
    • Capex modest (PPE +₹36 lakhs net); cash flow supports expansion/scaling via investments or new showrooms (lease premiums noted).
    • Single-segment focus enables agility; EPS H1 ₹3.10 (FY25 ₹5.09) implies upside if revenue accelerates to 15-20% FY26.

Overall Summary: Bullish with Cautions. Tailwinds from profitability and cash flow dominate, supporting 20-30% PAT growth prospects in FY26 amid jewelry sector boom. However, headwinds like tepid revenue and inventory pile-up cap upside; monitor Q3 for festive sales. Risks are manageable (low leverage) but commodity/WC-focused. Attractive for value/growth investors if gold demand sustains; target PE 8-10x FY26 EPS (~₹6.5-7) implies 20-30% stock upside from current levels (assuming ~₹50-60/share).
