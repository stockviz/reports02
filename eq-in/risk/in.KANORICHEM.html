Analysis of Kanoria Chemicals & Industries Ltd (KANORICHEM)

Company Overview: Kanoria Chemicals & Industries Ltd (CIN: L24110WB1960PLC024910) is a Kolkata-based chemical manufacturer listed on NSE (KANORICHEM) and BSE (506525). Core business is chemicals (acetaldehyde, acetic acid, etc.), with legacy exposure to textiles (via Ethiopia-based subsidiary) and electronics (via deconsolidated Swiss entity APAG Holding AG). FY26 H1 results (ended Sep 30, 2025) show a turnaround driven by core ops growth and one-time gains, but underlying challenges persist. All figures in INR Lakhs unless stated.

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Core Chemicals Strength: H1 rev +17% YoY (standalone), EBITDA margins ~11% (up). Stable demand, cost control (materials 70% of expenses managed).
  • De-risking via Deconsolidation: APAG exit (loss-making electronics) unlocks ₹9,766 gain, sheds ₹55k+ assets/₹50k liabilities, cuts NCI drag (now zero), reduces forex/group losses.
  • Liquidity Surge: Cash triples YoY (standalone +₹3,948); enables capex (₹2.7k H1) without equity dilution.
  • Debt Moderation: Net debt/equity improves post-deconsol; interest coverage ~2x (continuing ops).
  • Governance: Clean limited reviews (Singhi & Co.); AGM unanimous; NIL physical share transfers (regulatory compliance).

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Subsidiary Drag: Textiles (Kanoria Africa Textiles PLC, Ethiopia) persistent losses (₹937 H1); forex unrealized gains/losses volatile (₹2k swing in cash flow).
  • Exceptional Volatility: Standalone ₹1,064 impairment on APAG equity; prior FY impairments (₹4.5k).
  • Margin Pressure: High finance costs (₹1,594 H1, up); employee/other expenses up 10-20%.
  • Discontinued Legacy: Solar ops sold (Jun 2024); APAG deconsol masks prior losses (₹5.4k FY25).
  • Macro: Input costs (materials +20% YoY); potential chemical cycle slowdown.

Growth Prospects

  • Medium-Term (1-2 Yrs): Chemicals focus post-APAG exit; H1 momentum (Q2 rev +16% QoQ) suggests 10-15% rev CAGR if volumes/pricing hold. Capex (PPE +₹1.8k) signals capacity expansion.
  • Strategic Shifts: Loan repayments to subs (₹2.5k inflow); potential textile turnaround or exit (Ethiopia risks high).
  • Catalysts: Strong cash (₹4.5k) for dividends/buybacks; EBITDA growth to ₹8-10k FY26 (ex-one-offs).
  • Valuation Upside: PAT swing implies EPS normalization ~₹5-7 FY26; trading discount to book (P/B ~1x historical).
