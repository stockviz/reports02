Summary Analysis for Shree Cement Ltd. (SHREECEM)

Shree Cement’s Q2 & H1 FY26 results (ended 30 Sep 2025) reflect robust YoY growth driven by volumes, premiumization, and cost efficiencies, despite QoQ softness likely due to seasonal monsoon impacts. Capacity expansions are accelerating, with a clear path to 80+ MTPA. Financials remain strong with healthy margins, low leverage (D/E 0.08x consolidated), and shareholder returns (₹80 interim dividend). Below is a structured analysis of tailwinds, headwinds, growth prospects, and key risks based on the provided documents.

Tailwinds (Positive Factors)

  • Strong Financial Momentum (YoY): Standalone Q2 revenue +15% (₹4,303 Cr), EBITDA +44% (₹1,008 Cr; press release notes ₹851 Cr post-adjustments), PAT +198% (₹277 Cr). H1 PAT ₹896 Cr (+118% YoY). Consolidated H1 revenue ₹10,042 Cr (+9%), PAT ₹953 Cr (+168%). Cash profit strong at ₹841 Cr (Q2).
  • Volume & Mix Improvement: Standalone cement volumes +6.8% YoY; premium products share rose to 21.1% (from 14.9%). UAE ops excelled: volumes +34% YoY (13.19 MnT), revenue +50%, EBITDA +158%.
  • Operational Efficiencies: EBITDA margins expanded (standalone Q2 ~23%, H1 26%). Green power at 63% of consumption (592 MW standalone, 612 MW group), water positivity >8x, Zero Liquid Discharge—all reducing costs and earning awards (e.g., Times of India Ecopreneur).
  • Favorable Macros: Benign inflation, good monsoon, GST rationalization (full pass-through from 22 Sep 2025), and infra/housing demand tailwinds per press release.
  • Shareholder Value: Interim dividend ₹80/share (record date 3 Nov 2025); payout from 14 Nov.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • QoQ Declines: Standalone Q2 revenue -13%, EBITDA -31%, PAT -55% vs Q1 (seasonal monsoon weakness in demand).
  • One-Time Hit: ₹20.68 Cr asset write-off (power transmission transfer) impacted Q2 EBITDA by ~₹30/PMT (Note 6).
  • Working Capital Pressure: Trade receivables doubled QoQ (standalone ₹1,444 Cr from ₹781 Cr); inventories +5% (₹2,181 Cr). Cash from ops slowed H1 (₹752 Cr vs ₹1,539 Cr YoY).
  • Rising Short-Term Debt: Standalone current borrowings jumped to ₹1,120 Cr (from ₹90 Cr); total debt/assets 6%. H1 financing cash inflow ₹602 Cr mainly from short-term debt.
  • High Opex: Power/fuel (₹1,040 Cr Q2) and freight (₹942 Cr) remain ~45% of revenue; employee costs steady but elevated.

Growth Prospects

  • Capacity Ramp-Up: Jaitaran (Rajasthan) integrated plant fully commissioned (3.65 MTPA clinker + 3.0 MTPA cement; announced 12 Nov 2025). Kodla (Karnataka) 3.0 MTPA nearing completion. CWIP up 9% QoQ (standalone ₹3,399 Cr). Target: 80+ MTPA (from current ~50 MTPA implied).
  • Diversification: RMC business at 24 plants (new Raipur entry); first solar-powered RMC in Jaipur. UAE growth scalable.
  • Strategic Focus: Premiumization (“value over volume”), green energy expansion (e.g., 20 MW solar commissioned), and cost leadership. H1 capex ₹827 Cr (standalone); FY26 EBITDA guidance intact per investor meets (12 Nov 2025).
  • Industry Tailwinds: GDP pickup H2 FY26, infra push, GST 2.0 benefits. Analysts/investors meet signals confidence (no UPSI shared).
  • Balance Sheet Strength: Net worth ₹21,928 Cr (standalone); investments ₹12,498 Cr (liquid buffer). EPS H1 ₹248 (standalone, not annualized).

Key Risks

  • Execution & Capex Intensity: H1 investing cash outflow ₹1,459 Cr; delays in Kodla/other projects could strain cash (net cash burn ₹105 Cr H1). High CWIP signals ongoing spends.
  • Demand Cyclicality: Cement tied to infra/housing; monsoon QoQ hit volumes. Competition intense (top-3 player but peers expanding).
  • Cost Volatility: Fuel/power ~25% of expenses; forex exposure in UAE (though minor).
  • Leverage & Liquidity: Short-term debt spike (DSCR 25.6x healthy but monitor); cash equivalents low (₹7.5 Cr standalone).
  • Regulatory/Governance: Postal ballot for new Independent Director (Mr. Chandra Kumar Dhanuka, DIN 00005684; approval needed by 12 Dec 2025). GST pass-through protects but erodes realization if sustained.
  • External: No UPSI in investor meets, but schedule changes possible. Consolidated subsidiaries (e.g., UAE, Indian units) not fully reviewed by auditors (materiality low).

Overall Outlook: Bullish medium-term on expansions and efficiencies (target 80 MTPA unlocks volumes/margins). Near-term watch QoQ recovery, debt trajectory, and H2 demand. Low D/E, green focus, and dividend support valuation. Positive catalysts: Kodla commissioning, Q3 results. Risks mitigated by strong liquidity and track record.
