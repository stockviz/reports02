Shree Cement Limited (SHREECEM) Analysis

Based on Q2/H1 FY26 Results (ended Sep 30, 2025), Announcements, and Press Release

Shree Cement delivered robust YoY performance in Q2 FY26 (Standalone: Revenue +15% to ₹4,303 Cr, EBITDA +44% to ₹851 Cr adjusted/₹1,008 Cr reported, PAT +198% to ₹277 Cr; Consolidated similar trends). Volumes grew 6.8% YoY with premium products at 21.1% mix. H1 PAT ₹896 Cr (Standalone). Declared ₹80/share interim dividend. Key capacity milestone: 6.65 MTPA Rajasthan plant fully commissioned; Karnataka 3 MTPA nearing completion. Goal: >80 MTPA capacity. UAE ops surged 50% revenue YoY. Strong balance sheet (D/E 0.08x, Net Worth ₹21,928 Cr Standalone).

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Volume & Realization Growth: 6.8% YoY cement sales rise; premiumization (21% mix vs. 15% prior) boosted margins. UAE volumes +34%, EBITDA +158%.
  • Operational Efficiency: EBITDA margins ~23-24%; cost controls in power/fuel (despite ₹20-24 Cr one-time asset write-off). Green power at 63% of mix, water positivity >8x.
  • Capacity Ramp-Up: Rajasthan plant (3.65 MTPA clinker + 3 MTPA cement) fully live; Kodla (Karnataka) final stages. RMC: 24 plants, new solar-powered Jaipur facility, East India entry.
  • Macro Support: GST rationalization (full pass-through w.e.f. Sep 22), benign inflation, strong monsoon/kharif sowing, infra/housing push. GDP pickup expected in H2 FY26.
  • Sustainability Edge: 612 MW green capacity (post 20 MW solar add); ZLD plants; awards (e.g., Times Ecopreneur, CSR). Enhances premium pricing/ESG appeal.
  • Financial Strength: Cash EBITDA strong (₹555/share H1); high coverage ratios (Interest Service >25x); ₹288 Cr cash (Consol).

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • QoQ Weakness: Revenue -13%, EBITDA -31%, PAT -55% (seasonal monsoon impact on construction).
  • Cost Pressures: Power/fuel (22% of expenses), freight (21%); inventories/receivables up (trade receivables +85% QoQ to ₹1,444 Cr Standalone).
  • One-Offs: ₹21 Cr power asset transfer charge impacted EBITDA by ~₹30/tonne.
  • Capex Intensity: ₹827 Cr PPE spend H1; CWIP ₹3,399 Cr (Standalone). Short-term borrowings +₹1,030 Cr to ₹1,120 Cr for funding.
  • Working Capital Strain: Ops cash flow ₹752 Cr but receivables/inventories rose; current ratio dipped to 1.88x.

Growth Prospects

  • Capacity Expansion: From ~50 MTPA to >80 MTPA; new plants add 10+ MTPA soon. RMC scale-up for value-added revenue.
  • Demand Tailwinds: Infra/housing acceleration; GST cuts to spur consumption. Premium/UAE focus for 15-20% CAGR potential.
  • Margin Expansion: Green energy ramps to cut costs 10-15%; EBITDA/tonne could hit ₹1,200+ on efficiencies.
  • Strategic Moves: Investor meets signal confidence; new Independent Director adds governance. H1 momentum projects FY26 revenue >₹19,000 Cr, PAT >₹2,200 Cr (Standalone est.).
  • Long-Term: UAE/international growth; sustainability leadership for exports/ESG funds.

Key Risks

Risk Category Description Mitigation
Demand/Cyclical Monsoon slowdowns, election/infra delays; competition from UltraTech/ACC. Diversified regions (North/South/East); premium focus.
Cost Inflation Fuel/power volatility (45% expenses); freight hikes. Green power (63% mix), alternative fuels.
Execution Capex delays (Kodla/Rajasthan ramps); integration risks. Proven track record; low debt aids flexibility.
Financial Receivables aging (Debtors Turnover 21x annualized); forex (UAE). Strong liquidity (₹7 Bn investments); D/E low at 0.06-0.08x.
Regulatory Env. norms, GST/tax changes; director age (new ID turns 75 mid-term). ZLD/green compliance; Postal Ballot approval sought.
External Geo-political (UAE ops); raw material shortages. Hedging; multi-source supply.

Overall Outlook: Strong tailwinds from expansions/macros outweigh seasonal headwinds. FY26 growth projected at 12-15% volumes, 20%+ EBITDA (est. ₹18,000-20,000 Cr revenue). Risks manageable with debt discipline (Total Debt/Assets 6%). Buy/Hold for long-term; monitor Q3 demand pickup. (Ratings as of Nov 2025 filings).
