NRB Bearings Limited (NRBBEARING) Analysis

Overview: NRB Bearings is India’s leading independent bearing manufacturer (#1 in cylindrical/needle roller bearings; 30% value/50% volume share). FY26 H1 results show revenue +7.5% YoY (consolidated ₹635 Cr), EBITDA +17.4% (20.3% margin), PAT +19% (₹73 Cr). Standalone PAT +37.9%. Management targets ₹2,500 Cr revenue by FY31 (~13% CAGR) via diversification, capex (₹500 Cr planned), and lifetime orders (₹750 Cr).

Tailwinds

  • Market Leadership & Diversification: Powers 90%+ Indian vehicles; EV-agnostic products supply top OEMs (domestic: Hero, TVS, Maruti, Tata; global: BMW, Mercedes, Daimler). 70:30 domestic:export split; no customer >10%, top-10=50%. Industrial now 15% revenue (target 20%).
  • Strong Financials: Low debt (<20% D/E), cash reserves enable capex. Margins resilient (consolidated EBITDA 20.3%; PAT 11.5%). ROE ~15% (target 17-18%).
  • R&D & Innovation: 2 govt-recognized centers; mass-customization edge; lifetime nominations ₹750 Cr (₹600 Cr international). Thailand plant efficient.
  • Post-Settlement Clarity: Promoter control >51%; brand/IP secured; fire impact resolved (full capacity FY25).
  • Sustainability: Solar power acquisition (8.5% Ampere Pvt Ltd).

Headwinds

  • Past Disruptions: Waluj fire (2023) caused modest CAGR (FY22-25); high inventory (350 days, +50% in 10 yrs) and working cap cycle persist (fire rebuild, 2W confidence, US tariffs).
  • Cyclical Exposure: Automotive ~80-85%; Indian CV weak; GST delays hit Q2 demand.
  • Industrial Entry Barriers: MNCs dominate; lower volumes vs. auto require adaptation (batch production DNA helps).
  • Macro: Currency volatility, tariffs (US inventory buildup).

Growth Prospects

  • Core Auto: Import substitution; next-gen EV/hybrid (Mercedes EQC, BMW X-series); +industry growth via share gains.
  • Diversification: Industrial (off-highway, robotics, drones, gearboxes) to double-digits soon; aerospace (acquisition for certification speedup, HAL/Waluj plant).
  • Capex Ramp: ₹200 Cr Phase 1 (Dec FY26 start; ₹150 Cr revenue); total ₹500 Cr (cyl/needle/taper/ball bearings, size range expansion).
  • Global/JVs: DIFC holding for subs (Thailand/USA/Germany); follow OEMs; Thailand as tariff-proof model.
  • Outlook: H1 momentum (double-digit post-fire); 13% CAGR feasible via 8-10% industry + share/mix gains.

Key Risks

Risk Category Details Mitigation
Execution Capex delays; new segment ramp-up (industrial/aero certification 3.5 yrs without acquisition). Phased capex; R&D prowess; low debt.
Financial High inventory/working cap (fire/tariffs); forex/FEMA delays (₹23 Cr receivables). Reduction planned; insurance claims ongoing.

Investment Thesis Summary: Strong tailwinds from leadership, R&D, and diversification outweigh resolved headwinds. Growth prospects robust (₹2,500 Cr target credible), but monitor inventory normalization and industrial execution. Risks moderate (execution/financial); buy on dips for 13%+ CAGR potential.
