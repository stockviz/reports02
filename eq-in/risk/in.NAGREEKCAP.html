Analysis of Nagreeka Capital & Infrastructure Ltd (NAGREEKCAP)

Nagreeka Capital & Infrastructure Ltd is a listed NBFC (primarily engaged in lending, securities trading, and investments) with strong historical profitability but showing signs of moderation in FY26 H1. Key metrics: H1 FY26 revenue ₹1,778 Cr (↓52% YoY), PAT ₹647 Cr (↓23% YoY), EPS ₹5.13 (↓23% YoY). Balance sheet robust (Equity ₹2,760 Cr), but debt remains high at ~₹8,749 Cr. Trading dominates income (securities sales ~56% of H1 revenue).

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Robust Profitability & Cash Generation: PAT margins ~36% in H1 despite revenue drop; operating cash flow strong at ₹1,124 Cr (supports deleveraging).
  • Deleveraging Progress: Borrowings ↓9% QoQ to ₹7,549 Cr; finance costs manageable at ₹388 Cr (despite ↑41% YoY).
  • Asset Quality: Loans stable at ₹2,749 Cr; minimal impairments (net reversal ₹1.3 Cr H1); high dividend income (₹86 Cr).
  • Shareholder Alignment: AGM resolutions (e.g., director re-appointments, MD remuneration, RPTs) passed with 97-99% approval from public shareholders; promoter confidence evident.
  • Liquidity: Cash ↑ to ₹32 Cr; Other Equity ↑44% YoY to ₹2,129 Cr.

Headwinds (Negative Pressures)

  • Revenue Volatility & Decline: Total revenue halved YoY; securities sales ↓52% (₹990 Cr H1), other operating income swung from +₹750 Cr (Q1) to -₹83 Cr (Q2).
  • Trading Dependency: Inventories ballooned to ₹6,226 Cr (↑14% YoY), signaling inventory buildup amid market fluctuations; changes in inventories caused ₹757 Cr drag.
  • Rising Costs: Finance costs ↑41% YoY; employee/other expenses up amid restructuring.
  • Low Activity: Interest income low (₹33 Cr H1, <2% of revenue); loans contracted 16% YoY.

Growth Prospects

  • Trading & Investment Upside: Core strength in securities/dividends (₹1,076 Cr H1 income); potential recovery if markets rally (past FY25 full-year sales ₹5,204 Cr).
  • NBFC Expansion: Lending portfolio could grow post-deleveraging; interest income steady QoQ.
  • Cost Efficiencies: Debt reduction + deferred tax benefits (₹218 Cr H1) support margins; rental/other income stable.
  • Strategic Moves: Internal audit change for “restructuring”; new secretarial auditor approved for 5 years. Medium-term EPS potential 12-15 if trading normalizes (FY25 EPS ₹14.71).
  • Outlook: Modest 10-15% revenue growth FY26 if equity markets supportive; ROE >20% sustainable.

Key Risks

  • Market & Concentration Risk: 56% revenue from volatile securities trading; inventory overhang exposes to price crashes.
  • Debt Burden: Leverage high (Borrowings/Equity ~3.2x); interest coverage ~2.2x H1 (strained if rates rise).
  • Governance/Regulatory: Material promoter RPTs approved (despite abstention); forced internal auditor resignation (“personal reasons” post-company request) raises red flags. NBFC under RBI scrutiny (impairments noted historically).
  • Low Engagement: AGM voting turnout low (9-62%); promoter group abstained on conflicted items.
  • Execution/External: Auditor limited review notes no material issues, but deferred tax assets ₹2,380 Cr (reliant on future profits); forex/economic slowdown could hit trading/lending.

Overall Summary: NAGREEKCAP benefits from a profitable trading-NBFC model and deleveraging tailwinds, but faces near-term headwinds from revenue slowdown and volatility. Growth hinges on market recovery; high risks from trading reliance and governance warrant caution. Investment Stance: Hold for traders (monitor Q3); avoid for conservative investors. Target P/B ~1.5x if PAT stabilizes at ₹1,500 Cr FY26.
