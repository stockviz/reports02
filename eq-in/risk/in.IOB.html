Summary Analysis for Indian Overseas Bank (IOB)

IOB, a public sector bank listed on BSE (532388) and NSE (IOB), demonstrated robust Q2/H1 FY26 performance (ended Sep 30, 2025) with record profits, strong growth, and improving asset quality. Below is a structured analysis of headwinds, tailwinds, growth prospects, and key risks based on the provided documents (financial results, analyst call transcript, disclosures, EGM notices, press release).

Tailwinds (Positive Factors Supporting Performance)

  • Exceptional Profitability & Efficiency: H1 net profit hit all-time high of ₹1,226 Cr (+57.79% YoY), driven by NII growth (+20.53% to ₹3,059 Cr in Q2). NIM expanded to 3.21% (+13 bps YoY), cost-to-income ratio improved to 45.76% (-321 bps YoY). ROA 1.20% (+38 bps), ROE 19.95% (+305 bps).
  • Business Momentum: Total business ₹6.17 lakh Cr (+14.1% YoY), advances ₹2.78 lakh Cr (+20.78% YoY), deposits ₹3.39 lakh Cr (+9.15% YoY). CD ratio healthy at 81.98% (+789 bps YoY).
  • Asset Quality Strength: GNPA 1.83% (-89 bps YoY), NNPA 0.28% (-19 bps), PCR 97.48% (+42 bps). Slippage ratio low at 0.11%, recovery ₹874 Cr (incl. ₹461 Cr from write-offs). Credit cost 0.18%.
  • Capital Buffer: CRAR 17.94% (CET1 15.53%), well above 11.5% regulatory minimum. Recent QIP (₹1,400 Cr in FY25) reduced govt stake to 94.61%.
  • Operational Scale: 3,373 branches (+104 YoY), 98% digital transactions, customer base +21 lakh in H1. Awards for grievance redressal (No.1 PSB for 3 months).

Headwinds (Challenges Impacting Performance)

  • Regulatory Penalty: RBI imposed ₹31.8 lakh fine (Sep 30, 2025) for collecting loan charges on PSL accounts ≤₹25,000 (no material financial impact).
  • Provisioning Pressures: Potential ECL provisioning ₹2,700-2,800 Cr under draft RBI guidelines (ballpark; to be phased). Standard provisions ~₹4,000 Cr.
  • Deposit Mobilization Lag: Deposits grew slower (9.15%) vs. advances (20.78%), pressuring CD ratio. CASA ratio dipped to 40.52% (-192 bps YoY).
  • Tax & Forex Exposure: ₹1,141 Cr IT refund (non-P&L); GTA ~₹3,000 Cr deferred tax assets (reversal ongoing). Shift to new tax regime planned Q3/Q4 FY26.
  • Legacy Issues: IIB Malaysia winding-up (₹200 Cr investment; full recovery expected Q3 FY26). Migration discrepancies (₹31 lakh pending reconciliation).

Growth Prospects (Opportunities Ahead)

  • Credit Expansion: FY26 guidance 12-15% credit/12-13% deposit growth (min; realistic 17-18% credit per MD). ₹15,000 Cr corporate pipeline; organic growth in retail/agri/MSME (all branches eligible). GIFT City SBU approval imminent.
  • Non-Fund Income: Fintech tie-ups for credit cards, govt business focus, PSLC sales, recoveries from write-offs. Para-banking (insurance/cards) ramp-up.
  • Digital & Network: 98% digital transactions; refurbished mobile app; AI/tech budget ₹1,700 Cr. 240 new branches (42 opened FY26); 86 lakh new customers in 2.5 yrs.
  • ESG & Segments: Green products (Surya/Tejas loans) gaining traction. Retail/MSME secured portfolio ₹49,000 Cr (micro ₹30,000 Cr).
  • Capital Infusion: Board approval for ₹4,000 Cr QIP (FY26) to dilute govt stake to 75%. FY26 profit retention to boost CRAR to ~19.2%.
