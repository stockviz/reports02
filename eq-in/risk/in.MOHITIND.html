Analysis of Mohit Industries Limited (MOHITIND)

Company Overview: Mohit Industries Ltd. is a Gujarat-based textile company (yarn/textiles segment) listed on BSE/NSE. FY26 H1 (ended Sept 30, 2025) unaudited standalone results show revenue growth amid losses, with a proposed Rs. 25 Cr Rights Issue. All key metrics in Rs. lakhs unless noted. Share capital ~Rs. 14.16 Cr; market cap implied low given penny stock status.

Headwinds

  • Persistent Losses: H1 FY26 net loss Rs. 58 lakhs (improved from Rs. 143 lakhs YoY), but quarterly losses continue (Q2: Rs. 15 lakhs). FY25 full-year loss Rs. 246 lakhs. Negative OCI drags total comprehensive income (-Rs. 137 lakhs H1).
  • High Debt Burden: Total borrowings Rs. 66 Cr (up from Rs. 62 Cr YoY); short-term Rs. 55 Cr. Finance costs Rs. 202 lakhs H1 (up YoY). Interest coverage weak.
  • Low Liquidity: Cash equivalents minimal (Rs. 3.8 lakhs); relies on operating cash flow (positive Rs. 46 lakhs H1, but volatile).
  • Auditor Qualification: Non-compliance with Ind AS 19 (no accrual for post-employment benefits; quantum unquantified but could inflate profits).
  • Low Shareholder Participation: AGM e-voting turnout low (public: ~3.28 lakhs shares out of ~3.3 Cr free float); minor dissent (0.02-0.6%).

Tailwinds

  • Revenue Momentum: H1 revenue Rs. 66.78 Cr (+42% YoY from Rs. 46.89 Cr); Q2 Rs. 34.54 Cr (+34% YoY). Single-segment (textiles) benefits from volume/cost efficiencies.
  • Cost Optimizations:
    • Solar power: Reduced energy costs; Q2 at 65% capacity (weather-hit), expected ramp-up.
    • SIS guideline change: Raw materials off mandatory list, enabling cheaper imports.
  • Improved Margins: EBITDA-like pre-tax loss narrowed to Rs. 78 lakhs H1 (-52% YoY). Employee costs up but controlled.
  • Capex Progress: PPE up 46% to Rs. 266 Cr, signaling expansion (cash outflow Rs. 834 lakhs H1).
  • Rights Issue Approved: Rs. 25 Cr via equity to eligible shareholders (committee formed); could deleverage/de-risk.

Growth Prospects

  • Short-Term (FY26): Revenue growth >40% sustainable via cost savings (solar full capacity, imports). Rights Issue funds capex/working capital; target profitability in H2 per notes.
  • Medium-Term: Textile sector tailwinds (export demand, Surat hub). Investments in assets (PPE +46%) position for capacity expansion. Associates (Mohit Overseas/Yarns) add minor profit share (Rs. 1 lakh H1).
  • Long-Term: Debt reduction post-Rights could improve ROE (currently negative). Consolidated investments Rs. 149 Cr provide OCI buffer/volatility hedge.
  • Catalysts: Improved solar output, raw material savings, Rights success (record date pending).

Key Risks

Risk Category Description Mitigation
Financial High leverage (debt/equity ~2x); covenant breaches possible. Negative working capital cycle (trade payables down 88%). Rights Issue proceeds.
Operational Weather-dependent solar (65% Q2 utilization); raw material volatility/textile cycles. Diversified sourcing post-SIS change.
Compliance/Governance Ind AS 19 deviation (could lead to restated losses); related-party approvals (Saboo family) with minor dissent. Promoter voting dominance (96% shares). Board notes “negligible” impact; passed resolutions.
Market Textile price pressure; low liquidity (cash Rs. 3.8 lakhs). Rights dilution (~15-20% at current levels). Revenue growth track record.
Execution Rights Issue delays/undersubscription; capex delays (CWIP down to zero). Committee oversight; strong promoter holding.

Overall Summary: Mohit shows turnaround signs (revenue +42%, loss halving) driven by cost efficiencies, but high debt and losses remain drags. Rights Issue is pivotal for deleveraging/growth. Investment Stance: Speculative buy for risk-tolerant investors betting on textile recovery/cost savings; monitor Rights pricing/record date and Q3 results. Positive: Growth trajectory; Negative: Debt overhang. Target FY26 breakeven plausible if trends hold.
