Analysis of SAB Events & Governance Now Media Limited (SABEVENTS)

SAB Events & Governance Now Media Limited (CIN: L22222MH2014PLC254848, listed on BSE/NSE) operates in a single segment: Digital Media Websites & MICE (Meetings, Incentives, Conferences, Exhibitions). The company is facing severe financial distress, as evidenced by ongoing losses, negative equity, high leverage, and initiation of Pre-Packaged Insolvency Resolution Process (PPIRP) under Section 54A of the Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code (IBC), 2016. Below is a structured analysis based on the provided documents (financial results for H1 FY26 ended Sep 30, 2025; board outcomes; AGM resolutions; creditor committee intimation).

Headwinds (Key Challenges)

  • Persistent Losses and Deteriorating Financials:
    • H1 FY26 net loss: ₹38.31 lakhs (vs. ₹63.70 lakhs in H1 FY25); Q2 FY26 loss: ₹14.01 lakhs.
    • Revenue stagnant/low: H1 FY26 ₹95.93 lakhs (up slightly from ₹72.94 lakhs YoY but far below FY25 full-year ₹173.88 lakhs).
    • Negative equity: ₹(237.58) lakhs as of Sep 30, 2025 (worsened from ₹(199.27) lakhs as of Mar 31, 2025).
    • Current liabilities 4.44x current assets; unable to service debt obligations.
  • Insolvency Proceedings: PPIRP approved at Aug 14, 2025 board meeting and Sep 25, 2025 AGM (99.99% approval). 1st Creditors’ Committee meeting on Dec 1, 2025. Indicates acute liquidity crisis.
  • Auditor Qualifications (P. Parikh & Associates’ review report):
    • No impairment test on ₹22.58 lakhs goodwill (strong indication due to losses).
    • Unprovided interest ~₹6.04 lakhs (Q2 FY26) + prior periods (Oct 2022-Mar 2025), understating expenses/borrowings (non-compliant with Ind AS 109/36).
  • Material Going Concern Uncertainty: Explicit note in financials highlights inability to generate cash flows or raise long-term funds without PPIRP success.

Tailwinds (Supportive Factors)

  • Structured Restructuring via PPIRP: Pre-packaged process allows faster resolution (vs. standard CIRP), with board/creditors’ approval. Resolution Professional (Mr. Kedar Parshuram Mulye) appointed (99.99% AGM approval). Potential for base resolution plan negotiation pre-filing.
  • Leadership Stability: Kailasnath Markand Adhikari (DIN: 07009389) appointed Chairman (Nov 12, 2025) with strong credentials (LSE double postgrad, ex-Planning Commission). Son of outgoing promoter Markand Adhikari (reclassified to public category, 4.59% stake).
  • Slight Revenue Recovery: Q2 FY26 revenue ₹55.25 lakhs (down QoQ from ₹40.68 lakhs but stable YoY). Notes mention gradual resumption of ground events.
  • Shareholder Alignment: High AGM voting turnout/support (37.56% polled, ~99.99% in favor across resolutions, including capital increase).
  • Low Debt Visibility: Finance costs minimal (₹0.01 lakhs in H1); borrowings not prominently listed (focus on trade payables ₹200.71 lakhs).

Growth Prospects

  • Limited Near-Term; Moderate Long-Term Potential:
    • Digital Media & MICE Tailwinds: India’s digital media market growing at 15-20% CAGR (ad spends, online events). MICE sector rebounding post-COVID (government push via “Incredible India” campaigns).
    • Revenue from operations forms 100% of income (no other income in H1). Cash from operations positive ₹1.12 lakhs in H1 FY26 (vs. negative ₹26.75 lakhs FY25), aided by working capital adjustments.
    • PPIRP Upside: Successful resolution could inject capital, impair legacy liabilities (e.g., goodwill, unprovided interest), and enable expansion. Authorised capital increase approved at AGM.
    • Challenges Capping Growth: Single-segment vulnerability; FY25 full-year revenue only ₹173.88 lakhs. EPS negative (H1: -₹0.37). Prospects hinge on PPIRP outcome and event revival (noted as “gradual”).
  • Outlook: Neutral-low. FY26 revenue could stabilize at ₹200-250 lakhs if events pick up, but profitability unlikely without restructuring. Long-term: 10-15% growth if insolvency resolved and digital pivot succeeds.
