Thermax Limited (THERMAX) Analysis: Q2 FY26 Overview

Thermax Limited, a leading energy and environment solutions provider, reported consolidated Q2 FY26 results with order book growth amid revenue and profit declines due to one-off factors. Below is a structured analysis based on the provided filings (financial results, investor presentation, order announcements, and related disclosures). All figures are in Rs. Crore unless stated.

Headwinds (Challenges Pressuring Performance)

  • Profitability Pressure: Consolidated PBT down 35% YoY to 174 (from 266), PAT down 40% to 119 (from 198). Key drags: Absence of Rs. 66 Cr PSI incentive (received in Q2 FY25), Rs. 42 Cr additional cost provisions in Industrial Infra due to overruns (mainly one project). Industrial Infra PBIT negative at -15 (-1.6% margin).
  • Revenue Dip: Consolidated revenue down 5% YoY to 2,474 (from 2,616), driven by product mix shifts and lower Industrial Infra execution.
  • Segment Weakness: Industrial Infra order bookings down 16% YoY; Chemicals profitability down due to higher fixed costs, input costs, and mix changes. Global resin market challenges from geopolitics.
  • Execution Risks: Cost overruns highlight project management issues in large EPC contracts.

Tailwinds (Positive Momentum)

  • Robust Order Book: Q2 bookings up 6% YoY to 3,551; order balance up 6% to 12,300 (H1 FY26). Quality improving—low-margin orders reduced (e.g., FGD/BioCNG backlog down). Strong inflows from Metals (24%), Power, Petrochem, F&B.
  • Standalone Strength: Revenue 1,505 (down 1% YoY), but PAT up 109% to 226, boosted by Rs. 141 Cr dividend from subsidiary.
  • Strategic Wins: Rs. 580 Cr international order from Dangote (Nigeria) for 4x400 TPH boilers (22-month execution). Repeat business builds trust. Other highlights: 100MW+ data center cooling, biomass/ethanol/CBG plants, HRSG retrofits, emission controls.
  • Margin Focus: Reducing low-contribution orders; Green Solutions margins up due to efficiency/insurance claims.

Growth Prospects

  • High-Growth Areas: Data centers (next-gen cooling), biofuels (1G ethanol entry, CBG ~2,700 TPD), renewables (wind commissioning, hybrid projects). Metals/Power/Petrochem OB strong; water desalination/emission controls gaining.
  • Diversification: International exposure (e.g., UAE HRSG, Nigeria); 16 plants, 90+ countries. Subsidiary expansions (TBSPL now wholly owned).
  • Order Pipeline: H1 FY26 OB up across Industrial Products (+18%), Green Solutions (+415%, aided by TOESL reporting change adding Rs. 197 Cr visibility). FY26 revenue potential from Rs. 12,300 Cr backlog.
  • Long-Term: Energy transition tailwinds (clean energy/water/air); digital solutions, maintenance services for recurring revenue.

Key Risks

  • Legal/Contingencies: Rs. 218 Cr arbitral award (provision Rs. 50 Cr; stay granted, Rs. 218 Cr deposited—refundable if won). Excise demands Rs. 1,383 Cr under Supreme Court appeal (no provision, confident of win).
  • Execution/Overruns: Industrial Infra vulnerable to cost escalations in complex projects (e.g., TBWES retrofits).
  • External: Commodity volatility (steel down 2%, copper/aluminium up 7-10%); geopolitics impacting resins/exports. Flooding risks (e.g., Tamil Nadu solar claims settled Rs. 28 Cr).
  • Subsidiary Changes: Recent acquisitions/divestments (e.g., Covance sale, TBSPL full ownership) may cause short-term volatility; impairment reversals noted.
  • Macro: Sugar/Distillery OB drop; dependency on capex cycles in metals/power.

Summary

Overall Outlook: Positive with Near-Term Caution. Thermax demonstrates resilience via 6% order growth and quality backlog (Rs. 12,300 Cr), fueled by strategic wins like Rs. 580 Cr Dangote order and green initiatives (data centers, biofuels). Tailwinds from sector demand (metals/power) and international repeat business outweigh headwinds like cost overruns and one-offs (no PSI). Profits dipped sharply but standalone shines on dividends. Growth prospects strong in energy transition, targeting execution efficiency for margin recovery (target PBIT 8-10%+). Key Monitor: Project overruns and legal resolutions. Stock likely supported by OB visibility; suitable for long-term investors eyeing India’s infra/green push. (Ratings context: Hold/Buy on dips; FY26 revenue/EBITDA growth est. 10-15% if execution improves.)
