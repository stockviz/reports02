Thermax Limited (NSE: THERMAX) - Q2 FY26 Analysis

Thermax Limited, an engineering company focused on energy and environment solutions (boilers, cooling, water treatment, chemicals, renewables), reported consolidated order booking growth of 6% YoY to Rs. 3,551 Cr and order book expansion to Rs. 12,300 Cr (+6% YoY). However, revenue declined 5% YoY to Rs. 2,474 Cr, with PBT/PAT down 35%/40% to Rs. 174 Cr/Rs. 119 Cr due to Industrial Infra cost overruns (Rs. 42 Cr provision) and absence of Rs. 66 Cr PSI incentive (received last year). Standalone PAT surged 109% to Rs. 226 Cr, boosted by Rs. 141 Cr dividend from subsidiary. A major Rs. 580 Cr international boiler order from Dangote (Nigeria) was announced post-results, signaling execution strength. Order quality is improving (low-margin orders down from 12% to 5% of backlog).

Below is a structured summary of headwinds, tailwinds, growth prospects, and key risks based on the provided documents (Q2 results, investor presentation, order filings, financial statements).

Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts)

  • Robust Order Inflows & Backlog: +6% YoY order book growth; strong sector demand from Metals (24%), Power/Refinery/Petrochem (16-17%), F&B (10%). Low-margin orders reduced (H1 FY26: Rs. 646 Cr vs. Rs. 1,153 Cr in H1 FY25).
  • International Expansion: Rs. 580 Cr repeat order from Dangote (Nigeria) for 4x400 TPH boilers (22-month execution); prior US$157 Mn project history. Export order booking up (Q2: Rs. 1,224 Cr).
  • Segment Momentum: | Segment | Order Booking YoY | Key Wins | |———————-|——————-|———-| | Industrial Products | +18% | Data centers (100MW+), biomass boilers, emission control (FGD), ultrapure water for semiconductors. | | Green Solutions | +415% | CBG production (~2,700T), biofuels (1G ethanol), hybrid renewables (140MW Jafrabad, TN wind). | | Chemicals | Flat | Water treatment orders (Rs. 12 Cr), construction chemicals pipeline. |
  • Strategic Moves: TBSPL (Bioenergy) now wholly owned (+ve for consolidation); reporting change at TOESL adds Rs. 197 Cr visibility without revenue impact.
  • Standalone Strength: Dividend income (Rs. 141 Cr) and order book +9% to Rs. 6,981 Cr.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Profitability Pressure: PBT margin down to 7% (10.2% YoY) due to Industrial Infra losses (-1.6% PBIT margin; cost overruns). Chemicals margins fell (9.8% vs. 16.1%) from fixed costs/input hikes/product mix.
  • Revenue Dip: -5% YoY; Industrial Infra order booking -16% (lumpy prior year).
  • Sector Weakness: Sugar/Distillery orders down (historical key contributor).
  • One-offs: No PSI incentive (Rs. 66 Cr last year); YoY not directly comparable.
  • Input Costs: Metals (Cu/Al/Zn) up 7-10% QoQ; Steel down 2%, Nickel stable – minor net impact but volatile.

Growth Prospects

  • High-Growth Areas: Data centers, semiconductors, biofuels/CBG/renewables (e.g., 22 TPD CBG plants commissioned). Green Solutions PBIT margin up to 6.1% (operational efficiency + insurance claims).
  • Order Pipeline: OB improving in Power/Petrochem; total OB Rs. 12,300 Cr (~2.5x trailing revenue).
  • Diversification: 16 plants globally; 90+ countries; new Jhagadia resin plant operational. Ventures into 1G ethanol, hybrid solar-wind.
  • Medium-Term Outlook: Manufacturing PMI ~58.4 supports demand; export/domestic mix balanced. FY25 revenue Rs. 10,389 Cr base; analysts call transcript (available on website) likely highlights execution from strong backlog.
  • Potential Upside: Rs. 580 Cr Nigeria order adds to Infra; global resin alliances for high-purity/specialty products.
