Gillette India Limited (GILLETTE) Analysis

Based on Q2 & H1 FY26 (ended Sep 30, 2025) Unaudited Results, Segment Data, Balance Sheet, Cash Flows, and Announcements

Gillette India delivered solid Q2/H1 performance with revenue growth of 4%/6% YoY (₹811 Cr/₹1,518 Cr) and PAT growth of 8%/16% YoY (₹144 Cr/₹289 Cr), driven by Grooming (82% of sales) and Oral Care segments. Margins expanded (PBT ~24% in Q2) amid productivity gains, with strong cash generation (₹304 Cr operating cash flow in H1) and balance sheet growth (total assets +12% YoY to ₹2,149 Cr; cash +42% to ₹590 Cr). Press release highlights “balanced growth” via innovation and execution; new Executive Director appointment signals stability.

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Robust Topline & Bottomline Momentum: Grooming revenue +3% YoY (₹666 Cr Q2), Oral Care + flat/+6% H1; overall H1 sales +6% despite prior FY’s 9-month structure. PAT growth outpaced revenue due to cost productivity.
  • Margin Expansion & Efficiency: PBT ₹193 Cr (+9% Q2 YoY); EPS ₹44 (Q2, +8%). Controlled ad/sales promo (15% of sales) and other expenses.
  • Strong Liquidity & Cash Flows: Cash pile at ₹590 Cr (+42% YoY); H1 op. cash flow ₹304 Cr supports dividends (₹153 Cr paid) and capex (₹43 Cr).
  • Strategic Focus: MD emphasizes “superiority” in products/packaging/comms/execution in high-frequency categories (shaving/oral care). P&G backing ensures brand strength.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Cost Pressures: Raw material costs +44% YoY (₹248 Cr Q2), driving total expenses +2% YoY. Inventory build-up (₹510 Cr, +12% YoY) signals potential demand softening or stocking.
  • Segment Imbalance: Grooming dominates (82% revenue), but Oral Care growth muted Q2 (+ flat YoY); total segment results pressured by finance costs.
  • Comparability Issues: Prior FY (Jul’24-Mar’25) was 9 months; H1 FY25 combines audited/unaudited quarters (Note 8), inflating YoY base slightly.
  • Working Capital Strain: Trade receivables stable but inventories/financial assets up, partially offset by payables growth.

Growth Prospects

  • High (Medium-Term): Integrated strategy targets sustainable growth in India’s expanding FMCG market (shaving/oral care ~daily essentials). H1 revenue +6% positions FY26 for 10-12% growth if productivity sustains. Grooming (razors/blades) benefits from premiumization/innovation; Oral Care upside from brushes/toothpastes. No subsidiaries/JVs keeps ops lean; CSR (P&G Shiksha) aids brand equity.
  • Catalysts: Superior retail execution, productivity across value chain, agile org structure. Cash hoard enables capex/investments. FY26 full-year alignment post-FY change improves reporting.

Key Risks

Risk Category Description Mitigation
Cost Inflation Raw material volatility (e.g., metals/plastics for razors); Q2 spike eroded some gains. Productivity efforts; hedging (minor FX unrealized loss noted).
Competition Intense in razors (local blades) and oral care (Colgate, etc.); market share pressure. Brand superiority; P&G R&D.
Demand/Macro Inventory build signals potential rural slowdown or monsoon impact; FMCG volume risks. Premium focus; urban strength.
Forex/Regulatory Import reliance exposes to USD/INR; SEBI compliance (e.g., FY change). Strong cash buffers.
Execution High dividend payout (47% of H1 PAT); capex modest (₹43 Cr). People costs +11% YoY. Stable leadership (new DIN allotted); no debt.

Overall Outlook: Positive with tailwinds outweighing headwinds. Steady growth (6-10% revenue FY26e) likely, supported by 25%+ margins and ₹1,000 Cr+ cash fortress. Risks manageable but monitor costs/competition. Stock implications: Earnings beat supports premium valuation (trading ~50x FY26e EPS est.). Buy/Hold on dips.
