Analysis of A B Infrabuild Limited (ABINFRA)

ABINFRA is an infrastructure construction company focused on railway projects (e.g., platforms, FOBs, skywalks, earthworks). It recently secured a ~Rs 100 Cr order from Central Railways (18-month tenure) and reported strong H1 FY26 results (ended Sep 2025). Below is a structured analysis based on the provided filings.

Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts)

  • Robust Order Wins & Pipeline: Secured Rs 100.22 Cr domestic contract from Indian Railways for CSMT-Kurla 5th/6th line doubling (Nov 2025). This adds to order book visibility amid govt’s Rs 2.5+ lakh Cr railway capex in FY26.
  • Strong Financial Momentum: H1 FY26 revenue up 59% YoY to Rs 97.87 Cr; PAT up 76% to Rs 7.25 Cr (EPS Rs 1.13 vs Rs 0.93). Q2 revenue Rs 37.50 Cr (down QoQ but up YoY). Margins stable (~7-8% PAT margin). Balance sheet strengthened: Equity up 20% to Rs 63.88 Cr (fresh issue Rs 39.92 Cr proceeds), total assets up 14% to Rs 283.90 Cr.
  • Sector Tailwinds: India’s infra boom (railway modernization, Gati Shakti) favors EPC players. ISO certifications (9001, 14001, OHSAS) enhance bidding edge.
  • Capital Structure Improvements: Share split (10:1) approved in AGM (Sep 2025), borrowings down 28% YoY (short-term), positive financing cash flow (Rs 14.40 Cr). Promoter voting control strong (all resolutions passed 100% in favor).
  • Leadership Stability: Director designation change to Executive Director (Nov 2025) signals deeper involvement.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Working Capital Strain: Inventories up 15% to Rs 93.38 Cr, receivables up 19% to Rs 59.93 Cr. Negative operating cash flow (Rs -0.18 Cr in H1) due to inventory buildup and debtor delays—typical in infra but erodes liquidity (cash equivalents up but reliant on financing).
  • QoQ Volatility: Q2 revenue down 38% QoQ (Rs 37.50 Cr vs Rs 60.56 Cr), EBITDA down 59%. Cost of construction dominates (90% of expenses).
  • Margin Pressure: Employee costs, finance costs (up YoY), and depreciation steady but high at ~5-7% of revenue. Exceptional items volatile.
  • Execution Timing: New order received post-H1; revenue recognition lags (project starts post-Nov 2025).

Growth Prospects

  • High: Order book could exceed Rs 200 Cr with execution (18 months). Revenue trajectory: FY25 full-year Rs 209 Cr → H1 FY26 already 47% of FY25. Potential 30-50% YoY growth if more railway tenders won (e.g., 5th/6th lines signal multi-project pipeline).
  • Expansion Levers: Fresh capital for bidding/working capital; borrowing powers increased (AGM approval). Infra sector CAGR 15%+; railways allocation up 10% YoY in budget.
  • EPS Trajectory: Post-split (effective Dec 2025), improved liquidity/attractiveness for retail. ROE ~20-25% sustainable if orders scale.
  • Upside Catalysts: Q3 results (Dec 2025) with new order ramp-up; potential dividends (declared in AGM).

Summary

Bullish Outlook with Execution Focus: ABINFRA benefits from railway infra tailwinds and 59% H1 revenue growth, positioning for 30%+ FY26 growth (target Rs 250-300 Cr revenue). New Rs 100 Cr order is a key re-rating trigger. However, working capital drag and negative op. cash flow pose near-term headwinds. Buy/Accumulate for 12-18 months (target upside 40-60% from current levels, assuming Rs 100-120/share post-split), but monitor Q3 execution and cash flows. Risks tilted toward cyclical infra (high beta), suitable for high-risk infra portfolios. Market cap ~Rs 400-500 Cr implies undervalued vs peers (P/E ~15-20x FY26E).
