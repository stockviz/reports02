Analysis of Everest Industries Limited (EVERESTIND)

Everest Industries Limited operates in two primary segments: Building Products (roofing sheets, boards) and Steel Buildings (pre-engineered structures). The company reported weak H1 FY26 results (ended Sep 30, 2025), with revenue declines and losses amid segment-specific challenges. Non-core asset sales provide liquidity relief. Below is a structured analysis based on the provided disclosures (Q2/H1 FY26 results, property sale, and AGM outcomes).

Headwinds (Key Pressures)

  • Revenue Decline & Segment Weakness: H1 standalone revenue fell 13% YoY to ₹775 Cr (consolidated ₹809 Cr, down ~10%). Steel Buildings segment revenue halved YoY (₹205 Cr vs. ₹311 Cr), with losses widening due to weak demand (possibly industrial slowdown). Building Products stable but Q2 revenue down 3% YoY.
  • Profitability Erosion: H1 standalone pre-tax loss of ₹7 Cr (vs. ₹15 Cr profit YoY); consolidated loss ₹21 Cr (vs. ₹8 Cr profit). Q2 losses deepened (standalone ₹17 Cr, consolidated ₹25 Cr) due to high raw material costs, inventory buildup (₹367 Cr inventories), and forex/other expenses.
  • Cash Flow Strain: Negative operating cash flow (standalone ₹13 Cr outflow; consolidated ₹17 Cr), driven by working capital (inventories up, receivables steady at ₹166 Cr, payables down). Borrowings rose (standalone short-term up to ₹90 Cr).
  • Cyclical Exposure: Dependence on construction/infra sectors amid potential economic slowdown (post-monsoon demand weakness).

Tailwinds (Supportive Factors)

  • Asset Monetization: Active divestment of non-core assets for liquidity:
    • Noida property sold in H1 FY25 (₹3.8 Cr exceptional gain).
    • Mumbai office sold Nov 3, 2025 for ₹3.75 Cr (₹3.3 Cr cash pending).
    • 23.32 acres Tamil Nadu land approved for sale (classified as held-for-sale at ₹0.65 Cr).
    • Advances received (₹7 Cr in H1).
  • Incentives & Cost Management: Prior GST incentives (₹95 Cr, partly exceptional). Inventory reduction in H1 (+₹57 Cr YoY drawdown). Employee/share-based costs adjusted lower.
  • Balance Sheet Stability: Equity base solid (₹615 Cr standalone), low net debt relative to assets (total assets ~₹1,210 Cr). Unallocable income supports.
  • Governance: Overwhelming AGM approval (Sep 18, 2025) for MD/CEO reappointment, ESOP amendments, and auditors (99%+ votes).

Growth Prospects (Medium-Term Opportunities)

  • Core Business Recovery: Building Products (70% revenue) showed resilience (H1 ₹570 Cr); potential from housing/infra push (e.g., PMAY, industrial parks). Steel Buildings could rebound with capex cycle (e.g., data centers, logistics).
  • Capacity Expansion: Lakhmapur plant incentives to drive efficiency/margins. New products/export via subsidiaries (Mauritius/UAE).
  • Liquidity Boost: Asset sales (~₹4-10 Cr near-term) to deleverage (finance costs ₹10-14 Cr H1). Positive investing cash flow from sales/ICDs.
  • Strategic Flexibility: Promoter support strong (50% holding, 100% voting). ESOP scheme for talent retention.

Outlook: FY26 revenue growth low-single digit if Steel Buildings stabilizes; EBITDA margins ~5-7% possible with cost controls/asset gains. Target: Break-even by FY26-end.

Key Risks (High-Impact Concerns)

Risk Category Description Mitigation
Operational Prolonged Steel Buildings slump (40% revenue drop); high inventories/working capital drag. Asset sales, inventory optimization.
Financial Rising borrowings (₹110 Cr consolidated); negative CFO persists. Forex/interest rate volatility. Divestments, ICD repayments.
Market Infra/construction slowdown (e.g., monsoon delays, elections). Raw material inflation (steel). Segment diversification, exports.
Execution Asset sale delays (e.g., TN land conditions). Exceptional items mask core losses. Board approvals in place.
Regulatory Compliance (SEBI LODR disclosures); tax disputes (GST incentives). Unmodified audit reviews.

Overall Summary: Everest faces near-term headwinds from Steel Buildings weakness and cash burn, leading to H1 losses, but tailwinds from ₹10+ Cr asset sales and incentives offer deleveraging runway. Growth hinges on infra recovery and execution; risks center on cyclical demand and liquidity. Neutral outlook – monitor Q3 for segment turnaround. Stock trades at ~1x FY26E sales (value territory), but volatility high due to small-cap status. Investors: Buy on dips for asset value play.
