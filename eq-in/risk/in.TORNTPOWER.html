Summary Analysis: Torrent Power Limited (TORNTPOWER)

Torrent Power, an integrated power utility (part of ~₹45,000 Cr Torrent Group), reported strong Q2 FY26 results with 10% YoY revenue growth to ₹7,876 Cr, 19% EBITDA growth to ₹1,584 Cr, and 48% TCI/PAT growth to ₹730 Cr, driven by merchant gas/LNG sales and lower finance costs. H1 FY26 showed flat TCI at ₹1,469 Cr amid softer volumes. The company has ~5 GW installed capacity (thermal dominant, growing RE mix), low distribution losses (<3% in licensed areas), and a robust pipeline targeting ~10 GW generation + 3 GW PSP. Market cap ~₹61,333 Cr (as of Sep 30, 2025). Below is a structured analysis of headwinds, tailwinds, growth prospects, and key risks.

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Operational Excellence in Distribution: Lowest AT&C losses in India (2.34% FY25 licensed areas; Bhiwandi reduced from 58% to <10%). 99.9% power availability, serving 4.21M customers across key regions (Gujarat, Maharashtra, UP, UTs). ~48% of thermal/RE capacity tied to own discoms for stable cash flows.
  • Merchant Gas/LNG Upside: Flexible 1.5 GW (~50%) gas capacity capitalized on spot power/LNG sales, boosting Q2 contribution by 20% YoY. Direct LNG imports provide cost edge.
  • Financial Health: Net debt/EBITDA 1.41x, debt/equity 0.4x (FY25); ROE 18.4%, ROCE 12.7%. Finance costs down (H1: ₹428 Cr vs ₹532 Cr YoY).
  • Group Synergies: Torrent Group’s pharma/CGD scale aids talent/financing. Cleaner fuel mix (81% low-carbon).
  • Policy Support: Deemed license renewals (25 yrs post-2025/28), SHAKTI coal policy for new thermal.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Volume/Margin Pressure: H1 revenue down 3% YoY; thermal PLF dipped to 32% (vs 46% YoY) due to gas unavailability/high import costs. RE PLF stable but merchant-dependent.
  • High Capex Drag: CWIP ₹3,625 Cr (Sep’25); H1 capex ~₹2,700 Cr strained cash (investing outflow ₹3,567 Cr). Depreciation up (H1: ₹779 Cr vs ₹730 Cr).
  • Segment Volatility: Renewables EBITDA share ~19% but under development (~3.8 GWp); distribution stable but franchisee losses higher (e.g., SMK ~29%).
  • Comparative Restatement: Q2/H1 FY25 restated post-RE demerger to subsidiary (impacts YoY growth optics).

Growth Prospects (High Potential)

  • Capacity Ramp-Up: Installed ~5 GW → ~10 GW (RE to 5.4 GWp; 1.6 GW coal under dev; PSP 3 GW operational by FY28). Pipeline: 8.4 GW PSP, transmission (e.g., 104 km 400kV).
  • RE & Green Molecules: 3.8 GWp RE under installation (tariffs ₹2.94-4.87/kWh); green H2/ammonia pilots (Gorakhpur plant: 72 TPA). Flexible RTC pooling (RE+gas) for merchant sales.
  • Transmission/Franchisee: New awards (Solapur, Khavda RE evacuation); franchise extensions possible (Bhiwandi +5 yrs).
  • Thermal Expansion: LoA for 1,600 MW MP coal (₹22,000 Cr, ₹5.83/kWh, 25-yr PPA; SCOD 66/72 months).
  • EBITDA Trajectory: H1 ₹3,172 Cr; FY26E potential >₹11,000-12,000 Cr on capex fruition. Integrated model (~60% EBITDA from gen+dist) supports 15-20% CAGR.

Key Risks

Risk Category Description Mitigation
Fuel/Commodity Gas scarcity/high LNG prices (DGEN impaired ₹2,300 Cr earlier; intermittent ops). Coal linkages critical for MP project. Direct imports, SHAKTI policy, merchant flexibility.
Regulatory/Execution Tariff delays, bidding losses, NCLT approvals (e.g., RE scheme). License revocations (unlikely per rules). Proven track record (e.g., Bhiwandi model); CERC RoE 15.5%.
Financial Debt rise to ₹9,063 Cr (Sep’25); forex/interest rate volatility. Strong coverage (DSCR 4.5x, ISCR 7.3x); low leverage.
Impairment/Operational Underutilized assets (DGEN VIU test passed but sensitive to fuel/demand assumptions). Weather/PLF variability in RE. Periodic reviews; diversified portfolio.
Market/Competition Power exchange volatility; RE oversupply pressuring tariffs. Integrated ops, 47% promoter stake for agility.

Overall Outlook: Positive (Buy/Hold). Tailwinds from execution and merchant upside outweigh headwinds; growth prospects strong amid India’s energy transition (RE/PSP focus). Monitor fuel prices, project timelines, and H2 FY26 PLF. Investor Day (Dec 5, 2025) likely to elaborate pipeline. Target FY26 TCI ₹6,000+ Cr on capacity addition.
