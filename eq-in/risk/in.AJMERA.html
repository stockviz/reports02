Ajmera Realty & Infra India Ltd. (AJMERA) Analysis

Overview: Ajmera Realty & Infra India Ltd., a Mumbai-based real estate developer, reported strong H1 FY26 results (ended Sep 30, 2025) with presales surging 48% YoY to INR 828 Cr, driven by new launches like Manhattan 2 (INR 666 Cr contribution). Revenue grew 20% YoY to INR 481 Cr, but margins compressed. The company maintains a healthy balance sheet (D/E 0.55x), robust revenue visibility (~INR 8,000 Cr), and a massive owned land bank (10.5 MSF potential). A 1:5 stock split (Rs.10 to Rs.2 face value) was approved by the board to boost liquidity, pending shareholder nod via postal ballot. Overall, the company is on track for FY26 sales guidance of INR 1,600 Cr, with Wadala/Kanjurmarg as key growth drivers.

Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts)
  • Robust Demand & Sales Momentum: H1 presales +48% YoY (INR 828 Cr), Q2 record-high at INR 720 Cr. New launches (Manhattan 2, Thirty3.15) contributed 83% of sales; 38-81% inventory sold in ongoing projects.
  • Strong Collections & Cash Flow: +52% YoY to INR 454 Cr; net operating cash flow negative but improving (projected INR 1,526 Cr surplus from ongoing + INR 1,016 Cr from launches).
  • De-Leveraging Balance Sheet: D/E at 0.55x (down from 0.97x in FY23); avg. cost of debt ~11.5% (vs. repo 6.5%), secured debt reduced.
  • Prime Land Bank & Pipeline: 10.5 MSF owned land (Wadala: INR 12,450 Cr GDV; Kanjurmarg: 82L sq ft, INR 29,000 Cr GDV). Revenue visibility INR 3,599 Cr (OC/ongoing) + INR 4,357 Cr launches = INR 7,956 Cr.
  • Strategic Positioning: Focus on high-demand Mumbai micro-markets (Wadala, Andheri); diversification into uber-luxury/office. Portfolio scaling to 3.4 MSF; 46k+ families served.
  • Corporate Actions: Stock split to enhance retail participation/liquidity (current free-float ~32%, mcap INR 3,896 Cr).
  • ESG & Governance: Initiatives in sustainability, CSR (education/healthcare); strong policies (insider trading, whistleblower).
Headwinds (Challenges)
  • Margin Pressure: EBITDA margin dipped to 29% (-384 bps YoY) due to higher expenses (+27% YoY); PAT margin 15% (-254 bps). Q2 EBITDA -7% YoY despite 184% sales growth.
  • Revenue Recognition Lag: Booked revenue (INR 481 Cr) trails presales (INR 828 Cr) – typical in realty but signals slower conversion.
  • QoQ Slowdown: Q2 sales +567% QoQ but collections -6%; EBITDA -24% QoQ – lumpy quarterly trends.
  • Macro Sensitivity: High interest rates, urban slowdown could impact affordability; collections reliant on buyer financing.
  • Geographic Concentration: Heavy Mumbai/Bangalore exposure (micro-markets like Wadala); limited diversification.
Growth Prospects
  • Near-Term (FY26): On track for INR 1,600 Cr sales (+48% YoY); H2 launches: Boutique office (Wadala, INR 1,800 Cr GDV, 6L sq ft), Codename projects (total pipeline GDV INR 4,357 Cr). ~1,000 possessions targeted.
  • Medium-Term (FY27+): Wadala unlocks (INR 12,450 Cr GDV over 4 years); Kanjurmarg masterplan (INR 29,000 Cr). Total potential ~INR 44,000 Cr GDV; portfolio to 126L sq ft via phases/JVs.
  • Strategic Roadmap: 5X growth via owned land unlocking, inorganic (JVs/JDA/redev), diverse segments (luxury/office). Sales trajectory: FY23 INR 431 Cr → FY26E INR 1,600 Cr → FY27+ INR 1,600+ Cr.
  • Upside Levers: RERA timelines on track (e.g., Manhattan1 Jun’27); de-leveraging supports capex; stock split aids visibility.
Key Risks
Risk Category Description Mitigation
Execution Delays in launches/possessions (e.g., approvals, construction); 8 ongoing projects (1.8 MSF). Strong track record (533 units delivered H1); RERA-compliant timelines.
Financial Debt ~INR 652 Cr (despite low D/E); interest cost sensitivity. Margin erosion from cost inflation. De-leveraging trend; projected INR 2,872 Cr cash inflows.
Market/Regulatory Realty cyclicality, RERA penalties, policy shifts (e.g., taxes, affordability). Competition in Mumbai. Owned land reduces land cost risk; diversified pipeline.
Concentration 68% promoter holding; Wadala/Kanjurmarg dependency. Inorganic expansion; healthy liquidity (INR 106 Cr cash).
External Economic slowdown, rising rates impacting buyer sentiment; forex (intl. subs). 52% YoY collections growth; low project debt.

Overall Outlook: Positive with Moderate Risks. Tailwinds from land bank and sales momentum outweigh headwinds; growth prospects strong (multi-year GDV visibility supports 20-30% CAGR). Stock split signals confidence. Monitor margins and execution. Target FY26 guidance achievable; potential re-rating on Wadala unlocks.
