Vardhman Textiles Limited (VTL) Analysis

FY26 Context: Q2 revenue stable at ₹2,468 Cr (down 4% YoY), EBITDA margin at 15.5% (down 110 bps YoY), PAT ₹189 Cr (down 14% YoY). H1 revenue up 1% YoY. Resilient 90-95% utilizations amid headwinds.

Headwinds

  • US Tariffs (50% duty): Order delays (30-45 days), 20-25% volume cuts from US-linked customers; diversions to Vietnam/Bangladesh. Q3/Q4 impact expected (export-heavy quarters).
  • Cotton Cost Disadvantage: Domestic prices $0.80-0.84/lb vs. global $0.76-0.78; MSP up 8%, CCI monopoly pricing (NY futures +$0.13-0.14). Yarn prices down 2-3%.
  • Margin Pressure: EBITDA/PAT contraction; acrylic subsidiary breakeven (demand substitution by polyester).
  • Surplus Capacity: ~11M spindles shut (small units); imports/inventories building.

Tailwinds

  • Operational Resilience: 90-95% spinning/fabric utilization; high-value yarns (melange, sustainable) sustaining margins.
  • Raw Material Mitigation: Duty-free cotton imports (till Dec 31); rupee depreciation aiding realizations.
  • Diversification: Balanced domestic (55%) vs. export (45%); non-US focus (Bangladesh ~50% yarn exports stable).
  • Cost Management: Imported cotton, brand business outperformance; forex gains (though Q2 reversal).

Growth Prospects

  • Capex Ramp-Up: +30% capacity via synthetic fabric plant (18M mtrs, Q3 capex ₹300 Cr), MP line (Q3 ₹200 Cr), spindle expansions (15.6K+17K completed). Utilizations 20-30% in Q4 FY26; modernizations by Dec boosting efficiency.
  • Market Expansion: UK/EU FTAs; diversification to EU/UK/Australia/Canada/SL. Domestic festive/GST boost.
  • Value-Add: Performance/circular yarns for activewear; garment doubling (from 6-7K shirts/day); 200 acres in MP MITRA Park for decade-long growth.
  • Outlook: Spread normalization in Q4 FY26 if US deal resolves + cotton parity; 31M bale crop stable.

Key Risks

  • Policy/External: US tariff extension (no deal by Nov); post-Dec cotton duty/CCI pricing monopoly; crop damage (rains in North India).
  • Demand: Elasticity from higher US retail prices; further order postponements/cancellations.
  • Execution: Capex ROI tied to demand recovery; acrylic margins thin (100% util. but pressured).
  • Financial: Rising depreciation (₹113 Cr/Q2, +14% YoY); inventory buildup; forex/derivative volatility.

Overall: Short-term pressured (Q3/Q4 caution), but diversified ops + capex position VTL for recovery. Cautiously optimistic mgmt. view aligns with structural shifts (FTAs, value-add). Monitor US talks/cotton policy.
