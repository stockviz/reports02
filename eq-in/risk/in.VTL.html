Analysis of Vardhman Textiles Limited (VTL) - Q2 FY26 Overview

Vardhman Textiles Limited (VTL), India’s largest vertically integrated textile player (yarn, fabric, garments), reported resilient Q2 FY26 results amid global headwinds. Standalone revenue was stable at ₹2,468 Cr total income (down 4% YoY), with EBITDA margin at 15.5% (down 110 bps YoY) and PAT at ₹189 Cr (down 14% YoY). Capacities remain well-utilized (90-95% in spinning/fabric), supported by diversification and cost management. Consolidated figures are similar, with minor contributions from acrylic subsidiary and associates. Below is a structured summary of headwinds, tailwinds, growth prospects, and key risks, derived from the earnings call transcript, presentation, and financial results.

Headwinds (Key Pressures on Margins & Demand)

  • US Tariffs (50% duty on imports): Major disruption; US customers delaying orders (30-45 days), reducing volumes (20-25%), and shifting to low-tariff hubs (Bangladesh, Vietnam). Q3/Q4 (export-heavy) likely impacted; indirect exposure via US brands/garmenters absorbing costs but squeezing suppliers.
  • Cotton Cost Disadvantage: Domestic prices elevated (NY futures +$0.13-0.14/lb via CCI monopoly) vs. global ($0.74-0.75/lb landed in Vietnam). MSP up 8% for 2025-26 exacerbates; yarn spreads compressed (yarn at $2.75-3/kg).
  • Excess Capacity & Pricing Pressure: Yarn prices down 2-3%; ~11M spindles shut (mostly small units, unlikely to restart fully). Acrylic fibre demand weak (substitution by cheaper polyester; Vardhman Acrylics breakeven vs. peers).
  • Demand Weakness: Cautious global buying (EU/UK slowdown from Russia-Ukraine war); domestic stable but festive/GST sentiment unproven.

Tailwinds (Supporting Resilience)

  • Raw Material Relief: Temporary 11% import duty waiver (till Dec 31, 2025?) enabled bulk imports; mills stocked up, aiding Q2 margins (via imported cotton & rupee depreciation).
  • Operational Strength: 90-95% utilizations; focus on high-value yarns (melange, cellulosic, performance) protected spreads. Fabric division steady (90% util).
  • Diversification: Non-US exports/domestic stable (yarn exports 100-120M kg/month, 50% to Bangladesh). Strategic customer support without major profitability hit.
  • Currency & Other: Rupee depreciation boosted dollar realizations; forex gains (partly reversed in Q2).

Growth Prospects (Capex & Strategic Focus)

  • Capacity Expansion (+30% Overall):
    • Synthetic performance fabric plant (15-18L meters; commissioning Q3 FY26, 20-30% util Q4).
    • MP line (additional capacity; mid-Nov operational).
    • Yarn: 15,600 + 17,000 spindles done; 6,624 rotors planned. Modernization complete by Dec/Jan.
    • Garments: Doubling from 6-7K shirts/day (small base, viability focus).
  • Market/Geography Expansion: EU/UK FTAs (UK done, EU progressing); targeting Australia/Canada/Sri Lanka. New functional/circular yarns for activewear.
  • Long-Term: 200 acres in MP PM MITRA Park; green initiatives (Net Zero 2045, 40% green power by FY26). Historical ROE/RoCE ~8-11%; debt/equity low (0.13-0.20).
  • Outlook: Q4 FY26 normalization if US deal (Nov?) + cotton parity; H1 capex to drive efficiency.

Overall Summary: VTL demonstrated resilience (stable sales, 15.5% EBITDA margins) despite US tariffs and cotton woes, aided by imports and diversification. Growth hinges on Q4 catalysts (US deal, cotton relief, capex online), potentially restoring historical spreads. FY26 could see modest revenue growth (via expansions) but margins pressured until stabilization. Valuation supported by low debt, high utils, and vertical integration; monitor US/ cotton developments closely. Positive AGM voting reflects shareholder confidence. Recommendation: Hold/Buy on dips for long-term players; target normalization by FY26-end.
