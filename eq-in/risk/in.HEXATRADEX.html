Analysis of Hexa Tradex Limited (HEXATRADEX / 534328)

Hexa Tradex Limited is a holding investment company with two primary segments: Trading & Other Activities (loss-making operations) and Investment & Finance (dominant, investment-heavy). Key assets are non-current investments (~₹4.51L Cr standalone, ₹5.56L Cr consolidated as of Sep 2025), driving balance sheet growth via fair value gains. FY26 H1 results show operational losses but strong OCI. Delisting process is near completion (final approval pending). Below is a structured analysis based on the provided disclosures (warning letters, H1 FY26 financials, AGM minutes).

Headwinds (Challenges Pressuring Performance)

  • Regulatory Scrutiny & Compliance Issues: Received warning letters from BSE/NSE (Oct 14, 2025) for non-attendance of Audit Committee and Stakeholders Relationship Committee Chairpersons at AGM (Aug 20, 2024), violating SEBI LODR Reg 18(1)(d) & 20(3). Mandatory disclosures made. Pending SEBI Show Cause Notice (Mar 2024) on violations; settlement applications filed.
  • Persistent Operational Losses: Standalone H1 FY26 revenue ₹0.44 Cr vs. expenses ₹189.63 Cr (employee costs ₹98.7 Cr, finance costs ₹65 Cr). Consolidated revenue ₹310.93 Cr but PBT loss ₹55 Cr. Trading segment deeply loss-making (₹124 Cr loss standalone H1).
  • Delisting Uncertainty: Reverse delisting substantially complete (payments made), but final BSE/NSE approval pending. Reduces liquidity, visibility, and institutional interest.
  • High Cost Structure: Employee benefits (₹98-198 Cr annualized run-rate) disproportionate to revenue; finance costs elevated amid borrowings (₹1,350 Cr current, ₹634 Cr non-current consolidated).

Tailwinds (Supportive Factors)

  • Robust Investment Portfolio: Massive unrealized gains in OCI (standalone H1: ₹14,039 Cr; consolidated: ₹33,506 Cr), boosting net worth (standalone ₹3.89L Cr; consolidated ₹4.80L Cr, up ~7-8% QoQ). Investments grew 4-7% QoQ.
  • Strong Shareholder Alignment: AGM (Sep 26, 2025) resolutions passed with 93-100% approval (e.g., financials adoption, director re-appointments, RPT extension for ₹54.8 Cr preference shares redemption, secretarial auditor appointment).
  • Subsidiary Contribution: Hexa Securities & Finance adds revenue (₹310 Cr H1 dividends) and assets (₹1.27L Cr), with reviewed results integrated.
  • Cash Flow Stability: Positive investing cash flows from dividends (₹310 Cr consolidated H1); net cash up slightly.

Growth Prospects

  • Moderate, Investment-Led: Potential from ₹5.5L+ Cr investment book if equity markets remain buoyant (historical OCI gains: ₹83-114K Cr annually). Dividend income stable (₹0.38-310 Cr). Post-delisting flexibility for private maneuvers (e.g., RPTs like Colorado Trading preference shares extension to 20 years).

Summary

Hexa Tradex is a classic investment holding play with strong tailwinds from asset appreciation (net worth doubled+ in recent years) but significant headwinds from ops losses and regulation. Growth is defensive and market-tied (5-15% via investments), not aggressive. Primary risk is market downturn eroding book value; delisting adds binary outcome. Suitable for long-term holders betting on portfolio recovery, but avoid if seeking operational growth. Monitor SEBI resolution and delisting approval.
