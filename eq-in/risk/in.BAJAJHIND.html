Analysis of Bajaj Hindusthan Sugar Limited (BAJAJHIND)

Overview: Bajaj Hindusthan Sugar Ltd., one of India’s largest integrated sugar producers (136k TCD crushing, 800 KLPD distillery, 449 MW cogeneration), reported weak H1 FY26 results (ended Sep 30, 2025) with standalone revenue down 5% YoY to ₹2,395 Cr and net loss of ₹268 Cr (vs. ₹118 Cr loss YoY). Consolidated loss ₹279 Cr. Key positives include debt reduction via treasury share sale (₹68 Cr proceeds) and ethanol policy tailwinds, but headwinds from operational stress, high debt (OCDs), and auditor qualifications dominate. AGM resolutions passed but with public institution dissent (~65% against financial adoption/Chairman re-appointment).

Headwinds (Key Challenges)

  • Operational Weakness: Sugar segment (90%+ revenue) posted losses (H1: -₹127 Cr) due to constrained sugarcane supply, low recovery from delayed farmer payments, and seasonal inventory drawdown (inventories fell 76% to ₹634 Cr). Distillery/Power also loss-making amid off-season.
  • Profitability Pressure: EBITDA positive historically but H1 EBITDA negative; finance costs low (₹4 Cr standalone) but offset by unallocable expenses. Revenue flat/slightly down YoY.
  • Liquidity/Debt Strain: Negative operating cash flow (-₹18 Cr standalone) from working capital swings; cane dues persist, limiting capex/cane development.
  • Auditor Red Flags: Qualifications on ₹3,949 Cr unrecognized OCD YTM liability + ₹40 Cr interest (if booked, net worth turns negative ₹25 Cr standalone/₹188 Cr consolidated); subsidiary exposures ₹2,556 Cr (deferred interest ₹56 Cr); material uncertainty on going concern.

Tailwinds (Supportive Factors)

  • Debt Deleveraging: Fully repaid sustainable debt; sold 3.11 Cr treasury shares (Sep 2025) for ₹68 Cr (net ₹65 Cr to securities premium, used for loans); OCD 1st installment + YTM paid (treated as advance pending resolution).
  • Policy Boost: Ethanol blending target (20%+) enables sugarcane diversion; power tariff revision added ₹14 Cr revenue. Stable sugar prices (~₹4,000/quintal).
  • Improved Liquidity: Cash generation from investments/shares sale (investing inflow ₹631 Cr); lower finance costs post-debt repayment.
  • Asset Base: Strong capacities position it for ethanol/power upside; potential ₹1,927 Cr sub-judice benefits under UP Sugar Policy 2004.

Growth Prospects

  • Medium-Term Upside (1-2 Years): Ethanol programme could drive distillery revenue (H1: ₹259 Cr, but Q2 weak); better cane cycle with liquidity for farmer payments/development. Resolution plan approval could slash debt burden, unlock capex for efficiency.
  • Long-Term Potential: Integrated model benefits from EBP (ethanol blending programme) reforms; power co-gen from bagasse. FY26 sugar season (starts Oct) could rebound with stable prices/govt support.
  • Catalysts: Lender resolution (under discussion), policy payout, subsidiary recovery. Mgmt targets viability via internal accruals.

Investment Summary: High-Risk Turnaround Play. Tailwinds from ethanol/debt resolution offer multi-bagger potential if executed, but near-term headwinds (losses, qualifications) cap upside. Positive net worth (₹3,964 Cr standalone) hinges on contingencies. Monitor Q3 debt update/resolution progress; suitable for high-conviction investors. Target FY26 recovery in crushing season.
