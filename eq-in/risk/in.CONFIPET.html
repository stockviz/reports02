Summary Analysis for Confidence Petroleum India Ltd (CONFIPET)

Company Overview: Confidence Petroleum India Ltd is engaged in LPG bottling/marketing (dominant ~96% of revenue), cylinder manufacturing, and emerging CNG/Auto LPG segments. It operates through multiple subsidiaries/JVs (e.g., Hemkunt Petroleum, Confidence Go Gas). FY25 full-year revenue was ~314,576 Lacs; H1 FY26 shows strong growth. Funds raised via preferential issue (₹250 Cr in Feb 2024) are being deployed for Capex with no deviations reported as of Sept 2025.

Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts)

  • Robust Revenue & Profit Growth: H1 FY26 consolidated revenue up 38% YoY to ₹2,09,500 Lacs (driven by LPG division: ₹2,01,170 Lacs, +49% YoY). PAT up 10% to ₹4,086 Lacs (EPS ₹1.22). Q2 FY26 revenue ₹98,301 Lacs (flat QoQ but strong YoY). Segment results strong: LPG EBITDA ₹16,100 Lacs (+7% YoY).
  • Capex Execution: ₹176 Cr (70%) of preferential funds utilized for high-growth areas (Auto LPG: ₹75 Cr, CNG/Pack Cylinders/General Corporate: balance). Monitored by Care Ratings; supports capacity expansion.
  • Strategic Moves: New wholly-owned subsidiary (Confidence Green Energy Pvt Ltd, Jul 2025) signals pivot to renewables. Analyst meet (Sep 22, 2025) indicates proactive investor outreach.
  • Financial Health: Positive operating cash flow (₹8,610 Lacs H1 FY26). Equity base stable (₹3,322 Lacs); other equity up to ₹1,34,353 Lacs. Dividend declared (₹0.10/share, 10% payout).
  • AGM Outcomes: All resolutions passed overwhelmingly (98-99% favor), including independent director appointments and RPT approvals.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Regulatory Scrutiny:
    • Income-tax search (Oct 2025) at premises/residences; no outcome yet, but disclosed under Reg 30.
    • DGGI Show Cause Notice (Sep 2025): ₹233 Cr outward supplies allegedly taxed at 5% vs 18% (Mar 2020-Mar 2024); company contesting (prior similar notices quashed).
  • Audit Qualifications: Limited review report qualified on:
    • GST ITC mismatch (books vs GST portal); impact unquantified.
    • Non-recognition of PF liability per Supreme Court ruling (2019) on “wages” including allowances.
  • Margin Pressure: Gross margins stable but finance costs up (₹4,515 Lacs H1, +16% YoY) due to borrowings (total debt ₹59,983 Lacs). Employee/other expenses rising.
  • Investor Dissent: Public institutions voted against/ low support for some RPTs (e.g., Gaspoint Petroleum: 99% against from institutions, though overall passed).

Growth Prospects

  • Core LPG/Cylinder Expansion: LPG division (96% revenue) poised for 20-30%+ YoY growth amid rising demand (auto-fuel switch, industrial use). Cylinder division scaling (assets up to ₹39,657 Lacs).
  • Diversification: CNG/Auto LPG Capex (remaining ~₹74 Cr deployable) taps green mobility boom. Go Gas branding and 10+ step-down JVs (e.g., Bangalore Go Gas) enhance distribution.
  • Green Energy: New subsidiary positions for hydrogen/biofuels/LNG, aligning with India’s net-zero goals.
  • Outlook: H1 momentum suggests FY26 revenue >₹600,000 Lacs (20%+ growth). Remaining Capex + subsidiaries (42+ entities) could drive 15-20% PAT CAGR. Analyst meet may unlock visibility.

Key Risks

Overall Outlook: Positive with Caution. Strong H1 growth and Capex execution are tailwinds, but regulatory overhangs pose near-term risks. Monitor tax outcomes/DGGI resolution. Target Price upside on green energy execution; suitable for growth investors tolerant of volatility. (Analysis based solely on provided docs; dated Nov 2025 financials.)
