Shivalik Rasayan Limited (SHIVALIK) Analysis

Based on Q2/H1 FY26 Unaudited Results (ended Sep 30, 2025) and 47th AGM Proceedings

Shivalik Rasayan Ltd. (Scrip: 539148 BSE / SHIVALIK NSE), a diversified player in Agrochemicals, Pharma API, and Pharma Formulations, reported strong revenue growth but softer profitability amid capex intensity and working capital pressures. Standalone revenue surged 34% YoY to ₹9,294L (HY), driven by volume/mix improvements, while consolidated revenue grew 17% YoY to ₹18,315L. However, net profit declined sharply standalone (₹341L, -48% YoY) and mildly consolidated (₹713L, -14% YoY), impacted by API segment losses and higher costs. Balance sheet remains robust (equity ~₹41K L standalone), with ongoing expansions. AGM approved 10% dividend, director re-appointments, and routine items; voting results pending.

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Robust Revenue Momentum: Standalone +34% YoY (HY), Consolidated +17% YoY; Q2 sequential growth intact (₹4,667L standalone).
  • Diversified Segments & Associate Contribution: Agrochem/Pharma mix; Medicamen Biotech (associate) added ₹211L profit share (Q2). Unallocated revenue supports top-line.
  • Capex for Capacity Expansion: PPE up 1% QoQ standalone (₹25,141L); CWIP/investments signal growth investments (₹775L HY capex).
  • Strong Balance Sheet: Equity base solid (₹40K L standalone); total assets +3% QoQ to ₹52,614L. Low leverage ( borrowings ~10% of assets).
  • Shareholder Returns: 10% dividend declared (FY25); EPS steady at ₹2.17 (standalone HY basic).
  • Clean Audits: Limited review reports unqualified; Ind AS-34 compliant.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Profit Pressure: Standalone PBT -20% YoY (₹491L); API segment loss widened to ₹591L (HY). Consolidated profit attributable to owners dipped to ₹491L.
  • Margin Squeeze: Finance costs up (₹269L standalone HY); unallocable expenses drag. EBITDA margin implied ~8-10% (sub-total segment results ₹750L).
  • Working Capital Drag: Negative Op. Cash Flow (-₹443L standalone, -₹1,684L cons.); inventories +11% YoY, receivables +27% YoY, trade payables volatile.
  • Segment Imbalance: API consistently loss-making (₹-295L Q2); Agrochem/Pharma Formulation details sparse but supportive.
  • Cash Burn: Net cash down sharply (standalone ₹316L vs ₹2,421L FY25-end); reliant on financing inflows (₹-237L net).

Growth Prospects

  • High (Medium-Term): Revenue trajectory positions for 20-30% FY26 growth if margins stabilize. Capex (₹2K L+ FY25) targets capacity ramp-up in high-growth Agrochem/Pharma API (India mkt ~15-20% CAGR). Associate integration boosts consolidated scale (assets ₹80K L).
  • Catalysts: Pharma Formulations upside; export potential in Agrochem. Dividend policy + equity raises (warrants/shares) fund expansions. FY25 full-year audited profit ₹1,387L standalone sets base for recovery.
  • Outlook: EPS trajectory (₹10.60 FY25) could rebound to ₹12-15 FY26 with cost controls; target revenue ₹30K L+ FY26.
