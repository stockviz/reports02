Reliance Industries Limited (RELIANCE) Analysis

Based on Q2 FY26 Results (Sep 30, 2025), Announcements, and Related Filings

Reliance Industries (RIL) reported robust Q2 FY26 performance with consolidated gross revenue up 9.9% YoY to ₹283,548 Cr ($31.9 Bn), EBITDA up 14.6% to ₹50,367 Cr ($5.7 Bn; margin 17.8%), and PAT (post-associates/JVs) up 14.3% to ₹22,092 Cr ($2.5 Bn). Capex remained high at ₹40,010 Cr ($4.5 Bn), focused on growth areas. Balance sheet healthy: Net debt/EBITDA at 0.58x. Key segments (Jio, Retail, O2C) drove growth amid macro volatility.

Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts)

  • Digital Services (Jio Platforms): Milestone 500 Mn+ subscribers (506 Mn total); 234 Mn 5G users (~50% wireless traffic); fixed broadband at 23 Mn (9.5 Mn JioAirFiber). ARPU up 8.4% YoY to ₹211.4; EBITDA up 17.7% YoY to ₹18,757 Cr (margin 51.6%, +140 bps). Data traffic +29.8% YoY.
  • Retail (RRVL): Revenue +18% YoY to ₹90,018 Cr; EBITDA +16.5% to ₹6,816 Cr. Grocery/Fashion +23%/22% YoY; quick commerce orders +200% YoY. 412 new stores (total 19,821); 369 Mn registered customers (+13% YoY).
  • O2C: EBITDA +20.9% YoY to ₹15,008 Cr (margin +130 bps to 9.3%) on fuel cracks recovery (gasoil +37%, gasoline +24%). Jio-bp volumes +34% (HSD), network at 2,057 outlets.
  • AI/Media/New Energy: Meta JV (₹855 Cr initial; RIL 70%); JioStar 400 Mn MAUs, record EBITDA (28.1% margin); giga-factories progressing (4 solar PV lines commissioned).
  • Macro: GST cuts boosting consumption; strong domestic demand; resilient balance sheet supports capex/growth.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Regulatory Penalty: ₹57.07 Cr GST penalty (vessel hiring classification); appeal planned (no ops impact).
  • O2C Volatility: Weak polyester deltas (-9%); global overcapacity; Brent down 14% YoY to $69/bbl.
  • E&P Decline: EBITDA -5.4% YoY; KGD6 gas -8.4% (natural decline).
  • Rising Costs: Finance costs +13.5% YoY (5G spectrum); depreciation +11.9%.
  • Macro Pressures: Monsoon deferrals; energy oversupply; FMCG ad cuts impacting media.

Growth Prospects

Segment Key Drivers Outlook
Jio 5G scale (234 Mn users); AirFiber (9.5 Mn subs, +1 Mn/month); AI/digital (JioPC, AICloud, JioFrames); ARPU/monetization. 2x EBITDA FY24-28; global tech export potential.
Retail Quick commerce (200% YoY orders); store expansion; premiumization (Stella McCartney, Fenty); JioMart in 5K pincodes. Largest digital commerce platform; omni-channel scale.
O2C Jio-bp (2,057 outlets, +34% vols); domestic focus; ethane economics. Margin stabilization; energy transition (CBG/CNG).
New Energy Solar PV (20 GWp target), battery (100 GWh); Kutch site progress. Integrated green ecosystem; gigafactories commissioning FY26.
AI/Media Meta JV (Llama models); JioStar (400 Mn MAUs, 34.5% TV share). “AI Everywhere”; content/sports leadership.
Overall High capex (₹70K Cr H1); net debt low; India consumption boom. 15-20% EBITDA CAGR; new engines to drive 30%+ group growth.

Key Risks

  1. Regulatory/Compliance: GST penalty (₹57 Cr; appeal risk); SEBI scrutiny on related-party deals (RPT approvals passed overwhelmingly at AGM).
  2. Commodity Volatility: Oil/chemical prices (Brent $69/bbl); global oversupply/polyester weakness.
  3. Execution/Capex: High spend (₹70K Cr H1) in New Energy capex delays; tech ramps (5G/AI).
  4. Competition: Telecom ARPU pressure; retail/e-comm rivals (e.g., quick commerce).
  5. Macro/Geopolitical: Energy demand slowdown; monsoon/rain impacts; US elections/China slowdown.
  6. Debt/Leverage: Finance costs rising; net debt stable but monitor amid capex.
  7. AGM Insights: High promoter voting (100% in favor); some institutional dissent (e.g., 18% against director re-appointments? – data shows mixed but passed).

Investment Summary: Strong Buy/Bullish. Tailwinds from Jio/Retail scale and new growth engines outweigh headwinds. Q2 beat expectations; guidance intact for FY26 growth. Risks manageable with fortress balance sheet. Target upside 20-30% in 12M (est. FY26 EBITDA ₹2L Cr+). Monitor O2C macros and New Energy milestones.
