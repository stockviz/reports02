Summary Analysis for Salasar Techno Engineering Limited (SALASAR)

Salasar Techno Engineering Ltd. (SALASAR), a listed entity on NSE/BSE (Symbol: SALASAR, Scrip: 540642), specializes in galvanized/non-galvanized steel structures and EPC projects, primarily in power transmission, railways, and telecom infrastructure. The analysis is based on the provided documents: Q2/H1 FY26 unaudited financials (standalone/consolidated ended Sep 30, 2025), a major contract award (Nov 26, 2025), and 24th AGM outcomes (Sep 27, 2025). FY26 shows robust H1 growth amid power sector tailwinds, bolstered by a landmark order, but with execution and liquidity challenges.

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Strong Financial Momentum: Consolidated H1 FY26 revenue up 26% YoY to ₹727 Cr (from ₹576 Cr), driven by EPC (30% YoY growth to ₹307 Cr) and Steel Structures (21% to ₹435 Cr). Net profit rose 23% to ₹24.8 Cr (EPS ₹0.14). Q2 alone delivered ₹428 Cr revenue (+52% YoY), with margins stable at ~5-6% PBT.
  • Major Order Inflow: ₹695 Cr contracts from RVNL (Nov 2025) for procurement/erection of E&M items under RDSS scheme (HPSEBL circles). ~75% of H1 revenue size; 20-month execution to fuel FY26-27 topline. No related-party issues.
  • Segment Strength: EPC (42% of revenue) and Steel Structures (58%) both expanding; EPC assets/liabilities up sharply, signaling project ramp-up.
  • Board Support & Governance: AGM resolutions (financials adoption, auditor reappointment, director approvals) passed with 99%+ approval. Funding authorization for MD to secure working capital/capex. Trading window closure post-results indicates no immediate insider concerns.
  • Macro Tailwinds: Govt push via RDSS (loss reduction in power distribution), rail infra (RVNL), aligns with India’s capex cycle.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Working Capital Intensity: Inventories surged 39% to ₹472 Cr (standalone), trade receivables up 13% to ₹488 Cr. Net working capital changes dragged operating cash flow despite ₹35 Cr PBT (H1 standalone CFO: ₹21 Cr).
  • High Debt & Finance Costs: Borrowings up (current: ₹326 Cr, +10% QoQ); finance costs at ₹27 Cr H1 consolidated (+13% YoY). Interest coverage ~1.3x strains margins.
  • Compliance Lapse: Fine for Reg 31 non-compliance (Q1 FY26 shareholding pattern); Board noted to avoid recurrence.
  • Margin Pressure: Cost of revenue ~86% of sales; other expenses up 12% YoY. Consolidated EBITDA margin ~7-8%, vulnerable to input costs (steel, labor).
  • Subsidiary/JV Dependence: Unreviewed results from 6 entities (₹109 Cr assets, minor losses); adds opacity.

Growth Prospects

  • Order Book & Revenue Visibility: ₹695 Cr RVNL order + prior EPC/steel pipeline could drive FY26 revenue >₹1,500 Cr (vs. FY25 ₹1,447 Cr), with EPC scaling to 40-50% mix. 20-month tenure ensures multi-quarter execution.
  • Sector Boom: Exposure to power T&D (RDSS), rail electrification positions for ₹2-3 lakh Cr govt infra spend (FY26 budget). Potential JV expansions (e.g., Salasar RVNL JV).
  • Balance Sheet Expansion: Assets up 8% to ₹1,848 Cr consolidated; equity strong (₹632 Cr). Funding nod for capex/working capital could support 20-25% CAGR if debt managed.
  • EPS Trajectory: H1 EPS ₹0.14 (FY25 full-year ₹0.11); full execution could yield ₹0.40-0.50 FY26, with ROE ~8-10%.
  • Upside Catalysts: More PSU orders, debt refinancing, inventory turnover improvement.

Key Risks

Risk Category Description Mitigation/Impact
Execution Risk (High) Delays in RVNL/HPSEBL projects due to site issues, approvals, or supply chain (E&M procurement). EPC liabilities up 25% signals advances. Diversified JVs; track record in infra. Potential 10-20% revenue deferral.
Liquidity/Debt Risk (Medium-High) Negative investing CF (₹20-22 Cr outflow); CFO positive but thin. Debt:Equity ~0.6x but rising. Funding authorization; govt payments reliable. Interest rate hikes could add ₹5-10 Cr costs.

Overall Outlook: Positive with Cautious Optimism. Tailwinds from orders/infra boom outweigh headwinds; expect 20-30% FY26 growth if execution smooths. Monitor Q3 results for order progress and WC cycle. Target P/B ~1.5-2x (current implied post-results); suitable for infra-themed portfolios.
