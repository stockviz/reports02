Hester Biosciences Limited (HESTERBIO) Analysis: Q2/H1 FY26

Hester Biosciences, a leading Indian animal health company focused on poultry and animal healthcare vaccines/products, reported mixed H1 FY26 results. Standalone revenues declined 13% YoY to INR 1,281 Mn (Poultry up 10%, Animal down 41% due to govt program delays), with PAT down 15% to INR 150 Mn amid stable margins (~21% EBITDA). Consolidated revenues fell 7% to INR 1,551 Mn, but PAT surged 99% to INR 316 Mn, driven by Africa turnaround (profit up sharply) and forex gains (TZS appreciation vs USD); Nepal was tepid. Management emphasized cost discipline, H9N2 vaccine milestone, and H2 recovery. CWIP (INR 182 Cr) to capitalize by Mar’26, boosting capacity.

Headwinds (Challenges Pressuring Near-Term Performance)

  • Govt Tender Delays: Core issue in Animal Healthcare (50% Q2/41% H1 decline); PPR/LSD programs slowed across states—timing mismatch, not demand erosion. Expect H2 pickup (esp Q4), but lumpiness persists.
  • International Unevenness: Nepal Q2 loss (INR 11 Mn) from order timing/logistics disruptions; Africa topline down 35% Q2 due to political delays/project approvals.
  • Topline Pressure: Standalone divisional sales down 13% H1; consolidated down 7%, reflecting tender dependency (~high exposure).
  • Margin Squeeze: Standalone EBITDA/PAT down 13-15% despite cost controls, due to volume weakness.

Tailwinds (Supportive Factors Driving Resilience)

  • Poultry Strength: 18% Q2/10% H1 growth (volume-led); H9N2 market authorization unlocks holistic solutions (vaccines + biosecurity).
  • Operational Discipline: Gross margins stable/improved (standalone 69%, consolidated 73%); forex gains (INR 89 Mn unrealized) boosted consolidated PAT 99%.
  • Africa Turnaround: Q2/H1 profits INR 48/103 Mn (vs losses prior); stable Tanzania ops + GALVmed collaboration (Tanzania/Kenya/Nigeria).
  • Balance Sheet: Debt repayment on track; cash from ops INR 157/175 Mn (standalone/consolidated); CWIP nearing completion.

Growth Prospects (Medium-Term Catalysts: FY26-28)

  • Capacity Ramp: INR 182 Cr India plant fully online by Mar’26 → significant vaccine scale-up (2-3x potential); BSL-3 repurposing (human-to-vet) adds ~1 Bn doses (over existing 6.25 Bn), license by Q1 FY27.
  • Product Pipeline: H9N2 sales post-Dec’25 license (exports key); LSD vaccine mid-FY27 (vs current Goat Pox); Pet vaccines (rabies/9-in-1) via existing facilities (India/Africa, nascent); recombinant vaccines on track (mid-long term).
  • Diversification: Reduce tender reliance → commercial/private/export focus; Pet Care urban push; Africa breakeven by FY28.
  • Market Leadership: #1 global PPR (75% share), #1 India Goat Pox (70%, LSD proxy), #2 poultry vaccines India (35%). Sector tailwinds: 5-6% dairy growth post-LSD.

Key Risks

  • Tender/Regulatory Dependency: High govt exposure (Animal Health); delays/outbreaks could swing volumes (e.g., no major poultry outbreaks yet).
  • Geopolitical/FX Volatility: Africa/Nepal instability (politics/logistics); forex swings (positive now, but USD/TZS reversal possible).
  • Execution Delays: CWIP capitalization, licenses (H9N2 mfg, BSL-3, LSD); R&D timelines for recombinants/pets.
  • Competition: Bharat Biotech/govt tech transfers; strain-specific allocation across plants limits flexibility.
  • Capex/Debt: Routine capex ongoing (~no major new); debt ~INR 1,135 Mn non-current consolidated, but repayment disciplined.

Overall Outlook: Neutral to Positive. H1 weakness transitional (tender timing), with consolidated profitability resilient (20% PAT margin). H2 inflection likely from capacity unlock, H9N2 exports, and program resumption—targeting balanced portfolio (less tender-dependent). FY26 growth hinges on execution; undervalued if catalysts hit (e.g., 15-20% rev CAGR feasible FY26-28). Monitor Q3 tenders/Africa orders. Recommendation: Accumulate on dips for 2-3 yr horizon.
