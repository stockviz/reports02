Analysis of Tinna Rubber and Infrastructure Limited (TINNARUBR)

Tinna Rubber and Infrastructure Limited (TRIL) is a leading ELT (End-of-Life Tyres) recycler in India, with a diversified portfolio spanning crumb rubber, CRMB, reclaim rubber, and industrial products. FY25 revenue was ~₹505 Cr (consolidated), with H1FY26 at ₹250 Cr (down 2% YoY). Capacity utilization remains strong (India 77%, Oman 85% in H1FY26). Below is a structured summary of headwinds, tailwinds, growth prospects, and key risks, derived from Q2-H1FY26 earnings, investor presentation, board outcomes, and AGM results.

Tailwinds (Supportive Factors)

  • Strong Operational Metrics: H1FY26 EBITDA margin at 17% (standalone; consolidated similar), ROCE 26% (FY25), low net debt/equity (0.73x). High interest coverage (6x FY25).
  • Capacity & Volume Growth: Tyre crushing capacity expanding from 185k MT (FY25) to 235k MT (FY26E/FY27E). H1FY26 volumes: India 65.8k MT (-2% YoY), Oman 6.4k MT (+ve).
  • Product Diversification: Infrastructure (46% revenue), Industrial (27%), Steel (20%), Consumer (6%). Exports up 7% YoY; value-added focus (CRM +75% YoY).
  • Sustainability Edge: 99% material recovery from ELTs; REACH/PAH/RoHS compliant. Solar power savings ₹1 Cr (H1FY26), targeting 50% renewable usage (capex for 3x expansion).
  • Fundraising Success: QIP ₹78.7 Cr deployed (debt reduction ₹23 Cr fully used, solar/capex ongoing). CARE BBB rating upgraded.
  • Policy Support: EPR credits ₹19.6 Cr (H1FY26 revenue); govt push for modified bitumen in highways.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Revenue Pressure: H1FY26 consolidated revenue -2% YoY (₹250 Cr vs. ₹254 Cr); Q2 down 8% QoQ. Infrastructure segment -23% YoY due to low-margin cuts.
  • Margin/Volume Dips: PAT margin 9.4% (down from 11.2% YoY); tyre volumes -2%/-3%. Extended monsoon hit Varle plant (66% utilization).
  • Input Cost Volatility: Oman EBITDA hit by high ELT costs (₹27L Q2 loss); mitigated via imports/consent.
  • Segment-Specific Issues: Steel revenue +6% but volumes +21% offset by falling prices (cheap imports). Consumer stable but price corrections.
  • Startup Costs: Mbodla (SA) loss ₹73L; Tinna Rubber Arabia delay to mid-FY27.

Growth Prospects

  • Domestic Infra Boom: CRMB demand to grow (GOI mandating modified bitumen; NIP/AMRUT capex). Road construction recycled rubber market: 0.23 MnMT (2019) → 0.41 MnMT (2030F).
  • International Expansion: Oman revenue ₹15 Cr H1FY26 (40% GCC exports); SA Phase 1 operational (breakeven Mar’26); Saudi 24k MTpa by FY27. Targets 10 locations by 2028.
  • Capex & New Ventures: ₹100 Cr over 2 yrs (₹56 Cr H1FY26 spent); rCB commissioning Q3FY26; polymer compounding (3% H1 revenue, 750T sales).
  • Vision 2028: Revenue ₹1,000 Cr (25%+ CAGR from FY25 ₹505 Cr); EBITDA 18%+; ROCE 30%+; 30% export volume growth by Q4FY26.
  • Associate Upside: TP Buildtech PAT ₹0.98 Cr H1FY25 (stable despite new products/Kolkata ramp-up); construction chemicals market CAGR 7.2% to $5 Bn (FY30E).
  • R&D/ESG: 3% PAT to R&D; GHG study; Innovation Award 2025.

Key Risks

Risk Category Description Mitigation
Execution/Operational Delays in capex (rCB, intl. plants); capacity ramp-up (PCMB to 33% FY26). Phased rollout; QIP funds.
Commodity/Price ELT/rubber/steel volatility; global headwinds. ELT diversification (10-15% savings); exports/alternatives.
Regulatory EPR changes; envt. norms. Compliance (ISO certified); EPR reliance (₹19.6 Cr H1).
Geopolitical/Currency Intl. ops (Oman/SA/Saudi); forex exposure. Hedging; local sourcing.
Competition/Related Party Recycling peers; promoter-related resolutions (5-9) saw 2-3% against votes (institutional). Diversification; governance (AGM approvals passed).
Financial Debt ₹129 Cr (H1); working capital cycle. Debt reduction via QIP; strong ratios.

Overall Outlook: Moderately positive. H1FY26 dip strategic (margin focus), with tailwinds from expansions/infra policy outweighing headwinds. FY26E revenue growth likely 20-25% on capacity/export push, but monitor execution and commodity prices. Stock up ~250% in 1-yr (outperformed BSE Sensex). Recommended for long-term ESG/infra play.
