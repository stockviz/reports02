Bharti Hexacom Limited (BHARTIHEXA) Analysis

Bharti Hexacom (Airtel subsidiary operating in Rajasthan & North-East circles) reported robust Q2/H1 FY26 results (ended Sep 30, 2025), with revenue up 10.5% YoY to ₹2,317 Cr, EBITDA margins expanding to 54.2% (+433 bps YoY), and ARPU rising 10.1% YoY to ₹251. Customer base grew 4.1% YoY to 28.6 Mn, driven by data (27% YoY usage growth) and Homes/Offices (+47% revenue). Leverage improved sharply (Net Debt/EBITDAaL ex-lease: 0.64x vs. 1.35x YoY). Below is a structured summary of headwinds, tailwinds, growth prospects, and key risks.

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Premiumization & ARPU Momentum: Mobile ARPU +10.1% YoY; smartphone data customers at 78% of base (+8.1% YoY), data usage +27% to 30.7 GB/user/month. Supports sustained revenue growth.
  • Margin Expansion: EBITDA +20.1% YoY (54.2% margin); EBITDAaL +21.9% (47.4% margin). OpEx discipline (e.g., access charges -25% YoY) and cost optimization.
  • Homes/Offices Surge: Revenue +46.9% YoY; +60K customers (total 0.6 Mn across 117 cities). Asset-light FTTH/FWA partnerships driving scalability.
  • Strong Balance Sheet: Net Debt down 26% YoY; leverage at comfortable 0.64x (ex-lease). Cash from ops ₹24.7 Bn (H1); dividend payout ₹5 Bn.
  • Operational Efficiency: Churn down to 2.6%; towers +357 YoY; 92K+ broadband base stations.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Customer Base Pressure: -0.2% QoQ decline; net mobile loss of 110K in Q2 (though +3.4% YoY).
  • Cost Pressures: Network ops +11% YoY; other expenses +59% (H1); employee costs up amid expansion.
  • Segment Imbalance: Homes/Offices EBIT negative (high capex ₹1.3 Bn Q2); reliant on mobile (97% revenue).
  • Sequential Slowdown: Revenue +2.4% QoQ; capex moderated but spectrum payments ongoing.

Growth Prospects

  • Data & 5G Upside: Explosive data traffic (1,935 PBs Q2); smartphone penetration room to grow. 5G base stations scaling; potential for higher ARPU via bundles.
  • Homes/Offices Expansion: Target deeper penetration in underserved circles via partnerships; ARPU stable at ₹484. Aims for multi-play (fiber + FWA).
  • Digital Ecosystem: Perplexity AI partnership (free Pro for all customers); anti-spam AI. Enhances retention/stickiness.
  • Industry Tailwinds: India telecom growth (rising data demand, tariff hikes possible); Airtel synergies for network/tech.
  • Projections: Consensus implies 12-15% revenue CAGR FY26-28; EBITDA margins sustainable at 52-55% on leverage reduction.

Key Risks

  • Competition: Jio dominance in data/low-cost plans; potential tariff wars or market share erosion in circles.
  • Regulatory: Spectrum/license fees ~11% of revenue (up 16% H1 YoY); AGR dues/deferred payments (₹28.7 Bn); DoT policy changes.
  • Execution: High capex (₹5.9 Bn H1) for network/Homes; capex productivity key amid rising cumulative investments (₹233 Bn).
  • Macro/External: Rural slowdown in North-East/Rajasthan; forex volatility (debt INR-denominated but global Airtel links); inflation on opEx.
  • Financial: Dividend policy strains cash (₹5 Bn Q2); negative working capital (-0.59x long-term debt ratio).

Overall Outlook: Strong tailwinds from premiumization and diversification outweigh headwinds; low leverage enables growth capex. Bullish medium-term (12-18 months) with 15-20% EPS growth potential, but monitor competition/churn. Valuation attractive at ~17x EV/EBITDA (annualized). Rating: Buy/Hold for long-term investors.
