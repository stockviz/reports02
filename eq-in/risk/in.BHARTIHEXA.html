BHARTIHEXA (Bharti Hexacom Limited) Analysis: Q2/H1 FY26 (Sep 30, 2025)

Bharti Hexacom (Airtel brand in Rajasthan & North-East circles) delivered robust Q2 FY26 results, with revenue up 10.5% YoY to ₹2,317 Cr, EBITDA margins expanding 433 bps to 54.2%, and net profit surging 66.4% YoY to ₹421 Cr (pre-exceptional). Key drivers include ARPU growth (₹251, +10.1% YoY), data usage (+27% YoY to 30.7 GB/user/month), and hyper-growth in Homes/Offices (+47% YoY revenue). Leverage improved sharply (Net Debt/EBITDAaL at 0.64x vs. 1.35x YoY). Overall, the company benefits from premiumization and operational leverage, but faces telecom sector headwinds like competition.

Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts)

  • Premiumization & Data Momentum: ARPU up 10% YoY; smartphone data users at 78% of base (+8.1% YoY). Data traffic +27% YoY supports sustained revenue growth.
  • Margin Expansion: EBITDA margin at 54.2% (+433 bps YoY) and EBITDAaL at 47.4% (+444 bps), driven by opex efficiency (opex/revenue ~31%) and lower access charges (-25% YoY).
  • Homes/Offices Hyper-Growth: Revenue +47% YoY, customers +60K QoQ (total 0.6 Mn across 117 cities). Asset-light FTTH/FWA model via LCO partnerships scales efficiently.
  • Healthy Balance Sheet: Net debt down 26% YoY to ₹630 Cr; leverage at 0.64x (ex-leases). Strong FCF (EBITDA-Capex: ₹889 Cr).
  • Network & Strategic Initiatives: +357 towers YoY; AI partnership (Perplexity Pro free for users) enhances stickiness.
  • Customer Scale: Base at 28.6 Mn (+4.1% YoY), with low churn (2.6%).

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Customer Metrics Softening QoQ: Base -0.2% QoQ; churn +20 bps to 2.6%; net mobile adds negative (-110K).
  • Opex Pressures: Employee/sales costs stable but other expenses +22% YoY; charity/donation impacted H1 EBITDA.
  • Segment Imbalance: Mobile (96% revenue) growth slowed to 9.6% YoY; Homes EBIT negative due to high capex (₹125 Cr QoQ).
  • External Factors: High spectrum/license fees (9-11% of revenue); dividend payout (₹50 Cr) drains cash amid capex needs.
  • Macro/Regulatory: Potential tariff headwinds in competitive Indian telecom; forex volatility (debt has USD exposure).

Growth Prospects

  • High (Medium-Term: 12-15% Revenue CAGR): Data-led premiumization (ARPU to ₹300+), 5G rollout, and Homes expansion (target 1-2 Mn customers) could drive 12-15% YoY growth. FY26 revenue guidance implied at ₹9,500-10,000 Cr.
  • Digital/Value-Add Upside: AI/spam-blocking partnerships boost retention; FWA/FTTH penetration in underserved North-East/Rajasthan.
  • EBITDA Margin Potential: 55-57% by FY27 via efficiencies; ROCE at 22% supports reinvestment.
  • M&A/Synergies: Airtel group backing for spectrum sharing/tech upgrades.
  • Triggers: Analyst meets (Dec 5, Macquarie) may signal IPO-like visibility post strong FY25 listing.

Key Risks (High-Impact)

Risk Category Description Mitigation/Probability
Competition Jio’s aggressive pricing/data wars could cap ARPU/growth (High probability; sector-wide). Premium focus, Airtel brand strength (Medium mitigation).
Regulatory Spectrum auctions (2026+), AGR dues, or tariff curbs (High impact). Strong balance sheet; govt. support for consolidation (Medium).
Execution Homes capex drag; customer de-growth if network lags (Medium). Proven track record; +3.6K sites YoY (High mitigation).
Macro/Economic Rural slowdown, inflation hits disposable income (Medium). Defensive data demand (Low-Medium).
Debt/Liquidity Rising rates on ₹560 Cr borrowings; forex risk (Low post-debt reduction). FCF covers interest 10x (Low).
Others Churn spike from spam/AI glitches; dependency on Airtel ecosystem (Low). Diversifying partnerships (Medium).

Investment Summary: Strong tailwinds from data/Homes growth outweigh headwinds; prospects bright for 15-20% EPS CAGR FY26-28. Risks tilted competitive/regulatory—buy on dips for long-term holders. Target multiples: 20-25x FY27E EBITDA (upside to ₹1,000/share). Monitor Q3 customer adds.
