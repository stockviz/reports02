Analysis of Kridhan Infra Limited (KRIDHANINF)

Kridhan Infra Limited, a Mumbai-based infrastructure company (CIN: L27100MH2006PLC160602), operates primarily in steel structures, pipes, and civil engineering. Listed on BSE (533482) and NSE (KRIDHANINF), it faces acute financial distress but is pursuing revival through equity infusion, acquisitions, and related-party synergies. Analysis based on provided documents (AGM Notice Sep 2025, Q2/H1 FY26 results Nov 2025, NSE clarification Sep 2025). All figures in INR Lakhs unless stated (~1 Lakh = 0.01 Cr).

Headwinds (Key Challenges)

  • Severe Financial Distress: Net worth fully eroded (Standalone: -27,996; Consolidated: -28,078 as on Sep 30, 2025). Accumulated losses persist; Q2/H1 FY26 shows profits only from exceptional items (provision reversals of ~92 Lakhs). Auditors flag material uncertainty on going concern due to defaults on working capital loans and erosion.
  • Ballooning Liabilities: Current liabilities dominate (~31,416 Standalone), driven by borrowings (2,272) and other financial liabilities (29,039 – likely interest accruals/deferred payments). Cash burn evident: Net cash decrease of 196 (Standalone H1).
  • Operational Stagnation: Minimal revenue traction; single-segment infra with negligible Singapore subsidiary contribution (under liquidation, fully impaired). Associate Vijay Nirman excluded from results (impaired).
  • Compliance & Governance Issues: XBRL filing delays (Q1 FY26) due to technical glitches, leading to NSE queries. Auditor transition post sudden demise of prior statutory auditor.
  • Liquidity Crunch: Cash & equivalents dropped to 26 (Standalone) from 222 (Mar 2025); heavy reliance on loans/advances recovery.

Tailwinds (Supportive Factors)

  • Equity Infusion Momentum: In-principle BSE/NSE approval (Nov 24, 2025) for 10.27 Cr warrants (Rs 4.05/share, premium Rs 2.05) to promoters/non-promoters – potential ~41.6 Cr raise to bolster equity base and fund operations.
  • Profitability from Clean-Ups: H1 FY26 pre-tax profit of 150 (Standalone)/149 (Consolidated) driven by exceptional reversals (prior loan provisions). Positive operating cash flow (185 Standalone).
  • Cost Controls: Management cites “cost reduction measures” and “future business prospects” to sustain going concern basis.
  • Related-Party Ecosystem: Approved transactions up to Rs 50 Cr with Vijay Nirman (41.47% stake currently), Rs 10 Cr with subsidiary Kridhan Infra Solutions – could provide operational support (loans, materials, services).

Growth Prospects

  • Strategic Acquisition: Special resolution for up to 100% stake in Vijay Nirman Company Pvt Ltd (CIN: U45200AP2005PTC047097, Vizag-based infra specialist in roads/bridges/metros/piling). Aims at synergies in mega civil projects, market expansion, and diversification from steel into turnkey infra (public/private clients pan-India). Board approved Sep 2, 2025; post-acquisition holding strengthens control.
  • Infra Sector Tailwinds: India’s capex push (roads, railways, ports) aligns with Vijay Nirman’s expertise; potential for order wins post-integration.
  • Funded Revival: Warrant proceeds + AGM approvals (secretarial auditor, RPTs) enable scaling. Historical impairment reversals suggest asset clean-up unlocking value.
  • Outlook: Modest near-term (H1 revenue not detailed, but equity/debt relief could stabilize); medium-term growth via infra pivot (target: consolidate position, tech/expertise gains). FY26 could see turnaround if acquisition closes and funds deploy.
