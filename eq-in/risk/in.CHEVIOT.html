Cheviot Company Limited (CHEVIOT) Analysis

Business Overview: Cheviot is a legacy jute goods manufacturer (since 1897) with a single primary segment in jute products. It maintains a significant investment portfolio for treasury income. H1 FY26 (ended Sep 2025) showed robust revenue growth amid volatile investment gains/losses. Recent minor acquisition of NSEIL shares (0.0065% stake, ~₹18 Cr) signals long-term investment strategy.

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Strong Operational Performance: H1 FY26 revenue surged 36% YoY to ₹267 Cr (Q2: +44% YoY), driven by higher jute sales volumes/pricing. Gross margins resilient despite cost inflation.
  • Cash Generation & Balance Sheet Strength: Operating cash flow doubled YoY to ₹505 Cr; net debt negligible (borrowings ~₹9 Cr). Equity grew 7% to ₹698 Cr; investments expanded 25% to ₹383 Cr, yielding historical treasury income.
  • Investment Portfolio: Diversified holdings (FVTPL/FVTOCI) provide upside from market rallies; NSEIL buy reflects confidence in India’s capital markets growth.
  • Low Leverage & Capex: PPE up 4% to ₹232 Cr with ongoing expansions; minimal finance costs (₹0.2 Cr H1).

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Investment Volatility: Q2 other income turned negative (₹-2.4 Cr) due to FVTPL fair-value losses, dragging total income; H1 OCI swung wildly (₹81 Cr gain vs. prior loss).
  • Working Capital Strain: Inventories down 26% (₹91 Cr), but receivables up 30% (₹36 Cr); cash dipped to ₹3.7 Cr amid investment outflows.
  • Jute Sector Cyclicality: Rising raw material costs (jute up implied in COGS +36% YoY); export dependence vulnerable to global slowdowns/synthetics competition.
  • Margin Pressure: Employee/other expenses grew faster than revenue; Q2 profit dipped 20% YoY.

Growth Prospects

  • Jute Demand Tailwinds: Govt mandates (e.g., 100% jute packaging for foodgrains) and eco-push amid plastic bans; export recovery potential (company’s historical strength).
  • Treasury Yield: Investment book (45% of assets) poised for 10-15%+ returns in bull markets; recent NSE bet leverages India’s booming equities/derivs volumes (NSE FY25 profit ₹11,246 Cr).
  • Volume/Expansion: H1 revenue pace projects FY26 >₹850 Cr (+>90% YoY); capex/CWIP supports capacity addition. EPS trajectory: H1 ₹74.6 (76% of FY25 full-year).
  • 3-5 Yr Outlook: 15-20% CAGR feasible via jute volumes (20% market share) + treasury compounding, targeting ₹100+ EPS if markets align.

Key Risks

  • Market/Investment Risk (High): 50%+ assets in equities/mutuals; FVTPL losses could erase profits (e.g., Q2 impact).
  • Commodity/Supply Risk (Medium): Jute price volatility (agri-dependent); import competition from Bangladesh.
  • Concentration Risk (Medium): 100% jute revenue; no subs/JVs for diversification.
  • Execution/Regulatory (Low-Medium): WC inefficiencies, forex exposure (unrealized gains/losses noted), policy shifts (e.g., jute pricing controls).
  • Macro: Inflation, rupee depreciation, global trade tensions.

Summary Verdict: Strong fundamentals with revenue momentum and treasury upside outweigh near-term volatility. Bullish on growth (target ₹1,800-2,000/share medium-term), but monitor investment marks-to-market. Buy/Hold for patient investors; hedge volatility risk.
