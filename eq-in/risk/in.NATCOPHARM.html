NATCO Pharma Limited (NATCOPHARM) Analysis

Date of Analysis: Based on Q2 FY26 earnings (quarter/half-year ended Sep 30, 2025), earnings call (Nov 14, 2025), financial results, board updates, AGM voting, and recent disclosures (e.g., FDA inspection Nov 21, 2025).

Natco Pharma, a generics-focused company with emphasis on complex/Para IV products (oncology, high-barrier molecules), operates in formulations (domestic/export), API, and Crop Health Sciences (CHS). FY26 H1 showed robust performance: consolidated revenue ₹2,692 Cr (+ve YoY trend), PAT ₹998 Cr (EPS ₹55.8), EBITDA margins ~46%. Balance sheet strong (net cash ~₹27,000 Cr in deposits/investments, assets ₹1.04 lakh Cr). Management guides FY26 PAT at ₹1,275-1,300 Cr (on track).

Headwinds
  • Revlimid/Lenalidomide Erosion: Q2 was peak; intense competition expected in Q3/Q4 FY26 and beyond. No aggressive budgeting for sales; ROW/US markets highly competitive post-patent expiry.
  • Elevated Expenses: Q2 “other expenses” up due to bioequivalence R&D (Para IV/clinical trials), one-time employee bonuses, inventory provisions, and patent litigation reserves. Impacted margins despite high EBITDA (46.4%).
  • Regulatory Scrutiny: US FDA issued 7 procedural observations at Manali (Chennai) API unit (Nov 17-21, 2025 inspection). Company confident of resolution, but delays/risks in approvals/supply could arise (prior Kothur warning letter lifted, aiding Canada supplies).
  • Crowded Generics Space: Para IV playbook now adopted by larger peers (e.g., “big five” Indian firms); domestic Semaglutide market competitive (Emcure/Novo in-licensed 9 months ahead).
Tailwinds
  • Adcock Ingram Acquisition: Completed Nov 11, 2025 (35.75% stake via SA sub for USD 225 Mn/ZAR 3,873 Mn). Steady PAT ~₹135-150 Cr/quarter from Q3 FY26; adds stable base (cough/cold portfolio), diversifies from volatile US generics (25-30% of future base earnings). Bought at 10-12x EBITDA (vs. India’s 30-40x).
  • CHS Momentum: Q2 revenue ₹52 Cr (near EBITDA breakeven); combinations performing well. In-principle demerger approved (Sep 25, 2025); wholly-owned sub incorporated (Nov 14, 2025) for separate 2026 listing – unlocks pharma value, provides focus/flexibility.
  • High Margins & Cash Generation: OPM ~46%; H1 operating cash flow ₹1,181 Cr. Net cash hoard supports M&A/dividends (₹1.5/share interim Q2; total FY26 ~₹7.5/share implied).
  • ROW Stability: Brazil (first-to-generic Carfilzomib), Saudi, Canada ramping post-warning lift; offsets US slowdown.
Growth Prospects
Opportunity Timeline/Details Potential Impact
Semaglutide (Ozempic generic) Domestic: First-wave launch Mar/Apr 2026 (Phase III on track; file Jan 2026). US: FTF (subject to approval/litigation), 2-3 yrs away. Domestic: +₹80-90 Cr (~20% domestic growth); ROW/US via Viatris JV. High-volume GLP-1 market despite competition.
Para IV Pipeline 3-4 new filings H2 FY26; launches: 1-2 in FY27, bigger in FY27-28 (Ibrutinib, Olaparib, Erdafitinib). Risdiplam launched (doing well, niche neurology entry). US/regulated markets jackpot potential; base +10-15% annual growth.
Domestic Formulations Stable ₹105 Cr/Q; Semaglutide/Risdiplam to drive FY27+. 10-20% CAGR; oncology/neurology focus.
Exports/Subsidiaries Brazil/Canada/Saudi scaling; Viatris partnerships. H1: ₹1,147 Cr/Q; stable post-Revlimid.
CHS Demerger Listing 2026; retain minority stake + TSAs. Value unlock; EBITDA positive trajectory.
M&A/Investments Adcock integration; niche bets (e.g., eGenesis pig-kidney transplant, CAR-T). Diversification; upside if breakthroughs.

Overall Outlook: FY26 steady (PAT flat YoY due to Revlimid cliff); FY27+ acceleration via pipeline/Acquisitions. Base business ~10-15% growth; “jackpot” upside from FTFs.

Key Risks
  1. Pipeline Execution/Regulatory: Para IV success hinges on FTF status, litigation wins, FDA approvals. Semaglutide US delayed; FDA observations at API site.
  2. Competition & Pricing: Revlimid/Semaglutide margins erode quickly; US dependency (~80% exports).
  3. Forex/Geo-Political: High US/ROW exposure; currency volatility (e.g., ZAR/USD in Adcock).
  4. Capex/Debt: Borrowings ~₹2,528 Cr; AGM approved Section 180 limits for expansion (98.7% approval). Demerger complexities.
  5. Valuation/Market Sentiment: Stock trades at premium for pipeline; demerger/M&A integration risks. Provisions/litigation could surprise.
  6. Macro: R&D intensity (~high single-digit % revenue); employee costs up.
Summary

Natco is navigating Revlimid cliff with resilience (Q2 PAT ₹518 Cr, margins 46%), bolstered by Adcock (stable ₹135-150 Cr/Q PAT from Q3) and CHS demerger (2026 listing). Tailwinds from cash fortress, ROW ramps, and Semaglutide (domestic FY27 kickstart) outweigh headwinds like competition/FDA notes. Growth hinges on Para IV launches (FY27-28 inflection); FY26 PAT ~₹1,300 Cr guided. Rating Outlook: Positive (Buy/Hold for pipeline believers); risks tilted to execution/regulatory. Target multiples: 30-35x FY27 EPS on base + upside. Monitor FDA response and Q3 results for Revlimid reality.
