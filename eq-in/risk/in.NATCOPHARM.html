Natco Pharma Limited (NATCOPHARM) Analysis

Overall Summary: Natco Pharma delivered a robust Q2 FY26 with consolidated revenue of ₹1,463 Cr (+2% YoY), EBITDA margins at 46.4% (₹679 Cr), and PAT of ₹518 Cr, driven by formulations exports (78% of revenue). Management guided H2 FY26 PAT at ₹275-300 Cr (full FY26 ~₹1,275-1,300 Cr), reflecting caution on Revlimid erosion. Balance sheet remains fortress-like (total assets ₹10,430 Cr, net cash position). Tailwinds from pipeline, ROW stability, and Adcock investment outweigh near-term headwinds like Revlimid competition and FDA observations. Growth anchored in Para-IV FTFs and Semaglutide, with 10-15% base business CAGR. Risks center on US generics volatility and regulatory hurdles. Stock poised for re-rating on demerger value unlock and pipeline execution.

Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts)

  • High-Margin Export Dominance: Formulations exports (incl. profit share/subsidiaries) at ₹1,147 Cr (78% revenue), stable despite Revlimid peak. ROW (Brazil Carfilzomib first-to-file, Saudi, Canada post-Kothur warning lift) provides diversification.
  • Strong Domestic Momentum: ₹105 Cr revenue; Risdiplam launch gaining traction in neurology/SMA niche. Semaglutide Phase-III on track for India first-wave launch (Mar/Apr 2026), potential +20% domestic boost (₹80-90 Cr annualized).
  • Adcock Ingram Acquisition: 35.75% stake (₹1,873 Cr/ZAR 3,873 Mn) adds stable ₹135-150 Cr quarterly PAT from Q3 FY26 (cough/cold base business, 25-30% of future earnings). Bought at 10-12x EBITDA vs. India’s 30-40x.
  • Agro (Crop Health Sciences) Turnaround: Revenue ₹52 Cr (+270% YoY), nearing EBITDA breakeven. Demerger approved (Wholly-owned sub incorporation); separate 2026 listing to unlock value (minority stake retained for R&D/TSAs).
  • Robust Balance Sheet & Shareholder Returns: Assets up to ₹10,430 Cr; cash/deposits ₹27k Cr+. Interim dividends: ₹1.50/share (Q2), prior ₹2/share. R&D spend signals pipeline aggression.
  • Strategic Investments: Finite stakes (₹40-50 Cr/yr) in disruptives like eGenesis (pig kidney transplant), CAR-T, Stenoir (mobile drug delivery) for upside/relationships.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Revlimid Erosion: Q2 last “strong” quarter; intense Q3/Q4 competition (no major revenue budgeted). H1 FY26 revenue flat YoY despite this; base reset expected.
  • Elevated Expenses: Q2 “other expenses” ₹414 Cr (+74% YoY) from Para-IV bioequivalence R&D, clinicals (India first-time generics), one-time bonuses, litigation/inventory provisions.
  • Regulatory Scrutiny: US FDA Form-483 with 7 procedural observations at Manali (Chennai) API unit (Nov 17-21, 2025); management confident of resolution. Past Kothur warning lifted, but ongoing vigilance needed.
  • Agro Drag: Segment loss ₹66 Cr (Q2); high R&D but improving.
  • Pipeline Timing: FY26-27 “dull” (1-2 smaller US launches); bigger FTFs (e.g., Ibrutinib, Olaparib, Erdafitinib) in FY27-29.
