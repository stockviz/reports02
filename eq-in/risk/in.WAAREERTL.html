Analysis of Waaree Renewable Technologies Limited (WAAREERTL)

Company Overview: WAAREERTL is the EPC arm of Waaree Group (subsidiary of Waaree Energies Ltd.), specializing in solar EPC, O&M, and IPP development. It benefits from vertical integration (modules to EPC/O&M), with FY25 revenue of Rs. 1,598 Cr (+114% CAGR FY22-25). Q2/H1 FY26 showed record performance: H1 revenue Rs. 1,378 Cr (+81% YoY), EBITDA Rs. 275 Cr (+145% YoY, 20% margin), PAT Rs. 203 Cr (+148% YoY, 15% margin). Unexecuted order book: 3.48 GWp (12-15 months execution); bidding pipeline: 27+ GWp. Key recent wins include 980 MWp enhancement (Rs. 1,018 Cr value) and 1,218/29.4 MWp projects.

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Explosive Financial Momentum: Q2 revenue +48% YoY (Rs. 775 Cr), PAT +117% (Rs. 116 Cr); H1 margins expanded (EBITDA 20% vs. 15% prior). Asset-light model, ROE 65%, ROCE 62%.
  • Robust Demand & Order Visibility: Solar EPC tailwinds from India’s 500 GW RE target by 2030 (solar at 127 GW installed as of Sep 2025). Strong pipeline, marquee clients (Reliance, JSW, Adani), lenders/consultants ties.
  • Policy Support: Govt incentives (PLI, auctions), projected India EPC demand Rs. 1,700 Bn by FY28. Global solar additions ~500 GW annually.
  • Strategic Strengths: Group synergies (Waaree modules), in-house design/procurement/EHS, O&M portfolio (769 MWp), IPP expansion (approved 66 MWp capex). Credit rating upgrades (A stable).
  • Execution Track Record: Delivered 2+ GWp historically; large projects (e.g., 2 GWp Bikaner) progressing.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Working Capital Intensity: Receivables up to Rs. 613 Cr (H1), inventories +Rs. 47 Cr; cash from ops flat (Rs. 84 Cr) despite profit surge due to project cycles.
  • Margin Pressure Potential: Employee/other expenses rising (Q2: Rs. 13/12 Cr); EBITDA margins volatile (13-20%).
  • Execution Scale-Up: Managing 3.48 GWp amid supply chain/logistics in diverse sites (Rajasthan, Maharashtra, etc.).
  • Competition: Crowded EPC space with players like Sterling & Wilson, Tata Power Solar.
  • Macro Sensitivity: Monsoon delays, land acquisition in remote areas.

Growth Prospects

  • High Visibility: 3.48 GWp order book execution to drive FY26 revenue >Rs. 3,000 Cr (implied from pipeline). Recent wins (1.2 GWp Q2) signal momentum.
  • Diversification: O&M growth (third-party), IPP (107 MWp underway + 66 MWp approved), BESS (40 MWh order), substations/transmission, international EPC.
  • Market Expansion: India solar EPC market to Rs. 1,700 Bn; group ecosystem aids tender wins. Bidding 27+ GWp; global tailwinds (energy transition).
  • Projections: H1 pace suggests FY26 revenue Rs. 2,800+ Cr (+75% YoY), margins sustained at 18-20% via scale/efficiencies. Long-term: 20-30% CAGR via RE services (hybrid, green H2, pumped hydro).
  • Capital Allocation: ESOP amendment approved (postal ballot 99.85% yes); strong cash (Rs. 68 Cr) supports IPP capex.

Key Risks

Risk Category Description Mitigation
Execution Delays in large projects (e.g., land, approvals); 12-15 month timeline. Proven track record, in-house teams, EHS focus.
Policy/Regulatory Tender cancellations, subsidy cuts, net-zero policy shifts. Domestic focus (no promoter interest in orders), diversified clients.
Financial Debt (Rs. 105 Cr non-current), forex (minor international), WC cycle. Net cash positive, low leverage (ROCE 62%).
Operational Supply chain (modules, panels), weather, labor shortages. Vendor alliances, group supply.

Summary Outlook: Strong Buy/Outperform thesis intact. Tailwinds from India’s solar boom, order book, and execution prowess outweigh headwinds. Growth prospects excellent (3x revenue potential in 3-5 years via EPC/IPP), but monitor WC and execution. Risks manageable via group backing; analyst meet (Nov 20) to provide updates. Valuation likely premium (peer multiples 20-30x FY26E EPS ~Rs. 40+).
