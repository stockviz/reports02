TVS Supply Chain Solutions Limited (TVSSCS) - Q2 & H1 FY26 Analysis

TVSSCS, a tech-led, asset-light 3PL/4PL provider with two segments—Integrated Supply Chain Solutions (ISCS, ~75% revenue) and Global Forwarding Solutions (GFS, ~25% revenue)—reported resilient performance amid segment-specific challenges. Consolidated Q2 revenue grew 6% YoY to ₹2,663 Cr, with Adj. EBITDA margin stable at 6.7%. PAT surged 54% YoY to ₹16 Cr, driven by ISCS strength and cost discipline. H1 revenue +4% YoY, PAT +384% to ₹87 Cr (boosted by JV gains). New wins (₹204 Cr) and ₹6,200 Cr pipeline signal visibility. Medium-term PBT target: 4% by Q4 FY27 via Project One (₹110-120 Cr annualized savings).

Headwinds (Challenges Pressuring Performance)

  • GFS Segment Weakness: Revenue flat YoY (₹670 Cr in Q2), EBITDA margin compressed to 2.2% from 4.2% due to persistent freight rate/pricing pressure, soft demand, and macro uncertainties (e.g., tariffs minimally impacting India exports). Peers also stressed; H1 revenue -2% YoY.
  • Cost Pressures: Employee costs +12% YoY (inflation, new projects); other expenses +38% YoY (rentals, repairs in UK/Europe). Sequential increases in freight/sub-contracting tied to GFS volumes.
  • India ISCS Softness: Flat revenue (exiting low-margin accounts, project delays); expected to recover in H2.
  • Net Debt Rise: To ₹286 Cr (from ₹232 Cr) due to North America capex.

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • ISCS Strength: Q2 revenue +8% YoY to ₹1,993 Cr, EBITDA margin expanded to 8.7% (from 8.2%) via operational excellence, rightsizing, right-shoring, and early Project One benefits. Europe/NA recovery key.
  • Cost & Efficiency Gains: Project One (UK/Europe integration) on track for ₹50-60 Cr in-year savings. Lease optimization reduced ROU depreciation/interest.
  • Strong Cash Flow: H1 ops cash ₹105 Cr (vs. negative prior); disciplined WC management.
  • Business Wins: ₹204 Cr new orders (8% of Q2 revenue); diversified across auto, consumer, IT, energy.

Growth Prospects

  • Revenue Trajectory: Mid-teens targeted; ISCS to sustain 8-10% (India 4-5% QoQ, NA to $500M organically). GFS sequential uptick (volumes +rates); stabilization by FY26-end.
  • Pipeline & Encirclement: ₹6,200 Cr order book (1/3 India); focus on wallet-share expansion, tech (IoT, analytics), new markets (NA/Europe).
  • Margin Expansion: ISCS to 8.5%+; GFS to 3.5-4%; overall PBT to 4% by Q4 FY27 via leverage, mix shift.
  • Strategic Levers: C3 framework (Customer/Capability/Country); $2.5B revenue vision long-term; 91 Fortune 500 clients.

Key Risks

  • GFS Macro Volatility: Freight cycles, tariffs, geopolitics; prolonged pricing pressure could delay recovery.
  • Execution Risks: Project One delivery; integration synergies realization amid Europe costs.
  • Forex/Inflation: Exposure to multi-currency ops (4 continents); employee cost inflation.
  • Competition/Geopolitics: Intense 3PL space; tariff/trade shifts (e.g., India exports).
  • Capex Dependency: NA expansion funding via debt; inventory/receivables cycles.

Overall Outlook: Bullish on ISCS (core growth engine), cautious on GFS (stabilizing). Strong BD pipeline, cash discipline, and 4% PBT goal position TVSSCS for mid-teens growth FY26+. Stock trades at ~1x FY26E sales; monitor GFS recovery.
