NAVA Limited (NSE/BSE: NAVA) - Analysis Summary

NAVA Limited, a diversified multinational in metals (ferro alloys), energy (thermal/solar power), mining, agribusiness, and emerging sectors (healthcare/pharma), reported solid H1 FY26 results (ended Sep 30, 2025) with revenue growth but moderated PAT due to one-offs. Key metrics: Consolidated revenue ₹2,16K Cr (flat YoY), PAT ₹438 Cr (-28% YoY attributable to parent), strong cash ₹1.74K Cr, ROE ~11-12% implied. Declared ₹3/share interim dividend (300% payout). Below is a structured analysis of headwinds, tailwinds, growth prospects, and key risks based on the provided filings (Q2/H1 results, auditor notes, press release, board outcomes).

Tailwinds (Supportive Factors)

  • Operational Resilience & Volume Growth: Ferro alloys sales volumes surged 70% YoY to 32.8K MT (silico manganese +92% YoY), driving segment revenue up. Energy PLF steady at 80%+ (MEL Zambia at 80.4%), with Odisha CPP transitioning to IPP (Nov 1, 2025) for better revenue visibility.
  • Cash Generation & Dividend Flow: Net cash from ops ₹1.57K Cr (despite taxes); subsidiaries (esp. MEL) distributed US$72.8M dividends since Apr 2025 (NGPL: US$24M cumulative). Balance sheet strong: Cash ₹1.74K Cr (up 76% YoY), borrowings up but net debt low (~₹1.6K Cr non-current).
  • Cost Efficiencies: ECL reversal ₹9.95K Cr boosted PBT; coal price cuts by Singareni improving domestic energy margins.
  • Shareholder Returns: Consistent dividends (₹3/share; record date Nov 14), signaling confidence amid 15%+ YoY stock returns implied in context.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Profitability Pressure: Consolidated PAT down 28% YoY to ₹438 Cr (parent share) due to higher dep (₹18.5K Cr), employee costs (+28% YoY), and unallocable expenses. Q2 sequential drop (₹130 Cr vs. ₹308 Cr in Q1).
  • Receivables Delays: ₹489 Cr overdue from MEL customer (down from ₹1,375 Cr; US$55M pending), despite sovereign guarantee & arbitration win—ECL ₹47 Cr taken, but cash flows lagged.
  • Segment Weakness: Ferro alloys margins thin (₹16 Cr H1 profit); discontinued ops loss ₹0.7 Cr; “Other” segment loss-making (agri/pharma ramp-up phase).
  • FX & Input Volatility: Unrealized FX gains but exposure to USD/ZMW (Zambia); higher capex strained working capital (inventories down but receivables collected selectively).

Growth Prospects (High Potential)

  • Energy Leadership (Core Driver, 90%+ Revenue): MEL 300MW expansion on-track; 100MW solar (MSEL) construction started (H2 FY27 COD); IPP shift unlocks merchant sales upside. Zambia power demand + mining synergies position for 20-30% capacity growth.
  • Metals/Mining Ramp: Ferro alloys throughput up; mining steady (4.6% YoY coal volume growth at MEL). Potential external sales expansion.
  • Diversification: Zambia agri (avocados first yield H2 FY26; sugar EPC finalizing); SE Asia healthcare/pharma via Singapore subs. Total assets up 11% YoY to ₹12.5K Cr, CWIP ₹1.37K Cr signals ₹50-100 Cr+ revenue add in 2-3 yrs.
  • M&A/Investments: ₹1.48K Cr investments (up 28% YoY); strategic oversight via NGPL Vice-Chairman (postal ballot pending). Analyst meet (Nov 25) may signal M&A.
  • Outlook: 15-20% revenue CAGR feasible (FY25 base ₹4K Cr), driven by energy (65% EBITDA); EPS ₹15.5 (H1, post-split) supports 20%+ ROE.
