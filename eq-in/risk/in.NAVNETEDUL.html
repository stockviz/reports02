Navneet Education Limited (NAVNETEDUL) Analysis: Q2/H1 FY26 (Sep 2025)

Navneet Education Ltd., a leader in educational publishing (print/digital) and stationery (domestic/export), reported standalone H1 revenue of INR 1,038 Cr (flat YoY), with Q2 down 9% YoY to INR 246 Cr due to seasonality and export weakness. H1 EBITDA margin stable at ~20% (ex-exceptional items). Publication segment grew 12% YoY in Q2; stationery exports fell 22% due to US tariffs. Company declared interim dividend of INR 1.50/share (75%). AGM resolutions passed with >99% approval.

Headwinds (Challenges Pressuring Performance)

  • Export Stationery Weakness: 22% YoY decline in Q2 (INR 155 Cr) due to high US tariffs (50% vs. 20-25% peers); US inflation reduced customer orders by 12-15%. Heavy US reliance (~87% of exports).
  • Realization Pressure: Domestic stationery flat (INR 38 Cr Q2) despite volume growth, hit by lower paper prices (down 6-7% YoY at ~INR 65k/ton).
  • Cost Headwinds: 6% GST hike on publication paper (to be passed on); stationery GST confusion delaying purchases (potential 3-4% cost drop if clarified).
  • Subsidiary Drag: Indiannica (CBSE publishing) H1 revenue INR 3.5 Cr, loss INR 16 Cr; overall consolidated Q2 PBT loss INR 17 Cr.
  • Seasonality: Q2/H1 weak quarters; full-year view needed.

Tailwinds (Supportive Factors)

  • Publication Momentum: 12% Q2 growth (INR 91 Cr) from lower-grade curriculum changes; cycle expected to extend to higher grades over years.
  • Export Recovery Outlook: Tariff normalization expected by Dec 2025; management confident of regaining preference over competitors (Indonesia/Brazil/China).
  • Domestic Resilience: Stationery volumes up ~4% H1; Youva brand (non-paper) strong early response (6-9% of domestic revenue), geometry boxes/writing instruments gaining traction.
  • Financial Stability: H1 working capital cycle stable (84-88 days); cash reserves strong (INR 322 Cr standalone); no major debt.
  • Shareholder Confidence: Robust AGM voting (>99% approval); consistent dividends.

Growth Prospects

  • Publications (Key Driver): 15%+ FY27 growth from curriculum cycle; CBSE (Rise/Indiannica) from 5% to 10-11% of revenue in 2 years; EBIT margin 25-26% FY26, +2% FY27. Digital integration (Rise app) boosting adoption in Maharashtra/Gujarat.
  • Stationery Expansion: Domestic paper 12-15% volume growth FY26; non-paper 100% YoY for 3 years. Exports: 12-14% growth post-tariffs; diversification to Middle East/Europe (13% now). Molded plastics plant operational, new categories for US/domestic.
  • Overall Guidance: 14-15% revenue growth FY27 if tariffs normalize; capex focused on stationery capacity/utilization (domestic fallback). Indiannica revenue target INR 65 Cr FY26 (profitable). Potential K12 Techno IPO stake monetization (14.3% holding ~INR 770 Cr valuation).
  • Long-Term: Technology integration, product diversification, pan-India/CBSE push; H1 core ops stable despite macros.

Key Risks

Overall Outlook: Short-term export/tariff pressure offsets publication tailwinds, but H1 core stability and FY26-27 guidance (12-15% growth) signal resilience. Bullish on curriculum cycle and domestic diversification; monitor US trade updates. Valuation supported by dividends and steady margins. Recommendation: Hold/Buy on dips for long-term education sector growth.
