Summary Analysis for PASUPTAC (Pasupati Acrylon Limited)

Company Overview: Pasupati Acrylon Ltd. (CIN: L50102UP1982PLC015532) manufactures acrylic fibres, CPP films, and recently launched a 150 KL/day grain-based ethanol distillery (operational from Mar 2025). Listed on BSE (500456) & NSE (PASUPTAC). H1 FY26 (Apr-Sep 2025) showed robust growth: Revenue ₹496 Cr (+78% YoY), PBT ₹24 Cr (+28% YoY), driven by ethanol ramp-up. Equity capital ₹89 Cr; total assets ₹645 Cr (↑8%). All AGM resolutions (Sep 2025) passed unanimously. Minor promoter group change (demise of Shri Manish Jain, Nov 2025).

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Ethanol Segment Boom: New distillery contributed ₹171 Cr revenue (35% of H1 total) and ₹13 Cr profit; Q2 alone ₹116 Cr revenue/₹13 Cr profit. Aligns with India’s ethanol blending push (EBP targets 20%+ by 2025).
  • Strong Financial Momentum: Revenue doubled YoY; net profit +28%; EPS ₹2.01 (vs ₹1.57). Positive operating cash flow (₹5 Cr vs -₹42 Cr YoY) signals improving efficiency.
  • Diversified Segments: Fibre (55% revenue, steady ₹275 Cr) and CPP films (stable ₹50 Cr) provide base; total segment assets ₹645 Cr.
  • Liquidity & Balance Sheet: Cash ↑ to ₹32 Cr; current investments ₹66 Cr. Promoter holding ~66% (5.87 Cr shares); governance intact (AGM approvals).

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Working Capital Strain: Inventories +28% (₹174 Cr), receivables +53% (₹76 Cr), trade payables +27% (₹117 Cr). Led to large adjustments in cash flow.
  • Rising Costs: Finance costs surged to ₹7 Cr (vs ₹1 Cr YoY) due to borrowings (total ₹110 Cr); depreciation ↑ (₹6 Cr).
  • Comparability Issues: H1 FY26 not directly comparable to prior periods due to ethanol startup; Q1 weak (revenue ₹216 Cr, profit ₹2 Cr).
  • Promoter Transition: Loss of promoter group member (Manish Jain) – potential minor impact on group stability (per Reg 31A).

Growth Prospects

  • High: Ethanol capacity utilization ramp-up could drive FY26 revenue >₹1,200 Cr (from ₹632 Cr FY25). Govt EBP incentives (pricing, subsidies) supportive. Fibre/CPP steady at 5-10% growth.
  • Capex Pipeline: CWIP ₹3 Cr; prior ethanol investment (~₹250 Cr assets) yielding quick returns.
  • Expansion Potential: No subsidiaries; scope for ethanol scaling, exports, or fibre capacity add-ons. Analyst upside if ethanol sustains 30-40% margins.

Key Risks

Risk Category Description Mitigation
Operational Ethanol execution (feedstock availability, utilization); commodity volatility (grain/ethanol prices). Grain-based (diversified input); early profitability shows ramp-up.
Financial Debt servicing (interest cover ~3.5x); WC cycle (DPO/DRO stretch). Positive cash flow; ₹66 Cr liquid investments.

Overall Outlook: Positive (Buy/Hold) – Ethanol tailwind dominates, offsetting headwinds. Target sustained 20-30% revenue CAGR if EBP delivers. Monitor Q3 results for ethanol consistency and WC trends. Market cap ~₹400-500 Cr (est.); undervalued vs. growth.
