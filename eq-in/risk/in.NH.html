Narayana Hrudayalaya Limited (NH) - Q2 FY26 Analysis

Overview: NH delivered a stellar Q2 FY26 with consolidated revenue of ₹16,438 Mn (+20.3% YoY), EBITDA of ₹4,265 Mn (25.9% margin, +28.3% YoY), and PAT of ₹2,583 Mn (15.7% margin). India ops grew 8.8% YoY on payer/case-mix optimization; Cayman surged 78% YoY post new hospital/insurance ramp-up. Net debt/equity at 0.06x. Recent UK acquisition (Practice Plus Group, GBP 190 Mn) adds foothold. Strong tailwinds from ops efficiency/digital, but headwinds in select units/acquisitions.

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Operational Excellence in India: Payer-mix shift, niche procedures (e.g., 197 robotic cardiac surgeries/Q), higher ARPP (₹149k IP/OP), stable ALOS (~4.3 days). EBITDA margins hit 23.8% (ex-clinics/ins). Flagships (Bangalore/Kolkata) lead growth.
  • Cayman Momentum: Hospital revenues ~US$42.6 Mn (43-44% margins); insurance doubled QoQ (US$9 Mn GWP, stabilizing losses). Market: US$300-350 Mn TAM; large employers shifting.
  • Integrated Care Scaling: India clinics/insurance revenues +90% YoY; losses declining. Retail/SME traction; synergies with hospitals.
  • Digital/Tech Edge: Medha AI/Athma rollout (e.g., Kenya/Saudi); apps (3.5 Mn installs, 40% MAU); AI for ICU/early warnings; 50% drop in incidents.
  • Policy Boost: CGHS hikes (~₹2-2.5 Cr/month revenue, 70-80% EBITDA flow).
  • Financial Health: Low leverage; ₹3,000 Cr 3-yr capex (Greenfield like HSR Bangalore/Rajarhat Kolkata).

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Underperformer Units: Mumbai breakeven struggles (pediatric-to-adult transition); early Q2 negative EBITDA, October uptick but no firm timeline.
  • Intl Fluctuations: Bangladesh volumes volatile (27% QoQ but winding down); intl not core focus.
  • Insurance Volatility: Cayman/India loss ratios swing QoQ; too early for breakeven guidance (rolling 3-4Q view advised).
  • Reporting Delays: Offshore consolidations (Cayman/UK) slow results; automation underway.
  • Capex Front-Loading: Q2 spend ₹166 Mn (FY26 guidance on track but spillovers possible).
