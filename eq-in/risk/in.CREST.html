Analysis of Crest Ventures Limited (CREST): Headwinds, Tailwinds, Growth Prospects, and Key Risks

Overview: Crest Ventures Limited (NBFC with focus on investments, financial services, broking, and real estate) reported strong H1 FY26 results (ended Sep 30, 2025). Standalone total income ₹6,985 lakhs (down QoQ but up YoY), net profit ₹2,493 lakhs; consolidated total income ₹9,265 lakhs, net profit ₹2,948 lakhs. Total assets grew to ₹1,69,012 lakhs (consolidated), driven by investments (₹32,260 lakhs) and inventories (₹42,559 lakhs). Board approved NCD issuance up to ₹100 Cr. High CAR (71.63% standalone). Below is a structured summary based on filings.

Tailwinds (Positive Factors)

  • Robust Profitability & Balance Sheet Strength: H1 net profit up YoY (standalone +YoY from ₹6,269 lakhs prior H1; consolidated similar). OCI boosted by ₹4,069 lakhs (standalone) from FVTOCI equities. Equity ₹1,13,609 lakhs (standalone), Net Worth ₹1,26,185 lakhs (consolidated). Debt/Equity low at 0.17x.
  • Diversified Revenue Streams: Investing/financial (44% consolidated revenue), broking (14%), real estate (42%). Real estate revenue ₹2,182 lakhs (H1), supported by inventories +₹34,088 lakhs YoY.
  • Fundraising & Liquidity: Approved ₹100 Cr NCDs (private placement). Cash ₹7,897 lakhs (consolidated, +₹2,256 lakhs H1). Operating cash flow +₹1,639 lakhs.
  • High Adequacy & Shareholder Returns: CAR 71.63%; dividend ₹1/share approved at AGM (10% payout).
  • Strategic Moves: Analyst meets, new subsidiaries (e.g., Crest Shelters, Crest Prime post-Q2), ESOP incentives.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Revenue Volatility: Q2 total income down sharply QoQ (standalone ₹2,078 lakhs vs ₹4,907 lakhs Q1; consolidated similar). Interest income halved QoQ (₹1,499 lakhs). Real estate revenue volatile (Q2 ₹379 lakhs vs Q1 ₹1,803 lakhs).
  • Rising Costs: Employee expenses +177% YoY H1 (₹1,525 lakhs consolidated), finance costs stable but high (₹937 lakhs). Share of losses from LLPs (₹124 lakhs standalone H1) and associates (₹205 lakhs consolidated).
  • Segment Pressures: Real estate segment loss in Q2 (₹322 lakhs); broking growth muted.
  • Unsecured Exposure: ₹1,553 Cr deposits with counterparties (real estate JVs); auditors’ Emphasis of Matter flags recoverability reliance on management (title deeds/valuations held, no extra provision).

Growth Prospects

  • High (Medium-Term: 2-3 Years):
    • Real Estate Expansion: Inventories signal project pipeline; unsecured deposits tied to JVs with development potential. New sub-acquisitions (e.g., Sutlej Housing).
    • Financial Services Leverage: Loans ₹51,656 lakhs (+YoY), investments active (net buys ₹15,849 lakhs H1 standalone). NCD raise to fund lending/investments.
    • Diversification: Broking steady; subsidiaries contribute (6 reviewed subs: ₹795 lakhs Q2 income). Segments assets: Real estate ₹78,534 lakhs (47% total).
    • Capital Efficiency: Projected PAT growth (H1 EPS ₹10.25 consolidated, up YoY); low leverage supports scaling. Target: Asset base >₹2,000 Cr via fundraising/projects.
  • Catalysts: RPT approvals (₹ limits for subs/parent), ESOP retention, RBI-compliant NBFC status.
