Analysis of Shiv Aum Steels Limited (SHIVAUM)

Shiv Aum Steels Limited is a steel trading company (not a manufacturer) listed on NSE Emerge (SME platform), approved for migration to the NSE Main Board effective November 14, 2025. This analysis is based on the provided documents: migration approval letters/circulars and unaudited H1 FY26 financial results (ended Sep 30, 2025; all figures in ₹ Lakhs unless stated). Key financial trends show revenue and PAT decline YoY (standalone revenue -11%, PAT -65%), driven by lower trading volumes amid high inventories and working capital intensity. The company has ~13.6 Cr equity shares outstanding, market lot of 1 post-migration, ISIN INE719F01016.

Tailwinds (Positive Factors)

  • Main Board Migration: Effective Nov 14, 2025, shifts from SME Emerge to Capital Market Main Board, enhancing liquidity, visibility, and access to institutional investors (e.g., FIIs/MFs). Trading suspension on Emerge from the same date ensures seamless transition.
  • Stable Balance Sheet: Equity base solid (₹1,360 Cr share capital + ₹10,460 Cr reserves standalone). Net worth ~₹11,820 Cr. Cash balances up to ₹768 Cr standalone (FY26 H1), supporting operations.
  • Subsidiary Contribution: Wholly-owned Shivoham Ventures Pvt Ltd adds minor revenue (₹1,038 in H1 FY26) with contained losses (₹2.93 PAT loss).
  • Steel Sector Backdrop: India’s infra push (e.g., highways, housing) supports steel demand; trading model benefits from volume growth if prices stabilize.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Revenue & Profitability Decline: Standalone H1 FY26 revenue ₹22,091 (-11% YoY from ₹24,860); consolidated ₹22,949 (-9% YoY). PAT halved+ (standalone ₹261 vs ₹751 YoY; EPS ₹1.92 vs ₹5.52). Driven by lower purchases of traded goods (core business) and negative inventory changes (₹1,453 expense).
  • Margin Pressure: Gross margins thin (trading-focused); finance costs high (₹383 standalone H1, 17% of expenses). Employee/other expenses stable but not scaling with revenue.
  • Working Capital Strain: High inventories (₹9,665, 48% of assets), trade receivables (₹4,264 standalone), and short-term borrowings (₹6,256, down from ₹7,968 FY25 end but still elevated). Negative cash flow from ops adjustments in prior periods.
  • Macro Steel Volatility: Global steel prices softened in H1 FY26; domestic oversupply/imports could persist.

Growth Prospects

  • Moderate (Short-Term Cautious; Long-Term Sector-Driven):
    • Trading volumes could rebound with Main Board liquidity boost and FY26 infra capex (e.g., govt budgets). Full FY25 revenue was ₹54,820 (up from prior); H1 FY26 at 40% of that pace suggests potential annualization to ~₹44k-50k if Q3/Q4 improves.
    • EPS trajectory: FY25 ₹7.11; H1 FY26 ₹1.92 (not annualized). Reserves grew modestly (₹10,460 standalone).
    • Expansion via subsidiary or inventory optimization; current investments (₹1,135 current + ₹234 non-current) could yield if deployed.
    • Outlook: 10-15% revenue CAGR possible if steel demand hits 7-8% (India Steel Assoc est.); migration unlocks 2-3x liquidity vs SME.
  • Catalysts: NEAPS filings for compliance; website dissemination of results signals transparency.

Overall Summary

Neutral to Mildly Positive Outlook. Migration to Main Board is a strong tailwind for liquidity/valuation re-rating (potential 20-30% stock pop post-listing), but H1 FY26 results highlight headwinds from steel trading cyclicality—revenue/PAT down sharply amid high working capital. Growth hinges on volume recovery in steel demand (infra-led), targeting FY26 revenue ~₹50k+ and PAT ~₹600-800 (conservative). Investment Stance: Accumulate on dips post-migration for traders; hold for long-term if steel cycle turns. Monitor Q3 results (due Feb 2026) and steel prices (e.g., via MCX/HRC index). Market Cap implied pre-migration ~₹200-300 Cr (at ₹150-200/share est.); post-migration upside to ₹300-400/share if execution improves. No red flags on governance (signed by Whole-time Director Sanjay Bansal, DIN 00235509).
