Analysis of Techno Electric & Engineering Company Limited (TECHNOE)

Overview: Techno Electric & Engineering Company Limited (listed on NSE: TECHNOE, BSE: 542141) is an EPC player in power transmission, distribution, renewables, data centres, and smart metering (AMI). Q2/H1 FY26 (ended 30 Sep 2025) results show robust topline growth (consolidated H1 revenue ₹13,694 Cr, +68% YoY) and profitability (H1 PAT ₹2,401 Cr, +25% YoY), driven by EPC execution. Strong balance sheet (standalone equity ₹39,089 Cr, cash/investments ~₹20k Cr). Recent QIP (₹12,500 Cr raised Jul 2024) supports expansion. However, overdue receivables (₹896 Cr) and project risks persist. Dividend of ₹9/share declared (yield ~4-5% at current prices).

Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts)

  • Explosive Revenue & Margin Expansion: Q2 consolidated revenue ₹8,434 Cr (+91% YoY); EBITDA margins healthy (~18-20% implied). EPC order execution in power T&D and data centres scaling up.
  • QIP Capital Infusion: ₹12,500 Cr deployed efficiently; minor deviation (₹2,000 Cr reallocated from cancelled NERGS-I power project to Chennai Data Centre) approved by Audit Committee—within original objects, no shareholder approval needed.
  • Subsidiary Diversification: 14 subs (e.g., Techno Data Center, AMI Solutions, NERES/NERGS transmission SPVs) contributing revenues (H1 ₹868 Cr aggregate). Overseas presence (Singapore sub).
  • Shareholder Returns: ₹1,047 Cr dividend paid (H1 FY26); AGM resolutions passed overwhelmingly (99%+ approval).
  • Strong Liquidity: Standalone current assets ₹44,929 Cr (investments ₹19k Cr); low debt (borrowings <₹350 Cr).
  • Board Stability: Audit Committee reconstituted with new independent director (Ms. Dipali Khanna as Chair).

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Working Capital Strain: Negative operating cash flow (standalone H1: -₹779 Cr; consol -₹1,746 Cr) due to sharp trade receivables rise (standalone +₹4,589 Cr YoY)—ties up liquidity despite high profitability.
  • Overdue Receivables: ₹896 Cr (10-15% of revenue) overdue/substantially past due (Bengal Energy arbitration ₹118 Cr; Afghanistan termination ₹501 Cr; REC differential ₹177 Cr). No provisions taken (management confident on recovery via legal/regulatory routes), but auditors flagged emphasis-of-matter.
  • Discontinued Operations Volatility: Q1 PAT boost (₹252 Cr net from LPS claim), but one-off; absent in Q2.
  • Segment Concentration: No formal segments reported (Ind AS 108), but heavy reliance on EPC (power infra ~80% implied).

Growth Prospects (High Potential)

  • Data Centre Pivot: ₹2,000 Cr QIP allocation to Chennai DC (construction, GIS substation)—taps India’s booming DC market (projected 30%+ CAGR to 2030 amid AI/cloud demand).
  • Power Infra Boom: NERGS/POWERGRID projects; renewables (RECs, wind); AMI/smart metering subs scaling. Order book likely strong (investor presentation link hints at pipeline).
  • Transmission SPVs: NERES XVI, NERGS-I subs position for tariff-based awards (stable annuities post-commercial ops).
  • Capex Ramp-Up: H1 investing cash positive (₹2,759 Cr consol) from subs investments; total addressable market in T&D/digital infra >₹5 lakh Cr (Govt PLI/PM GatiShakti).
  • FY26 Guidance Implicit: H1 already >50% of FY25 revenue; QIP enables 20-30% CAGR potential if receivables normalize.
