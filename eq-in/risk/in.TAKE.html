Analysis of TAKE Solutions Limited (BSE: 532890, NSE: TAKE)

Overview:
TAKE Solutions Limited, a life sciences and support services company, reported unaudited Q2/H1 FY26 results (ended Sep 30, 2025) on Oct 27, 2025. Standalone results show negligible revenue (₹1.60 Mn Q2), ongoing losses (₹27.93 Mn H1 total), and a severely depleted balance sheet (total assets ₹288.94 Mn, cash ₹0.34 Mn). Consolidated results reflect no continuing ops revenue, but a H1 profit of ₹53.80 Mn driven by discontinued ops gains (₹65.93 Mn from divestment of Take Consultancy Services Inc.). Both standalone and consolidated reports carry qualified conclusions from auditors (Sundar Srini & Sridhar), citing material uncertainties on going concern, tax asset recoverability (₹88-119 Mn), indirect tax credits (₹52-76 Mn), litigations (₹108-721 Mn contingent), and business viability. FY25 saw ₹697 Mn loss from Ecron Acunova divestment. Company relies on diversification/M&A (expected FY26 closure) post non-compete in CRO space. AGM (Sep 30, 2025) approvals were near-unanimous (99.97-99.99% in favor), but low public participation (17.5% of non-institutional shares polled). Auditors resigned post-period (Oct 8, 2025). Overall Outlook: Distressed shell company with high insolvency risk; trading at deep discount to book value amid going concern doubts.

Headwinds (Challenges Pressuring Performance)

  • Operational Stagnation: No meaningful revenue from continuing operations (standalone H1 revenue ₹1.60 Mn; cons. ₹0). Business “severely impacted” with “significant deterioration” over 2 years; single segment (life sciences/support services) collapsed.
  • Cash Burn & Liquidity Crunch: Standalone operating cash flow -(₹31.36 Mn) H1; cons. -(₹31.39 Mn). Cash equivalents dwindled to ₹0.34 Mn standalone/₹0.99 Mn cons.; negative working capital changes exacerbate.
  • Profitability Erosion: H1 standalone loss ₹27.93 Mn; cons. continuing ops loss ₹12.13 Mn. ECL provisions (₹15.79 Mn), finance costs (₹2.94-3.48 Mn), and employee expenses persist despite low activity.
  • Regulatory & Compliance Pressures: Significant unpaid statutory dues; trade payables/other liabilities unconfirmed/reconciled (₹8-19 Mn in Navitas LLP). Auditor resignation signals governance risks.
  • Low Shareholder Engagement: Only 33.4% total votes polled at AGM; public institutions nil; reflects waning interest.

Tailwinds (Supportive Factors)

  • Asset Monetization Gains: H1 discontinued ops profit ₹65.93 Mn cons. (₹0.10 Mn standalone) from selling Take Consultancy Services Inc. (Sep 26, 2025). Prior Ecron Acunova sale (Q3 FY25) cleared immediate statutory/debt obligations, providing short-term liquidity buffer.
  • Tax Settlements Progress: Settled FY09/FY11 liabilities under Direct Tax Vivad Se Vishwas Scheme 2024; management expects favorable outcomes on pending litigations/refunds.
  • Lean Balance Sheet: Post-divestments, non-current assets minimal (₹92-129 Mn, mostly tax assets); equity stable at ₹147-146 Mn (FV ₹1/share).
  • Board/AGM Stability: Unanimous approvals for financials, director re-appointment (Srinivasan H.R.), and new secretarial auditor (5-year term).

Growth Prospects

  • Limited & Speculative: No organic growth visibility; zero continuing revenue signals full pivot needed. Management’s “pragmatic” strategy focuses on non-cash M&A/partnerships in new verticals (post-CRO non-compete), with “positive closure expected FY26.” Navitas LLP share of income/loss remains minor (₹0.32 Mn loss H1).
  • Upside Scenarios: Successful diversification could unlock value from tax assets (₹92-129 Mn) and other financial assets (₹144 Mn). Past divestments demonstrate execution capability.
  • Base Case: FY26 profitability hinges on M&A; without it, cash runway <6 months. EPS H1 cons. ₹0.37 (basic); FY25 audited cons. profit ₹374 Mn was anomaly from write-backs/gains.
  • Long-Term: Single-segment vulnerability resolved via diversification, but unproven; potential in tech/analytics (company name) if pivots succeed.

Key Risks

  1. Going Concern (Highest Risk): Auditors flag “material uncertainty” – huge FY25 loss, net worth erosion, no ops, litigations, dues. No adjustments made despite doubts; potential asset write-downs/liability realizations could wipe equity.
  2. Tax & Litigation Uncertainty: ₹88-119 Mn tax assets + ₹52-76 Mn indirect credits at risk; contingent liabilities ₹108 Mn standalone/₹721 Mn cons. (direct tax disputes up to FY21). Outcome unascertainable.
  3. Execution Risk on Pivot: Diversification/M&A “under discussion”; non-cash deals may dilute/fail amid weak fundamentals.
  4. Liquidity/Insolvency: Near-zero cash; negative CFO; statutory dues could trigger defaults.
  5. Governance/Audit Risks: Auditor resignation; unconfirmed liabilities; Navitas LLP write-backs (₹46.53 Mn FY25) lacked evidence.
  6. Market/Stock Risks: Low liquidity/participation; promoter holding ~19.3% (28.5 Cr shares); potential delisting/suspension if non-compliant.

Growth depends on unproven M&A (FY26 catalyst), but going concern doubts dominate. Monitor Q3 results, auditor appointment, and M&A updates.
