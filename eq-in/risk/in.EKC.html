Analysis of Everest Kanto Cylinder Limited (EKC) – Headwinds, Tailwinds, Growth Prospects, and Key Risks

EKC, a leading manufacturer of seamless high-pressure gas cylinders (CNG, industrial, hydrogen), reported steady Q2 FY26 results (ended Sep 30, 2025): Consolidated revenue ₹360 Cr (flat YoY), EBITDA ₹43 Cr (11.9% margin, down YoY), PAT ₹14 Cr (impacted by ₹11.3 Cr exceptional SEZ penalty). H1 FY26 revenue up 5% YoY to ₹747 Cr, EBITDA up 10% to ₹104 Cr. Standalone utilization at 70% with healthy order book (~₹1,000 Cr, 1-year executable). Management confident on 12-14% EBITDA margins and FY26 standalone revenue of ₹900-1,000 Cr.

Headwinds (Short-term Pressures)

  • One-time Penalty Hit: ₹11.29 Cr penalty from Kandla SEZ for NFE shortfall (2020-25 block under SEZ Rules); impacted Q2 PAT sharply (to 3.8% margin). Next assessment in 5 years; company seeking SEZ exit/amendments.
  • CNG Volume Softness: Temporary dip in India due to GST transition in automotive end-users; led to lower dispatches and margin compression (11.9% vs. 14.5% YoY).
  • US Operations Volatility: Order-driven nature caused Q2 dispatch dip; higher opex from team/capability build-up squeezed margins (despite H1 growth of 10%).
  • Margin Moderation: Product mix shift, rising employee costs (+18% YoY), and opex up pressured EBITDA (Q2 down 19% YoY).
  • Dubai Branch Closure: Non-operational EKC Industries Dubai shut down; UAE showed early recovery but order book was weak historically.
  • GST Demands: Industry-wide notices (strong case per mgmt.); High Court case ongoing, no clear timeline.

Tailwinds (Supportive Factors)

  • Demand Normalization: CNG volumes recovering post-GST; underlying India demand strong. Industrial segment steady.
  • Robust Order Visibility: US book at $80M (12-18 months execution); total ~₹1,000 Cr (pan ops, 1-year horizon). Defence orders secured.
  • Geographic Resilience: India (65% revenue) up 8% H1 YoY; US H1 up 10%; UAE +4.5% Q2 YoY with improving book/margins (tax-free).
  • Utilization Headroom: Standalone at 70%; scope to 90% without capex.
  • Low Leverage: Debt/Equity ~0.1x; Debt/EBITDA ~0.2x; interest coverage healthy (~15x).
  • Cost Efficiencies: COGS down 5-10% YoY; EBITDA margins improved H1 (standalone +500 bps YoY).

Growth Prospects (Medium-Term Outlook)

  • Capacity Expansion: Mundra (India, ₹160 Cr total capex, commercial by Mar 2026; +₹200 Cr revenue potential) and Egypt (₹126 Cr total, trial by Jan 2026; +₹100 Cr). To boost output to serve CNG/industrial export/domestic.
  • CNG Leadership: Key driver (India push); complementary to hydrogen/EV transition.
  • Industrial Diversification: New avenues in semiconductors, solar, defence; hydrogen projects (IOCL/HPCL) early-stage but ecosystem build-up supportive.
  • International Scale: US H2 visibility strong; UAE turnaround; total capacity ~1.5M cylinders/year.
  • Guidance & Visibility: FY26 standalone revenue ₹900-1,000 Cr; EBITDA 12-14%; hydrogen as “next big fuel” (high-pressure storage fit).
  • Market Tailwinds: Clean energy shift (CNG/H2), industrial gas demand (aerospace/medical/fire safety).
