OSWALPUMPS (Scrip Code: 544418) – Investment Analysis Report

Based on Q2 FY26 Earnings Call (November 14, 2025) & Press Release (November 13, 2025)

1. Tailwinds Driving Growth

1.1 Strong Government-Driven Demand

  • PM-KUSUM (Phase I): A major catalyst for revenue, with over 80,000 systems executed cumulatively by October 2025.
  • State-Level Schemes: States like Maharashtra (Magel Tyala), Haryana, MP, Karnataka, and Goa are launching localized solar pump tenders. These programs are running parallel to PM-KUSUM, ensuring continued demand even if national schemes face delays.
  • PM-KUSUM 2.0 Anticipated: Expected by March 2026, with government intentions to scale installations to 1 million pumps/year. Oswal’s deep execution experience positions it strongly.

1.2 Robust Order Book & Pipeline

  • Order Book: Exceeds 18,800 pumps.
  • Near-Term Pipeline: Over 30,000 pumps across key states.
  • This provides strong visibility into H2 FY26, supporting the revenue guidance of 50–60% YoY growth.

1.3 Integrated & Backward-Integrated Business Model

  • Vertically Integrated: Manufactures pumps, motors, solar modules, controllers, structures, BOS, cables in-house.
  • Key Benefits:
    • Cost control and supply chain resilience.
    • Strong value engineering capabilities mitigate pricing pressure.
    • Protects margin profile (25.5% H1 FY26 EBITDA margin) vs. peers.
    • Owns Oswal Solar Structure Private Limited, enhancing scalability.

1.4 Capital Allocation Efficiency & IPO Fund Utilization

  • Of IPO proceeds of ₹842 crores, ₹478 crores deployed toward:
    • Debt reduction
    • Capacity expansion
  • Company remains debt-free, funding growth organically.

1.5 Capacity Expansion & Asset Optimization

  • Relocating solar module expansion project to adjacent land:
    • 28,000 sq m vs. earlier 13,983 sq m → allows better layout, storage, scalability.
    • Operational Synergies: Shared R&D, lab, admin, electricity, workforce.
    • Faster Execution: Only requires amendments to existing approvals → no new regulatory hurdles.
  • First phase already underway; expected to boost EBITDA by 100 bps by Q4 FY26.

1.6 Broader Market Presence

  • Presence in 20+ states including remote ones (Ladakh, Sikkim, Assam), building long-term geographic resilience.
  • Expanding focus on export markets and domestic non-subsidized (private) networks — a growth avenue for FY27+.
2. Headwinds & Margin Pressure

2.1 Pricing Pressure in Tenders

  • Tender rates down ~7.5% YoY in PM-KUSUM/Magel Tyala due to:
    • Increased competition from new EPC players (especially regional SMEs).
    • Aggressive bidding by price-sensitive entrants.
  • Impacted over 80% of core revenue, lowering EBITDA margins QoQ.

2.2 One-Time Margin Compression in Q2 FY26

  • Operating EBITDA margin: 23.7% (vs. ~30% earlier), down 368 bps QoQ.
  • Contributing factors:
    • ₹40 crore (~₹400 million) in low-margin module-only sales (vs. full solar combos).
    • ₹2.5 crore (~₹25 million) one-time cost for subsidiary capital increase.
  • These are non-recurring, and management expects recovery in Q3 FY26.

2.3 Extended Receivable Cycles

  • Receivable days: 138 days (from 126 days in Q1) due to:
    • RMS (Remote Monitoring System) mechanism: Payments released only after 90 days of pump operation.
    • Monsoon extended → lower pump usage → delayed RMS validation.
    • Temporary bank disbursement delays in Magel Tyala.
  • Expected improvement in Q3 as monsoon ends and payments normalize.

5. Key Risks & Concerns

5.1 Policy & Scheme Delay Risk

  • PM-KUSUM 2.0 is critical for 1M pump vision. Any delay could slow volume growth.
  • However, state-level schemes reduce near-term dependency on central program timing.

5.2 Competitive Intensity

  • Rise of EPC-only players who bid low to win tenders.
  • Risk: Price wars leading to margin erosion, even if not sustainable.
  • Mitigation: Oswal’s cost advantages and backward integration limit vulnerability.

5.3 Raw Material Inflation

  • Input costs rising, but value engineering and in-house production buffer impact.
  • However, sustained price hikes could squeeze margins.

5.4 Government Payment Risk

  • RMS-linked disbursement delays increased receivables.
  • A systemic risk under PM-KUSUM; likely to remain but improves post-monsoon.

5.5 Execution & Scalability Risk

  • Rapid scaling from 2 lakh to 5 lakh pump capacity.
  • Requires flawless execution in supply chain, labor, logistics.
  • Company claims strong readiness, but execution risk remains during aggressive expansion.

7. Summary: Investment Rationale

✅ Buy Case (Bullish View)

  • Strong execution in high-growth solar pump market.
  • Backward integration provides cost edge and margin sustainability.
  • Visible order pipeline supports 50–60% revenue growth in FY26.
  • Debt-free balance sheet with disciplined capex.
  • New solar module plant will further improve efficiency and margin by 2026–27.

⚠️ Cautions (Risks to Monitor)

  • Margin pressure from aggressive bidding.
  • Heavy reliance on government subsidies (~70–75% of revenue from solar).
  • PM-KUSUM 2.0 launch timing is pivotal for medium-term volumes.
  • Increasing competition from low-cost EPC players.
Final Verdict: Favorable Long-Term Outlook with Near-Term Volatility

Oswal Pumps Limited is well-positioned to capitalize on India’s clean energy shift, driven by favorable policy tailwinds and a unique vertically integrated model. While Q2 FY26 saw margin pressure due to tender price cuts and one-time items, the core business remains resilient.

Grade: Strong Buy (Long-Term Horizon)

  • Target EBITDA Margin: 26–27% by Q4 FY26.
  • Target Revenue: ₹2,200 Crores (~55% growth).
  • Expected Value Creation: Solar module expansion, private market growth, and PM-KUSUM 2.0 rollout.

Investors should view short-term margin dip as temporary, with recovery expected and fundamentals intact. First-mover advantage, operational excellence, and strategic asset optimization make OSWALPUMPS a standout player in the solar pumping ecosystem.
