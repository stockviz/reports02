Trejhara Solutions Limited (TREJHARA) Analysis

Company Overview: Trejhara Solutions Limited is an IT-enabled logistics solutions provider (CIN: L72900MH2017PLC292340), listed on BSE (542233) and NSE (TREJHARA). It recently completed a merger with LP Logistics Plus Chemical SCM Pvt Ltd (effective 01/04/2024, NCLT-approved 14/10/2025), expanding into chemical logistics. Subsidiaries include Auroscient Outsourcing (India), Trejhara Pte Ltd (Singapore), and Trejhara Logistics Services LLC (Saudi Arabia). FY25 was audited; Q2/H1 FY26 (ended 30/09/2025) results show post-merger consolidation under Ind AS (pooling of interests method). Single reportable segment: digital logistics solutions.

Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts)
  • Merger Synergies Unlocked: Integration of LP Logistics boosted scale. Consolidated revenue surged 44% YoY to ₹6,657 lakhs (H1 FY26 vs. revised ₹4,639 lakhs H1 FY25); Q2 FY26 revenue ₹3,393 lakhs (+32% YoY). PAT up 73% YoY to ₹424 lakhs (H1). Standalone revenue also doubled post-merger.
  • Robust Profitability: Q2 margins strong (PBT ₹485 lakhs); forex gains (₹328 lakhs in Q2) from international ops. EPS improved to ₹1.80 (H1 FY26, basic/diluted).
  • Capital Infusion: Board approved preferential allotment of 6.11L equity shares + 79.97L warrants at ₹216 (05/11/2025), signaling growth funding.
  • Shareholder Approvals: Postal ballot (Nov 2025) passed re-appointment of Independent Director (99% favour) and RPTs (98% favour), ensuring governance continuity.
  • International Footprint: Subsidiaries contribute (e.g., one unreviewed sub with ₹582 lakhs revenue H1); forex tailwinds from SGD/SAR exposure.
Headwinds (Challenges)
  • Integration Delays: Accounting mismatches (Transferor: AS; Transferee: Ind AS) delayed AGM (ROC extension granted 11/09/2025 to Dec 2025). Pre-merger financials restated, adding complexity.
  • Forex Volatility: ~10% of Q2 income from FX gains (₹328/₹3,776 lakhs total); H1 forex ₹322 lakhs. Adverse swings could erode profits.
  • Subsidiary Reporting Gaps: One sub reviewed by other auditor (₹6,105 lakhs assets); two unreviewed/management-compiled (₹1,710 lakhs assets, not material but flags oversight).
  • Working Capital Strain: Trade receivables down but still high (₹1,891 lakhs consolidated); cash from ops ₹236 lakhs (H1), impacted by WC changes (-₹26 lakhs).
  • Pre-Merger Auditor Change: Predecessor auditor handled prior periods; current (Chokshi & Chokshi LLP) issued unmodified limited review.
Growth Prospects
  • High: Logistics digitalization tailwind in India (market >$200B, CAGR 10-12%). Merger creates unified platform for analytics/innovation in chemical SCM.
  • Revenue Trajectory: H1 FY26 at 58% of FY25 full-year (₹11,575 lakhs revised); Q2 acceleration (₹3,393 vs. Q1 ₹3,264 lakhs). Targets expansion via subsidiaries (Saudi/Singapore ops).
  • Capital & Strategic Moves: ₹1,700 Cr+ potential from preferential issue; RPT approvals enable group synergies.
  • Balance Sheet Strength: Equity ₹24,120 lakhs (90% of assets); low debt (borrowings ₹880 lakhs current). Cash ₹650 lakhs up 38% QoQ.
  • Projections: If trends hold, FY26 revenue could exceed ₹20,000-25,000 lakhs (+75-115% YoY), driven by logistics scale and forex.
