Analysis of Universal Cables Limited (UNIVCABLES)

Universal Cables Limited, an MP Birla Group company and pioneer in power cables, conductors, capacitors, and turnkey projects, reported record H1 FY26 performance (standalone revenue ₹1,414 Cr, +25% YoY; PAT ₹56 Cr, +170% YoY; consolidated PAT ₹81 Cr). Driven by strong domestic power sector demand and export growth, the company is expanding capacities amid a ₹2,945 Cr order book. Below is a structured analysis of tailwinds, headwinds, growth prospects, and key risks based on the provided announcements (Q2/H1 FY26 results, board outcomes, press release, AGM voting).

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Robust Financial Momentum: Record Q2 revenue (₹814 Cr, +28% YoY) from favorable product mix (EHV/MV/LV cables, capacitors), operational efficiencies (EBITDA margin +152 bps to 10.3%), and broad-based growth in cables/capacity business.
  • Strong Order Book & Exports: ₹2,945 Cr pending orders (incl. EHV projects). New wins: USD 38M (₹320 Cr+) Middle East EHV export (first for Indian cable firm); USD 25M Latin America turnkey. Regular exports to Europe/Australia/Africa.
  • Capacity Ramp-Up: Ongoing ₹525 Cr capex (up from ₹505 Cr) – 2/4 CCV lines commissioned; remaining by FY26/Q1FY27; new LV plant (Satna) by Dec’25; Goa LT/building wire fully operational. Enhances MV/HV/EHV/HVDC capabilities.
  • Sector Tailwinds: India’s power boom (renewables, electrification, EVs, data centers, metro expansions, transmission reforms). JV (Birla Furukawa Fibre Optics) surging on global optical fibre demand.
  • Governance: Smooth AGM (100%+ approval on key resolutions); fresh KMP authorizations; new CFO (Gopal Agarwal) appointed Nov’25.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • High Debt & Finance Costs: Borrowings up (non-current ₹409 Cr, current ₹603 Cr); H1 finance costs ₹51 Cr (stable YoY but elevated). Interest expense pressured margins.
  • Working Capital Strain: Negative operating cash flow (-₹37 Cr H1) due to receivables surge (₹1,110 Cr, +22% QoQ), inventory build-up (₹448 Cr, +13% YoY), despite payables growth.
  • Capex Funding Pressure: Heavy investments (₹102 Cr PPE capex H1) amid rising total assets (standalone ₹2,649 Cr, +21% YoY).
  • Input Volatility: Exposure to copper/aluminum/oil prices; raw material costs ~65-70% of revenue.

Growth Prospects

  • Medium-Term Revenue Upside: Full capex fruition to drive 20-30%+ topline growth (target: optimal utilization in LV/MV/HV). Order book execution + new EHV pipeline (domestic Amravati project; overseas hyperscale data centers).
  • Export Acceleration: 2-3 year horizon for sizable international growth (Middle East/Latin America entry); niche EHV/HVDC positioning.
  • Domestic Leverage: Power sector capex (DPI reforms, renewables); continuity in LV/MV orders for capacity fill.
  • EBITDA/PAT Expansion: Margins to sustain/improve via scale, efficiency; consolidated share from JV/associates (₹322 Cr profit H1).
  • Projections: FY26 outlook “steady/buoyant”; potential 25%+ revenue CAGR if exports materialize.

Key Risks

Risk Category Description Mitigation/Notes

Financial Debt servicing (total liabilities ₹1,743 Cr); forex/MTM losses (₹48 Cr unrealized H1). Treasury mgmt.; new CFO expertise in fundraising.

Regulatory SEBI compliance; unclaimed shares (9,458 frozen). High AGM approvals; KMP authorizations.

Summary

Bullish Outlook with Managed Risks: UNIVCABLES is in a high-growth phase, fueled by India’s power infra boom and export breakthroughs, with record metrics and ₹2,945 Cr order book signaling 20-30% FY26 growth. Tailwinds from capacity expansion and efficiencies outweigh headwinds like debt/working capital, but execution and input costs are watchpoints. Recommendation: Positive (Buy/Hold for growth investors); monitor Q3 debt metrics and capex milestones. FY26 PAT could exceed FY25 (₹57 Cr standalone) by 50%+ if trends hold. Market cap/re-rating potential on EHV exports.
