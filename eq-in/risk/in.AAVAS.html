AAVAS Financiers Limited (AAVAS): Analysis Summary

Company Overview: AAVAS is a retail-focused Housing Finance Company (HFC) targeting affordable housing loans for low/middle-income, self-employed customers in Tier 2-5 markets across 14 states (405 branches). H1FY26 performance shows resilient growth amid a competitive NBFC/HFC landscape, with AUM at ₹213.6 bn (+16% YoY), PAT ₹3.04 bn (+11% YoY), and pristine asset quality (GNPA 1.24%).

Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts)
  • Robust Financial Momentum: AUM growth of 16% YoY, disbursements +21% YoY in Q2FY26 (₹15.9 bn), NIM expansion to 7.81% (+20 bps YoY), spreads at 5.23% (+34 bps YoY) due to yield optimization (13.08%) and falling CoB (7.85%, -30 bps YoY). ROA 3.40% (+46 bps QoQ), ROE 14.31%.
  • Pristine Asset Quality: GNPA stable at 1.24%, NNPA 0.85%, 1+DPD 3.99% (industry-leading); lifetime write-offs ~11 bps on ₹368 bn disbursements. Credit costs low at 16 bps.
  • Strong Balance Sheet: CRAR 46.42%, Net Worth ₹46.8 bn (+16% YoY), liquidity surplus ₹42.6 bn (high-quality ₹21 bn). Diversified liabilities (35+ lenders, 50% term loans, long tenor ~126 months).
  • Tech & Operational Efficiency: Major transformation complete (Oracle Flexcube LMS, Salesforce CRM); TAT reduced 54% to 6 days, paper usage -59%, digital agreements in 223 branches. Opex/Assets 3.51%, Cost/Income 43.7% (-262 bps QoQ).
  • Strategic Positioning: In-house model (80% sourcing), contiguous expansion, focus on <₹15L tickets (84% portfolio), average LTV 55%. Good ESG rating (67/Strong), AA/Positive credit ratings.
  • Macro Support: PMAY 2.0 subsidies benefiting 2,300+ customers (₹75 mn disbursed).
Headwinds (Challenges)
  • Margin Pressures: NIM as % slightly flat YoY (7.81% vs. 7.61%); sequential opex/Assets +5 bps to 3.51% amid branch expansion (8 new in Q2).
  • Slight DPD Creep: 1+DPD +2 bps YoY to 3.99%; Stage 2 at 1.53% (up from 1.47% in Mar’25).
  • Funding Costs: Absolute borrowings up (₹144 bn), reliant on banks/NHB (69% mix); potential rate volatility.
  • Competition & Slowdown: HFC sector growth moderating; self-employed segment (61% portfolio) sensitive to economic cycles.
  • Execution in Expansion: Recent branch adds in TN; scaling tech in new geographies.
Growth Prospects
  • High Visibility: Targeting ₹500 bn AUM by FY30E (CAGR ~20% from FY25); H1FY26 disbursements ₹27.1 bn signal momentum. Sanction-to-disbursement ~80%.
  • Scalable Model: Aavas 3.0 phase emphasizes institutionalization (10+ CXOs), tech leverage, operating leverage (ROA improving). Contiguous focus in high-yield states (Rajasthan 33%, Maharashtra 20%).
  • Product Diversification: HL 67%, LAP 33%; <₹15L tickets 84%; salaried mix rising (39%).
  • Capital & Liquidity: Internal accruals funding growth; surplus liquidity to ₹49 bn by Q2FY27.
  • Tailwind from Policy: PMAY, housing push in underserved markets; 3.75L+ families served.
  • Upside Potential: ROE trajectory to 20%+ with NIM/spread stability; potential for equity raise if needed.

Overall Outlook: AAVAS is well-positioned for 18-20% AUM CAGR through FY30, driven by execution excellence and macro tailwinds. Risks are manageable with strong buffers; stock likely to benefit from sector re-rating on asset quality and growth. Monitor DPD trends and funding costs.
