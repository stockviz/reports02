Analysis of Kohinoor Foods Limited (KOHINOOR, BSE: 512559, NSE: KOHINOOR)

Kohinoor Foods Limited is a food products company (primarily rice manufacturing/trading) facing severe financial distress, with ongoing debt restructuring via One-Time Settlement (OTS), regulatory litigations, and qualified audit opinions. Below is a structured summary based on the provided filings (DRT intimation, Q2/H1 FY26 unaudited results, AGM outcomes).

Headwinds (Key Challenges)

  • Overwhelming Debt Burden: Debt Recovery Tribunal (DRT-III, Delhi) order (28 Oct 2025, TA No. 30/2022) mandates payment of ~INR 926 Cr (plus 11% interest) to banks within 30 days, recoverable via asset sales if unpaid. Company contests it; cumulative unprovisioned NPA interest ~INR 807 Cr (standalone).
  • Persistent Losses & Weak Operations: H1 FY26 revenue ~INR 64.6 Cr (down from FY25 full-year INR 86 Cr); net loss INR 2.74 Cr (Q2: INR 0.68 Cr). Negative equity (standalone: -INR 167 Cr; consolidated: -INR 526 Cr as of Sep 2025).
  • Litigation Overload: Multiple fronts—NCLT IBC petitions (not admitted, interim stay till Feb 2026), property/bank account attachments (stayed with deposits), municipal eviction notice on HQ, vendor execution petitions, tax demands (e.g., PVAT INR 4.56 Cr), SEBI show-cause notice.
  • Qualified Audit & Accounting Issues: Auditors flag non-provision of NPA interest/penalties, MSME interest, tax demands; going concern reliant on OTS success. No asset impairment despite distress.
  • Operational Shrinkage: Demonetized/sold rice unit (INR 190 Cr proceeds to lenders); now leasing operations in Gujarat—revenue remains subdued.

Tailwinds (Positive Factors)

  • OTS Progress: Revised OTS (Dec 2024) for INR 227 Cr; INR 205 Cr paid (incl. recent INR 5 Cr); seeking extension to Dec 2025 despite 2 banks’ reluctance. Lenders issued NOC for sold assets.
  • Cash Flow Resilience: H1 operating cash inflow INR 123 Cr (driven by payables surge INR 116 Cr); net debt reduction (short-term borrowings down INR 125 Cr).
  • Legal Defenses: Contesting DRT order (next hearing Dec 2025); NCLT stays; deposited INR 2.12 Cr in courts for stays; all AGM resolutions (incl. capital increase) passed overwhelmingly.
  • Asset Monetization: Rice unit sale provided liquidity to lenders; management cites “imminent buyer interest” in remaining assets.

Growth Prospects (Limited Near-Term)

  • Core Business Revival Potential: Single-segment food products (rice trading/manufacturing). Post-OTS resolution, could sustain via leased Gujarat unit and trading. FY25 had exceptional gain (INR 383 Cr, likely asset sale), boosting PAT to INR 365 Cr—but normalized ops show low growth (H1 revenue flat YoY).
  • Restructuring Upside: Successful OTS extension + asset sale could deleverage balance sheet (current borrowings ~INR 77 Cr long-term + INR 77 Cr short-term consolidated). Capital increase (AGM-approved) signals fundraising intent.
  • Outlook: Modest if debt settles (targeting going concern); however, revenue trajectory (Q2: INR 48 Cr) suggests <10% CAGR without major revival. Long-term: Brand legacy (“Kohinoor” rice), but execution risks high.

Key Risks (High Overall)

  1. Debt Enforcement (Highest): DRT recovery (Dec 2025 hearing); asset liquidation if OTS fails—could wipe out operations.
  2. Insolvency Trigger: NCLT admission (next: Nov 2025) leads to CIRP; IDBI petition pending.
  3. Liquidity Crunch: Cash ~INR 1.6 Cr; reliant on payables/OTS. Failed extension risks default.
  4. Regulatory/Compliance: SEBI penalties, tax appeals, court attachments; potential trading suspension (negative equity, qualifiers).
  5. Going Concern: Auditor emphasis; unprovisioned liabilities could balloon losses >INR 900 Cr.
  6. Operational: Low inventory/receivables turnover; HQ eviction risk; NPA status blocks fresh funding.

Overall Assessment: High-Risk Distressed Play. Tailwinds hinge on OTS/buyer success (management’s “going concern” bet), but headwinds dominate—debt overhang, losses, and litigations signal potential value erosion. Stock likely volatile; monitor DRT/NCLT hearings (Nov-Dec 2025). Not suitable for conservative investors; speculative for turnaround believers. Market Cap implied low given equity erosion.
