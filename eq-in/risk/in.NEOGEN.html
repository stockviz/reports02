Neogen Chemicals Limited (NEOGEN) Analysis

Based on Q2 FY26 Earnings (Sep 30, 2025), Announcements, and AGM Outcomes

Neogen Chemicals, a specialty chemicals player focused on bromine/lithium-based products and expanding into LIB materials, showed revenue resilience (+8% YoY consolidated to ₹209 Cr) amid challenges like the Dahej fire. EBITDA/PAT declined due to costs/debt, but expansions signal long-term growth. Below is a structured summary:

Headwinds (Short-term Pressures)

  • Fire Incident Impact (Dahej SEZ Plant, Mar 2025): Net loss of ₹14 Cr (consolidated) recognized; partial insurance payout (₹81 Cr received, ₹268 Cr receivable pending). Led to outsourcing, capacity outage, and higher insurance premiums/job work costs.
  • Cost Inflation: EBITDA margins down 349 bps to 14.4% from higher employee costs (performance incentives, hires), finance costs (+53% YoY to ₹19.5 Cr due to capex/debt for rebuild/expansions), and low Dahej utilization.
  • Debt Burden: Consolidated net debt ₹899 Cr (up from ₹566 Cr); interest coverage (TTM ISCR 2.37x) and DSCR (consolidated TTM 0.74x-1.99x) strained. Raised ₹200 Cr NCDs, but servicing risks noted.
  • LIB Startup Drag: Neogen Ionics (battery arm) revenue ₹5.4 Cr but losses due to ramp-up delays; consolidated PAT -69% YoY.
  • External: Geopolitical volatility, pricing pressures, seasonality (H1 weaker).

Tailwinds (Supportive Factors)

  • Revenue Resilience: +8% YoY despite fire; volumes up in core organic/inorganic (88% domestic, 12% exports). Gross margins steady/improved (+16% YoY).
  • Diversified Portfolio: 258 products across pharma/agro/EV; R&D (116 team, 11 PhDs) drives innovation/custom synthesis.
  • Insurance/ Funding Support: Partial claims received; NCD raise provides liquidity for rebuild/growth.
  • Governance: Separated Chairman/MD roles; new appointments (e.g., Anurag Surana as Non-Exec Chairman).
  • Seasonality: H2 stronger (Europe/HVAC/agro demand peaks Oct-Mar).

Growth Prospects (Medium-Long Term)

  • LIB Battery Materials (High Potential): Early mover in non-FEOC electrolytes/salts/additives. Capacities: Dahej (2kt electrolyte commissioned; 400-2.1kt salts by FY27); Pakhajan greenfield (30kt electrolyte/3kt salts by FY27). JV with Morita (80:20) for LiPF6. Peak revenue ₹2,500-2,950 Cr by FY29 (Capex ₹1,500 Cr). Customer PPAP approvals (Indian giga-factory, intl. players); aligns with PLI/50 GWh ACC push.
  • Core Recovery: Dahej organic rebuild on track (FY26 end); base business volumes intact.
  • Historical CAGR: Revenue 21% (FY20-25); net worth ₹789 Cr.
  • Market Tailwinds: India EV/ACC ramp (Ola 5.9 GWh by FY26); global non-China demand (3kt electrolyte/358kt salts by 2030).

Overall Outlook: Near-term muted (H1 EBITDA/PAT pressure), but strong positioning in high-growth LIB (20-30% India CAGR). Target H2 recovery post-rebuild; monitor debt/claims. Buy/hold for long-term EV exposure (target upside 20-30% if expansions deliver).
