Apollo Micro Systems Limited (AMS) Analysis

Company Overview: AMS is a defence & aerospace player specializing in electromechanical systems, electronics, and allied services. FY25 consolidated revenue: ~₹562 Cr. Strong Q2/H1 FY26 results show revenue/PAT growth amid India’s defence indigenization push (Atmanirbhar Bharat). Recent developments include warrant conversions (capital infusion), IDL Explosives acquisition (₹107 Cr paid, pending transfer), and MoU with IIT-Chennai/Indian Navy.

Tailwinds
  • Defence Sector Boom: India’s ~₹6.2 Lakh Cr defence budget (FY26 est.) and 75% indigenization target favor AMS. Revenue surged 42% YoY in H1 FY26 (standalone: ₹358 Cr vs. ₹252 Cr), driven by orders in EW, guidance, armaments.
  • Capital Infusion: Multiple warrant conversions (e.g., 28.9L shares in Dec’25 for ₹24.7 Cr; prior 21L shares). Pending 3.6 Cr warrants provide dry powder for growth. Paid-up capital now ~₹357 Cr.
  • Strategic Partnerships: Tripartite MoU (Nov’25) with IIT-Chennai & Navy (DGNAI) for R&D in high-tech areas (EW, precision guidance, high-energy weapons). Extensible to Army/Airforce/Space. Enhances IP, tech validation.
  • Diversification: Entry into explosives via IDL Explosives (probable step-down sub). Subsidiaries (Apollo Defence, Ananya SIP RF) contribute; consolidated results reflect group strength.
Headwinds
  • Working Capital Intensity: Inventories up 17% QoQ (₹705 Cr standalone), receivables doubled YoY (₹360 Cr). H1 operating cash flow negative (₹-18 Cr standalone) due to WC buildup (₹-10 Cr inventories, ₹-12 Cr receivables).
  • Dilution from Warrants: Ongoing conversions (total 3.8 Cr warrants allotted Jun’25) expand equity base rapidly, pressuring EPS (diluted EPS H1: ₹1.23 vs. basic ₹1.62).
  • Capex Burden: CWIP ₹109 Cr (consol.), PPE up 13% YoY. Investing cash outflow ₹-216 Cr H1 (standalone), funded by financing (₹419 Cr from shares/warrants).
  • Debt Rise: Borrowings up (non-current ₹97 Cr standalone; current ₹209 Cr). Finance costs H1: ₹20 Cr.
