Analysis of United Nilgiri Tea Estates Company Limited (UNITEDTEA)

Overview: UNITEDTEA, a tea plantation and property company (part of Amalgamations Group), reported unaudited H1 FY26 results (ended 30 Sep 2025). Despite a ~9.7% YoY revenue decline to ₹432.8 Cr (driven by Plantation segment weakness), PAT surged 27.5% YoY to ₹129.6 Cr, fueled by cost efficiencies, lower material costs, and robust investment income (₹56.6 Cr, +21% YoY). Balance sheet remains strong with low debt (total liabilities ~5.6% of assets), rising investments (₹15,426 Cr, +13% YoY), and equity at ₹23,232 Cr. Operations generated ₹60 Cr operating cash flow (vs. ₹42.5 Cr prior), though cash dipped to ₹363 Cr post-capex/dividends. Tea seasonality and rainfall dependency noted as key qualifiers.

Tailwinds (Positive Factors)

  • Cost Discipline & Margin Expansion: Total expenses fell 18% YoY to ₹338.7 Cr despite revenue drop; gross margins improved via lower material costs (down 20%) and inventory gains. Plantation segment EBITDA resilient at ~24% of sales.
  • Diversified Income Streams: Other income (₹566 Cr, 13% of total) from investments (mutual funds, deposits) up 21% YoY, offsetting core revenue weakness. Property segment stable (~7.5% of revenue, high margins ~67%).
  • Pristine Balance Sheet: Net worth up 5% to ₹23,232 Cr; investments yield steady returns (fair value gains noted). Low leverage (debt/equity <0.01), high liquidity (₹623 Cr in bank balances).
  • Cash Generation: Op. cash flow doubled YoY; supports capex (₹244 Cr in H1) and dividends (₹100 Cr paid).

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Core Revenue Pressure: Plantation revenue (92% of ops) down 10.6% YoY to ₹400 Cr; Q2 alone -17% vs. prior Q2, signaling weak tea prices/volumes amid seasonality and rainfall variability.
  • Quarter-on-Quarter Softness: Q2 revenue -4.6% (₹211 Cr) and PBT -36% (₹589 Cr), with higher opex (employee costs up 20% QoQ).
  • Inventory & Receivables Build: Inventories +62% QoY (₹600 Cr), trade receivables +46% (₹100 Cr), tying up working capital (~₹56 Cr op. cash drag from changes).
