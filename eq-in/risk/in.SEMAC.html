Semac Construction Limited (SEMAC) Analysis

Company Overview: SEMAC (formerly Semac Consultants Ltd., CIN: L42900TZ1977PLC000780) is a listed EPC (Engineering, Procurement, Construction) services player focused on infrastructure/construction. Listed on BSE (505368) and NSE (SEMAC). Q2/H1 FY26 (ended Sep 30, 2025) results show strong revenue turnaround amid infrastructure tailwinds. Recent events include full ownership of subsidiary Semac Construction Technologies India Pvt Ltd (SCTIL) and pending NCLT approval for SCTIL merger.

Tailwinds (Positive Momentum)

  • Revenue Surge & Profitability Turnaround: Standalone H1 revenue doubled YoY to ₹10,007 lakhs (Q2: ₹5,283 lakhs). Total income up 122% YoY. PBT/PAT flipped to profit (H1 PBT: ₹127 lakhs vs. ₹477 lakhs loss; PAT: ₹83 lakhs vs. ₹598 lakhs loss). EPS improved to ₹2.65 (vs. -₹19.19). Cost efficiencies in other expenses and better margins signal operational leverage.
  • Strategic Expansion: Acquired remaining 50% stake in SCTIL (now 100% subsidiary) on Jun 26, 2025, adding scale. Pending amalgamation (filed Jul 30, 2025) under Ind AS 103 could unlock synergies (no accounting impact yet).
  • Subsidiary Contributions: Consolidated results include 100% SCTIL (India) and 65% Semac and Partner LLC (Oman). Oman sub contributed ₹447/₹881 lakhs revenue in Q2/H1 with profits (₹27/₹37 lakhs PAT).
  • Shareholder Alignment: 48th AGM (Sep 12, 2025) saw near-unanimous approvals (99%+ assent) for financials, auditors, related-party deals (REIL, RCSL), and MD reappointment.
  • Sector Boom: India’s infra capex (₹11+ lakh cr budget FY26) favors EPC players.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Working Capital Strain: Trade receivables up sharply (Standalone: ₹2,527 lakhs vs. ₹3,782 lakhs prior; Consolidated: ₹3,672 lakhs). Payables dropped (₹692 lakhs decrease), leading to negative CFO (-₹34 lakhs Standalone; -₹217 lakhs Consolidated). Inventory up ₹132 lakhs.
  • High Debt/Finance Costs: Finance costs H1: ₹265 lakhs (Standalone), up from prior. Short-term borrowings repaid ₹838 lakhs via related-party funding.
  • Historical Losses: FY25 full-year PAT loss ₹612 lakhs; ongoing OCI drag from remeasurements.
  • Related-Party Dependence: Material RPTs approved with REIL/RCSL (₹42k shares voted 97% in favor, promoters recused). Raises governance flags.
  • Cash Burn: Cash equivalents down (Standalone: ₹37 lakhs; Consolidated: ₹595 lakhs). Heavy FD investments (₹654/₹672 lakhs) tie up liquidity.
