NHPC Limited (NHPC.NS) Analysis: Headwinds, Tailwinds, Growth Prospects, and Key Risks

NHPC, India’s leading hydroelectric power PSU (Navratna status), released Q2/H1 FY26 (ended Sep 30, 2025) unaudited results showing robust YoY growth amid capacity additions. Standalone revenue grew 15% YoY to ₹5,710 Cr (H1), PAT (incl. Regulatory Deferral Account) up 4% to ₹1,997 Cr (EPS ₹1.99). Consolidated revenue +14% YoY to ₹6,579 Cr, PAT up 9% to ₹2,350 Cr (EPS ₹2.08). Balance sheet expanded (assets +7% YoY standalone), cash from ops +ve (₹1,233 Cr), healthy ratios (DSCR 2.57; debt-equity 1.04), no defaults/deviations on ₹2,000 Cr NCD raise. Key highlights: Parbati-II (800 MW) & partial Karnisar Solar (214 MW) commissioned; tariff petitions filed.

Tailwinds (Positive Momentum)

  • Strong Financial Performance: Revenue/PAT growth driven by capacity ramps (unbilled sales ₹913 Cr H1 from new projects). Operating margins stable ~43%; net margins ~35%.
  • Capacity Additions & Revenue Recognition: Parbati-II (Apr 2025) & Karnisar Solar boosting topline; CERC tariff regs (2024-29) enable provisional AFC billing.
  • Liquidity & Debt Management: Positive ops cash flow; DSCR/ISCR >2x/5x; 100%+ security cover on NCDs; MAT credit ₹429 Cr recognized.
  • PSU Stability: Govt backing, no defaults (total debt ₹41,474 Cr), healthy ratios (current ratio 1.06).
  • Investor Engagement: Participation in 3 Mumbai conferences (Nov 17-19, 2025).

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Seasonality & QoQ Dip: Hydro-monsoon dependent; Q2 revenue -8% QoQ (₹2,732 Cr); employee costs up (H1 ₹682 Cr).
  • High Capex Burden: CWIP ₹30,149 Cr standalone (32% of assets); PPE jumped to ₹29,790 Cr (post-mergers).
  • Debt & Costs: Debt up 9% QoQ; finance costs ₹522 Cr H1; depreciation doubled YoY post-commissions.
  • Restatements: Figures restated for Lanco Teesta merger; prior YoY impacted.
  • Trade Receivables: Up sharply (₹6,080 Cr current), signaling delayed payments.

Growth Prospects

  • Capacity Pipeline: High CWIP signals 5-10 GW+ hydro/renewable additions; solar ramps (Karnisar full by Oct 2025); renewables focus aligns with India’s green push.
  • Tariff Upside: Pending CERC approvals for 2019-24 truing-up & 2024-29 tariffs; regulatory deferral gains ₹274 Cr H1.
  • M&A/Subsidiaries: JPCL merger progressing (2nd motion filed Oct 2025); consolidated subs (e.g., NHDC 51%) contribute 10-15% revenue.
  • Renewables & Diversification: Solar/wind pilots; single-segment hydro but trading/consultancy add-ons.
  • FY26 Outlook: 10-15% revenue growth implied; MAT utilization signals sustained profits. Long-term: Hydro dominance (7 GW+ installed) + green energy tailwinds.

Key Risks

Risk Category Description Mitigation
Regulatory Tariff delays/rejections (₹913 Cr unbilled provisional); CERC petitions pending. Filed proactively; historical approvals favorable.
Operational/Hydro Water scarcity, monsoons, climate risks; execution delays (CWIP ₹43 Bn cons.). Diversifying to solar; govt project support.
Financial Debt ₹46,940 Cr cons. (DE 1.14); rate hikes; forex (hedge reserves noted). Strong coverage ratios; govt-guaranteed bonds.
Execution/Legal Project delays (Subansiri, Ratle subs); JPCL merger NCLT risks. 50+ yrs track record; MCA hearings advanced.
Market Power demand volatility; competition from IPPs/solar. PSU monopoly in hydro; long-term PPAs.

Overall Summary: NHPC’s FY26 off to strong start with capacity-led growth outweighing seasonal dips. Tailwinds from commissions & tariffs support 12-15% CAGR medium-term, but capex/debt headwinds need monitoring. Low-risk PSU profile (no defaults, healthy ratios); Buy/Hold for infra/green energy exposure. Risks tilted regulatory/execution, but govt ownership buffers. Target upside on tariff awards & FY26 beats.
