Summary Analysis: Tirupati Forge Limited (TIRUPATIFL)

Tirupati Forge Limited, a forging and machining company serving sectors like oil & gas, automotive, power, mining, aerospace, and now defence, reported Q2FY26 (Sep 2025) results showing revenue growth but margin compression. H1FY26 revenue rose 17% YoY to ₹719.7 Cr, but PAT fell 48% YoY to ₹27.5 Cr due to higher depreciation, finance costs, and employee expenses tied to expansions. Balance sheet expanded (assets up 14% to ₹1,667 Cr), funded by equity/warrants and capex (PPE up 65% to ₹474 Cr). Cash flow turned negative on ops (-₹28.6 Cr) amid heavy investing (-₹185 Cr). Stock trades on NSE; focus is on defence pivot amid forging market growth.

Tailwinds

  • Revenue Momentum: Q2 revenue up 23% QoQ/YoY to ₹397 Cr (H1 +17% YoY), driven by higher US tariffs/export volumes (55% revenue overseas: US/Canada/Europe).
  • Defence Foray: New fully automated 1.2 lakh TPA shell body plant (155mm M107 focus) on track—civil complete Dec 2025, prod Q1FY27, full capacity H2FY27. Strong inbound demand; expansion planned FY27. Aligns with India’s defence push (budget ₹6.8 Lk Cr, 75% domestic procurement).
  • Solar Synergy: Captive solar plant to achieve full utilization post-defence ramp-up, slashing energy costs.
  • Fundraises & Capacity: ₹190 Cr warrants + ₹467 Cr equity (Jan 2025) funding expansions; installed capacity 15k TPA forging + new lines (Lasco Press, ring rolling).
  • Market Tailwinds: India forging CAGR 9% (to ₹95 bn by 2030); defence/ammo CAGR 13%/4.9%; “Make in India” boosts indigenization.

Headwinds

  • Margin Squeeze: EBITDA flat QoQ (₹43 Cr), PAT down 5% QoQ/49% YoY (Q2 ₹13.4 Cr) due to elevated dep (up 11% QoQ), finance (up 9% YoY from debt/solar), employee costs (hiring for defence), and US 50% duties.
  • Cash Burn: Ops cash flow -₹28.6 Cr (vs +₹58 Cr prior FY); inventories/receivables up 14%/26% (working capital strain).
  • Cost Pressures: Other expenses up 35% QoQ (scaling pains); forex volatility in other income.
  • Muted Quarter: Temporary profitability hit from capex ramp-up before revenue inflection.

Growth Prospects

  • Near-Term (FY26-27): Defence commercial ops Q1FY27 to drive topline (among few 100% automated Indian players); solar savings + full plant utilization to boost margins 200-300 bps. H1FY27 topline expected to reflect pivot.
  • Medium-Term (FY27+): Defence expansion; forging demand from auto/infra (global CAGR 7%); diversified portfolio (flanges 34%, rings 30%, gears 16%). Target: Defence as key growth engine amid ₹130 bn India military spend (5 yrs).
  • Catalysts: Order inflows, capacity utilization >80%, export recovery post-duties. Projections: Revenue CAGR 25-30% feasible with defence scaling.

Key Risks

Risk Category Description Mitigation
Execution Delays in defence plant (machinery Jan 2026); trials Q4FY26. On-track progress; specialized hiring.
Financial Debt up (total borrowings ₹17.7 Cr); negative FCF; overdue FDs (₹1.2 Cr provisioned, legal action). Equity-funded capex; no deviations in fund use.
Market/External US duties/tariffs (key market); forex/geopolitical risks; competition in forging/defence. 45% domestic revenue; certifications (IATF/ISO).
Operational WC bloat (receivables ₹216 Cr); employee cost inflation. Defence ramp-up to improve efficiency.
Regulatory Ind AS compliance; SEBI disclosures (no investor complaints). Limited review clean; audit committee oversight.

Overall Outlook: Positive with caveats. Strategic defence/solar pivot positions for multi-year growth (inflection FY27), outweighing near-term headwinds. Valuation could re-rate on execution; monitor Q3FY26 for plant updates. Buy on dips for long-term investors; risks tilted to timing. (Data as of Nov 11, 2025 filing.)
