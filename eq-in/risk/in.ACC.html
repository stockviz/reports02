Summary Analysis for ACC Limited (Adani Cement)

ACC Limited, a subsidiary of Ambuja Cements (consolidated under Adani Cement), demonstrates robust operational and financial momentum in Q2/H1 FY26, driven by volume growth, cost efficiencies, and expansion plans. Below is a structured analysis of headwinds, tailwinds, growth prospects, and key risks based on the provided filings (financial results, investor presentation, tax intimations, regulatory updates, and media releases).

Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts)

  • Strong Financial Performance: Q2 FY26 standalone revenue ₹5,817 Cr (+28% YoY), PAT ₹1,112 Cr (driven by ₹671 Cr tax reversal). Consolidated (Ambuja+ACC) cement volume 16.6 MnT (+20% YoY, 5x industry avg.), EBITDA ₹1,060/PMT (+32% YoY), PAT ₹2,302 Cr (+364% YoY). H1 EBITDA margin 19.1% (+410 bps YoY). Existing assets (Ambuja+ACC) delivered ₹1,189/PMT EBITDA.
  • Cost Leadership: Total cost down 5% YoY; power & fuel -5% YoY, freight -7% QoQ. Targeting ₹3,650/PMT by FY28 via green power (32.9% share, targeting 60%), AFR maximization (32%), and Adani synergies (coal, logistics).
  • Sustainability Edge: First Indian cement firm to adopt TNFD (disclosures from FY26); SBTi-validated net-zero targets; 673 MW RE (target 1,122 MW FY27); water positive (29.6x); 7.1 Mn trees planted. Premium products (35% trade sales) boost realizations (+3% YoY).
  • Adani Ecosystem: Logistics (CONCOR MoU, 7 vessels ordered, sea share to 5%); group power/fly ash supply; market share +1 pp to 16.6%.
  • Debt-Free Balance Sheet: Net worth ₹69,493 Cr; Crisil AAA/A1+ rating; cash ₹1,813 Cr.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Demand Volatility: Industry Q2 growth -4% due to early monsoon; rural/infra rebound post-monsoon uncertain.
  • Tax/Regulatory Burdens: Penalties of ₹14.22 Cr (AY15-16) + ₹8.86 Cr (AY18-19) on disallowances (to be appealed); historical excise refunds (₹637 Cr received) offset some impact.
  • Margin Pressures: Raw material/power costs elevated (clinker production +43% led to inventory buildup); depreciation up on new assets (Orient/Penna).
  • Integration Costs: Recent acquisitions (Orient, Penna, Sanghi) add opex/depreciation; EBITDA/PMT for Ambuja standalone dipped 14% YoY.
  • Reclassification Impacts: Minor regrouping of sales promotion/trade payables (no P&L effect).

Growth Prospects

  • Capacity Ramp-Up: Current 107 MTPA → 118 MTPA by FY26 → 155 MTPA FY28 (+15 MTPA debottlenecking at $48/MT capex). Key projects: Bhatapara (4 MTPA trial started), 9 MTPA GUs in Q3/Q4 FY26.
  • Demand Tailwinds: Cement demand +7-8% FY26 (housing 6-7%, infra 7.5-8.5%); policy boosts (GST 18%, coal cess removal, PMAY-2, ₹135 Bn infra spend, high-speed rail).
  • Revenue Levers: Premiumization (13 blenders for higher realizations); logistics (rail/sea shift); CINOC (AI ops center); RBD focus. FY28 EBITDA target ₹1,500/PMT.
  • M&A/Consolidation: Penna/Orient fully integrated; Adani Cementation merger by FY26 end.
  • Long-Term: 10% of India capacity; backed by Adani infra (ports, power, roads).

Key Risks

  • Litigation/Regulatory: CCI penalties (₹1,148 Cr + ₹35 Cr; SC/NCLAT hearings Nov 2025; no provision); limestone royalty dispute (Wadi mine; HC pending); tax appeals (penalties contested).
  • Execution Risks: Capex intensity (₹10,473 Cr CWIP); delays in 12.6 MTPA FY26 additions; integration of new assets.
  • Macro/Commodity: Fuel/raw material volatility; monsoon/inflation impacts; competition in consolidating sector (top-5 share ~55% FY26).
  • Environmental/Compliance: TNFD/SBTi adoption adds disclosure burden; biodiversity/mining risks.
  • Financial: Working capital strain (trade receivables up); forex/interest rate sensitivity (minimal debt).

Overall Outlook: ACC benefits from Adani’s scale, cost discipline, and green push amid India’s infra boom (6.8% GDP FY26). Q2 PAT surge (tax reversal) masks underlying strength (volumes/EBITDA growth). Risks are litigation-heavy but manageable (strong legal stance). Target upside from capacity/demand; monitor Q3 monsoon recovery and expansions. Debt-free status supports growth. Recommendation: Positive; watch regulatory outcomes.
