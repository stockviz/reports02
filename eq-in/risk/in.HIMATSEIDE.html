Analysis of Himatsingka Seide Limited (HIMATSEIDE) – Q2 FY26 Overview

Summary: Himatsingka Seide Limited, an integrated textiles player focused on home textiles (bedding, bath, drapery, yarn), reported range-bound operations in Q2 FY26 amid US tariff headwinds. Consolidated revenue declined 9.3% YoY to ₹629 Cr due to US market pressures (60-65% exposure), but EBITDA margins expanded to 25% (vs. 20.6% YoY) driven by forex gains (₹66 Cr unrealized). PAT grew 94% YoY to ₹42 Cr. Net debt deleveraged to ₹2,436 Cr (-9% YoY). India business showed resilience with YoY growth via brand expansion. Management eyes non-US diversification (<50% US revenue target), capacity ramp-up post-tariff clarity, and ₹500 Cr equity raise for further deleveraging. Outlook cautiously optimistic, contingent on tariff resolution.

Headwinds (Key Challenges)

  • US Tariff Overhang: 50% tariffs (since Aug 2025) caused ~9% revenue dip and 400 bps EBITDA margin dilution; effective blended impact ~4-5% after mitigations (price concessions, US cotton exemptions, efficiencies). No resolution yet despite media reports.
  • Subdued US Demand: Stable offtake but marginal order disruptions; higher Q3 impact possible if tariffs persist.
  • Range-Bound Operations: Capacity utilizations stagnant (Spinning: 99%, Sheeting: 60%, Terry: 66%); delayed ramp-up due to tariffs.
  • High Debt & Costs: Net debt at ₹2,436 Cr; finance costs ~₹75 Cr/quarter. Elevated trade receivables (₹1,134 Cr, up YoY) and inventories (₹1,082 Cr, +8% QoQ) signal working capital strain.
  • Margin Pressures: Operational EBITDA slipped due to pricing support for US clients; forex gains masked underlying weakness.

Tailwinds (Supportive Factors)

  • Forex Gains: ₹77.7 Cr other income (1200% YoY surge), primarily unrealized forex from INR depreciation vs. USD (₹66 Cr total, ₹8 Cr realized). Boosted PBT 148% YoY.
  • India Business Momentum: Consistent YoY revenue growth via brands (Himeya, Atmosphere, Liv); expanded in MBOs, large-format stores, e-com/quick commerce, and private labels.
  • Deleveraging Progress: Net debt down 9% YoY; strong operating cash flow (₹181 Cr H1 FY26).
  • Global Diversification: Serves 38 jurisdictions; non-US focus gaining traction.
  • EBITDA Resilience: 22% YoY growth to ₹177 Cr despite revenue dip, aided by forex and efficiencies.

Growth Prospects

  • Non-US Expansion: Target US revenue <50% in 2 years; early traction in other markets and India (reinforcing domestic portfolio).
  • Capacity Utilization Ramp: Post-tariff clarity, aim to maximize idle capacities (Sheeting/Terry); stable demand pipelines from India/non-US.
  • Balance Sheet Strengthening: ₹500 Cr equity raise planned (short-term, <12 months) via optimal instruments for deleveraging; QIP proceeds (₹400 Cr prior year) fully deployed.
  • Domestic Tailwinds: E-com/quick commerce penetration and private label growth; three brands covering diverse price points/demographics.
  • US Recovery: Stable shipments; potential pickup if tariffs ease (media hints at revision).

Investment Thesis Snapshot: Short-term tariff resolution critical for re-rating; strong India/non-US pivot and deleveraging provide medium-term upside. Monitor Q3 results for tariff updates and forex realization. FY26E revenue growth muted (~flat YoY) but margins/EBITDA could sustain if diversifications scale. Valuation likely supported by debt reduction and domestic growth.
