Avanti Feeds Limited (AVANTIFEED) Analysis

Avanti Feeds is a leading player in India’s aquaculture sector, with ~52% market share in shrimp feed, integrated operations in shrimp processing/export (via subsidiary Avanti Frozen Foods), and nascent pet care (via Avanti Pet Care). Q2/H1 FY26 results show resilience: consolidated PBT up 40%/39% YoY despite raw material inflation and US tariffs. Feed remains core (~73% revenue), processing (~25%) growing fast, pet care ramping up.

Headwinds (Challenges Pressuring Performance)

  • Rising Raw Material Costs: Key inputs (fish meal up to ₹125/kg from ₹97/kg; soya to ₹47/kg from ₹42/kg; wheat ₹32.5-33/kg) due to global supply shortages (Peru/Chile fish catches down), Indian MSP hikes, and export permissions. Compressed feed margins (Q2 PBT margin down QoQ to ~15%; FY26 expected at 9-10% vs. H1 ~16%).
  • US Reciprocal Tariffs (50%): Ongoing since Apr’25; passed on via pricing so far, but risks volume slowdown if prolonged. North America salience ~65-67%, though US-specific share declining via diversification.
  • Seasonality: Feed sales down 7% QoQ (Q1 peak season); H2 expected softer.
  • Pet Care Incubation Costs: High marketing/digital spends; sales small (₹95L Q2, up 149% QoQ) amid competition (Mars, Drools).

Tailwinds (Supportive Factors)

  • Strong Volume/Margin Execution: Feed sales +15% YoY H1; processing +46% YoY volumes, 62% YoY revenue growth (better ASP, FX gains, lower ocean freight).
  • Diversification Momentum: Shrimp exports shifting to China/EU/SE Asia/Japan/ME; focus on value-added products (higher margins). Domestic push via govt initiatives (NFDB, military supply chains).
  • Govt Support: ₹25,000 Cr Export Promotion Mechanism (EPM) for tariff-hit sectors like marine products; trade talks may ease US tariffs.
  • Financial Strength: Net cash position (₹67k Cr liquid investments); other income (₹10k Cr H1) from FDs/mutual funds/FX forwards. Dividend payout sustained.
  • Pet Care Traction: Positive early response; dog food launch (65% market), e-commerce (Amazon live Nov’25), Tier 2/3 expansion.

Growth Prospects

  • Feed: FY26 sales ~575k MT (+~10% YoY from FY25), backed by CY25 shrimp production 7-8 lakh MT (feed demand 12-13 lakh MT). Maintain 51-53% share.
  • Processing/Export: FY26 ~17k MT (+20% YoY); sustained global demand (shrimp as lean protein), value-add shift.
  • Pet Care: Trading ramp-up (cat/dog flavors); manufacturing plant (30 acres near Hyderabad) by end-FY27 for scale. Fish feed trials (Thai Union import) for diversification.
  • Overall: FY26 consolidated revenue/PBT growth >15-20% feasible if tariffs stabilize; long-term aquaculture tailwinds (global demand, sustainability focus). ROE ~20-25% sustainable.

Key Risks

Risk Category Description Mitigation
Tariff/Geo-Political Prolonged 50% US duties or ADD/CVD hikes could cut processing volumes 10-20%; speculative US Supreme Court refund unlikely. Diversification (non-US up), pricing pass-through, EPM support.
Commodity Volatility Fishmeal/soya/wheat spikes (e.g., govt procurement, exports) erode feed margins >2-3%. Hedging, cost absorption, volume leverage.
Operational Aquaculture risks (diseases, weather); pet care execution delays/competition. R&D (fish feed trials), brand building.
Macro FX volatility (though gains noted), global slowdown in shrimp demand. Forward contracts, cash buffers.
Regulatory Trade negotiations delays; subsidy mechanics pending. Govt diversification push.

Summary: Avanti’s H1 FY26 beat demonstrates operational resilience amid headwinds (RM inflation, tariffs), with tailwinds from diversification, govt aid, and pet care upside driving FY26 growth (feed +10%, processing +20%). Prospects bright for 15-20% revenue/PBT CAGR, but risks tilt to tariffs/RM prices—watch Q3 for margin trajectory. Investment Stance: Positive (Buy/Hold) for long-term aquaculture exposure, with 10-12% FY26 EPS growth potential at ~25x FY26E PE. Monitor US tariff updates and RM trends.
