Avanti Feeds Limited (AVANTIFEED) Analysis: Headwinds, Tailwinds, Growth Prospects, and Key Risks

Overview: Avanti Feeds is a leading Indian aquaculture player focused on shrimp feed (standalone core, ~75-80% revenue), shrimp processing/export (~20-25%), and nascent pet care/fish feed ventures. Q2/H1 FY26 results (ended Sept 30, 2025) show robust performance: Consolidated revenue ~INR 3,316 Cr (+13% YoY), PBT INR 476 Cr (+39% YoY), driven by volume growth in feed/processing despite US tariffs and rising raw material (RM) costs. Standalone feed PBT INR 404 Cr (+36% YoY), processing PBT INR 78 Cr (strong YoY jump). Balance sheet remains fortress-like (net cash ~INR 200+ Cr invested, low debt). Market share in shrimp feed ~51-53%. FY26 guidance: Feed sales ~575k MT, shrimp exports ~17k MT.

Headwinds (Key Pressures)
  • US Reciprocal Tariffs (50% ADD/CVD): US (largest market, ~65% FY26 H1 exposure) tariffs since Apr 2025 persist, front-loaded Q2 shipments but risk volume/margin compression in H2. North America saliency stable at ~65%, but management notes US-specific share declining via diversification. No refund likely even if US Supreme Court rules favorably (speculative, non-retrospective).
  • Rising RM Costs: Fish meal (INR 125/kg, up from 93-97; global supply tight from Peru/Chile), soya (INR 47/kg, up due to 10% production drop + MSP hike), wheat flour (INR 33/kg). Feed margins expected to revert to 9-10% (from H1 highs), as feed price hikes unlikely. Cumulative impact: Q3/Q4 profitability challenge.
  • Seasonality & Aquaculture Cycles: Feed sales peak Q1/Q2 (320k MT H1 FY26 vs. 293k H1 FY25); H2 slowdown expected. Shrimp production forecast 700-800k MT CY25 (feed demand 1.2-1.3 Mn MT).
  • Macro/Policy: Potential US-India trade friction; govt wheat/soya procurement may tighten domestic supply.
Tailwinds (Supportive Factors)
  • Demand Resilience & Diversification: Global shrimp demand steady (exports up 3-8% FY25); shifting to China (2nd largest), EU, SEA, Japan, ME. Value-added products (higher margins) emphasized. Domestic push via govt (NFDB integration into military/food chains, health protein promotion).
  • Govt Export Incentives: INR 25k Cr 5-year scheme (announced Nov 12, 2025) for tariff-hit sectors incl. marine products; details expected soon, aiding H2/FY27.
  • Operational Efficiency: Processing volumes +15-46% YoY, ASP gains, lower ocean freight/FX hedging benefits. Other income strong (INR 10 Cr H1 consolidated; FDs/mutual funds + FX forwards).
  • Pet Care Momentum: Sales INR 95L Q2 (vs. 38L Q1); cat/dog foods gaining traction (e-com on Supertail/Amazon soon). Brand “Avant Furst” via digital marketing.
  • Balance Sheet Strength: Cash equivalents INR 3.6 Bn consolidated; high liquidity funds capex (pet plant near Hyderabad, fish feed trials with Thai Union).

Key Risks
  1. Tariff Prolongation (High): If 50% persists into FY27, processing volumes could miss 17k MT; margins erode if unpassable (currently passed via customer contracts).
  2. RM Volatility (High): Further spikes (fish meal >INR 130/kg) could drag feed EBITDA/kg; sensitivity: ~1% margin hit per INR 1/kg fish meal rise (mgmt estimate).
  3. Aquaculture Risks (Medium): Disease/climate impacts on shrimp cycles; farmer sentiment (currently confident, good farm-gate prices).
  4. Competition/Execution (Medium): Pet care brand-building vs. incumbents; fish feed viability unproven.
  5. Forex/External (Low-Medium): Rupee volatility (mitigated by forwards); global shrimp oversupply if Ecuador/India ramp.
  6. Regulatory (Low): ADD/CVD review ongoing (no final update); US Supreme Court petition speculative.

Balanced View: Avanti’s diversified model, market leadership, and liquidity buffer strong performance amid headwinds. FY26 likely resilient (PBT ~INR 900-1,000 Cr possible), but H2 RM/tariff risks cap upside. Positive on diversification/domestic tailwinds; pet care a multi-year wildcard. Recommendation: Accumulate on dips; target FY26 EPS ~INR 50-55 (at 20-25x PE, fair value INR 1,400-1,600). Monitor Q3 results (Feb 2026) for tariff/RM updates.
