IndiGo (InterGlobe Aviation Ltd.) Analysis

Company Overview: IndiGo is India’s largest airline by market share, operating a fleet of 417 aircraft (as of Sep 30, 2025) across 94 domestic and 41 international destinations. It reported Q2 FY26 (ended Sep 30, 2025) consolidated revenue of INR 196 bn (+10.4% YoY), but a PAT loss of INR 25.8 bn (worse than INR 9.9 bn loss YoY) primarily due to INR 29 bn forex losses from rupee depreciation. Ex-forex, PAT was positive at INR 1 bn (vs. loss YoY). Strong cash balance of INR 535 bn supports growth.

Tailwinds (Positive Factors)

  • Operational Strength: Leading on-time performance (89.8% at metros), low cancellations (0.5%), and technical reliability (99.89%). Capacity (ASK) grew 7.8% YoY to 41.2 bn; passengers up 3.6% to 28.8 mn. Load factor stable at 82.5%.
  • Revenue Momentum: RASK up 2.3% to INR 4.55; yield up 3.2%. Ancillary revenues +14.2% YoY. Total income +10.4% despite seasonal weakness.
  • Cost Efficiencies: Fuel CASK down 16.3% to INR 1.45 (absolute fuel costs -9.7%). EBITDAR ex-forex up 42.5% to INR 38 bn (20.5% margin).
  • Strong Balance Sheet: Total cash INR 535 bn (free cash INR 385 bn, up 58% YoY); net cash from ops INR 108 bn (HY25).
  • Strategic Initiatives: USD 820 mn investment in wholly-owned IFSC subsidiary for aircraft ownership (shifting from lease-heavy model). Fleet net addition of 1 aircraft in Q2; inducted 58 in FY25.
  • Market Leadership: Awards (Skytrax ‘Best in India/South Asia’); peak 2,244 daily flights.

Headwinds (Negative Factors)

  • Forex Impact: INR 29 bn loss (Q2) from rupee depreciation (avg. USD/INR 87.06 vs. 83.66 YoY); total forex loss HY25 INR 30 bn. Eroded profitability (EBITDAR margin down to 6% incl. forex).
  • Rising Costs: CASK ex-fuel ex-forex up 3.9% to INR 3.01; employee costs +10.6%, depreciation +26.5%, airport fees +9.4%. Total expenses +18.3% YoY.
  • Seasonality & Losses: Q2 is weak; HY25 PAT loss INR 4 bn despite Q1 profit. High lease liabilities (INR 730 bn).
  • Regulatory Penalty: INR 20 lakh DGCA fine for procedural lapse at Udaipur airport (no material impact).

Growth Prospects

  • Capacity Expansion: FY26 ASK growth guided to “early teens” (up from prior); Q3 high-teens YoY. International push (41 destinations, +9 YoY).
  • Fleet & Network: From 410 (Sep’24) to 417; mix shifting to efficient A320neo/A321neo/ATRs. Ownership via IFSC sub (turnover INR 290 Cr FY25) to optimize costs.
  • India Aviation Boom: Structural demand; IndiGo’s low-cost model positions it for 10-15%+ industry growth. International & freighter expansion (3 A321 freighters).
  • Capital Allocation: Prudent (dividend paid Rs. 10/share); investor meets signal confidence.
  • Governance: New director (Amitabh Kant) adds policy expertise.

Projected Trajectory: Ex-forex profitability improving; H2 scaling up. Potential for positive FY26 PAT if rupee stabilizes/fuel softens.

Summary

Bull Case: IndiGo’s market dominance, capacity discipline, and fleet modernization position it for 10-15% revenue growth in FY26, with ex-forex margins expanding to 15-20% via ownership shift. Strong cash enables resilience; India’s aviation TAM supports re-rating. Bear Case: Persistent rupee weakness and cost inflation could deepen losses; regulatory overhang adds uncertainty. Overall Outlook: Positive long-term (Buy/Hold for growth investors) despite near-term forex drag. Monitor USD/INR (<85 favorable) and Q3 results for H2 recovery. Target multiples: 8-10x FY26 EV/EBITDAR (peers ~7x). No major red flags from disclosures.
