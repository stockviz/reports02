Analysis of IVP Limited (IVP.NS)

IVP Limited, a 95-year-old chemical manufacturer (part of Allana Group), specializes in phenolic resins (foundry applications) and polyurethane (PU) resins (footwear, flexible packaging, composites). It has two plants with 50,000+ MT annual capacity. FY25 revenue was ~₹539 Cr, with H1FY26 at ₹285 Cr (+10% YoY). Q2FY26 showed strong sequential/YoY growth (revenue +20% YoY, EBITDA +42%).

Headwinds

  • Raw Material Volatility & Margin Pressure: Chemical industry faces RM price swings, forex fluctuations, and global trade issues, eroding margins (H1FY26 EBITDA margin 5.0% vs. 5.4% YoY; phenolic resins at 3-5%).
  • Import Competition: Pricing pressures from cheap imports, especially in foundry chemicals.
  • Cyclical Demand: Foundry (35-40% revenue) tied to auto/manufacturing slowdowns; overall revenue dipped post-FY23 peak (₹661 Cr to ₹539 Cr in FY25).
  • Moderate H1 Performance: PAT down 7% YoY to ₹5.2 Cr due to higher finance costs (+18%) and flat EBITDA.

Tailwinds

  • Domestic Demand Revival: Festive season, GST rationalization, rural consumption, good monsoon, and govt spending boosted Q2 (revenue +20% YoY, PAT +85%). India’s GDP est. 6.8-7.2% in Q2FY26.
  • Operational Efficiencies: Cost discipline offset pressures; positive operating cash flow (₹96 Cr in H1FY26 vs. negative ₹18 Cr prior).
  • Deleveraging Progress: Short-term borrowings down to ₹100 Cr (from ₹104 Cr FY25); debt-equity improving (0.7x); no long-term debt.
  • Strong Clientele & Certifications: Marquee clients (Tata Steel, Ashok Leyland, Relaxo); ISO 9001/14001/45001 certified, enhancing credibility.

Growth Prospects

  • PU Expansion (55-60% revenue, 5-7% margins): Shift to higher-margin non-foam (flexible packaging at 10% margins, 5-7% revenue share) using unutilized Tarapur land (12 acres); avoids capex-heavy greenfield.
  • Capacity Utilization & Efficiency: 50k MT capacity underutilized; modernization (SAP S/4HANA in 2024) and R&D to boost fixed asset turnover.
  • Market Tailwinds: Flexible packaging/flexibles growth; footwear recovery. Management targets domestic share gains, customer engagement.
  • Historical Proof: PU launch (2018) drove revenue from ₹290 Cr (FY21) to ₹661 Cr peak (FY23).

Key Risks

  • Commodity/External Shocks: High RM exposure (78% of expenses in H1FY26); forex/gre geopolitics could hit margins.
  • Execution Risk: Capex for PU expansion (~₹5 Cr annual investing CF); scaling flexible packaging unproven.
  • Liquidity & WC: Trade receivables high (₹183 Cr, 112 days); cash low (₹0.3 Cr); current ratio stable at 1.3-1.4x but inventory cycles volatile.
  • Sector Cyclicality: Foundry/auto dependence; any slowdown (e.g., global steel weakness) impacts 35-40% revenue.
  • Regulatory/Competition: Import duties, environmental compliance (ETP/STP in place but ongoing costs).

Summary

Bull Case (Tailwinds Dominate): IVP is well-positioned for FY26 recovery (Q2 momentum +10% H1 revenue growth), leveraging existing assets for high-margin PU diversification amid India’s 7% GDP tailwinds. Deleveraging and efficiency could lift ROE (7-21% historical) and dividends (consistent payer). Target: 15-20% revenue CAGR via packaging/footwear.

Bear Case (Headwinds Persist): RM volatility/imports cap margins at 5-6%; cyclical foundry drags growth to low single-digits. Risks amplified if global trade worsens.

Overall: Positive outlook (management optimistic); trading at attractive multiples given debt reduction and growth levers. Monitor Q3 for sustained Q2 momentum. Valuation merits: Lean balance sheet, 6%+ ROCE potential.
