Power Mech Projects Limited (POWERMECH) Analysis

Power Mech Projects Ltd. (PMPL) is a leading player in power/infra EPC, O&M, civil works, and mining (MDO), with a robust Q2/H1 FY26 performance (H1 revenue +24% YoY to INR 2,554 Cr; EBITDA +33% to INR 340 Cr; order book INR 56,353 Cr). FY26 guidance: ~INR 6,500 Cr revenue (+25% YoY). Below is a structured analysis based on the provided documents (earnings call, financials, orders, investor presentation).

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Robust Order Pipeline: INR 4,889 Cr new orders YTD (49% of FY26 INR 10,000 Cr target); marquee wins like INR 2,500 Cr+ BOP EPC from BHEL (38-month execution). Executable book (ex-MDO): INR 16,804 Cr (3+ years visibility).
  • Power Sector Boom: 79% revenue from power; 47.5 GW thermal ordered (INR 30-45k Cr BoP/ETC opportunity); PMPL targets 25-30% share via O&M (INR 1,200-1,500 Cr annual opps) and EPC.
  • High-Margin Segments Scaling: O&M (15-16% EBITDA) up 12-14% YoY; MDO ramping (KBP coal from Nov’25, Tasra washery by Sep’26; peak INR 2,000 Cr revenue at 23-25% margins by FY28).
  • Diversification Momentum: Mechanical +90% YoY; electrical +138%; mining +164%; infra (railways, steel via SAIL/NMDC). International O&M +25-30% CAGR.
  • Financial Discipline: Improving OCF (-INR 63 Cr H1 vs. -INR 166 Cr prior); D/E 0.37x; ROE/ROCE stable at ~3.4%/4.6%.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Receivables & Working Capital Strain: JJM/water dues INR 446 Cr (total unbilled INR 960 Cr); NCA days up to 151 (from 128); delayed certifications impacting cash flow and debt (net debt INR 360 Cr).
  • Segment Slowdowns: Civil -22% YoY (rains, water delays); EBITDA margin flat at 12.7% (Q2), PAT margin dip to 6.3% (higher finance/depreciation).
  • Seasonal/Execution Factors: Monsoons hit sites; Q2 revenue -4% QoQ (no repeat of prior Riverbed exceptional revenue).
  • Margin Pressure: Construction at ~10% EBITDA; dependent on O&M/MDO mix for upside.

Growth Prospects

  • Near-Term (FY26-27): 20-25% revenue CAGR to INR 7,500-8,000 Cr FY27; EBITDA margins +0.25-0.5% via MDO/O&M. Order target: INR 10k Cr FY26 (power EPC BoP INR 2,550 Cr already won).
  • Medium-Term (FY27-28): MDO peak (INR 2k Cr, 23-25% margins); power capex (80-100 GW addition, INR 3 lakh Cr TAM; PMPL bids INR 2 lakh Cr, 50-60% hit rate → INR 1 lakh Cr inflows over 5-7 yrs). Infra/steel (SAIL INR 1 lakh Cr, NMDC INR 50k Cr).
  • Long-Term: Renewables (solar/BESS 100 MW/250 MWh FY27); data centers; 40% annual book execution. Overseas O&M leadership (75 GW managed).
  • Capital Allocation: No immediate fundraising; ESOP plan (10 lakh options) for talent retention.

Key Risks

  • Execution/Receivables (High): Water/JJM delays (INR 446 Cr stuck); monsoon risks; working capital reliance (negative OCF).
  • Sector Concentration (Medium-High): 79% power-dependent; thermal post-2030 scrutiny (though O&M sustains 7-8 yrs).
  • Financial (Medium): Debt up (gross INR 839 Cr); interest rate sensitivity; forex in overseas (5% revenue).
  • Operational (Medium): MDO ramp delays (land/funding); competition in EPC/BOP; labor/safety in mining/infra.
  • Macro/Regulatory (Medium): Commodity inflation; policy shifts (e.g., thermal vs. RE); environmental clearances.

Overall Outlook: Strong tailwinds from power/infra capex and MDO outweigh headwinds; 20-25% CAGR feasible with receivable resolution. Maintain positive stance; monitor working capital. (Ratings context: Buy/Hold with target upside on orders.)
