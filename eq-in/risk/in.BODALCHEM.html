BODAL CHEMICALS LIMITED (BODALCHEM) – Business & Financial Analysis

(Based on Unaudited Financial Results for Q2 FY26 – Quarter and Half Year Ended Sept 30, 2025)

🚩 Summary Overview

Bodal Chemicals Limited (BODALCHEM), an Indian specialty chemicals manufacturer focused on synthetic dyes, pigments, and dispersions, recently filed its unaudited standalone and consolidated financial results for Q2 FY26 (ended Sept 30, 2025). The results indicate a mixed financial performance with modest recovery in revenues, sharp improvement in profitability compared to the same period last year (YoY), strategic exits through non-core asset sales, but also ongoing headwinds from hyperinflation impacts in Turkey and working capital volatility.

Despite challenges, the company has taken proactive steps to improve cash flow and balance sheet strength via divestment of idle assets and is on track to recover from a past fire incident that affected production.

🌬️ Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

1. Strong Revenue Growth & Operational Recovery

  • Standalone revenue grew 10.7% YoY in Q2, indicating demand recovery post-fire incident in Dec 2024 (Unit 7 Dyes Plant).
  • Consolidated H1 revenue up 8.9% YoY; operational capacity restoration likely complete by mid-FY26.
  • Improved pricing power or volume pickup observed despite macroeconomic headwinds.

2. Sharp Improvement in Profitability

  • Standalone net profit surged to ₹41.97 Cr vs ₹7.83 Cr in Q2 FY25 (up 435%).
  • Consolidated profits crossed break-even, earning ₹33.34 Cr (vs loss of ₹4.63 Cr H1 FY25).
  • Better cost optimization (controlled materials cost growth vs revenue), lower interest costs (down YoY), and profit from sale of idle assets boosted earnings.

3. Strategic Divestment of Idle Units (Unit II & III)

  • Sold non-operational units in Ahmedabad (Unit II B: ₹106.6 Cr profit; Unit III: ₹72.34 Cr profit).
  • These proceeds were credited under Other Income:
    • Standalone: Other income → ₹198.83 Cr (Q2), includes ₹178.94 Cr from asset sales.
    • Consolidated: Other income → ₹199.26 Cr (Q2), includes same gains.
  • Monetization reduces idle asset burden, improves cash reserves, and allows capital reallocation.

4. Favorable Tax Adjustments

  • Deferred tax reversal of ₹(4.67) Cr (benefit of ~₹4.67 Cr credit in Q2 consolidated results).
  • Indicates past provisioning conservatism or NDTA utilization – improving bottom line.

5. Strong Cash Conversion

  • Net operating cash flow at ₹778.82 Cr (H1 consolidated) despite lower net profit than prior year.
  • Large inventory drawdown (+₹407 Cr) was key driver; suggests improved inventory turnover and working capital discipline.
  • Company generating significant operational liquidity despite low profitability segment.
☁️ Headwinds & Challenges

1. Hyperinflation Impact in Turkish Subsidiaries

  • Applies Ind AS 29 to Turkish subsidiaries: Sener Boya Kimya and Senpa Dis Ticaret.
  • Due to Turkish Lira’s extreme depreciation, non-monetary items (assets/equity) revalued using CPI.
  • Result: Loss of ₹18.89 Cr (Q2) and ₹33.34 Cr (H1) recorded in P&L – large material drag.
  • Reversal of earlier hyperinflation gains may continue impacting future earnings.

2. Foreign Exchange Translation Losses

  • FX translation loss of ₹17.13 Cr (Q2) and ₹18.35 Cr (H1) due to foreign subsidiary reporting.
  • Weakness in EUR/USD vs INR pressures OCI and equity.
  • OCI (Other Comprehensive Income) negative — not affecting net income now but erodes reserves.

3. Declining Operating Cash Flows (YoY)

  • OCF dropped to ₹778.82 Cr (H1 FY26) vs ₹1,120.88 Cr (H1 FY25) — 30.5% YoY decline.
  • Driven by:
    • Increased trade receivables: ₹398.67 Cr increase YTD (cash outflow).
    • Higher advance tax payments.
  • Despite solid operations, working capital efficiency remains inconsistent.

4. High Net Debt & Financial Liabilities

  • Total borrowings (short + long term) = ₹8,580.46 Cr as of Sept 30, 2025, up from ₹9,059.36 Cr (March 2025).
  • Net cash position weakened from ₹103 Cr (Mar ’25) to ₹(32.89) Cr (Sept ’25) post-asset sales.
  • Finance cost remains high: ₹415 Cr (H1 FY26), though slightly better than FY25 (~₹455 Cr).

5. Limited Auditor Review Scopes

  • The statutory auditors (BNP S & Associates LLP) note they did not review interim results of:
    • 2 subsidiaries (total assets: ₹458 Cr, revenue ₹77 Cr/140 Cr for Q/H1).
    • 4 subsidiaries where management-certified data used (assets ₹227 Cr).
  • While concluded “no material misstatements found”, reliance on third-party reviews and management certifications increases audit risk.
🔍 Growth Prospects

1. Operational Normalization Post-Fire

  • Dec 2024 fire at Unit 7 (Dyes Plant) caused damage to inventory and PPE.
  • Insurance claim of ₹59.91 Cr now fully settled.
  • Only residual ₹0.30 Cr fire-related loss charged this quarter — business stabilized.

2. Integration & Expansion via Niche Segments

  • Focus remains on specialty chemicals, dispersions, pigments, dyes – high-margin segments.
  • International presence via Turkey and Indonesia supports global integration.
  • Potential to expand in EU/ME markets through existing units if Turkish operations stabilized.

3. Balance Sheet Cleanup Underway

  • Exiting non-core Indian manufacturing units frees capital.
  • Possibility of debt reduction or reinvestment in core technologies.
  • Strong working capital management signals improved financial discipline.

4. Digital & Regulatory Compliance Leadership

  • Prompt e-filing, digital signing, timely disclosures show strong corporate governance.
  • Aligns with SEBI norms and investor expectations —有利于 institutional interest.
