Analysis of Vraj Iron and Steel Limited (VRAJ)

Vraj Iron and Steel Limited (NSE: VRAJ, BSE: 544204), a steel manufacturer (TMT bars and products, IS:1786:2008 certified), operates primarily in Chhattisgarh with plants in Raipur and Bilaspur. Incorporated in 2004, it listed recently (post-IPO inferred from equity issuance in cash flows). Analysis based on provided documents: credit enhancement (Sep 2025), Q2/H1 FY26 unaudited results (ended Sep 30, 2025), and AGM voting (Sep 2025). All figures in ₹ Mn unless noted.

Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts)

  • Revenue Momentum: H1 FY26 standalone revenue at 2,713 (+34% YoY from 2,021); Q2 at 1,330 (up sharply from Q2 FY25’s 875). Driven by steel demand; single reportable segment (steel products).
  • Capex-Driven Expansion: CWIP surged to 314 (from 33 YoY), funded by new ₹38 Cr term loan for Solar Power Project (captive use at Raipur unit). Expected to cut energy costs, boost efficiency, reduce carbon footprint, and enable “green steel” branding (ESG tailwind).
  • Debt Capacity Boost: Total sanctioned facilities now ₹2,128 Cr (up from prior; includes LC to ₹50 Cr, CC ₹40 Cr, new TL). Supports working capital and ops scale-up.
  • Strong Balance Sheet: Equity at ~₹3,996 (standalone); net debt low (~₹253 Cr borrowings vs. ₹4,512 assets). Cash ops generated ₹3.5 Mn (H1); promoter voting 100% support at AGM.
  • Profit Visibility: PAT H1 at 145 (consolidated 153, incl. ₹8 associate share); EPS ₹4.40 basic (H1). OCI positive from investments.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Margin Pressure: PBT H1 down 40% YoY to 196 (from 328); PAT margins compressed despite revenue growth. Higher depreciation (₹105 H1 vs. ₹27 YoY) from capex; materials cost up 55% YoY.
  • Working Capital Strain: Inventories up 32% to 670; trade receivables steady but other assets ballooned (capex drag). Ops cash low (₹3.5 Mn vs. ₹49 FY25 H1); heavy investing outflow (₹247 Mn).
  • Cost Inflation: Employee expenses +48% YoY; other expenses +16%. Finance costs low (₹6) but borrowings doubled (non-current ₹50 Cr new).
  • Low Liquidity/Participation: Cash equivalents down to ₹6.5; public voting only ~1.3% of non-institutional shares at AGM (despite 75% overall poll).

Growth Prospects

  • High (Medium-Term: 2-3 Years): Solar project aligns with India’s green infra push (RE focus); expected to lower energy costs 20-30% (est.), improve EBITDA margins (current ~10-15% implied). Total facilities ₹2,128 Cr signal capacity/ops ramp-up.
  • Revenue Potential: Steel demand from infra/housing (India FY26 budget tailwinds). H1 growth +34%; FY25 full-year revenue ₹4,750 projects FY26 >₹9,000+ if sustained.
  • ESG/Market Edge: Captive solar positions products as “green” (premium pricing); associate (Vraj Metaliks) adds ₹8-23 Mn profit share annually.
  • Projections (Rough, Based on Trends): Revenue CAGR 25-30% FY25-27; PAT recovery post-depreciation peak/solar ops (FY26E PAT ₹800-1,000 Mn if margins stabilize 12-15%).
