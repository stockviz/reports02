APAR Industries Limited (APARINDS) Analysis: Headwinds, Tailwinds, Growth Prospects & Key Risks

Summary Overview:
APAR delivered a stellar H1 FY26 with record revenue (₹10,820 Cr, +25% YoY) and EBITDA (₹1,000 Cr, +25.5% YoY), driven by conductors (+39% revenue) and cables (+30%), strong exports (US +122% YoY), and premium mix. PAT at ₹515 Cr (+30% YoY). Order book robust (Conductors: ₹7,168 Cr; Cables: ₹1,836 Cr). Fundamentals intact amid power sector boom (India: 25 GW RE addition H1 FY26), but Q3 pressures from metal prices/US tariffs loom. Long-term outlook positive with capex (₹1,300 Cr FY26) and diversification.

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Robust Demand: India RE surge (21.7 GW solar H1), hydro/transmission push (₹6.4 Tn plan), substations +74% YoY. Global grid modernization/data centers favor conductors/cables.
  • Export Momentum: 34.7% mix (vs. 29.8% YoY); US +129% Q2 (data centers/wind/solar). New markets (Europe/LatAm/Canada).
  • Margin Expansion: Conductors EBITDA/ton ₹39k-41k (+4-9% YoY) via 45% premium mix (HTLS/copper). Cables EBITDA 10.2% (+50 bps).
  • Domestic Strength: +14.5% Q2 revenue; H2 execution pickup expected (better weather, ROW resolution).
  • Strategic Wins: Dark fiber PPP (Karnataka 6,100 km OPGW, 15-yr recurring revenue, 60:40 split, no capex).

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Commodity Volatility: Al +$2,900/ton, Cu +$11,200/ton → global ordering freeze (wait-and-watch); impacts new solar cables/transmission.
  • US Tariff Disruptions: Section 232 (50% on Al/Cu/steel), reciprocal duties (India 54% vs. ME/UK 10%) → Aug-Sep order halt; Q3 revenue/profit hit (DDP terms delay recognition to Q4).
  • Execution Lags: India transmission lines -27% YoY (39% of target); oils volumes soft (global transformer -4.6% due to supply chain).
  • Seasonality: Q2 domestic slowdown (monsoon); milestone billing delays orders.

Growth Prospects

  • Medium-Term (FY26-28): 25%+ revenue CAGR guided; capex adds ₹6,000 Cr cables capacity (to ₹10,000 Cr), conductors by Mar’26. Reconductor/regrid (HTLS demand +150-200% capacity).
  • US Recovery: IRA incentives (construction by Jul’26, ops by Dec’27) to drive Q4 orders despite tariffs (fluid, potential India-US deal).
  • Diversification: Dark fiber monetization (telecom/data centers); wind/railways H2 pickup.
  • Outlook: H1 strength + H2 execution = FY26 beat; EBITDA/ton sustained ~₹30k-40k; export mix to rise.

Key Risks

Risk Category Description Mitigation
Commodity/FX Metal spikes delay orders (high exposure: 70-80% costs); forex open-period volatility. Fully hedged book; pass-through pricing.
Geopolitical/Trade US tariffs erode margins (30-50% non-metal hit); no deal = sustained slowdown. Price adjustments/customer absorption; diversify to domestic/non-US exports.
Execution/Competition Transmission shortfalls; new conductor capacities from rivals pressure margins. Strong order book; premium focus (45% mix).
Capex/Utilization ₹1,300 Cr FY26 spend; delays in commissioning (cables Q1-Q2 FY27). Advance planning; 75-85% current util. ramping.
Macro RE slowdown/global recession impacts grid spend. Multi-segment presence (gen/trans/dist); balanced domestic/export.

Investment Thesis: Strong buy for long-term (power infra tailwinds outweigh Q3 blip). Target FY26 revenue ₹22,000-23,000 Cr; monitor US tariffs/metal prices for Q3 earnings (Nov’25). ROE ~25-30% sustainable.
