APAR Industries Limited (APARINDS) Analysis: Headwinds, Tailwinds, Growth Prospects, and Key Risks

Overall Summary:
APAR Industries delivered a stellar H1 FY26 with record revenue of ₹10,820 Cr (+25% YoY), EBITDA of ₹1,000 Cr (+25.5% YoY), and PAT of ₹515 Cr (+29.9% YoY), driven by volume growth across segments (Conductors +39% revenue, Cables +30%), premium product mix, and robust exports (US up 122% YoY). Fundamentals remain strong amid India’s renewable/grid boom (25GW H1 capacity addition), but near-term Q3 pressure looms from metal price spikes (Al ~$2,900/ton, Cu >$11,200/ton) and US tariffs. Long-term outlook positive with capacity expansions (₹1,300 Cr FY26 Capex), new recurring revenue from dark fiber PPP, and reconductoring opportunities. Stock trades at premium valuations; monitor US tariff resolution and metal prices.

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Robust Demand Ecosystem: India’s renewables hit record 25GW H1 addition (21.7GW solar); hydro master plan (208 projects, ₹6.4 Tn transmission); substation capacity +74% YoY. Global grid modernization supports conductors/cables.
  • Segment Strength: | Segment | Key Positives | |————-|—————| | Conductors | Revenue +39% H1 YoY to ₹5,885 Cr; volume +16.8%; premium mix 44.5% (up from 39.9%); EBITDA/MT ₹41,421 (+9%); order book ₹7,168 Cr (+new orders ₹5,256 Cr H1). | | Cables | Revenue +30% H1 to ₹2,954 Cr; US +128% YoY; export mix 41.8%; EBITDA margin stable ~10%; order book ₹1,836 Cr. | | Oils | Volume +8.1% H1; domestic transformer oil +17%; EBITDA/KL +4%. |
  • Export Momentum: Exports 33% of H1 revenue (+25% YoY); US critical (₹1,600 Cr H1, matching FY25 full year).
  • Domestic Resilience: +25% H1 growth; less tariff exposure.
  • Strategic Wins: Dark fiber PPP with KPTCL (6,100 km OPGW network, 15 yrs; 60:40 revenue share; no Capex, ₹5 Cr annual rental; recurring telecom/data center revenue).
  • Operational Leverage: EBITDA margins stable (9.2% H1); hedged metal book protects margins.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Metal Price Volatility: Al/Cu spikes delaying global/domestic orders (customers wait for correction); EPCs/developers hedged at old prices executing first.
  • US Tariff/Geopolitical Fluidity:
    • Section 232: 50% duty on Al/Cu/Steel (equalizes vs. all countries).
    • Reciprocal duties: India at 54% on non-metal (vs. 10% ME/UK); Aug-Sep order halt; Q3 revenue/profit pressure (new US orders DDP, book in Q4).
    • Customers absorbing some duties, but margins hit short-term.
  • Execution Lags: India transmission lines -27% YoY (39% of target); right-of-way/milestone billing delays; monsoon/Q2 slowdown.
  • Supply Chain: Oils transformer volume -4.6% QoQ (Middle East/export delays).
  • Financial Strain: Cash from ops ₹169 Cr H1 (vs. ₹429 Cr H1 FY25) due to WC expansion (receivables/inventories up); Capex ₹407 Cr H1.

Growth Prospects

  • Near-Term (FY26): 25%+ revenue guidance intact; Q4 rebound from US orders (IRA incentives deadline Jul’26-Dec’27); domestic H2 acceleration (post-monsoon, ROW clearance); wind/railways pickup.
  • Medium-Term (FY27+):
    • Capacity ramp: Conductors by Mar’26; Cables ₹800 Cr Capex (₹10,000 Cr revenue potential); fungible lines for HTLS/reconductoring.
    • Reconductoring Boom: Cheaper/faster than new lines (+150-200% throughput); policy push; data centers driving urban demand.
    • Renewables/Data Centers: US wind/solar corridors; India data centers; wind cables strength.
    • Diversification: Dark fiber (telecom/hyperscalers); Europe/LatAm/Canada expansion; copper products (busbars, transpose).
    • EBITDA Guidance: Conductors ₹30k-40k/MT sustained; Cables 10-12%.
  • Long-Term: Global T&D leader; brownfield infra (OPGW); recurring passive infra revenue.
