Analysis of Nahar Spinning Mills Ltd. (NAHARSPING)

Overview: Nahar Spinning Mills, a textile manufacturer (single segment), reported flat H1 FY26 revenue (₹1,598 Cr vs ₹1,597 Cr YoY) but a sharp profit turnaround (PAT ₹11.3 Cr vs loss ₹10.8 Cr YoY), driven by cost controls and working capital efficiency. Balance sheet strengthened with ~35% reduction in current borrowings (₹596 Cr vs ₹916 Cr). Q2 FY26 saw a loss (₹-4.6 Cr) vs Q1 profit (₹15.9 Cr), amid US tariffs. AGM resolutions (including dividend of ₹1/share) passed overwhelmingly. Strong operating cash flow (₹332 Cr) funded debt repayment.

Headwinds (Challenges Pressuring Performance)

  • US Tariffs on Textiles: Explicit note on direct/indirect profitability impact from recent US import duties, hurting export margins (textiles export-dependent).
  • Flat Revenue & Q2 Loss: H1 revenue stagnant YoY; Q2 EBITDA negative due to higher power/fuel costs (up QoQ) and material expenses.
  • Inventory Drawdown: Sharp 22% drop (₹671 Cr vs ₹860 Cr FY25-end), signaling potential weak demand or excess stock liquidation.
  • Cyclical Sector Pressures: High finance costs (down but still ₹28.6 Cr H1), employee expenses, and volatile input costs (cotton/power).

Tailwinds (Positive Momentum)

  • Cost Discipline & Profit Recovery: Expenses down 2% YoY (₹1,589 Cr vs ₹1,626 Cr) via lower materials, finance (↓26%), and inventory changes; PBT swung to ₹14 Cr profit.
  • Deleveraging: Current borrowings slashed ₹319 Cr; total debt lower, improving interest coverage. Operating cash flow surged to ₹332 Cr (working capital release ₹263 Cr).
  • Balance Sheet Health: Equity up to ₹1,535 Cr; investments rose 33% (₹184 Cr). Cash used for capex (₹91 Cr) and dividend (₹36 Cr).
  • Shareholder Approval: 99.99%+ votes for key resolutions (dividend, directors, auditors, related-party deals), signaling governance confidence.

Growth Prospects (Opportunities Ahead)

  • Margin Expansion Potential: If costs stabilize (e.g., lower finance post-debt cut), EBITDA margins can recover (H1 ~9% implied). Debt reduction frees ~₹50 Cr annual interest.
  • Operational Efficiency: Strong cash generation (₹332 Cr H1) supports modest capex (₹333 Cr fixed assets + CWIP). Inventory optimization positions for demand rebound.
  • Export Recovery: Textile cycle upturn possible if tariffs ease or alternate markets (e.g., domestic/Asia) grow. No subsidiaries limits diversification but focuses core spinning.
  • Valuation Upside: EPS turnaround (₹3.14 H1 vs loss); dividend yield attractive. FY26 full-year PAT could double FY25 (₹123 Cr) if H2 improves.
