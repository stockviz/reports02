TD Power Systems Limited (TDPOWERSYS) Analysis

Overall Outlook: Strongly positive with robust H1 FY26 performance (42% YoY revenue growth to ₹8,243 Cr consolidated, 45% PAT growth to ₹1,108 Cr). Exports drive 76-84% of orders, order book at ₹1,587 Cr (83% exports), and revised FY26 guidance to ₹18,000 Cr top-line. Declared ₹1/share interim dividend (50%). Diversified global footprint (7,604 generators in 111 countries) supports sustained growth amid energy transition.

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Explosive Order Growth: Q2 FY26 inflow +45% YoY (₹524 Cr), H1 +39% (₹916 Cr). Key wins in data centers (US gas turbine/engine gens), waste-to-energy (Japan/Europe), hydro (India/Vietnam/Nepal), steam (Nigeria/Slovenia/Italy), and 2-pole gens (UAE/Saudi; maiden ADNOC supply).
  • Export Dominance & Diversification: 76-84% inflows from exports; order book split: 62% exports (excl. deemed), 14% deemed exports, 24% domestic. Segments balanced (hydro, gas, steam, motors).
  • Financial Strength: EBITDA margin stable ~19%, PAT margin 13.4%; net cash position (₹6.25 Cr cash eq.); equity up 12% YoY to ₹9,614 Cr; low debt (borrowings <3% of assets).
  • Operational Scale: Third plant operational Q4 FY26; automation/robotics enhancing efficiency; 45+ global OEMs.
  • Shareholding Stability: Promoters 24%, MFs/AIFs 27%, FIIs 23%; top MFs (Aditya Birla, HDFC, ICICI).

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Margin Pressure: Gross margin down 250 bps QoQ/H1 YoY to 33-34% (raw materials/inventory up 14% to ₹3,897 Cr).
  • Working Capital Strain: Trade receivables +17% QoQ (₹5,121 Cr); inventories +4% (₹3,897 Cr); operating cash flow positive but moderated by capex (₹382 Cr H1 outflow).
  • Subsidiary Concerns: Indian sub (DF Power) has going concern doubt (net worth positive via creditor write-backs, but ₹30 Cr impairment provisioned FY25); not material to group.
  • Forex/External Volatility: Heavy export reliance exposes to INR volatility; Japan branch losses (₹29 Cr H1).

Growth Prospects

  • FY26/FY27 Upside: ₹18 Bn guidance (vs. FY25 ₹12.9 Bn); hydro “very strong” FY27; 7x 2-pole gens FY26 (milestone); data centers/gas as “key strategic vertical”; domestic hydro refurb tenders.
  • Secular Trends: Energy transition (renewables, gas plants, grid stabilization, WtE, geothermal); data center boom (US/Europe orders); rail (Germany/US); Ukraine/WHRP opportunities.
  • Expansion: New motors vertical (2022); Turkey/Europe/US/Japan presence; total installations +ve momentum.
  • CAGR Potential: Historical revenue CAGR 21% (FY21-25); PAT 41%; poised for 30-40%+ if execution holds.
