TD Power Systems Limited (TDPOWERSYS) Analysis

Company Overview: TD Power Systems Ltd. is a leading manufacturer of AC generators (up to 250 MVA) and motors for turbines, engines, hydro, wind, marine, oil & gas, and special applications. It has a global footprint (111 countries, 7,604+ installations), strong OEM tie-ups (Siemens, Alstom, INNIO, etc.), and export-led growth (76-84% of recent orders). FY26 H1 results show robust performance: Revenue ₹8,294 Cr (+42% YoY), EBITDA ₹1,566 Cr (18.9% margin), PAT ₹1,108 Cr (+45% YoY), Order Inflow ₹9,159 Cr (+39% YoY), Order Book ₹15,870 Cr. Revised FY26 guidance: ₹18,000 Cr revenue. Interim dividend of ₹1/share (50%) declared.

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Explosive Order Growth & Book: Q2 inflow +45% YoY (₹5,241 Cr, 84% exports); H1 +39%. Diversified book (Steam 25%, Hydro 28%, Gas Engine 15%, etc.; 56% exports). Key wins: Data centers (US/Europe), WtE (Japan/Italy/Slovenia), Hydro (India/Vietnam/Nepal), 2-pole gensets (UAE/Saudi).
  • Export Momentum: 76% H1 orders from exports; strong in Europe (1,420 installs), Asia (5,277), N. America. Presence via sales offices (US, Germany, Japan) and Turkey factory.
  • Sector Tailwinds: Energy transition (gas turbines/engines for data centers, renewables, grid stabilization); hydro refurbishments; booming data centers (US follow-on orders expected Q3/Q4).
  • Operational Strength: World-class Bangalore facility with automation/robotics; in-house value chain; tech from Toyo Denki/Siemens/VOITH. New motors vertical (2022); 3rd plant operational by Q4 FY26.
  • Financial Health: PAT margins stable ~13%; low debt (borrowings ₹357 Cr); ROE implied >18%; dividend payout signals confidence. Shareholding stable (Promoters 23.6%, MFs/AIFs 26.9%).
  • Management: Experienced leadership (MD Nikhil Kumar 30+ yrs); board with industry/tech experts.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Domestic Slowdown: Marginal growth expected FY26; reliant on large hydro tenders (uncertain).
  • Margin Pressure: Gross margins dipped 170 bps YoY H1 (34.3%) due to higher COGS (₹5,453 Cr, 66% of revenue); EBITDA margins flat/slight dip despite revenue surge.
  • Capex Intensity: ₹482 Cr CWIP; ongoing investments in automation/expansion strain cash (H1 CFO ₹126 Cr vs. FY25 ₹395 Cr).
  • Subsidiary Drag: Indian sub (DF Power) has going concern doubts (net worth positive but impaired ₹300 Cr provision); minor forex branch losses (Japan).
  • Cyclicality: OEM-dependent (45+ global OEMs); delays in turbine orders could hit inflows.

Growth Prospects

  • High Visibility: Order book covers ~9-10 months revenue; FY26 guidance +40% (from FY25 ₹12,911 Cr). Hydro “very strong” FY27; 7x 2-pole gensets FY26 (milestone).
  • Strategic Verticals: Data centers/gas (large US pipeline); WtE/Geo-thermal; motors expansion; railway (Germany/US). Exports to drive 40-50% CAGR (Asia/Europe/N. America).
  • Capacity Ramp: 3rd plant boosts execution for 20-25%+ annual growth; global facilities (Turkey/Europe/US) de-risk supply.
  • Medium-Term: ₹20,000+ Cr FY27+ feasible on energy transition (renewables/gas/oil&gas); hydro refurb boom; 98-country footprint expandable.
  • Valuation Upside: Strong ROIC; dividend yield ~1-2%; MFs increasing stake (Nippon, HDFC, ICICI).
