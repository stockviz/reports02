Analysis of Bafna Pharmaceuticals Limited (BAFNAPH)

Overview: Bafna Pharmaceuticals, a small-cap Indian pharma manufacturer (CIN: L24294TN1995PLC030698), reported unaudited H1 FY26 results (Apr-Sep 2025) showing flat-to-declining revenue but strong profit growth driven by cost efficiencies. Heavy capex signals expansion, but liquidity squeeze and regulatory overhangs persist. Single-segment focus on pharma formulations. Data from Q2/H1 FY26 filings and 30th AGM summary (Sep 2025).

Tailwinds (Positive Factors)

  • Profitability Surge: H1 PAT jumped 244% YoY to ₹651 lakhs (EPS ₹2.75 vs ₹0.80), despite 5% revenue dip. Q2 profit ₹312 lakhs (up from ₹262 lakhs YoY) via sharp cuts in material costs (10% down), manufacturing expenses (16% down), and other expenses. Margins expanding (PBT margin ~9.3% vs 2.6% YoY).
  • Balance Sheet Strength: Equity up 10% YoY to ₹9,123 lakhs; total assets +20% to ₹16,068 lakhs, driven by PPE (+20% to ₹6,500 lakhs).
  • Operational Cash Flow: Robust ₹931 lakhs from ops (despite WC drag), supporting capex.
  • Governance: Smooth 30th AGM (Sep 19, 2025) with all resolutions passed (e.g., director re-appointments, auditors); no red flags.

Headwinds (Negative Factors)

  • Revenue Pressure: H1 revenue -5% YoY to ₹7,009 lakhs; Q2 -12% to ₹3,547 lakhs. Attributed to lower exports/domestic sales amid competitive pricing or volume softness.
  • Liquidity Crunch: Cash plummets 93% to ₹25 lakhs (from ₹343 lakhs FY25-end); net cash burn ₹318 lakhs in H1. WC tied up in inventories (+10% YoY) and receivables.
  • Debt Build-Up: Total borrowings +50% YoY to ~₹3,240 lakhs; finance costs +39% to ₹177 lakhs, pressuring margins.
  • Receivables Overhang: Trade receivables steady at ₹3,257 lakhs (~46 days sales); overdue exports ₹213 lakhs (RBI violation per auditor).

Growth Prospects

  • Capex-Driven Expansion: ₹1,642 lakhs invested in PPE/CWIP (H1 outflow ₹1,491 lakhs total investing); CWIP surges to ₹1,467 lakhs (+7x YoY). Positions for higher volumes in formulations/exports.
  • Profit Momentum: If revenue rebounds (pharma sector tailwinds: India exports +10-15% CAGR), H2 could accelerate growth. FY25 full-year PAT base low (₹415 lakhs post-exceptionals).
  • Outlook: Management highlights (AGM) focus on FY26 performance; potential from single pharma segment amid domestic demand and US/EU filings.
