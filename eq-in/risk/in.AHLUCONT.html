Analysis of Ahluwalia Contracts (India) Ltd (AHLUCONT)

Company Overview: Ahluwalia Contracts (India) Ltd is an integrated EPC construction firm with 45+ years of experience in buildings (residential, commercial, hospitals), infrastructure (airports, railways), and urban projects. It has a strong order book of Rs. 18,058 Cr (as of Sep 2025, ~2.5-year execution visibility), diversified across segments (Residential: 44%, Infra: 22%, Commercial: 21%) and clients (Private: 69%, Govt: 30%). Pan-India presence (15 states + Nepal). FY25 revenue: Rs. 4,099 Cr; PAT: Rs. 202 Cr.

Guidance: 15-20% FY26 revenue growth; double-digit EBITDA margins; Rs. 8,000 Cr order inflows.

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Robust Order Book & Inflows: Rs. 18,058 Cr unexecuted (↑14% YoY); FY26 YTD inflows Rs. 4,521 Cr (+ strong pipeline of Rs. 6,500 Cr + L1 Rs. 1,620 Cr). Top-10 projects (e.g., CSMT Rs. 24.5 Bn, Jewelry Park Rs. 21.6 Bn) contribute ~63%.
  • Margin Expansion: Scale + mechanization (heavy cranes, batching plants, aluminum shuttering) driving 10%+ EBITDA. H2 historically stronger.
  • Diversification: Private sector dominance (DLF, Signature Global) with de-risked terms (interest-free advances, no BGs). Govt projects stable.
  • Execution Ramp-Up: Key projects like DLF Dahlias/Downtown, airports (Varanasi/Darbhanga), CSMT accelerating (30-40% revenue in FY26/27).
  • Strategic Investments: Digitization (SAP, Power BI), CAPEX Rs. 400 Cr FY26 (machinery for high-rises, M80 concrete). Labor mitigation via automation.
  • Industry Tailwinds: India construction market to $2.13 Tn by 2030 (CAGR 12%); real estate $1 Tn by 2030; NIP $1.4 Tn infra spend.
  • Balance Sheet Strength: Net cash Rs. 1,000 Cr+ (Rs. 615 Cr free); low debt (working capital days ↓ to 87); ROE ~11-12%.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • NCR Disruptions: ~45% order book (Rs. 8,084 Cr) exposed; graft ban halted work (mitigated by client labor retention, but H2 revenue/margins at risk).
  • Labor/Execution Issues: Shortages due to festivals (Diwali/Chhath), Bihar elections; skilled labor migration.
  • Cost Pressures: Employee costs ↑ (Q2: Rs. 121 Cr vs. Rs. 91 Cr in Q1 due to arrears/increments); depreciation steady at Rs. 20 Cr/qtr (to rise with CAPEX).
  • Competition: Intense in govt bids; private orders being refused to maintain 60:40 mix.
  • Working Capital: Rs. 70 Cr GST stuck (Maharashtra/Assam); retention Rs. 418 Cr, unbilled Rs. 552 Cr.

Growth Prospects

  • Near-Term (FY26): 15-20% revenue growth to ~Rs. 4,700-4,900 Cr (H2 stronger). Order inflows Rs. 8,000 Cr (on track). Revenue from new projects: Gems/Jewelry (30-35% in FY27), Dahlias (Rs. 100-125 Cr FY26), CSMT (Rs. 400 Cr FY26).
  • Medium-Term (FY27+): Similar inflows/growth; 2x scale in 2-3 years via digitization, talent hiring (50-60 engineers/year), high-rise focus. Private realty steady (no downturn); expansion in Bihar/Assam/Bengal/IT campuses.
  • Long-Term: Maintain 15-20% CAGR; diversify adjacencies (tech tie-ups/acquisitions in 2-3 years). Order book to sustain 4-5x revenue visibility.

Risks (NCR/labor) contained; tailwinds from execution ramp + industry boom outweigh. Stock likely to re-rate on sustained double-digit margins/order wins. Monitor H2 NCR resolution. Buy/Hold for growth investors (Target multiples: 15-20x FY27 EPS).
