Comprehensive Analysis of Solex Energy Limited (SOLEX)

Stock Symbol: SOLEX
Date of Analysis: Based on Disclosures up to November 14, 2025

SUMMARY OVERVIEW

Solex Energy Limited has transitioned from a small EPC provider and OEM manufacturer into a leading Indian solar PV module manufacturer with a 4 GW fully automated module facility in Surat, Gujarat. The company is currently scaling vertically into high-efficiency N-Type TOPCon cell manufacturing and has achieved key financial, operational, and strategic milestones in FY26.

Despite short-term headwinds such as extended monsoon delays and working capital pressure, Solex is on track for strong H2 performance driven by a robust order book (>₹4,000 crore), full capacity utilization post-Q2 FY26, and improved margins. The company recently migrated from NSE Emerge to the NSE Main Board, signaling maturity and investor confidence.

TAILWINDS (Favorable Factors)

1. Strong Financial Performance & Margin Expansion

  • H1 FY26 Revenue: ₹4,157 Cr → +51.6% YoY
  • PAT Growth: ₹305 Cr → +133.2% YoY
  • EBITDA Margin: Expanded to 14.7% (from 9.6% FY25)
  • PAT Margin: Improved to 7.3% (from 4.8%)
  • Driven by economies of scale, shift from OEM to in-house brands, and operational efficiencies.

2. Successful Capacity Scale-Up

  • Commissioned Line-3 and Line-4 (2.5 GW capacity) in October 2025
  • Total module capacity now: 4 GW (fully operational)
  • All lines now running → expect higher volume, better absorption, improved cash flow in H2
  • H2 revenue visibility is high due to ₹153 Cr finished inventory (to be delivered Q3 FY26)

3. Robust Order Book & Revenue Pipeline

  • Order Book: >₹4,000 Crore as of Sep 30, 2025
    • Includes ₹100 Cr in EPC orders
    • Major portion to be executed by March 2026 (₹1,450 Cr)
  • Includes large domestic supply order worth ₹544.62 Cr from Zelestra Group (India entities)
  • Strong presence with marquee IPPs and C&I customers

4. Vertical Integration Strategy: Entry into Cell Manufacturing

  • Plans to build 2.2 GW N-Type TOPCon Plus solar cell plant by March 2027
  • R&D collaboration with ISC Konstanz (Germany) – world-renowned PV R&D institute
  • Strategic technology partnership to ramp-up efficiency, avoid trial-and-error, and reduce time-to-market
  • Long-term ambition: Develop Indianized rear-contact cell technology, targeting global export markets

5. Favorable Policy & Market Tailwinds

  • India’s solar installations rising from 8–10 GW/year (pre-2019) to ~40 GW/year (FY26)
  • All modules now sourced domestically (from 5% domestic in 2019 → 100% in 2025)
  • Government focus on domestic value chain under schemes like PLA (Production Linked Incentive)
  • Battery Energy Storage Systems (BESS) projected to double solar demand in 5–7 years
  • Repowering of old plants (PERC → N-Type) to drive replacement demand
  • Export opportunities opening in US & Europe – India expected to get quotas; exports rising from 500 MW (2019) to 5–7 GW (2025)

6. Successful Migration to NSE Main Board

  • October 8, 2025: Officially migrated from NSE Emerge to Main Board
  • Increases visibility, investor base, liquidity, and credibility
  • Validation of scaled operations and financial stability

7. Funding & Capital Structure Clarity

  • Planning ₹1,500 crore capex for cell + module + working capital
    • ₹1,100 Cr (cell line), ₹200 Cr (new module line), ₹100 Cr (WC)
  • Funding plan:
    • ₹1,000 Cr debt, ₹500 Cr via QIP (equity)
    • QIP in advanced discussion – will increase market cap
HEADWINDS (Challenges)

1. Extended Monsoon Disrupted Q2 & H1 Delivery Cycles

  • Monsoon extended to ~6 months (vs. normal ~4 months)
  • Site readiness delays for IPP projects → inventory piled up (₹153 Cr finished goods)
  • Impacted revenue recognition in H1 despite strong production
  • Working capital days increased from 61 (FY25) to 102 (H1 FY26)
  • But temporary issue: deliveries commenced; recovery expected in Q3–Q4

2. Sequential Decline in Q2 Revenue

  • H1 FY26: ₹4,157 Cr
    • Q1 FY26: ₹2,610 Cr → Q2 FY26: ₹1,546 Cr (-40.8% QoQ)
  • Explained by delayed dispatches, and post-GST reduction confusion (GST dropped from 12% to 5% on Sept 21, 2025)
  • Not a demand issue – more of cyclical timing mismatch

3. Future Capacity & Site Expansion Uncertainty

  • Company planning +2.5 GW of module capacity
    • Options: Expand existing facility, co-locate with new cell plant, or shift to southern India (to reduce logistics cost)
  • Final site not yet confirmed → could delay execution
  • Potential execution risk if infra (power/water) not secured timely

4. Competition in Module Manufacturing

  • Indian module capacity has scaled rapidly
  • Many small, unviable PERC module players may exit → but could become short-term discounters
  • N-Type transition is inevitable, giving early movers like Solex an edge, but requires significant investment

RECOMMENDATION & OUTLOOK

✅ Positive Outlook (BUY / ACCUMULATE)

  • H2 FY26 is key inflection – expect strong revenue surge (Q3–Q4) and margin improvement
  • Valuation uplift post Main Board listing – improved liquidity and institutional interest
  • Strategic cell project aligns with national priority; potential for long-term competitive moat
  • Export & repowering offer multi-year demand visibility

⚠️ Monitor Points:

  1. Timely dispatch of ₹153 Cr inventory and COGS conversion in H2
  2. QIP execution – clarity on timing and pricing
  3. Water & power confirmation for cell plant site
  4. Actual margin delivery in H2 – target: 16–18% EBITDA

Conclusion

Solex Energy is at an inflection point – transitioning from a modular manufacturer to a technology-integrated solar leader with deep vertical scalability. While short-term delivery delays have weighed on H1, the fundamentals are strong, and H2 looks set for a major rebound.

The company’s first-mover edge in N-Type, global R&D partnerships, and export vision position it well for the next phase of India’s solar revolution. With prudent execution, SOLEX can emerge as a top-tier solar integrated player in India.

Analyst View: Accumulate for medium to long-term – strong growth runway supported by policy, technology, and demand tailwinds.
