Analysis of Borosil Renewables Limited (BORORENEW)

Borosil Renewables is India’s largest solar glass manufacturer (~1,000 TPD / 6.5 GW capacity), focusing on textured/tempered glass for PV modules. Q2 FY26 showed robust standalone growth (sales +42% YoY to ₹378 Cr, EBITDA margin 33.2%), driven by domestic solar boom, but consolidated results reflect European impairments.

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Explosive Domestic Solar Demand: FY26 installations ~35 GW (glass need ~50 GW); module capacity at 110 GW (to 150 GW by FY27). Renewables at 39% of India’s 501 GW capacity; solar target 280 GW by 2030.
  • Protective Policies: ALMM (Apr 2024) mandates local modules/glass; 5-yr ADD on China/Vietnam imports (Dec 2024) floors prices; schemes like PM Surya Ghar (rooftop), PM-KUSUM, PLI boost localization.
  • Pricing Power & Efficiency: ASP ₹147.5/sq.m (+29% YoY); near-full utilization (99% Q2); captive power savings; innovations (2mm tempered glass, AR/AS coatings, antimony-free).
  • Funded Expansion: 600 TPD (~4 GW) by Dec 2026 (total 1,600 TPD / 10.5 GW); ₹950 Cr capex funded by preferential issues (₹889 Cr raised YTD, more post-quarter).
  • Fund Raises: Recent allotments (Oct 2025: ₹371 Cr equity) strengthen balance sheet.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • European Subsidiary Woes: GMB insolvency (Jul 2025, deconsolidated); Interfloat impairments (₹34 Cr Q2, total ₹58 Cr exposure); overseas EBITDA negative (₹7.7 Cr loss Q2).
  • Persistent Imports: 70% glass imported despite ADD; module inventory build-up (monsoon/GST delays).
  • Margin Pressures: Other expenses up QoQ (one-offs, overseas costs); global oversupply from China erodes module margins (though not yet glass).
  • Capex Delays: European furnace rebuilds/additions faced issues; manpower shortages limit aggressive scaling.

Growth Prospects

  • Volume-Led Expansion: Capacity to >double by CY26; domestic supply gap (17 GW vs. 50 GW demand) positions for import substitution.
  • Margin Sustainability: Management confident of 30%+ EBITDA (efficiencies, power savings); exports ~12% (high-margin).
  • Sector Tailwinds: Rooftop/utility growth; bifacial/glass-glass modules favor thinner glass (Borosil’s 2mm edge).
  • 3-5 Yr Outlook: Revenue/EBITDA compounding at 40-50%+ if solar hits 30 GW+/yr; market cap ~₹9,800 Cr offers leverage.

Key Risks

Risk Category Description Mitigation
Operational Expansion delays (1.5-yr lead time); skilled labor shortages. On-schedule progress; brownfield expansion.
Financial Further European write-offs (₹36 Cr H1 impairments); debt ₹20 Cr (low but rising capex). Funds raised; cash ₹2 Cr standalone.
Market New competition (Reliance 12 GW, others ~16 GW by 2026); China dumping despite ADD. First-mover; superior quality (91.7% transmission).
Policy/External ADD expiry (5 yrs); module price crash; monsoon/inventory delays. Robust demand outlook; 65% renewable power mix.
Execution Inorganic opportunities unconfirmed; no module backward integration. Focus on core glass; past M&A success (Interfloat).

Overall Outlook: Strong tailwinds from India’s solar push outweigh European drag. Standalone momentum sustainable; target 30%+ margins, 40%+ YoY growth FY26+. Monitor competition/execution. Buy/Hold for long-term solar play (rating: Positive).
