Analysis of The Anup Engineering Limited (ANUP)

Company Overview: ANUP is a leading Indian manufacturer of static process equipment (e.g., heat exchangers, vessels, reactors) serving Oil & Gas, Petrochemicals, Fertilizers, Hydrogen, and other sectors. Part of the Arvind Group, it reported strong Q2/H1 FY26 results (ended Sep 30, 2025): Consolidated Revenue ₹408 Cr (+20% YoY), EBITDA ₹92 Cr (22.5% margin), PAT ₹58 Cr (+3% YoY). Orderbook stands at ₹568 Cr (sufficient for 15-20% FY26 growth guidance).

Tailwinds

  • Robust Financial Performance & Margins: 20% YoY revenue/EBITDA growth with industry-leading 22%+ margins sustained. ROCE at 27% and 3x working capital turns reflect operational efficiency.
  • Strong Order Visibility: ₹568 Cr orderbook (as of Oct 31, 2025; incl. ₹49 Cr LOI) – 55% domestic (pick-up in demand), 41% exports (no US exposure, mitigating tariff risks). ₹850 Cr inquiry pipeline supports FY27 buildup.
  • Capacity Expansion: Phase-2(A) at Kheda plant commissioned (₹150-200 Cr annual potential, capacity till FY27). Phase-2(B) ready by Q3 FY26 end (₹100-150 Cr potential). Multi-plant setup (Ahmedabad, Kheda, Chennai) enhances scale.
  • Balanced Portfolio: Q2 revenue mix – Oil & Gas (42%), Petrochemicals (30%), Fertilizers (14%), Hydrogen (4%). Product mix healthy (Heat Exchangers 58%). Global licenses (e.g., Germany, Italy) and engineering capabilities (ASME, TEMA) bolster competitiveness.
  • Leadership Stability: Smooth succession – Punit Lalbhai elevated as Chairman; family/group backing ensures continuity.
  • No Material Disruptions: Graham agreement cessation has zero impact (no orders executed); pre-existing buyer-supplier ties continue.

Headwinds

  • Profitability Pressure: PAT flat/slightly down (-1.5% Q2 YoY, +3% H1) due to higher net interest costs (short-term debt up to ₹149 Cr from ₹21 Cr on Mar 2025) and tax normalization (25% vs. 14% prior due to ESOP benefits).
  • Working Capital Strain: Current liabilities down but short-term borrowings surged (transitory, tied to business growth/lower advances). Inventories/trade receivables up, impacting cash flows (negative operating cash flow in H1).
  • Sector Concentration: ~72% Q2 revenue from cyclical Oil & Gas/Petrochem; vulnerable to oil price volatility or capex delays.
  • Capex Intensity: Ongoing expansions (₹3.8 Cr H1 capex) strain free cash flow amid negative operating cash (-₹62 Cr consolidated H1).

Growth Prospects

  • Near-Term (FY26): 15-20% revenue growth on ₹568 Cr orderbook; EBITDA margins ~22%. Q3/rest-of-year execution to drive momentum.
  • Medium-Term (FY27+): ₹850 Cr inquiries + capacity ramps (total ₹350-500 Cr new potential) position for sustained 15-20% CAGR. Domestic/export balance (45:55) aids diversification; Hydrogen/Power sectors emerging.
  • Strategic Enablers: Debt-free core (net debt low), advanced tech (HTRI, ANSYS), clean rooms, U-stamp certifications, and proximity to ports (e.g., Mundra, Chennai) support export scaling. Arvind Group synergies for advanced materials.
  • Upside Catalysts: Inquiry conversion (decent strike rate), Phase-2(B) commissioning, domestic capex revival in energy/fertilizers.

Overall Summary

ANUP exhibits strong tailwinds from execution momentum, capacity buildout, and a de-risked orderbook, positioning it for 15-20% FY26 growth with sticky 22% margins – a standout in India’s heavy engineering space. Headwinds are largely transitory (WC/debt, tax optics), but sector cyclicality warrants monitoring. Bullish outlook with low downside risk (no major disruptions, healthy ROCE); stock likely supported by guidance reaffirmation and domestic/export balance. Investors should track Q3 order inflows and debt normalization for sustained re-rating. Valuation context: Trades at premium multiples justified by growth/margins (not provided in docs). Recommendation: Positive; accumulate on dips.
