Jai Balaji Industries Limited (JAIBALAJI) Analysis

Based on Q2/H1 FY26 Unaudited Results (ended 30 Sep 2025) and 26th AGM Voting Results (18 Sep 2025)

Jai Balaji Industries Ltd., a Kolkata-based steel manufacturer (primarily pig iron, billets, TMT bars, etc.), reported a sharp YoY decline in revenue and profitability for H1 FY26 amid steel sector headwinds. Standalone revenue fell 17% to ₹2,711 Cr (from ₹3,275 Cr in H1 FY25), with Q2 PBT at ₹40 Cr (down 81% YoY). Balance sheet remains solid with debt reduction and healthy equity (~₹2,222 Cr), but cash burn and high inventories signal caution. AGM resolutions (financials adoption, director re-appointments, AOA amendment) passed overwhelmingly (~99-100% approval), affirming promoter control (65% holding).

Key Risks - Commodity Volatility: Raw material (iron ore/coal) & steel price swings; high cost of materials (73% of expenses).

Overall Outlook: Short-term pressured by steel downcycle (headwinds dominate), but debt reduction and capex position for FY26 recovery. Monitor Q3 results for pricing rebound; trading at low multiples could offer upside if India infra sustains. Risks tilted toward cyclical volatility—suitable for tactical longs.
