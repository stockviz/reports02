Analysis of Dhunseri Tea & Industries Limited (DTIL)

Overview: DTIL, a tea producer with operations in India and subsidiaries in Africa (Malawi/Tanzania focus via Dhunseri Petrochem & Tea Pte Ltd), reported strong H1 FY26 results (ended Sep 30, 2025). Consolidated revenue grew ~8% YoY to ₹286 cr (India: ₹199 cr, ex-India: ₹87 cr), with PAT swinging to ₹35.5 cr (vs. ₹23 cr H1 FY25; FY25 full-year loss of ₹20 cr). Standalone India ops drove profitability (PAT ₹37.8 cr). Recent MOU to sell Balijan (North) Tea Estate (10% of FY25 revenue at ₹28 cr) for ₹35 cr (expected Mar 2026) signals portfolio rationalization. Results audited/reviewed with clean opinions.

Tailwinds

  • Robust India Performance: Peak tea season (Q2) delivered high margins (standalone PBT ₹42 cr on ₹199 cr rev). Employee/power costs controlled; inventories built for sales.
  • Asset Monetization: Exceptional gains from Deohall estate sale (₹2 cr); prior FY25 sales (Dilli/Jaipur: ₹27 cr total) boosted FY25. Balijan sale adds ₹35 cr cash, improving liquidity (standalone cash ops +₹1 cr).
  • Profitability Turnaround: Cons PAT margins ~12% H1 FY26 (vs. FY25 loss). Other equity up to ₹564 cr standalone.
  • Operational Efficiency: India segment results strong (₹41 cr profit H1); rationalization reduces low-margin assets.

Headwinds

  • Subsidiary Drag: Ex-India ops volatile (H1 profit ₹1.7 cr vs. FY25 ₹187 cr loss); hyperinflation in Malawi (Ind AS 29 loss ₹3.6 cr non-cash).
  • Seasonality & Costs: Tea cycle causes quarterly swings (Q1 weak); high employee expenses (₹157 cr cons H1, 55% of expenses), finance costs (₹101 cr).
  • Revenue Dip from Sales: Balijan divestment sheds 10% FY25 turnover; inventory changes volatile (negative ₹39 cr cons H1).
  • Debt Burden: Cons borrowings ₹234 cr (interest coverage ~3.5x); forex translation losses possible.

Growth Prospects

  • Core Tea Recovery: India focus post-divestments; H1 rev stable YoY despite sales. Full FY26 PAT could double FY25 if seasonality holds.
  • Cash Inflows: ₹35 cr from Balijan + prior disposals (₹24 cr investing cash H1 cons) fund capex (₹15 cr H1) or debt reduction.
  • International Upside: African estates (tea/coffee/macadamia) contribute 30% rev; hyperinflation adjustments stabilizing. Potential macadamia expansion (seasonal tail).
  • Margin Expansion: Rationalization targets profitability (prior sales improved FY25). EPS H1 cons ₹33.8 (vs. FY25 -₹19); annualize to ₹60+ possible.

Key Risks

  • Commodity/Agricultural: Tea price volatility, weather/climate impacts (e.g., unplucked leaves as biological assets volatile).
  • Geopolitical/Macro: Malawi hyperinflation/kwacha devaluation (subsidiaries 50%+ assets); forex exposure (cash flows show exchange adjustments).
  • Liquidity/Debt: Net debt high (borrowings down slightly but cash down 16% to ₹17 cr cons); financing outflow ₹34 cr H1.
  • Execution: Divestment delays (Balijan to Mar26); segment concentration (tea 100%, India 70% rev).
  • Regulatory: SEBI compliance (disclosures clean); related-party free but monitor.

Investment Summary: DTIL shows turnaround momentum via India strength and asset sales, positioning for FY26 profitability (target ₹50-70 cr PAT). Tailwinds from rationalization outweigh headwinds, but monitor African subs and debt. Positive for value investors; growth hinged on tea cycle/commodity recovery. Market cap context: ~₹500-600 cr implied (EPS upside). Hold/Buy on dips.
