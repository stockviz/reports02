Summary Analysis for Indian Oil Corporation Ltd. (IOC)

IOC, India’s largest oil refining & marketing company, reported robust Q2 FY26 (standalone PAT ₹7,610 Cr, up 34% QoQ; H1 PAT ₹13,299 Cr) driven by GRM expansion ($10.66/bbl in Q2, H1 avg $6.32/bbl) and high refinery utilization (99.5%). Consolidated results similarly strong (H1 PAT ₹14,999 Cr). All AGM resolutions passed (Aug 30, 2025), approving dividends (₹3/share), directors, auditors, RPTs, and guarantees. Below is a structured analysis of headwinds, tailwinds, growth prospects, and key risks based on filings.

Tailwinds (Supportive Factors)

  • Strong Operational Metrics: Refinery throughput 17.6 MMT (99.5% util, up from 106.7% Q1 due to optimization); distillate yield 79.3%; high-sulfur crude utilization 54%. Pipeline throughput stable at 24.1 MMT.
  • Financial Momentum: EBITDA ₹16,106 Cr (Q2); revenue resilience despite crude volatility. Dividend payout signals confidence. Debt/Equity stable at 0.68x.
  • Strategic Initiatives: “SPRINT” transformation project highlighted in investor deck; expanding gas (1.84 MMT sales), petrochemicals, and renewables (wind/solar mentions).
  • RPT Approvals: Shareholder nod for key JVs/subs (e.g., Petronet LNG, Adani Gas, Lanka IOC) ensures seamless FY26-27 transactions.
  • Market Position: Dominant PSU with refining capacity leadership; improving GRM amid global crack spreads.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Cost Pressures: Forex losses (crude liability -₹741 Cr, others -₹1,398 Cr in Q2); interest expense ₹2,169 Cr (up QoQ). Debt at ₹128,239 Cr (standalone).
  • Subsidy/Regulatory Drag: LPG buffer negative ₹25,768 Cr; pending MoP&NG compensation (₹14,486 Cr phased from Nov 2025). Defence deductions (₹690 Cr contested).
  • Volume Dip: Sales down QoQ (24.3 MMT vs 26.3 MMT); HSD/LPG soft.
  • Margin Volatility: Inventory gains/losses impacted GRM; core GRM $7.89/bbl (H1 post-adjustments).
  • Capex Intensity: CWIP ₹76,855 Cr signals heavy investments amid high debt service coverage (2.17x H1).

Growth Prospects

  • Refining/Petrochem Expansion: High utilizations + JVs (e.g., Cauvery Basin, Hindustan Urvarak) position for 5-10% volume growth. Petrochem sales steady (0.77 MMT H1).
  • Diversification: Gas/LNG via Petronet, Adani; green energy (NTPC JV, GPS Renewables); upstream (exploration JVs).
  • Export/ATF Upside: ATF sales 1.16 MMT; potential GRM tailwind if cracks sustain >$10/bbl.
  • Capex Pipeline: ₹30,000+ Cr annual outlay (implied from CWIP/debt); FY26 GRM guidance positive post-Q2 surge.
  • PSU Reforms: Govt nominee directors + auditor continuity; RPT approvals unlock JV synergies (e.g., ₹481 Cr guarantee for Indofast).

Overall Outlook: Bullish near-term on refining rebound (PAT tripled YoY Q2 est.); cautious medium-term on debt/subsidies. Target multiple expansion if GRM >$8-10/bbl sustained; monitor oil prices (~$70-80/bbl). Stock trades at ~7x FY26E EV/EBITDA (peer avg 6-8x).
