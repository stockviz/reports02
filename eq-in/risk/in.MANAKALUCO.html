Summary Analysis for MANAKALUCO (Manaksia Aluminium Company Limited)

Company Overview: MANAKALUCO is an aluminium products manufacturer (single segment: Metals) listed on BSE (Scrip: 539045) and NSE (Symbol: MANAKALUCO). H1 FY26 (Apr-Sep 2025) showed YoY revenue growth of 14% to ₹26,562 Lacs and PAT up 11% to ₹267 Lacs, but QoQ weakness in Q2 and high debt remain concerns. Promoter holding ~74% (strong control). Recent AGM (Sep 2025) approved 7% dividend, director re-appointments, and auditors.

Tailwinds (Positive Factors)

  • YoY Financial Recovery: H1 revenue +14%, PBT +9%, driven by operational efficiencies despite cost pressures. Margins stable (PBT margin ~1.3% H1).
  • Strategic Expansions: New wholly-owned subsidiaries – Manaksia Aluminium Inc. (USA, Aug 2024) and Metal Star Ceiling Panel FZE (UAE, Jul 2025) – signal diversification into international markets. UAE sub already commenced operations (self-funded via customer advances).
  • Capex Momentum: CWIP at ₹2,853 Lacs; PPE at ₹12,244 Lacs supports capacity growth.
  • Shareholder Confidence: Overwhelming AGM resolutions (99.9%+ approval); 7% dividend signals stability.
  • Industry Backdrop: MD’s AGM speech highlighted positive aluminium outlook amid Indian economic growth.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • QoQ Weakness: Q2 revenue down 2% QoQ (₹13,121 Lacs) and PBT down 31% to ₹141 Lacs, due to higher material costs (+15% YoY in Q2) and inventory changes.
  • High Leverage & Costs: Finance costs at ₹1,412 Lacs H1 (41% of PBT); total borrowings ₹26,704 Lacs (debt/equity ~1.9x). Interest coverage strained (~1.2x H1).
  • Cash Burn: Operating cash flow negative ₹1,151 Lacs H1 (inventory up ₹2,759 Lacs, working capital drag ₹3,282 Lacs); cash equivalents critically low at ₹13 Lacs.
  • No Consolidation: Subsidiaries not consolidated due to pending RBI ODI approvals, masking true group performance.

Growth Prospects

  • Medium-Term International Play: USA/UAE subs position company for exports/ceilings panels; UAE ops already active. Potential revenue boost post-RBI nod and consolidation.
  • Domestic Aluminium Demand: Capacity utilization improvements via capex; FY25 full-year revenue base of ₹51,037 Lacs provides scale.
  • EPS Trajectory: H1 EPS ₹0.41 (up from ₹0.37 YoY); annualizing to ~₹0.82 suggests room for 10-15% growth if debt refinanced.
  • Outlook: Aluminium sector tailwinds from infra/auto demand in India; target 15-20% revenue CAGR if global ops ramp up by FY27.

Key Risks

Stock suitable for long-term investors betting on international pivot; monitor Q3 debt metrics and RBI updates. Target P/B ~1x (current book value ~₹210/share); debt reduction key catalyst.
