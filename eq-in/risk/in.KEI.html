II. Headwinds (Challenges & Concerns)

1. Volatility in Raw Material Prices

  • KEI is highly exposed to copper and aluminum price fluctuations, which form a major portion of input costs.
  • While cost pass-through mechanisms exist, sudden spikes can compress margins unless hedged effectively.
  • Although exports provide some FX hedge, input cost volatility remains a key operational challenge.

2. Slowing LT Cable Growth vs.¬†High Base

  • LT cables, the largest product segment (INR 38,870 Mn in FY25), saw strong growth due to real estate and retail, but housing sector softness may slow future ramp-up.
  • Margins in LT/Building wires are typically lower than EHV, limiting upside unless mix shifts favor high-margin products.

3. EHV Market Competition & Execution Risk

  • While KEI aims to gain share in the EHV segment, it faces stiff competition from established players like Polycab, Sterlite Tech, and Havells.
  • EHV projects are capital-intensive, longer-cycle, and involve rigorous pre-qualification norms.
  • FY25 EHV revenue dipped to INR 4,040 Mn from INR 6,594 Mn (FY24), indicating possible project lull or bidding pressure.

4. Working Capital Pressures

  • High inventory (INR 21,060 Mn in H1 FY26) and trade receivables (INR 16,633 Mn) suggest working capital intensity.
  • Order book growth is positive, but project-based sales can delay collections, impacting cash conversion cycles.
  • Despite strong cash balances, sustained working capital outflow may increase short-term borrowing needs.

5. Commercialization Risk of New Sanand Plant

  • While trial production has begun, full commercial ramp-up by December 10, 2025 (as announced) carries execution risk.
  • Delays in product certification, manpower ramp-up, or client acceptance could defer ROI on new capex.
