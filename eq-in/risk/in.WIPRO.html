Tailwinds

  • Acquisition Synergies: DTS (Harman) completed for ~USD 375 Mn; adds ER&D, embodied AI, embedded software, device engineering. Integrates with Wipro Intelligence™ for AI-led engineering services; enhances go-to-market in auto/tech sectors.
  • Revenue Momentum: IT Services +1.4% YoY H1; sequential Q-o-Q growth. Strong Americas 1/APMEA; total segment revenue +1.2% YoY.
  • Profitability & Cash: Margins stable (~14.7% PBT H1); net profit +5.4% YoY. Robust op. cash flow supports M&A/dividends.
  • Engagement: Analyst/investor meets (Nov-Dec 2025) signal confidence; bonus shares (1:1 FY25) boost liquidity.
  • Cost Discipline: Sub-contracting/facility expenses controlled; insurance claims offset other expenses.

Headwinds

  • Segment Weakness: Europe (-3.7% YoY Q2), Americas 2 flat/slight decline; signals softer demand in BFSI/hi-tech.
  • Cost Pressures: Employee expenses ~60% of costs (₹270 Bn H1, +1.3% YoY); lifetime ECL ₹2 Bn (up sharply).
  • Cash Flow Dip: Op. cash -9% YoY due to working capital (unbilled receivables +₹10 Bn); financing cash outflow from debt repayment/dividends.
  • OCI Volatility: H1 net OCI +₹17 Bn (FX hedges/translation gains), but hedges show losses (e.g., forwards -₹2.4 Bn).
  • Restructuring: Q1 costs ₹2.5 Bn; past impairments (₹1.2 Bn FY25 on intangibles).

Growth Prospects

  • High: Engineering/ER&D via DTS (scale AI innovation, client wins in auto/consumer); IT Services expansion in Americas 1/APMEA (digital transformation, cloud/AI demand).
  • Medium: Global footprint (230K+ employees, 65 countries); Wipro Intelligence™ platforms for co-innovation. H1 revenue +1.3% sets base for FY26 double-digit IT Services growth if macros improve.
  • Catalysts: M&A pipeline (DTS-like); segment rebalancing (Americas growth >20% of revenue); strong cash (₹131 Bn) for buybacks/investments.
  • Outlook: Analysts expect 5-8% FY26 revenue CAGR; EPS growth via margins (target 15-16%) and share buybacks.

Key Risks

  • Demand/Macro: Client concentration, IT spending slowdown (e.g., Europe/BFSI); wage inflation/talent attrition in India.
  • Execution: DTS integration (cultural/tech); rising borrowings (₹129 Bn) amid high interest (₹7.2 Bn H1).
  • FX/Volatility: Hedges expose to USD/INR swings (evident in OCI); 70%+ revenue international.
  • Competition/Regulatory: Intense rivalry (TCS/Infosys/Accenture); immigration curbs, geopolitical tensions (e.g., subsidiaries in Ukraine/Russia).
  • Other: Cyber/IP theft, ECL provisions on receivables (₹119 Bn); economic slowdown impacting capex.

Overall Summary: Wipro shows resilient H1 growth (+1.3% revenue, +5% profit) amid steady IT Services and DTS tailwind for AI/engineering pivot. Headwinds from regional softness/costs temper momentum, but strong balance sheet/cash supports M&A. Growth prospects solid (8-10% FY26 revenue) if execution delivers; monitor Europe recovery and DTS synergies. Rating Outlook: Neutral-Positive (stable macros, acquisition upside). Risks skewed to demand/FX.
