Analysis of Kothari Sugars and Chemicals Ltd (NSE: KOTARISUG) – H1 FY26 (Apr-Sep 2025)

Financial Summary

  • Revenue: Declined 29.5% YoY to ₹13,473 lakhs (H1 FY25: ₹19,121 lakhs). Q2 FY26 revenue at ₹6,804 lakhs (down 33% YoY).
  • Profitability: Net loss of ₹598 lakhs (H1 FY25 profit: ₹571 lakhs). Q2 FY26 loss: ₹723 lakhs. EPS: -₹0.72 (basic).
  • Segments: | Segment | H1 Revenue (₹ lakhs) | YoY Chg | H1 Segment Result (₹ lakhs) | |———–|———————-|———|—————————–| | Sugar | 7,068 | -42% | -1,821 (loss widened) | | Distillery| 5,899 | -10% | +1,618 (profit down 47%) | | Power | 1,493 | -17% | -207 (loss vs minor loss) |
  • Balance Sheet: Assets down 7% to ₹41,917 lakhs (inventory -33% to ₹6,535 lakhs). Equity stable at ₹28,330 lakhs. Net debt stable ~₹4,794 lakhs.
  • Cash Flow: Operating cash flow negative ₹852 lakhs (H1 FY25: +₹7,982 lakhs) due to working capital outflow (trade payables -32%). Investing cash inflow ₹1,229 lakhs from investments.
  • Key Note: Lower sugar crushing due to deficit rainfall & pest attack; exceptional income ₹156 lakhs (Q1 reversal of power provision).

Headwinds (Negative Factors)

  • Supply Constraints: Sugarcane availability down sharply (deficit rainfall, pests), causing 42% YoY sugar revenue drop and widened losses (-₹1,821 lakhs in H1).
  • Revenue & Profit Decline: Core sugar segment (60% revenue) in deep loss; overall H1 loss driven by high inventory changes (+₹2,601 lakhs expense) and unallocable costs.
  • Cash Burn: Negative operating cash flow signals liquidity strain; reliant on investment sales for inflows.
  • Seasonality Exposed: Q2 (peak crushing) underperformed, indicating structural weakness vs. prior strong H1 FY25.

Tailwinds (Positive Factors)

  • Inventory Reduction: -33% YoY to ₹6,535 lakhs, improving working capital efficiency post FY25 highs.
  • Distillery Resilience: Remains profitable (+₹1,618 lakhs), contributing ~90% of segment profits; stable demand for ethanol/power co-products.
  • One-off Gains: Exceptional income (₹156 lakhs) and investment income (₹652 lakhs other income) cushioned Q1 loss.
  • Debt Control: Borrowings stable; deferred tax liability down 9%.

Growth Prospects

  • Medium-Term Recovery: Sugar crushing could rebound in H2 FY26/H1 FY27 if monsoon normalizes (historical cyclicality). Distillery expansion potential amid India’s ethanol blending push (govt target 20% by 2025).
  • Diversification: Power segment has upside if co-gen capacity utilized better (prior FY25 profits ₹217 lakhs). High investments (₹11,580 lakhs current/non-current) could yield dividends/interest.
  • Capex: ₹1,150 lakhs invested in FY26 H1 (CWIP up to ₹1,047 lakhs), signaling plant upgrades for efficiency.
  • Outlook: FY26 full-year revenue could stabilize at ₹30,000-35,000 lakhs if sugar recovers; EPS breakeven possible with volume rebound. Long-term tailwind from sugar export quotas/ethanol policy.

Key Risks

  • Agricultural Dependency: Weather/pest/govt cane pricing policies could prolong sugar weakness (80%+ revenue from agri-commodities).
  • Commodity Volatility: Sugar price crashes or ethanol price cuts (distillery risk).
  • Liquidity & Debt: Negative op-cash flow; rising finance costs (₹313 lakhs H1); overdraft reliance (negative cash equivalents ₹4,773 lakhs).
  • Operational: High fixed costs (depn ₹725 lakhs); segmental losses eroding equity (down 2%).
  • Regulatory: SEBI compliance, power subsidies reversal (past exceptional item), GST/input tax risks.
  • Macro: Deficit rainfall persisting into FY27; no subsidiaries limits inorganic growth.

Overall Summary

Bearish Short-Term: Severe headwinds from sugarcane shortage led to 30% revenue drop and H1 loss; cash flow weakness flags liquidity risk. Cautious Optimism Medium-Term: Distillery strength and inventory unwind provide buffers; growth hinges on monsoon recovery and ethanol demand. Recommendation: Hold for value investors eyeing cyclical rebound (trading at low multiples implied by equity base); monitor Q3 crushing volumes. Key watch: Sugarcane arrivals, sugar prices, op-cash flow inflection. (Analysis based solely on provided Q2/H1 FY26 results; cross-check latest market data.)
