Summary Analysis of Inox Wind Limited (INOXWIND)

Inox Wind Limited (IWL), a leading Indian wind energy solutions provider (part of INOXGFL Group), reported robust Q2/H1 FY26 results with record execution (348 MW H1, on track for >1.2 GW FY26) and strong financials (H1 revenue +45% YoY to ₹2,025 Cr, EBITDA +44% to ₹491 Cr, PAT +61% to ₹218 Cr). Order book stands at >3.2 GW, with subsidiaries Inox Green (12.5 GW O&M portfolio) and Inox Renewable Solutions driving synergies. Below is a structured analysis of headwinds, tailwinds, growth prospects, and key risks based on the provided documents (earnings call, presentation, financials, disclosures, press releases).

Tailwinds (Positive Factors)

  • Strong Financial & Operational Momentum: Best-ever Q2 despite monsoons (202 MW execution); H1 EBITDA margins >22% (guidance 18-19%). Net cash balance sheet (₹222 Cr as of Sep’25); all NCRPS eliminated post-rights issue/merger.
  • Favorable Policy Environment: GST on wind components cut to 5%; ALMM mandates domestic sourcing (75-80% components); CERC amendments enable hybrid ISTS projects. Wind complements solar for grid stability; hybrid/RTC/FDRE tenders rising (3 GW wind/FDRE awarded in 7M FY26).
  • Sector Growth: India targets 122 GW wind (from 53 GW); repowering (25 GW), offshore VGF (1 GW), green H2 (125 GW RE add). H1 FY26 added 3.1 GW wind nationally.
  • Group Synergies: INOXGFL integration (solar modules, IPP via Inox Clean); Inox Green as largest O&M player; substation demerger approved (eliminates ₹50-55 Cr annual depreciation).
  • Backward Integration: In-house nacelles, transformers, cranes; new South India blade/tower plant (2026).

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Execution Seasonality: H1 only 29-30% of FY26 target (monsoon impact); past FY24/FY25 shortfalls raised concerns. H2 ramp-up critical (70% execution).
  • Regulatory Scrutiny: SEBI administrative warning to CS/Compliance Officer for not closing trading window on Mar’25 153 MW order (PIT Regulations violation; no penalty).
  • Input Cost Pressures: Aluminium/copper inflation; limited pass-through in some orders (mitigated via pricing ~₹8 Cr/MW turnkey).
  • Working Capital: Targeting 120 days; incomplete sets in receivables/inventory (no quantification provided).
  • Order Inflow Moderation: 380 MW H1 inflows; tender pipeline >3 GW but selective bidding due to risk management (e.g., PPA cancellations/rebidding).

Growth Prospects

  • Near-Term Visibility: 3.2 GW order book (18-24 months); framework agreements for >1 GW annual recurring (group IPP: 500-700 MW; others). FY26 close with strong net book; >2 GW execution FY27.
  • Strategic Partnerships: MoU with KP Energy for 2.5 GW wind/hybrid development (IWL supplies WTGs/EPC/O&M; KP handles BoP).
  • Product Expansion: 4X MW turbine launch (low-wind optimized); 3.3 MW ramp-up.
  • Subsidiary Upside: Inox Green profitability to multiply FY27 (post-consolidation of 6.5 GW assets; demerger boosts ROE/ROCE). Inox Renewable listing to unlock EPC value.
  • Market Opportunity: ₹5 Tn wind OEM market; C&I demand; hybrid shift (FDRE/RTC). Targeting 10 GW annual India additions.

Overall Outlook: Bullish with strong tailwinds from policy/sector revival and execution track record. Growth anchored in order visibility/recurring inflows, but H2 execution and regulatory resolutions critical. Target FY26 margins 18-19%; stock poised for re-rating on 2+ GW scale-up. Investors should monitor Q3 execution and SPV outcomes.
