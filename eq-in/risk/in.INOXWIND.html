Summary Analysis of Inox Wind Limited (INOXWIND)

Inox Wind (IWL), a leading Indian wind turbine manufacturer and integrated solutions provider (part of INOXGFL Group), reported robust Q2/H1 FY26 results amid favorable sector tailwinds. Key metrics: Revenue ₹2,025 Cr (+45% YoY H1), EBITDA ₹491 Cr (+44% YoY, ~24% margin vs. 18-19% guidance), PAT ₹218 Cr (+61% YoY), execution 348 MW (on track for 1.2 GW FY26), order book >3.2 GW (56% turnkey). Net cash ₹222 Cr post-rights issue. Subsidiaries (Inox Green: 12.5 GW O&M portfolio; Inox Renewable Solutions: EPC ramp-up) add value. Recent MoU with KP Energy for 2.5 GW development signals recurring inflows.

Tailwinds (Positive Factors)

  • Strong Execution & Financial Momentum: Best-ever Q2 (202 MW executed despite monsoons); H1 ahead of FY26 guidance (30-35% annual execution in H1 achieved). Backward integration (nacelles, transformers, cranes) and royalty savings (~₹6L/MW) drive margins >22% in H1.
  • Robust Order Visibility: 3.2+ GW book (diversified: PSUs like NTPC/NLC, IPPs like ReNew/Hero, group IPP); 380 MW inflows H1; >3 GW tender pipeline; frameworks for >1 GW annual recurring (500-700 MW from group IPP).
  • Subsidiary Synergies: Inox Green (56% stake) at 12.5 GW O&M (revenue ₹130 Cr Q2, +101% YoY); demerger of substations to list Inox Renewable Solutions (unlocks EPC value).
  • Sector Boost: Policy tailwinds (GST 5% on components, ALMM mandating domestic sourcing, hybrid/FDRE tenders ~3 GW awarded 7M FY26); India wind target 122 GW (from 53 GW); repowering/offshore potential.
  • Group Ecosystem: INOXGFL integration (solar modules, IPP, chemicals for BESS/H2) enables hybrids; MoU with KP Energy for 2.5 GW (IWL supplies WTGs/EPC/O&M).
  • Balance Sheet Strength: Debt-free (net cash); FY26 capex ₹200 Cr; rights issue funds deployed.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Execution Variability: Past shortfalls (FY24/FY25 < guidance); H2 ramp-up steep (70% of 1.2 GW); monsoon impacts noted.
  • Working Capital Pressure: 120-day cycle targeted; incomplete sets/inventory tied up (details in Clause 41); turnkey cash lags commissioning.
  • Regulatory Scrutiny: SEBI administrative warning to CS/Compliance Officer for trading window lapse on Mar’25 order announcement (no penalty, but compliance emphasis).
  • Commodity Inflation: Al/Cu price rises; limited pass-through in some orders (mitigated via escalations).
  • Order Inflows Lagging Peers: Selective bidding (robust risk process); H1 inflows 380 MW vs. sector leader consistency.

Growth Prospects

  • Near-Term (FY26-27): 1.2 GW+ execution; close FY26 with “very strong” net book (18-24 months visibility); EBITDA margins sustained 18-19%+ via integrations.
  • Medium-Term: >2 GW annual capacity (FY27 target); 1 GW+ recurring via frameworks/group IPP; 4X MW turbine launch; South India blade/tower plant (2026, ₹400 Cr capex).
  • Long-Term: Multi-decadal wind story (695 GW onshore potential); hybrids/RTC/FDRE dominance; Inox Green to India’s largest O&M (~17 GW in 2 yrs); group IPP/solar/BESS expansion; KP MoU scales to 2.5 GW.
  • Value Unlocks: Inox Renewable listing post-demerger; O&M portfolio growth (organic/inorganic).

Overall Outlook: Positive. Strong fundamentals, sector tailwinds, and partnerships position IWL for 2 GW+ scale. Risks are manageable (execution-focused), with net cash buffer. Monitor H2 execution and inflows for upgrades. Target upside from recurring orders and listings.
