Summary Analysis for AERONEU (Aeroflex Neu Limited)

Aeroflex Neu Limited (formerly Sah Polymers Limited; CIN: L24201RJ1992PLC006657), a Rajasthan-based manufacturer in the polymers/plastics/chemicals sector (focused on industrial products like polyweave/fabrics), reported solid profitability growth in Q2/H1 FY26 (ended Sep 30, 2025) despite revenue contraction. Consolidated Q2 revenue was ₹345 Cr (standalone ₹268 Cr), with PAT of ₹107 lakhs (EPS ₹0.40 basic). H1 consolidated PBT jumped to ₹200 lakhs (vs. ₹81 lakhs YoY), driven by cost efficiencies. Balance sheet remains equity-heavy (69% of assets), bolstered by ₹162 Cr upfront from 7.2 Cr convertible warrants (at ₹90/warrant; 25% paid). However, cash flows highlight liquidity pressures. Below is a structured analysis of tailwinds, headwinds, growth prospects, and key risks.

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Profitability Surge: Q2 consolidated PBT/PAT at ₹107 lakhs (up sharply from standalone trends); H1 PBT +147% YoY to ₹200 lakhs. Margins improved via lower material costs (₹2045 lakhs, ~59% of revenue) and controlled expenses despite other expenses at ₹1062 lakhs. Standalone EPS steady at ₹0.39 (Q2).
  • Capital Infusion: ₹162 Cr received from warrants (allotted Jul 2025; convertible in 18 months). Boosts “Other Equity” to ₹7943 lakhs (consolidated), funding capex/debt reduction.
  • Debt Moderation: Total borrowings down to ₹2830 lakhs (non-current ₹424 lakhs stable); repaid ₹200 lakhs in H1. Interest costs manageable at ₹61 lakhs (Q2).
  • Asset Quality: Strong equity base (₹10522 lakhs consolidated); PPE at ₹5075 lakhs signals capex continuity. Subsidiary (Fibcorp Polyweave) adds ₹1031 lakhs Q2 revenue, diversifying group ops.
  • Auditor Comfort: Unmodified limited review reports from H R Jain & Co.; compliant with Ind AS 34/Reg 33.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Revenue Decline: Consolidated Q2 ops ₹3452 lakhs, but standalone H1 ops -6.5% YoY (₹5163 Cr vs. ₹5535 Cr). Q2 standalone ops down 14% YoY (₹2684 Cr vs. ₹3133 Cr), signaling weak demand/seasonality in polymers sector.
  • Cash Burn: Consolidated H1 operating cash flow -₹337 lakhs (standalone -₹72 lakhs), driven by working capital outflows (e.g., loans +₹1223 lakhs increase, inventories -₹20 lakhs). Reliant on financing inflows.
  • High Expenses: Other expenses ₹1062 lakhs (29% of Q2 revenue); employee costs ₹263 lakhs up. Forex unrealized gains helped (₹147 lakhs), but volatile.
  • Liquidity Strain: Net cash equivalents negative post cash credit adjustments (-₹286 lakhs H1 change); large FD movements (₹1386 lakhs outflow) indicate parking excess funds amid ops weakness.

Growth Prospects

  • High: Warrant proceeds (potential ₹648 Cr full conversion) position for expansion in polymers (e.g., capex ₹97 lakhs H1; CWIP ₹5.7 lakhs). Improving PAT trajectory (H1 +147% YoY) amid cost discipline supports 15-20% EPS growth if revenue rebounds.
  • Medium-Term Catalysts: Subsidiary scaling (₹1031 lakhs Q2 revenue, ₹9.8 lakhs PAT); segment info (Annexure-1, not provided) likely shows industrial demand recovery. Rebranding to Aeroflex Neu and website updates signal strategic pivot/marketing push.
  • Outlook: FY26 revenue could stabilize/grow 10-15% if sector tailwinds (e.g., infra/plastics demand) emerge; equity raise de-risks balance sheet for M&A/capex.
