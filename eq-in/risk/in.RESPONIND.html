Analysis of Responsive Industries Limited (RESPONIND)

Responsive Industries Ltd., a plastics/polymers manufacturer focused on waterproofing membranes and related products (single operating segment per Ind AS 108), reported solid H1 FY26 (ended Sep 30, 2025) consolidated results amid revenue softness. Key metrics: Revenue down 2.5% YoY to ₹652 Cr (Q2 down 10% YoY); PAT up 5.6% to ₹103 Cr; Total Comprehensive Income up 26% to ₹126 Cr (boosted by ₹23 Cr OCI, likely revaluation/forex gains). Standalone PAT modest at ₹9 Cr (group profits driven by foreign subs). Balance sheet expanded 7% to ₹1,860 Cr (equity up 9.5%), with debt reduction. Cash from ops at ₹48 Cr (down YoY). Clean limited review by Shah & Taparia; AGM resolutions passed unanimously.

Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts)

  • Profit Resilience & Margin Expansion: Expenses fell 3.4% YoY (better material costs, inventory management), driving PBT growth (+3%) despite revenue dip. Gross margins implied strength in traded goods/core plastics.
  • Infra Project Wins: Successful Phase I execution at Kaiga Generating Station (prestigious nuclear power project) validates “Made-in-India” waterproofing tech; signals credibility for more govt/infra orders (e.g., power, nuclear push).
  • Subsidiary Strength: Foreign subs (HK, USA) contribute ~90% PAT, ₹1,069 Cr assets, ₹374 Cr H1 revenue; forex gains in OCI.
  • Balance Sheet Health: Equity surged (retained earnings + OCI); net debt down (total borrowings -13% QoQ); positive investing cash flow (₹14 Cr from capex efficiency).
  • Shareholder Alignment: Overwhelming AGM approval; dividend history (₹0.10/share FY25).

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Revenue Pressure: H1 down 2.5% YoY/Q2 down 10%; cost of traded goods high (40% of revenue), signaling pricing/volume weakness in polymers/waterproofing.
  • Working Capital Strain: Trade receivables ballooned 22% QoQ to ₹711 Cr (39% of assets); inventory steady at ₹198 Cr – potential sales slowdown/collections lag.
  • Standalone Weakness: Core India ops PAT <1% of revenue; group reliance on subs exposes to execution gaps.
  • Macro Slowdown: Infra delays/polymer price volatility (materials 22% of expenses).

Growth Prospects

  • High (Infra-Led): India’s capex cycle (nuclear/power infra via Kaiga-like projects) + export via subs (37% H1 group revenue). Waterproofing demand from construction boom; FY26 revenue potential +10-15% if orders scale.
  • Margin Upside: Efficiency gains (depn stable, finance costs controlled) could lift PAT 15-20% YoY; OCI tailwind if forex favorable.
  • Expansion: Capex moderating (₹99 Cr H1 vs ₹351 Cr prior); subs’ growth (USA/HK assets up).
  • Valuation: EPS ₹3.87 (H1, not annualized); low debt/EV supports rerating.
