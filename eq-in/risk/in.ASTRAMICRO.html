Analysis of Astra Microwave Products Limited (ASTRAMICRO)

Astra Microwave Products Ltd. (Astra), a key player in RF/microwave subsystems, radars, EW, and space electronics for defense/aerospace, reported solid H1 FY26 results (ended Sep 30, 2025):
- Standalone: Revenue ₹410 Cr (+7.2% YoY), EBITDA ₹85 Cr (20.6% margin, +170 bps YoY), PAT ₹34 Cr (+13.5% YoY).
- Consolidated: Revenue ₹414 Cr (+7.7% YoY), EBITDA ₹89 Cr (21.4% margin, +240 bps YoY), PAT ₹40 Cr (+23.3% YoY).
- Order Book: Standalone ₹1,916 Cr (+5% QoQ), Consolidated ₹2,209 Cr (strong visibility, ~2.5x H1 revenue). Q2 orders: ₹238 Cr (standalone).
- Cash Flow: Operating cash flow turned positive at ₹236 Cr (standalone), driven by receivables unlock (trade receivables down ~22% QoQ).

However, Q2 revenue dipped YoY due to execution timing. Management reiterated rear-ended growth, targeting 2x revenue in 3-4 years (~₹2,250-2,500 Cr by FY30) via key programs, with long-term $1 Bn ambition.

Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts)

  • Robust Order Pipeline & Visibility: 70% defense (radars/EW), diversified across radars (Ashlesha, Rohini), space, metrology. Recent wins: Long-range radar refurbishment, Su-30 EW suite (LSI role), ARC JV’s ₹286 Cr MoD order for IAF Special Forces comms.
  • Margin Expansion: Favorable mix (systems > subsystems), gross margins ~47-51%. EBITDA margins defensible at 20-22%+; potential upside from AMCs/multi-year contracts.
  • Government Tailwinds: India’s 15-yr defense roadmap (₹79,000 Cr approvals), indigenization (Make-II/NCNC), rising capex (27% of budget). Space budget ₹13,416 Cr; PLI schemes for semis/PCBs.
  • JV/Subsidiary Strength: ARC (Astra-Rafael) on track for $42 Mn FY26 sales, $100-120 Mn order book; Navictronics (NavIC chips) new JV.
  • Improved Liquidity: Cash conversion cycle shortening; net debt down QoQ.
  • Export Push: 12-14% revenue; NATO/Europe partnerships in discussion; proven tech (Uttam, EW) for global sales.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Quarterly Volatility: Q2 revenue -6-7% YoY; H1 growth back-loaded (execution par with targets but space project delays).
  • Working Capital Pressure: Historical high receivables (improved but monitored); inventory stable at ~₹609 Cr.
  • Execution Timing: Defense “build-to-spec” leads to lumpy orders/revenue; reliant on client schedules (e.g., QRSAM, Uttam full production FY28+).
  • Other Expenses Spike: Q2 “other expenses” ₹26 Cr (up YoY) due to one-offs (Keysight maintenance, CSR).
  • Competition: Peers (Data Patterns, Paras) posting stellar quarters; bid-based losses in counter-drone (L2 in 3 bids).

Growth Prospects

  • Near-Term (FY26): ₹1,150-1,200 Cr revenue (standalone); Q3 orders ₹400 Cr+, Q4 ₹600 Cr+. ARC: $42 Mn sales.
  • Medium-Term (FY27-30):
    | FY | Revenue Guidance (Standalone) | Key Drivers | |—-|——————————-|————-| | FY27 | ₹1,400-1,500 Cr | QRSAM ramp-up | | FY28 | ₹1,650 Cr | +Uttam radars (Tejas) | | FY29 | ~₹2,000 Cr | +Su-30 EW | | FY30 | ₹2,250-2,500 Cr | Full production (QRSAM/Uttam/Su-30/Virupaksha) |
  • Long-Term: $1 Bn (~₹8,500 Cr) via space (Astra SAT-1 launch <24 months, LEO/near-LEO sats), EW/counter-drone, exports (complete systems). TAM: ₹24-25,000 Cr opportunities till FY28 (radars 40%, space/missiles/EW ~60%).
  • Strategic Shifts: Systems integrator (3 Astra radars soon); LEAP framework (Learn-Expand-Accretive-Protect); R&D ₹53 Cr FY25 (up from ₹23 Cr FY21).
  • Upside Levers: 15-20% CAGR targeted; space exports; metrology leadership (Mausam Mission).

Overall Outlook: Positive with rear-ended bias. Strong fundamentals (order book, margins, cash flows) outweigh Q2 softness. Growth hinged on execution of marquee programs; $1 Bn vision achievable if indigenization accelerates. Buy/Hold for long-term investors; monitor Q3 orders. Valuation: Trades at ~8-10x FY27E EV/EBITDA (peer premium justified by visibility).
