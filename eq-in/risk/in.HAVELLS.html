Havells India Limited (HAVELLS) Analysis

Period Analyzed: Q2 & H1 FY26 (ended Sep 30, 2025); other disclosures up to Nov 2025.
Overall Summary: Mixed quarter with revenue growth resuming in Q2 (+5% YoY) after H1 dip (-1%), driven by margins (+16% EBITDA YoY) despite consumer durables weakness from poor summer. Strong cables/switchgears offset Lloyd drag. Balance sheet robust (net cash ~Rs 1,449 cr), but working capital pressure evident. Trademark settlement and solar investment are strategic positives. FY26 growth likely 8-12% if seasonal recovery materializes.

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Margin Resilience: EBITDA margin expanded to 9.3% (+90 bps YoY) via cables mix (17.7% contribution margin vs 12.3% YoY), cost controls (SG&A ~in-line with revenue), and asset sale gain (Rs 45 cr other income). PAT margin steady at 6.7%.
  • Core Segments Strength: Cables (+12% Q2/+19% H1 YoY, highest growth), Switchgears (+8%), Lighting (+7%), Others (+18%) – 87% of revenue, showing pricing power/stabilization.
  • Brand/Legal Clarity: Rs 129.6 cr settlement ends ‘HAVELLS’ trademark disputes (Delhi HC/Supreme Court), affirming rights since 1971; HPL Group drops mark/renames entities.
  • Strategic Moves: Rs 600 cr minority stake in Goldi Solar (associate, equity-accounted); renewables entry amid India push. Product launches (Lloyd Kolors fridge on Flipkart) boost e-com/digital reach.
  • Financial Health: ROE 17.6%, ROCE 23.6%; low debt (finance cost <0.2% revenue); dividend paid (Rs 376 cr H1).

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Consumer Durables Weakness: Lloyd (-19% Q2/-31% H1 YoY) due to inventory overhang, GST changes, weak summer (fans/ACs/coolers). ECD (-2% Q2/-9% H1). Led to H1 revenue contraction and Lloyd loss (Rs -99 cr Q2 segment result).
  • Working Capital Drag: Inventory +20% YoY (Rs 4,807 cr) from cables/Lloyd buildup; net WC days 49 (vs 31 YoY). Operating cash flow negative Rs 405 cr H1 (vs positive prior).
  • Capex Intensity: Rs 697 cr H1 (CWIP Rs 475 cr); total cash outflow Rs 1,904 cr, cash equivalents down to Rs 1,449 cr.
  • Seasonal/Macro Pressure: Poor summer explicitly hit demand; broader inventory/channel issues signal soft consumer spending.

Growth Prospects

  • High: Cables/switchgears (core profitability engines); lighting stabilization. H1 sub-total (ex-Lloyd) +8%. Renewables via Goldi Solar could add long-term revenue (early equity pickup Rs 4.6 cr).
  • Medium: Seasonal recovery in Q3/Q4 (post-monsoon/winter ramp-up); e-com expansion (Flipkart tie-up), brand investments. Analyst meets (Nov-Dec) signal confidence.
  • Outlook: FY26 revenue ~Rs 22,500-23,500 cr (+4-8% YoY), PAT +10-15% if margins hold 9%+. Medium-term: FMEG tailwinds (urbanization, housing), solar diversification.

Key Risks

  • Demand/Seasonal: Prolonged weakness in durables/Lloyd (30%+ H1 revenue); inventory liquidation could pressure margins via discounts.
  • Execution/Investment: Goldi Solar integration risk; high capex (Rs 1,400+ cr FY26?) strains cash if WC doesn’t normalize.
  • Operational: Raw material volatility (despite cables hedge); under-absorption in Lloyd (negative margins).
  • External: Competition (e.g., in ACs/fans), regulatory (GST/trade), macro slowdown (rural/urban consumption). Trademark settlement cost (Rs 130 cr) one-off but cash hit.
  • Valuation: Trading at ~50x FY26 EPS (implied); downside if growth <8%.

Investment View: Hold/Buy on dips. Strong fundamentals offset near-term headwinds; monitor Q3 consumer recovery and WC trends. No UPSI shared in disclosures.
