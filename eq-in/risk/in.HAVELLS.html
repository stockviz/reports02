Havells India Limited (HAVELLS) Analysis

Summary Overview: Havells reported a mixed Q2 FY26 (ended Sep 30, 2025), with standalone revenue up 5.2% YoY to ₹4,767 cr, EBITDA margins expanding to 9.3% (+160 bps YoY), and PAT up 16.5% YoY to ₹317 cr, aided by strong Cables/Switchgears, asset sale gains (₹45 cr), and controlled expenses. However, H1 revenue dipped 1.2% YoY due to weak seasonal demand in Lloyd Consumer (-31% H1) and ECD (-9% H1). Balance sheet remains solid (net debt-free, equity ₹8,677 cr), but cash & equivalents fell to ₹1,449 cr amid inventory build-up and ₹600 cr investment in Goldi Solar (renewables associate). Upcoming ₹130 cr trademark settlement is a one-off. Overall, core FMCG-like segments show resilience, but consumer durables face headwinds; renewables entry signals diversification.

Tailwinds

  • Segment Strength in Core Businesses: Cables delivered robust +12% Q2/+19% H1 growth with margin expansion (17.7% contrib. margin vs. 12.3% YoY), driven by steady demand. Switchgears (+8%) and Lighting (+7%, aided by LED price stabilization) provided stability, contributing ~80% of sub-total revenue.
  • Margin Resilience & Expense Discipline: EBITDA margins improved to 9.3% despite revenue softness; ad/sales promo stable at ~2.7% of revenue. Contribution margins held at 20.1% overall.
  • Strategic Moves: ₹600 cr minority stake in Goldi Solar positions Havells in high-growth renewables (equity-accounted profit ₹5 cr in Q2). Asset sale from Faridabad relocation unlocked ₹45 cr gain.
  • Legal Resolution: Trademark settlement with HPL Group (₹130 cr one-time payment) ends multi-year disputes, securing ‘HAVELLS’ mark exclusivity.
  • Distribution & Product Momentum: E-commerce push (Lloyd Kolors Refrigerator on Flipkart from ₹18,990) enhances reach; ongoing brand investments support premiumization.

Headwinds

  • Weak Seasonal/Consumer Durables: Lloyd Consumer revenue -19% Q2/-31% H1 due to inventory overhang, GST changes, and poor summer (ACs, fans, coolers). ECD -2% Q2/-9% H1 with margin compression (21% contrib. vs. 23% YoY) from weak fans/coolers despite brand spends.
  • Working Capital Strain: Inventories up to ₹4,807 cr (+20% QoQ), driven by Cables/Lloyd stocking; net working capital days rose to 49 (vs. 31 YoY). Cash flow from ops negative ₹405 cr in H1 (vs. +ve prior year).
  • H1 Topline Pressure: Overall revenue -1% YoY, reflecting summer demand weakness and Lloyd de-growth.
  • One-Off Cash Outflow: ₹130 cr settlement payment imminent, plus high capex (₹697 cr H1).

Growth Prospects

  • Short-Term (FY26): Recovery in H2 via festive demand, channel inventory normalization, and summer product rebound. Cables/Switchgears to sustain 10-15% growth; Lighting benefits from pricing stability. Lloyd margins could improve with volume pickup and offers.
  • Medium-Term (2-3 Years): Renewables exposure via Goldi Solar taps India’s solar boom (target: 500 GW by 2030). E-commerce/omni-channel expansion (e.g., Flipkart tie-up) targets tier-2/3 urban growth. Brand investments to drive premium ECD/Lloyd recovery.
  • Long-Term: Diversification beyond electricals (FMCG-like Cables stable at 40% revenue); export potential via subsidiaries. ROE/ROCE at 18%/24% TTM support 12-15% EPS CAGR if consumer rebounds.
  • Triggers: Analyst meets (Nov-Dec 2025) for visibility; Q3 festive results key.

Key Risks

Demand/Seasonality Prolonged weak summer/consumer spending (Lloyd/EC D 50%+ of revenue); inventory overhang risks price cuts. Festive H2 pickup; high inventory days (81) signal caution. High impact.
Execution High capex (CWIP ₹475 cr) amid negative FCF; Goldi Solar integration risks (minority stake). Strong balance sheet (current ratio 1.7); debt-free. Medium impact.
Macro/Competition Rural slowdown, inflation, GST tweaks; rivals in durables (V-Guard, Voltas). Diversified portfolio (Cables resilient). Medium impact.
Cash/One-Offs ₹130 cr payout + inventory ties up cash (down 56% YoY); dividend policy strains. ₹1,449 cr liquidity; unallocable income supports. Low-medium impact.
Regulatory Forex (subsidiaries), commodity volatility (copper in Cables). Hedging; 14 debtor days low. Low impact.

Investment Outlook: Neutral-positive. Buy on dips for Cables/renewables stability; watch Q3 for Lloyd recovery. Target multiples: 45-50x FY26E EPS (~₹50-55), implying 15-20% upside from current levels (assuming ~₹1,600 spot). Risks tilted to consumer demand.
