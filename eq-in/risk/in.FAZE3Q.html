Analysis of Faze Three Limited (FAZE3Q) – Q2/H1 FY26 Results & Presentation

Faze Three Limited, a home/technical textiles exporter (90%+ revenue from exports to US/UK/EUR), reported strong 35% YoY revenue growth to ₹423 Cr in H1 FY26 (consolidated), driven by China+1 tailwinds and capacity expansions. However, reported EBITDA (₹34 Cr) and PAT (₹7.6 Cr) declined YoY due to a one-off ₹14-17 Cr MTM forex loss on USDINR forwards (non-cash, from 85.7 to 88.8). Ex-MTM, EBITDA margins held at ~11% (vs 13% FY25). Balance sheet remains robust: net worth ₹426 Cr, zero LT debt, net debt/EBITDA ~2x, expansions self-funded (₹300 Cr capex since FY19). TTM revenue ₹820 Cr (+17%), PAT ₹43 Cr.

Tailwinds

  • China+1 Momentum: Retailers (e.g., Walmart, Sainsbury’s; top 12 = 80% revenue) targeting 15-20% diversification from China; India’s MMF leadership (PLI scheme) positions Faze as beneficiary in rugs/bathmats/TOB (China dominates 20x India’s exports).
  • Customer Demand: Strong pipeline despite tariffs; US share stable at 62%; untapped potential (10x appetite in existing lines).
  • Capacity Ready: Brownfield expansions (Silvassa, Panipat, Aurangabad) add ₹1,650 Cr revenue potential; utilizations 30-50%; vertically integrated (yarn-to-product).
  • Financial Strength: Debt-free LT, CARE A/A1 rating; 95% FOB exports, domestic RM; cash generative (CFO ₹27-37 Cr H1).
  • Policy Support: UK FTA, impending EU FTAs; PLI-2 for MMF; rooftop solar/ESG investments.

Headwinds

  • US Tariffs: 50% reciprocal duties (India=China levels); extra 25% on Russian oil imports (impacting margins; expected lift in 2 months). Costs absorbed to retain volumes, pressuring EBITDA to 10.8% ex-MTM.
  • Forex Volatility: Sharp USDINR rise caused ₹17 Cr MTM hit (Q2); no hedge accounting, routed via P&L.
  • Margin Pressure: Employee/other costs up; inventory days 103 (from 112 FY25); working capital strain amid growth.
  • Global Slowdown Risks: Retailer caution in $8-25 price band products, though resilient (3-4x retail markup cushions tariffs).

Growth Prospects

  • Near-Term: FY26 revenue to exceed 25% guidance (H1 already +35%); FY27 at 22-25% on order visibility, product diversification (MMF/cotton balance shifting to MMF).
  • Medium-Term: Tap $200 Bn global trade (India ~7%; target 15% by 2030); new NY showroom, categories (outdoor/patio mats); capex peaks FY27, freeing 40-45% CFO.
  • Catalysts: Tariff relief, capacity ramp-up (to 70-80% util), customer addition; ROCE ~13-14% sustainable.

Overall Outlook: Positive. Tailwinds from China+1 and capacity outweigh near-term tariff/FX noise. Ex-MTM performance validates growth story; stock likely undervalues expansions and 20%+ CAGR potential. Monitor tariff resolution and Q3 forex. Target multiples: 12-15x FY27 EPS (~₹20) implies upside.
