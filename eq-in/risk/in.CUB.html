Summary Analysis of City Union Bank Ltd. (CUB) - Q2 FY26 (Sep 30, 2025)

City Union Bank (CUB), a 121-year-old private sector bank focused on MSME, retail/trading, and agri lending, reported robust Q2/H1 FY26 results amid a competitive banking landscape. Deposits grew 21% YoY to ₹69,486 Cr, advances 18% to ₹57,561 Cr, and PAT 15% to ₹329 Cr (Q2)/₹635 Cr (H1). Asset quality improved sharply (GNPA 2.42%, NNPA 0.90%), supported by strong capital (CRAR 21.68%) and digital initiatives. Below is a structured analysis of headwinds, tailwinds, growth prospects, and key risks.

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Strong Balance Sheet Growth: Deposits +21% YoY (CASA 28%), advances +18% YoY (CD ratio 83%), driven by granular MSME/trading loans (42% of portfolio) and gold loans (28%, low LTV ~61%).
  • Profitability Momentum: NII +14-15% YoY, ROA stable at 1.59% (above long-term 1.50%), ROE 13.35% (Q2). Non-interest income +14-20% from fees/recoveries.
  • Asset Quality Turnaround: GNPA -112 bps YoY to 2.42%, NNPA -72 bps to 0.90%; PCR 82% (with TW). Recoveries > slippages, credit cost near-zero.
  • Capital & Efficiency: CRAR 21.68% (Tier-1 20.71%), well above RBI norms. Stable NIM 3.63% (Q2 sequential uptick).
  • Digital & Network Expansion: 900 branches (strong TN/South focus, 78% deposits), new UPI features (e.g., Circle, Help, biometric auth), MSME cards, and partnerships (e.g., Finzere, Perfios) boosting low-cost acquisition.
  • Legacy & Ratings: 121-year profitability track record; ICRA/CRISIL/CARE ratings AA-/A1+.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Rising Costs: Cost-to-income 49.16% (+2.1% YoY), operating expenses +20% YoY (employee costs +22%), pressuring margins.
  • NIM Pressure: Slight YoY dip to 3.63% (from 3.67%); cost of deposits stable but yield on advances marginally down (9.66% vs 9.81%).
  • Deposit Repricing: Faster advances growth vs. deposits could strain liquidity if rates rise.
  • Regional Concentration: 73% business from South India (557 TN branches), vulnerable to regional slowdowns.
  • Provision Volatility: Bad debt provisions ₹110 Cr (H1), though lower YoY; ECL/standard asset provisions added.

Growth Prospects

  • High: Double-digit CAGR potential (historical 10%+ business growth). MSME/trading untapped market, granular portfolio (top-20 groups <7%), agri/gold loans expansion.
  • Network & Digital Scale: 889-900 branches (Pan-India push), 1,709 ATMs; UPI/digital lending (LOS digitized), new products (CUB Desire savings, Depend credit line, NCMC cards) targeting retail/MSME.
  • Profit Leverage: ROA/ROE upside from NIM stabilization (3.5-3.7%), NPA compression, and fee income (CEB +25-29% YoY).
  • Capital Headroom: Excess CRAR supports inorganic growth (e.g., QIP history); FII limit raised to 40% (headroom 14%).
  • Outlook: FY26 guidance implicit: CIR 48-50%, sustained 15-20% business growth, ROA >1.5%.

Overall Outlook: Positive. Tailwinds from growth/quality dominate; headwinds manageable via cost discipline/digital scale. FY26 PAT could exceed FY25 (₹1,124 Cr) at 15%+ growth, with stock upside on MSME recovery/NIM stability. Monitor macros (e.g., monsoon, rates) and Q3 execution. Valuation attractive vs. peers (ROA/ROE peers).
