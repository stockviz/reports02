Analysis of Federal-Mogul Goetze (India) Limited (FMGOETZE)

Company Overview: FMGOETZE is an auto components manufacturer (pistons, rings, etc.) primarily serving OEMs in India and exports, part of the Tenneco Group. It operates in a single reportable segment with geographical exposure to India and overseas. H1 FY26 (ended Sep 30, 2025) standalone results show robust YoY growth: revenue +7.4% to ₹956 Cr, PAT +25.7% to ₹93 Cr (EPS ₹16.69), with PBT margins at ~13%. Consolidated figures are similar (revenue +7.2% to ₹974 Cr, PAT attributable to owners ~+25%). Balance sheet remains strong (net worth ₹1,287 Cr standalone, cash & equiv. ₹569 Cr). AGM outcomes (Sep 29, 2025) confirm smooth governance with all resolutions passed, including director appointments and MD elevation.

Tailwinds (Positive Factors)

  • Revenue Momentum: H1 YoY growth of 7-8% driven by volume recovery in auto sector; QoQ up 1.2% in Q2. Exports and domestic OEM demand supportive.
  • Profitability Expansion: PAT growth outpaces revenue (25%+ YoY) due to operational leverage, controlled expenses, and high other income (₹35 Cr H1, ~35% YoY growth from investments/dividends).
  • Pristine Balance Sheet: Zero debt (negligible lease liabilities), cash reserves up 12% to ₹569 Cr standalone (₹657 Cr cons.), enabling capex/dividends without funding stress. ROE implied >14% annualized.
  • Group Synergies: Tenneco backing provides tech/R&D edge; subsidiary (Federal-Mogul TPR India) adds piston ring synergies (contributes ~2% to cons. revenue).
  • Governance Stability: Unmodified Deloitte review; clean AGM with key appointments (e.g., Amit Mittal as MD/CFO).

Headwinds (Negative Factors)

  • Q2 YoY Softness: Revenue dipped 2.8% YoY in Sep’25 quarter (₹481 Cr vs ₹495 Cr), signaling uneven demand amid auto sector slowdowns.
  • Working Capital Pressure: Inventories +11% YoY (₹194 Cr standalone), trade receivables +4% (₹358 Cr); ops cash flow halved to ₹77 Cr due to ₹46 Cr higher tax outflow and WC buildup.
  • Cost Inflation: Raw material costs +15% YoY (39% of revenue); employee expenses +6% (22% of revenue), squeezing gross margins slightly.
  • Capex Drag: ₹40 Cr H1 spend (CWIP ₹40 Cr), with total non-current assets flat YoY, indicating expansion but short-term cash outflow.

Growth Prospects

  • High (Medium-Term: 10-15% CAGR): Auto sector tailwinds in India (PV/CV recovery post-elections, EV transition favoring components). Exports (part of dual geography) could drive 20%+ growth. Cash pile supports M&A/capex (e.g., new plants). PAT trajectory suggests 20%+ FY26 growth if Q3/Q4 sustains H1 momentum. Subsidiary integration enhances product portfolio.
  • Catalysts: Dividend payout potential (strong cash), margin expansion to 14-15% via efficiencies, geographical diversification.

Growth prospects solid (target 12-15% revenue CAGR FY26-28) if auto demand rebounds. Buy/Hold rating for long-term investors (fair value ~₹600-650/share est., based on 20x FY26 EPS). Monitor Q3 results for demand confirmation; key positive: dividend announcement expected. Risks manageable given fortress BS (D/E ~0).
