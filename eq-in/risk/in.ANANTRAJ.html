Anant Raj Limited (ANANTRAJ) Analysis

Overview: Anant Raj Limited, a real estate developer with diversification into data centers and cloud services, reported strong H1 FY26 results (ended Sep 30, 2025). Standalone revenue grew 28% YoY to ₹723 Cr, PAT up 50% to ₹144 Cr. Consolidated revenue up 24% to ₹1,223 Cr, PAT up 34% to ₹264 Cr. Key highlights include ₹1,100 Cr QIP closure (Oct 2025), debt prepayment of ₹125 Cr (net cash positive), robust real estate pipeline, and data center expansions. Company is debt-light with healthy liquidity (standalone cash ₹223 Cr).

Tailwinds (Positive Momentum)

  • Robust Financial Performance: H1 revenue/PAT growth driven by real estate deliveries and other income. Margins stable (standalone PBT margin ~24%); cash from operations strong at ₹156 Cr (standalone).
  • Capital Infusion: ₹1,100 Cr QIP (1.66 Cr shares at ₹662) fully subscribed by FPIs/DIIs, funding growth without straining balance sheet. Reduced debt (standalone borrowings down to ₹433 Cr from ₹421 Cr net).
  • Real Estate Momentum: Approvals for luxury projects (e.g., “The Estate One” ~1.09 Mn sq ft), township expansions (Phase-IV, 6 acres), and nearing launches (Group Housing-3). Deliveries ramping (Project Navya Phase-2 from Dec 2025; Ashok Estate near completion).
  • Data Center/Cloud Tailwinds: Hosted “BHARAT BUILT” event showcasing expansions (Manesar/Panchkula as DR sites). Anant Raj Cloud targeting 117 MW IT load by FY28 across 3 sites (Rai: 20 MW initial, 200 MW planned). Capex fully funded.
  • Liquidity & Efficiency: Inventories down 20% QoQ (consolidated ₹873 Cr), trade receivables collected sharply. Dividend paid (₹0.73/share).

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Working Capital Intensity: Despite collections, other financial assets up sharply (e.g., standalone ₹1,124 Cr from ₹787 Cr), indicating project advances/receivables buildup. Cash dipped slightly (consolidated ₹292 Cr from ₹330 Cr).
  • Macro Sensitivity: Real estate exposed to interest rates, buyer sentiment, and regulatory delays (e.g., Group Housing-3 approvals targeted Q4 FY26).
  • Capex Drag: Heavy investments in data centers/PPE (₹55 Cr in H1 consolidated), leading to negative investing cash flow (₹190 Cr outflow).
  • Dilution Impact: QIP increased equity capital 5% (to ₹72 Cr; ~36 Cr shares), potentially pressuring EPS if growth lags.

Growth Prospects

  • High Visibility Pipeline: Real estate development potential >5-6 Mn sq ft near-term (Gurugram focus: Golf Course Extension Road). Data centers poised for hyperscaler demand (India’s DC market growing 30%+ CAGR).
  • Diversification Upside: Data/Cloud segment scaling rapidly (funded to FY28); could contribute 20-30% revenue mix long-term. JV deliveries (Avarna Projects) boosting credibility.
  • Projections: H1 growth + backlog suggest FY26 revenue >₹2,500 Cr (consolidated), PAT ₹500-600 Cr. QIP enables 20-25% CAGR in next 2-3 years, targeting net debt zero.
  • Analyst Engagement: Upcoming Nuvama conference (Dec 2025) signals institutional interest.

Key Risks

Risk Category Description Mitigation
Execution Delays in project launches/approvals or DC commissioning (e.g., Rai site). Strong track record; approvals progressing “as scheduled”.
Market/Cyclical Real estate slowdown, rising rates impacting affordability. Premium Gurugram locations; net cash buffer.
Financial Forex/interest rate volatility; over-reliance on advances (63% current assets). Low gearing (debt/equity ~0.1x); fixed-rate debt.
Operational DC competition (Adani, Reliance); tech obsolescence. Strategic DR setup; funded expansions.
Regulatory SEBI compliance (e.g., share transfers rejected in Aug); land approvals. Proactive disclosures; clean audit reviews.
Equity QIP dilution (~5% EPS impact if not accretive). Growth capex to drive 15-20% EPS CAGR.

Summary Verdict: Strong Buy/Accumulate outlook. Tailwinds from QIP-funded expansions and H1 momentum outweigh headwinds. Growth in real estate + data centers positions ANANTRAJ for 20%+ CAGR; monitor DC execution and realty demand. Risks manageable with net cash status (standalone equity ₹3,821 Cr vs. assets ₹4,490 Cr). Stock likely to re-rate on FY26 delivery.
