Suprajit Engineering Limited (SUPRAJIT) Analysis

Overview: Suprajit is a leading global manufacturer of control cables (~300 Mn units/year), halogen lamps (~80 Mn/year), and emerging “beyond cables” products (actuators, electronics, braking systems). FY26 H1 (ended Sep 30, 2025) showed resilient standalone revenue growth (+6.1% YoY to ₹8,774 Mn) and consolidated ex-SCS EBITDA growth (+17% to ₹2,151 Mn, 13.4% margin). Q2 was stronger (standalone revenue +8.3%). Indian auto industry grew 5.8%; company outperformed. Debt rose to ₹7,384 Mn (group), but investments stable at ~₹2,500 Mn. Recent SCS acquisition drags consolidated profitability (EBITDA loss ₹242 Mn H1), but improving.

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Outperformance vs. Industry: Consolidated ex-SCS revenue +6.4% (H1), +7.6% (Q2); EBITDA +17%/+18.7%. Divisions like SCD (+52% EBITDA), SED (+68% EBITDA to 10.7% margin), DCD (+9% revenue) show operational leverage.
  • Diversification: Balanced across PV/2W/3W/off-highway/aftermarket (no customer >10% revenue). Global footprint (India, US, Mexico, Europe, China) with onshore/nearshore/offshore model wins contracts.
  • Beyond Cables Traction: SED/digital clusters/throttle grips/sensors/actuators growing fast (e.g., throttle grips record sales). Braking systems (Blubrake ABS JV) and actuators with OEMs like TVS, Ola, JLR.
  • Restructuring Success: SCD EBITDA margin to 11.6%; DCD robust at 16-17%. SAP rollout, capacity expansions (Chakan, AURIC land).
  • EV Agnostic: Cables/actuation low-cost for EVs; new wins in EV 2W/3W clusters/sensors.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • SCS Acquisition Drag: Post-insolvency buy (Germany/Canada/China); H1 revenue ₹1,987 Mn but EBITDA loss ₹242 Mn (-12% margin). Q2 loss narrowed to -₹67 Mn (-6%). Restructuring (Juarez closure, Morocco shift, headcount cuts) ongoing till Dec 2025.
  • Macro Pressures: Geopolitics, US tariffs/China rare earth curbs, Europe port congestions, Middle East export drop (PLD revenue -5%, EBITDA -22%).
  • Division-Specific: PLD muted (export halts); some US/EU program delays/shelving.
  • Margin Pressure: Standalone EBITDA flat (16.6%); forex/hedge losses in OCI.
  • Cash Flow: Operating cash positive (standalone ₹620 Mn, cons. ₹663 Mn), but investing outflows (capex ₹467-646 Mn, SCS payments) and financing (buyback/dividends).

Growth Prospects

  • Near-Term (H2 FY26): Stronger H2 expected (auto GST cuts, restructuring complete, SCS EBITDA positive by Q4). Ex-SCS cons. revenue/EBITDA trajectory: 6-8% revenue, 14%+ margins.
  • Medium-Term (3-5 Years): Organic (tech center: 150+ R&D FTEs driving actuation/electronics/braking; new JVs like Suprajit Chuhatsu); inorganic (8 prior deals). Target 5-10% above global industry CAGR (cables/lamps stable, beyond-cables ramps). Order book strong (EV/OEM wins).
  • Strategic: 40th anniversary focus on premiumization/systems. EICMA Milan debut positive. STC building underway.
  • Projections: Consensus implies 10-15% revenue CAGR; EBITDA margins 12-14% sustained.

Key Risks

  • Execution/Integration: SCS turnaround failure (insolvency legacy, tariffs); capex heavy (₹442-551 Mn CWIP).
  • Macro/Geopolitical: US tariffs (mitigated by alt. sourcing), China/Europe disruptions, delayed launches.
  • EV/Tech Shift: Speedo/throttle cable decline (offset by SED portfolio, but ramp-up risk).
  • Financial: Debt up 12% YoY (short-term heavy); forex volatility (hedge accounting adopted Apr 2025); buyback/dividends strain cash (₹1,125 Mn buyback noted).
  • Regulatory/Competition: Tariff escalation, Chapter 11 rivals (PLD opportunity but volatile). Promoter-related resolution (Item 6 AGM) saw 12% dissent/invalidation.
  • Liquidity: Cash ₹107-1,533 Mn; working capital strain (inventories +₹157-710 Mn, receivables +₹415-172 Mn).

Summary Verdict: Suprajit demonstrates resilience (beats industry, division strength) amid headwinds (SCS, macros). Tailwinds from diversification/tech pivot position for 10%+ growth, but SCS/debt execution critical. Positive outlook for H2/FY26; hold/buy on dips for long-term compounding (EV/beyond-cables). Monitor Q3 results for SCS progress. (Data as of Nov 2025 filings.)
