Analysis of Linde India Limited (LINDEINDIA)

Tailwinds (Positive Factors)

  • Strong Profitability Momentum: H1 FY26 standalone PBT surged 27% YoY to ₹3,685 Mn, driven by Q2’s exceptional 59% QoQ jump to ₹2,263 Mn. Net profit up 27% YoY to ₹2,741 Mn (EPS ₹32.14). Consolidated figures similarly robust (PBT +26% YoY). Key driver: Power & fuel expenses halved YoY (₹1,702 Mn H1 vs. ₹2,610 Mn prior), aided by ₹900 Mn reversal from contractual reassessments.
  • Gases Segment Resilience: Core segment (84% of revenue) delivered 32% YoY profit growth H1 (₹3,447 Mn), with Q2 margins expanding sharply. Supports stable industrial/medical gas demand amid India’s manufacturing push.
  • Healthy Operating Cash Flows: ₹3,953 Mn generated H1, funding capex/dividends internally. ROE implied ~14% annualized (equity ₹39.5 Bn).
  • Balance Sheet Strength: Equity grew 4.5% to ₹39.5 Bn standalone (debt-free core ops, minor leases). Total assets +4% to ₹53.3 Bn, with CWIP at ₹12.7 Bn signaling expansion.
  • Dividend Discipline: ₹1,020 Mn paid H1 (120% payout ratio), signaling confidence.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Revenue Stagnation: H1 standalone flat-to-down 6% YoY at ₹12,153 Mn; Q2 up 13% QoQ but flat YoY. Project Engineering (16% revenue) volatile (H1 revenue -16% YoY), offsetting Gases growth.
  • Margin Volatility: Power/fuel remains 14% of expenses; Q1 spike (₹1,288 Mn) pressured profits before Q2 reversal. Other expenses up 6% YoY H1.
  • Cash Strain: Cash & equivalents down 30% to ₹1,013 Mn from FY25-end, due to ₹3.3 Bn investing outflows (capex) and dividends. Free cash flow negative H1.
  • Segment Imbalance: Heavy Gases reliance (90%+ profits); Project Engineering margins thin (5-10%).

Growth Prospects

  • Capex-Led Expansion: ₹12.7 Bn CWIP (up 30% YoY) targets Gases capacity, positioning for India’s industrial gas demand (e.g., steel, pharma, green H2). H1 capex ₹3.4 Bn implies FY26 outlay ~₹15-20 Bn.
  • Sector Tailwinds: Industrial gases market ~15% CAGR India (to ₹30,000 Cr by 2030); Linde’s tech edge in cryogenics/medical gases aligns with Atmanirbhar/PLI schemes.
  • Renewables Pivot: Exited one SPV (FP Solar Shakti) at cost; retains others for green power offtake, hedging energy costs long-term.
  • Earnings Trajectory: If revenue grows 8-10% FY26 (consensus-like), with cost discipline, EPS could hit ₹55-60 (12-15% growth). ROCE ~20% sustainable.

Key Risks

  • Regulatory Overhang (High Impact): Ongoing SEBI probes/JV issues with Praxair India (business allocation, RPT aggregation). SEBI Order (Jul 2024) mandates aggregate RPT testing & NSE-led JV valuation (ongoing; appeals pending SAT/SC). Auditor emphasis: “Indeterminate impact” on results; potential shareholder approvals, fines, or JV renegotiation could disrupt ~20-30% revenue allocation.
  • Commodity/OpEx Volatility: Power/fuel ~25-30% costs; energy price spikes (as in Q1) erode margins (effective tax ~25%).
  • Execution/Capex Risk: High CWIP delay could impair returns; negative FCF if ops soften.
  • Litigation/Market: SEBI summons continue (Jun/Jul 2025); cyclical project orders; competition from Air Liquide/INOX.
  • Macro: Industrial slowdown (e.g., steel) hits Gases; forex (imports).

Summary

Linde India exhibits resilient profitability (27% YoY H1 profit growth) amid flat revenue, fueled by cost optimizations and Gases strength—clear tailwinds for near-term earnings beats. Growth prospects are solid (capex-driven 10-15% EPS CAGR), leveraging India’s infra/industrial boom. However, headwinds from revenue softness and cash burn temper enthusiasm, while SEBI regulatory risks loom largest (pending appeals/valuation could trigger volatility/disclosures). Overall, positive outlook (target multiples 40-45x FY26 EPS) but monitor regulatory updates; hold/buy on dips for long-term compounding. FY26 revenue/EBITDA est.: ₹26,000 Mn / ₹7,500 Mn.
