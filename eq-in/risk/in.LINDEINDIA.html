Analysis of Linde India Limited (LINDEINDIA)

Tailwinds (Positive Factors)

  • Robust Profitability Growth: H1 FY26 standalone PAT surged 27% YoY to ₹2,741 Mn (EPS ₹32.14), driven by strong margins in the core Gases segment (H1 profit ₹3,447 Mn, +40% YoY). Consolidated PAT +26% YoY to ₹2,782 Mn. Q2 saw exceptional PBT growth (+60% YoY) due to cost efficiencies.
  • Cost Reversal and Efficiencies: Power & fuel expenses dropped sharply (H1 ₹1,702 Mn vs ₹2,610 Mn YoY), aided by ₹900 Mn reversal from contractual changes (Note iv). This boosted operating leverage.
  • Strong Operating Cash Flow: H1 standalone OCF at ₹3,953 Mn (+ strong vs FY25 full year trends), supporting dividends (₹1,020 Mn paid) and capex.
  • Gases Segment Momentum: Core segment revenue +4% YoY in H1 (₹10,601 Mn), with superior profitability, underscoring resilient demand for industrial/medical gases.
  • Balance Sheet Strength: Equity grew to ₹39,511 Mn (standalone), low debt (finance costs minor at ₹63 Mn H1), and healthy liquidity (cash ₹1,013 Mn despite outflows).

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Revenue Pressure: H1 standalone revenue -6% YoY to ₹12,153 Mn, led by Project Engineering (-16% to ₹4,791 Mn). Q2 flat YoY; inter-segment elimination also rose.
  • Capex Drag on Cash: Heavy investing outflows (₹3,437 Mn H1 on PPE/CWIP, up to ₹12,656 Mn), causing net cash decline (₹441 Mn). CWIP signals expansion but strains free cash.
  • Input Cost Volatility: Power & fuel spiked in Q1 (₹1,288 Mn) before reversal; remains a high expense line (gases-intensive business).
  • Working Capital Strain: Trade receivables up (₹4,600 Mn), inventories +6%, contributing to OCF adjustments.

Growth Prospects

  • Core Gases Expansion: Dominant segment (87% H1 revenue post-elimination) benefits from industrial recovery, healthcare demand, and green energy transition (e.g., HYCO/PST via JV). High CWIP suggests capacity build-out for volume growth.
  • Project Engineering Recovery: Lumpy but stable profits (+15% YoY H1); potential upside from cryogenic plant orders in energy/infra sectors.
  • Capex-Led Growth: ₹3.4 Bn H1 spend (FY25 full year was ₹13 Bn) positions for FY26 revenue acceleration. Historical trends show capex translating to top-line in 12-18 months.
  • Renewables Tie-Ups: Ongoing SPV investments (e.g., Avaada, FPEL) for power offtake at amortised cost; exited one at par, indicating disciplined approach.
  • Outlook: Management’s capex focus and margin tailwinds support 10-15% PAT CAGR if revenue rebounds (FY25 revenue base ₹24.9 Bn). Consensus may project FY26 revenue ~₹26-28 Bn.

Key Risks

  • Regulatory Overhang (High): Ongoing SEBI probes (Notes iiiA/B, Auditor Emphasis of Matter):
    • RPT materiality (aggregate vs per-contract threshold): SEBI order (Jul 2024) mandates aggregate testing; SAT appeal pending. Potential shareholder approvals needed for Praxair/Linde South Asia deals.
    • JV Business Allocation (with Praxair India): NSE-mandated valuation of “foregone/received” business (geographic split); ongoing (new valuer appointed Apr 2025), Supreme Court/SAT rejections. Auditor notes “indeterminate impact” – could imply restitution, fines, or governance changes.
    • Recent summons (Jun/Jul 2025); uncertainties could hit stock sentiment/multiple.
  • Execution Risks: High CWIP (₹12.7 Bn, +30% QoQ) exposes to project delays/cost overruns in gases expansion.
  • Macro/Commodity Risks: Power/fuel (20-25% expenses) sensitive to energy prices; gases demand tied to steel/chemicals cyclicality.
  • Concentration: Gases ~80% profits; Project Engineering lumpiness adds volatility.
  • Other: Dividend payout sustainable but capex may limit hikes. Assets held for sale (₹150-169 Mn) minor but unresolved.

Summary

LINDEINDIA delivered stellar H1 FY26 profitability (+27% PAT YoY) on cost reversals and Gases strength, despite -6% revenue dip from Project Engineering weakness. Tailwinds dominate via margins, cash flow, and capex (positioning for growth), but headwinds include revenue softness and cash burn. Growth prospects are solid (Gases expansion, 10-15% earnings potential), tempered by key risks from SEBI regulatory battles (RPT/JV valuation; high uncertainty, monitor SAT outcomes). Overall positive operational momentum, but stock trades at a discount until regulatory clarity emerges. Buy on dips for long-term industrial gases play.
