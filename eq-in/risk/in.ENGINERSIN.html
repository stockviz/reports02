Engineers India Limited (ENGINERSIN) - Analysis Summary (Q2 FY26)

Engineers India Limited (EIL), a Navratna PSU and leading engineering consultancy firm, specializes in consultancy & engineering (C&E), turnkey/LSTK projects in hydrocarbons, infrastructure, fertilizers, and emerging areas like hydrogen, coal gasification, and bio-refineries. FY26 H1 showed robust standalone growth amid a record order book, but consolidated results were tempered by JV losses. Below is a structured analysis based on Q2 FY26 earnings transcript, financial results, investor presentation, order disclosures, and AGM outcomes.

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Record Order Book & Inflows: ₹13,131 Cr (all-time high, +8% QoQ; 59% C&E). H1 inflows ₹3,765 Cr (+58% YoY); already >₹4,000 Cr, targeting >₹8,000 Cr FY26 (50% C&E mix, 20-25% international). Key wins: Africa fertilizer PMC/EPCM (₹618 Cr), UAE/Kuwait consultancy (>₹1,600 Cr international YTD).
  • Strong Financial Momentum (Standalone): Q2 turnover ₹900 Cr (+33% YoY), EBITDA ₹160 Cr (17% margin, +45% YoY), PAT ₹115 Cr (+45%). H1 PAT ₹185 Cr (+38%), EPS ₹3.29. C&E margins ~28% Q2 (25% H1 guidance intact); LSTK 5-6%.
  • Pipeline Visibility: Domestic (IOCL Paradip Ph-2 ~₹900 Cr by FY26-end, Andhra refinery, BPCL petchem ₹95k Cr); International (Middle East, Africa). Oil/gas capex tailwinds: IOCL ₹1.66L Cr, Barmer ₹72k Cr.
  • Balance Sheet Strength: Cash ₹900-1,000 Cr; ROCE healthy; dividend ₹2/sh (FY25 final paid Oct’25).
  • Diversification: Infrastructure > hydrocarbons; new areas (bio-refinery, coal-to-SNG via NTPC).
  • Shareholder Approval: AGM (Sep’25) passed all resolutions overwhelmingly (e.g., 99.9%+ for financials/dividend; 86-97% for new directors).

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • JV Drag on Consolidateds: Ramagundam Fertilizer (26% stake, ₹491 Cr invested) losses ₹25 Cr Q2 (45-day shutdown; H1 total ~₹34 Cr), pulling consolidated PAT to ₹83 Cr Q2/₹149 Cr H1. NRL dividend delayed (₹20 Cr expected Q3).
  • Overseas Revenue Ramp Lag: C&E overseas turnover flat/declining QoQ (Dangote/Mongolia tapering), despite strong book; expected pickup in H2.
  • Margin Mix Pressure: LSTK (53% H1 revenue) at 5% margins vs. C&E 22-25%; turnkey-heavy quarters dilute blends.
  • Provision Volatility: ₹35 Cr write-back (Dangote DLP) boosted Q2, but net provisions fluctuate (±₹12 Cr Q2).

Growth Prospects

  • Near-Term (FY26): 25%+ revenue growth (annualized ₹3,800-3,900 Cr); order inflows >₹8k Cr (40-50% consultancy). H2 execution from new wins (e.g., Africa 36-48 months). EBITDA margins 14-17%; PAT growth 30-40% standalone.
  • Medium-Term (FY27+): Order book execution (3-4 yr horizon: 10-15% Year1, 25-30% Y2-3). Pipeline ₹3-4L Cr capex (refineries/petrochem). International 20-25% inflows; diversification (infra/hydrogen) to 50%+ mix.
  • Catalysts: Andhra/Paradip awards (end-FY26); RFCL stabilization (₹500 Cr annual profit potential, EIL share ₹130 Cr); NRL expansion dividends.
  • Valuation Upside: EPS trajectory ₹6-7 FY26; healthy dividend yield; Net Zero by 2035 positioning.
