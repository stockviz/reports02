Engineers India Limited (ENGINERSIN) - Analysis Summary

Engineers India Limited (EIL), a Navratna PSU and leading engineering consultancy firm, specializes in consultancy/engineering (higher margins) and turnkey/LSTK projects across hydrocarbons, infrastructure, fertilizers, and emerging areas like hydrogen and gasification. FY26 H1 results show robust execution amid record order inflows, though consolidated profits are tempered by JV losses. Promoter holding ~51%. Below is a structured analysis based on Q2/H1 FY26 earnings, investor presentation, financials, disclosures, and AGM outcomes.

Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts)

  • Record Order Book & Inflows: INR 13,131 Cr (all-time high, +8% QoQ, 59% consultancy). H1 inflows INR 3,765 Cr (+58% YoY); already >INR 4,000 Cr YTD, targeting >INR 8,000 Cr FY26 (vs. INR 8,214 Cr FY25). Strong mix: 50-50 consultancy-turnkey; overseas ~INR 1,600 Cr (20-25% of total).
  • Revenue & Margin Expansion: H1 standalone turnover INR 1,757 Cr (+37% YoY); Q2 INR 900 Cr (+33%). Consultancy margins 28% (Q2), guiding 25%; turnkey 5-7%. EBITDA margin 14% (H1).
  • International Momentum: New INR 618 Cr PMC/EPCM fertilizer contract (Africa); overseas consultancy inflows dominant. Execution ramp-up expected in H2 from prior large wins.
  • Diversification & Pipeline: Infrastructure now > hydrocarbons. Domestic pipeline: IOCL Paradip Phase-2 (~INR 900 Cr), Andhra refinery, petchem expansions. Govt capex tailwind: INR 1.25-1.66 lakh Cr in refining/petrochem (IOCL, BPCL, ONGC).
  • Balance Sheet Strength: Net cash INR 900-1,000 Cr; ROCE healthy; dividend payout (INR 2/share FY25 final).
  • AGM Approval: All resolutions passed overwhelmingly (99%+ for most), signaling governance stability.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • JV/Associate Drag: Ramagundam Fertilizer (26% stake, INR 491 Cr investment) losses widened (H1: ~INR 25 Cr in Q2 due to 45-day shutdown); consolidated H1 PAT INR 149 Cr (vs. standalone INR 185 Cr). NRL dividend muted due to expansion.
  • Turnkey Mix Pressure: Higher turnkey revenue (53% H1) caps overall margins (vs. consultancy’s 25%).
  • Overseas Revenue Lag: Quarterly run-rate INR 70-75 Cr despite large book; prior projects (Dangote, Mongolia) tapering.
  • Provision Volatility: Q2 write-back INR 35 Cr (Dangote DLP); net reversals INR 12 Cr, but routine fluctuations.
  • Pay Commission: Potential FY27 margin hit (provisioned).
