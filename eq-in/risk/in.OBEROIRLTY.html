Oberoi Realty Limited (OBEROIRLTY) Analysis

Based on Q2/H1 FY26 Results (ended Sep 30, 2025), Announcements, and Related Disclosures

Oberoi Realty, a premium Mumbai-focused real estate developer (residential ~95% of revenue), reported robust H1FY26 performance: bookings +17% YoY to ₹2,938 Cr, consolidated revenue +5% YoY to ₹2,767 Cr, PAT flat YoY at ₹1,181 Cr (Q2 PAT +80% QoQ to ₹760 Cr). High margins (OPM 55-57%, NPM 40%), low leverage (D/E 0.18), and ₹2/share interim dividend underscore financial strength. Retail/hospitality leasing gains traction; ESOP approval aids talent retention.

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Demand Surge: H1 bookings ₹2,938 Cr (+17% YoY), Q2 ₹1,299 Cr; strong residential absorption in integrated projects (e.g., Mumbai luxury).
  • Financial Resilience: PAT margins ~40%, EBITDA H1 ₹1,693 Cr; net cash from ops ₹1,279 Cr (pre-tax); liquidity ratios strong (current 4.3x).
  • Retail/Commercial Momentum: Leasing ramp-up at Sky City Mall (Borivali) & Commerz III; awards for Oberoi/Sky City Malls boost brand.
  • Strategic Moves: NCR expansion (Gurugram office); festive season tailwinds; upcoming analyst meets (Nov 2025) signal confidence.
  • Capital Discipline: NCD redemption (₹600 Cr); funds utilized per plan (construction/working capital); ESOP 2025 approved (90-99% votes).

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Slow Inventory Turnover: 1,519-1,641 days (vs. 1,551 FY25); high unsold inventory (₹9,607 Cr) pressures working capital.
  • Cost Pressures: Finance costs H1 ₹146 Cr (+32% YoY); employee/other expenses up amid expansion.
  • Mumbai Concentration: ~90% assets/revenue in Mumbai; vulnerable to local slowdowns (e.g., regulatory delays).
  • Hospitality Lag: Minor revenue (₹87 Cr H1, flat YoY); EBITDA contribution low (~2%).

Growth Prospects

  • High: Pre-sales conversion driving revenue ramp (Q2 rev +80% QoQ); diversified pipeline (residential, offices, malls, hospitality).
  • Pipeline Catalysts: Festive demand; NCR entry; retail leasing (Sky City/Commerz); potential new launches (implied by bookings).
  • FY26 Outlook: 15-25% bookings growth feasible on premium branding, economic resilience; investor presentations highlight Q2 update.
  • Long-Term: Mixed-use developments; debt capacity for expansion (total debt ₹3,016 Cr, coverage 5.9x).

Key Risks

Overall Summary: Strong Buy Bias. Tailwinds from bookings/margins outweigh headwinds; growth intact via diversification/NCR push. Risks manageable with fortress BS. Target upside 20-30% in 12M (trading at ~8x FY26 EPS est.). Monitor festive bookings/inventory turnover.
