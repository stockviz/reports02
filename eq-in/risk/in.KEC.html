Analysis of KEC International Ltd. (NSE: KEC, BSE: 532714)

KEC International, an RPG Group flagship, is a global EPC player in Power T&D, Civil, Transportation, Renewables, Oil & Gas Pipelines, and Cables & Conductors. FY25 revenue was Rs. 21,847 Cr with OB + L1 at Rs. 44,000 Cr+. Q2/H1 FY26 results (ended Sep 30, 2025) show robust growth: Revenue +19%/15% YoY, EBITDA margins at 7.1%/7.0% (+80/60 bps), PAT +88%/65% YoY. YTD order intake Rs. 17,066 Cr (+17% YoY), OB Rs. 39,325 Cr (+15% YoY). Below is a structured analysis based on the provided documents (Q2 investor presentation, financial results, order wins, PGCIL clarification, etc.).

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Strong T&D Momentum (67% of H1 Revenue): 44% YoY growth to Rs. 4,080 Cr in Q2. Record OB/L1 Rs. 29,000 Cr. International tailwinds in Middle East (Saudi Arabia, UAE, Oman transmission initiatives), Americas (Brazil, Mexico, US uptick), and India (HVDC, STATCOM, digital substations; private players/TBCB rise).
  • Order Book & Pipeline Strength: Total OB + L1 Rs. 44,000 Cr (highest ever). Tender pipeline Rs. 1,80,000 Cr; L1 position Rs. 4,000 Cr+. YTD intake diversified (T&D 70%, domestic 41%).
  • Diversification Wins: Maiden Middle East O&G order (Rs. 1,016 Cr new orders). Civil inflows Rs. 3,000 Cr YTD (luxury villas, steel upstream). Cables +19% YoY; Renewables executing large solar projects.
  • Margin Expansion: EBITDA margins improved via execution efficiency, benign commodity prices, strategic inventory. Interest cost down 50 bps to 2.8-2.9% of sales.
  • ESG & Strategic Moves: Happiness score 84%, diversity +57%, LTIFR -84%. Capacity expansions (towers, E-Beam cables).

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Civil Execution Delays: Q2 revenue -16% YoY (Rs. 968 Cr) due to prolonged monsoon, labor shortages, delayed Water segment payments. OB strong at Rs. 10,000 Cr but impacted cash flows.
  • Transportation Weakness: Revenue -15% YoY (Rs. 425 Cr); muted OB due to competition from road players, project delays affecting margins/cash. Focus on receivables collection.
  • Working Capital Pressure: NWC 138 days (vs 130 last year); net debt + Rs. 1,049 Cr to Rs. 4,615 Cr (total debt Rs. 6,480 Cr). Spillover collections expected in Oct’25.
  • Oil & Gas: Low domestic tendering; shifting to international focus post-prequalification.
  • Macro: Labor shortages persisting in select areas; geopolitical unrests.

Growth Prospects

  • High Confidence in FY26 Targets: MD commentary reaffirms growth amid 17% YTD order growth. T&D to drive 65%+ revenue share; non-T&D diversification (Civil real estate/metals boom, Renewables govt push on Solar/Wind/BESS).
  • Geographic/Market Expansion: 110+ countries; GCC entry (O&G, T&D). Private capex uptick in India; international Civil/Transpo/O&G opportunities.
  • Capacity & Product Ramp-Up: Tower expansions (Butibori, Dubai, etc.); Cables E-Beam/Elastomeric by YE25. Tender pipeline supports sustained inflows.
  • Profitability Trajectory: Margins trending up (EBITDA CAGR 34% Q2 FY23-26); debt normalization expected with collections.
  • YTD Trajectory: OB execution + new wins position for 15-20% revenue growth; PAT margins stable at 2.6%.

Summary

Bull Case: KEC is firing on T&D cylinders with record OB, margin expansion, and diversification (e.g., Middle East O&G). YTD 17% order growth + Rs. 1.8L Cr pipeline signals 15-20% FY26 revenue potential, reaffirming growth targets. International (45% OB) and renewables tailwinds offset domestic headwinds.

Bear Case: Transportation/Civil drags + PGCIL overhang (low but noisy) could pressure cash flows/debt. Probe adds uncertainty, though deemed immaterial.

Overall Outlook: Positive. Strong execution track record (revenue CAGR 14%, PAT 43% Q2 FY23-26), robust fundamentals position KEC for profitable growth. Monitor PGCIL resolution, Q3 execution, and debt normalization. Trading at reasonable multiples given OB visibility; suitable for infrastructure/growth investors. FY26 PAT could exceed FY25’s Rs. 571 Cr if trends hold.
