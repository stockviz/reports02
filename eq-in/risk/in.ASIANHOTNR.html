Analysis of Asian Hotels (North) Limited (ASIANHOTNR)

Company Overview: ASIANHOTNR owns and operates Hotel Hyatt Regency Delhi. It operates in the hospitality segment with stable revenue (~₹147 Cr H1 FY26) but faces severe debt distress. Recent EGM (Nov 8, 2025) approved ₹764.94 Cr preferential equity issue at ₹305.73/share fair value to repay overdue borrowings. Q2/H1 FY26 results (ended Sep 30, 2025) show operational stability but losses due to defaults/penal interest.

Headwinds (Key Challenges)

  • Massive Debt Defaults: Overdue principal ₹589.97 Cr + interest ₹173.27 Cr (incl. ₹56.74 Cr penal) as of Sep 30, 2025. Breached loan covenants; high finance costs (₹37 Cr H1 FY26) erode profitability.
  • Persistent Losses & Weak Liquidity: H1 FY26 net loss ₹77 Cr (EPS -₹39.62); negative operating cash flow ₹125 Cr. Current liabilities (₹1,288 Cr) >> current assets (₹55 Cr).
  • High Operating Leverage: Employee costs (28% of expenses), finance costs (20%), and other expenses dominate; EBITDA margin pressured despite revenue stability.
  • Dilution Risk: Preferential issue will significantly dilute existing shareholders (~2.5 Cr new shares vs. current ~1.95 Cr outstanding).

Tailwinds (Supportive Factors)

  • Debt Restructuring Momentum: EGM resolutions passed overwhelmingly (99.97% for capital increase, 99.96% for preferential issue). Proceeds targeted for full debt repayment within 6 months, slashing future interest (~₹37 Cr H1 run-rate).
  • Revenue Resilience: H1 revenue ₹147 Cr (flat YoY); Q2 up 9% QoQ on improving occupancy/tariffs. Other income ₹7.6 Cr in Q2 (asset sales).
  • Prior Cleanup: FY25 one-time settlements (₹301 Cr gain) reduced legacy debt; non-current borrowings dropped to near-zero.
  • Auditor Comfort: Unmodified review report; emphasis on going concern justified by ops improvement & repayment plan.

Growth Prospects

  • Post-Debt Payoff Turnaround: Debt reduction could cut annual finance costs by 80-90%, flipping losses to profits (pre-exceptional H1 operating loss ₹26.5 Cr; FY25 had ₹232 Cr profit pre-tax). Potential EPS positivity in FY26/H1 FY27.
  • Hospitality Sector Tailwinds: Delhi luxury hotel demand (business/leisure recovery); Hyatt brand strength. Management cites “significant operating improvement” & cost cuts.
  • Asset Monetization: Recent PPE sales (₹20 Cr cash inflow); scope for more non-core sales.
  • Medium-Term: Debt-free balance sheet enables capex/expansion (PPE ₹1,396 Cr core asset). Target ROE recovery to 10-15% if occupancy >70%.

Key Risks

Overall Summary: ASIANHOTNR is at an inflection point—debt overhang caps upside, but approved ₹765 Cr infusion offers deleveraging lifeline, potentially transforming losses into profits. Short-term volatility from dilution/defaults; medium-term bull case if executed (target price upside to fair value ₹306). High-risk/high-reward; monitor allotment timeline & Q3 results. Neutral rating pending debt payoff.
