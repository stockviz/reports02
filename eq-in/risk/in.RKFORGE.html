Analysis of Ramkrishna Forgings Limited (RKFORGE)

Overview: RKFORGE, a forging and machining player serving auto (PV/CV/EV), railways, oil & gas, and mining, reported weak Q2/H1 FY26 results amid global headwinds. Consolidated revenue fell 10.6% QoQ to ₹908 Cr (H1: +4% YoY), EBITDA margin dipped to 13.5% (H1: 14.1%), with PAT loss of ₹9.5 Cr due to one-offs (₹25 Cr forex/tariff/Mexico/JV impact). Capacity utilization slipped to 60% (from 69%). Despite this, domestic traction (railways) and ₹1,116 Cr Q2 order wins signal recovery. Promoter warrants (₹200 Cr new + ₹51 Cr upfront) to deleverage debt (₹2,500 Cr). FY26 guidance: double-digit revenue growth, 17-18% margins.

Headwinds (Short-term Pressures)

  • Export Weakness (56% revenue): US tariffs (25-50%) disrupted shipments; reciprocal duties raised end-prices, slowing OEM demand. Mexico ops loss (₹3 Cr); overall NA subdued post-Q1.
  • Forex & One-offs: ₹6.8 Cr forex loss on equipment imports; tariff settlements (₹10.8 Cr). Utilization drop (60%) hit operating leverage.
  • High Debt & Costs: Debt up to ₹2,500 Cr (+₹600 Cr H1) from ₹485 Cr capex; finance costs rose. Currency volatility (INR depreciation) adds notional hits till JV ops (Mar’26).
  • Global Macro: Geopolitics, supply chain disruptions, OEM inventory rationalization.

Tailwinds (Supportive Factors)

  • Domestic Strength (44% revenue, +8% H1 YoY): GST cuts revived auto sentiment; railways traction (bogies/castings: ₹496 Cr orders; 45k MT casting sold out at 80-85% FY27 util.).
  • Order Inflow: Q2: ₹1,116 Cr (69% auto intl., 27% railways); H1 Europe ₹927 Cr (CV), NA ₹307 Cr (PV). Cumulative ₹6-7k Cr book to yield ₹1k Cr+ FY28 revenue.
  • Promoter Commitment: ₹200 Cr warrants (Chaitanya Jalan) + prior ₹51 Cr; pledges secured (Tata Capital). Debt cut ₹500-600 Cr by Mar’26.
  • Capex Completion: Most done (<₹100 Cr H2); new lines (8kT/3kT/1.6kT press, cold forging 40% util., aluminum bulk from Nov’25).

Growth Prospects (Medium-Term Outlook)

  • FY26: Double-digit revenue (+18-20% H2 volumes); margins to 17-18% (standalone 20% by Q4). H2 ramp from GST/railways/Europe/aluminum/castings.
  • FY27+: 400k MT capacity → ₹6,200-6,500 Cr peak revenue (85% util.); railways JV (40k wheels, ₹1,600-1,700 Cr FY28 rev.); Mexico ₹200 Cr machining (Apr’26).
  • Diversification: Railways/non-auto up (27% Q2 orders); Europe/NA new wins; EV focus (aluminum/cold forging).
  • Long-term: Aspirational >20% margins via value-add (assemblies/B2C); negligible FY27 capex.
