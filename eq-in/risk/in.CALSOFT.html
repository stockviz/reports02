Analysis of California Software Company Limited (CALSOFT)

Company Overview: CALSOFT is an IT/software development company listed on NSE (Symbol: CALSOFT) and BSE (Code: 532386). Incorporated in 1992, it reports modest revenue growth in H1 FY26 (ended Sep 30, 2025) but faces significant financial red flags highlighted in qualified auditor reviews. Recent board actions (Nov 14, 2025) include approving unaudited results and major fundraising/acquisition plans via postal ballot (dispatched Nov 17, 2025, voting Nov 19-Dec 18, 2025).

Headwinds
  • Severe Receivables and Liquidity Issues: Trade receivables ballooned to ₹2,095.52 lakhs (vs. H1 revenue of ₹309.07 lakhs), with long-outstanding dues, no ECL provisions, negative debtors turnover, and no ageing schedule. Auditors note a “liquidity crunch.” Cash equivalents dropped to ₹14.81 lakhs (standalone) from ₹203.75 lakhs (FY25 end), with negative operating cash flow (-₹132.06 lakhs in H1).
  • Auditor Qualifications: Multiple red flags in limited review reports (standalone & consolidated), including unconfirmed payables (₹64 lakhs), unreconciled current tax (₹380.02 lakhs), unverified fixed assets (no register, ₹232.46 lakhs), unimpaired subsidiary investment (₹311.38 lakhs at cost), opening balance mismatches, and lack of age-wise asset/liability details. Subsidiary (Aspire Communications) contributes nil revenue/profit (unreviewed).
  • Weak Balance Sheet: Consolidated other equity negative (-₹209.77 lakhs). High leverage risk from planned borrowings. Employee costs up 73% YoY (₹177.27 lakhs), squeezing margins amid stagnant other income.
  • Historical Context: Notes mention lingering COVID impact, but core issues (receivables, liquidity) persist from prior periods.
Tailwinds
  • Revenue & Profit Momentum: H1 FY26 revenue up 44% YoY (₹309 lakhs vs. ₹215 lakhs), PBT up 69% (₹57 lakhs), PAT up 69% (₹42 lakhs). Q-o-Q stability (Q2 revenue ₹154 lakhs). EPS ₹0.07 (basic/diluted, not annualized).
  • Aggressive Capital Raise Plans: Board approved QIP (₹200 Cr equity/convertibles), FDI/FCCB/FCCPS (USD 100 Mn ~₹850 Cr), auth capital hike (₹175 Cr to ₹225 Cr), and investments/loans up to ₹500 Cr (over Section 186 limits). In-principle nod for acquisitions (Indian/foreign).
  • Strategic Positioning: IT/software focus with no reportable segments; potential for expansion via acquisitions. Postal ballot seeks shareholder nod, signaling proactive growth intent.
  • Governance Steps: Re-appointment of Independent Director (Mr. R.S. Chandan, pharma-IT expert) for 2nd term; scrutinizer appointed for voting.
Growth Prospects
  • High-Upside Fundraising: Successful QIP/FCCB could inject ₹1,050 Cr+ for acquisitions, working capital, capex, and expansion (new territories, portfolios). Aims at “strategic business expansion” and synergies.
  • Acquisition Pipeline: In-principle approval for M&A (subject to approvals), leveraging funds for inorganic growth in IT/software.
  • Operational Leverage: If receivables recover (management notes process ongoing), cash flow could improve. Revenue trajectory (FY25: ₹545 lakhs) suggests scalability in post-COVID normalization.
  • Market Opportunity: IT sector tailwinds (digital transformation); potential forex gains from FDI. Dilution risk offset by growth funding (equity shares at ₹10 FV, post-issue capital flexibility).

Projected Potential: If funds raised and deployed effectively, FY26 revenue could double+ via acquisitions; however, base case remains constrained by collections.
