Analysis of Chembond Material Technologies Limited (CHEMBOND)

Overview: Chembond (formerly Chembond Chemicals) reported strong top-line growth in Q2/H1 FY26 (ended Sep 30, 2025), with consolidated revenue up ~20% YoY to ₹11,542 lakhs (restated), driven by Specialty Chemicals (89% of revenue) and Animal Health (11%). PAT attributable to shareholders was ₹782 lakhs (H1), with EPS ₹5.81 (basic, not annualized). Results reflect post-demerger restatement (NCLT order Apr 2025), focusing on core chemicals and animal nutrition. Balance sheet remains solid (equity ₹16,165 lakhs, low debt), but margins dipped and working capital strained. Standalone revenue ₹9,785 lakhs, PAT ₹580 lakhs.

Tailwinds

  • Robust Revenue Momentum: H1 revenue +20% YoY; Q2 +16%. Specialty Chemicals up ~13% (₹9,851 lakhs), Animal Health surged ~34% (₹1,969 lakhs) with profitability turnaround (segment PBT ₹205 lakhs vs prior loss).
  • Diversified Segments: Two reportable segments (Specialty Chemicals, Animal Health incl. probiotics/feed additives) align with growth areas like industrial chemicals and animal nutrition in India.
  • Strong Balance Sheet: Net worth up 4% to ₹16,165 lakhs; low leverage (borrowings ₹599 lakhs current); liquid assets (investments ₹6,802 lakhs, cash/banks ₹1,479 lakhs).
  • Investment Income: Other income ₹278 lakhs (H1), from gains on sales (₹1,243 lakhs proceeds).
  • Capex Progress: PPE up 20% to ₹4,012 lakhs; supports capacity expansion.

Headwinds

  • Margin Compression: Segment PBT margins softened (Specialty Chemicals 3.4% vs prior higher); consolidated PBT ₹821 lakhs (-31% YoY excl. prior exceptional). High material costs (₹6,616 lakhs, 57% of revenue) and finance costs (₹1,819 lakhs).
  • Working Capital Drag: Trade receivables +26% to ₹5,015 lakhs; other assets +343% to ₹1,108 lakhs → negative operating cash flow (-₹138 lakhs H1).
  • Restated Comparatives: YoY growth appears muted post-demerger restatement (e.g., prior H1 revenue halved from published ₹23,248 lakhs).
  • Cash Burn: Net cash down ₹135 lakhs H1; dividend payout ₹235 lakhs amid capex (₹1,131 lakhs outflow).

Growth Prospects

  • High: Animal Health segment’s turnaround (from loss to ₹205 lakhs PBT) signals potential; overall revenue trajectory +20% YoY amid India’s chemicals/animal feed demand (rising livestock sector, industrial recovery).
  • Strategic Focus: Post-demerger purity (specialty chems + nutrition); ongoing capex (CWIP ₹147 lakhs); subsidiary (Chembond Biosciences) integrated.
  • Projections: FY26 revenue could hit ₹22,000-24,000 lakhs (20%+ growth) if margins stabilize at 7-8%; EPS potential ₹13-15 annualized. Benefits from Ind AS compliance and tax holiday continuity.
  • Catalysts: Export potential in probiotics/enzymes; domestic capex cycle in water treatment/construction chems.

Key Risks

  • Working Capital/Liquidity: Receivables collection cycle risk (ECL provision noted); could worsen cash flows if sales credit terms loosen.
  • Margin Volatility: Commodity input costs (materials 57% of sales); forex exposure (gains/losses noted).
  • Execution Post-Scheme: Demerger integration (IND AS 103 applied); restated figures may mask true trends.
  • Segment Concentration: 89% revenue from Specialty Chemicals; Animal Health still nascent (11%).
  • Macro/Regulatory: Chemicals cyclicality (industrial slowdown); insider trading window closures; SEBI scrutiny (recent clarification on results).
  • Low Debt but High Finance Costs: ₹1,819 lakhs H1 suggests embedded costs or lease-related.

Investment Summary: Positive outlook with revenue tailwinds outweighing near-term margin headwinds. Growth prospects strong in focused segments, but monitor working capital (key red flag). Suitable for long-term investors eyeing 15-20% CAGR; target P/E 12-15x FY26 EPS. Hold/Buy on dips, with risks tilted to execution.
