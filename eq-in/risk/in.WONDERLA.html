Wonderla Holidays Limited (WONDERLA) Analysis

Overview: Wonderla operates India’s leading chain of amusement parks (Kochi, Bangalore, Hyderabad, Bhubaneswar) with resorts, focusing on family entertainment. Q2 FY26 showed revenue growth (19% YoY to ₹80 Cr) and EBITDA turnaround (8x to ₹7.5 Cr), driven by ARPU (up 5% to ₹1,478) amid weak seasonal footfalls (5.05L). H1 revenue up 3% YoY to ₹248 Cr, PAT ₹51 Cr. Strong balance sheet (net cash ₹190 Cr+), debt-free. Chennai park (₹611 Cr capex, 64 acres) opens Dec 2025, marking 5th park.

Headwinds

  • Seasonality & Weather: Q2 (monsoon) weakest quarter; rains/cyclones hit Hyderabad/Bangalore footfalls (e.g., H1 Hyderabad -3% revenue). Mature parks yet to surpass FY23 post-COVID peaks.
  • Flat Mature Park Growth: Footfalls stable (Bangalore ~5L H1, Kochi 4.3L) but capacity-constrained; no same-store sales surge.
  • High Costs: Q2 PAT negative (₹1.75 Cr loss) due to depreciation (₹17 Cr), employee costs (₹21 Cr). Maintenance capex ~10% revenue.
  • New Park Ramp-Up: Bhubaneswar low footfalls (1.2L H1), EBITDA breakeven; Chennai expected 3-4 yr ramp to 10-12L annual visitors.

Tailwinds

  • ARPU Premiumization: Non-ticket revenue (30% mix) double-digit growth (SPH ₹461, +7% YoY); F&B/merch strong even in Tier-2 (Bhubaneswar).
  • Digital/Marketing Shift: ~50% bookings digital; campaigns (e.g., Onam, Independence Day) drove Q2 footfall resilience.
  • Resort Strength: Isle Bangalore occupancy 55% (Q2), revenue +₹2 Cr upside; EBITDA +2%.
  • Cost Efficiencies: In-house rides (1/3 of portfolio) at 1/4th import cost (e.g., Ferris wheel ₹3-4 Cr vs. ₹12 Cr); easier maintenance/safety.
  • Robust Balance Sheet: Cash ₹165 Cr (up sharply), investments ₹265 Cr; funds expansions via internal accruals/debt-equity mix.

Growth Prospects

  • Chennai Launch: Large-format park (43 rides incl. India’s first B&M Inverted Coaster); capacity 6,500/day; Tier-1 market potential (10-12L footfalls long-term).
  • Expansion Pipeline: 2 parks/3 yrs in Tier-1/2 cities (govt discussions ongoing); underutilized land (30-40% in mature parks) for rides/resorts.
  • ARPU/Non-Ticket: Target premium F&B/events; digital loyalty post-transformation; 8-10% overall revenue CAGR guided.
  • Resort Rollout: Integrate at Chennai/other parks; Isle success scalable.
  • Innovation: New rides (e.g., ₹20 Cr US-import coaster in Bangalore FY27); events/marketing for lean seasons.

Key Risks

  • Execution Delays: Govt approvals/land acquisition lumpy (e.g., Chennai took years); capex overruns (CAGR 20-25% H1).
  • External Shocks: Weather (monsoons 40% risk), elections, economic slowdowns hit discretionary spends (13-24 yr demographic vulnerable).
  • Competition/Fragmentation: Nascent India market; pricing pressure in new parks (Bhubaneswar ATP down).
  • Margin Volatility: High fixed costs (depreciation 22% revenue); EBITDA margins swung (Q2 9% vs. H1 34%).
  • Regulatory: Tax changes (e.g., LTCG indexation removal); safety standards (EN/IS compliant but scrutiny risk).

Summary

Bull Case (Tailwinds Dominate): Debt-free cash machine with premium brand (46M+ lifetime visitors); Chennai unlocks South India scale (20-25% revenue add in 3 yrs); ARPU levers + expansions = 15-20% CAGR to FY28E. Target: ₹800-1,000/sh (P/E 40-50x FY27E). Bear Case (Headwinds Persist): Seasonality caps earnings (Q2 losses); expansion delays erode moat; weather risks footfalls. Target: ₹500-600/sh (P/E 25-30x). Overall: Positive outlook (Buy/Hold); growth reaccelerating post-Chennai (FY26E PAT ₹140-160 Cr). Risks mitigated by cash buffer (₹1,900 Cr assets), in-house efficiencies. Monitor Q3 footfalls/Chennai ramp. (Valuation: FY26E P/E ~45x vs. peers 30-40x; justified by 20%+ ROCE.)
