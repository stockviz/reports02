Analysis of Aarti Drugs Limited (AARTIDRUGS)

Aarti Drugs Limited, a diversified pharma player focused on APIs (81% revenue), formulations, specialty chemicals, and intermediates, reported strong Q2/H1 FY26 results amid export resilience offsetting domestic softness. Consolidated revenue grew 9%/8% YoY to ₹653 Cr/₹1,244 Cr, with EBITDA margins expanding 150/120 bps to 12.9%/12.8% and PAT surging 29%/45% YoY. Backward integration (Sayakha amines plant operational) and capacity ramps are key margin drivers, with debt reduced to ₹571 Cr (D/E: 0.39x). Recent High Court win nullified ₹20.72 Cr GST demand. All AGM resolutions passed overwhelmingly.

Tailwinds (Positive Factors)

  • Margin Expansion & Cost Discipline: EBITDA margins improved sequentially/QoQ due to favorable export mix, stabilizing raw material prices (post-FIFO unwind), and backward integration. Gross margins up 310 bps YoY to 37.5% in Q2.
  • Export Strength: Exports drove 9% YoY revenue growth (33% standalone export YoY); 42% of standalone revenue from exports. Diversified across 100+ countries (Asia 52%, no region >2/3rd).
  • Backward Integration Success: Sayakha (methylamines) commenced Sep 2025; 40-50% captive Metformin needs met (full by FY26-end), reducing RM dependency (10-14% revenue impact). Side products monetized externally (18-20% EBITDA target).
  • Debt Reduction & Cash Flows: Net debt down ₹41 Cr H1; strong operating cash flow (₹152 Cr H1). Low leverage (D/E 0.39x) supports growth capex (₹150-200 Cr FY26).
  • Regulatory & ESG Progress: EU/USFDA certifications underway (Metformin US post-FY26); CRISIL ESG-52 rating enhances appeal.
  • Legal Win: Bombay HC set aside CGST order (₹20.72 Cr refund demand + penalty dropped; no financial impact).

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Domestic Weakness: Antibiotics (36% API revenue) saw lackluster demand (-2% volume YoY); Q3 typically lean, pickup expected Q4.
  • New Capacity Ramp Risks: Salicylic acid (Tarapur) absorbing ₹3.5 Cr losses (stabilizing at 300 tpm; EBITDA+ at 800 tpm). Sayakha low utilization initially.
  • Incident at Tarapur: Temporary voluntary closure of chlorosulfonation (HCL leak Sep 8); no material impact (inventory/alternates available).
  • Formulation Scale: Still small (13% revenue); domestic antibiotic pressure indirect hit.

Growth Prospects

  • Short-Term (FY26 H2): High single-digit revenue growth; EBITDA margins to 13-15% via ramps (salicylic 500 tpm Q4, Sayakha full captive).
  • Medium-Term (FY27): Mid-teens volume growth; 15% EBITDA target (pre-COVID levels). Salicylic chain (1,600 tpm by FY26-end) to drive import substitution.
  • Pipeline: Bicalutamide (US) this quarter; anti-diabetics (Europe/UK); ₹60-70 Cr top-line from new regulated products (12-18 months). Formulations: 330 filings (16 geographies); oncology USFDA-approved.
  • Capex/Long-Term: ₹200 Cr FY26/FY27 (Metformin expansion); 5-10 yr portfolio for ₹5,000-10,000 Cr revenue (GLP-1 evaluation). 15-20% CAGR targeted.
  • Industry Tailwinds: India API #3 globally (8% share); PLI/Atmanirbhar support; regulated market entry.
