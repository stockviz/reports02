AIROLAM Limited (Scrip: AIROLAM) Analysis

Company Overview: AIROLAM Limited (CIN: L20211GJ2007PLC052019) is a Gujarat-based manufacturer of laminates, plywood, panels, and doors. Listed on NSE, it reported strong H1 FY26 (ended Sep 30, 2025) standalone results with revenue of ₹115.8 Cr (up 12.5% YoY), PAT of ₹4.15 Cr (vs loss of ₹0.56 Cr YoY), and improving margins. Consolidated figures are similar, with minor subsidiary contributions (indirect sub: loss-making; associate: negligible). 18th AGM (Sep 25, 2025) saw unanimous approval of resolutions. Balance sheet shows asset growth to ₹231.7 Cr, but high debt (₹86.8 Cr total borrowings).

Tailwinds (Positive Factors)

  • Robust Revenue & Profit Recovery: H1 revenue +12.5% YoY to ₹115.8 Cr; Q2 alone ₹57.2 Cr (+11.7% YoY). PAT swing from loss to ₹4.15 Cr; EPS ₹2.76 (vs -₹0.37). Laminates segment (86% revenue) drove 14% YoY growth to ₹151 Cr (gross).
  • Margin Expansion: Operating margin 8% (Q2), NPM 3.6% (up from negative YoY). Cost efficiencies in materials (despite volatility) and lower inventory changes.
  • Operational Cash Flow Strength: ₹6.56 Cr from ops (despite WC drag), supporting capex (PPE ₹55.9 Cr).
  • Liquidity Stability: Current ratio ~1.34 (stable/improving); cash equivalents up to ₹0.35 Cr.
  • Segment Momentum: Laminates profitable (₹8.95 Cr segment profit H1); plywood turnaround (₹0.33 Cr profit vs loss YoY).
  • Governance: Unanimous AGM voting; timely disclosures/compliance.

Headwinds (Negative Factors)

  • High Leverage & Finance Costs: Debt/equity 1.08 (standalone); total debt ₹86.8 Cr (37% of assets). Finance costs ₹4.19 Cr H1 (up YoY); DSCR 1.29 (moderate coverage).
  • Working Capital Pressure: Trade receivables +15% to ₹70 Cr; inventory ₹83.9 Cr (high days). Ops cash down 75% YoY due to ₹9.3 Cr receivables buildup and ₹11.6 Cr inventory rise.
  • Plywood Weakness: Still low-margin (3% of profits); historical losses.
  • Subsidiary Drag: Indirect sub (Airolam Asia Pacific) reported ₹0.32 Cr Q2 loss (assets ₹2.95 Cr, revenue ₹0.52 Cr H1); non-material but forex/export risks.
  • Cyclical Exposure: Real estate/housing slowdown could hit demand (80% domestic).

Growth Prospects

  • High (Medium-Term: 15-20% CAGR Potential):
