Analysis of Bajaj Holdings & Investment Ltd. (BHIL / BAJAJHLDNG) – H1 FY26 (Ended 30 Sep 2025)

Overview:
BHIL, a pure-play holding & investment company, reported robust H1 FY26 results driven by strong associate performance (Bajaj Auto - BAL, Bajaj Finserv - BFS) and a one-time gain from partial sale of BFS stake (₹1.04 Cr shares sold for ₹1,522 Cr profit consolidated). Consolidated PAT surged 66% YoY to ₹5,046 Cr (excl. BFS sale gain: +20% YoY). Standalone PAT jumped 277% to ₹4,217 Cr. Investment portfolio market value hit ₹236,429 Cr (up 6% QoQ). NAV/share at ₹21,244 (book value ₹2,045). Interim dividend of ₹65/share (₹723 Cr) declared. Company aligning to RBI CIC norms; insurance stake acquisition from Allianz progressing.

Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts)

  • Associate Strength: BAL delivered record domestic revenue (premium bikes + double-digit CV growth; EV Chetak scaling despite supply issues; exports strong; ₹14,244 Cr surplus cash). BFS consolidated PAT +19% YoY (Bajaj Finance loans +24%; insurance GWP +9-20% adjusted).
  • One-Time Boost: BFS stake sale funded insurance buyout; BFS remains key associate (40.85% stake).
  • Macro Support: Recent GST reforms to aid auto/Finserv growth.
  • Investment Portfolio: Strategic holdings (BAL/BFS) market value ₹217,786 Cr (+4% QoQ); debt/FV gains steady.
  • Capital Returns: High dividend payout; realignment to CIC guidelines unlocked ₹2,073 Cr realized gains on non-group equities.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Non-Recurring Gain: ~30% of PAT from BFS sale; core growth reliant on volatile associate profits (₹3,511 Cr share, 70% of total).
  • Supply Constraints: BAL’s EV scooter (Chetak) hampered by erratic supply.
  • Associate-Specific: Pierer Bajaj AG (KTM, 49.9% via BAL) faced past impairments/restructuring losses; quarterly data gaps noted in audit (modified review opinion).
  • Tax/OCI Volatility: Deferred tax hit from FY25 capital gains tax changes (₹91 Cr one-time OCI impact); OCI swung negative in Q2.
  • Portfolio Realignment: Shift to RBI CIC norms led to equity sales, reducing liquidity in non-strategic holdings.

Growth Prospects

  • High (Strong/Medium-Term):
    • Insurance Expansion: Approvals (CCI/IRDAI/RoC) secured for 26% Allianz stake buyout in Bajaj General/Life Insurance; BHIL to acquire up to 19.95% (~next few months). Boosts direct exposure to high-growth insurance (GWP +16-20% adj.).
    • Auto/Finserv Tailwinds: BAL’s premium/EV/CV scaling + exports; BFS loan book/AUM growth (₹28,814 Cr AUM). GST reforms to accelerate volumes.
    • CIC Re-Categorization: Portfolio realignment enhances regulatory compliance; potential for optimized yields.
    • NAV Leverage: Investments yield ₹21,244/share NAV (10x book); dividend income +28% YoY (₹2,205 Cr).
  • Projected Trajectory: Ex-BFS sale, PAT +20% YoY sustainable; insurance deal could add ₹5-10 Bn annual earnings (est. based on current insurance PAT).

Insurance acquisition is key unlock for diversification/growth. Risks tilted to concentration/execution, but strong balance sheet (no debt, high liquidity) and NAV premium support premium valuation. Monitor CIC nod & PBAG updates. Target upside on insurance closure; suitable for long-term holding. (Data as of 11 Nov 2025 filings).
