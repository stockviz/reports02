Analysis of ESAB India Limited (ESABINDIA, Scrip Code: 500133)

ESAB India Limited, a leader in fabrication technology (welding equipment, consumables, and automation), reported robust unaudited H1 FY26 results (ended Sep 30, 2025). Revenue grew 10% YoY to ₹73,357 Lakhs, driven by volume/mix improvements. PAT surged 37% YoY to ₹12,014 Lakhs (EPS ₹78.06), boosted by a ₹3,091 Lakhs exceptional gain from land sale at Khardah (now fully disposed). Core PBT grew 5.5% YoY to ₹12,522 Lakhs. Balance sheet remains strong (net cash positive, equity up 15% to ₹41,665 Lakhs), but operating cash flow moderated to ₹4,922 Lakhs due to working capital pressures. Management declared ₹25/share interim dividend (payout ~32% of H1 PAT). Single operating segment (Fabrication Technology) per Ind AS 108.

Below is a structured analysis of headwinds, tailwinds, growth prospects, and key risks, followed by an overall summary.

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

Factor Details Impact
Revenue Momentum Q2 revenue +13% YoY (₹38,155 Lakhs); H1 +10%. Strong sequential growth (Q1 to Q2: +8%). Other income stable. Supports market share gains in industrial/welding demand.
Profitability Surge Core PBT +5.5% YoY (H1); PAT +37% incl. exceptional. Employee expenses +17% YoY but controlled; raw materials as % of revenue down (supports margins ~28-30% implied). EPS +37% YoY; ROE ~30% annualized.
Exceptional Gain ₹3,091 Lakhs one-time profit from land disposal (asset held for sale cleared). Boosts equity; enables dividends/capex.

Operational Efficiency Depreciation steady; forex gains aiding. Multi-plant setup (Ambattur, Irungattukottai, Kalmeshwar) scaling. Cost controls evident in “Other expenses” flat QoQ.

Growth Prospects

  • High: Industrial capex cycle in India (infrastructure, shipbuilding, auto, energy) favors welding/fabrication. H1 growth outpaces GDP; potential 15-20% FY26 revenue CAGR if trends hold.
  • Expansion Enablers: Ongoing capex (CWoIP ₹487 Lakhs); new plants operational. Single segment but diversified end-markets.
  • Shareholder Returns: Progressive dividends (₹67/share FY26 so far vs. FY25 ₹42 final); buyback potential from cash pile.
  • Outlook: Management confidence via interim dividend. FY26 guidance implicit: 12-15% revenue growth, sustained 20%+ margins ex-exceptional.

Overall Summary

Bullish with Cautions: ESABINDIA delivered stellar H1 FY26 (revenue +10%, PAT +37%), fueled by organic growth and land sale gain, reinforcing its premium positioning in niche fabrication. Tailwinds from demand and efficiency outweigh headwinds like working capital strain. Growth prospects strong (double-digit FY26 trajectory) amid India’s infra boom, but normalized earnings ~₹20,000-22,000 Lakhs FY26 (ex-gain). Key watch: Receivables cycle and ops cash flow recovery. Recommendation: Accumulate on dips; attractive for dividend yield (~2-3%) + growth (PEG <1). Risks moderate, balance sheet fortress-like. Full FY results key for confirmation.
