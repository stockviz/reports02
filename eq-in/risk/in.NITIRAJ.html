Analysis of Nitiraj Engineers Limited (NITIRAJ)

Nitiraj Engineers Limited (NSE: NITIRAJ, ISIN: INE439T01012) is engaged in manufacturing industrial electrical equipment, electronic weighing scales/systems, currency counting machines, fare meters, and home automation products (single reportable segment per Ind AS 108). It operates from Dhule (corp office/works) with a debt-free balance sheet (no borrowings). Below is a structured analysis based on the Q2/H1 FY26 unaudited financials (ended Sep 30, 2025), FY25 audited results, balance sheet, cash flows, and 26th AGM outcomes (Sep 23, 2025). All figures in Rs. Lacs unless stated.

Tailwinds (Positive Factors)

  • Robust Revenue Growth & Profit Turnaround: H1 FY26 revenue from operations grew 60% YoY to ₹2,692.61 (Q2: +30% YoY to ₹1,638.89). Total H1 revenue up 60% to ₹2,769. Shifted from H1 FY25 loss of ₹162.79 to H1 FY26 profit of ₹130.38 (PAT margin ~4.7%). Q2 alone delivered ₹162.78 PAT. Builds on strong FY25 (revenue ₹7,652, PAT ₹483; EPS ₹4.71).
  • Debt-Free & Improving Liquidity: Zero borrowings; cash & equivalents up to ₹265 (from ₹205 at FY25-end). Net cash increase of ₹60 in H1. Promoter confidence via AGM approvals for director remuneration hikes/reappointments (all resolutions passed 99.99-100% valid votes).
  • Asset Expansion: PPE up 11% to ₹3,538 (capex ₹523 in H1); intangibles under development ₹197. Equity strong at ₹8,412 (Other Equity +2% YoY).
  • Shareholder Alignment: High promoter holding (~69%; 7.08M shares); 83% voter turnout at AGM. Dividend declared for FY25 (quantum not specified).

Headwinds (Negative Factors)

  • Working Capital Pressure: Sharp inventory buildup (+26% to ₹2,073) and trade receivables doubling (+99% to ₹1,345) strained ops cash flow to -₹815 (vs. -₹319 H1 FY25). Driven by 430% YoY receivables increase in H1.
  • Quarterly Volatility: Q1 FY26 loss of ₹32 (PBT -₹43); profitability concentrated in Q2. Other expenses high (33% of Q2 revenue).
  • Investment Dependence: H1 investing inflow (+₹879) mainly from ₹1,725 investment sales (current investments down 81% to ₹349). Reinvested ₹225 in new investments.
  • Modest Margins: Gross margins implied ~49% in Q2 (post-material costs/inventory changes), but EBITDA-like pre-tax margin ~13% in H1. Finance/depreciation costs persist (₹3.6/₹172 H1).

Growth Prospects

  • High Revenue Trajectory: FY25 revenue ₹76 Cr sets base for FY26 doubling (H1 already 35% of FY25). Tailwinds from industrial/automation demand (weighing scales, electrical equipment). Single-segment focus aids scalability.
  • Capex Momentum: Ongoing investments in PPE/intangibles signal capacity expansion (potentially for new products like RPTO/home automation). Positive inventory changes suggest order book buildup.
  • Profit Scaling: If Q1 weakness reverses (seasonal?), H1 PAT pace implies FY26 PAT >₹400 (EPS >₹3.9). Debt-free status supports organic growth; cash from investing could fund ops if receivables cycle improves.
  • Market Opportunity: NSE-listed small-cap (10.25M shares, FV ₹10); potential re-rating on 60% growth. Website/NSE disclosures indicate improving governance.

Projected FY26 (Optimistic): Revenue ₹14,000-16,000 Lacs (+85-110% YoY); PAT ₹400-500 (if margins hold 5-6%).

Summary

Nitiraj Engineers shows strong growth momentum (60% H1 revenue surge, profit turnaround) with tailwinds from debt-free status, capex, and governance approvals, positioning for FY26 doubling. However, headwinds from working capital strain (negative ops cash, receivables spike) pose near-term risks, potentially capping upside if not managed. Growth prospects are solid (industrial demand, capacity build) but hinge on cash conversion. Overall Outlook: Positive (Buy/Hold for growth investors) with key watch: Q3 results for sustained profitability and receivables trend. Target multiples: 1.5-2x FY26 sales (small-cap premium). Risk-reward favors longs if macros hold.
