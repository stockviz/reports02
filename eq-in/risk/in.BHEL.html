BHEL Analysis: Headwinds, Tailwinds, Growth Prospects, and Key Risks

BHEL, a leading PSU in power equipment and EPC, shows signs of recovery in Q2FY26 (ended Sep 2025) with revenue growth and a major order win, but faces working capital pressures and legacy receivable issues. H1FY26 standalone revenue grew 8% YoY to ₹12,999 Cr (Power segment up 5% YoY), but net loss of ₹87 Cr persisted due to Q1 weakness. Order intake boosted by NTPC’s ₹6,650 Cr+ supercritical thermal project. Below is a structured summary based on the filings.

Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts)

  • Strong Order Inflow: Secured ₹6,650 Cr+ (excl. GST) EPC NOA from NTPC for 1x800 MW Darlipali STPP Stage-II (48-month execution). Enhances order book in core power segment amid thermal capacity addition push.
  • Revenue Momentum: Q2 revenue ₹7,512 Cr (+14% YoY), driven by Power (₹5,676 Cr). H1 segment results improved (Power: ₹84 Cr profit vs. prior loss).
  • Regulatory Wins: Favorable GST appellate order sets aside ₹10.76 Cr demand + ₹7.74 Cr interest + ₹1.07 Cr penalty for FY21, aiding cash flow.
  • Strategic Flexibility: Postal ballot to amend AoA aligns with Maharatna status, enabling easier JVs/subsidiary formation and divestments per govt. guidelines.
  • PSU Leverage: Benefits from govt. infra/power focus (e.g., NTPC as key client); Industry segment resilient (H1 profit ₹587 Cr).

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Profitability Volatility: H1 net loss ₹87 Cr (Q2 profit ₹368 Cr offset Q1 ₹455 Cr loss); high finance costs (₹376 Cr H1) and other expenses drag margins (net profit ratio -0.67%).
  • Working Capital Strain: Inventories up 26% to ₹12,475 Cr; negative operating cash flow ₹1,182 Cr H1 due to receivables/inventories buildup. Borrowings rose to ₹10,748 Cr; CP outstanding ₹1,300 Cr.
  • Segment Imbalance: Power segment Q1 loss ₹510 Cr; execution delays/inventory pile-up evident.
  • High Employee Costs: ₹2,942 Cr H1 (up YoY), reflecting PSU wage structure.

Growth Prospects

  • Order Book Expansion: NTPC win signals thermal power revival (supercritical tech expertise). Potential for more PSUs (e.g., power capacity targets); Industry segment (34% H1 revenue) steady at ₹586 Cr profit.
  • Revenue Trajectory: FY26 guidance implicit via H1 growth; full-year revenue could exceed FY25 ₹28,339 Cr if Q3/Q4 sustains Q2 momentum.
  • Diversification: Maharatna-enabled JVs/subsidiaries for overseas/renewables entry; JV contributions (e.g., BGGTS ₹28 Cr H1 profit share).
  • EBITDA Potential: Q2 operating margin 7.7%; scaling execution could drive FY26 PAT positivity (FY25 ₹513 Cr).

Key Risks

  • Receivables/Provisioning: Overdues ₹211 Cr (Sudan civil war; no provision per Board) + ₹98 Cr potential (RVUNL Suratgarh; partial payments received). Auditor emphasis; could impair PBT by ₹309 Cr if provisioned.
  • Liquidity/Cash Burns: CFO negative; reliant on short-term borrowings/CP (A1+ rated, but rising). Debt-to-assets 0.15x stable but monitored.
  • Execution/Geo-Political: Project delays (e.g., RVUNL extensions), forex volatility (unrealized gains ₹270 Cr H1), commodity inflation.
  • Macro/Competition: Slow power capex, private sector rivalry (L&T, Siemens); no subsidiaries, JV losses (e.g., RPCL/NBPPL fully impaired).
  • Regulatory/Policy: GST/audit scrutiny; dividend payout (₹174 Cr H1) amid losses strains equity.

Overall Outlook: Moderately positive with order tailwinds offsetting H1 headwinds. Focus on receivables resolution and inventory optimization key for FY26 turnaround. Stock likely supported by PSU rally/govt. orders, but monitor Q3 results for sustained profitability. (Ratings: CARE A1+/Ind A1+ for CP).
