Analysis of HINDPETRO (Hindustan Petroleum Corporation Ltd.) based on Q2 & H1 FY26 Results and Announcements

HPCL delivered a stellar H1 FY26 with standalone PAT surging 731% YoY to ₹8,201 Cr (Q2: ₹3,830 Cr, +507% YoY) and consolidated PAT up to ₹7,970 Cr (+926% YoY), driven by robust GRM (H1: US$5.95/bbl vs. US$4.03 prior), record refining throughput (13.23 MMT, +9.7% YoY), and marketing volumes (25.11 MMT, +3.5% YoY). Revenue stable at ~₹230K Cr (standalone ops). Debt equity improved to 1.07x (standalone) from 1.38x FY25-end. Interim dividend of ₹5/share declared (record date Nov 6, 2025). Key capex of ₹6,117 Cr focused on expansions.

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Refining Strength: Record throughput (Visakh/Mumbai at 105-108% capacity); 5 new crude grades processed in H1 enhances flexibility. GRM jump supports margins amid stable crude prices.
  • Marketing Momentum: Domestic sales +2.7-3.6% YoY; LPG +6.2%; aviation +6.1% (vs. industry -2%). Pipeline throughput up; retail outlets at 24,252 (+351 in Q2).
  • Infrastructure Push: HRRL 89% complete (crude-in expected CY25); RUF Visakh pre-commissioning; new pipelines (BPPL, Bhatinda-Sangrur); India’s largest LPG cavern commissioned.
  • Sustainability & Diversification: 17 CBG plants (136 LOIs, 332 TMT capacity); 94% solarized outlets; 1.5 MWp floating solar; CGD expansion (13K+ inch-km steel pipeline). Project Samriddhi accruals: ₹823 Cr.
  • Financial Discipline: Debt reduction; strong cash from ops (₹19K Cr standalone); govt compensation (₹7,920 Cr for LPG under-recoveries phased from Nov 2025).
  • Awards/Recognition: SCOPE Eminence for governance; ET Martech+ for campaigns.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • LPG Under-Recoveries: Negative buffer ₹14,241 Cr unrecognized (no revenue booked pending GOI nod), pressuring working capital.
  • Forex & Other Costs: ₹798 Cr forex loss in H1 other expenses (vs. ₹44 Cr prior); elevated finance costs (₹1,510 Cr H1).
  • Crude Quality Issues: HOECL-sourced crude caused Mumbai Refinery shutdowns in Oct 2025; impact under assessment.
  • Margin Volatility: Dependent on GRM; Q2 exports modest (0.91 MMT).
  • High Debt Base: Outstanding debt ₹55.8K Cr (standalone); current ratio 0.59x signals liquidity pressure.

Growth Prospects

  • Capacity Ramp-Up: HRRL (9 MMTPA) & RUF Visakh to boost refining to ~25 MMTPA+; new tech (HP-NTO licensing).
  • Retail/CGD Expansion: Target 2,113 CNG outlets; PNG connections to 133K+; aviation ASF to 58.
  • Energy Transition: CBG (scale to 332 TMT); SAF, renewables; HPGRDC’s 684 patents (272 granted) for green tech.
  • Pipeline/Infra: Extended network for efficiency; UDAN-aligned aviation growth.
  • Export/Margin Upside: New crudes, high utilization could sustain GRM >US$6/bbl if oil stable.
  • Long-Term: Govt PSU with Maharatna status; FY26 capex focus on EVs, green energy positions for net-zero.

Overall Summary: Strong Buy Signal – Tailwinds from ops excellence, expansions, and GRM outpace headwinds like subsidies/forex. Growth anchored in refining/marketing scale-up and green energy. Risks manageable via govt backing and cash generation, but monitor oil prices and project timelines. Target upside from HRRL commissioning; maintain positive outlook for FY26 PAT >₹15K Cr (standalone est.). ESG disclosure neutral.
