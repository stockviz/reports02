Analysis of VIJI Finance Limited (VIJIFIN)

The provided documents cover (1) ROC approval for a 3-month AGM extension for FY25 (to Dec 2025) and (2) Q2/H1 FY26 (ended Sep 30, 2025) unaudited results, showing persistent losses amid stable revenue but elevated costs. Below is a structured analysis of headwinds, tailwinds, growth prospects, and key risks, based solely on the documents.

Headwinds (Challenges Pressuring Performance)

  • Persistent Losses and Margin Pressure: H1 FY26 net loss of ₹42.69 lakhs (vs. ₹30.66 lakhs loss in H1 FY25; FY25 full-year profit ₹16.87 lakhs). Q2 loss ₹9.69 lakhs driven by high expenses (total ₹174.92 lakhs, up 11% YoY), including finance costs (↑106% YoY to ₹34.94 lakhs), employee expenses, depreciation, and one-offs like ₹40.75 lakhs provision on substandard assets (Q1) and ₹28.23 lakhs listing fees (Q2).
  • Asset Quality Deterioration: Provisions signal non-performing/substandard loans, impacting profitability in a high-interest-rate environment where finance costs are rising sharply.
  • Operational Inefficiencies: Other expenses spiked (₹83.02 lakhs in H1, including legacy right-issue costs). Employee costs remain elevated (₹40.56 lakhs) despite no growth indicators.
  • Governance/Delay Signals: AGM extension (due to unspecified reasons) and resignation of Non-Executive Non-Independent Director (Aryaman Kothari, eff. Nov 3, 2025) may indicate internal disruptions or compliance strains.

Tailwinds (Supportive Factors)

  • Stable Core Revenue: Interest income grew modestly (H1 ₹113.77 lakhs, ↑4% YoY), with total income up 14% to ₹131.45 lakhs, bolstered by non-operating interest on FDRs (₹17.35 lakhs).
  • Deleveraging and Liquidity: Borrowings reduced 8% to ₹1,154.90 lakhs; cash equivalents + bank balances healthy at ₹535.36 lakhs (end-H1). Positive operating cash flow (₹90.78 lakhs) despite losses, aided by provisions and working capital adjustments.
  • Balance Sheet Resilience: Equity stable at ₹1,425 lakhs (face value ₹1); reserves dipped modestly to ₹671.14 lakhs. Loans stable at ₹2,693 lakhs, with low leverage (borrowings ~34% of assets).
  • Regulatory Compliance: ROC granted AGM extension; results reviewed by auditors (unmodified opinion) and disclosed per SEBI Reg 30/33.

Growth Prospects

  • Moderate Upside in Lending: Core lending book (₹2,693 lakhs) stable; potential to expand if provisions reverse and interest income scales (historical FY25 growth). Single-segment focus (finance services) allows nimble operations.
  • Recent Capital Infusion Benefits: FY25 right issue (₹860+ lakhs proceeds per cash flow) and listing (NSE/BSE/CSE) provide visibility/funding base. Deleveraging could lower future finance costs, enabling NIM expansion.
  • Cash Generation Potential: Positive CFO (₹90.78 lakhs) supports reinvestment. FY26 H1 total assets ₹3,444 lakhs (slight dip); room for loan growth if asset quality stabilizes.
  • Outlook: Break-even possible in H2 FY26 if expenses normalize (e.g., no repeat provisions/listing fees) and lending picks up in a softening rate cycle. Long-term: Scale via digital/rural lending (inferred from Indore base), but FY25 profit reversion key.
