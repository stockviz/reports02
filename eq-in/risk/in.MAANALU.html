Analysis of Maan Aluminium Limited (MAANALU)

Maan Aluminium is a leading Indian aluminum extruder (top 5 by volume), focusing on manufacturing (core, ~70-80% revenue) with trading as a low-margin side business (1% EBITDA). FY26 H1 revenue grew 5% YoY to ₹402 Cr, EBITDA +21% to ₹18 Cr (5% margin), but margins remain depressed vs. FY23 peaks (9-18%). Company doubled extrusion capacity to 24k TPA, emphasizing value-add (anodizing, machining) for 15-18% margins vs. vanilla 8-10%. Exports ~45-50% (US-heavy), trading stable. Recent ₹83 Cr preferential issue (59L shares @ ₹141) to fund CAPEX/WC.

Tailwinds

  • Capacity & Value-Add Expansion: Extrusion doubled (Italian press online), foundry 12k TPA, anodizing 3.6k TPA, machining 1.4k TPA. Dewas plant (₹46 Cr CAPEX) for “first-in-India” precision tubing (import sub). Total 3-yr CAPEX ₹191 Cr (43% new plants).
  • High-Growth Sectors: Entering EV/auto (roof rails), defense/aerospace (7-series alloys, complex profiles), solar. OEM focus (Mahle, Subs, Force Motors) with niche quality (IATF, ISO certs).
  • Policy Support: Make in India, low India Al per capita (~2kg vs. global 10kg+). US ADD (~50-55%) levels field vs. China.
  • Financial Momentum: H1 FY26 EBITDA +21% YoY; strong ops cash flow (₹14 Cr). Preferential funds de-risk CAPEX (internal accruals funded Italian press).
  • Export Resilience: No customer loss despite ADD; adding clients in NA/UK/Europe/Australia/Israel.

Headwinds

  • Margin Pressure: EBITDA margins ~4-5% (vs. FY23 9%) due to US ADD (2023-on), recession-led volume drop (utilization to 27-28% during ramp), value-add dip.
  • US Exposure: 45-50% revenue; tariffs caused demand destruction (holiday seasonality Q4 hit). Broader ADD impacts global supply chain.
  • Trading Drag: Vanilla, 1% margins; revenue volatile with commodity prices.
  • Commodity Volatility: Al prices hedged 95-98% (MCX/LME), but 2-3% open exposure.
  • Dilution: Preferential issue adds 10% shares; promoters’ stake dips to ~56% (from 59%).

Growth Prospects

  • Near-Term (1-2 Yrs): Capacity ramp to 80% util.; margins to double-digits (management: FY23 levels ~15-18% EBITDA via value-add). H1 volumes ~4k MT; domestic clawback + US rebound.
  • Medium-Term (3-5 Yrs): 5x revenue/EBITDA (management target); Dewas online H2 FY26 (3-yr first-mover edge). Powder coating/roof rails CAPEX. Blended 15-18% EBITDA sustainable.
  • Catalysts: EV/defense indigenization; export mix diversification. Funds enable aggressive ramp without heavy debt.
  • Valuation Upside: Post-issue cap ~₹850 Cr; trading at ~4x FY25 EV/EBITDA (undervalued vs. peers).

Key Risks

Overall Outlook: Strong tailwinds from capacity/value-add shift outweigh headwinds (ADD/recovery phase). High growth potential (3-5x in 3 yrs) if execution delivers; monitor US demand/Q3 volumes. Buy on dips for 2-3 yr horizon (target 20-25% CAGR).
