Analysis of Supreme Holdings & Hospitality (India) Limited (SUPREME)

Company Overview: Supreme Holdings is a Pune-based real estate developer (CIN: L45100PN1982PLC173438) focused on residential/commercial projects. It has a strong balance sheet with minimal debt (total borrowings ~₹6 crore consolidated), high equity base (~₹568 crore attributable to owners), and assets ~₹597 crore (as of Sep 2025, all figures in ₹ crore unless stated). FY25 revenue was ~₹66 crore (mostly H1), but H1 FY26 revenue dropped sharply to ~₹0.26 crore (standalone), reflecting post-project lull. Profits held up via other income (~₹2.3 crore H1 consolidated). Listed on BSE/NSE; market cap implied small-cap. AGM resolutions (Sep 2025) passed unanimously.

Tailwinds (Positive Factors)

  • Robust Balance Sheet & Liquidity: Net worth ~₹568 crore; cash equivalents ~₹1.6 crore (consolidated). Low leverage (debt/equity <0.1x); high other equity (~₹530 crore) from retained earnings/investments. Positive operating cash flow (~₹2.2 crore standalone H1 FY26).
  • Investment Income Buffer: Other income (~₹2.3 crore H1) from interest (~₹1.6 crore), investments (~₹24 crore current/non-current), and gains offsets low ops revenue. Fair value gains and sales provide stability.
  • Regulatory Compliance & Governance: Timely SEBI disclosures; clean limited review reports (Mittal Agarwal & Co.); AGM voting >99% in favor across resolutions (high public participation ~56% of non-promoter shares).
  • Subsidiary Stability: Includes 100% subs (Helmet Traders, Belmac City Developers) and Visana Infra; minor NCI losses (~₹0.02 crore).

Headwinds (Negative/Challenges)

  • Revenue Volatility & Cyclical Downturn: H1 FY26 ops revenue ~₹0.26 crore (down 95% YoY from ₹46 crore H1 FY25, likely one-off project completion). Q2 even lower (~₹0.1 crore). Total income ~₹2.6 crore H1; dependent on project milestones.
  • Working Capital Strain: Inventories up 50% to ~₹39 crore (WIP buildup); advances to contractors up. Trade receivables collected sharply (~₹26 crore down to ₹6 crore), but ops cash tied in inventories (~₹13 crore increase).
  • Cash Burn in Investing: Net investing outflow ~₹6.5 crore H1 (investments/PPE/FDs); cash down ~40% to ~₹1.6 crore.
  • Low Ops Margins: Cost of sales near-zero but employee/other expenses persist (~₹2.2 crore H1); profitability relies on non-ops (~85% of H1 profit).

  • Project Pipeline (High Visibility): Two active projects; total GDV ~₹2,590 crore (~4x FY25 revenue): | Project | GDV | Status | Revenue Timeline | |———|—–|——–|——————| | Project 1 | ₹90 crore | Pre-revenue | Full recognition over 6 quarters from Q3 FY26 (starts ~Jan 2026). | | Project 2 | ₹2,500 crore | Approvals stage | Ops Q2-Q3 FY27; revenue Q3-Q4 FY27. |
    • Aligns with growth strategy; could drive revenue to ₹500-1,000 crore+ over FY26-28 if executed.
  • Multi-Year Upside: Phased recognition ensures steady topline; real estate tailwinds (urbanization, Pune market). EPS H1 FY26 ~₹0.09 (consolidated); potential 5-10x if GDV realized.
  • Capital Allocation: Prudent (low capex ~₹0.65 crore H1); focus on shareholder value. Warrants issuance possible (noted in cash flow).

Key Risks

  • Execution/Regulatory Delays: Large project (96% of GDV) at approvals; any slippage (common in RE) defers revenue 6-12 months. Revenue recognition tied to milestones (Ind AS 115).
  • Concentration Risk: 100% revenue from 2 projects; no diversification (single segment, Ind AS 108 not applicable).
  • Real Estate Cycles: Pune market sensitive to interest rates, policy (RERA compliance noted but unquantified), demand slowdown. Inventory buildup signals capex risk.
  • Profitability Volatility: 85%+ profit from investments (market/FV risk); ops EBITDA thin. Promoter voting low in some resolutions (0% polled in director re-appts), potential governance optics.
  • Liquidity/External: Minor borrowings up; taxes paid ~₹1 crore H1. Forex/inflation on imports nil, but RE input costs (land/labor) vulnerable.

Overall Outlook: Short-term muted (H2 FY26 revenue ramp-up expected); medium-term bullish on GDV pipeline (3-5x growth potential FY27+). Strong fundamentals mitigate risks, but monitor project updates. Suitable for patient investors; target upside on execution. (Analysis based solely on provided docs; dated Nov 2025.)
