Analysis of RAJMET (Rajnandini Metal Limited)

Company Overview: RAJMET (ISIN: INE00KV01022) is a Haryana-based metals trading/manufacturing company (CIN: L51109HR2010PLC040255). FY26 H1 results (Apr-Sep 2025) show severe revenue contraction amid ongoing tax disputes. Equity base: ~27.65 Cr shares (₹2,765 lakhs capital); promoter holding ~32% (87M shares).

Headwinds (Challenges Pressuring Performance)

  • Revenue Collapse: H1 FY26 revenue at ₹145 Cr (down 75% YoY from ₹592 Cr); Q2 at ₹71 Cr (75% down YoY from ₹278 Cr). Cost of materials remains high (~103% of revenue), squeezing margins.
  • Persistent Losses: H1 PBT loss ₹2.33 Cr (vs ₹3.28 Cr YoY); Q2 profit ₹0.13 Cr but Q1 loss ₹3.46 Cr. Cumulative FY25 loss ₹3.28 Cr.
  • Tax Disputes: GST demand ₹290.7 Cr (incl. ₹96 Cr ITC disallowance + interest/penalty from 2020 search); IT demand ₹17 Cr. Auditor issued qualified review opinion; no provision made, but appeals pending—potential existential threat (demands ~5-6x equity of ₹54.6 Cr).
  • Asset Shrinkage: Total assets down 23% YoY to ₹114 Cr; inventories -21% (₹73 Cr), trade receivables -43% (₹6.5 Cr)—signals weak demand/liquidity strain.
  • Sector Cyclicality: Metals trading exposed to commodity price volatility, supply chain disruptions (e.g., GST ITC issues hint at past fake invoice probes).

Tailwinds (Supportive Factors)

  • Debt Reduction: Current borrowings slashed 80% to ₹6.7 Cr (from ₹34 Cr); total debt down ~35%. Non-current borrowings stable at ₹8.7 Cr.
  • Strong Cash Generation: Operating cash flow +₹18 Cr (inventory liquidation +₹19 Cr); land sale (incl. CWIP) +₹12 Cr boosted investing cash. Cash balance up to ₹0.23 Cr.
  • Shareholder Mandate: 15th AGM (Sep 2025) passed all resolutions (99.98%+ favor); approved new MD (Sanjay Choudhary, 2-yr term), statutory auditor (Krishna & Associates), independent director, etc. Low public turnout (0.42%) but promoter 100% support.
  • Cost Discipline: Finance costs down 72% YoY (₹2 Cr); employee/other expenses controlled.

Growth Prospects (Outlook)

  • Low Visibility: No clear catalysts; revenue trajectory suggests contraction (possibly due to GST overhang curbing operations). Standalone ops only (no subs/JVs).
  • Potential Upside: Debt cleanup improves flexibility for revival. Metals sector recovery (e.g., infra push) could aid if disputes resolve favorably. Inventory/payables optimization hints at working capital efficiency.
  • Base Case: Stagnant/low single-digit growth in FY26 absent dispute closure; optimistic scenario: 20-30% revenue rebound if appeals win and steel demand rises (India infra boom).
  • Long-Term: Trading model limits scalability; diversification needed for sustained growth.

Key Risks (Ranked by Severity)

  1. Contingent Liabilities (High): ₹308 Cr demands—if upheld, could force insolvency/equity wipeout. Auditor flags inability to quantify impact.
  2. Revenue Concentration/Volatility (High): 100% metals-dependent; Q2 drop signals demand weakness or compliance issues.
  3. Liquidity/Going Concern (Medium-High): Positive cash flow but reliant on asset sales; trade payables stable at ₹35 Cr.
  4. Regulatory/Compliance (Medium): Auditor change (new firm post-AGM); past searches raise governance red flags.
  5. Market/Shareholder (Low-Medium): Thin public participation in AGM (minor dissent 0.02-6.8%); low liquidity risk given NSE listing.

Summary Verdict: High-Risk, Distressed Play. Balance sheet deleveraging and cash positivity are bright spots amid a revenue nosedive and massive tax overhang—treat as speculative. Avoid unless tax appeals resolve favorably; monitor Q3 results (expected Feb 2026) and appellate outcomes. Market cap likely depressed (est. <₹100 Cr based on peers/losses); upside capped by disputes. Recommendation: Sell/Hold; high volatility ahead.
