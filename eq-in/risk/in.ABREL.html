Analysis of Aditya Birla Real Estate Limited (ABREL) – Q2FY26 Update

ABREL (formerly Century Textiles) is undergoing a strategic pivot to a pure-play real estate platform, with Birla Estates Pvt Ltd (BEPL) as its key subsidiary driving residential projects. Q2FY26 showed resilient bookings (₹890 Cr, +111% QoQ) amid no new launches, but lumpy revenue recognition (completed contract method) led to EBITDA losses from continuing ops. Total GDV pipeline: ₹70,000 Cr. Divestments (pulp/paper to ITC, JV closure) are streamlining focus.

Headwinds

  • Cash Flow Pressure: H1FY26 operating cash flow negative at -₹4 Bn; collections (₹9 Bn) lagged project dev. costs (₹10 Bn) due to billing delays (e.g., Niyaara towers). Net debt rose QoQ by ₹2.8 Bn to ~₹50 Bn, driven by FSI premiums/approvals ahead of launches.
  • Execution & Approval Delays: Thane RERA expected Q4 but NGT/MoEF hurdles persist; Niyaara Tower C pushed to Mar’26 due to BMC land issues. Construction scaling (11 Mn sq ft FY26, 50 Mn FY27) strains capacity.
  • Lumpy Financials: Q2 continuing ops EBITDA loss of -₹547 Mn (H1: -₹828 Mn) due to high dev. costs (₹116 Cr H1) and no completions. Discontinued ops (pulp/paper/textiles) propped PAT.
  • Macro/Competitive: Luxury segment competition intense (Worli); Mumbai redevelopment supply risks pricing. Inflation at 7.21% but easing.

Tailwinds

  • Demand Strength: 80% launched area sold pan-India; Q2 bookings led by Niyaara (₹320 Cr, 113/148 Tower B units sold) & Evara (₹326 Cr). Premium (>₹10 Mn) sales +13% YoY nationally.
  • Brand & ESG Edge: Birla brand drives traction; GRESB 2025: #1 Asia/Global Residential (100/100 dev. score). Awards like British Safety Council Sword of Honour enhance premium positioning.
  • Strategic Moves: Divestments unlock value (ITC deal on track FY26, ₹350 Bn pulp GDV; JV closure yields ₹54 Cr). Partnerships: IFC (₹420 Cr for Pune/Thane), Mitsubishi (₹560 Cr Bengaluru).
  • Healthy Balance Sheet: Surplus cash potential ₹74 Bn from launched projects; FY26 collections guidance ₹2 Tn cumulative to Dec. Credit ratings: CARE AA Stable.

Growth Prospects

  • Launch Pipeline: FY26 GDV launches ₹13,900 Cr (e.g., Niyaara Ph3 ₹4,500 Cr, Pune ₹765 Cr, Thane ₹1,630 Cr, Gurugram). Total portfolio: 35 Mn sq ft, ₹70,000 Cr GDV (own + JV).
  • BD Momentum: ₹30,000 Cr advanced DD (Noida/Gurugram/Mumbai); target ₹10-15k Cr additions FY26. 3-5 Yr Vision: Top-5 player, ₹20,000 Cr annual bookings via scale, sustainability, customer focus.
  • Geographic Expansion: MMR (49% Q2 bookings), Bengaluru (37%), NCR/Pune scaling. Commercial: 1 Mn sq ft Worli office planned.
  • Efficiency Gains: BCG-led ops excellence; team expanded to 700+; phased launches for pricing power (e.g., Manjri ₹7-7.5k/sq ft).

Overall Summary

Bullish Outlook with Near-Term Volatility: ABREL’s real estate pivot is gaining traction – bookings exceed FY25 full-year pace, with multi-year visibility (₹70k Cr GDV). Tailwinds from demand, partnerships, and ESG leadership outweigh headwinds like cash timing and approvals. FY26 guidance intact (launches/collections); post-ITC closure, expect deleveraging and 25% EBITDA margins on ₹46k Cr launched GDV. Valuation Upside: Trades at discount to peers (DLF/Godrej); target 20-30% upside on execution. Monitor Q3 launches/cash flows. Recommendation: Accumulate for 3-5 Yr compounding.
