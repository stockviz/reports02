GRASIM Industries Limited (BSE: 500300, NSE: GRASIM) - Q2FY26 Analysis

Grasim’s Q2FY26 results reflect a diversified portfolio with robust revenue growth (17% YoY consolidated to ₹39,900 Cr) amid investments in high-growth areas like Paints and B2B E-com. Standalone revenue hit a record ₹9,610 Cr (+26% YoY). EBITDA margins held steady at 13%, but PAT (owner’s share) surged 76% YoY to ₹553 Cr. TTM revenue reached ₹1.60 lakh Cr (+8% YoY). Below is a structured summary of headwinds, tailwinds, growth prospects, and key risks based on the filings.

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Strong Diversification & Execution: Building Materials (55% of revenue) drove 28% YoY growth to ₹22,253 Cr; Cement (UltraTech) EBITDA/ton up 32% YoY to ₹966; Paints (Birla Opus) scaled to 2nd largest capacity (~24% share, 1,332 MLPA); B2B E-com (Birla Pivot) up 15% QoQ.
  • Financial Services Momentum: Lending portfolio +29% YoY to ₹1.78 lakh Cr; AUM +10% YoY to ₹5.50 lakh Cr; D2C platform (ABCD) at 7.6 Mn users.
  • Core Stability: Chemicals revenue +17% YoY, EBITDA +34%; Renewables capacity doubled to 1.93 GWp (43% captive).
  • Sustainability Edge: ‘Dark Green Shirt’ rating in Canopy Hot Button; 24% renewable power share; 49% recycled water usage.
  • Macro Alignment: Benefits from India’s infra/housing boom, Viksit Bharat push.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Input Cost Pressures: Cellulosic Fibres EBITDA -29% YoY to ₹350 Cr due to high raw material costs (partially absorbed); CFY realizations hit by cheap Chinese imports; Chemicals margins squeezed despite volume gains.
  • Demand/Seasonal Softness: Monsoon slowdown impacted Paints/B2B; CSF volumes -5% YoY (logistics resolved); global fibre prices down (CSF at $1.51/kg).
  • Investment Phase Drag: Paints/B2B capex (₹9,727 Cr cumulative) moderated segment EBITDA; overall EBITDA -19% QoQ.
  • Minor Regulatory: Maharashtra Pollution Board forfeited ₹15L BG (no material impact).

Growth Prospects

  • High-Conviction Bets: Paints market share gains accelerating (10,000+ towns, Opus Assurance/PaintCraft innovations); B2B targeting $1 Bn revenue by FY27; Cement to 240.8 MT by FY28.
  • Capacity Ramp-Up: Lyocell Phase-1 (55 KTPA) by mid-FY27; Chlorine Derivatives/Specialty Chem expansions; Renewables scaling.
  • Financial Services Scale: Lending/AUM growth; MSME focus via Udyog Plus (₹4,397 Cr AUM).
  • Long-Term Tailwinds: India’s underpenetrated markets (paints per capita 3.5 kg vs. global 10 kg; cement demand); TTM revenue trajectory supports 10-15% CAGR potential.
  • Capex Pipeline: FY26 planned ₹2,263 Cr (standalone), focused on returns.

Overall Outlook: Grasim is in a transformative phase with new engines (Paints/B2B/Renewables) offsetting core cyclicality. Strong parentage (Aditya Birla Group) and UltraTech/AB Capital subsidiaries provide stability. Stock poised for re-rating on execution, but monitor input costs and Q3 demand.
