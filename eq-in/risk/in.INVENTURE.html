Analysis of Inventure Growth & Securities Ltd. (INVENTURE)

Overview: Inventure Growth & Securities Ltd. (BSE: 533506, NSE: INVENTURE) is a financial services firm engaged in equity/commodity broking, proprietary trading, financing, margin trading facility (MTF), and merchant banking. The Q2/H1 FY26 (ended Sep 30, 2025) unaudited results show mixed standalone performance (H1 PAT ₹315 lakhs vs. ₹372 lakhs YoY) but improved consolidated profitability (H1 PAT ₹611 lakhs, up 41% YoY). Total assets stable at ~₹37,200 lakhs consolidated. A key corporate restructuring scheme (amalgamation of 4 subsidiaries + demerger of lending into another sub) is pending NCLT/SEBI approvals. Rights issue proceeds (~₹489 Cr raised earlier) largely utilized, but ₹15.7 Cr + ₹1.81 Cr pending, with shareholder-approved variations for MTF/working capital.

Tailwinds (Positive Factors)

  • Improving Consolidated Profitability: H1 revenue up to ₹2,494 lakhs (broking/proprietary ~80% of revenue). PAT growth driven by interest income (₹1,084 lakhs) and fees (₹1,089 lakhs). Segment results strong in financing (₹467 lakhs PBT) and broking (₹521 lakhs PBT).
  • Strong Capital Base & Liquidity: Equity ₹10,500 lakhs + other equity ₹17,418 lakhs consolidated. Cash equivalents ₹265 lakhs; bank balances ₹11,379 lakhs (incl. earmarked rights proceeds). Positive operating cash flow standalone (₹518 lakhs).
  • Rights Issue Deployment: ~96% of net proceeds (₹481 Cr) utilized for office acquisition, MTF expansion, working capital. Recent AGM (Sep 29, 2025) approved variations to redirect unutilized funds (~₹4 Cr) to high-ROI areas like MTF/computer hardware/stock-in-trade.
  • Restructuring Momentum: Scheme to amalgamate subsidiaries (finance, commodities, insurance broking, developers) and demerge lending business—could streamline ops, reduce complexity, and unlock value post-approvals.
  • AGM Outcomes: All resolutions passed unanimously, incl. director reappointments and loan powers to directors (under Sec 185).

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Quarterly Volatility: Standalone Q2 loss (₹116 lakhs PAT) due to net fair value losses (₹438 lakhs) and high expenses (total ₹1,163 lakhs). Consolidated Q2 profit muted (₹79 lakhs) vs. Q1 (₹531 lakhs).
  • Rights Issue Delays: ₹180 lakhs transferred from escrow but pending utilization (MTF/working capital); total unutilized ₹15.7 Cr in escrow. Auditor emphasis flags this as “Other Matter.”
  • High Costs & Provisions: Elevated finance costs (₹130 lakhs H1), impairments (₹90 lakhs), employee expenses (₹423 lakhs). Exceptional items incl. restructuring costs (₹20 lakhs H1) and litigation provisions.
  • Market/Revenue Pressure: Broking fees down YoY (₹1,089 lakhs H1 vs. ₹1,710 lakhs); dependent on volatile trading volumes/F&O margins.

Growth Prospects

  • High (Medium-Term):
    • Core Business Expansion: Leverage unutilized funds for MTF (₹150 Cr allocated), arbitrage/F&O margins (₹100 Cr), and stock-in-trade working capital (₹59 Cr). Broking/proprietary segment assets ₹24,898 lakhs (67% of total).
    • Post-Restructuring Upside: Amalgamation/demerger to consolidate ops into focused entities (broking + wealth mgmt). Merchant banking (₹40 lakhs revenue H1) poised for growth despite SEBI restraints.
    • Diversification: Financing segment PBT up sharply (₹467 lakhs H1); potential in insurance broking/commodities post-merger.
    • Capital Efficiency: ROE potential from ₹27,918 lakhs capital employed; EPS ₹0.06 H1 (diluted).
  • Catalysts: Scheme approvals, rights fund deployment, market upcycle in equities/derivatives.

Summary Recommendation: Moderate Buy/Hold for risk-tolerant investors. Tailwinds from profitability, liquidity, and restructuring outweigh near-term headwinds (volatility/delays). Growth hinges on scheme execution and market volumes; monitor Q3 results and NCLT updates. Target upside 20-30% if approvals materialize, but cap exposure due to regulatory overhang.
