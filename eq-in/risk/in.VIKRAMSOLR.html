Analysis of VIKRAMSOLR (Vikram Solar Limited)

Stock Code: VIKRAMSOLR on NSE & Scrip Code 544488 on BSE
Listing Date: August 26, 2025

Executive Summary

VIKRAMSOLR has demonstrated strong financial growth and operational momentum in Q2 and H1 FY26 (April–September 2025). The company leveraged its IPO success, strong ESG credentials, Tier-1 bankability status, and expanding domestic and export markets to deliver triple-digit YoY revenue and profit growth. It is well-positioned to capitalize on India’s accelerating solar adoption and policy tailwinds. However, it faces key regulatory disputes, working capital pressure, pending legal risks, and a competitive landscape.

This analysis evaluates the tailwinds, headwinds, growth prospects, and key risks based on disclosures up to October 16, 2025.

1. Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

1.1 Robust Financial Growth & Profitability

Key Insight: The IPO in August 2025 and efficient capital utilization have enabled strong margin expansion. The PAT surge is especially notable, reflecting improved operating leverage and scale.

1.2 Healthy Balance Sheet & Capital Access

  • Net Debt: Just ₹12 Cr (as on Sep 30, 2025) despite massive CapEx.
  • Debt / Equity: Only 0.03x, indicating negligible leverage and strong equity backing.
  • Cash & Equivalents: ₹11,172 Cr (up from ₹39 Cr in FY25), primarily from ₹14,145 Cr net IPO proceeds.
  • Shareholder Equity: Surged to ₹2,950 Cr from ₹1,242 Cr (FY25).

Implication: High financial flexibility to fund expansion without stress.

1.3 Large Order Book & Market Confidence

  • Order Book: 11.15 GW as of Sep 30, 2025 → 36% YoY increase from 8.21 GW in Sep 2024.
  • Module Sales (H1 FY26): 784 MW sold.
  • Geographical Split: 80% domestic, 20% exports.
  • Customer Portfolio: Growing share in C&I (20%) and IPP (52%), signaling diversification.

Outlook: Strong market demand and high visibility into future revenues.

1.4 Strong Policy Tailwinds in India

Policy/Regulation Impact
✅ GST reduced to 5% on solar equipment Improves affordability; boosts rooftop and off-grid demand
✅ ALMM-3 announced (domestic wafer/ingot requirement by 2028) Supports domestic manufacturers like Vikram Solar
✅ Anti-dumping probes on cells & encapsulants Curtails cheap imports; strengthens India-made modules
✅ India now >50% non-fossil power capacity Confirms sustained policy push; enhances investor confidence

India Solar Outlook: ~115 GW pipeline under execution; ~300 GW target by 2030 → massive TAM.

1.5 Strategic Capacity Expansion Plans

  • Current Module Capacity: 4.5 GW (all N-type, fully automated).
  • Planned Expansion: 17.5 GW by FY27, 12 GW solar cell capacity.
  • Technology Upgrade: 96% N-type production (vs. traditional p-type); higher efficiency.
  • Backward Integration: Aims 75% vertical integration to enhance cost stability.

Competitive Edge: Largest single-location cell gigafactory in India; future-ready technology.

1.6 ESG Leadership & Brand Value

  • Ranked Tier-1 by BloombergNEF for 6 consecutive quarters.
  • Top performer in PVEL Reliability Scorecard, 8 times.
  • EcoVadis Platinum rating (top 1% in industry).
  • SBTi & UN Global Compact endorsed; diversity (33% women on board).
  • Sustainability Projects: Water recycling, waste reduction, renewable usage (30% of own needs).

Brand Advantage: Trusted brand for ESG-conscious institutional investors and project developers.

2. Headwinds & Challenges

2.1 Major Receivables Disputes (Key Risk)

  • Rs. 843.88 Cr in Trade Receivables is disputed by several EPC clients.
  • Reason: Claims on liquidated damages, generation shortfall, or contractual disputes.
  • Status: Referred to arbitration/court, but not provided for in books.
  • Potential Impact: If negative outcome → up to 3.5x H1 PAT impact.

Risk Level: High. Requires stringent follow-up and possibility of provision adjustments.

2.2 Safeguard Duty Recovery Risk (Pending Litigation)

  • Paid ₹1,485 Cr in safeguard duty (2018–2021) on Chinese solar cell imports.
  • Not written off; shown as receivable based on legal opinion.
  • Case Sub Judice: Orissa HC & Supreme Court; no provision booked.
  • Auditor Note: “No modification, but attention drawn”.

Risk Level: High-impact, uncertain resolution. Dependent on court outcome.

2.3 Working Capital Pressure Despite Profits

  • Operating Cash Flow: ₹515 Cr (vs. –₹224 Cr in H1 FY25) — improvement.
  • However, net cash inflow from operations (₹5.13 Cr) still lags EBITDA (₹477 Cr).
  • Working Capital Drag: Due to:
    • High trade receivables (₹12,285 Cr).
    • Rising inventories (₹5,040 Cr) as pipeline builds up.

Insight: Strong profitability not fully converted to cash; depends on collections.

2.4 Foreign Exchange Exposure

  • International Subsidiaries: Units in Germany, USA, Singapore.
  • Exchange Differences: Reported in other comprehensive income (OCI) → impacts equity.
  • FX Movement: Can affect reported profits and equity volatility.

Requires active hedging strategy for growing export business.

Conclusion: Buy/Invest with Caution & Monitor Key Risks

Positives (Why Invest?)

  • Post-IPO financial strength with high scalability.
  • Leading Indian solar manufacturer with strong technology (N-type, automation).
  • Huge order book growth and global expansion.
  • Supported by India’s clean energy policy push.
  • Best-in-class ESG profile enhancing investor appeal.

Concerns (What to Watch For?)

  1. Resolution of ₹844 Cr receivables dispute.
  2. Progress on safeguard duty litigation.
  3. Working capital efficiency as revenues scale.
  4. Execution of 12 GW cell plant on time.
  5. Regulatory compliance with ALMM-3 and BCD norms.

Final Recommendation

VIKRAMSOLR is a strong growth stock with sustainable moat in technology and brand, but investment should be tempered by close monitoring of legal exposures. Suitable for ESG-focused and long-term thematic investors seeking exposure to India’s solar revolution. Short-term volatility possible if legal risks flare up.
