UFO Moviez India Limited (NSE: UFO) - Q2 & H1 FY26 Analysis

UFO Moviez, India’s largest in-cinema ad platform (3,795 screens across 1,346 cities, ~1.8 Bn annual viewers), reported strong recovery in Q2/H1 FY26: Revenue +15% YoY to ₹2,203 Mn (H1), EBITDA +145% to ₹411 Mn, PAT swing to ₹141 Mn (from ₹50 Mn loss). Ad revenue led growth (+37% YoY Q2 to ₹335 Mn), driven by higher minutes sold (4.55/show vs. 3.03 YoY). Business model leverages fixed MG with exhibitors (upside ~65% to PBT) amid post-COVID rebound.

Tailwinds

  • Ad Revenue Momentum: Corporate/hyperlocal ads up 36% YoY (₹264 Mn Q2); minutes sold nearing pre-COVID (4.55 vs. 5.4 FY19). Sharing % improved (55% vs. 69% YoY).
  • Theatrical Recovery: 462 films released Q2 (stable YoY); diverse slate (e.g., Rajinikanth’s Coolie, Akshay’s Jolly LLB 3) drove footfalls. Distributor (+16%) & exhibitor revenues (+1%) steady.
  • Financial Strength: Net cash ₹606 Mn; low Capex (₹40-45 Cr FY26 for maintenance). High gross margins (55% Q2) from ad leverage.
  • Reduced Govt Dependency: Corporate ads now ~82% of in-cinema revenue (vs. 80% YoY); stable even if govt pie shrinks.

Headwinds

  • Sub-Pre-COVID Levels: Ad yield ~10-12% below FY19 (₹55-60/min vs. ₹60-64); total minutes & footfalls lag big blockbusters. Q3 FY25 had Pushpa 2 boost (absent this year).
  • Govt Ad Shrinkage: Central (DAVP) spending down dramatically (state-led now dominant); historical 53-60% reliance reduced but impacts yield.
  • Industry Slowdown: Post-COVID content/OTT shift delayed single-screen/small-town expansion. Diwali muted; no monopoly in digital delivery (4-5 competitors).
  • Cost Pressures: Employee expenses flat but SG&A up slightly; lease/Ind AS 116 impacts (~₹47 Mn H1).

Growth Prospects

  • Ad Upside (Primary Driver): Target FY19 volumes (5.4 min/show) + pricing recovery with blockbusters (Avatar: Fire and Ash, De De Pyaar De 2). Q3/Q4 FY26 pipeline robust; potential 2x volume + 2x pricing = 4x ad revenue.
  • Network Expansion: 3,795 screens stable; focus on Tier-2/3 towns (1,300+ cities) post-stabilization. Product sales +12% YoY (₹550 Mn H1); new lines (sound equip).
  • Diversification: Caravan Talkies rebound (325% YoY Q2); international subs (e.g., Scrabble entities); synergies from mergers (Scrabble Digital).
  • Outlook: Management sees Q3/H2 momentum; 20% EBITDA margins sustainable if content holds. Long-term: Pre-COVID profitability (FY19 ad rev/screen ₹561K).

Key Risks

Risk Category Details Mitigation
Content/Footfall High dependence on hits; OTT competition erodes attendance. Diverse slate; robust pipeline.
Ad Market Govt policy volatility; yield pressure if corporates hesitate. Corporate shift (now reliable); fixed MG protects downside.
Competition/Economics Rivals in delivery; slowdown hits discretionary ad spend. Largest network; attractive pricing (sweet spot).
Operational Capex for replacements; rural volatility (Caravan). On-track Capex; govt/corporate pivot.
Financial Debt (~₹7.4 Bn gross); forex in intl ops. Net cash positive; steady cash flows (₹4.2 Bn H1 ops).

Overall: Strong turnaround play with ad leverage as key catalyst. Bullish on H2 FY26 if content delivers; monitor box office & govt ads. Valuation upside tied to industry normalization (pre-COVID rev ~₹160 Cr/qtr).
