Analysis of Harrisons Malayalam Limited (HARRMALAYA)

Harrisons Malayalam Limited (HML), a plantation company focused on tea and rubber production in Kerala and Tamil Nadu, reported strong H1FY26 results (ended Sep 30, 2025), marking a turnaround from H1FY25 losses. Revenue grew 6.8% YoY to ₹250.9 Cr, driven by rubber and a one-off gain in “Others” (likely asset sales), leading to PBT/PAT of ₹12.4 Cr (vs. ₹-1.5 Cr loss YoY). However, tea remains loss-making, debt is elevated, and liquidity is strained. Below is a structured summary of headwinds, tailwinds, growth prospects, and key risks.

Tailwinds (Positive Factors)

  • Operational Turnaround: H1FY26 PAT of ₹12.4 Cr (EPS ₹6.71) vs. prior loss; Q2 revenue up 15% QoQ to ₹134.4 Cr, boosted by rubber (₹74 Cr) and other income (₹11.8 Cr, incl. ₹9.6 Cr PPE sale gain).
  • Rubber Segment Strength: Contributed ~53% revenue (₹133 Cr, H1); segment profit ₹10.5 Cr, underscoring resilience amid tea weakness.
  • Revenue Momentum: YoY growth of 6.8% despite seasonal factors; tea revenue up 22% YoY to ₹116.6 Cr.
  • Capex Commitment: Replanting (₹6.4 Cr) and CWIP (₹97.5 Cr) signal long-term yield improvements.
  • Governance: Overwhelming AGM approval (99%+ votes) for key resolutions; new senior management (Head-Finance, Head-Tea) for execution focus.
  • Cash Generation: Positive operating cash flow (₹5.4 Cr); equity grew 7% to ₹165 Cr.

Headwinds (Negative Factors)

  • Tea Segment Drag: Persistent losses (H1: ₹2.2 Cr; Q2: ₹6 Cr loss); high costs (employee ₹96 Cr, materials ₹53 Cr) amid volatile prices/low yields.
  • High Leverage & Costs: Borrowings up 12% to ₹110 Cr (finance costs ₹6.4 Cr); interest expense erodes 52% of segment profits.
  • Liquidity Strain: Current liabilities (₹233 Cr) exceed current assets (₹105 Cr) by 122%; working capital tied in inventories (+36% YoY to ₹52 Cr).
  • One-Off Boost: “Others” profit (₹10.4 Cr) from asset sales masks core weaknesses; recurring profitability unproven.
  • Regulatory Fine: ₹0.02 Cr penalty for delayed FY25 annual report filing (paid; compliance caution advised).

Growth Prospects

  • Moderate (Core Business Recovery): FY26 revenue could grow 10-15% if rubber prices hold (natural rubber demand from tyres/auto) and tea yields improve post-replanting. Target: PAT ₹25-30 Cr (annualized H1).
  • Segment Opportunities: | Segment | H1FY26 Revenue | H1FY26 Profit | Outlook | |———|—————-|—————|———| | Tea | ₹116.6 Cr | (₹2.2 Cr) | Low-med; yield/capex upside, but price-sensitive. | | Rubber | ₹133.1 Cr | ₹10.5 Cr | High; export/global demand tailwind. | | Others | ₹1.2 Cr | ₹10.4 Cr | Low; non-recurring. |
  • Expansion Catalysts: CWIP (₹98 Cr, +11% YoY) for capacity; unutilized credit lines support working capital. Long-term: Premium tea/rubber branding, diversification into value-added products.
  • Base Case: Steady 5-8% CAGR revenue over 3-5 yrs if commodity cycle favorable; equity dilution risk low (18.45 Cr shares).

Key Risks (High-Impact Threats)

  1. Commodity Volatility (High): Tea/rubber prices weather-sensitive; H1 tea losses highlight vulnerability (global supply glut risk).
  2. Legal/Title Disputes (High): New Oct 2025 suit by Kerala Govt claims tea estates (Achoor, Sentinel Rock, etc.); potential asset loss/possession issues (material to 20-30% capacity).
  3. Liquidity & Solvency (High): Negative working capital; debt servicing (₹6.4 Cr interest H1) strains cash (₹0.23 Cr). Going concern note relies on future cash flows/bank lines.
  4. Carry-Forward Losses (Medium): ₹0 tax shield, but deferred tax on temp differences; prolonged losses could impair DTA recognition.
  5. Operational/External (Medium): Plantation risks (climate, labor); high employee costs (38% of expenses); forex nil but import dependence for inputs.
  6. Execution (Low-Medium): New management untested; capex delays could hurt yields.

Overall Outlook: Cautiously Positive Short-Term (H1 momentum), but High-Risk Profile due to debt, tea losses, and land suit. Stock suits contrarian investors; monitor Q3 rubber prices and legal updates. Valuation context: Trading at ~1x book (equity ₹165 Cr); potential 20-30% upside if risks contained.
