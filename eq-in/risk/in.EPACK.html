Analysis of EPACK Durable Limited (EPACK) – Headwinds, Tailwinds, Growth Prospects, and Key Risks

EPACK Durable Limited, India’s 2nd largest RAC ODM (24% market share), manufactures RACs (56% revenue), SDAs (19%), components, and LDAs. FY25 revenue: INR 21,919 Mn. H1 FY26 results show revenue decline (-23.9% YoY to INR 8,757 Mn) due to RAC weakness, but diversification gains in SDA/Components. Q2 FY26 was loss-making (PAT loss INR 222 Mn). Below is a structured summary based on filings (earnings presentation, financials, announcements).

Headwinds (Current Challenges)

  • RAC Seasonality & Demand Weakness: Q2 revenue down 76% QoQ (INR 617 Mn) due to unseasonal rains, GST cut delay (28%→18%), surplus Q1 inventory, and weak secondary sales. H1 RAC down 45.7% YoY.
  • Overall Revenue/EBITDA Pressure: H1 revenue -23.9% YoY; Q2 EBITDA margin 0.23% (-232 bps QoQ). High finance costs (up 30% YoY) and depreciation from capex.
  • Working Capital Strain: Cycle extended to 112 days (vs 57 FY25); trade payables down sharply (-55% QoQ), inventories high (106 days).
  • Debt Burden: Net debt up to INR 6,771 Mn (Net D/E 0.71x vs 0.37x FY25); interest expense INR 361 Mn H1 (+30% YoY).
  • Minor Regulatory Issue: Tax search at Dehradun (Aug 2025) – peaceful closure, no proven allegations, but precautionary Rs 50 Lac GST deposit (refund planned).

Tailwinds (Supportive Factors)

  • Product Diversification: RAC share down to 56% (from 80% FY23); SDA/LDA +36% YoY H1, Components +90% YoY. Q2: SDA +53% QoQ, Components +73% QoQ, LDA +466% QoQ.
  • Customer & Order Momentum: 4 new customers added Q2 (supply from Q4 FY26); 55+ clients (Voltas, Haier, Philips, etc.); top-2 dependency down to 44% (H1 FY26).
  • Backward Integration: In-house production of key components (PCBs, heat exchangers, copper tubing) – cost edge; 3 NABL labs, 4 plants (expanding to 6).
  • Policy Support: PLI/ECMS schemes, BIS certifications barrier imports (China/Thailand); GST cut boosting affordability post-Q2.
  • Festive Recovery: Post-GST, strong Navratri/Diwali demand; normalized inventories, improved channel movement.

Growth Prospects (Medium-Term Outlook)

  • Capacity Ramp-Up: INR 4,700 Mn capex H1 FY26 (total INR 4.5-5 Bn by Q1 FY27); new Sri City Hisense plant (Q3 FY26, $1 Bn 5-yr revenue potential, exports to SARC/MEA).
  • Strategic JVs/Partnerships: EPAVO (BLDC motors, Q2 FY26 start); Bumjin (speakers); tie-ups with Panasonic/Daikin for PCBA/copper; EMTPL WOS.
  • SDA/LDA/Components Expansion: SDA market CAGR 10-12%; new products (air fryers, nutri blenders, coffee makers, etc.); Components into energy meters.
  • Market Tailwinds: India RAC market INR 63K Cr (2025), 16% CAGR to 2030 (28 Mn units); low penetration, rising incomes, energy-efficient demand.
  • FY26 Guidance: Confident full-year growth via NCNP/NCEP/ECNP strategies; revenue diversification to SDA (target higher share), exports.

Key Risks

  • Customer Concentration: Top-2 clients ~44% revenue; any slowdown (e.g., Voltas/Haier) impacts.
  • Execution Risks: High capex (debt-funded) delays; new plants/JVs ramp-up (Sri City, EPAVO).
  • Cyclicality/External Shocks: RAC highly seasonal/weather-dependent; raw material volatility (copper, metals).
  • Financial Leverage: Rising debt (Gross debt INR 6,887 Mn); interest coverage strained in weak quarters.
  • Regulatory/Compliance: Ongoing tax refund (Rs 50 Lac); JV losses (INR 193 Lakhs H1); forex exposure.
  • Competition: ODM space competitive; import barriers help but global players (e.g., Hisense) add execution complexity.
  • Macro: Inflation, monsoon variability, festive slowdown if consumer sentiment weakens.

Overall Outlook: Short-term pressured by RAC weakness (mitigated by diversification), but strong tailwinds from capacity/products/policies position EPACK for FY26+ growth (target: revenue expansion, margin recovery to 7-8%). Monitor Q3 festive execution and debt management. Stock trades at CMP ~INR 348 (Sep 2025), mcap INR 33 Bn.
