Analysis of Motherson Sumi Wiring India Limited (MSUMI) – Q2 FY26 Overview

MSWIL, a leading wiring harness provider and JV between Samvardhana Motherson International Ltd. (SAMIL) and Sumitomo Wiring Systems, reported robust Q2 FY26 results: Revenue ₹2,762 Cr (+19% YoY vs. industry +4%), EBITDA ₹280 Cr (+12% YoY, 10.1% margin; 12.7% ex-Greenfields), PAT ₹165 Cr (+9% YoY). H1 revenue ₹5,256 Cr (+17% YoY). Debt-free, EV revenue share at 6.7-7% (up QoQ). Greenfields (₹190 Cr revenue) at 36% utilization, dragging profitability but ramping.

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Outperformance vs. Industry: 19% YoY growth driven by premiumization, volume gains, and presence on most new OEM models (ICE/EV/hybrids).
  • Greenfield Momentum: 3 plants operational/ramping (Navagam EV, Kharkhauda ICE, Pune EV+ICE); one SOP delayed but aligned with customer ramps. Utilization improving QoQ; expected to hit 70-80% profitability inflection.
  • EV Exposure: Share up from 4-5% to 7%; higher content per EV vehicle. Management localizing HV harness supply chain.
  • Financial Strength: Net cash position (₹53 Cr cash surplus ex-leases); H1 CFO ₹605 Cr. ROCE focus over margins; stable ex-Greenfield EBITDA margins ~12-12.7%.
  • Shareholder-Friendly: Bonus issue (1:2) completed; final dividend ₹0.35/share approved. CRISIL ESG rating ‘60/Adequate’.
  • Commodity Pass-Through: Copper price hikes (5% QoQ, 13% YoY) largely reimbursed with quarterly/semi-annual lag.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Greenfield Drag: Losses widened to ₹46 Cr in Q2 (vs. ₹31 Cr prior; adjusted ~₹70 Cr gross) due to ramp-up costs (manpower, fixed opex) at low utilization. Direct plant costs only, improving QoQ.
  • Input Cost Inflation: Copper up (₹929/kg avg. Q2), forex pressures (Yen/INR +5%, USD/INR +4% QoQ); RM costs up, partially offset by pass-through lag.
  • Opex Pressures: Employee costs steady at ₹475-480 Cr/quarter but to rise with shop-floor hiring for volumes. EV mix potentially softer initially (though mgmt. sees no ROCE variance).
  • Working Capital Strain: Inventories +14% YoY (₹1,461 Cr), receivables +17% (₹1,449 Cr); offset by payables growth.

Growth Prospects

  • Near-Term (FY26/FY27): Greenfields to drive incremental revenue; full ramp-up could add high-teens growth. EV share to double-digits by FY27 with OEM ramps. H1 ex-Greenfield +10%; sustained industry outperformance via new launches.
  • Medium-Term: Capacity expansions (CAPEX ₹210 Cr FY26); localization for EVs/HV harnesses. Broader auto recovery (PV +4% Q2) + premiumization tailwinds.
  • Projections: Mgmt. “wait-and-watch” on guidance but implies EBITDA margins >12% post-ramp; debt-free balance sheet supports growth. Potential 15-20% revenue CAGR if utilization hits targets.

Key Risks

  • Execution/Ramp Risks: Greenfield delays tied to customer SOPs/volumes; losses could persist if <70% utilization.
  • Cyclicality/Concentration: Auto OEM dependence; industry slowdowns (e.g., EV adoption pace) could hit volumes.
  • Commodity/FX Volatility: Copper/Yen exposure (13% YoY copper rise); lag in pass-through erodes short-term margins.
  • Margin Pressure: EV/lower-utilization mix; staff costs scaling with volumes. No explicit margin guidance.
  • Related Parties: Material transactions with SAMIL/Sumitomo approved but scrutiny risk (passed AGM with 98.5% votes).
  • Macro: Inventory/receivables buildup signals WC risk; forex (JPY/INR) impacts imports.

Overall Outlook: Strong tailwinds from growth/expansions outweigh headwinds; debt-free status buffers risks. Positive momentum post-Greenfield ramp; monitor Q3 utilization/copper trends. Buy/hold for auto/EV exposure. (Ratings/ESG supportive.)
