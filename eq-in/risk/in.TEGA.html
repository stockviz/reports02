Tega Industries Limited (TEGA) Analysis: Headwinds, Tailwinds, Growth Prospects, and Key Risks

Tega Industries, a leader in critical consumables (e.g., grinding media, screens) for mining, mineral beneficiation, and bulk handling, reported robust Q2/H1 FY26 results amid a landmark acquisition. Below is a structured summary based on the provided filings (financials, investor presentation, preferential issue details, EGM outcomes, and press release).

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Exceptional Financial Momentum: Q2 FY26 consolidated revenue +15% YoY (Rs. 4,211 Cr), EBITDA +78% (Rs. 849 Cr, 20% margin), PAT +523% (Rs. 449 Cr, 11% margin). H1: Revenue +10% (Rs. 7,927 Cr), EBITDA +26%, PAT +83%. Driven by higher gross margins (59% vs. 53% YoY), cost efficiencies, and strong other income (Rs. 157 Cr).
  • Fundraise Success: Raised Rs. 1,713 Cr via preferential allotment of 8.59 Mn shares at Rs. 1,994 (premium Rs. 1,984) to marquee investors (e.g., Tata MF, Bandhan MF, ICICI Pru MF, HNIs). EGM approvals secured with 96-99% favor (Oct 2025).
  • Strategic Acquisition: Definitive agreement for Molycop (USD 1.45 Bn EV, down from term sheet USD 1.48 Bn). In consortium with Apollo; expands to 26 global plants, 400+ mine customers in 40 countries. Bolsters grinding media leadership (SAG/ball mills for Cu/Gold).
  • Operational Strength: Global footprint (92 countries), innovation focus, recurring “critical-to-operate” products. Healthy cash flows (H1 operating cash Rs. 1,539 Cr).

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Elevated Execution Costs: Post-acquisition integration (Molycop adds scale but complexity). H1 capex Rs. 490 Cr; inventories up slightly (Rs. 3,957 Cr), trade receivables high (Rs. 4,478 Cr).
  • Margin Pressures in Past: Prior quarters showed volatile raw material costs (e.g., Q2 FY25 EBITDA margin 13%); forex unrealized gains/losses impacted (H1 net positive but volatile).
  • Borrowing Increases: EGM approvals for higher limits (Section 180); current borrowings Rs. 2,294 Cr (Sep 2025). Acquisition funding may strain balance sheet (equity raised ~USD 200 Mn vs. USD 1.45 Bn EV).
  • Cyclical Sector Exposure: Mining slowdowns could hit demand; H1 employee/other expenses up 11-12% YoY.

Growth Prospects

  • Acquisition Synergies: Post-close (subject to approvals), Tega becomes top global player in consumables amid surging mineral demand (e.g., EV/green energy transition boosting Cu/Gold). Combined revenue potential >USD 1 Bn; cross-selling to expanded base.
  • Organic Expansion: Mining capex cycle up (global peers signaling growth); 2 segments (Consumables ~80%, Equipment). International revenue dominant; H1 gross profit +16% YoY.
  • Fund Utilization: Rs. 1,713 Cr earmarked for Molycop; deleverages post-deal. Analyst meet (Dec 2025) signals confidence.
  • Projections: FY26 guidance implied strong (20% EBITDA margin sustained); EPS H1 Rs. 12 (annualized ~Rs. 24). EV/EBITDA ~15-20x post-deal attractive.

Overall Outlook: Strong Buy/Bullish. Tailwinds from acquisition and results outweigh headwinds; growth prospects transformative (global #1 in niche). Monitor deal closure (Q1 FY27?). Target upside 20-30% from current levels (~Rs. 1,800-2,000 CMP implied), but volatile near-term on funding/deal risks. ROE ~12% H1; debt discipline key.
