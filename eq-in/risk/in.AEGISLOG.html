Analysis of Aegis Logistics Limited (AEGISLOG)

Aegis Logistics operates India’s leading network of tank terminals and gas logistics infrastructure, divided into Liquids (3PL storage, EPC) and Gas (LPG logistics, distribution, sourcing) divisions. Q2/H1 FY26 results show robust growth: Consolidated Revenue +31%/+20% YoY, Normalized EBITDA +46%/+24%, PAT +61%/+35% YoY, driven by record LPG volumes and capacity additions. Balance sheet strengthened post-AVTL IPO (Rs. 2,800 Cr raised in FY25 for debt repayment/capex). Dividend of Rs. 2/share declared. Shareholder approval for subsidiary AGPL’s material RPTs secured.

Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts)

  • Record Operational Performance: Q2 FY26 marked highest-ever revenues/EBITDA in both divisions (Gas: +60% EBITDA YoY on +32% logistics volumes; Liquids: +25% EBITDA). H1 throughput/utilization up significantly.
  • Capacity Expansion Momentum: Added 61k KL at Mumbai Port; land at Haldia/JNPT/Mangalore/Kandla. FY25 completions (Pipavav/Kandla/Mangalore/Kochi) + Ammonia terminal (36k MT at Pipavav). “Necklace of Terminals” across 7 ports with rail/pipeline connectivity.
  • Strong Balance Sheet & Liquidity: Equity up 50% YoY (NCI boost from AVTL IPO); debt reduced (borrowings down from Rs. 2,353 Cr to Rs. 425 Cr non-current); cash equivalents Rs. 783 Cr. Operating cash flow positive (Rs. 639 Cr H1).
  • LPG Demand Tailwinds: India’s LPG import reliance + autogas/industrial shift; integrated chain (sourcing JV with Itochu, 10+ terminals, 100+ distributors).
  • Diverse Revenue: Blue-chip clients (Reliance, HP, Shell, Nayara); recurring 3PL + distribution (autogas stations growing).

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • High Capex Intensity: Investing outflow Rs. 1,398 Cr H1 FY26 (vs. Rs. 584 Cr H1 FY25), funding expansions (CWIP Rs. 436 Cr). FY25 revenue dipped -4% YoY amid capex peak.
  • Forex/Hedging Volatility: Normalized EBITDA excludes these (Rs. 21 Cr Q2 expense); Gas sourcing exposed to global LPG prices.
  • Margin Pressures: Cost of sales ~82-83% of revenue; unallocated expenses/depreciation up (Rs. 52 Cr Q2 dep.).
  • Seasonal/Volume Dependency: Q2 strength vs. prior softness; liquids throughput growth lagged gas in FY25.

Growth Prospects

  • High Double-Digit Outlook: FY26 guided by expansions (e.g., JNPT 101k KL commissioning, Mangalore cryogenic LPG). Gas division (80%+ revenue) poised for 20-30% volume CAGR on distribution/autogas push.
  • Strategic Initiatives (Project GATI): Greenfield/brownfield (Haldia/JNPA), M&A, new energy (ammonia/green hydrogen), big-ticket (LPG capex FY25 largest ever).
  • Infrastructure Edge: Multi-modal evacuation (rail/pipelines at Pipavav/Kandla); largest LPG terminal operator; pan-India footprint (Kandla to Kochi).
  • Subsidiary Leverage: AVTL (44.71% owned) post-IPO de-risked; AGPL RPTs enable LPG scaling.
  • Sustainability: Vision aligns with India’s energy transition (clean fuels); potential Rs. 1,000+ Cr EBITDA by FY27+ on 2-3 Mn MT LPG capacity.

Key Risks

Risk Category Description Mitigation
Execution/Capex Delays in 7-port expansions/pipelines (CWIP Rs. 436 Cr); high outflow strains cash (net cash down Rs. 628 Cr H1). Phased rollout; IPO proceeds used.
Commodity/Price LPG price swings (sourcing 10-15% revenue); forex gains/losses. Hedging; long-term contracts/JVs.
Regulatory/Competition SEBI/IEPF compliance (unclaimed dividends notice); port tariffs/energy policy shifts. Strong compliance (CS filings); 1st-mover terminal network.
Financial/Leverage Lease liabilities Rs. 1,568 Cr; working capital swings (inventories down, receivables stable). Debt reduction post-IPO; RoCE ~20-25%.
Operational/ESG Safety in gas/liquid handling; geopolitical import risks. Highest standards; diverse sourcing.
Related Party AGPL RPTs approved but scrutiny risk. Shareholder nod (99.99% favour).

Overall Summary: Strong Buy Case – Tailwinds from volumes/capex outweigh headwinds; 20-25% EBITDA CAGR feasible on LPG infra boom. Risks execution-focused but balance sheet buffers them. Target multiples: 25-30x FY26E EV/EBITDA (peer avg. 20x). Monitor Q3 volumes/FY26 guidance.
