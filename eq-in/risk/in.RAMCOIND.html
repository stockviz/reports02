Analysis of Ramco Industries Limited (RAMCOIND) – Q2/H1 FY26 (Ended 30.09.2025)

Ramco Industries Limited, a building products-focused company (fibre cement boards, sheets, etc.) with textiles and windmill segments, reported mixed results. Standalone H1 revenue dipped 5% YoY to ₹732 Cr amid textiles weakness, but PAT rose 10% to ₹69 Cr on cost efficiencies and other income. Consolidated H1 revenue flat at ₹892 Cr, but PAT surged 89% to ₹107 Cr driven by associates (₹40 Cr share). Balance sheet remains strong (low D/E 0.13x standalone), with robust cash flows. Key highlight: Board approval for ₹180 Cr greenfield Fibre Cement Board plant (58k MT p.a.) in Madhya Pradesh.

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Profitability Resilience: Standalone operating margins improved to 12% (H1) from 11% YoY; consolidated at 11%. High other income (₹35 Cr standalone H1 from dividends/royalty) and cost control offset revenue softness.
  • Building Products Strength: Core segment up 2% YoY to ₹617 Cr (standalone H1); 78% of revenue. Segment results healthy at ₹41 Cr pre-tax/finance.
  • Associate Boost: Consolidated PAT inflated by ₹40 Cr from associates (e.g., Ramco Cements, Ramco Systems, Rajapalayam Mills), reflecting group synergies.
  • Financial Health: Net worth up (standalone ₹1,333 Cr); low leverage (D/E 0.13x); interest coverage 13x. Positive CFO (₹68 Cr standalone) supports growth.
  • Liquidity & Efficiency: Current ratio 2.2x; debtors turnover improved; inventory optimization post-Q1 buildup.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Revenue Pressure: Standalone H1 down 5% YoY; Q2 plunged 37% QoQ/3% YoY due to seasonality and textiles collapse (₹91 Cr vs ₹151 Cr YoY, -39%). Consolidated flat amid sub-par Q2.
  • Segment Imbalance: Textiles loss-making (₹0.5 Cr H1 result); windmills minor (2% revenue). Building products Q2 soft (₹212 Cr vs ₹406 Cr Q1).
  • Inventory & Working Capital: H1 inventory changes added ₹34 Cr expense (standalone), signaling demand slowdown or overstocking.
  • Margin Volatility: Heavy reliance on unallocable income (52% of PBT standalone); operating margins dipped in Q2 (17% vs 9% Q1).

Growth Prospects

  • Capacity Expansion: New 58k MT Fibre Cement Board plant (₹180 Cr; 75% debt-financed) to ramp in 12 months, targeting market demand. Enhances building products (already 78% revenue).
  • Segment Potential: Building products poised for volume growth; windmills stable (₹15 Cr H1). Associates (e.g., cements) could sustain high contributions.
  • Internal Funding: Strong accruals (₹32 Cr investing inflow from dividends) and CFO support capex without equity dilution.
  • Market Tailwinds: Rising infrastructure/housing demand in India favors fibre cement boards. Low capex intensity historically yields quick ROI.

Key Risks

Overall Outlook: Positive with cautious optimism. Profits beat revenue amid efficiencies, but textiles/Q2 weakness caps upside. New plant unlocks 20-25% building products capacity growth, targeting 10-15% revenue CAGR FY26-28. Stock supported by low debt, dividends, and group ecosystem. Monitor textiles revival and Q3 demand. Target multiples: 15-18x FY26E EPS (~₹20-22), implying 20-30% upside from current levels (assuming ~₹250-300 CMP). Buy/Hold for long-term infrastructure play.
