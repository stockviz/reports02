Analysis of Unimech Aerospace and Manufacturing Limited (UNIMECH)

Unimech Aerospace and Manufacturing Limited (listed on BSE: 544322, NSE: UNIMECH) is a precision engineering player focused on aero tooling/MRO tooling (75-78% of revenue), ground support equipment (GSE), and precision components/assemblies for aerospace, defense, nuclear, semiconductors, and emerging sectors. Q2 FY26 (ended Sep 2025) results show revenue of ₹62 Cr (flat YoY), H1 revenue ₹125 Cr (+4% YoY), EBITDA margin ~30% (down YoY due to tariffs/capex), PAT ₹15.6 Cr (22% margin). Order book at ₹105 Cr (as of early Nov 2025), capacity utilization ~55%. Key documents highlight tariff headwinds offset by diversification and inorganic push. Below is a structured analysis.

Headwinds (Short-term Challenges)
  • US Tariffs/Geopolitical Impact: Primary drag; ~30% US end-consumption affected by elevated duties, leading to order deferrals, shift from build-to-inventory to build-to-order (longer lead times), and revenue slowness. Customers delaying pickups; non-US (65-70%) stable via drop shipments/FTWZ.
  • Margin Pressure: EBITDA down 20% YoY in Q2 due to lower sales, higher employee costs (capex hires/ESOP), subcontracting (now 5.7% of sales, down from 6.9%), and prove-outs/FAIs in new segments. Gross margins held at 68% but H1 slightly lower YoY.
  • Low Utilization: 55% (machine capacity 6.83 lakh hours), due to capex (146 machines, +5 in Q2), qualification orders, and tariff delays. Fixed asset turnover 1.9x (target 3-3.5x in 24-30 months).
  • Guidance Downgrade: FY26 revenue growth <40% target; aim to beat FY25 revenue but with lower margins. Nuclear tenders delayed (bids ₹800 Cr submitted, results pending).
  • Working Capital Strain: Days to rise to 150 from 110 due to nuclear/GSE/LTAs.
Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts)
  • Order Wins & Book: Healthy ₹105 Cr book (95% tooling) + recent ₹35 Cr GSE order (5-12 months execution, largest recent win). Pipeline strong in aero, nuclear, precision.
  • Diversification Progress: Precision engineering (22% revenue) onboarding (new aero UAS customer, semi FAIs nearing approval, robotics/medical/defense approvals). 50+ FAIs completed; nuclear bids active (PHWR/FBR reactors).
  • Industry Momentum: Airbus A320 ramp (60→75/month by 2027), LEAP engines +40% YoY, India UDAN expansion, nuclear push (10x700MW PHWRs, FBRs).
  • Operational Improvements: Subcontracting down, in-house efficiency up. Gross margins resilient at 68%. Non-US stable.
  • Capex for Scale: Strategic investments (new facilities, 243k sq ft total); SKU base +10% to 5,200+.
Growth Prospects (Medium-Term Outlook)
  • Revenue Trajectory: Target ₹1,000 Cr by FY29 (65% aero tooling, 35% precision/nuclear). H2 FY26 strength expected from nuclear wins, LTAs, GSE execution.
  • Segment Expansion:
    • Aero Tooling: Exclusive contracts, complex tools; FTWZ mitigates tariffs.
    • Precision/Nuclear: Semi (CY26 orders), defense OEMs, oil/gas RFQs; nuclear tenders Nov-Dec.
    • Inorganic/JVs: Advanced talks (Middle East oil/gas, US aero/defense); Dheya (micro turbines, AS9100 certified, CEMILAC cert pending).
  • Diversification Mix: Reduce US/export reliance; geo-shift to Middle East/Europe; blended GM 62-63% at FY29 peak.
  • IPO Proceeds Reallocation: Postal ballot seeks approval to redirect ₹613 Cr unutilized (from capex/WC) to M&A/greenfield/JVs (₹1,000 Cr loan limits sought).
  • Capacity Ramp: Utilization to improve 7-10% by mid-FY27; ROCE/ROE to recover.
Key Risks
  • Tariff/Geo Risk (High): Prolonged US duties/residual tariffs could cap aero growth; supply chain disruptions.
  • Execution/Approval Delays (Medium): FAIs, nuclear tenders (L1 bidding), semi/nuclear LTAs; new segment ramp-up.
  • Margin/Financial (Medium): Capex drag (depn +8% QoQ), WC ballooning, EBITDA sensitivity to sales drop (2-3% dip possible).
  • Concentration (Medium): 75% aero tooling; customer/order delays.
  • Inorganic/Regulatory (High): M&A/JV execution, approvals (Sec 185/186 limits sought); variation needs 90% shareholder vote or exit offer.
  • Macro/Competition: Nuclear slowness, global MRO slowdown, forex volatility.
Summary

Bull Case: Unimech is resilient amid tariffs via diversification (precision/nuclear ramp), strong order pipeline (₹140 Cr+ book post-GSE), and inorganic firepower (₹613 Cr redirect + ₹1,000 Cr loans). Industry tailwinds (aero/nuclear growth) support FY29 ₹1,000 Cr target; margins stabilize post-utilization ramp. Balanced Outlook: FY26 muted (beat FY25 revenue, 30% EBITDA), H2 pickup from orders/JVs. Bear Case: Tariff persistence caps growth at low-20s%; execution slips erode margins. Valuation Context: Trade at premium to peers on growth promise, but monitor Q3 order execution/nuclear wins. Recommendation: Accumulate on dips; watch postal ballot (Dec 2025) for inorganic greenlight. Overall, strategic pivot positions for 25-30% CAGR medium-term.
