Analysis of Som Distilleries & Breweries Limited (SDBL) - Q2/H1 FY26 Overview

SDBL, a leading alcobev player focused on beer (89% of volumes) and IMFL, reported resilient profitability amid volume headwinds. Consolidated Q2 FY26 total income was ₹270 Cr (-7% YoY), EBITDA ₹40.5 Cr (+15% YoY, 15% margin), and PAT ₹19.5 Cr (+4% YoY, 7.2% margin). H1 FY26: Income ₹800 Cr (-1% YoY), EBITDA +13% YoY. Beer volumes down 19% YoY (Karnataka drag), offset by IMFL +65% YoY. FY26 revenue guidance: ~₹1,600 Cr. Strategic push via UP greenfield (10 Mn cases capacity by FY27) and premiumization.

Headwinds (Challenges Pressuring Performance)

  • Volume Declines: Beer sales fell 19% YoY in Q2 (36L cases) due to Karnataka tax hikes, industry slump (peers like UB affected), and Odisha softness. H1 beer -8%; total volumes -15% YoY.
  • Revenue Pressure: Topline down 7% YoY in Q2, flat H1, from state-specific issues and subdued consumption.
  • Rising Costs: Interest up 64% YoY (CC utilization for capex); higher depreciation from expansions.
  • External Probes: Customs inquiry on imports/exports (no violations/impact noted).
  • Working Capital Strain: Trade payables down, receivables up; capex funded via short-term debt.

Tailwinds (Supportive Factors Driving Resilience)

  • Margin Expansion: Gross margins +110 bps to 41% (better glass bottle mix, stable RM/packaging costs); EBITDA margins +290 bps to 15%.
  • IMFL Momentum: Volumes +65% YoY (4.1L cases); realization ₹939/case; legacy brands (Black Fort) +96%, new launches (Mahavat) gaining traction in MP/Delhi.
  • Realization Gains: Beer avg. ₹607/case (+14% YoY) from premium/can/state mix (MP/Jharkhand).
  • Debt Discipline: Net debt/EBITDA at 0.4x; gross debt/equity 0.27x.
  • New Brand Success: Sunny Beaches (Karnataka) hit 5K outlets in 1 month; Woodpecker airport push.

Growth Prospects (Key Catalysts for Expansion)

  • H2 Recovery: Karnataka October rebound (+0.5% MS regain); Odisha ramp-up; Andhra tender pending. H2 revenue guidance ₹800 Cr.
