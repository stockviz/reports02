Analysis of Manali Petrochemicals Limited (MANALIPETC)

Manali Petrochemicals Limited (MPL), a Chennai-based petrochemical manufacturer focused on propylene glycol, polyols, and derivatives, reported unaudited H1 FY26 results (ended Sep 30, 2025). Standalone performance showed modest recovery (H1 PAT ₹322 lakhs vs. loss of ₹897 lakhs YoY), driven by revenue stability amid cost pressures. Consolidated results were robust (H1 PAT ₹3,249 lakhs, revenue ₹48,271 lakhs), bolstered by international subsidiaries. Key highlight: Completed sale of step-down subsidiary Notedome Ltd. (UK) for ~₹247 Cr on Nov 17, 2025, aiding portfolio restructuring. Below is a structured summary of headwinds, tailwinds, growth prospects, and key risks.

Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts)

  • Subsidiary Strength & Divestment Gains: Consolidated profitability (H1 PBT ₹4,095 lakhs) heavily driven by overseas subs (e.g., AMCHEM Singapore, Pennwhite UK/India). Sale of Notedome (PU elastomers business) for £21.17M (~₹247 Cr) provides significant cash inflow, reduces complexity, and enables debt repayment/focus on core polyols. Postal ballot approval (99.97% in favor) reflects strong shareholder support.
  • Cash Resilience: Consolidated cash & equivalents at ₹317 Cr (up from ₹189 Cr FY25-end), with H1 operating cash flow positive at ₹36 Cr. Healthy liquidity supports capex (CWIP ₹97 Cr cons.).
  • Revenue Momentum: Standalone Q2 revenue up 9% YoY (₹180 Cr); cons. H1 revenue +2% YoY. Geographic diversification (India 71%, EU/UK 29% of cons. revenue).
  • Strategic Refocus: Divestment sharpens emphasis on high-demand polyols applications (automotive, cold chain, construction, footwear) in India, with retained marketing rights for cast elastomers under new brand.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Thin Standalone Margins: H1 standalone PBT ₹433 lakhs (vs. loss YoY), pressured by high material costs (55% of expenses), power/fuel (12%), and inventory changes. EBITDA margins ~5-6%, vulnerable to raw material volatility.
  • Flat Domestic Growth: Standalone H1 revenue flat YoY; trading activity (₹17 Cr purchases) opportunistic but non-core.
  • Subsidiary Review Gaps: Two minor subs unreviewed by auditors (immaterial: <0.1% assets/revenue); three foreign subs reviewed externally, adding integration risks.
  • Macro Pressures: Petrochemical cyclicality, with prior flood impacts (Cyclone Michaung) lingering.

Growth Prospects

  • Core Polyols Expansion: Strategic pivot post-Notedome sale to scale polyols/derivatives in India (key markets: automotive, refrigeration). Ongoing capex (₹39 Cr H1 outflow) signals capacity build-up.
  • M&A Flexibility: Chairman highlighted potential global acquisitions in aligned sectors, funded by divestment proceeds.
  • Export/Subs Leverage: EU/UK revenue stable (~14% of cons. H1); retained elastomer branding supports niche growth.
  • Outlook: FY26 cons. revenue could grow 10-15% if polyols demand sustains (India focus + M&A). Notedome sale boosts FY26 PAT by ~₹150-200 Cr (post-tax/gains), targeting EPS >₹2 (vs. FY25 ₹1.70).

Key Risks

Operational/Lease Unit-II lease expired 2017 (renewal pending with TN Govt.; Ind AS 116 assumes 30-yr extension). Asset impairment (ROU assets ₹78 Cr cons.); production halt. Mgmt. confident (rent paid to 2026); historical allotments for industrial use.
Insurance Recovery ₹210 Cr flood claim receivable (net of ₹30 Cr advance) for PPE/inventories; inventory settled but PPE pending. Profit hit if denied (already expensed ₹98 lakhs exceptional). Claims crystallized for stocks; ongoing insurer assessment.
Commodity/FX Volatility Petrochem input costs (e.g., materials 52% cons. expenses); FX gains from subs (₹23 Cr OCI H1). Margin erosion; 20-30% revenue forex-exposed. Hedging/diversification via subs.
Execution/Post-Divestment Integration of sale (Notedome results included in H1); capex delays (CWIP up 60% YoY). Revenue dip if client transition fails. Retained India marketing rights; cash buffer.
Regulatory/External Auditor emphasis on lease/floods (unmodified conclusion); climate risks (floods). Stock volatility; compliance costs. Strong governance (board approval Nov 10, 2025).

Overall Summary: MPL is at an inflection point with Notedome divestment (~₹247 Cr liquidity boost) as a major tailwind, enabling core focus and potential M&As. Consolidated growth outlook positive (driven by subs/polyols), but standalone headwinds persist amid thin margins and uncertainties (lease/insurance). Valuation Context: Trade at ~1x FY25 BVPS (cons. equity ₹1,142 Cr); divestment could re-rate to 12-15x FY26 EPS. Monitor Q3 results for sale gains/claim updates. Recommendation: Accumulate on dips for 20-30% upside in 12 months, with lease renewal as key catalyst.
