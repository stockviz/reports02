Analysis of Cupid Limited (NSE: CUPID, BSE: 530843)

Cupid Limited, a manufacturer and exporter of condoms (male/female), lubricants, IVD kits, and expanding FMCG products (deodorants, perfumes, hair oils, etc.), reported record Q2 FY26 results (ended Sep 30, 2025). Revenue surged 103% YoY to ₹84.4 Cr (H1: +79% YoY), with net profit up 140% YoY to ₹24.1 Cr (H1: +114% YoY). EBITDA margins hit 34% (Q2), driven by execution efficiency, export orders, and FMCG traction. Balance sheet strengthened (cash ₹177 Cr; equity ₹361 Cr), with low debt and ₹96 Cr retained from expired warrants. Promoter stake rose to 45.56% via open-market buys. FY26 guidance: ₹335 Cr revenue (up ~82% from FY25 implied), >₹100 Cr net profit. Outlook positive, but execution risks persist.

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Explosive Financial Momentum: Q2 revenue +103% YoY, EBITDA +176% YoY; H1 net profit margins 27% (+446 bps YoY). Strong cash flow from ops (₹23 Cr H1) and investing (₹88 Cr from investments).
  • Promoter Confidence: CMD Aditya Halwasiya added 5L shares; group holding ~45.6%.
  • Product Diversification & Market Penetration: India FMCG (condoms, deodorants, pregnancy kits, hair oils) gaining retail/e-comm traction. Exports to 110+ countries (B2B strength via WHO/UNFPA prequal).
  • Certifications & Order Visibility: CE-IVD unlocks Europe/Africa/Asia; WHO PQ for malaria kits (FY27) and female condom ring. H2FY26 expected stronger.
  • Capacity & Efficiency: New Palava plant (2.5x capacity by FY27: +770M male/+75M female condoms annually). Procurement de-bottlenecking aids execution.
  • Robust Balance Sheet: Net cash position; no dilution post-warrants expiry.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Working Capital Pressure: Trade receivables ballooned to ₹89.7 Cr (Q2 revenue ₹84 Cr), up 34% QoQ – potential collection delays in exports/FMCG.
  • Inventory Buildup: +₹9.7 Cr QoQ to ₹42.6 Cr; minor change in inventories dragged margins.
  • Subsidiary Drag: Wholly-owned Cupid Invesco Ltd. (inc. Nov 2024) reported small loss (₹1.3L Q2); immaterial but adds complexity.
  • Seasonality/Macro: H2 historically stronger, but forex volatility (noted in cash flow) and global tender competition could impact exports (~major revenue source).
  • High Growth Base: Sustaining 80%+ YoY growth amid competition in saturated condom/FMCG markets.

Growth Prospects

  • Near-Term (FY26): On track for ₹335 Cr revenue (+82% YoY implied), ₹100+ Cr profit. H2 acceleration via order book, FMCG launches (facewash, talcum), and export allocations.
  • Medium-Term (FY27+): 2.5x capacity unlocks scale; certifications drive IVD/female condom tenders. FMCG retail expansion (modern/general trade, e-comm) positions as “young FMCG player.” Potential upside if H1 momentum sustains.
  • Long-Term: Global leadership in condoms (first WHO-prequalified for both genders); IVD/FMCG diversification reduces single-product risk. Exports + domestic B2C could drive 20-30% CAGR.

Key Risks

Risk Category Description Mitigation
Execution Capacity ramp-up delays; supply chain bottlenecks in expansion. Promoter-led; ongoing de-bottlenecking.
Regulatory Delays in WHO PQ/CE benefits; tender losses. Strong track record (existing prequals).
Financial Receivables/default risk (34% of assets); forex gains/losses (₹1.1 Cr Q2 impact). ₹177 Cr cash buffer; low debt (D/E <0.1).
Market/Competition Intense rivalry in condoms/FMCG; margin erosion from raw material inflation. High margins (34% EBITDA); brand investments.
Macro Rupee depreciation, global demand slowdown (exports heavy). Hedging (forward contracts noted); diversified markets.
Governance Promoter dominance (45.6%); minor AGM dissent (<0.003%). Clean auditor reviews; compliant disclosures.

Overall Summary: Strong Buy Thesis – Tailwinds dominate with hyper-growth, pristine finances, and strategic expansion outweighing manageable headwinds/risks. FY26 guidance achievable with upside (H2 record quarters). Monitor receivables and capacity milestones. High-conviction play on India’s wellness/FMCG export story; target multiple expansion on 20x FY26 EPS possible. (Ratings: Tailwinds 9/10, Risks 6/10).
