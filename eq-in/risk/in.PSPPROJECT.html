Executive Summary

PSPPROJECT Limited, an India-based construction and project execution company, has recently reported its unaudited financial results for the first half and second quarter ending September 30, 2025. While the company shows signs of revenue recovery and expanding margins in its core operations, it is experiencing structural changes in governance and audit oversight. A GST penalty notice issued in December 2025 and the resignation of one of its joint statutory auditors raise regulatory and investor perception concerns. Despite these headwinds, strong operational performance, a clear business focus, and active engagement with institutional investors suggest underlying growth momentum. This analysis presents a balanced view of tailwinds, headwinds, growth prospects, and key risks.

2. Tailwinds & Growth Prospects

✅ 1. Revenue Growth & Operational Recovery

  • Robust sequential revenue growth: Revenue rose from ₹51,276 lakh (Q1) to ₹69,369 lakh (Q2), up +35.3% QoQ, indicating acceleration in project execution.
  • Improvement in profitability: PBT surged from ₹41 lakh (Q1) to ₹2,056 lakh (Q2), signaling better cost control and margin recovery after a weak start.
  • The construction segment remains the sole reporting segment (per Ind AS 108), ensuring clear strategic focus.

✅ 2. Strong Balance Sheet Fundamentals

  • Total Assets up 13.3%: From ₹2,33,657 lakh (Mar’25) to ₹2,64,675 lakh (Sep’25) — growth driven by increased WIP and receivables.
  • Equity growth: Other equity (excluding reserves) rose from ₹1,16,910 lakh to ₹1,18,406 lakh.
  • Debt management: Long-term borrowings under control; lease liabilities stable.
  • Cash and Cash Equivalents: Improved to ₹54,470 lakh (up from ₹39,257 lakh), enhancing liquidity.

✅ 3. Investor Engagement & Transparency

  • The company is actively organizing analyst and institutional investor meetings (scheduled for Dec 8, 2025 in Mumbai), signaling transparency and investor outreach.
  • Timely compliance with SEBI LODR via disclosures for financial results, auditor change, and regulatory actions builds governance credibility.

✅ 4. New Statutory Auditor with Diversified Expertise

  • Appointment of M/s. G.K. Choksi & Co., a well-established CA firm with strong experience in Ind AS, M&A, and forensic audits, may bring enhanced scrutiny and advisory depth.
  • The auditor change was not due to audit disagreements or inability to obtain evidence — reassuring for stakeholders.

✅ 5. Consolidation Benefits

  • Consolidated PAT (to owners) grew +62.6% YoY in Q2 (₹1,022L → ₹1,617L), outpacing standalone, suggesting positive subsidiary/joint venture contribution despite limited scope.
  • The joint venture GDCL & PSP JV (49% ownership) likely contributes incremental value with low operational risk.
3. Headwinds & Challenges

⚠️ 1. Decline in H1 Profitability (YoY)

  • H1 PAT (Standalone): ₹1,508 lakh vs. ₹4,543 lakh in H1 FY25 — a -66.8% decline.
  • H1 PAT (Consolidated): ₹1,659 lakh vs. ₹4,490 lakh — down -63.1%.
  • This suggests either high-margin projects were completed in prior year, or cost inefficiencies in new projects are impacting margins.

⚠️ 2. Working Capital Pressure

  • Significant increase in trade receivables (₹43,514 lakh in Sep’25 vs. ₹39,257 lakh in Mar’25) reflects strong revenue but rising working capital cycle.
  • Trade payables decreased (₹32,257L → ₹32,254L), while inventories increased (₹35,818L → ₹40,667L) — possible indication of slower execution or input procurement buildup.

⚠️ 3. GST Penalty Order (Dec 2, 2025)

  • Received GST DRC-07 Order under Section 74 for FY2018–19, seeking a penalty of ₹14.43 lakh for ineligible ITC claims from:
    • Cancelled taxpayers
    • Non-genuine suppliers
    • Defaulters
  • Company response: Plans to appeal; claims no material financial impact.
  • Risk: This could indicate historical compliance lapses. Though minor in monetary terms, it may attract tax scrutiny in future years. Also, if more supplier fraud is uncovered, further penalties could arise.

⚠️ 4. Auditor Resignation & Perception Risk

  • Resignation of M/s. Prakash B. Sheth & Co., one of the joint auditors, citing resource constraints due to expected growth, is benign on surface.
  • However, any auditor resignation raises red flags among investors and rating agencies.
  • No concerns raised, no disagreements, and relying auditor (Kantilal Patel & Co.) confirmed reliability of results — mitigates immediate risk.
4. Key Growth Drivers (Forward-Looking)
  • Order Book Pipeline: Not disclosed, but the Q2 revenue spike suggests recent order execution or new project starts.
  • Focus on Infrastructure & Real Estate: Core sectors benefitting from government capex push (PM Gati Shakti, housing schemes).
  • Digital & ESG Transparency: ISO-certified; ongoing disclosures strengthen ESG credentials.
  • Institutional Investor Outreach: Scheduled analyst meetings may lead to increased visibility and portfolio inclusion.
  • Potential QIP Proceeds: Reference in cash flow for “Proceeds from QIP (net)” suggests capital raise may be in process or completed, funding future expansion.
