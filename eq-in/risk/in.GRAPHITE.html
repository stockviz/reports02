Graphite India Limited (GRAPHITE) Analysis

Based on Q2 & H1 FY26 Unaudited Results (Standalone & Consolidated, ended Sep 30, 2025)

Graphite India, a leading manufacturer of graphite electrodes (key input for electric arc furnaces in steelmaking), reported revenue growth but sharp profitability decline amid falling electrode prices. Standalone H1 revenue rose 8% YoY to ₹1,353 Cr (driven by Graphite & Carbon segment up 7% to ₹1,214 Cr), but PBT fell 37% to ₹299 Cr and PAT to ₹237 Cr (EPS ₹12.13). Consolidated trends similar: Revenue +2% to ₹1,394 Cr, PAT to parent ₹211 Cr (EPS ₹10.78). Balance sheet remains robust (equity ₹5,603 Cr standalone; net cash position supported by ₹2,890 Cr current investments), but operating cash flow turned negative (-₹12/-₹19 Cr) due to working capital drag.

Headwinds (Key Pressures)

  • Electrode Price Collapse: Persistent fall in realizations led to ₹78 Cr (standalone)/₹80 Cr (cons.) inventory write-down to NRV (down from ₹140/₹149 Cr YoY but still elevated). Graphite segment results halved to ₹63 Cr (vs. ₹137 Cr YoY).
  • Margin Compression: Higher input costs (materials +12% YoY, power stable but elevated) and forex impacts eroded EBITDA margins; PBT margins ~22% (H1 standalone, down from 38% YoY).
  • Working Capital Strain: Trade receivables up 25% to ₹569 Cr, inventories +4% to ₹1,130 Cr, driving negative op. cash flow despite strong EBITDA.
  • Subsidiary Drag: Overseas units (e.g., via Graphite India B.V.) reported losses (₹17.6 Cr Q2 cons.), weighing on group profits.

Tailwinds (Supportive Factors)

  • Revenue Momentum: Core Graphite segment +7-10% YoY growth (standalone/consolidated), Steel stable, signaling volume recovery amid steel demand.
  • Treasury Strength: Other income ₹237 Cr (H1 standalone, ~80% of PBT) from investment gains/dividends buffers ops volatility. Liquid assets (investments + cash) ~₹3,000 Cr; low debt (borrowings ₹163/₹267 Cr).
  • Dividend Discipline: ₹215 Cr payout maintained (yield ~5-6% at current prices), signaling confidence.
  • Cost Discipline: Employee/stores/power expenses stable YoY; unallocable income (₹309 Cr standalone) from treasury ops.

Growth Prospects (Moderate, Cyclical Recovery)

  • Steel Cycle Tail: Electrodes ~90% of revenue; global steel demand (esp. India/China EAF shift) could drive 10-15% volume growth FY26-27 if capex sustains (CWIP ₹100 Cr up from ₹66 Cr).
  • Capex Ramp: ₹76/₹79 Cr H1 spend (plants/expansion); new capacity could boost utilization >80%.
  • Diversification: Steel (stable 8-10% of rev) + Others (investments/subs); international ops (7 subs) add forex hedges but low contribution.
  • Outlook: FY26E revenue +10-15% feasible if electrode prices stabilize at $5-7k/tonne; PAT recovery to ₹800-900 Cr (P/E 10-12x) on volume/margin rebound. Long-term: EV/battery graphite opportunity (nascent via General Graphene sub).

Key Risks (High Volatility)

Risk Category Description Mitigation
Commodity Price Electrode prices volatile (80%+ revenue exposure); further fall could add ₹50-100 Cr write-downs. Treasury buffer; volume focus.
Cyclical Demand Steel slowdown (China exports, infra delays) hits volumes. India steel capex (₹2-3L Cr planned).
Working Capital Receivables/inventory buildup (₹156 Cr H1 drag); liquidity risk if prolonged. Strong cash gen history (₹384 Cr H1 FY25).
Tax/Regulatory ₹417 Cr refund under appeal (AY19-20); no provision but uncertainty. Prudent disclosure; robust equity.
Forex/Subsidiaries Overseas losses + FX swings (unrealized gains/losses). Hedging; 90% India-centric.
Execution Capex delays; inventory obsolescence. Proven track record.

Overall Summary: Graphite faces near-term headwinds from electrode price weakness (profitability halved YoY) and WC pressures, but robust balance sheet (net cash, 5.5x equity/assets) and revenue growth provide resilience. Growth hinges on steel recovery and price stabilization; FY26 muted but FY27 upside on capex. Investment Stance: Accumulate on dips (target ₹650-700; 12-15x FY27E); high beta to steel cycle. Monitor Q3 earnings (Nov 2025) for price trends.
