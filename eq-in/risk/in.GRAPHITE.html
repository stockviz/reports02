Analysis of Graphite India Limited (GRAPHITE)

Graphite India Limited (GIL), a leading manufacturer of graphite electrodes (key input for steel EAF/IF furnaces), steel, and carbon products, reported unaudited Q2/H1 FY26 results on Nov 10, 2025. Key highlights: Standalone H1 revenue up 8% YoY to ₹1,353 Cr (driven by Graphite & Carbon segment), but PAT down 39% to ₹237 Cr due to lower margins and inventory writedowns. Consolidated H1 revenue up 2% to ₹1,394 Cr, PAT down 51% to ₹209 Cr. Balance sheet remains robust with ₹5,603 Cr equity, high liquid investments (~₹3,975 Cr total), and low net debt. Significant other income (₹237 Cr standalone H1) from investment gains cushions operations. Inventory writedown at ₹78 Cr (standalone) reflects electrode price pressure.

Headwinds

  • Electrode Price Decline: Persistent fall in graphite electrode prices led to NRV-based inventory writedowns (₹78 Cr standalone as of Sep’25, down from ₹140 Cr YoY but still elevated). Graphite segment margins compressed (H1 segment result ₹63 Cr vs. ₹137 Cr YoY).
  • Margin Pressure: Q2 standalone EBITDA margin ~17% (down from ~39% YoY); higher material costs (+24% YoY), power/fuel (+13%), and unfavorable inventory changes (+₹87 Cr). Consolidated PBT margins fell to 19% (H1).
  • Working Capital Strain: H1 operating cash flow negative at -₹12 Cr (standalone) / -₹19 Cr (consol) due to ₹112 Cr/₹117 Cr rise in receivables and ₹42 Cr/₹33 Cr inventory build-up.
  • Subsidiary Drag: International subs (e.g., Graphite International B.V. group) reported losses (H1 net loss ₹32 Cr), impacting consolidated profits.

Tailwinds

  • Revenue Momentum: Standalone revenue +8% YoY (H1), Graphite segment +7% to ₹1,214 Cr. Consolidated +2% despite subs’ weakness. Volume-led growth amid steel demand recovery.
  • Strong Liquidity & Returns: ₹3,778 Cr investments (current + non-current), yielding high other income (30% of H1 total income standalone). Dividend payout sustained at ₹215 Cr (H1).
  • Cost Discipline: Employee costs flat YoY; finance costs minimal (₹2 Cr H1 standalone). Unrealized forex gains aided.
  • Asset-Light Model: Capex at ₹76 Cr (H1), funded internally; CWIP up to ₹100 Cr signals capacity expansion.

Growth Prospects

  • Steel Cycle Recovery: Electrodes tied to global steel production (EAF furnaces); India steel output growth (7-8% projected FY26) could drive volumes. Graphite revenue share ~90%, with pricing stabilization possible if needle coke costs ease.
  • Capacity Expansion: Ongoing capex (PPE + CWIP ₹1,049 Cr standalone) for efficiency upgrades; potential export push via subs (Europe/US focus).
  • Diversification: Steel segment steady (+6% H1 revenue); “Others” volatile but high-margin (e.g., investment income). Investor meet (Nov 26, 2025, with Kotak AMC) signals engagement.
  • Investment Income: Treasury portfolio (FVTPL gains ₹175 Cr H1 standalone) scalable with AUM growth; EPS resilient at ₹12.13 (H1 standalone basic).
  • Outlook: FY26 revenue growth 10-15% possible if electrodes stabilize; ROE ~17-20% supported by cash pile (net cash ~₹2,800 Cr post-borrowings).

Key Risks

  • Cyclical Volatility: Electrode prices highly sensitive to steel demand/Chinese exports; further declines could widen writedowns (historical peak ₹140 Cr).
  • Commodity Exposure: Needle coke/pet coke inflation (key raw material); power/fuel costs ~13% of expenses.
  • Forex & Geo-Political: 20% revenue international; EUR/USD volatility + subs’ losses (e.g., Bavaria group). Ukraine/Russia tensions impact Europe ops.
  • Tax Uncertainty: ₹417 Cr refunds received but appeals pending on deductions (AY19-20); ₹500 Cr current tax liability prudent but contingent.
  • Investment Risk: 30-40% profits from volatile FVTPL gains; equity market correction could hit.
  • Regulatory/Execution: Capex delays; MSME payables up 35% YoY signals supplier pressure.

Summary

GIL faces near-term headwinds from electrode price weakness and margin compression, eroding YoY profits despite revenue growth. Tailwinds include a fortress balance sheet, liquid investments, and volume recovery. Growth prospects hinge on steel demand stabilization and capex fruition, targeting mid-teens revenue CAGR. Key risks are cyclical pricing, forex, and tax disputes—monitor electrode prices and Q3 volumes. Investment Thesis: Hold/Buy for cash-rich defensive play (trading ~20x FY26 EPS); dividend yield attractive (~4-5%). Target upside 15-20% if margins rebound to 25%+.
