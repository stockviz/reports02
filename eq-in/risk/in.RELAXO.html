Analysis of Relaxo Footwears Limited (RELAXO) - Q2 & H1 FY26

Relaxo Footwears, India’s largest footwear manufacturer by volume, reported a revenue decline in Q2 FY26 (₹629 Cr, -7.5% YoY) and H1 FY26 (₹1,283 Cr, -10.1% YoY), driven by demand softness and GST transition. However, margins held steady or improved (H1 EBITDA margin +101 bps to 14.1%; PAT +4.9% YoY to ₹85 Cr at 6.6% margin) due to cost efficiencies. The company attributes past degrowth primarily to the 2022 GST hike (5% to 12%), now reversing with GST 2.0. Below is a structured analysis of headwinds, tailwinds, growth prospects, and key risks.

Headwinds (Challenges Impacting Performance)

  • Demand Weakness and Channel Destocking: Muted consumer sentiment in mass/mid-market segments led to delayed purchases. Distributors (45 days inventory) prioritized liquidating old high-MRP stock post-GST announcement (Sep 3, 2025), delaying primary bookings. Q2 volume growth estimated -7.5% (normalized: -4.5% to -5.5%). General trade (75% revenue) slowed; uniform decline across open (80% mix) and closed footwear.
  • GST Transition Friction: Inverted duty structure (output GST 5%, input imports 18%) creates refund delays; no govt relief yet. Full channel flush expected Dec-Jan 2026.
  • Macro and Competitive Pressures: Weak festive demand (early onset), ongoing distributor rationalization, and competition from unorganized players/fakes (North India). Organized peers (e.g., Bata) also affected.
  • Historical Drag: 4-year revenue stagnation linked ~90% to 2022 GST hike; Q1 FY26 -12%, Q2 -8%.

Tailwinds (Positive Factors Supporting Resilience)

  • GST 2.0 Benefits: Rate cut to 5% on <₹2,500 footwear (98% portfolio; 90%+ <₹1,000) enhances affordability/competitiveness vs unorganized. New MRPs reflected in North; secondary sales up 20% via Relaxo Parivaar App (60% contribution).
  • Margin Resilience: Stable Q2 EBITDA (12.9%); H1 expansion via cost control, backend optimization, and higher other income (treasury + hedging gains). Ad spends ~4% of revenue.
  • Operational Strengths: 10.5L pairs/day capacity (sufficient 2-3 years); 80-20 open-closed mix stable; in-house mfg (9 plants, certifications); diversified channels (75% GT, 10% exports/retail/e-com).
  • Portfolio and Distribution: Strong brands (Sparx 40%, Hawaii/Relaxo/Bahamas 37-40%); pan-India network (~550 distributors, 70K retailers, 414 EBOs); e-com traction.

Growth Prospects

  • Near-Term Recovery (FY26): Q3 flat/-3-4% (better than Q2’s -8%); Q4 growth from festivals, GST normalization (Dec-Jan), and restocking. H1 volume -YoY but ASP stable (~₹151/pair).
  • Medium-Term (FY27+): Return to pre-2022 10%+ trajectory; volume-led gains as GST levels playing field. Focus: Premiumization (athleisure/sneakers/premium PU Flite), secondary sales push, distributor addition/logistics (RPA app), product innovation.
  • Strategic Levers: Capex ₹100-150 Cr (efficiency/warehouses, no expansion); 20% secondary growth; exports/e-com/LFR momentum. Management optimistic: “Next year definitely better.”
  • Industry Tailwinds: Organized segment growth (65% unorganized); rising sports/eco-friendly demand; digital transformation.

Key Risks

Risk Category Description Mitigation
Demand/Channel Prolonged destocking or weak sentiment delays recovery; GT dependency (75%). GST revival; app-driven secondary focus; distributor rationalization.
GST-Related Inverted duty refunds <100%; no govt relief. Manageable per mgmt; monitor 2-3 months; full price pass-through.
Competition Unorganized/fakes/D2C influx; MNC copies at low prices. GST competitiveness; brand actions; differentiated consumer (Sparx vs Nike fakes).
Operational No near-term capex expansion; uniform category decline. Sufficient capacity; maintenance capex sustains efficiency.
Macro/External Inflation/rate cuts uncertain; forex (imports); BIS minimal impact on China. Hedging gains; net cash positive (₹292 Cr cash surplus FY25).
Financial Working capital strain (inventories up to ₹594 Cr); trade receivables ₹273 Cr. Strong balance sheet (net worth ₹2,109 Cr; ROE 8.3%; current ratio 2.4x).

Summary Outlook

Relaxo faces transitional headwinds from GST 2.0 destocking and mass-market softness, but structural tailwinds (GST cut, efficiencies) preserved/improved margins amid revenue dip. Growth prospects are promising for H2 FY26/FY27 (10%+), driven by demand revival, channel normalization, and market share gains in a consolidating industry. Risks are moderate (GST execution, competition), offset by robust fundamentals (debt-free, capacity buffer). Stock trades at ~11,100 Cr mcap (52W: ₹390-822); positive on GST normalization, but monitor Q3 primary sales. Recommendation: Accumulate on dips for medium-term recovery.
