Relaxo Footwears Limited (RELAXO) Analysis

Fiscal Year Context: FY26 refers to April 2025–March 2026. Q2/H1 FY26 results (ended Sep 30, 2025) show revenue decline amid GST transition, but stable/improved margins due to cost control. Company focuses on mass-market footwear (98% portfolio < INR 2,500), with strong in-house manufacturing (10.5L pairs/day capacity) and distribution (~550 distributors, 70K+ retailers, EBOs, e-commerce).

Headwinds (Challenges Impacting Performance)

  • Demand Softness & GST Transition: Revenue down 7.5% YoY in Q2 (INR 629 Cr) and 10.1% in H1 (INR 1,283 Cr) due to muted mass-market demand and distributor destocking (30-35 days company inventory + 45 days channel). Old high-MRP inventory liquidation delays primary bookings until Dec-Jan.
  • Consumer Sentiment: Persistent weakness across categories since GST hike (Jan 2022); Q1 -12%, Q2 -8%, Q3 expected flat to -3-4%.
  • Inverted Duty Structure: Post-GST 2.0 (output 5%, imports 18%), potential non-refundable duty hit; monitoring for 2-3 months, but could pressure margins if unresolved.
  • Competition: Unorganized players gained during 12% GST era; fakes/copies in North India persist (though customer profiles differ).
  • Channel Dynamics: General trade (75% revenue) slowed; early festive shift and distributor caution impacted Q2 volumes (-7.5% normalized).

Tailwinds (Positive Factors Supporting Resilience)

  • GST 2.0 Benefits: Rate cut to 5% for <INR 2,500 (covers 98% portfolio, 90%+ <INR 1,000) enhances affordability/competitiveness vs unorganized. New MRPs already in Northern markets, boosting footfall/secondary sales.
  • Margin Resilience: EBITDA margin stable at 12.9% (Q2) and expanded 101 bps YoY to 14.1% (H1) via cost rationalization, efficiencies (lean mfg., yield improvement). PAT margin up 34 bps (Q2) / 95 bps (H1) to 5.8%/6.6%.
  • Other Income Boost: Higher treasury (INR 12 Cr Q2 vs INR 7 Cr prior) from elevated cash; hedging gains; potential rate cuts ahead.
  • Distribution & Tech: RPA app drove 20% secondary growth (60% sales contribution); adding distributors, last-mile logistics focus.
  • Portfolio Strength: Diversified brands (Sparx 40%, Flite 38%, Relaxo/Bahamas 25%); 80% open/20% closed footwear stable.

Growth Prospects

  • Near-Term Recovery (H2 FY26): Q4 growth expected from festivals, GST tailwinds, inventory normalization. Primary sales to improve mid-Dec; aim flat/slight growth in Q3.
  • Medium-Term (FY27+): 10%+ volume-led growth trajectory (pre-2022 levels) as GST levels playing field; market share gains in mass/mid segments. Normalized growth post-channel flush-out.
  • Strategic Drivers:
    • Premiumization: Athleisure/sneakers (Sparx/Flite); premium PU launches; ASP uptick.
    • Channels: E-com/LFR growth; exports to 37 countries; 414 EBOs.
    • Efficiency: Capacity sufficient (no expansion needed); capex INR 100-150 Cr (molds, warehouses, HO).
    • Industry Tailwinds: Organized segment growth (45% unorganized market); sports/eco-trends.
  • Outlook: Optimistic; “next year definitely better”; focus on consumer offtake, innovation.

Key Risks

Risk Category Description Mitigation
Demand/Macro Prolonged weak sentiment/economic slowdown delays recovery. GST benefits, festivals; broad portfolio.
GST/Regulatory Inverted duty refunds delayed; BIS on imports minor (insignificant for key brands). Monitoring; full price pass-through (no margin retention).
Competition Unorganized/fakes erode share; D2C/MNCs in premium. GST parity; legal actions; differentiated consumer base.
Operational Inventory overhang; raw material volatility. 30-45 day inventory; hedging; in-house mfg. efficiencies.
Financial Capex overrun; forex/debt (net cash positive). Low debt (net cash INR 292 Cr); ROE 8.3%, ROCE 12%.
Execution Distributor rationalization impacts volumes. Ongoing additions; app/tech focus.

Overall Summary: RELAXO faces short-term headwinds from GST transition and demand weakness (revenue -7-10% YoY), but tailwinds from 5% GST (boosting competitiveness) and cost discipline (margin expansion) position it for H2 recovery and FY27 growth (10%+). Risks are manageable (transitionary destocking, competition), with strong fundamentals (debt-free, capacity headroom). Buy/Accumulate on dips for long-term mass-market leader; target recovery to pre-GST growth trajectory. (Valuation context: Market cap ~INR 11,100 Cr; EV ~11,103 Cr as of Sep 2025).
