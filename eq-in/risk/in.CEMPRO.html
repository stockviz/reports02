Analysis of Cemindia Projects Limited (CEMPRO)

Cemindia Projects Limited (formerly ITD Cementation India Limited), an Adani Group entity since 2025, is a diversified engineering, procurement, and construction (EPC) player focused on maritime structures, urban infra (metros/airports), industrial buildings, hydro/tunnels, data centers, and more. It reported strong Q2 FY26 results (ended Sep 2025): consolidated revenue up 9% YoY to ₹2,175 Cr, EBITDA up 19% to ₹242 Cr (11.1% margin), PAT up 49% to ₹108 Cr. Order book stands at ₹20,646 Cr (multi-year visibility), with H1 inflows of ₹6,189 Cr. Balance sheet healthy (Net D/E 0.25x). Recent auditor switch to Price Waterhouse aligns with Adani’s global standardization. Listed on NSE (CEMPRO) and BSE (509496); market cap ~₹14,500 Cr (Oct 2025).

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Robust Order Pipeline & Execution: Order book diversified (Maritime 33%, Urban Infra 24%, Industrial 23%); H1 inflows ₹6,189 Cr signal strong bidding success. Key wins: Data center (₹1,307 Cr), Kolkata Metro (₹930 Cr), Vizhinjam Port (₹362 Cr).
  • Financial Momentum: Revenue/EBITDA/PAT growth (H1: +8%/+13%/+42% YoY); margin expansion (EBITDA 10.5-11.1%). Net cash from ops improved to ₹279 Cr (H1 FY26 vs. outflow in prior).
  • Adani Group Synergies: Backing enhances funding access, project pipeline (e.g., ports, infra). Credit upgrades: CARE/ICRA A+ (Stable).
  • Sector Tailwinds: India’s infra boom (Gati Shakti, ₹11L Cr capex); metros, ports, data centers growing. International exposure (Sri Lanka, Bangladesh, UAE).
  • Balance Sheet Strength: Low leverage (Net Debt ₹510 Cr); cash reserves up to ₹449 Cr.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Working Capital Pressure: High receivables (₹1,372 Cr), unbilled revenue (₹2,039 Cr); H1 trade payables down ₹273 Cr strained liquidity despite ops cash positivity.
  • Margin Volatility: Construction cyclical; subcontracting (30% costs) and material inflation risks. EBITDA margins (10-11%) below peers like L&T (~14%).
  • Auditor Transition: Resignation of T R Chadha (post clean limited review) due to Adani’s global firm preference; minor perception risk during handover to PWCA LLP.
  • Macro Sensitivity: 49% private, but 40% govt/PSU reliant; monsoons, elections could delay tenders.

Growth Prospects

  • Order Book Execution: ₹20,646 Cr book (~2.3x FY25 revenue) implies 15-20% CAGR over 2-3 years, driven by maritime/urban (56% book).
  • High-Growth Segments: Data centers (6% book, new EPC push); urban metro (e.g., Mumbai/Chennai breakthroughs). Target: Top infra EPC via Adani ecosystem.
  • Margin/Scale Upside: Efficiency gains (safety awards, ISO certs); potential 12-14% EBITDA margins with volume. International/repeat clients (51% private).
  • Outlook: FY26 revenue ~₹10,000-11,000 Cr possible (20%+ growth); PAT CAGR 25-30% if execution holds. Multi-year visibility from govt infra spend.

Key Risks

Risk Category Description Mitigation
Execution Delays/cost overruns in complex projects (tunnels, marine; e.g., Vizhinjam). Diversified book; strong track record (90+ yrs); safety focus.
Financial Input inflation (materials 33% costs); forex (intl projects). WC cycles could spike debt. Hedging; low D/E; rating upgrades aid borrowing.
Regulatory Tender delays, land acquisition, clearances (govt heavy). Adani influence; 15-state presence.
Market Infra competition (L&T, HCC); cyclical slowdown. Niche expertise (marine, foundations).
Group/External Adani scrutiny (Hindenburg echoes); auditor change transition. Clean audits; global auditor alignment.

Overall Summary: Bullish outlook with strong tailwinds from order book, Adani synergies, and infra boom outweighing manageable headwinds (WC, cyclicality). Growth prospects solid (15-25% CAGR near-term) via execution and diversification, but monitor execution risks and margins. Buy/Hold for infra exposure; target upside 20-30% from current levels if FY26 guidance met. Risks moderate; healthy BS provides buffer. (Data as of Nov 2025 filings.)
