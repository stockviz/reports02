Analysis of Cemindia Projects Limited (CEMPRO): Headwinds, Tailwinds, Growth Prospects, and Key Risks

Cemindia Projects Limited (formerly ITD Cementation India Limited; NSE: CEMPRO, BSE: 509496) is an infrastructure EPC player with ~93 years of history, recently acquired by an Adani Group entity (Renew Exim DMCC). It specializes in maritime structures, urban infra/MRTS/airports, industrial buildings, hydro/tunnels, data centers, foundations, highways/bridges, and water/wastewater. As of Sep 30, 2025, it boasts a robust order book of ₹20,646 Cr (up from ₹18,300 Cr in FY25), diversified clientele (40% govt, 9% PSU, 51% private), and operations across 15 Indian states/1 UT plus international projects (Sri Lanka, Bangladesh, UAE). Q2/H1 FY26 results show revenue growth (9%/8% YoY), EBITDA (19%/13% YoY), and PAT (49%/42% YoY), with EBITDA margins at 11.1%/10.5% and net debt/equity at 0.25x. Market cap ~₹14,500 Cr (Oct 30, 2025).

Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts)

  • Strong Order Inflows & Visibility: Secured ₹3,258 Cr in Q2 FY26 (H1 total: ₹6,189 Cr), including marquee wins like ₹1,307 Cr data center (Maharashtra), ₹930 Cr Kolkata Metro, and ₹362 Cr Vizhinjam Port breakwater. Order book offers 2-3x FY25 revenue visibility.
  • Adani Group Synergies: Acquisition enhances funding access, bidding firepower, and project pipeline (e.g., ports, airports align with Adani infra). Auditor switch to Price Waterhouse (global affiliate) signals standardization.
  • Margin Expansion & Balance Sheet Strength: EBITDA margins improved (Q2: 11.1% vs 10.3% YoY); low leverage (net debt ₹510 Cr); credit upgrades (CARE/ICRA to A+/Stable).
  • Sector Tailwinds: India’s infra boom (metro, ports, data centers); diversified portfolio reduces cyclicality; international exposure adds growth.
  • Execution Momentum: Key milestones (e.g., Mumbai/Chennai Metro tunnels, JNPT wharf) build credibility.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Working Capital Intensity: High receivables (₹1,372 Cr consolidated) and unbilled revenue (₹2,039 Cr) strain cash flows; H1 FY26 ops cash flow positive at ₹279 Cr but prior periods volatile.
  • Cyclical Sector Exposure: 49% govt/PSU reliance vulnerable to budget delays/election cycles; subcontracting (30%+ costs) exposes to labor/supply chain issues.
  • Auditor Transition: Resignation of T R Chadha & Co (post clean limited review) due to Adani’s global auditor alignment; no issues flagged, but may trigger short-term sentiment/governance scrutiny.
  • Macro Pressures: Rising input costs (materials 33% of revenue); monsoon/execution delays in marine/hydro projects.
  • Competition: Intense rivalry from L&T, HCC, etc., in EPC bids.
