Mastek Limited (NSE: MASTEK, BSE: 523704) Analysis

Mastek, a mid-cap IT services firm focused on AI-led digital engineering, cloud transformation, and enterprise apps (Oracle, Salesforce), reported solid Q2FY26 results amid a competitive IT landscape. Key metrics: Q2 revenue ₹940 Cr (+8.4% YoY), EBITDA ₹146 Cr (15.5% margin), PAT ₹97 Cr (+6% QoQ but -24% YoY). H1 revenue +10.4% YoY, 12-month backlog +13.2% YoY to ₹2,484 Cr. Below is a structured summary of headwinds, tailwinds, growth prospects, and key risks based on the provided filings (Q2 results, investor presentation, earnings call invite, AGM voting, CFO appointment, etc.).

Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts)

  • AI & Digital Momentum: 25+ new AI deals in Q2 (total ~100 active engagements); “Lead with AI” strategy driving wins (e.g., AI-driven legacy modernization, Agentforce, ADOPT AI suite). Recognized as Leader/Major Contender in ISG, Forrester, Everest reports for AI enablement, AWS data modernization, public sector services (UK/Australia).
  • Robust Order Book & Wins: 12-month backlog +13.2% YoY (+6% QoQ); key multi-year deals in UK govt (immigration, defence), US healthcare payer ($1.5B), ME health/govt, Oracle HCM, Salesforce PSS. Added 13 new clients; pipeline resilient.
  • Geographic Strength: UK/Europe (64% revenue) led growth (+ strong healthcare/govt traction); AMEA stable; US steady despite headwinds.
  • Financial Health: Cash +19% QoQ to ₹656 Cr; DSO improved to 80 days; ROE/ROCE ~16-18%. Dividend payout confirmed (₹23/share FY25).
  • Operational Discipline: Attrition ↓ to 18.5% (LTM); utilization 87.5%; new CFO (Deepak Kedia) with global P&L/IR experience to drive efficiency/AI finance.
  • Investor Engagement: Earnings call (Oct 17), analyst meet (Nov 20); high AGM approval (e.g., 100% promoter support).

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Margin & Profitability Pressure: EBITDA margin -103 bps YoY to 15.5%; PAT margin -455 bps YoY to 10.2% (H1 PAT -5% YoY). Normalized YoY base (exceptionals like impairment reversals last year); other income volatile.
  • Client Concentration & Moderation: Active clients ↓ to 314 (QoQ); top-5 clients 35% revenue (up QoQ). US stable but geo-political drag; overall QoQ revenue +2.8% (muted).
  • Employee Headcount Dip: Total headcount 4,767 (-1% QoQ); offshore 67%.
  • FX & Macro: Rupee depreciation aided rupee growth (USD +4.5% YoY), but hedges cover only 12 months; wage inflation risks.
  • XBRL/Disclosure Queries: Minor clarification on standalone “Other Income” tagging (no material impact).

Growth Prospects (High Potential)

  • AI/Digital Tailwind: 80+ AI agents, 100+ use cases, 3,600+ trained pros; partnerships (MS, AWS, NVIDIA, Oracle, Salesforce) position for GenAI boom. Expect sustained 25+ AI deals/quarter.
  • Backlog Conversion: ₹2,484 Cr 12-month backlog implies ~13% revenue visibility; total backlog +33% YoY. CEO confident of “sustainable profitable growth.”
  • Sector/Geography Expansion: Public sector/healthcare (51% revenue) strong (UK immigration, ME health); new verticals (gaming, auto marketplace). Target: Scale AI in 400+ clients across 40+ countries.
  • FY26 Outlook: H1 +10% YoY sets base for 10-15% full-year growth (analyst consensus ~12-14%); AI pipeline, efficiencies to aid margin recovery to 16%+.
  • Capital Allocation: Strong cash for M&A (recent integrations), buybacks/dividends; new CFO to optimize IR/capital.
