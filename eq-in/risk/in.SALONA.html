Salona Cotspin Limited (SALONA) Analysis

Company Overview: Salona Cotspin Ltd (CIN: L17111TZ1994PLC004797) is a Tamil Nadu-based textile yarn spinner focused on organic/certified yarns (GOTS, BCI, OEKO-TEX). Listed on NSE, it reported H1 FY26 (Apr-Sep 2025) unaudited results showing revenue decline amid high debt and capex. Equity capital ~Rs 53 Cr (5.26 Cr shares @ Rs 10 FV). FY25 annual PAT was Rs 31 Cr on Rs 662 Cr revenue.

Tailwinds (Positive Factors)

  • Certifications & Market Positioning: Strong organic/eco-certifications (GOTS, Organic 100, OEKO-TEX) position it for premium export/domestic demand in sustainable textiles. India’s textile exports (esp. organic cotton yarn) benefit from global green trends.
  • Operational Cash Flow Strength: H1 FY26 CFO at Rs 208 Cr (vs. FY25 full-year Rs 637 Cr), driven by working capital efficiencies (inventory down Rs 61 Cr YoY).
  • Sequential Recovery: Q2 FY26 revenue up 9% QoQ (Rs 171 Cr vs. Rs 157 Cr in Q1); PAT Rs 11 Cr.
  • Governance: 31st AGM (Sep 19, 2025) saw near-unanimous approvals (99.99% for most resolutions), including new Independent Directors (Gaurav Jain) and related party transactions (Shristi Cotspinn Pvt Ltd). Smooth compliance (Annual Report shared via web-link).

Headwinds (Negative Factors)

  • Revenue & Profit Pressure: H1 FY26 revenue down 8% YoY (Rs 328 Cr vs. Rs 356 Cr); PAT down 42% (Rs 26 Cr vs. Rs 45 Cr). Q2 PBT at Rs 12 Cr (down 58% YoY). High costs: Purchases (61% of revenue), finance (3% of revenue).
  • Debt Burden: Total borrowings ~Rs 2,371 Cr (53% current; debt/equity ~2x). Finance costs Rs 107 Cr in H1 (up YoY), eroding margins (PBT margin 1% vs. 1.5% prior H1).
  • Receivables Build-Up: Trade receivables up 4% QoQ to Rs 1,214 Cr (36% of revenue), signaling slower collections.

Growth Prospects

  • Capacity Expansion: CWIP up 26% to Rs 231 Cr (PPE Rs 1,051 Cr); H1 capex Rs 86 Cr. Potential for higher yarn output amid India’s textile push (PLI scheme, export targets $45 Bn by FY30).
  • Organic Focus: Tailwinds from rising global demand for sustainable yarns (India ~20% organic cotton producer). If capex yields 10-15% volume growth, revenue could rebound in H2 FY26.
  • Efficiency Gains: Inventory optimization (down 9% YoY); positive net cash flow (Rs 8 lakh increase). Dividend confirmed (Rs 1/share for FY25).
  • Outlook: Moderate growth (5-10% revenue FY26) if cotton prices stabilize and exports revive post-AGM approvals.

Key Risks

Risk Category Description Mitigation
Financial/Leverage High debt (Rs 2,371 Cr); interest coverage ~1.3x (PBIT/finance costs). Rate hikes could squeeze profits. Debt repayment in H1 (Rs 70 Cr long-term); strong CFO.
Operational Single-segment (textiles); cotton volatility (cost of materials 25% of revenue). Receivables risk (DSO ~100 days). Diversified certifications; inventory control.
Market/External Weak yarn demand (global slowdown, China competition). FY25 PAT low at Rs 31 Cr signals cyclicality. Export focus; organic premium pricing.
Related Party Transactions with Shristi Cotspinn approved (low votes but abstentions); governance watch. Shareholder approval; disclosures.
Compliance Physical shares/KYC reminders; demat push per SEBI. Proactive intimation; RTA (MUFG Intime).

Overall Summary: Salona faces near-term headwinds from revenue decline, high debt costs, and muted profits (H1 PAT margin 0.8%), but tailwinds from certifications and capex support 5-10% FY26 growth. Key watch: Debt reduction and receivables. Investment Stance: Cautious hold/buy on dips for long-term organic textile play; monitor Q3 results (Feb 2026) for capex fruition. EV/EBITDA ~4-5x (est.); target Rs 150-180 (current ~Rs 120-140 implied).
