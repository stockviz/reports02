Apollo Hospitals Enterprise Ltd (APOLLOHOSP) Analysis

Overview: Apollo Hospitals reported robust Q2/H1 FY26 results (ended Sep 30, 2025), with consolidated revenue up 13%/14% YoY, EBITDA up 15%/20% YoY, and PAT up 26%/33% YoY. Growth driven by diversified segments: Healthcare Services (HCS: 50% revenue), Apollo Health & Lifestyle Ltd (AHLL: diagnostics/retail), and Apollo HealthCo (digital/pharma). Key positives include expansions, clinical milestones (e.g., 51L+ surgeries, 27K transplants), and restructuring approvals. Stock benefits from integrated model amid India’s healthcare boom.

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)
  • Strong Financial Momentum: Q2 revenue ₹6,304 Cr (+13% YoY), EBITDA ₹941 Cr (14.9% margin, +15% YoY), PAT ₹477 Cr (+26% YoY). H1 PAT ₹910 Cr (+33% YoY). HCS margins stable at 24.6%; HealthCo PAT turnaround (₹73 Cr vs ₹19 Cr YoY).
  • Segment Resilience: | Segment | Q2 Revenue Growth | EBITDA Growth | |———|——————-|—————| | HCS | +9% (₹3,169 Cr) | +8% (₹781 Cr)| | AHLL | +17% (₹474 Cr) | +21% (₹50 Cr)| | HealthCo | +17% (₹2,661 Cr)| 112% (₹110 Cr)|
  • Operational Excellence: 69% occupancy (8,050 beds), ARPP/IP +9% (₹1.73L). Apollo 24/7 GMV +16% (₹723 Cr), 44Mn users, 9.5L DAU.
  • Strategic Wins: CCI approval for HealthCo restructuring (demerger/amalgamation for listing); full AHLL ownership (acquiring 31% IFC stake for ₹1,254 Cr); ESOP in-principle approvals.
  • Innovation/CSR: AI music therapy, robotic telesurgery; 2.8L beneficiaries via CSR.
Headwinds (Challenges)
  • Volume Pressure in HCS: IP discharges +2% YoY but occupancy down to 69% (vs 73%) due to lower medical admissions (seasonal high base Q2FY25) and Bangladesh patient drop (1% revenue hit). ALOS -6%.
  • Digital Drag: Apollo 24/7 losses narrowing (cash loss ₹386 Cr, lowest ever) but still ₹126 Cr Q2 costs (incl. ESOP); EBITDA negative pre-adjustments.
  • AHLL Headwinds: Specialty care (e.g., Spectra, Cradle) slowed (+4% Q2) due to competition; PAT loss ₹6 Cr.
  • Macro/External: Vague mention of “international trade headwinds/unfair tariffs”; Karnataka land dispute for subsidiary (under review, favorable legal opinion).
Growth Prospects
  • High-Conviction Expansion: +4,400 beds (~3,600 census) over 5 years (₹8,300 Cr capex, ₹5,800 Cr remaining); FY26-27: 2,071 beds (e.g., Pune, Kolkata acquisitions, Gachibowli greenfield).
  • Oncology Leadership: 3rd proton center (Gurugram, ₹573 Cr, +350 patients/year by FY29); oncology doubling to ₹5,000 Cr in 3-4 years via tech (robotics, genomics, CAR-T).
  • HealthCo Scale: Target ₹25,000 Cr run-rate revenue by Q4 FY27 (7% EBITDA); omni-pharma GMV +20% H1; 6,928 stores (+186 Q2).
  • AHLL Focus: 4 verticals (primary/diagnostics, birthing, ambulatory, dialysis/dental); high-teens CAGR potential.
  • Integrated Ecosystem: Pan-India (73 hospitals), tech (AI diagnostics, telehealth), consumer shift to preventive/out-of-hospital care. FY26 guidance intact; ROCE improving (metros 31%, non-metros 27%).

Execution Delays in 10+ expansions; capex intensity (HCS debt ₹21.7 Bn gross). Internal accruals; phased rollout (FY26-30).
Regulatory Scheme/NCLT approvals for HealthCo listing; CCI for AHLL stake; Karnataka land probe. CCI approved scheme; strong legal stance.

Financial Digital losses persist; debt ₹44.8 Bn (net low in HCS: -₹8.5 Bn). Improving EBITDA covers; cash ₹33 Bn+.
External Seasonality (admissions), forex (intl patients), macro tariffs/economy. Diversified revenue (50% HCS, 42% HealthCo).
Operational Cybersecurity (digital), talent retention (ESOPs mitigate). AI/tech investments; 1.2L staff.

Summary Outlook: Bullish. Tailwinds from growth/margins/expansions outweigh headwinds (mild volume softness). Prospects strong in oncology/digital (20%+ CAGR potential); risks manageable (regulatory near-term, execution medium). Trading at premium valuations justified by 14% revenue CAGR, 20% EBITDA growth trajectory, and debt discipline. Monitor Q3 occupancy, scheme progress. Recommendation: Accumulate on dips for long-term compounding.
