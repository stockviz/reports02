Analysis of EaseMyTrip (Easy Trip Planners Limited - EASEMYTRIP)

EaseMyTrip, India’s leading profitable OTA (Online Travel Agency), reported Q2/H1 FY26 results (ended Sept 30, 2025) amid aviation sector challenges but strong non-air and international growth. Key metrics: Consolidated GBR H1 FY26 at ₹4,025 Cr (flat YoY implied from prior trends), Revenue ₹232 Cr (+ve mix shift), EBITDA ₹19 Cr (margin ~8%), but PAT loss of ₹356 Cr due to ₹510 Cr exceptional provision on airline receivables. Pre-exceptional PAT positive at ₹54 Cr. Company boasts 30Mn+ users, global footprint (10 countries), and bootstrapped profitability since inception.

Tailwinds

  • Non-Air & Diversification Momentum: Hotels/holidays room nights +93% YoY (4.2 Lac Q2), Trains/Buses +16% (3.3 Lac). Revenue mix: Non-air up to 27% (H1), reducing flight reliance (61%).
  • Dubai Operations Hyper-Growth: GBR ₹362 Cr Q2 (+110% YoY), H1 ₹680 Cr (+127% YoY), driven by international travel scale-up.
  • Operational Efficiency: EBITDA margins highest among peers (~10% Q2); employee/marketing costs <2% of GBR. Cash reserves ₹2,515 Cr (FY25 end), debt-light.
  • Tech & Innovation: AI chatbots, WhatsApp bookings, ONDC integration, EMTDesk (corporate), zero-upfront hotels, carbon tracking. High repeat (94%), NPS 59%.
  • Global Recognition & Expansion: Top 10 in Forbes DGEMS 2025 (global potential). 19 subs (e.g., Brazil, Saudi, NZ), acquisitions (Spree Hospitality 2K+ keys, YoloBus, Pflege Health).
  • Market Tailwinds: India OTA market to $31B by 2030 (10.5% CAGR); digital users to 1B by 2025, middle-class boom.

Headwinds

  • Aviation Exposure & Exceptional Hit: Flights ~76% GBR but revenue down (60.9% mix); ₹510 Cr provision (GSA deposits/advances to UDAAN airline, e.g., SpiceJet-like). Recovery uncertain, impacting PAT (Q2 loss ₹360 Cr).
  • GBR Stagnation: Q2 GBR ₹1,959 Cr (flat/decline vs. prior ₹2,075 Cr implied); discounts up to 3.8% of GBR, pressuring take rates (6%).
  • Macro Pressures: Airline capacity cuts, competition (MakeMyTrip, Yatra), rising costs (employee +48% CAGR FY21-25).
  • Cash Flow Volatility: H1 OCF negative ₹175 Cr (working capital drag); investments heavy (₹1,082 Cr capex).

Growth Prospects

  • High-Margin Segments: Non-air (hotels/packages) to drive margins (target 200 Spree properties); EV buses (Easy Green Mobility + YoloBus: 2K buses by FY28, ₹200 Cr invest, 28% CAGR market).
  • International Scale: Dubai trajectory + new subs/markets (Brazil WOS, 49% Planet Education); Explore Bharat inbound tourism.
  • B2B/Corporate Push: EMTDesk, EMTMate, MICE; 1,706 agents, 25 franchise stores.
  • Funding & Capital Raise: Proposed ₹514 Cr preferential issue (56 Cr shares @ ₹9.19); supports EV/hospitality.
  • Projections: Adjusted revenue CAGR 41% FY21-25; OTA market +EV/mobility synergy positions for ₹60B India travel by 2030.

Key Risks

  • Receivables Recovery (High): ₹510 Cr (4% of assets) tied to distressed airline; prolonged delay erodes cash/EBITDA.
  • Regulatory/Scrutiny (Medium): ED search (Apr 2025, minor cash seizure); no further action, but overhang.
  • Dilution & Valuation: Preferential at ₹9.19 (potential discount to market); 56 Cr shares = ~14% dilution.
  • Execution/Competition (Medium): Acquisitions integration (19 subs, associates); OTA wars, airline volatility.
  • Forex/Currency (Low): Global ops expose to USD/AED volatility.
  • Liquidity: Negative OCF; heavy capex (₹331 Cr H1 investing outflow).

Summary

Bull Case (Tailwinds Dominate): EaseMyTrip’s non-air/Dubai pivot (100%+ YoY) + tech edge positions it for 15-20% revenue CAGR, leveraging India’s $31B OTA/digital boom. EV/hospitality diversification de-risks aviation; Forbes nod signals global scale. Pre-exceptional profitability intact; cash hoard funds growth. Target: Top-3 OTA via margins > peers.

Bear Case (Headwinds Weigh): ₹510 Cr hit masks core weakness (flat GBR, flight decline); recovery failure + dilution caps upside. ED overhang adds volatility.

Overall Outlook: Positive with Caution. Growth prospects strong (non-air/international 20-30% CAGR), but Q3 recovery on airline dues critical. Hold/Buy on dips if stock <₹10; monitor preferential voting (Dec 2025). FY26 PAT guidance: ₹800-1,000 Cr pre-exceptional.
