Analysis of Tejas Networks Ltd. (TEJASNET): Headwinds, Tailwinds, Growth Prospects, and Key Risks

Tejas Networks, an indigenous telecom equipment maker (part of Tata Group), specializes in wireless (4G/5G RAN), wireline (DWDM/OTN, FTTx), and Satcom products. FY25 saw explosive growth (revenue ~Rs 8,916 Cr standalone, driven by BSNL 4G/5G), but H1 FY26 (ended Sep 30, 2025) marked a sharp reversal with revenue at Rs 463 Cr and PAT loss of Rs 501 Cr (standalone). Order book stands at Rs 1,204 Cr (93% India). Below is a structured summary based on the provided filings (investor presentation, Q2/H1 FY26 results, PLI disclosure, project wins, etc.).

Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts)

  • Major Project Execution & Wins: BSNL 4G/5G rollout (world’s largest single-vendor network; 100K+ sites, 22M subs) driving revenue ramp-up expected in H2 FY26. Recent PowerTel (PGCIL subsidiary) 400Gbps DWDM upgrade win reinforces utility sector presence.
  • Government Support & Indigenization: Rs 85 Cr PLI tranche received (Q4 FY25; more expected); “Trusted Source” certification; Class-1 local supplier under PMI. BharatNet Phase-III and strategic sectors (railways, defense) favor domestic players.
  • Product Innovation & Partnerships: Advanced portfolio (1.2T DWDM, 64T mMIMO 5G, alien wavelengths, O-RAN). Tie-ups with NEC/Rakuten Symphony for global 5G; D2M trials with Intel/Lava. 100+ 3GPP contributions; 585+ patents.
  • Macro Tailwinds: AI-driven infra supercycle (edge AI, DCI); 5G/FWA growth (TAM ex-China: $49B in 2024 → $58B by 2029); India policy push (RDI scheme Rs 1L Cr fund).
  • Tata Backing & Scale: 2,400+ employees (70% R&D); asset-light EMS model; 16x shipment capacity scale-up in FY25.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Revenue Volatility & Delays: H1 FY26 revenue down ~89% YoY (Rs 463 Cr vs Rs 4,372 Cr); impacted by BSNL order delays, inventory/warranty provisions (Rs 145 Cr obsolescence + Rs 44 Cr warranty in Q2).
  • Profitability Pressure: Gross margins fell to 22% (FY25: 28%); EBIT loss Rs 626 Cr in H1 FY26 (FY25 profit: Rs 905 Cr). High depreciation (Rs 197 Cr), finance costs (Rs 159 Cr), ECL (Rs 34 Cr).
  • Working Capital Strain: Inventory Rs 2,383 Cr; receivables Rs 4,027 Cr (net WC Rs 4,906 Cr); borrowings up to Rs 4,156 Cr. Cash burn in ops/investments (Rs 511 Cr + Rs 398 Cr in H1).
  • Customer Concentration: 93% order book India; heavy BSNL reliance (FY25 revenue mix: Ind Govt heavy).
  • Execution Issues: Contract mfg losses, design changes led to provisions; H1 FY26 ramp-up delayed.

Growth Prospects

  • Short-Term (FY26-27): BSNL 5G NSA upgrade, MAAN backhaul completion; BharatNet-III GPON; international 4G/5G POCs (e.g., Africa, private 5G mines). Order book conversion + new wins (PowerTel, US ISPs) could drive H2 recovery. PLI inflows (~Rs 312 Cr FY25 total).
  • Medium-Term (FY27-30): 5G-Adv/6G, AI edge/middle-mile (400G/800G DWDM, DCI); Satcom-IoT/D2M; private networks (CNPN). Revenue mix diversification (Intl 20%, Pvt India 57% targeted). Global footprint (75+ countries, 500+ networks).
  • Long-Term: TAM expansion (mobile infra 12.8% CAGR; FWA 200% growth); indigenization secures India market share (incumbent in all telcos). R&D capex (~Rs 550 Cr FY23-25) positions for 6G, NTN. Revenue 9x growth FY23-25 precedent suggests multi-year upside if execution improves.

Projected Trajectory: FY26 revenue could rebound to Rs 7,000-9,000 Cr if BSNL ramps (consensus implied); EBITDA margins 10-15% post H1 normalization.

Overall Outlook: Positive with Caution. Tailwinds from BSNL ramp, policy, and AI/5G mega-trends outweigh H1 headwinds (transitory delays/provisions). Stock likely volatile near-term but positioned for 20-30% CAGR if execution holds. Monitor Q3 results for BSNL progress. (Analysis as of Nov 2025 filings; real-time market data advised.)
