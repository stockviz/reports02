Analysis of Tejas Networks Ltd. (TEJASNET): Headwinds, Tailwinds, Growth Prospects, and Key Risks

Tejas Networks, a Tata Group company and India’s leading indigenous telecom equipment maker, specializes in 4G/5G RAN, wireline (DWDM/OTN, FTTx), and SatCom products. It has executed large-scale projects like BSNL’s pan-India 4G/5G network (100K+ sites, 13K+ routers shipped). FY25 revenue surged ~9x YoY to ₹8,923 Cr, driven by BSNL, but H1 FY26 showed sharp decline (revenue ₹463 Cr, PAT loss ₹501 Cr) due to delays/provisions. Order book stands at ₹1,204 Cr (93% India). Below is a structured analysis based on the provided documents (investor presentation Nov 2025, Q2 FY26 results, announcements, press releases).

Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts)

  • Strong Project Execution & Govt Backing: BSNL 4G/5G rollout (world’s largest single-vendor RAN, ~4 PB daily traffic, 22M+ subs) positions Tejas as incumbent. Recent PowerTel (PGCIL sub) win for 400Gbps DWDM upgrade reinforces utility sector strength. PLI incentives (₹123 Cr FY24, ₹312 Cr FY25); “Trusted Source” certification; PMI Class-1 status aids indigenization push.
  • Macro & Policy Support: India-focused “Atmanirbhar Bharat” (RDI scheme ₹1L Cr fund); 5G FWA CAGR 12.8% (2025-30); AI traffic explosion (46% edge AI at 100G/200G); fixed broadband to 1.9B users by 2030. Global TAM (ex-China): Mobile infra to $58B by 2029.
  • R&D & Tech Edge: 65% workforce in R&D (2,400+ employees, 1.7x growth since FY23); 585+ patents (364 granted); SDN-ready platforms, alien wavelength tech, 1.2T/ch DWDM. Partnerships (NEC, Rakuten Symphony, Intel/Lava for D2M).
  • Financial Momentum: FY23-25 revenue CAGR ~200%+; H1 FY26 order pursuits in BharatNet-III, private 5G, international POCs.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Revenue Volatility & Delays: H1 FY26 revenue ₹463 Cr (down 89% YoY from H1 FY25 ₹4,372 Cr); impacted by BSNL order delays, ramp-up slowdown. FY25 revenue mix skewed to BSNL (India Govt 23%, Pvt 57%).
  • Profitability Pressure: Q2 FY26 standalone loss ₹307 Cr (EBITDA negative); gross margins fell to ~22% (FY25) from 28% (FY24). High provisions: Inventory obsolescence/write-down ₹145 Cr (Q2), warranty ₹44 Cr; ECL ₹34 Cr (H1).
  • Working Capital Strain: Inventory ₹2,383 Cr (stable but high); receivables ₹4,027 Cr (BSNL-heavy); NWC ₹4,906 Cr. Borrowings up to ₹4,156 Cr; cash burn ₹277 Cr (H1 ops + capex).
  • Volume Spurt Clarification: Nov 13 announcement noted no material info for price/volume movement, signaling potential speculation risks.

Overall Summary

Bull Case: Tejas is well-positioned for India’s 5G/telecom self-reliance boom (BSNL anchor, policy tailwinds), with tech leadership (R&D, patents) and Tata credibility enabling global scale. FY26 could rebound on order execution/AI infra, targeting sustained 20-30%+ CAGR post-H1 slowdown.

Bear Case: Near-term H1 FY26 losses highlight execution risks in BSNL-heavy portfolio; high WC/debt could pressure if delays persist, eroding margins amid competition.

Investment Thesis: High-growth play (rating: Accumulate/Buy on dips) with 2-3x upside potential from current levels if FY26 orders materialize (watch Q3 results). Risks skewed to execution; monitor BSNL ramps, intl diversification. Market cap ~₹15,000-20,000 Cr implied valuation reasonable at 2-3x FY26E sales.
