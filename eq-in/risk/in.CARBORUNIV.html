Carborundum Universal Limited (CARBORUNIV) - Q2 FY26 Analysis

Overview: CUMI reported modest topline growth (consolidated sales +6.4% YoY to ₹1,287 Cr in Q2; H1 +4.2% to ₹2,493 Cr) amid segment-specific challenges, particularly sanctions-hit Russian subsidiary VAW (Electrominerals). PAT declined sharply (Q2: ₹75 Cr vs. ₹116 Cr YoY; H1: ₹136 Cr vs. ₹229 Cr YoY) due to VAW losses (₹51 Cr impact in H1), RHODIUS (₹26 Cr), and lower standalone dividends. Management maintained FY26 guidance: 5.5-6.5% sales growth, segment-specific margins, ₹350 Cr capex. Balance sheet remains strong (D/E 0.06; net debt ~0 excl. VAW cash).

Headwinds (Challenges Pressuring Performance)

  • VAW Sanctions Impact: Electrominerals drag – H1 sales -21% in INR terms (local currency -31%); PAT down 61% to ₹31 Cr. Q2 sales -37% YoY in RUB; cash ₹240 Cr trapped due to US OFAC SDN listing (Jan 2025). Geopolitical uncertainty persists; no sourcing from VAW to India anymore.
  • Abrasives Weakness: H1 standalone degrowth -2.8%; margins fell to 4.1% (vs. 8.2% YoY). RHODIUS Q1 logistics disruption led to 9% H1 sales drop (EUR 30.6 Mn); expected FY26 loss €3.5-4 Mn post-PPA. Retail subdued; dealer inventories low but demand soft.
  • Ceramics Mix/Volume Issues: Standalone Q2 flat; margins weakest in 4 yrs (~20%) due to adverse mix (wear ceramics/refractories delays in steel/cement/glass projects). H2 order book strong but execution risks.
  • Macro/Competition: Subdued industrial demand, Chinese dumping in alumina (Electrominerals stable but monitored). Other expenses up marginally; forex volatility (RUB depreciation).

Tailwinds (Supportive Factors)

  • Ceramics Resilience: H1 +9.4% growth (₹601 Cr); engineered ceramics/metallized cylinders +20%. Australia subsidiary strong; H2 loaded with projects.
  • Sequential Recoveries: Abrasives Q2 +15% QoQ (standalone +7.5%, RHODIUS +32% QoQ post-logistics fix). Electrominerals standalone +6.5% H1 (export-led).
  • Subsidiary Momentum: AWUKO H1 +5.3% (Q2 +31% YoY); FY26 sales +20% expected. Foskor sales + growth but loss-making.
  • Financial Strength: Debt-free standalone; consolidated cash ₹215 Cr (ex-VAW); capex H1 ₹162 Cr on track for new growth areas. Dividend income boosted Q1 PAT.

Growth Prospects (Medium-Term Outlook)

  • FY26 Maintained: Ceramics 16-18% growth, Electrominerals 1-2%, Abrasives 4-5%; overall PBIT margins 8.2-8.5%. H2 stronger (Abrasives retail pickup, Ceramics orders).
  • Long-Term (to FY30): 2x revenue guided previously via LTS. Key drivers: | Segment | Opportunities | |———|—————| | Ceramics | Semiconductor fab equipment (FY27 contrib.), aerospace/defense (vehicle/body protection; DRDO/CGCRI collab.; certifications secured). | | Abrasives | NPD, go-to-market sharpening, new geos; AWUKO/RHODIUS scaling. | | Electrominerals | Value-added alumina (treated grits, thermal spray); zirconia/zirmul expansion. |
  • Capex Focus: Majority (~₹350 Cr FY26) on new lines (semicon, HP SiC pilot, thin wheel relocation). H1 spend highest ever; returns based on anchor customers.

Investment Summary: Near-term muted (VAW headwind caps upside; H1 PAT -40% YoY), but H2 recovery + structural tailwinds in Ceramics/new products signal mid-teens growth potential. Strong balance sheet supports capex; monitor VAW geopolitics. Attractive for long-term industrial/tech ceramics play; FY26 beat unlikely without sanctions relief. Rating Outlook: Hold/Accumulate on dips (target multiples: 25-30x FY27 EPS, factoring 10-12% CAGR).
