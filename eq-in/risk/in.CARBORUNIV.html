Analysis of Carborundum Universal Limited (CARBORUNIV) - Q2 FY26 (Sep 2025)

Carborundum Universal Limited (CUMI), part of the Murugappa Group, is a leading player in Abrasives (~44% of H1 sales), Ceramics (~24%), and Electrominerals (~32%). Q2/H1 FY26 results show modest topline growth amid profitability pressures, primarily from its Russian subsidiary VAW (Volzhsky Abrasive Works). Consolidated H1 sales grew 4.2% YoY to ₹2,493 Cr, but PAT fell ~41% YoY to ₹136 Cr (ex-NCI). Standalone H1 sales +3% YoY to ₹1,410 Cr, PAT +16% YoY to ₹209 Cr (boosted by one-off dividend). Balance sheet remains robust (net debt-free, D/E 0.06, capex on track at ₹162 Cr/₹350 Cr FY target). Management maintains FY26 guidance: 5.5-6.5% sales growth, 8.2-8.5% PBIT margin.

Headwinds (Challenges Impacting Performance)
  • Geopolitical Sanctions on VAW (Electrominerals): Core issue; H1 sales down 21% YoY in INR terms (volumes -25%), PAT down 61% to ₹31 Cr. US OFAC SDN listing (Jan 2025) blocks USD transactions/US assets; ₹24 Cr cash trapped. No interco sourcing from VAW anymore; full-year volume drop guidance maintained at 25%. Dragged consolidated PBIT by ~₹51 Cr in H1.
  • Subsidiary Weakness: | Subsidiary | H1 FY26 Impact | |————|—————| | RHODIUS (Abrasives, Germany) | Sales -9% YoY (EUR 30.6 Mn); Q1 logistics disruption; FY loss guidance EUR 3.5-4 Mn (post-PPA). | | AWUKO (Abrasives, Germany) | Sales +5% YoY but flat losses; scaling to EUR 12-14 Mn FY target. | | Foskor Zirconia (Electrominerals, SA) | Sales +16% but losses widened to ZAR 42 Mn. |
  • Standalone Softness: Abrasives -2.8% YoY H1 (retail subdued, inventories low); Ceramics flat Q2 (wear/refractories delays in steel/cement/glass; product mix hit margins to ~20%, lowest in 4 yrs).
  • Margin Compression: Consolidated H1 PBIT ₹192 Cr (-37% YoY); Abrasives margins 4.1% (vs 8.2%), Ceramics/EMD stable but topline-dependent fixed costs hurt.
  • Macro: Chinese competition in alumina; global demand softness; forex volatility (RUB depreciation cushioned INR sales drop at VAW).
Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)
  • Sequential Recovery: Q2 sales +6.6% QoQ; Abrasives +15% QoQ (standalone +7.5%, RHODIUS +32% post-logistics fix).
  • Ceramics Strength: H1 +9.4% YoY consolidated (+4.9% standalone); Metallized Cylinders/Engineered Ceramics +20% (high-margin); Australian sub key driver. H2 order book strong.
  • Standalone Electrominerals Resilience: H1 +6.5% YoY (export-led, volumes/pricing); Q2 stable QoQ.
  • Balance Sheet & Capex: Cash ₹215 Cr (ex-VAW); H1 capex ₹162 Cr (semicon, aerospace/defence, HP SiC, thin wheel relocation). No standalone debt.
  • Other Positives: Festival destocking in abrasives retail aids H2 pickup; “Others” (PLUSS, SEDCO, IT) turned PBIT positive after 14-qtr losses.
Growth Prospects
  • FY26 Guidance (Maintained): | Segment | Sales Growth | PBIT Margin | |———|————–|————-| | Consolidated | 5.5-6.5% | 8.2-8.5% | | Ceramics | 16-18% | 23.5-23.7% | | Electrominerals | 1-2% | 4.5-5.5% | | Abrasives | 4-5% | 6-6.5% |
  • Long-Term (LTS 2030): Aim to double revenue by FY30 via:
    • New Products/Markets: Semiconductor ceramics (fab equipment, starts FY27), aerospace/defence (vehicle/body protection; DRDO/CGCRI tie-ups, certifications), HP SiC pilot (FY28+).
    • Abrasives: Retail/industrial pickup (auto OEMs), NPD, go-to-market sharpening, geopol-specific expansion.
    • Electrominerals: Alumina value-add (treated grits, thermal spray); zirconia scaling.

  • Capex Trajectory: Majority to new areas; strong anchor customers.
  • M&A/Subs: RHODIUS/AWUKO stabilization; PLUSS scaling.

Overall Outlook: Steady execution amid VAW overhang; H2 weighted for recovery (abrasives destocking, ceramics projects). Long-term bullish on diversification into high-tech (semicon/defence), positioning CUMI for 2x growth by FY30. Stock likely supported by debt-free status and capex pipeline, but VAW resolution key for re-rating. FY26 likely in-line with guidance; monitor geopolitics closely.
