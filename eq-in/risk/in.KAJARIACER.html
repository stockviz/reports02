Kajaria Ceramics Limited (KAJARIA CER) - Analysis (Q2 FY26)

Headwinds (Challenges impacting near-term performance): - Weak Demand Environment: Tiles/building materials demand sluggish for 3+ years (soft market, heavy Q2 rains); volumes flat YoY (Q2: +1%, H1: +1% MSM). Industry-wide issue (except cement/steel). - Transition Disruptions: Sales unification (from 3 verticals to 1) caused short-term volume loss/destocking; plywood closure (discontinued ops, small Q2 loss: ₹0.57cr). - Revenue Pressure: Consolidated revenue flat YoY (Q2: +1%, H1: 0%); tile segment flattish (₹1,051cr Q2).

Tailwinds (Positive drivers supporting resilience): - Margin Expansion: EBITDA margin at multi-quarter high (Q2: 17.94%, +447bps YoY/+122bps QoQ) via cost optimization (₹30-35cr annual savings on packing, procurement, 250 headcount cut); sustainable vs. COVID peaks. - Diversification Gains: Bathware +14% YoY (₹102cr Q2), Adhesives +78% (₹32cr); healthy PAT growth (Q2: +58% YoY to ₹134cr). - Financial Strength: ROE ~18-22%, ROCE ~22%, WC days 56 (down 2), net cash position (net debt -₹593cr), strong FCF; dividend ₹8/share (record date Oct 24). - Dealer/OpEx Feedback: Unification positive (dealers happier, fewer visits); consultant aiding dealer rationalization/exclusives (450 showrooms).

Growth Prospects (Medium-term opportunities): - Volume Recovery: H2 FY26 growth expected (market uptick post-Diwali, unification benefits, architect/project teams); target above-industry growth via 1,850 dealers + white spaces. - Strategic Reset (Kajaria 2.0): Led by younger leadership (Chetan/Rishi elevated); pillars: >industry growth, superior margins (14-18% orbit), high FCF. Capacity 87.8 MSM (focus 100% util., 25% outsourcing flexibility). - Segments: Projects 30% mix (push govt/private), Bathware/Adhesives scaling; industry exports ~₹18k cr FY26 (+10% H1). - Capex Discipline: No new plants; run existing optimally.

Key Risks (Potential downsides to monitor): - Demand Dependency: Prolonged slowdown (realty/infra); inability to predict/forecast (mgmt: “demand has to come”). - Execution/Transition: Quantifying unification volume loss hard; new teams/consultant unproven. - Competition/Imports: Morbi unbranded (20% price gap); outsourcing reliance if surge. - Subsidiary/JV: Nepal (small, inventory overhang); discontinued plywood losses. - External: Input volatility (power/fuel down Q2 due to low prod.); ad spend ramp-up may dent margins short-term. - Governance: Promoter re-appointments (postal ballot Nov-Dec 2025) at high remuneration (up to 5% net profit each).

Overall Summary: Resilient quarter with flat revenue offset by exceptional margins/ PAT growth (operational leverage shines). Transformation (unification, cost cuts, leadership refresh) positions for H2 rebound, but demand recovery critical. Strong balance sheet (low debt, FCF) de-risks; outlook positive if volumes inflect (target: industry+ growth, 17-20% margins sustainable). Valuation supportive at current levels.
