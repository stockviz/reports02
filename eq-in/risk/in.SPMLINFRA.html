SPML Infra Limited (SPMLINFRA) Analysis

SPML Infra is an established EPC player in water infrastructure (core ~90% revenue), power T&D/substations, with emerging focus on BESS. FY26 H1 showed stable execution amid seasonal headwinds, with improving margins from new orders. Strong order pipeline, debt resolution, and BESS entry position it for growth, but legacy issues and execution risks persist.

Tailwinds (Positive Factors)

  • Robust Order Book & Pipeline: H1 FY26 wins of INR 3,772 Cr (Jharkhand, MP, Rajasthan, TN); existing book INR 1,600 Cr; L1 for INR 1,125 Cr. Targeting INR 5,000 Cr new orders FY26 from INR 25,000 Cr tenders (water/power/BESS). Selective bidding on funded, high-margin (>10%) projects.
  • Financial Strengthening: Debt down to INR 400 Cr (backed by INR 647 Cr arbitration awards); bank facilities up to INR 505 Cr (incl. BG); ICRA “Stable” rating; promoter infusions (warrants ~INR 150 Cr). Net worth up to INR 852 Cr; liquidity ~INR 463 Cr post-infusions.
  • Margin Expansion: H1 EBITDA 9.8%, PAT 7.6% (Q2: 10%/7.7%); legacy low-margin orders phasing out, new ones >10%. BESS to add 4-5% margins post-manufacturing.
  • ** Govt. Policy Support**: Water (INR 17L Cr: Jal Jeevan, AMRUT); BESS (INR 5L Cr by 2030, 236 GWh market); power substations tenders surging.
  • BESS Momentum: Phase 1 (2.5 GWh) by Q1 FY27; Energy Vault tie-up; EPC-ready now; 2 containers/day capacity post-Apr’26; thermal RE tenders expanding scope.
  • Arbitration Upside: INR 647 Cr awards imminent; INR 4,600 Cr claims (~INR 1,500 Cr expected awards).

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Seasonal/Legacy Drag: H1 slower due to monsoons/elections; legacy orders (low margins 5-7%) still ~INR 1,600 Cr, diluting blends until Q3/Q4 ramp-up.
  • Revenue Moderation: H1 standalone revenue INR 363 Cr (flat YoY); Q2 +2% YoY. Execution ramp needs 3-6 months post-design approvals.
  • High Debtors: Trade receivables ~INR 62K Cr consolidated; working capital strain despite supplier networks.
  • Debt Restructuring Ties: NARCL holding maintenance (12.5%) via preferential issues; ongoing arbitration dependencies.

Growth Prospects

  • Short-Term (FY26): Revenue INR 850-1,000 Cr+ (old + new orders at ~8-9%/quarter); EBITDA/PAT margins 9-10%+; order book closure INR 5,000 Cr+; BESS EPC wins from INR 5,000 Cr tenders.
  • Medium-Term (FY27+): Water/power 50:50 mix by 2030; BESS revenue INR 2,500-5,000 Cr potential at 5 GWh capacity; arbitration cash inflows for deleveraging; total orders >INR 5,000 Cr/year.
  • Long-Term: BESS market (USD 57 Bn by 2031); water mega-schemes; O&M annuities (10-12 yrs); aim multi-GWh deployments.

Key Risks

Risk Category Description Mitigation
Execution Delays in design approvals, monsoons, elections; cost overruns in EPC/infra. Selective funded projects; strong supplier network; 40+ yrs experience.
Arbitration/Legal Delays in INR 647 Cr awards/INR 4,600 Cr claims (41% historical success); govt. challenges. Strict timelines (34-37 months); 75% deposit for appeals; Vivad Se Vishwas settlements.
Financial WC pressure (high debtors INR 62K Cr); interest costs if arbitration slips. Escrow mechanisms; liquidity infusions; debt conversion to equity.
Market/Competition Tender delays; BESS new entrants/imports; China cell dependency (28-30% BOM). Pre-qualifications; Make-in-India localization; Energy Vault IP.
Regulatory Policy shifts in RE mandates/ESO; NARCL compliance. Fully funded central/state projects; stable ratings.
Operational BESS ramp-up risks (Q1 FY27 commissioning); JV dependencies. On-track progress; end-to-end turnkey model.

Overall Summary: SPML is at an inflection point with tailwinds from orders, liquidity, and BESS outweighing legacy headwinds. Growth to INR 5,000 Cr+ orders/FY26 feasible, with margins/EBITDA expanding. Risks are manageable via arbitration/debt resolution, but monitor execution and tenders. Positive outlook for 20-30% revenue CAGR if BESS scales. Valuation upside on infra multiples.
