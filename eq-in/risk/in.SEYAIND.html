🔍 Overview

Seya Industries Ltd is a specialty chemical intermediates manufacturer currently under insolvency proceedings initiated under Section 7 of the IBC. The company is being managed by the Interim Resolution Professional (IRP), Bhavesh Mansukhbhai Rathod, registered with IBBI. Despite ongoing financial stress and litigation, the company continues operations and disclosure compliance under SEBI listing norms, particularly due to the existence of a single key business segment.

The latest disclosures include unaudited standalone financial results and AGM proceedings for FY2025 (full year) and H1/FY2026 (quarterly updates), reviewed externally but not audited.

✅ Tailwinds / Positive Indicators

    • Production assets (Plant & Equipment worth ₹64,215 Lakh) are intact and likely generating cash through other means or contract operations.
  2. Significant Other Income (Rs 903 Lakh)
    • This income has nearly offset operating losses.
    • Source appears non-operational — possibly interest rebates, asset rentals, insurance proceeds, or legal settlements.
    • Though not sustainable long-term, it provides temporary liquidity.
  3. Positive Investing Cash Inflow (₹903 Lakh)
    • Reflects realizations from non-core assets or advances.
    • Could signal strategic divestment or working capital release.
  4. Active Engagement with Lenders & Court Litigation
    • Company is contesting lender classification of facilities as NPAs, which could lead to eventual restructuring in favor of management.
  5. Segment Clarity
    • Single reportable segment (“Specialty Chemical Intermediates”) implies clear core operations — valuable for potential buyers in resolution.
  6. Asset Base is Substantial

⚠️ Headwinds / Red Flags
  1. Zero Revenue from Core Business
    • Most alarming sign: No revenue from operations for Q2 and H1 FY26.
    • Business model disrupted — production likely stalled or commercial demand missing.
    • Long-term survival questionable without revival in operating income.
  2. Net Loss of ₹155 Lakh (H1 FY26)
    • Driven by massive depreciation & amortization (₹1,023.52 Lakh) and other expenses (₹59.86 Lakh).
    • Even after large non-operating income, still in loss.
    • Depreciation cost alone exceeds entire income — indicative of high fixed cost base.
  3. Massive Depreciation Charges (₹1,023 Lakh in 6 months)
    • Indicates highly capital-intensive plant.
    • Suggests aging or underutilized infrastructure — “idle capacity charge.”
  4. Negative Operating Cash Flow (₹888.66 Lakh used)
    • Core operations consume cash, despite zero revenue.
    • This is unsustainable and suggests losses are being funded via asset sales or cash reserves.
  5. Corporate Insolvency Resolution Process (CIRP) Underway Since 2023
    • Initiated on Nov 2, 2023 — over two years ago.
    • Committee of Creditors (CoC) constitution stayed by NCLAT — no decision on revival or liquidation.
    • Prolonged uncertainty reduces prospects of timely resolution.
  6. Disputed NPAs & Interest Non-Provisioning
    • Lenders classified exposure as NPA; company disputes legitimacy.
    • No interest accrual on loans worth ₹730–1,439 Lakh (operating) and ₹3,102–6,159 Lakh (project assets).
    • This is aggressive accounting — if challenges fail, huge retroactive interest burden may arise.
  7. High Leverage
    • Long-term borrowings: ₹69,621 Lakh (~₹6,962 Crore).
    • Financial liabilities dominate the liability side.
    • Equity is just ₹2,657 Lakh — meaning debt-to-equity is astronomically high (~26x).
  8. Management by IRP - Not Strategic Leadership
    • IRP duty is to protect asset value and facilitate resolution — not drive growth.
    • No incentive to invest or expand.
🔮 Growth Prospects

⚫ Realistic Outlook: Preservation of Assets Value / Resolution-Driven Exit

  • No organic growth expected in near term given:
    • Zero revenue from operations.
    • Management focus is compliance and resolution, not commercial expansion.
  • Potential upside lies in asset monetization or resolution plan approval:
