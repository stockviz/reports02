Analysis of Genus Power Infrastructures Ltd. (GENUSPOWER)

Company Overview: Genus Power is a leading Indian player in smart metering solutions (electricity, gas, water), AMISP services, and related software (HES, MDMS). It benefits from India’s RDSS-driven smart meter rollout (~250Mn+ meters targeted). Q2FY26 (ended Sep 2025) showed explosive growth: standalone revenue +136% YoY to ₹1,149 Cr, EBITDA margin 21.3% (+456 bps), PAT +162% to ₹148 Cr. Order book: ₹28,758 Cr (net of taxes; ~8-10 yr concessions via SPVs/GIC JV). Capacity: 20Mn smart meters p.a. by FY26 end.

Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts)

  • Robust Order Book & Execution: ₹28,758 Cr visibility (incl. GIC SPVs); balanced EPC (upfront) + O&M (annuity). H1FY26 revenue +132% YoY to ₹2,091 Cr on project ramp-up.
  • Margin Expansion: EBITDA margins scaled to 21.2% H1FY26 (+517 bps YoY) via operating leverage, in-house integration (meters + software), cost discipline.
  • Policy/Govt Support: RDSS/AMRUT push; exclusive AMISP with end-to-end capabilities (meters, comms, HES/MDMS).
  • Strategic Partnerships/Capacity: GIC JV (USD 2Bn platform; Genus as OEM), DFC USD 49.5Mn investment. 825K sq ft facilities; hiring +4K (total 15.5K workforce).
  • Diversification: Gas/water meters, exports (Africa/APAC/ME), software sales, analytics/smart grid.
  • Talent/ESOP: New ESOP Trust (Oct 2025); 30% women workforce, CSR focus.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Working Capital Strain: Ops cash burn ₹158 Cr H1FY26; inventories +69% to ₹1,437 Cr, receivables ₹1,570 Cr. Cycle extension amid scale-up.
  • Debt Burden: Borrowings +37% to ₹1,744 Cr; finance costs +56% YoY. Net debt rise despite strong profits.
  • Institutional Pushback: AGM (Sep 2025): 17.5% institutions voted against Ishwar Chand Agarwal’s re-appointment; 25% against exec commission.
  • Execution Intensity: Ramp-up risks in multi-state AMISP (TOTEX model; SPV dependencies).

Growth Prospects

  • Core Metering: 18-20Mn p.a. capacity supports 10-15% India market share; O&M annuity post-EPC stabilizes FY27+ revenues.
  • Adjacent/Exports: Gas/water (15-41% TAM growth), software/analytics (USD 1.5Bn global by 2025), smart grid/AI predictive maintenance. Exports via urbanization in EMs.
  • Financial Trajectory: Target 18%+ EBITDA; FY26 guidance strong (H1 ahead). ROE potential 30%+ on order conversion.
  • Multi-Year Visibility: Order book implies 3-5x revenue growth potential; GIC SPVs add scale without full capex.

Key Risks

Risk Category Description Mitigants
Regulatory/Legal ED search (Dec 2024; no further notice, mgmt confident). Policy shifts in RDSS/AMISP. Demerger (Strategic Investments hived off Apr 2025). Compliance focus; single-segment metering now.
Execution/Financial Project delays, WC cycles, forex/raw mat volatility. High debt (interest coverage ~5x). In-house integration; GIC/DFC funding; improving cash conversion.
Market/Competition AMISP tenders competitive; 70%+ reliance on discoms/SPVs. Tech edge (DLMS-certified, CMMI L3); diversified products/partners.
Governance Family promoters (Agarwals); AGM dissent on re-appointments/commission. Independent directors (e.g., Subhash Garg); ESOP alignment.
Macro Slow discom payments, election cycles delaying tenders. Global slowdown hits exports. 28K Cr book buffers; TOTEX reduces upfront risk.

Overall Outlook: Strong Buy/Bullish. Tailwinds dominate with multi-year growth from orders/policy. FY26 could deliver 2-3x revenue (₹5,000-7,000 Cr est.), 20%+ margins. Risks manageable but monitor ED/debt. Valuation premium justified on execution. (Data as of Nov 2025 filings.)
