Analysis of Thangamayil Jewellery Limited (THANGAMAYL)

Thangamayil Jewellery Limited reported stellar Q2/H1 FY26 results (ended Sep 30, 2025), with H1 revenue at ₹3,260 Cr (+36% YoY), PAT at ₹104 Cr (+167% YoY), and a record October 2025 monthly revenue of ₹1,032 Cr (+178% YoY). The company expanded to 66 outlets, focusing on Chennai metro, with strong volume growth (gold +77% in Oct) and hedging (95%). Below is a structured summary of headwinds, tailwinds, growth prospects, and key risks, derived directly from the filings.

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

Factor Details Impact
Robust Demand & Revenue Surge H1 sales ₹3,260 Cr (+36% YoY); Q2 ₹1,705 Cr (+45% YoY); Oct ₹1,032 Cr (first >₹1,000 Cr, +178% YoY). Gold volume stable/maintained despite price hikes. Fuels profitability; gross margins up to 11.17% (+381 bps YoY).
Expansion Success 9 new outlets in H1 (total 66); Chennai metro Phase 1 complete (₹660 Cr capex); new stores performing “satisfactorily” and contributing ~20% future revenue. Urban revenue share up to 40% (from 29%). Scales network; same-store sales +10.31%.
Operational Efficiency Hedging at 95% (gold full, silver 35%); customer advances +76% (₹1,129 Cr); non-gold sales +42% YoY (8.44% of retail). EBITDA ₹193 Cr (+130% YoY). Protects margins; interest cover 6.17x (up 44%).
Financial Strength Net worth ₹1,167 Cr; ROE 18.37%; BVPS ₹375.55 (+100% YoY). Right issue proceeds fully utilized. Supports self-funded growth.

Headwinds (Challenges)

Factor Details Impact
Expense Inflation Total expenses +30% YoY to ₹3,125 Cr; employee/advertising costs up (₹216 Cr vs ₹121 Cr YoY) due to 9 new stores/staff. Expenses as % of sales 6.65% (+162 bps). Pressures EBITDA margins (6.14% vs 3.64% YoY but QoQ improving).
Working Capital Strain Inventory +32% to ₹2,617 Cr; CFO negative at -₹109 Cr (vs -₹20 Cr YoY) from inventory build-up (₹638 Cr increase). Turnover slowed to 2.84x (-22% YoY). Ties up cash; cash equivalents down to ₹11 Cr.
Margin Volatility Q2 not fully comparable YoY due to prior customs duty hit; per sq ft sales down to ₹4.82 lakh (-5%). Potential dilution if volumes lag.

Growth Prospects

  • Near-Term (FY26): Phase 2 Chennai expansion (10 outlets in 15 months, ₹700 Cr capex via internal accruals + advances + moderate debt). Festive Oct momentum (gold volume +77% YoY) to sustain H2; non-gold/diamond volumes +16-20% YoY. Expected net debt/EBITDA ~2x by FY28 post-expansion.
  • Medium-Term (FY27-28): Full ramp-up of 76+ outlets could drive 20%+ revenue from new stores; urban shift boosts non-gold mix (8%+ of sales). Volume offtake stable (H1 gold 3,000 kg, -1% YoY but Oct +77%); invoices +20% YoY signals customer base growth.
  • Overall Outlook: 30-40% revenue CAGR potential if gold prices stabilize; EPS ₹33.53 (diluted, +135% YoY); ROCE 13.27% (+32%).

Key Risks

Risk Details Mitigation/Status
Gold Price Volatility Escalating prices (52% YoY rise) may curb volumes; “steep increases could adversely impact offtake.” Fluctuations make FY26 trend prediction hard. 95% hedging; focus on realization over volume.

Competitive/Operational Slowing SSS (10% vs 15% YoY); rural-urban shift exposes to metro competition. Strong brand; 316k invoices (+20% YoY), avg ticket ₹91k (+13%).

Overall Summary: THANGAMAYL is in a strong growth phase with tailwinds from expansion and festive demand outweighing headwinds like capex-driven expenses and inventory buildup. Growth prospects are bright (20%+ revenue from new stores), but gold price volatility remains the top risk—monitor for volume sustainability. At current metrics (ROE 18%, debt moderate), the stock appears resilient for FY26 upside, assuming no sharp price shocks. Positive limited review by auditors adds credibility.
