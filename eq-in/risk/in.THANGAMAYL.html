Analysis of Thangamayil Jewellery Limited (THANGAMAYL) - H1 FY25-26 & October 2025

Thangamayil Jewellery Limited reported robust H1 FY25-26 results with record revenue of ₹3,260 Cr (+36% YoY), PAT of ₹104 Cr (+167% YoY), and improved margins (gross margin 11.17% vs. 7.36% YoY). Q2 standalone showed ₹1,705 Cr revenue (+45% YoY) and PAT ₹59 Cr. October 2025 hit an all-time high ₹1,032 Cr revenue (+178% YoY) with gold volume +77% YoY. Expansion (66 outlets, Chennai Metro Phase 1 complete) drove growth, though leverage and inventory build-up are concerns. Below is a structured analysis.

Tailwinds

  • Strong Execution & Demand Momentum: Record festive sales (Oct ₹1,032 Cr), H1 revenue growth led by retail (+36% YoY), with gold jewelry +35% and non-gold +42%. Volumes stable/maintained (gold ~3,000 kg H1), urban revenue share up to 40% from 29%.
  • Margin Expansion: Gross margins surged 381 bps YoY to 11.17% due to better realizations; EBITDA margins +250 bps to 6.14%. Prudent hedging (95% gold, 35% silver) insulated profits from price volatility.
  • Operational Efficiency: Same-store sales +10.31% YoY; customer advances +76% to ₹11.29 lakh base; invoices +20% YoY, average ticket size +13% to ₹90,961.
  • Expansion Success: 9 new outlets added in H1 (total 66), Chennai Metro Phase 1 (₹660 Cr capex) performing as expected (target 20% FY revenue contribution). Non-gold mix up to 8.44% of retail sales.

Headwinds

  • Cost Pressures: Expenses rose to 6.65% of sales (+162 bps YoY) due to staff additions, ads, and new store fixed costs (₹216 Cr H1 vs. ₹121 Cr YoY). Q2 spend up but showing QoQ moderation.
  • Inventory & Working Capital Strain: Inventory +32% to ₹2,617 Cr; turnover slowed to 2.84x (-22% YoY) amid expansion and gold price rise (+52% YoY). Negative operating cash flow (-₹109 Cr) from inventory build-up.
  • Leverage Increase: Current borrowings +48% to ₹1,005 Cr; total debt/equity implied rise (TOL/TNW 0.81x vs. 1.34x YoY). Average borrowing cost up to 6.08% due to GML rates.
  • Muted SSS & Volume Dip: SSS slowed to 10.31% (vs. 15.19% YoY) due to high gold prices; H1 gold volume -1% YoY.

Growth Prospects

  • Aggressive Expansion: Phase 2 Chennai Metro (10 outlets, 15 months, ₹700 Cr capex) funded by internal accruals, advances, and moderate debt; post-completion, net debt ~2x FY27-28 EBITDA. Targets 20%+ revenue from new outlets; total outlets could hit 76+ by FY26-end.
  • Festive & Volume Tail: Oct volume +77% YoY signals sustained demand; first 7M FY26 volume +8.6% YoY. Non-gold/diamonds growing faster (silver +16%, diamonds +20% H1 YoY).
  • Diversification & Efficiency: Rising urban/non-gold mix (8.4% sales), Digi Gold schemes boosting advances. ROE +18% annualized; per sq.ft sales ₹4.82 lakh (stable). Full-year revenue could exceed ₹7,000 Cr+ if trends hold.
  • Outlook: Management cautious on remaining 5M due to price fluctuations but endorses Chennai growth. Potential 30-40% revenue CAGR medium-term from 66+ stores.

Key Risks

  • Commodity Price Volatility: Steep gold/silver rises (52% YoY gold) could crimp volumes/affordability; unhedged silver (65%) exposed. Past customs duty cuts hit inventory (₹15 Cr Q2 FY25).
  • Debt & Liquidity: High short-term borrowings (₹1,005 Cr); cash equivalents down to ₹11 Cr from ₹89 Cr. Negative CFO persistent if inventory grows.
  • Execution/Competition: New store ramp-up risks (e.g., slower SSS); jewelry sector competitive (Tanishq, Kalyan). Expenses may linger if revenue lags.
  • Macro/Regulatory: Discretionary spend sensitive to economy/inflation; pending tax appeals (₹14 Cr + ₹? for AY22-23). Rural slowdown if prices persist.
  • Other: Gold loan fluctuations (part of WC); forex/hedging gaps if prices spike further.

Summary: THANGAMAYL is in a strong growth phase with tailwinds from expansion, festive demand, and margin gains outweighing headwinds like costs and leverage. FY26 prospects bright (30%+ revenue growth likely), but risks from gold prices and debt need monitoring. Positive for investors; target multiples could expand on 18% ROE and 2x debt/EBITDA trajectory. Stock reaction likely positive post-results.
