Analysis of Sanghi Industries Limited (SANGHIIND)

Sanghi Industries Limited (Sanghi), a cement manufacturer with ~6.1 MTPA capacity in Telangana, is a subsidiary of Ambuja Cements Limited (part of Adani Cement). Q2 FY26 (ended Sep 2025) showed subdued performance due to monsoons/floods, but it benefits from Adani Group synergies. Standalone results reflect losses amid ramp-up, while consolidated Adani Cement metrics are robust. A shareholder-approved merger scheme with Ambuja (pending NCLT) signals structural simplification. Below is a summary of headwinds, tailwinds, growth prospects, and key risks.

Headwinds (Challenges Constraining Near-Term Performance)

  • Low Capacity Utilization & Volumes: Q2 FY26 utilization at 36% (vs. 23% YoY), sales 0.6 MnT (up 60% YoY but flat QoQ). Heavy monsoons/floods disrupted operations; maintenance on Kiln Line II and power transmission added downtime/costs (Rs 38 Cr other expenses).
  • Persistent Losses: Standalone Q2 loss Rs 117 Cr (H1 Rs 192 Cr); high finance costs (Rs 53 Cr), depreciation (Rs 93 Cr), and power/fuel (Rs 119 Cr). EBITDA Rs 25 Cr (up 711% YoY on low base) but PMT EBITDA Rs 444/ton.
  • Cost Pressures: Freight up YoY due to shift from ex-plant MSA sales (to Ambuja/ACC) to FOR basis with other Adani entities. Other expenses inflated by maintenance.
  • Weather/Seasonal Factors: Subdued Q2; festive slowdown lingers.

Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts Supporting Recovery)

  • Adani Group Integration: Full rebranding to Adani Cement brands (positive dealer response). Access to group synergies (logistics, power, raw materials). Debt-free parent (Crisil AAA); Sanghi issued Rs 2,200 Cr RPS to Ambuja for debt repayment.
  • EBITDA Momentum: YoY surge (711%) despite low volumes; PMT EBITDA up 408%. Q1 similar strength.
  • Merger Progress: 99.85% shareholder approval (Nov 20, 2025) for Scheme of Arrangement with Ambuja (12:100 swap ratio). NCLT meeting outcome strengthens path to completion (expected FY26 end).
  • Sustainability Edge: First Indian cement firm to adopt TNFD (nature disclosures from FY26); aligns with Adani’s green push (60% green power by FY28).

Growth Prospects (Medium-Term Opportunities)

  • Utilization Ramp-Up: 70-75% targeted for H2 FY26 post-monsoon/festives. Clinker/CLC sales growth (88% YoY).
  • Adani Cement Expansion: Sanghi contributes to group’s 107 → 155 MTPA by FY28 (debottlenecking at $48/MT capex). Cost leadership to Rs 3,650 PMT (vs. current ~Rs 4,000); premium products, 13 blenders, logistics (5% sea share).
  • Merger Value Unlock: Consolidation simplifies structure; Adani Cement’s 20% volume growth, Rs 1,060 PMT EBITDA (Q2 FY26). Sanghi’s assets (e.g., power plant rehab) enhance group efficiency.
  • Demand Tailwinds: Infra/housing boom (7-8% FY26 cement growth); GST reforms, PMAY-2.0. Adani’s CINOC (AI ops), 7 vessels for logistics.
  • Outlook: H1 FY26 sales 1.3 MnT; full-year profitability via leverage, synergies.

Key Risks (Potential Downside Factors)

Risk Category Description Mitigation
Operational Weather disruptions, maintenance delays; low per capita cement use (300 kg vs. global 540 kg) limits near-term volumes. Group infra (e.g., transmission rehab), 70-75% utilization target.
Financial Ongoing losses (H1 FY26 Rs 192 Cr); Rs 2,485 Cr debt (mostly RPS to Ambuja); forex/unrealized gains volatility. Merger cash flows; parent debt-free status.
Regulatory/Litigation Electricity duty dispute (Rs 43.9 Cr provision + Rs 180 Cr contingent; partial indemnity from ex-promoters). Merger NCLT approval pending. Strong legal case (Guj HC interim relief); Rs 40 Cr indemnity received.
Market/Competition Cement oversupply, pricing pressure; 55% top-5 share by FY26. Dependence on Adani sales (MSA/FOR shifts). Premiumization, Adani brands; cost leadership.
Execution Capex delays in group expansions; integration risks post-merger. Proven Adani track record (e.g., Orient/Penna integration).

Overall Summary: Sanghi faces near-term headwinds from weather/low utilization but strong tailwinds from Adani synergies/merger. Growth hinges on H2 ramp-up and NCLT approval; medium-term prospects bright within Adani Cement’s Rs 1,500 PMT EBITDA target. Buy/Hold for long-term investors betting on consolidation; monitor Q3 volumes/merger timeline. Valuation supported by group multiples (~Rs 1,000-1,200/share implied post-merger).
