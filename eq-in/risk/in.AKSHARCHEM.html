Aksharchem (India) Limited: Analysis (Based on Q2/H1 FY25-26 Results & AGM Disclosures)

Overview: Aksharchem (India) Ltd., a Gujarat-based chemical manufacturer (single reportable segment: Chemicals), reported unaudited standalone H1 FY25-26 results on Nov 12, 2025. H1 revenue grew ~13% YoY (inferred from Q2 figures), driven by volume/capacity, but Q2 saw a ₹137L PAT loss (EPS -₹1.70) due to high costs and a Q-o-Q revenue dip (₹8,930L vs ₹9,090L in Q1). Balance sheet remains solid (total assets ₹42,690L, up 3%), with aggressive capex (PPE +16%). Operating cash flow strong at ₹1,077L. AGM resolutions (Sep 15, 2025) passed with ~99.99% approval. Fire incident (May 2024) recovery ongoing via insurance (₹170L exceptional gain in Q2).

Tailwinds (Positive Factors)

  • Revenue Momentum: Q2 revenue +13% YoY (₹8,930L vs ₹7,892L); H1 likely ~₹18,000L+. Indicates demand recovery post-fire.
  • Capex-Driven Expansion: PPE surged to ₹26,862L (+16% QoQ), CWIP down 67% (₹1,454L), signaling project completions and higher capacity utilization ahead.
  • Robust Cash Generation: H1 operating cash flow ₹1,077L (prior FY ₹3,482L), funding capex internally despite debt rise.
  • Insurance Support: ₹170L exceptional gain from fire claim (part of ₹543L received + ₹29L pending); offsets prior ₹402L loss (netted earlier).
  • Strong Governance: Overwhelming AGM approvals for re-appointments, auditors; promoter voting 100% aligned.

Headwinds (Negative Factors)

  • Profitability Pressure: Q2 pre-exceptional PBT loss ₹310L (vs Q1 profit ₹103L); high power/fuel (₹1,395L, +4% YoY), materials (₹4,478L), and inventory buildup (₹433L change). Gross margins squeezed.
  • Q-o-Q Weakness: Revenue -2%, expenses flat-to-up; PAT swing to loss drags H1 net profit down (~₹ -66L inferred).
  • Margin Erosion: Employee costs +25% YoY, depreciation +17% (capex impact), finance costs steady at ₹138L.
  • Equity Dilution Minor: Other equity down to ₹25,410L (-1%); dividend payout (₹58L) adds pressure.

Growth Prospects

  • High (Medium-Term): Capex cycle (₹1,528L H1 investing outflow) positions for 15-20% revenue CAGR via capacity addition in pigments/dyes chemicals. Single-segment focus aids efficiency.
  • Demand Tailwinds: Chemical sector recovery (post-FY25); YoY revenue growth + inventory optimization (₹6,967L, -1%) suggest utilization ramp-up.
  • Cash Flow Leverage: Strong ops cash (30% of revenue) supports debt repayment/capex; trade receivables up 19% but managed.
  • Upside Catalysts: Full fire recovery (additional claims), export potential (Gujarat hub), stable promoter holding (~63%).
