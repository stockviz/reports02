Analysis of Gillanders Arbuthnot and Company Limited (GILLANDERS)

Overview: Gillanders Arbuthnot (CIN: L51909WB1935PLC008194) reported unaudited standalone and consolidated financial results for Q2/H1 FY26 (ended Sep 30, 2025). Standalone H1 revenue was ₹200.7 Cr (flat YoY), with net profit of ₹12.8 Cr (down 16% YoY due to Q1 loss). Consolidated H1 revenue grew 4% YoY to ₹220.7 Cr, profit ₹8.8 Cr (down 24% YoY). Tea segment (seasonal) drove Q2 recovery (standalone Q2 profit ₹16.9 Cr vs. Q1 loss). Key metrics: EPS (standalone H1) ₹6.01; total assets up 5% to ₹502 Cr (standalone). Limited review by JKV&S Co.; reliance on other auditors for Engineering (MICCO) division.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Seasonality in Tea: Tea (55-60% revenue) caused Q1 loss (₹4.1 Cr standalone); H1 results not indicative of full year.
  • Loss-making Textiles: Consistent drag (H1 segment loss ₹2.6 Cr); Q2 loss ₹0.8 Cr amid high employee costs (₹64 Cr H1 consolidated).
  • Cash Flow Pressure: Standalone H1 operating cash flow negative (₹-2.8 Cr) due to working capital buildup (inventories +₹8.5 Cr, receivables +₹18 Cr).
  • Rising Costs: Employee expenses up 9% H1 YoY (₹64 Cr consolidated); finance costs ₹6.4 Cr; borrowings up 12% to ₹97 Cr consolidated.
  • Flat Revenue Growth: Standalone H1 revenue marginally down YoY; inter-segment elimination minor but persistent.

Tailwinds (Positive Supports)

  • Tea Segment Strength: Q2 boom (revenue ₹70 Cr standalone, segment profit ₹18 Cr); H1 Tea revenue +0.6% YoY to ₹98 Cr, assets up 36% to ₹145 Cr (inventory/biological assets surge).
  • Engineering (MICCO) Resilience: Steady revenue (₹44 Cr H1, +12% YoY), profit ₹3.2 Cr; assets ₹123 Cr (stable).
  • Property Stability: Consistent profit (₹2.8 Cr H1); low volatility.
  • Profit Recovery: Q2 standalone PBT ₹18.9 Cr (vs. Q1 loss); consolidated total income +3% YoY H1.
  • Balance Sheet Health: Equity up 5% to ₹281 Cr (standalone); low leverage (net debt/equity ~0.3x); recent USD 50k (~₹0.4 Cr) investment in Mauritius subsidiary signals expansion.

Growth Prospects

  • High: Tea core (60% consolidated revenue) poised for H2 surge (seasonal peak); international exposure via Mauritius sub (Naming’omba Tea Estates) and forex reserves could boost exports.
  • Medium: Engineering growth (FY25 revenue doubled YoY); property rentals scalable. Capex ₹4-5 Cr H1 (PPE up) supports capacity.
  • Opportunities: Subsidiary investment (55k shares) for overseas tea ops; unallocated income offsets costs. FY26 revenue potential ₹850-900 Cr (extrapolating seasonality); EPS growth if Tea sustains.
  • Outlook: Bullish on Tea cycle recovery; diversified segments (Engineering/Property) provide buffer. Analyst targets could rise post-results.
