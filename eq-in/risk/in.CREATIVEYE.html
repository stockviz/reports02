Analysis of Creative Eye Limited (CREATIVEYE / BSE:532392, NSE:CREATIVEYE)

Creative Eye Limited, a listed media and content production company (primarily TV serials), disclosed voting results from its 39th AGM held on September 30, 2025 (FY25 financials). All 5 resolutions passed with ~96% approval on polled votes (38.3% turnout of 20.06M shares), driven by promoters (56.3% holding, 65% polled, 100% in favor). Public non-institutions (43.7% holding) showed only 3.6% turnout, with 90% of their votes against all items—a red flag for dissent. No financial details provided, but resolutions included FY25 accounts adoption and key board/auditor changes.

Tailwinds (Positive Factors)

  • Promoter Alignment & Control: Promoters voted unanimously in favor (7.36M votes), ensuring swift passage of continuity-focused resolutions (e.g., re-appointment of Zuby Kochhar, new executive director Ashutosh Kochhar—likely family—and independent director Sarita Soni). Supports stable governance amid media sector volatility.
  • Regulatory Compliance: Timely AGM via VC/OAVM, full SEBI/Companies Act adherence, and scrutinizer report. Results uploaded to exchanges/company website (www.creativeeye.com).
  • Board Stability: Special resolutions for long-term roles (e.g., 5-year independent director, 3-year executive) signal management confidence in execution.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Public Shareholder Dissent: 90.5% of public votes (287k of 318k) opposed all resolutions—unusual uniformity suggesting coordinated discontent (possibly governance/retribution concerns). Low public turnout (3.6%) implies apathy or boycott.
  • Low Overall Participation: Only 38% shares polled; public institutions absent. Reflects potential illiquidity/low investor interest in a small-cap media stock.
  • Family/Promoter Entrenchment: Kochhar family dominance (multiple appointments) may alienate minority shareholders, echoing broader Indian small-cap governance issues.

Growth Prospects
