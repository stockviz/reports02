Analysis of Himadri Speciality Chemical Ltd (HSCL)

Overview: HSCL reported record profitability in Q2 & H1 FY26 (ended Sep 2025), with standalone H1 EBITDA at ₹477 Cr (+23% YoY) and PAT at ₹369 Cr (+43% YoY), despite flat volumes and ~7% revenue dip due to lower raw material (RM) prices (fully passed through). Margins expanded to ~22% via high-value specialty mix (65-70% contribution), operational efficiencies (yield, waste heat recovery), and backward integration. Core businesses (Coal Tar Pitch - CTP, Specialty Carbon Black - SCB) remain resilient, with expansions and diversification into battery materials, specialty chemicals, and tyres driving future growth. Balance sheet strong (net debt ₹113 Cr standalone, cash positive by Mar 2026), capex funded internally.

Tailwinds

  • Margin Resilience & Operational Excellence: EBITDA/kg improved (₹15 to ₹17), bucking global/peers’ stress in carbon black via niche specialty focus (60+ grades), consistent RM quality from in-house CBN oil, and low tyre-grade exposure. High-value shift + efficiencies sustain 22%+ margins.
  • Export Momentum: Supplies to 56 countries; first liquid CTP export from Mangalore (3,600 MT to Middle East, Nov 2025) opens west coast corridor (Haldia complements). Visibility for incremental CTP demand (Middle East smelters).
  • Sustainability Edge: ISCC+, EcoVadis Platinum (top 1%), CDP ‘A’ (suppliers), ZLD plants, 100% in-house clean power – premium pricing, customer preference (Scope 3 benefits).
  • Strong Liquidity & Low Leverage: D/E ~0.2x; commercial papers (₹300 Cr) backed by bank deposits. Robust cash flows fund expansions.
  • Diversified Demand: Aluminum/graphite stable; SCB for EV/batteries, inks, plastics.

Headwinds

  • Revenue Pressure: Q2/H1 top-line down 6-7% YoY from RM deflation (13-15%) and deferred Q3 exports. Volumes capped at peak utilization (~80-90%).
  • Tyre Segment Weakness: Birla Tyres revenue low (Q2 ₹26 Cr, consol adjustment issues); 10% utilization, startup phase. Ramp-up to 30-40% FY27.
  • Global Macro: Carbon black spreads stressed for commodity peers (e.g., Orion/Cabot); softer Europe/Asia demand noted, though HSCL insulated.
  • Capex Intensity: ₹1,771 Cr committed (LFP ₹1,125 Cr, SCB ₹220 Cr, etc.); delays could strain execution.

Growth Prospects

  • Near-Term (FY26): SCB brownfield doubles to 130k MT (Q3 end, world’s largest single-site); CTP debottlenecking to 600k MT; Durofresh naphthalene launch. H1 PAT already nears FY24 base (₹411 Cr); double by FY27 ahead of schedule.
  • Medium-Term (FY27-28): Specialty chemicals (Anthraquinone/Carbazole, Q2FY27); LFP Phase 1 (40k MT, Q3FY27, scaling to 200k MT/100 GWh); Birla Tyres PCR for EV/SUV (install in 12 months); anode tech (natural/synthetic/SiCx via Sicona 17.6% stake).
  • Battery Leadership: India’s first LFP ex-China; partnerships (IBC 16%, Invati 40%); pilot trials positive (e.g., IBC Prabal 2000). Targets EV/ESS amid India PLI push.
  • Long-Term: FY28 diversified portfolio; double-digit volume growth post-expansions; exports (Middle East/Americas focus).
  • Catalysts: Asia Cup sponsorship boosts Birla Tyres brand; Vihaan talent program.

Key Risks

Risk Category Description Mitigation
Execution Delays in expansions (SCB/LFP/chemicals); tyre ramp-up. On-track per mgmt (e.g., SCB Q3FY26); internal funding.
Commodity RM volatility (coal tar/crude); global oversupply in carbon black. 100% pass-through; specialty differentiation; low commodity exposure.
Geopolitical/Trade US tariffs (minimal impact via diversification); China competition. 56-country presence; non-China LFP USP.
Financial High capex (~₹1,800 Cr); subsidiary losses (tyres, new cos). Net cash positive; low debt; H1 CFO ₹54 Cr standalone.
Regulatory Tax regime shift (115BAA); EV subsidies/PLI changes. One-time DT reversal absorbed; Atmanirbhar alignment.
Market EV adoption slowdown; aluminum capex flat in India. Export focus (Middle East); multi-chemistry anodes.

Overall Summary & Outlook: HSCL’s FY26 is off to a stellar profitability start (record H1 PAT), driven by execution and differentiation amid headwinds like RM deflation. Tailwinds from expansions, exports, and battery pivot position it for 20-30%+ PAT CAGR through FY28, exceeding prior doubling guidance. Risks are manageable (execution-focused), with a de-risked balance sheet and sustainability moat. Buy/Hold rating implied for long-term compounding (target FY27 PAT ₹800 Cr+); monitor Q3 exports/capex progress. Stock poised for re-rating on battery milestones.
