Analysis of Himadri Speciality Chemical Ltd (HSCL) - Q2 & H1 FY26

Overview: HSCL delivered record profitability in Q2FY26 despite softer revenue (down 6% YoY standalone to Rs 1,070 Cr), driven by high-value product mix (65-70% contribution), operational efficiencies (yield improvements, waste heat recovery), and deferred export recognition (to Q3). EBITDA margins expanded to ~23% (H1: Rs 477 Cr, +23% YoY), PAT to Rs 369 Cr (+43% YoY). Balance sheet remains strong (net debt Rs 113 Cr, cash positive by Mar’26). Core growth from capacity expansions (SCB, CTP), battery materials (LFP/anodes), and Birla Tyres revival. FY27 PAT doubling target (from FY24 Rs 411 Cr) already in sight.

Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts)

  • Margin Resilience & Efficiency: EBITDA/tonne up from Rs 15/kg to Rs 17/kg YoY; backward integration (own CTP for SCB) ensures quality consistency/yield advantages. 100% RM price pass-through neutralizes volatility.
  • Export Momentum: 56 countries; new Mangalore terminal enables liquid pitch exports (Middle East demand visibility). Large Q3 order to boost volumes.
  • Sustainability Edge: ISCC Plus, EcoVadis Platinum (top 1% global), CDP ‘A’ rating command pricing premiums and customer loyalty (Scope 3 reductions).
  • Strong Liquidity: Internal accruals fund Rs 1,771 Cr capex (SCB Rs 220 Cr, LFP Rs 1,125 Cr, etc.); low leverage (D/E 0.20x).
  • Strategic Partnerships: Stakes in Sicona (17.6%, silicon anodes), IBC (16.2%), Invati (40%) accelerate battery tech.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Revenue Pressure: Flat volumes (135-140k MT, capacity-capped); RM price drop (13-15% YoY) reduced realizations despite margins holding.
  • Tyre Segment Startup Losses: Birla Tyres revenue Rs 26 Cr (Q2), low utilization (10%); consol gap due to inter-co eliminations/subsidiary sales deferral.
  • Macro/Commodity Cycles: Global carbon black stress (peers like Cabot/Orion report spreads compression); aluminium demand static domestically.
  • Capex Intensity: Heavy FY26-28 spends could strain short-term cash if delays occur.

Growth Prospects (High Potential)

  • Near-Term (FY26-27): SCB capacity doubles to 130k MT (Q3FY26, world’s largest single-site); CTP to 600k MT (Q4FY26 impact); speciality chems (Anthraquinone/Carbazole Q2FY27). Volumes + double-digits FY27; FY28 diversified portfolio adds meaningful revenue.
  • Battery Materials (Transformational): India’s first ex-China LFP plant (40k MT Phase 1 Q3FY27, 200k MT total for 100 GWh); anodes R&D (natural/synthetic/silicon). Aligns with EV/ESS boom (India LiB demand 4.7 GWh by 2030).
  • Birla Tyres Ramp: 29 distributors/350+ dealers (pan-India); 30-40% util FY27; PCR/EV tyres in 12 months. Huge EBITDA potential (>>Rs 50 Cr quarterly in 3-4 yrs).
  • Long-Term: FY28 revenue from LFP full ops, tyres scale-up; PAT far exceeds FY27 doubling. Global events (Abrafati, Battery Show) boost visibility.

Key Risks

  • Execution Risk (High): Delays in expansions (SCB/LFP) or customer approvals for battery materials could defer growth.
  • Commodity/Competition: Carbon black oversupply globally; Chinese cost edge in pitch/black (mitigated by speciality focus/exports).
  • Geopolitical/Trade: US tariffs minimal impact (diversified); forex volatility (net loss Rs 5 Cr Q2).
  • Demand Dependency: Aluminium/graphite electrodes flat; EV policy shifts.
  • Regulatory/Capex Funding: Tax regime change (115BAA); subsidiary losses (tyres/battery JVs).
  • ESG/Supply Chain: RM security for LFP (lithium/phosphate); Scope 3 reductions.

Overall Outlook: Strong Buy/Bullish. Tailwinds from de-risked core expansions, battery leadership, and efficiency dominate headwinds. Growth inflection Q4FY26 onward; PAT trajectory supports 2x+ from FY24 by FY27 (early). Risks execution-focused but mitigated by cash generation/track record. Target multiples: 25-30x FY27 EPS on 20%+ CAGR. Monitor Q3 exports/battery pilots.
