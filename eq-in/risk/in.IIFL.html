IIFL Finance Limited (NSE: IIFL) Analysis: Q2 FY26 Overview

Company Snapshot: IIFL Finance is a retail-focused NBFC with subsidiaries in housing (IIFL Home Finance) and microfinance (IIFL Samasta). Consolidated AUM reached a record ₹90,122 Cr (+35% YoY, +7% QoQ), driven by gold loans (38% mix, ₹34,577 Cr, +220% YoY). PAT (pre-NCI) at ₹418 Cr (+52% QoQ; H1 ₹692 Cr). GNPA improved to 2.1% (-21 bps QoQ), with PCR at 93%. Focus on secured lending (gold + home = 74% AUM), strong liquidity (₹8,170 Cr), and CRAR (standalone 18.6%). Recent approvals for ₹2,000 Cr NCD shelf issuance signal funding confidence.

Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts)

  • Robust Gold Loan Recovery: Post-RBI embargo lift (Sep 2024), AUM hit record highs with 16% QoQ tonnage growth, >98% retention, and stable 0.12% GNPA. Yields rising to 18.56%; momentum expected to sustain.
  • Secured Portfolio Shift: Core products (home, gold, secured MSME, micro) = 98% AUM (+37% YoY). Low LTVs, AI underwriting, and bank partnerships (34% off-book via assignments/co-lending) enhance scalability.
  • Improving Financials & Ratings: ROA 1.9% (H1), ROE 9.8%; net gearing 3.6x. Fitch upgraded outlook to Positive (‘B+’). Opex/AUM at 4%, cost of borrowing down 7 bps QoQ.
  • Leadership & Strategic Moves: New MD/CEO (Girish Kousgi) for Home Finance; discontinued high-risk segments (micro-LAP, digital unsecured). Strong phygital model (4,800 branches, 4.6 Mn customers).
  • Macro Support: RBI liquidity, govt push on MSME/housing/digital infra; PSL-eligible loans aid co-lending (expected rebound post Jan 2026 guidelines).

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Legacy Asset Quality Stress: Elevated GNPA in discontinued segments (micro-LAP 20.45%, unsecured MSME 7.51%, MFI 5.03%). Credit costs skewed by these (H1 3.5%; full-year guidance 2.8-3%, H2 2.2-2.4%).
  • MFI Sector Headwinds: 26% YoY AUM decline due to RBI guardrails (3-lender cap, ₹2L limit); Bihar (22% book) collections at 99% but arrears 6%. Loan losses ~8% expected.
  • Opex Pressures: Gold loan competition led to higher incentives/bonuses (+21% QoQ). Cost/income at 45.2%.
  • Growth Moderation: Home loans flat QoQ (cautious disbursals amid collections focus); MSME down 3% QoQ (recalibration to secured).
  • Funding/Regulatory Delays: Co-lending dip due to new RBI guidelines (CLM-1, effective Jan 2026); quarterly borrowing cost up 23% YoY.

Growth Prospects

  • Medium-Term Target: 15-20% AUM CAGR via gold (strong momentum), home (15-18%), secured MSME/LAP. H2 disbursals to accelerate.
  • Off-Book Expansion: Target 40-45% (vs. 34%); derecognition income sustainable (₹350 Cr Q2).
  • ROA/Profitability: FY26 ROA 2.5-2.8%; leverage operating efficiencies, margin expansion.
  • Diversification: MSME secured scaling (AI/co-lending); rural/MSME tailwinds from govt schemes. Potential equity raise for buffer.
  • Upside Levers: Gold price tailwinds, rating upgrades enabling cheaper funding, H2 credit cost normalization.

Key Risks

Risk Category Description Mitigation
Credit/Asset Quality Legacy NPAs in MFI/unsecured (2-4% book but skewing costs); Bihar-specific stress. Discontinued high-risk products; focus on collections; ARC sales planned; ECL provisions > RBI norms.
Regulatory MFI guardrails, co-lending changes, SBR compliance for upper-layer NBFC. Strong governance (dedicated CCO, board committees); quarterly stress tests.
Competition/Funding Intense gold loan rivalry (higher opex); rising borrowing costs (9%+). Off-book strategy, liquidity buffer; NCD shelf for diversification.
Macro/Execution Gold price volatility, inflation/geopolitics; leadership transitions. Phygital model, AI risk systems; experienced mgmt (e.g., new Home CEO).
Capital/Liquidity Standalone CRAR 18.6% (target 20%); 20% growth may need equity. Internal accruals + co-lending suffice short-term; monitoring closely.

Overall Outlook: Positive with cautious optimism. Tailwinds from gold/home recovery and secured shift outweigh headwinds, positioning IIFL for 15-20% growth and ROA expansion. Risks concentrated in legacy segments (contained at ~4% book), with improving trajectory. Stock likely to benefit from rating positivity and H2 momentum, but monitor MFI execution and regulatory clarity. Valuation appears reasonable given rebound (ROE ramp-up potential to 15-18%).
