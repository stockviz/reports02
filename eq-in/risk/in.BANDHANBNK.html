Bandhan Bank Limited (BANDHANBNK) Analysis

Bandhan Bank, a universal bank with a focus on underserved segments (especially Emerging Entrepreneurs Business or EEB/microfinance), reported Q2FY26 (ended Sep 30, 2025) results showing modest balance sheet growth amid asset quality pressures and profitability contraction. Key metrics: Advances ₹1,400 bn (+7% YoY), Deposits ₹1,581 bn (+11% YoY), GNPA 5.0%, NIM 5.8% (-152 bps YoY), PAT ₹1.1 bn (-88% YoY). The bank is diversifying away from EEB (now ~37% of book) toward secured retail/housing/wholesale, with strong capital (CRAR 18.6%) and digital traction (98% retail txns digital).

Headwinds (Challenges Pressuring Performance)

  • Asset Quality Stress: Elevated GNPA (5.0%, +34 bps YoY) driven by EEB slippages; credit cost at 3.4% (+144 bps YoY). Board approved sale of ₹3,212 Cr NPAs (>180 DPD) and ₹3,719 Cr written-offs (Swiss challenge/auction), signaling cleanup but potential capital hit.
  • Profitability Squeeze: NII down 12% YoY (₹26 bn), PAT -88% YoY due to high provisions (₹11.5 bn), opex, and NIM compression (5.8%). ROA/ROE at 0.2%/1.8% (multi-year lows).
  • EEB Dependency: EEB book flat (-2% QoQ), collections at 97.8% (stable but below pre-COVID peaks), with stress pools (SMA/NPA) persisting.
  • Regulatory/Minor Costs: GST demand order (₹2.18 Cr tax+penalty+interest for FY19-23; no material impact claimed, appeal planned).

Tailwinds (Supportive Factors)

  • Deposit Momentum: +11% YoY growth, retail deposits 71% of mix (CASA 28%), low-cost base (avg SA balance stable).
  • Diversification Progress: Non-EEB advances +24% YoY (₹883 bn); secured book 55% (+790 bps YoY); wholesale/housing/retail growing (29%/33%/~37% shares).
  • Capital & Liquidity Strength: CRAR 18.6% (CET1 17.8%), liquidity coverage ample; enables NPA sales without dilution.
  • Digital/Operational Scale: 32 mn customers, 1,754 branches/4,593 units, 98% digital retail txns, 92% digital SA onboarding; productivity up 18% YoY.

Growth Prospects (Medium-Term Opportunities)

  • Portfolio Rebalancing: Target non-EEB (wholesale ₹408 bn +ve momentum, housing disbursements steady); non-East loans 62% (+228 bps YoY).
  • Digital & Product Expansion: WhatsApp banking, QR collections, merchant acquiring (Jio tie-up), agency business (tax collections); 100%+ YoY growth in digital deposits/UPI.
  • Network/Geography: Pan-India presence (35 states), rural/semi-urban focus; potential 10-15% AUM CAGR via secured lending.
  • NPA Resolution: Portfolio sales could unlock ₹7 Cr recovery potential, improve RoA toward 1.5-2% by FY27.
  • Macro Tailwinds: Improving rural economy, credit demand in housing/SME; est. 12-15% loan CAGR FY26-28 if asset quality stabilizes.

Key Risks

Risk Category Description Mitigation

Profitability Medium NIM pressure from deposit costs (+78 bps YoY); high credit cost >3%.

Overall Summary: Bandhan faces near-term headwinds from legacy EEB NPAs and weak profitability (FY26 PAT growth muted at <20%), but tailwinds from deposits, capital, and diversification position it for recovery. Growth prospects hinge on NPA cleanup and non-EEB ramp-up (target RoA 1.5%+ by FY27). Valuation Context: Trades at ~0.8x FY26E BVPS (cheap vs. peers); buy on dips if Q3 asset quality improves. Monitor NPA sale outcomes and EEB collections. (Analysis based solely on provided disclosures; current market price/date not considered.)
