Analysis of Antelopus Selan Energy Limited (formerly Selan Exploration Technology Limited)

Company Overview: Antelopus Selan Energy Limited (scrip: ANTELOPUS / 530075) is an oil & gas exploration and production company operating under Production Sharing Contracts (PSCs) in India. Key highlights from Q2/H1 FY26 (ended Sep 30, 2025) include restated unaudited financials post-amalgamation (effective Jul 5, 2025) with Antelopus Energy Pvt Ltd (pooling of interests), equity dilution via new share issuance (capital up ~131% to ₹35 Cr), ongoing capex in oil/gas assets, new production from Mukkamala field (Dangeru PSC), and pursuit of 100% Participating Interest (PI) in Cambay field. Financials show steady ops (H1 net revenue ~₹10.6 Cr, net profit ~₹2.3 Cr, EPS ~₹3.2), strong balance sheet (assets ₹732 Cr, equity ₹588 Cr, cash/investments ~₹14.6 Cr), and positive operating cash flows (₹42.6 Cr). No material undisclosed events per Nov 24 disclosure; share price volatility deemed market-driven.

Tailwinds (Positive Factors)

  • Amalgamation Benefits: Merger with Antelopus Energy (effective Jul 2025) boosts scale (oil/gas assets up to ₹315 Cr from ₹290 Cr; CWIP ₹158 Cr). Restated pre-merger metrics strong (FY25 net profit ₹74 Cr).
  • Production Ramp-Up: Commercial production started from Mukkamala field (Q2 FY26), supporting revenue stability.
  • Asset Expansion: Secured 50% PI/operatorship in Cambay field (Jul 2024 GoI approval; ₹30 Cr capitalized); Heads of Terms signed for remaining 50% (initial ₹4.7 Cr paid; relieves carry obligations post-full acquisition).
  • Financial Strength: Robust liquidity (cash ₹5.8 Cr, investments ₹53 Cr, other bank balances ₹87 Cr); H1 op. cash flow ₹42.6 Cr covers capex (₹60 Cr outflow).
  • Cost Discipline: Lower op. expenses vs. prior; high EBITDA margins (~65-70% implied).
  • Regulatory Compliance: Name change approved (Aug 2025); registered office shift; clean AGM outcomes (99.99% approvals).

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Revenue Pressure: Q2 FY26 net revenue ₹55 Cr (down ~9% QoQ from ₹51 Cr? Table inconsistencies noted, but H1 PBT ₹25-30 Cr lower YoY post-restatement). Dependent on crude realizations (intl. USD prices + INR/USD).
  • High Fixed Costs: Depreciation/amortization ~₹28 Cr (H1, 55% of expenses); royalties ₹21 Cr (~20% of revenue).
  • Equity Dilution: Share capital doubled to ₹35 Cr; EPS dilution risk if growth lags.
  • Capex Intensity: ₹60 Cr H1 outflow (PP&E/CWIP up 20-30%); FD movements indicate liquidity parking amid investments.
  • Share Price Volatility: Recent movements prompted regulatory clarification (no undisclosed events), signaling potential speculation or low liquidity.

Growth Prospects

  • High (Medium-Term: 2-3 Years):
    • Cambay field full acquisition (100% PI targeted post-SPA/GoI nod) could double/add material PI; carry relief + production bonuses unlocked.
    • Ongoing capex (CWIP ₹158 Cr) signals field developments (Dangeru, Cambay); new Mukkamala output scalable.
    • Pre-merger trajectory (FY25 revenue ₹258 Cr, profit ₹74 Cr) + synergies position for 20-30% CAGR if oil prices stable ($70-80/bbl).
    • Pipeline: Farm-in opportunities; single-segment focus aids execution.
  • Catalysts: GoI approvals, Q3 results (Dec 2025), full-year guidance adherence.
