Analysis of Aegis Vopak Terminals Limited (AEGISVOPAK)

Company Overview: AVTL is India’s largest independent tank storage terminal operator for LPG and liquids, with ~1.7 Mn CBM liquid capacity and 200.8k MT LPG capacity across 6 ports. It benefits from JV parents Aegis Logistics (local expertise) and Royal Vopak (global standards). Q2 FY26 showed robust growth: Revenue +26% YoY to ₹188 Cr, EBITDA +26% to ₹137 Cr (73% margin), PAT +142% to ₹54 Cr. H1 PAT +111% YoY. Expansions and debt reduction (finance costs -61% YoY) drive performance.

Tailwinds

  • Strong Demand Drivers: Rising LPG imports (India’s domestic production static at ~9 Mn MT; demand ~30 Mn MT), chemicals trade (CAGR 3-19%), and ammonia for fertilizers/green H2. Pipeline connectivities (KGPL 8.25 Mn MT, JLPL 3.5-6.5 Mn MT) and VLGC berthing to boost volumes/utilization.
  • Operational Ramp-Up: New Pipavav/Mangalore LPG terminals (130k MT added) now revenue-accretive; gas EBITDA/ton ~₹1,100 (target ₹1,200).
  • Financial Discipline: EBITDA margins ~73%; low gearing (0.6x EBITDA); cash flows strong (H1 operating cash ₹2.8 Bn). Recent ₹660 Cr NCD allotment funds capex.
  • ESG/Policy Support: Green ammonia push, govt subsidies (PMUY), 100% FDI in chemicals.

Headwinds

  • Ramp-Up Delays: New terminals show lower throughput turns (14-15 vs. 25-30 target); full benefits from pipelines/VLGC in Q4 FY26 onward. Q3 FY26 may remain subdued.
  • Cost Pressures: Depreciation up (due to expansions/IndAS 116); finance costs persist despite reductions.
  • Capex Intensity: ₹10,000 Cr (~USD 1.2 Bn) by FY27 across 8-12 ports requires execution amid land/regulatory hurdles.
  • Low Public Float Voting: Promoter voting absent in RPT resolutions (8.4% turnout), signaling reliance on institutions.

Growth Prospects

  • High Visibility Capex: JNPA (₹1,675 Cr: +318k CBM liquids, 77k MT LPG), Pipavav Ammonia (36k MT, 15-yr take-or-pay), HALPG acquisition (25k MT LPG, East Coast entry), +120k CBM liquids (Kochi/Mangalore/Kandla). Target: 3 Mn CBM liquids, USD 5 Bn cumulative capex by 2030.
  • Volume/Margin Expansion: Gas throughput H1 1.2 Mn MT (+ ramp to 12 Mn MT via pipelines); liquids realization ₹2,500/CBM (up from ₹2,250). Diversification: Ammonia (8-10 terminals), industrial terminals, Vadhavan Port MoU (₹20,000 Cr potential).
  • Projections: EBITDA/ton stable at ₹1,100-1,200 (gas), ₹1,700/CBM (liquids); sustained 15-25% revenue CAGR via 3x capacity growth.
  • Strategic: Pan-India footprint (6→8+ ports), multi-modal (rail/pipeline/road), promoter synergies (RPTs approved for Aegis Gas/Vopak).

Key Risks

Risk Category Description Mitigation
Execution Delays in capex (e.g., JNPA partial FY26, pipelines Q3-Q4 FY26), land allotments. Proven track record; non-binding MoUs progressing.
Financial Debt-funded growth (new NCDs at 6.92%, max gearing 3.5x); forex/interest rate volatility. Strong cash flows (FY25 ₹4.8 Bn op. cash); FACR 1.3x covenant.
Market/Operational Volume dependency on OMCs (HPCL exclusivity at HALPG till 2038); competition; lower new-terminal realizations. Diversified customers (OMCs/MNCs); 84x design throughput turns.
Regulatory/RPT Approvals (PNGRB, ports); RPT scrutiny (Aegis Gas/Vopak transactions approved but low turnout). Shareholder approvals secured; strong governance.
External Commodity volatility, geopolitical (LPG imports), green transition shifts. Long-term contracts; ammonia diversification.

Overall Outlook: Strong Buy/Bullish. Tailwinds from India’s energy infra boom outweigh headwinds; 20-30% EPS CAGR feasible with execution. Monitor Q3 volumes and JNPA progress. Market cap ~₹3 Bn (post-listing growth); undervalued vs. peers on EV/EBITDA (~10-12x FY27E). Risks moderate, tilted toward growth execution.
