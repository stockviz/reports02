Analysis of SANDUMA (The Sandur Manganese & Iron Ores Limited - SMIORE)

Company Overview: SMIORE is a leading private merchant miner of manganese (2nd largest in India) and iron ore (3rd largest in Karnataka), with integrated operations in ferroalloys, coke, power (WHRB & renewables), and specialty steel (via Arjas Steel acquisition effective Nov 2024). FY26 capacities: Mn ore 0.599 MTPA, Fe ore 4.45 MTPA, steel 0.585 MTPA. Strong reserves (15 MT Mn, 113 MT Fe till 2033), A+/Stable ratings, and sustainability focus (7-star mine rating).

Q2/H1 FY26 performance (consolidated): Revenue ₹1,245/₹2,395 Cr (+336%/+163% YoY), EBITDA ₹285/₹599 Cr (23%/25% margins), PAT ₹139/₹306 Cr (+332%/+73% YoY). Standalone even stronger (EBITDA margins ~43-47%). Mining drove growth; steel integration underway.

Recent: MPAP allocation (Nov 2025) enhances Mn output by ~22% to 0.039 MTPA FY26; bonus issue (2:1) completed Sep 2025; AGM resolutions passed overwhelmingly.

Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts)

  • Mining Ramp-Up: Recent MPAP boost (Mn +0.007 MTPA; Fe 0.037 MTPA pro-rata). Q2 sales: Mn +335% YoY/+66% QoQ (1.28 LT prod.), Fe +30% YoY (8.93 LT prod.). High margins (mining OPM ~50-60%).
  • Steel Integration: Arjas Steel (SBQ auto-grade, top-5 player) adds 0.585 MTPA capacity, marquee clients (Hyundai, Tata, etc.). Q2 volumes stable (+4% QoQ); synergies via captive ore/power.
  • Energy Self-Sufficiency: 42.9 MW hybrid RE (JV), 32/21 MW WHRB, 20.4 MW solar. Reduces costs, supports ferroalloys/steel.
  • Financial Strength: ROCE 48% FY25; net cash flows robust; low leverage (standalone D/E 0.35, consol 0.64). Dividend ₹1.25/share approved/paid.
  • Sustainability Edge: 88% lease forest cover, zero-discharge, employee welfare; attracts ESG capital.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Seasonal/External Pressures: Monsoon curbed Fe prod.; QoQ realization dips (Mn -5%, Fe -9%, ferro -6%). Coke own-prod paused (contract mfg at 46% capacity).
  • Commodity Volatility: Soft steel/ferro demand; broader metals moderation impacted Q2 EBITDA (-9-11% QoQ).
  • Integration Costs: Steel segment drags consol margins (23% vs standalone 43%); higher finance costs post-acquisition.
  • Regulatory Delays: Pending CFO expansions; Supreme Court-monitored mining limits cap output.

Growth Prospects

  • High: Multi-fold via mining (to 0.599/4.45 MTPA), steel value-add (SBQ margins > commodity steel), RE scaling. H1 FY26 +163% revenue signals momentum. QIP approved for fundraising; cash flows fund capex (₹11 Cr Q2 invest.).
  • Target: Integrated miner-to-steel play; export/Railways/EV push. FY26 guidance: Mining leverage, steel synergies → 20-30%+ EBITDA growth if prices hold.
  • Long-Term: Reserves support 10+ yrs; niche SBQ (auto/OEMs) + renewables position for premium valuations.
