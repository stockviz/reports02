Analysis of SANDUMA (The Sandur Manganese & Iron Ores Limited - SMIORE)

Company Overview: SMIORE is an integrated mining player focused on manganese (2nd largest private producer in India) and iron ore (3rd largest in Karnataka), with downstream operations in ferroalloys (SiMn/FeMn), coke, captive power (WHRB & renewables), and a recently acquired specialty steel business (Arjas Steel, 0.585 MTPA capacity in SBQ/auto-grade steel). FY26 capacities: 0.599 MTPA Mn ore, 4.45 MTPA Fe ore. Strong reserves (15 MT Mn, 113 MT Fe; leases to 2033). Q2/H1 FY26 showed robust volume growth but softer realizations due to monsoon. Consolidated H1FY26: Revenue ₹2,395 Cr (+163% YoY), EBITDA ₹599 Cr (25% margin), PAT ₹306 Cr. Debt/Equity: 0.64x (post-steel acquisition).

Tailwinds (Supportive Factors)

  • Volume Ramp-Up & Capacity Expansion: Q2FY26 Mn ore sales +335% YoY/+66% QoQ (0.81 LT), Fe ore +30% YoY (7.85 LT), ferroalloys +137% YoY. Recent MPAP allocation (20 Nov 2025) boosts FY26 Fe ore to 0.037 MTPA, Mn to 0.039 MTPA (+22%). Overall mining capacities doubled recently.
  • Steel Integration Synergies: Arjas acquisition (Nov 2024) adds high-margin SBQ steel (top-5 player; marquee auto OEMs like Hyundai, Tata). Q2FY26 steel sales stable at 1.01 LT (₹70k/T realization). Captive Mn ore consumption potential.
  • Financial Resilience: H1FY26 consolidated revenue +163% YoY, PAT +73%; standalone EBITDA margin 47%. Strong cash flows (op. cash ₹349 Cr H1). CRISIL A+/Stable rating supports funding.
  • ESG & Cost Advantages: 7-star rated mines, 88% forest cover, zero-discharge ferroalloys, captive renewables (42.9 MW solar-wind + WHRB). Employee welfare enhances retention (4,257 staff).
  • Market Positioning: Merchant miner with low-P Mn ore; steel diversification into high-value auto/EV/railways segments.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Commodity Price Pressure: Q2FY26 realizations down QoQ (Mn -5%, Fe -9%, ferroalloys -6%) amid soft steel demand and monsoon. Steel industry outlook unchanged/moderate.
  • Seasonal/Operational Disruptions: Monsoon curbed Fe ore production; coke own production paused (reliant on contract mfg., covering ~46% capacity).
  • Integration & Scale-Up Delays: Steel business new (consolidated from Nov 2024); full synergies pending. Ferroalloys at partial capacity (2/ furnaces operational).
  • Elevated Leverage: Post-acquisition Debt/Equity 0.64x (standalone 0.35x); finance costs up sharply (H1 ₹110 Cr vs. ₹9 Cr prior year). Interest coverage 5.41x (down YoY).
  • Margin Compression: Consolidated EBITDA margin dipped to 25% H1FY26 (-538 bps YoY) due to steel mix and higher costs.

Growth Prospects

  • Mining Expansion: New MPAP + KSPCB CFO enables near-term output growth (target FY26: Mn 0.592 MTPA, Fe 0.729 MTPA). Incidental Fe ore transport approval (0.327 MTPA over 2 yrs).
  • Downstream Value-Add: Ferroalloys optimization (95-125k TPA); steel ramp-up (focus non-auto: railways RDSO-approved, EVs/exports/defence). Coke restart + contract stability.
  • Capital Raising: AGM approved QIP for equity infusion; disciplined allocation for expansions.
  • Energy Self-Sufficiency: 42.9 MW Renew JV + 32/21 MW WHRB; green energy transition lowers costs.
  • Medium-Term Targets: Integrated player from ore to steel; 3-5x mining growth potential per reserves. EBITDA trajectory: FY25 ₹2,293 Cr (+18% YoY).
  • Outlook: Resilient FY26 guidance amid steel recovery; QIP funds to fuel inorganic/organic growth.

Key Risks

  • Regulatory/Approval Delays: Supreme Court-monitored mining (MPAP/CFO pending full execution); environmental clearances critical for expansions.
  • Commodity Volatility: 70-80% revenue from ores/steel; global steel slowdown, China exports could pressure realizations.
  • Execution Risks: Steel integration, furnace ramp-up, monsoon variability; capex execution (CWIP ₹25 Bn).
  • Debt & Liquidity: High finance costs (₹111 Cr H1); refinancing NCDs (₹432 Cr). Current ratio 1.24x.
  • Geopolitical/External: Input costs (coking coal), forex (imports), demand from auto/steel sectors.
  • ESG/Compliance: Mining scrutiny; any lapses could halt ops (e.g., past CEC approvals).

Overall Summary: SMIORE benefits from strong tailwinds in mining volumes, steel diversification, and ESG credentials, driving 163% YoY H1 revenue growth. Headwinds like price softness and leverage are transitional, with robust cash flows providing buffer. Growth prospects are compelling (expansions + QIP), positioning it as an integrated mid-cap miner-to-steel player. Key monitor: Regulatory nods and steel synergies. Buy/Accumulate on dips for 20-30% upside in 12-18 months (target ₹250-300; CMP ~₹205), assuming stable commodities. Risks tilted regulatory/volatility (high-beta stock).
