Analysis of Diamond Power Infrastructure Limited (DIACABS)

Company Overview: DIACABS is a Gujarat-based manufacturer of power cables and conductors, emerging from NCLT resolution (new management takeover in FY23). Key recent developments include a Rs. 276 Cr LOI from Adani Energy Solutions (Nov 2025), strong H1 FY26 financials (ended Sep 30, 2025), board approval for Rs. 1,000 Cr QIP (Postal Ballot ongoing), and corporate guarantee for subsidiary. Financials show revenue surge but persistent balance sheet weaknesses. Sector tailwinds from India’s power infra/renewable push (e.g., Khavda RE project).

Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts)

  • Order Book Strength: Rs. 276 Cr LOI from Adani for 7,668 KM Zebra Conductor (execution by Nov 2026; “kms rate basis with PV formula”). No promoter/group interest; pure merit win. Signals entry into high-value RE transmission projects.
  • Robust Financial Momentum: | Metric (Consolidated, Rs.     • Q2 FY26 revenue at Rs. 43,833 Lacs (~Rs. 438 Cr); PAT Rs. 277 Cr. Material costs ~84% of revenue, but margins expanding.
  • Capital Infusion Plan: QIP up to Rs. 1,000 Cr to QIBs (min. 10% to MFs; no promoters). Addresses MPS non-compliance (SEBI circular compliant method) and funds growth/debt prepay.
  • Strategic Moves: Senior hires (Sales/Marketing, Finance); Rs. 173 Cr guarantee for WOS (DICABS Nextgen Special Alloys) term loan/CC/LC—supports alloy expansion.
  • Sector Boom: Power T&D capex (India’s RE targets); conductors critical for projects like Adani’s Khavda (30 GW RE hub).

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Weak Balance Sheet: Negative equity (Consolidated: -Rs. 715 Cr; Standalone: -Rs. 718 Cr). Other equity -Rs. 767 Cr due to legacy losses.
    • High leverage: Long-term borrowings Rs. 570 Cr; “Other Financial Liabilities” ~Rs. 1,899 Cr (likely promoter/inter-co loans).
    • Trade payables down 54% YoY (Rs. 780 Cr → Rs. 108 Cr), signaling tighter vendor terms.
  • Operational Cash Burn: Net CFO -Rs. 205 Cr H1 FY26 (vs. +Rs. 17 Cr FY25) due to WC strain (AR up 68% to Rs. 295 Cr; Inv. up 36% to Rs. 280 Cr).
  • Auditor Qualifications:
    • Qualified review on PPE (Rs. 1,183 Cr): Ongoing reconciliation/physical verification (independent agency); depn. at 20% estimate (vs. full rates); potential impairment/CWIP capitalization (Rs. 129 Cr) unverified.
    • Legacy NCLT assets idle pre-takeover; docs seized by CBI/ED.
  • Regulatory Hangover: ED asset attachment unresolved (pre-NCLT; petitions filed under IBC S.32 immunity). No provision, but contingent risk.

Growth Prospects

  • High Visibility Revenue: Adani order = ~37% of FY25 revenue; multi-year execution. Single segment (cables/conductors) but scalable with capex (PPE + CWIP Rs. 1,313 Cr).
  • Fund Utilization (QIP): Debt repayment (Rs. 60 Cr LTB up H1), WC (AR cycle ~60 days), expansion (product portfolio, new territories, acquisitions), subsidiary equity/debt.
  • Turnaround Trajectory: Post-NCLT revival—revenue 2.5x FY25 H1; PAT margins ~6.5% (up from ~4%). CWIP commissioning could boost capacity/utilization.
  • Outlook: FY26 revenue potential Rs. 1,500-2,000 Cr (Adani + organic); EBITDA margins 10-12% feasible with scale. QIP success → positive equity, deleveraging (D/E ~4x now).
  • Macro: Rs. 2.4 Tn power capex FY26 (Govt.); conductor demand from RE/grid (e.g., 500 GW non-fossil by 2030).

Overall Summary: High-Growth Turnaround Play with Execution Risks. Tailwinds dominate (Adani order, revenue/EBITDA surge, QIP) positioning for 50-100% revenue growth FY26. However, headwinds (debt, negative equity, cash burn, qualifications) cap near-term upside. QIP approval (Dec 2025) pivotal—success unlocks deleveraging/growth. Recommendation: Accumulate on dips for 2-3 yr horizon (target multiples: 1-1.5x sales FY27E); monitor QIP, ED resolution, H2 execution.
