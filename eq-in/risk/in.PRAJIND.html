PRAJIND (Praj Industries Ltd.) Analysis: Q2/H1 FY26

Summary Overview: Praj Industries reported muted H1 FY26 results amid headwinds in domestic ethanol (post-EBP20 achievement) and GenX underutilization, leading to revenue decline (consol. -2.2% YoY) and sharp profit drop (PAT -82.2% YoY). However, a robust order backlog (₹44.2 Bn, +3% QoQ), healthy Q2 intake (₹8.1 Bn), and diversification efforts provide resilience. Bioenergy remains core (64% revenue), with international tailwinds in low-carbon fuels. Management eyes slow recovery in H2 FY26, pivoting GenX for FY28 peak, while long-term prospects in SAF/CBG/2G remain intact. No guidance given; stock trades at ~4x FY25 sales (down sharply from peaks).

Headwinds (Key Challenges)

  • Domestic Bioenergy Slowdown: EBP20 target met; greenfield ethanol projects delayed/slow due to funding gaps, civil delays, and policy overhang (negative news rebutted by stakeholders). Shift to brownfield/services, but execution cycles extended.
  • GenX Drag: Fixed costs ₹8.5-9 Cr/month under-absorbed; energy transition projects stalled/on-hold. Peak capacity (₹1,500 Cr revenue) delayed to FY28 (from FY27). Contributed to EBITDA margin contraction (Q2: 6.6% vs. 10.6% YoY).
  • Margin Pressures: Higher other expenses (+46% YoY) from execution ramp-up; material costs lower due to services mix, but overall EBITDA/PAT down sharply. ETR elevated at 35-37% due to deferred tax on GenX losses.
  • US/International Headwinds: Tariffs (effective 10-30%) extend customer payback by 5-6 months; collections tied to project funding delays.
  • H1 Impact: Revenue flat-to-down; operating cash flow negative (-₹45 Cr consol.) due to working capital buildup (CIP/inventories up).

Tailwinds (Supportive Factors)

  • Strong Order Book: Q2 intake ₹8.1 Bn (+2% QoQ; 71% bioenergy); backlog ₹44.2 Bn (stable QoQ, 65% domestic). Diversified geography (46% export revenue).
  • Execution Momentum: Q2 revenue +31% QoQ; focus on controllables delivering despite macros.
  • Services & Brownfield: Healthy bookings for efficiency upgrades, CO2 capture, distillers corn oil modules.
  • Financial Strength: Net debt-free; cash ₹4.37 Bn (standalone). ROCE FY25: 23%.
  • Segment Resilience: PHS (10% revenue) gaining in pharma/EV/semicon; brewery greenfield revival; ZLD competitive edge (modular, O&M).

Growth Prospects

  • Bioenergy (Core ~70% Orders): International low-carbon ethanol pipeline (US 45Z credits; first project commissioning FY26-end). Policies in LatAm (Panama/Argentina), Indonesia (E10), Vietnam (E20 by ’26), Africa. Domestic: Brownfield focus; 2G ethanol exports enabled; diesel blending trials (isobutanol tech ready, 2.5-3x petrol opportunity).
  • Emerging Fuels: SAF demo operational (world-first integrated ethanol-to-jet); CBG ramp (Napier/press mud pipeline, first Napier project executing; awaits gas grid).
  • Engineering Pivot: GenX targeting O&G/piping (4 audits done; orders from Q3/Q4). PHS expansion (fermenters, ultrapure water for batteries/solar).
  • R&D Pipeline (Praj Matrix): Bioplastics (discussions ongoing), bio-bitumen, ZLD (largest IOCL project). Vision 2030 intact (diversified biofuels/biochemicals); H2 FY26 recovery via diversification.
  • Medium-Term: Inflows to rebound (double-digit FY27?); global ethanol/SAF/CBG tailwinds.
