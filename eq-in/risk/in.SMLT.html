Analysis of Sarthak Metals Limited (SMLT) - Q2/H1 FY26 Overview

Sarthak Metals Limited, a cored wire manufacturer, reported muted Q2/H1 FY26 results amid steel sector headwinds but highlighted diversification into welding consumables and biotechnology as key growth drivers. H1 revenue declined ~28% YoY to ₹82.5 Cr (Q2: ₹36.3 Cr, -21% YoY), PAT at ₹1.82 Cr (-~56% YoY annualized), with EBITDA margins pressured. Balance sheet remains strong (debt-free core, equity ₹121 Cr), but working capital ballooned (inventories +63% to ₹44.8 Cr). Management emphasized resilience via new segments during the Nov 13, 2025 earnings call.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Steel Sector Pressures: Low-cost imports (esp. from Asia at 5-yr lows) squeezing domestic margins; CapEx slowdown by majors (JSPL, JSW). Core cored wire volumes -14% YoY in Q2; aluminium coil segment paused due to “unethical competition.”
  • Demand/Seasonal Factors: Monsoon impacted Q2; subdued orders from steelmakers amid price drops.
  • Financial Strain: Negative operating cash flow (-₹150 Cr H1) from inventory buildup (+₹17 Cr) and receivables; short-term borrowings up to ₹2.85 Cr.
  • Cyclical Exposure: 90%+ revenue still tied to volatile steel cycle.

Tailwinds (Positives)

  • Debt-Free Balance Sheet: Virtually zero long-term debt; ₹14 Cr cash/bank balances provide firepower for growth.
  • Diversification Progress: Welding at >100 tons/month (dealer-driven, 10+ dealers across 5 states); biotech in advanced talks with ethanol distilleries.
  • Regulatory/Policy Support: Welding gains BIS/RDSO approvals (railway gold standard); biofuels push aligns with govt’s ethanol blending targets.
  • Sustainability Edge: Recycled scrap usage, 400 kW solar plant positions for green steel transition (e.g., EU CBAM).

Growth Prospects

Segment Key Highlights Targets/Outlook
Core (Cored/Aluminium Wires) Stable market share via quality/tech; ethical execution to regain share. Long-term steel demand to 500MT by 2047; resume aluminium on rationalization.
Welding Consumables 5 SKUs → 10; 90% dealer sales, institutional ~10%. ₹25 Cr sales in 2 yrs; EBITDA 9-10% (high single/low double-digit post-branding).
Biotechnology Enzymes/bio-consumables for ethanol (₹1/litre value); >₹1,000 Cr market (OMCs tender undershot). Revenue “very soon”; healthy double-digit margins; tech partnerships.
Overall 3-pillar model derisks cyclicity (infra + consumption trends). FY28: All segments ~10-15% EBITDA contribution possible.
  • Near-Term Catalysts: Biotech revenue ramp-up; welding order surge from infra/construction.
  • Long-Term: Diversified revenue (steel + high-margin biotech/welding); FY26 guidance conservative but optimistic on stabilization.

Key Risks

  1. Import Dumping: Prolonged low steel prices could further erode core volumes/margins; govt duties/quality controls critical.
  2. Execution in New Ventures: Welding cyclical (tied to fabrication); biotech unproven (dependency on distilleries, tender overcapacity).
  3. Working Capital/Liquidity: High inventories/rec (~₹86 Cr combined) risks cash burn if sales lag.
  4. Macro/Regulatory: Steel CapEx delays; biofuel policy shifts; forex gains (₹0.26 Cr unrealized) volatile.
  5. Diversification Dilution: MoA biotech amendment via postal ballot (Dec 12 deadline) signals pivot, but capex/competence risks in nascent bio-segment.
  6. Single-Segment Reporting: Still classified as “cored wires” (Note 7), potentially understating diversification.

Investment Summary: Short-term defensive (steel headwinds cap growth), but medium-term bullish on diversification (welding/biotech ~high teens margins). Monitor import curbs, biotech revenue start, and WC efficiency. Strong balance sheet mitigates downside; undervalued if execution delivers (trading ~₹57/share implied by EPS). Risks tilted macro/execution; suitable for patient growth investors.
