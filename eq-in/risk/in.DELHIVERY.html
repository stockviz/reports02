Analysis of Delhivery Limited (DELHIVERY) - Q2FY26 Outlook

Headwinds (Challenges Pressuring Performance)

  • Ecom Express Integration Costs: ₹90 Cr incurred in Q2FY26 (one-time: facility shutdowns, employee exits), dragging reported consolidated PAT to -₹50 Cr (vs. +₹91 Cr QoQ). Total FY26 costs expected within ₹300 Cr envelope, but temporary EBITDA/PAT suppression.
  • Margin Pressure QoQ: Express/PTL service EBITDA margins dipped to 13.5% (from 14.8% in Q1FY26) due to festive capacity build-up (extra week of costs from GST shift delaying sales). Express yield fell 11% QoQ on lighter Ecom mix.
  • GST Rate Hike Impact: ~8% of invoicing shifted from 12% to 18% GST; fleet vendors also up to 18%. Minor WC hit expected, but no material P&L/competitive loss.
  • Leadership Transition: CFO Amit Agarwal resigning (personal reasons, 13-year tenure); Vivek Pabari (current Head-Corporate Finance/IR) takes over Jan 1, 2026. Smooth handover noted, but execution risk.
  • Operational Disruptions: Heavy rains, holidays, GST amendment shifted volumes to late Sep/Oct, inflating peak costs.

Tailwinds (Positive Momentum)

  • Robust Volume Growth: Record 246 Mn Express parcels (+32% YoY, +18% QoQ); PTL 477K tons (+12% YoY). Sep/Oct hit 100 Mn+ monthly shipments, highest single-day 7.2 Mn.
  • Revenue/EBITDA Strength (Ex-Ecom): Services revenue ₹2,546 Cr (+16.3% YoY); EBITDA ₹150 Cr (5.9% margin, +163% YoY); PAT ₹59 Cr (2.2% margin).
  • Ecom Synergies Emerging: Acquisition (Jul 2025, ₹1,369 Cr) boosted market share; D2C/SME volumes +40% YoY. Network rationalized (retained 1.3 Mn sq ft); overheads down 85%.
  • Efficiency Gains: Net WC days ~16 (from 40+ in FY21); capex 6.6% of H1 revenue (trending to 4% long-term); corporate overheads stable at ~9.3% of revenue.
  • Strong Balance Sheet: Cash ₹4,223 Cr; H1 operating cash flow ₹215 Cr.

Growth Prospects

  • Scale from Ecom: Inorganic volumes + organic e-comm/festive demand; expect sustained share gains, Q3 volumes 100 Mn+/month.
  • Margin Expansion: Express/PTL to 16-18% service EBITDA by FY26-end (via utilization, productivity); Supply Chain at 12.8% (normalized 10%).
  • New Verticals: Rapid-commerce (₹80-100 Cr revenue near-term, NCR launch Q3); Delhivery Direct (₹28 Cr run-rate, app on 4 Mn phones, metro expansion FY26-end).
  • Festive/Peak Tail: Sep-Oct spillover; PTL yield +3% QoQ.
  • Network/Tech: 18,830 pincodes, 50 automated sorters (+11 QoQ), AI-driven ops; Supply Chain growth in e-comm/auto/durables.
  • Long-Term: Corporate overheads to 6-7% of revenue; capex decline supports ROIC.

Key Risks

  • Regulatory/Tax (High): ₹49 Cr GST demand (+interest +100% penalty) on rate interpretation (FY19-23); appeal expected, but escalation could impact cash/ops. Ongoing GST ambiguities.
  • Integration/Execution (Medium-High): Ecom non-express exit, client migration; any delays could extend costs beyond ₹300 Cr.
  • Macro/Competitive (Medium): E-comm slowdown, fuel inflation, competition (e.g., other integrators); festive seasonality (Q3 profitability key).
  • Financial (Medium): Lower finance income QoQ (post-Ecom payout, MTM gains absent); debt low but lease liabilities rising.
  • Operational (Low-Medium): Weather/holiday disruptions; heavy reliance on partners (64K agents).

Summary

Delhivery posted a strong operational Q2FY26 amid festive peaks and Ecom integration, with ex-acquisition metrics shining (16%+ revenue growth, record volumes, improving margins). Tailwinds from scale, efficiencies, and new services outweigh near-term headwinds like integration costs and CFO change, positioning for 16-18% service margins and robust FY26 growth. Prospects are bright (Ecom synergies, festive carryover), but GST litigation and execution risks warrant monitoring. Overall Outlook: Positive (Buy/Hold bias for long-term investors); target sustained 10-15% revenue CAGR with profitability inflection. Cash fortress (₹4,223 Cr) provides buffer.
