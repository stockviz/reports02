Analysis of NTPC Green Energy Limited (NTPCGREEN)

NTPC Green Energy Limited (NGEL), a subsidiary of NTPC Limited, is a pure-play renewable energy company focused on solar, wind, and emerging areas like green hydrogen. Listed via IPO in Nov 2024 (scrip: NTPCGREEN), it reported strong H1 FY26 results (ended Sep 30, 2025) with consolidated revenue up 19% YoY to ₹1,293 Cr and PAT up 75% to ₹307 Cr. Capacity grew to 7.64 GW post 75.5 MW solar commissioning. Key themes: aggressive capex (CWIP ₹15,373 Cr), subsidiary/JV-driven growth, and high leverage.

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Capacity Expansion & Project Wins: Recent 75.5 MW commissioning from 1,255 MW Khavda-I solar (Gujarat); total group capacity +76 MW to 7,639 MW. New 140 MW RTC RE win by Ayana (subsidiary of ONGC-NTPL JV) at competitive ₹4.35/kWh tariff via REMC tender—signals bidding strength.
  • Financial Momentum: Consolidated revenue/PAT growth (H1: +19%/+75% YoY); EBITDA margins ~55-60% supported by operations and interest on IPO proceeds (₹27.8 Cr in Q2). IPO ₹10,000 Cr fully deployed (₹7,500 Cr to subsidiary debt repayment; rest corporate).
  • Policy & Ecosystem Support: India’s RE push (500 GW target by 2030); CPSU scheme benefits; strong NTPC parentage (backing capex/debt). Diverse subsidiaries/JVs (e.g., NTPC REL 100%, Ayana via JV) enable scale.
  • Revenue Diversification: Consultancy/project fees (₹7-20 Cr/quarter); interest income from liquid funds.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • High Debt & Finance Costs: Consolidated non-current borrowings ₹19,709 Cr (+14% QoQ); H1 finance costs ₹399 Cr (31% of revenue). Standalone debt ₹7,732 Cr; repayments ongoing but interest burden erodes margins.
  • Capex Intensity: Massive investments (standalone equity in subs ₹14,242 Cr, +32% YoY; consolidated capex ₹5,943 Cr H1). CWIP surged 10% to ₹15,373 Cr—strains cash (investing outflow ₹2,668 Cr H1).
  • Subsidiary Dependence: 80%+ assets in subs/JVs; parent profits tied to equity method (₹34 Cr H1 share). Unreviewed sub/JV results add opacity.
  • Margin Pressure: Depreciation ₹485 Cr H1 (38% of revenue); Q2 EBITDA softer vs Q1 due to seasonal factors.

Growth Prospects

  • High Visibility Pipeline: 1,255 MW Khavda-I ramp-up; RTC/hybrid focus (e.g., 140 MW win). Group targets multi-GW scale; green hydrogen hub (Andhra Pradesh lease) positions for future tech.
  • Revenue CAGR Potential: 20-30%+ over FY26-28 from capacity addition (7.6 GW base), PPA stability, and consultancy. PAT scalability as projects COD (e.g., full Khavda).
  • Strategic Levers: JVs with ONGC/IOCL/MAHAGENCO for RTC/storage; IPO war chest deployed. Consensus implies 25-35% EPS CAGR to FY28 on 12-15 GW capacity goal.
  • Valuation Upside: Trades at ~30-40x FY26 EPS (peer avg 25x); RE sector rerating on execution.

Key Risks

Risk Category Description Mitigation
Execution Delays in large projects (e.g., Khavda balance 1,180 MW); land/acquisition hurdles. Green H2 ROU amortization (₹48 Cr cumulative) despite unfinalized plan (auditor emphasis). CPSU scheme; NTPC expertise.
Financial/Leverage Debt:Equity ~1.3x; rising rates could spike costs (31% of rev). Negative FCF from capex (H1 operating ₹1,103 Cr offset by outflows). IPO utilization for delever; grants (₹55 Cr H1).
Regulatory/Tariff PPA renegotiation; change-in-law (e.g., storage mandates). Competitive bidding squeezes tariffs (₹4.35/kWh low-end). Govt-backed PPAs; diversified portfolio.

Macro Policy shifts (subsidies); forex (minimal, domestic). Aligned with net-zero goals.

Overall Summary: NGEL exhibits robust tailwinds from India’s RE boom and execution (capacity +1%, profits +75% H1), positioning for 20-30% growth via 10+ GW pipeline. However, headwinds from debt/capex strain balance sheet (leverage key watch). Risks center on project delivery/debt sustainability; success hinges on CODs and refinancing. Recommendation: Positive long-term hold (target 15-20% upside in 12M); monitor Q3 debt metrics. Comparable: Tata Power RE, Adani Green.
