Summary Analysis of NTPC Green Energy Limited (NTPCGREEN)

Overview: NTPCGREEN, a subsidiary of NTPC Ltd., focuses on renewable energy (solar, wind, green H2) with a group capacity of ~7.64 GW as of Nov 2025 (post 75.5 MW Khavda-I solar commissioning). H1 FY26 consolidated results show revenue growth to ₹1,293 Cr (+19% YoY), PAT to ₹308 Cr (+74% YoY attributable to owners), driven by operations and equity investments. High CWIP (₹15,373 Cr consolidated) signals expansion, with IPO proceeds fully deployed. Recent 140 MW RTC RE bid win adds momentum. Backed by NTPC, it benefits from scale but faces debt and execution challenges.

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Capacity Ramp-Up & Execution: 75.5 MW commissioned (part of 1,255 MW Khavda-I), boosting group capacity 1% to 7,639 MW. Strong project pipeline via subsidiaries (e.g., NTPC REL) and JVs.
  • Financial Momentum: Consolidated revenue/PAT growth amid rising ops (₹612 Cr Q2 revenue). Healthy CFO (₹1,103 Cr H1) supports capex. Other income from IPO interest (₹28 Cr Q2) aided margins pre-utilization.
  • Policy & Bid Wins: Aligns with India’s RE targets; 140 MW RTC RE win (₹4.35/kWh tariff) via Ayana (JV sub) validates competitive edge.
  • Parent Support & Diversification: NTPC backing ensures funding/execution; mix of solar, consultancy fees (₹7 Cr Q2), and green H2 hub.
  • Asset Quality: PPE up 21% YoY to ₹26,343 Cr; no material audit qualifications.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • High Debt Burden: Consolidated non-current borrowings ₹19,709 Cr (+14% YoY); finance costs ₹399 Cr H1 (32% of revenue). Standalone borrowings ₹7,732 Cr.
  • Margin Pressure: Depreciation ₹485 Cr H1 (high asset base); Q2 standalone PBT dipped 45% QoQ to ₹121 Cr due to seasonal ops variability.
  • Cash Utilization: IPO funds fully used (₹7,500 Cr in NTPC REL debt repayment); investing cash outflow ₹2,668 Cr H1 on capex/investments.
  • Subsidiary Dependence: 49% standalone assets in sub/JV equity (₹14,242 Cr); group revenue skewed to REL (unreviewed sub with ₹28k Cr assets).
  • Interest Tapering: Q2 other income down 38% QoQ as IPO proceeds deployed.

Growth Prospects

  • High Visibility Pipeline: CWIP ₹15k+ Cr (37% of assets) across solar/RE projects; CPSU scheme (Khavda) and JVs (e.g., ONGC NTPC Green) expanding to 10+ GW medium-term.
  • Strategic Wins: RTC RE (140 MW), consultancy (₹15 Cr H1), green H2 hub (33-yr lease, ₹48 Cr amortized to CWIP).
  • Scale & Targets: Group capacity doubled from FY25; potential 20-30% CAGR via subs (e.g., Rajasthan/UP Green Energy) and bids. RE demand tailwind from India’s 500 GW goal.
  • Efficiency Gains: Debt repayment via IPO improving leverage; equity method JVs adding ₹34 Cr H1 profit share.

Investment Outlook: Strong tailwinds from RE boom and execution support 15-20% EPS CAGR near-term, but monitor debt (target <1x) and project timelines. Positive on capacity growth; risks tilted execution/debt. Trading multiple ~30-40x FY26E EPS justified by prospects.
