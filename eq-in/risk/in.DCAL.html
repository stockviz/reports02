Summary Analysis for Dishman Carbogen Amcis Limited (DCAL)

DCAL, a global CDMO with operations in India, Switzerland, France, China, UK, and Netherlands, reported strong H1 FY26 profitability (EBITDA up 64% YoY to 21.3% margins, PAT turnaround to INR 887 Cr) driven by high-margin late-phase CDMO work (e.g., Phase 3, Japanese ADC supplies) and Marketable Molecules growth (up 34% YoY). Revenue was flat (up 3.6% YoY) due to CDMO deferrals, but H2 guidance points to 8-10% FY26 revenue growth and 20% EBITDA growth. Key focus: ADC/bioconjugation expansion, Bavla/France ramps, and India-Switzerland integration. Net debt reduced to CHF 141M; recent refinancing adds CHF 40M (expandable to CHF 70M).

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Margin Expansion & Profitability Turnaround: CDMO margins up 726 bps YoY to 21.3% (Q2 at 25.3%) from late-phase/HPAPI work; MM margins doubled YoY. Demonstrates operating leverage.
  • Strategic Progress: GMP for France (ramping late-phase DP); Shanghai license; Celonic collab closes ADC gap (antibody + linker/payload + DP). Unified sales/finance/IT with India boosts Bavla pipeline.
  • Regulatory Wins: Naroda FDA-approved (EIR received); French GMP unlocks RFPs; Bavla EDQM nearing.
  • Demand Momentum: Strong ADC/oncology pipeline (12 late Phase III, 28 commercial molecules); CHF 250M order book (CHF 150M dev., CHF 100M comm.). CPHI buzz; Japanese co-investments (CHF 25M+).
  • Financial Flexibility: Syndicated facility enhanced to CHF 162M + EUR 50M (led by UBS); CapEx on track (INR 200-210 Cr FY26).
  • Market Tailwinds: Oncology/ADC boom (global spend CAGR 44% to 2029); specialty meds to 46% of spend.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Revenue Volatility: Q2 CDMO down 28% YoY (deferrals); H1 flat. Commercial shipments back-loaded to H2.
  • Loss-Making Assets: French EBITDA breakeven at EUR 18M (peak EUR 45M, 35% margins in 4 years); Bavla utilization 20-25% (target INR 800 Cr in 3 years).
  • High Costs: Finance costs up (INR 851 Cr H1; India at 10.5%); employee expenses steady at INR 685 Cr. FX translation hits Swiss costs.
  • Working Capital Pressure: ~80 days; inventory buildup for client mandates.
  • Integration Delays: Bavla ramp from FY27; refurb CapEx INR 50-60 Cr/year.
