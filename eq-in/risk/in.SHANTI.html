Summary Analysis for Shanti Overseas (India) Ltd (NSE: SHANTI)

Shanti Overseas (India) Ltd (CIN: L74110MP2011PLC025807; ISIN: INE933X01016) is a small-cap company primarily engaged in trading (stock-in-trade purchases dominate expenses). FY25 (ended Mar 31, 2025) showed significant losses (standalone net loss ₹247.66 lakhs; consolidated ₹279.25 lakhs) amid high revenue (₹2,265.94 lakhs standalone) but poor margins due to inventory changes and costs. H1 FY26 (ended Sep 30, 2025, unaudited) marks a turnaround with modest profits (standalone ₹4.05 lakhs; consolidated ₹4.59 lakhs), driven by revenue growth (consolidated ops revenue up 36% YoY to ₹614.74 lakhs) and other income (₹228.29 lakhs, likely interest/deposits). However, operations remain weak in standalone (ops revenue just ₹32 lakhs), with heavy reliance on subsidiary Shaan Agro Oils (100% owned, contributing ~₹583 lakhs revenue). Balance sheet shows stable equity (standalone ~₹1,691 lakhs) but rising receivables (consolidated ₹671 lakhs vs ₹244 lakhs prior) signal liquidity strain. Cash flows mixed (standalone ops +₹18 lakhs; cons -₹17 lakhs). Recent compliance includes ROC-approved AGM extension to ~Dec 30, 2025 and EGM approval for minor promoter group reclassification (330 shares).
