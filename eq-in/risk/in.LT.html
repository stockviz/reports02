Larsen & Toubro (LT) Analysis: Headwinds, Tailwinds, Growth Prospects, and Key Risks

Overview: Larsen & Toubro (LT), India’s leading EPC player (USD 30B multinational), reported stellar H1 FY26 results (ended Sep 30, 2025): order inflow +39% YoY to ₹2.10 lakh Cr (intl. 59%), order book +15% to ₹6.67 lakh Cr (intl. 49%), revenue +13% to ₹1.32 lakh Cr, PAT +22% to ₹7,543 Cr. Q2 alone saw ₹1.16 lakh Cr orders (+45% YoY). Recent wins include Saudi PT&D (Large* orders) and BvS10 defense contract with BAE Systems. Intl. revenue at 54-56%. FY26 outlook positive amid India capex surge and ME stability.

  • Order Momentum: Explosive inflows across Infra (₹52,686 Cr Q2, +6%), Energy (+100% YoY ultra-mega hydrocarbon wins), Renewables/PT&D. Defense entry via BvS10 (indigenous mfg. at Hazira).
  • Robust Order Book: ₹6.67 lakh Cr provides 3-4 yr visibility; execution ramp-up driving revenue growth.

  • Geo-Diversification: Intl. dominance (ME/Saudi NREP focus); India capex tailwinds (metro, hydel, data centers).
  • Strategic Moves: Hyderabad Metro divestment (Lakshya 2026 exit from concessions); tech-led growth (semiconductors, electrolysers).
  • Financial Health: EBITDA 10%, ROE implied strong; cash from ops +ve (₹3,816 Cr H1); debt/equity stable at 1.09.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Margin Pressure: Infra slowed by monsoons/water projects; Energy dip (7.3% vs 8.9%) from project variations/closures; overall EBITDA % flat at 10%.
  • Standalone Volatility: Q2 loss (₹3,591 Cr PAT) due to ₹5,413 Cr impairment on Hyderabad Metro SPV (divestment-related).
  • Execution Delays: Water/effluent projects lagged; high base in Infra orders.
  • Macro Drag: Global GDP ~3% (trade wars, conflicts); Others segment revenue -14% (realty handovers down).
  • Development Projects: EBIT down (provision for Nabha Power sub-judice matter).

Growth Prospects

  • High Visibility: Order book supports 12-15% revenue CAGR FY26-28; intl. 50%+ share.
  • Sector Tailwinds:
    • India: GDP 6.5-7%; infra/renewables/metro/hydel capex.
    • ME/GCC: Oil rebound, grid upgrades (e.g., Saudi 380kV substations +420km lines).
    • Emerging: Defense (BvS10 scalable), green H2/electrolyser mfg., data centers, semiconductors.
  • Projections: Analysts eye 15%+ revenue/PAT CAGR; EBITDA margins 9-10.5%; ROE 15-18%.
  • Catalysts: More hydrocarbon/offshore wins; IT&TS retail lending growth (loan book +10% to ₹1.07 lakh Cr, 98% retail).
