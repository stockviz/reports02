Summary Analysis for KREBSBIO (Krebs Biochemicals & Industries Limited)

Company Overview: Krebs Biochemicals is a single-segment biochemical manufacturer (primarily APIs/intermediates) with its Vizag plant closed since Feb 2025 due to an Andhra Pradesh Pollution Control Board order. FY26 H1 results show severe distress: revenue collapsed 63% YoY to ₹965 lacs (Q2: ₹417 lacs), net loss widened to ₹1,147 lacs (EPS: -₹5.32), driven by fixed costs amid negligible operations. Balance sheet reflects negative net worth (~₹-15,770 lacs), high debt (₹21,943 lacs total borrowings), and negative operating cash flow. AGM resolutions (Sep 26, 2025) passed with strong promoter support, approving MD appointment and Ipca RPTs.

Headwinds (Key Challenges)

  • Plant Shutdown: Core Vizag unit non-operational since Feb 2025 (pollution violation), causing 70%+ revenue drop YoY (H1 FY26: ₹965 lacs vs. FY25 full-year ₹4,331 lacs). No near-term resolution indicated.
  • Revenue Collapse & Margin Erosion: Q2 revenue at historic low (₹417 lacs); negligible material consumption signals inventory liquidation, not production.
  • Escalating Losses & Cash Burn: H1 loss ₹1,147 lacs (vs. ₹1,041 lacs YoY); high fixed costs (employee ₹544 lacs, dep. ₹522 lacs, finance ₹539 lacs) persist. Op. cash flow: -₹168 lacs.
  • Debt Overhang: Borrowings up to ₹21,944 lacs (non-current ₹16,380 lacs); finance costs consume all income.

Tailwinds (Supportive Factors)

  • Governance Continuity: All 9 AGM resolutions passed (99%+ favor from promoters holding ~73% shares). New MD (Manish Kumar Jain) appointed; statutory/secretarial/cost auditors ratified.
  • Clean Audit: Unmodified limited review report; IND AS compliant.
  • Related Party Support: Ipca Laboratories RPTs approved (promoters abstained; 95% public yes) – potential for sales/supply revival.
  • Asset Base: PPE at ₹14,415 lacs provides collateral; minor inventory (₹577 lacs) liquidation sustains minimal revenue.

Growth Prospects

Low (Turnaround-Dependent): No organic growth visibility without plant restart. Prospects hinge on pollution compliance/resolution (e.g., via Ipca tie-up or capex ₹205 lacs in H1). Biochemicals sector tailwinds (API demand) exist, but execution risk high. FY26 revenue could stabilize at ₹1,500-2,000 lacs if partial ops resume; profitability unlikely before FY27 without debt restructure. Long-term: Potential promoter infusion or asset monetization for revival.

Investment Outlook: Deep value/distressed play (negative book value, low multiples on assets), but high bankruptcy risk. Monitor plant reopening updates, debt refinancing, and Q3 results. Avoid unless revival catalysts emerge.
