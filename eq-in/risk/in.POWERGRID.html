POWERGRID (Power Grid Corporation of India Ltd.) Analysis

Overview: POWERGRID, India’s largest power transmission utility (Govt stake ~51%), reported steady H1 FY26 results (PAT Rs 7,197 Cr, flat YoY) amid execution challenges. Strong visibility from Rs 1.52 lakh Cr order book (67% TBCB), driven by India’s energy transition. Recent bond raise (Rs 3,800 Cr) supports capex ramp-up. Minor governance hiccup (board composition fine Rs 10.86L total, waiver sought).

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Robust Order Pipeline & Govt Support: Rs 1.52 lakh Cr works-in-hand (up 22% YoY CAGR); FY26 capex guidance Rs 28-30k Cr (TBCB Rs 19k Cr). Massive opportunities: NEP (Rs 9.15 lakh Cr total, Rs 3.5 lakh Cr ISTS pending), Brahmaputra basin (Rs 6.4 lakh Cr by 2035+), RE evacuation (600 GW non-fossil by 2032), BESS (47 GW), green H2, data centers.
  • Operational Excellence: 99.83% availability (max incentives), global asset mgmt benchmarking (score 4.14/5), innovations (insulated cross arms, mobile GIS, indigenous HVDC).
  • Diversification: Consultancy revenue up 222% YoY (Rs 821 Cr, smart meters key); telecom Rs 570 Cr (100% backbone availability).
  • Financial Strength: AAA domestic ratings, D/E 58:42 stable, 102% billing realization, PAT resilient (RoE ~15.5% constant).
  • ESG Momentum: 8 “pink” substations (women-led), 33% RE power use, 90% waste recycling; awards for HR/CSR.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Execution Delays: RoW issues (landowner resistance) delaying capitalization (H1 Rs 4.6k Cr vs FY26 target Rs 20k Cr). Govt guidelines (Mar’25) aiding (market-rate compensation), but valuer process adds 1-2 months/state adoption slow.
  • Revenue Pressure: Flat H1 income (Rs 23,115 Cr) despite capex ramp; asset maturity (post-12yr depr drop 5.3%→1.2%, interest normalization) reduces revenue ~9% on Rs 15k Cr old assets (~Rs 1,350 Cr hit). TBCB shift lowers revenue yield (11-12% vs RTM 17%).
  • Supply Chain Bottlenecks: Transformer/GIS/HVDC shortages (1+ yr lead); mitigated by bulk advance buys, but Leh-Ladakh pivot (HVDC→AC) hikes cost 20k→30k Cr, timeline to 2029.
  • State Dues: Rs 2,899 Cr outstanding (down from Rs 3,151 Cr), mainly Tamil Nadu/UP/Telangana (15 days receivable).

Growth Prospects

  • Capex/Capitalization Ramp: FY26: capex Rs 28-30k Cr, cap Rs 20k Cr; FY27: capex Rs 35k Cr, cap Rs 25-26k Cr; FY28: capex Rs 45k Cr, cap Rs 28k Cr. 2-yr lag from capex to cap.
  • TBCB Momentum: Rs 45k+ Cr bid pipeline (e.g., Khavda-South Olpad, Barmer-Kalamb HVDC soon); 67% HVDC in Brahmaputra.
  • Non-Core Upside: Consultancy (footprint 25 countries, 15 new domestic orders); telecom growth; data centers (1k racks by Q4 FY26).
  • Long-Term: $10T economy, OSOWOG global integration; RTM upgrades (e.g., Sasaram HVDC Rs 3,440 Cr indigenous).

Key Risks

Risk Category Details Mitigation
Execution RoW/supply delays; project changes (e.g., Leh AC shift). Govt guidelines, advance procurement, 4-5yr HVDC timelines.
Regulatory/Financial CERC O&M caps (past under-recovery normalized); EESL drag (Rs 126 Cr H1 loss); board non-compliance fine (minor). Waiver sought (Govt Co); MAT tax keeps effective rate <20%.
Payment State dues volatility. Improving realization (102%); major from 3 states.
Market TBCB cycle slowdown; RE/battery tender losses. Rs 1.52L Cr visibility; re-bid participation.
Macro Equipment import curbs (China reliance low via local mfg). Indigenous push (Atmanirbhar).

Summary: POWERGRID’s growth story intact with structural tailwinds from India’s RE/transmission buildout (Rs 3-6L Cr opportunities), but near-term headwinds cap FY26 growth (flat revenue, delayed cap). PAT resilient at Rs 14-15k Cr FY26E; RoE model ensures equity returns. Buy/Hold for long-term infra play; monitor RoW resolution & TBCB awards. Target multiple: 15-18x FY27E EPS (~Rs 18-20), implying 12-15% CAGR PAT to FY28.
