Analysis of Shaily Engineering Plastics Ltd (SHAILY)

Company Overview: Shaily Engineering Plastics Ltd is India’s largest exporter of engineering plastic components, operating 7 facilities in Gujarat with 200+ injection molding machines. Key segments: Healthcare (~21% of FY25 revenue, growing rapidly), Consumer (~71%), Industrial (~8%). FY25 revenue: ₹787 Cr (+22% YoY), EBITDA: ₹178 Cr (+45%), PAT: ₹93 Cr (+63%). Exports: ~75% of revenue. Strong credit rating (CARE A+/A1).

Tailwinds (Positive Factors)

  • Healthcare Segment Boom: Transformative growth driver (H1FY26: +178% YoY revenue share). Secured 8+ new projects (GLP-1, insulin, migraine therapies) with global pharma majors. Patented pen platforms (e.g., Shaily Axiom Max), 100+ meetings at CPHI/PODD. Capacity expansion: +19 machines in Q2FY26, targeting 40-50M units by Dec 2026 (₹150 Cr capex). Expected to reach 30-35% revenue mix in 2 years.
  • Robust Financial Performance: H1FY26 consolidated revenue ₹503 Cr (+36% YoY), EBITDA ₹152 Cr (+96%, margins +930 bps to 30.2%), PAT ₹92 Cr (+135%). Cash PAT +94%. ROCE 42.3% (+1,790 bps), ROE 32.7% (+1,420 bps). Debt/Equity low (0.3).
  • Export Leadership & Recognition: “Best Exporter Award” (FY24-25). 75%+ exports, stable forex gains. Subsidiaries in UK/UAE enhance global presence.
  • Diversified Portfolio & Efficiency: Consumer (+17% FY25) with new home furnishing/FMCG wins; Industrial (+12%) with auto knobs. Capacity utilization ~50% (to 80% by FY27). Margin expansion from mix shift, automation.
  • Operational Strength: 7 plants, ISO-certified (Class 8 cleanrooms for pharma). Exhibitions (CPHI, Cosmoprof) yielding orders.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • High Capex Intensity: H1FY26 investing outflow ₹90 Cr (PPE/capex). Total assets up 13% to ₹1,055 Cr, but could strain cash if ramps delay.
  • Working Capital Pressure: H1FY26 WC change -₹25 Cr (inventories +₹14 Cr, receivables +₹40 Cr). Trade payables slight dip.
  • Cost Pressures: Raw materials (polymers) ~41% of costs; power/fuel/employee expenses rising. Finance costs ₹8 Cr (H1).
  • Macro/Execution Delays: Virtual analyst meets note potential changes; exigencies in schedules.

Growth Prospects

  • High Visibility Pipeline: Healthcare order book strong (GLP-1 off-patent, biologics pipeline). New projects: 4 in Q2FY26 (GLP-1/other), consumer (5 projects), industrial (1 auto).
  • Capacity & Utilization Ramp: Post-expansion, utilization to 80% by FY27. Revenue potential: Healthcare to drive 20-25% CAGR; overall 20%+ guided.
  • Strategic Expansions: Dubai/UK subs for market access. Exhibitions yielding 100+ customer meets.
  • Margin/ROE Upside: EBITDA margins 30%+ sustainable; fixed asset turnover 2.0x. FY26 guidance implicit: Continued double-digit growth.
  • De-risked Model: 75% exports, diversified clients (marquee pharma/FMCG/auto), low debt.

Projected Trajectory: FY26 revenue ~₹950-1,000 Cr (20%+ growth), Healthcare-led. Long-term: 25-30% CAGR via pharma/devices.

Key Risks

Risk Category Description Mitigation
Execution/Operational Capex delays (₹150 Cr pharma expansion); capacity ramp to meet orders. Utilization risk if demand softens. Phased investments; proven track record (19 machines added Q2FY26).
Segment Concentration Healthcare ~21% but growing fast; GLP-1 dependency (regulatory approvals, competition). Diversification (Consumer 71%, Industrial 8%); 75% exports de-risks.
Forex/RM Volatility 75% exports exposed to USD/EUR; polymer prices (41% costs). Hedging (MTM gains noted); backward integration potential.
Geopolitical/Supply Chain China+1 shifts benefit but tariffs/logistics risks. Gujarat hubs optimize shipping to 40+ countries.
Financial WC strain (receivables up); capex funding (debt low but rising to ₹189 Cr). Strong ops cash ₹98 Cr (H1FY26); CARE A+ rating.
Regulatory/Competition Pharma compliance (ISO Class 8); generic erosion in GLP-1. Patented platforms; exclusive supplier status.
Other Employee costs up 36% YoY; no UPSI in analyst meets but market volatility. ESOPs; margin discipline.

Overall Summary: Strong Buy Case. Tailwinds from healthcare GLP-1 frenzy and exports dominate; Q2FY26 beat expectations with 34% revenue/134% PAT growth. Growth prospects excellent (20%+ CAGR) via capacity/monetization. Risks manageable (low debt, diversification). Monitor capex execution and RM inflation. Positive analyst intimation signals confidence. Target: 25-30% upside from current levels (as of Nov 2025 context).
