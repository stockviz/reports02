Summary Analysis for Eternal Limited (ETERNAL, Scrip: 543320)

Eternal Limited (formerly Zomato Limited) demonstrates robust top-line growth driven by its quick commerce segment (e.g., Blinkit), with consolidated H1 FY26 revenue surging ~130% YoY to ₹20,753 Cr, fueled by a shift to an inventory-led model (inventories up to ₹1,502 Cr). However, profitability remains muted at consolidated PAT of ₹80 Cr (down YoY), amid high operating costs and subsidiary losses. Standalone results are strong (PAT ₹1,293 Cr), reflecting investment income. Balance sheet expanded (assets ₹38,115 Cr), but cash dipped to ₹431 Cr with ongoing capex and leases. Recent developments include a minor GST order (₹18.4L, under appeal), investor roadshows (Nov 2025), and a new CSR foundation.

Tailwinds

  • Hyper-Growth in Quick Commerce: Q2 revenue exploded to ₹12,500 Cr (+161% YoY), driven by direct sales/inventory model (purchases ₹8,793 Cr in Q2). Blinkit and Hyperpure scaling, with acquisitions (e.g., OTPL/WEPL from Paytm for ticketing/events) adding synergies.
  • Investment Income Buffer: Standalone other income ₹841 Cr (H1), from mutual funds, bonds, and deposits. High liquidity (₹10,922 Cr non-current investments consolidated).
  • Capital Raising & Expansion: Prior QIP (₹8,300 Cr) and ESOP infusions bolster balance sheet. New wholly-owned Eternal Foundation for CSR enhances brand/image.
  • Investor Engagement: Upcoming physical meetings at CLSA/JM Financial forums signal confidence.

Headwinds

  • Thin Margins & Sub Losses: Consolidated PBT margins ~1% (₹217 Cr on ₹21,462 Cr income); 20+ subs/trust contributed ₹106 Cr H1 loss (unreviewed, but “not material”). High delivery (₹4,082 Cr H1), employee (₹1,691 Cr), and ad costs.
  • Cash Pressure: Net cash outflow (operating ₹38 Cr positive but down YoY); capex ₹788 Cr H1; lease payments ₹342 Cr; cash equivalents fell ₹235 Cr H1.
  • Inventory & Working Capital Strain: Inventories ballooned 8x YoY; negative WC changes (e.g., -₹1,344 Cr inventory) signal scaling risks.
  • Regulatory Drag: Ongoing GST disputes (e.g., ₹18.4L order; larger ₹441 Cr on delivery charges for 2019-23, under appeal—“strong case” per mgmt/external experts).

Growth Prospects

  • Quick Commerce Dominance: Model shift positions ETERNAL as grocery leader; H1 revenue mix shows acceleration (purchases/changes in stock-in-trade ~50% of revenue). Potential for 100%+ YoY growth if execution sustains.
  • Diversification: Food delivery stable; Hyperpure/Zomato Entertainment scaling; new ticketing/events from acquisitions. Subs like Blinkit Foods, Zomato Payments add verticals.
  • Profitability Ramp: Standalone EPS ₹1.42 (H1); consolidated could improve with scale/optimizations. FY25 audited PAT ₹527 Cr sets base for FY26 outperformance.
  • M&A/Investments: History of bolt-ons (e.g., 2024 Paytm assets); ₹24,533 Cr standalone investments for future yields.

Key Risks

  • GST/Regulatory Exposure: ₹441 Cr+ demands (with interest/penalty) on delivery charges; uncertainty noted by auditors (Note 6). Appeals pending; adverse rulings could hit ₹400-500 Cr + ongoing SCNs.
  • Execution in Quick Commerce: High capex (PPE ₹1,431 Cr), inventory writedowns (₹18 Cr H1), and sub losses (e.g., Blinkit accumulated ₹3,200 Cr); competition from Zepto/Instamart intense.
  • Cash Burn & Leverage: Negative financing CF (₹344 Cr H1); lease liabilities up to ₹3,351 Cr; declining cash could constrain growth if ops CF weakens.
  • Sub Impairment: Losses in Hyperpure (₹998 Cr accum.), Entertainment (₹157 Cr), Blinkit; mgmt commitment to fund raises going-concern flags.
  • Macro/External: Inflation on delivery/ads, forex volatility (OCI items), muted consumer spending; 22 subs expose to global ops risks.

Overall Outlook: Strong growth trajectory (revenue tailwind dominant), but profitability/cash headwinds cap upside. Bullish on quick commerce if GST resolves favorably and margins expand to 5-10%. Monitor Q3 results for sustained momentum; target multiple: 8-12x FY27E EV/Sales.
