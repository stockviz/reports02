Summary Analysis for Balkrishna Industries Ltd (BALKRISIND)

Balkrishna Industries Ltd (BIL), a leading Indian manufacturer of off-highway tires (OTR segment for mining, agriculture, construction), released unaudited Q2/H1 FY26 results (ended Sep 30, 2025) on Oct 31, 2025. Key Financial Highlights (Consolidated): - H1 Revenue: ₹5,153 Cr (flat YoY vs ₹5,134 Cr); Q2 down 8.6% YoY to ₹2,393 Cr (Q1 up 5.4% YoY). - H1 PBT/PAT: Down 32%/33% YoY to ₹752 Cr/₹561 Cr (EPS ₹29 vs ₹43); margins compressed (OPM 12.4% vs 17.8%). - Balance Sheet: Robust net worth ~₹10,443 Cr; D/E 0.35x; current ratio 1.02x. - Cash Flow: Operating inflow ₹1,263 Cr (strong); heavy capex outflow ₹1,684 Cr; net cash (decrease) ₹5 Cr. - Dividend: 2nd interim ₹4/share (200%; record date Nov 7; payout ~₹77 Cr). - Outlook Note: Capex on track; upcoming investor meets (Nov 19, CLSA, Mumbai; no UPSI).

Overall, results reflect demand softness and cost pressures amid global cyclical slowdown, but strong fundamentals support resilience.

Tailwinds

  • Financial Strength: Low leverage (D/E 0.35x, total debt/assets 0.22x), high interest coverage (74x H1), and net worth growth support aggressive capex (~₹1,675 Cr H1 standalone, on schedule for capacity expansion).
  • Cash Generation: Robust OCF (₹1,263 Cr H1) funds growth internally; dividend policy (400% FY26 so far) signals confidence.
  • Segment Leadership: Dominant in high-margin OTR tires (single reportable segment); global subsidiaries (Europe, USA, Canada) contribute ~8% revenue with forex hedges.
  • Macro Support: Expected recovery in mining/construction (e.g., India infra boom); export incentives and EPCG benefits.

Headwinds

  • Demand Weakness: Flat H1 revenue, Q2 decline driven by softer volumes (esp. exports); inventory build-up and FX headwinds (unrealized losses ₹138 Cr H1).
  • Margin Compression: Raw material costs stable but other expenses surged (Q2 ₹769 Cr, up YoY); operating margin down to 12.4% (vs 17.8% YoY); higher employee/depreciation costs from capex.
  • Working Capital Strain: Current ratio dipped to 1.02x; trade payables down, inventories up slightly.
  • External Pressures: Global slowdown in ag/mining; elevated capex amid moderating ROE.

Growth Prospects

  • Capacity Expansion: Ongoing capex to boost output (expected completion FY26); positions for 10-15%+ volume growth on mining/infra recovery.
  • Export/Subsidiary Leverage: ~70% export-oriented; subsidiaries growing (H1 contribution stable); potential from US/EU infra spending.
  • Margin Recovery: Cost discipline + pricing power in niche OTR could lift OPM to 16-18%; EPS growth to ₹85+ FY26 on normalization.
  • Valuation Upside: Trading at reasonable multiples (post-results); dividend yield attractive; analyst interactions signal visibility.

Projected Growth: 8-12% revenue CAGR FY26-28 (consensus-like), driven by capex fruition and sector tailwinds; PAT growth 15%+ post-H1 normalization.
