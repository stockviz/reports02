CHOLAFIN (Cholamandalam Investment and Finance Company Ltd.) – Investment Analysis Summary

Stock Codes: NSE: CHOLAFIN | BSE: 511243
Reporting Period: Quarter and Half Year Ended 30 September 2025 (Q2 and H1 FY25-26)

Overview

Cholamandalam Investment and Finance Company Ltd. (CHOLAFIN) reported strong financial performance in H1 FY26, with solid AUM and earnings growth across multiple segments. While profitability remains robust, asset quality shows slight deterioration, signaling short-term concerns. The company maintains a solid capital base, strong liquidity, and diversified loan book, positioning it for long-term growth in India’s growing non-banking financial space.

🔍 Key Financial Highlights
  • AUM Growth: ₹2,14,906 Cr (+21% YoY)
  • Net Income: ₹4,075 Cr (Q2, +26% YoY); ₹7,939 Cr (H1, +27% YoY)
  • PAT: ₹1,155 Cr (Q2, +20% YoY); ₹2,291 Cr (H1, +20% YoY)
  • ROE: 18.11% (Q2); 18.47% (H1) — healthy for an NBFC
  • CAR (Capital Adequacy Ratio): 20.00% (well above regulatory minimum of 15%)
  • Liquidity Position: ₹17,516 Cr (including cash of ₹16,991 Cr and undrawn credit lines)
🚀 Tailwinds (Growth Enablers)

1. Strong AUM Expansion (21% YoY)

  • AUM grew from ₹1,77,426 Cr (Sep-24) to ₹2,14,906 Cr (Sep-25), driven by consistent disbursements.
  • Reflects growing market share and investor confidence in brand and distribution.

2. Segment-Wise Growth Across Key Verticals

  • Loan Against Property (LAP): AUM +33% YoY — highest growth segment, indicating strong demand for secured credit.
  • Home Loans: +28% YoY AUM growth; disbursements rising steadily; tapping into housing finance demand.
  • SME Loans: AUM up 28% YoY — supports MSME-focused economy and diversifies risk.
  • Secured Business & Personal Loans (SBPL): AUM +57% YoY — fastest-growing segment with low risk profile (backed by collateral).
  • Even Vehicle Finance (VF), the largest segment (₹1.07L Cr AUM), grew 17% YoY, outperforming industry peers amid auto cycle recovery.

3. Healthy Profitability Metrics

  • Sustained 20% YoY PAT growth, despite rising finance costs.
  • ROE > 18%, indicating efficient capital utilization.
  • PBT-ROA at 3.1% (H1 FY26), stable performance in asset productivity.

4. Strong Capital and Liquidity Buffer

  • CAR of 20% provides resilience against credit shocks.
  • Tier-I capital at 14.59% (Common Equity Tier-I: 13.93%) — well above regulatory thresholds.
  • Liquidity position of ₹17,516 Cr reduces refinancing risk in turbulent interest rate environments.
  • ALM (Asset-Liability Mismatch) is compliant — no negative mismatches.

5. Diversified Loan Portfolio

  • Reduces dependence on any single product or risk category.
  • Growing footprint in higher-margin, secured products (LAP, SBPL, Home Loans) improves overall risk-adjusted returns.
⚠️ Headwinds (Challenges & Risks)

1. Deterioration in Asset Quality

  • Stage 3 (NPA > 90 days) rose to 3.35% (from 3.16% in Jun-25).
  • GNPA increased to 4.57% (from 4.29%), NNPA to 3.07% (from 2.86%).
  • Loan losses rose sharply:
    • ₹897 Cr (Q2 FY26) vs ₹624 Cr (Q2 FY25) — +44% YoY
    • ₹1,779 Cr (H1) vs ₹1,205 Cr — +48% YoY

Interpretation: Rising provisioning indicates stress in certain portfolios, possibly in CSEL (Consumer & Small Enterprise Loans) where AUM declined YoY (₹13,952 Cr vs ₹14,175 Cr). This segment likely carries higher unsecured credit risk.

2. Margin Pressure from Rising Finance Costs

  • Finance cost increased 19% YoY (H1) — faster than revenue growth (23%).
  • Net interest margin (implied) may be under pressure unless yield on assets improves.
  • In a high or volatile interest rate environment, cost of borrowings could weigh further on earnings.

3. CSEL Portfolio Stagnation

  • AUM declined YoY (₹13,952 Cr vs ₹14,175 Cr), and disbursement growth appears to have slowed.
  • Given higher losses in this segment, CHOLA may be tightening underwriting — impacting volume growth.

4. Macro Risks: Inflation & Rate Volatility

  • As an NBFC dependent on wholesale funding, rate hikes or risk-aversion in debt markets could increase funding costs.
  • Consumer sentiment, especially in retail and SME segments, may weaken if inflation persists.

Conclusion:
CHOLAFIN is executing well on growth, especially in secured and resilient segments, but asset quality is emerging as a key watchpoint. The company has the financial strength to absorb near-term stress, making it a potential contrarian opportunity if it stabilizes credit metrics. For conservative investors, wait for stabilization in Stage 3 trends; for growth-oriented investors, the current price may offer a value entry with margin of safety given strong capital and diversified model.

Verdict: Hold (with active monitoring), Upgrade to Buy if next quarter shows stabilization in NPA and controlled loan loss growth.
