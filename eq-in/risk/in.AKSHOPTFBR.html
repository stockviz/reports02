Analysis of Aksh Optifibre Limited (AKSHOPTFBR)

Aksh Optifibre Limited, a telecom/optical fiber manufacturing and services company, reported Q2/H1 FY26 (ended Sep 30, 2025) results showing persistent losses amid high debt, operational challenges, and regulatory contingencies. Standalone H1 revenue declined 14% YoY to ₹5,770L, with PAT loss widening to ₹501L (vs ₹42L profit YoY). Consolidated losses were steeper at ₹951L PAT. Key metrics and outlook summarized below.

Headwinds (Key Challenges)

  • Persistent Operational Losses: Manufacturing segment (66% of H1 revenue) reported ₹310L loss (standalone), driven by high costs (materials ~61%, employee ~16%, depreciation ~9%). Services (34%) marginally positive but insufficient to offset. EBITDA negative before exceptional items.
  • Revenue Pressure: H1 standalone revenue down 14% YoY; consolidated down 15%. Inventory buildup (increase ₹213L) signals weak demand.
  • High Debt Burden: Finance costs up 19% YoY to ₹343L (standalone H1), with borrowings at ₹6,289L current (standalone). SARFAESI notices from Union Bank (NPA since Apr 2021) and HDFC Bank (₹3,663L claim) indicate recovery actions; DRT stay granted but unresolved.
  • Auditor Qualification: Non-provision for ₹2,857L liabilities (interest ₹2,017L + duty ₹840L) under Advance Authorization/EPCG schemes, pending amnesty. Could add ~₹18L net loss (H1) if materialized; bulk (~₹1,491L) pre-FY26.
  • Leadership Transition: Interim CFO appointed (Oct 2025), signaling potential instability post prior CFO exit.
  • Forex Delays: Outstanding FCNR balances beyond FEMA limits; extensions sought but unresolved.

Tailwinds (Supportive Factors)

  • QoQ Improvement: Q2 revenue up 14% QoQ (standalone); loss narrowed to ₹125L (vs ₹376L in Q1).
  • Cash Flow Resilience: Standalone operating cash flow positive at ₹419L (vs ₹252L YoY), aided by working capital efficiencies (e.g., payables up ₹470L).
  • Segment Diversification: Services revenue up 39% QoQ; less loss-prone than manufacturing.
  • Asset Base: Strong non-current assets (₹14,431L standalone); ₹2,030L assets held for sale could enable deleveraging.
  • Exceptional Gains: ₹22L in H1 provided minor buffer.

Growth Prospects

  • Moderate/Low: Telecom fiber demand (5G rollout, BharatNet) offers tailwind, but execution hampered by losses and debt. H1 segment revenue: Manufacturing flat YoY, Services down 6%. Potential from EPCG amnesty resolution and asset sales for debt reduction. Long-term: US CPA-led interim CFO may aid compliance/finance prudence. FY26 revenue growth unlikely >10% without restructuring; profitability hinges on cost cuts (e.g., depreciation down 11% YoY) and manufacturing turnaround. Equity erosion (other equity ₹293L standalone, negative ₹8,289L consolidated) risks capital raise/dilution.

Overall Summary: AKSHOPTFBR faces severe headwinds from debt distress, unprovisioned liabilities, and manufacturing weakness, outweighing modest tailwinds like cash positivity and QoQ growth. Growth prospects dim without debt resolution/asset monetization; trading at deep discounts (negative book value consolidated) reflects high risks.
