Analysis of Mirza International Limited (MIRZAINT)

Overview: Mirza International Limited, a leading Indian leather footwear exporter (brands: Red Tape, Oaktrak, etc.), reported unaudited Q2/H1 FY26 (Sep 2025) results showing revenue decline (consolidated Q2: ₹1,700 Cr, -14% YoY; H1: ₹2,117 Cr, -14% YoY) amid weak footwear demand, offset by an exceptional gain (₹18.6 Cr in Q1) driving H1 PBT to ₹26 Cr (+351% YoY). Profit after tax H1: ₹21.7 Cr. Standalone balance sheet remains solid (equity ₹4,803 Cr). Operations normalized post recent IT search (Sep 2025). Stock implications: Short-term pressure from topline weakness, but asset monetization and restructuring support resilience.

Headwinds (Challenges Pressuring Performance)

  • Revenue Contraction: Footwear (85%+ revenue) down 16% YoY in Q2 (₹1,537 Cr); Tannery down ~26%. Total ops income -14% YoY, signaling weak domestic/export demand, inventory buildup (positive change in FG/WIP), or pricing pressure.
  • Cost Pressures: Employee expenses volatile (H1 ₹2,589 Cr); finance costs up slightly. Gross margins squeezed by higher material costs (leather volatility).
  • Segment Weakness: Tannery loss-making (Q2: -₹39 Cr); unallocated drag from inter-segment eliminations.
  • Macro Factors: Potential export headwinds (global slowdown, competition from Vietnam/China), rupee appreciation impacting forex gains.

Tailwinds (Supportive Factors)

  • Profitability Resilience: Q2 PBT +13% YoY (₹49 Cr) despite revenue drop, aided by other income (₹60 lakhs) and cost controls. H1 boosted by exceptional item (likely asset sale, +₹1,861 lakhs).
  • Strong Balance Sheet: Standalone equity ₹4,803 Cr (77% of assets); low debt (current borrowings ₹18 Cr). Cash from ops H1: ₹177 Cr (consolidated).
  • Asset Monetization: Investing cash inflow ₹104 Cr (H1 cons.), driven by PPE sales (₹245 Cr), funding capex (₹80 Cr).
  • Accounting Benefits: Ind AS 116 adoption added ~₹9-18 lakhs to PBT via lease reassessment.
  • Restructuring: Approved amalgamation with RTS Fashion (Dubai subsidiary) to streamline ops/no new shares issued.

Growth Prospects

  • Medium-Term Potential: Export-focused (Footwear domestic/export mix); subsidiaries (RTS Dubai, Genesis US/India) add global reach. H1 segment revenue still ₹3,427 Cr; full-year FY25 base ₹5,827 Cr suggests recovery scope if demand rebounds.
  • Strategic Moves: Amalgamation could cut costs/boost efficiency. Brand portfolio (Red Tape, etc.) targets premium leather segment; unallocated revenue up (Q2 ₹29 lakhs).
  • Cash Generation: Positive FCF supports dividends/capex (CFO H1 ₹116 Cr cons.). FY26 revenue could stabilize at ₹10,000-11,000 Cr if footwear volumes recover 10-15%.
  • Upside Catalysts: Export incentives, leather cycle upturn, IT search closure (no disruption).

  • Operational: Subsidiary losses (RTS Dubai: -₹165 lakhs Q2; unaudited for some). High trade receivables (₹1,294 Cr standalone).
