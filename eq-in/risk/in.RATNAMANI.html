Summary Analysis for Ratnamani Metals & Tubes Ltd. (RATNAMANI)

Ratnamani Metals & Tubes Ltd., a leading manufacturer of stainless steel (SS) and carbon steel (CS) pipes/tubes, reported strong Q2 FY26 results (ended Sep 30, 2025): standalone revenue +5% YoY to INR 940 Cr (volumes up, offset by softer prices/mix); consolidated +23% YoY to INR 1,191 Cr (driven by subsidiaries RTL and RFSS). EBITDA margins held steady at ~18-20% standalone/consolidated. Order book strong at ~INR 2,000 Cr standalone (CS ~INR 1,300 Cr). Management guides for consolidated revenue of INR 7,500 Cr in 2-3 years (mid-teens margins), supported by capex and subsidiaries.

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Subsidiary Momentum: RTL (bearing rings) +40% YoY revenue (INR 96 Cr, EBITDA margin 13% vs 9%); targets 15-20% CAGR, FY26 revenue INR 360-380 Cr. RFSS (nuclear spools) INR 110 Cr (+ve turnaround, ~33% margins), FY26 guidance INR 300+ Cr, order book INR 500 Cr (all nuclear, execution by Q1 FY27).
  • Export Strength: Robust GCC demand (Saudi Aramco/SABIC, ADNOC/Abu Dhabi); Europe hydrogen/gas projects. Pioneered India’s first hydrogen-compliant CS welded pipes to Europe.
  • Operational Milestones/Certifications: Odisha Phase-I commissioned (Phase-II coating by Dec 2025); Kutch API monogram (CRA line pipes) and CII energy award. Switzerland entity now 100% owned.
  • Capex Execution: Multiple projects on track (Kutch CSAW/HSAW expansions by Dec 2025/Mar 2026; RFSS capacity to 4,000 MT by Jun 2026; RTL auto forging by Dec 2026; Saudi SS cold-finishing trials by Dec 2026). Saudi CR received, activities from Jan 2026.
  • Efficiency & Diversification: Stable margins via cost control; product innovation (hydrogen, carbon capture, clad pipes, electro-polished tubes); nuclear/thermal/O&G spools diversification.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Subdued Domestic Demand: CS segment (60% oil/gas line pipes, 40% water in new orders) weak due to govt payment delays/underbidding; fewer tenders vs 2022-23. Water pipes causing margin pressure/WC drag.
  • Pricing/Mix Pressures: Softer input prices/product mix capped standalone revenue growth (volumes up but flattish revenue expected); SS competition and overseas tariffs (US anti-dumping/high duties, Europe quota risks post-Jan 2026).
  • Margin Variability: Blended SS/CS margins down in select areas (e.g., CS water line pipes); RFSS rejection/rework minimal but scaling risks.
  • Working Capital Fluctuations: Receivables/inventories down (product mix shift from water pipes), but could rise with water/O&G orders.

Growth Prospects

  • Revenue Scaling: Standalone INR 6,000 Cr (full capacity utilization post-expansions); subsidiaries RTL INR 700-750 Cr, RFSS INR 600-700 Cr → consolidated INR 7,500 Cr in 2-3 years. FY26 standalone volumes steady, H2 execution similar to last year (INR 1,800-1,900 Cr execution + INR 400-500 Cr new orders).
  • High-Growth Segments: RFSS nuclear orders (70-80% focus, blending to 20-22% margins); RTL import substitution/China+1 (7-10% bearing market CAGR to 2035); Saudi/GCC SS cold tubes (mother hollows from India).
  • Strategic Expansions: Greenfield/brownfield capex (Odisha spiral/CSAW, Kutch HSAW) adds 1-2 lakh MT CS capacity; new products/applications (hydrogen, fertilizer grades, nuclear/thermal spools).
  • Long-Term Outlook: Positive industry (energy transition, infra); healthy bid pipeline (exports GCC/Europe); ROCE mid-teens at peak utilization.
