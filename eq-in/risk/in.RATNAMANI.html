Analysis of Ratnamani Metals & Tubes Ltd. (RATNAMANI) - Q2 FY26 Update

Ratnamani, a leading manufacturer of stainless steel (SS) and carbon steel (CS) pipes/tubes, reported resilient Q2/H1 FY26 performance amid subdued domestic demand. Standalone revenue grew 5% YoY to INR 940 Cr (volumes up, realizations soft); consolidated up 23% to INR 1,191 Cr, driven by subsidiaries (RTL +40%, RFSS strong traction). EBITDA margins held steady at ~18-20%. Order book at ~INR 2,000 Cr standalone (CS dominant at INR 1,100 Cr). Management guides for consolidated revenue of INR 7,500 Cr in 2-3 years (standalone INR 6,000 Cr).

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Subdued Domestic Demand: CS water pipes weak due to govt payment delays; overall tenders muted vs. FY23 peaks. O&G/ERW recovery slow.
  • Margin Pressures: Softer input prices/product mix capped revenue growth; specific pressure in CS water line pipes. SS faces competition.
  • Overseas Trade Barriers: US high tariffs/anti-dumping limit large-diameter pipes; Europe risks quota cuts/tariffs post-Jan 2026.
  • Execution Volatility: Subsidiaries like RFSS show quarterly swings (Q2 INR 110 Cr vs. prior lows); rejection/rework risks.

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Robust Order Inflows: CS domestic bookings INR 750 Cr (60% O&G/CGD/product pipelines); total CS book INR 1,100 Cr. Exports strong in GCC (Aramco/ADNOC/SABIC).
  • Subsidiary Momentum: RTL (bearing rings) INR 95.6 Cr (+40%, margins 13%); RFSS (nuclear spools) INR 110 Cr (book INR 500 Cr, FY26 guide INR 300+ Cr, margins ~33% in Q2).
  • Capacity/Milestones: Odisha Phase-I commissioned (Phase-II Q3); API cert for Kutch (CRA pipes); hydrogen-compliant pipes pioneered for Europe; Saudi CR received (cold finishing from Jan 2026).
  • Efficiency Gains: Working capital optimized; awards (CII energy); product innovations (clad/hydrogen pipes).

Growth Prospects

  • Revenue Expansion: 2-3 year target: Standalone INR 6,000 Cr; RTL INR 700-750 Cr; RFSS INR 600-700 Cr; Consolidated INR 7,500 Cr (mid-teens EBITDA).
  • Strategic Diversification: Nuclear/thermal/O&G spools (RFSS 70-80% nuclear); bearing rings import substitution (China+1, Europe shift); Saudi/GCC presence; downstream (coatings, bends).
  • Industry Tailwinds: GCC projects; nuclear push (RFSS book fully nuclear); hydrogen/CCUS opportunities; long-term O&G/water recovery.
  • Capex Pipeline: Ongoing (Odisha coating/CSAW/HSAW/RFSS/RTL/Saudi); optimal utilization to drive ROCE.
