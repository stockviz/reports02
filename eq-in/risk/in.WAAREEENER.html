Analysis of Waaree Energies Limited (WAAREEENER)

Waaree Energies Limited (WEL), India’s largest solar module manufacturer (~18.7 GW module capacity, 5.4 GW cells), reported record Q2 FY26 results (ended Sep 2025): Revenue ₹6,227 Cr (+70% YoY), EBITDA ₹1,567 Cr (+155% YoY, 25% margin), PAT ₹878 Cr (+134% YoY). H1 FY26 revenue ₹10,492 Cr (+50% YoY), order book 24 GW/₹47,000 Cr (55% India), pipeline 100+ GW. Company reaffirmed FY26 EBITDA guidance of ₹5,500-6,000 Cr amid expansions and acquisitions. Below is a structured summary of headwinds, tailwinds, growth prospects, and key risks based on the provided documents.

Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts)

  • Explosive Financial Momentum: Q2 production hit 2.64 GW modules; EBITDA margins expanded 840 bps YoY to 25% due to scale, favorable mix (47% domestic retail/EPC/enterprise). ROCE 41.8%, ROE 34.8% (H1 FY26 rolling). Dividend ₹2/share declared (record date Oct 24, 2025).
  • Robust Demand & Order Book: 692 MW new orders (570 MW India + 122 MW US). Backlog supports visibility; India solar additions 22 GW in H1 FY26, targeting 280 GW by 2030. US demand strong (18 GW H1 2025 additions, data centers to 176 GW by 2035).
  • Policy Support: India PLI (6 GW each module/cell/ingot), ALMM (cells now, ingots June 2028), lower GST (3-4% cost cut), state budgets (PM Surya Ghar). US IRA 45X credits (₹162 Cr recognized Q2 via US sub).
  • Strategic Expansions/Acquisitions: +3 GW modules (Chikhli); total FY26 module capex to 26.7 GW. New ₹8,175 Cr capex (BESS 20 GWh, electrolyser 1 GW, inverter 4 GW). Acquired Kotsons (64%, transformers ₹192 Cr), Meyer Burger US assets ($18.5 Mn, +1 GW modules), Racemosa (76%, smart meters ₹53 Cr).
  • Diversification: EPC/O&M, power infra (413 MW PPAs), retail (2,800+ points), global footprint (25 countries, 480 franchises).

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Margin Pressures from Scale-Up: High capex (₹25,000 Cr planned) led to H1 operating cash flow drop to ₹575 Cr (vs. ₹1,493 Cr prior). Inventory build-up (₹4,709 Cr) and receivables signal working capital strain.
  • US Market Uncertainties: CBP investigation (Sep 2025) on India-made module origins (since 2021 exports); no adjustments yet, but potential duties/penalties.
  • Tax/Regulatory Scrutiny: Recent Income Tax raid (Nov 2025, completed without disruption), but highlights compliance risks.
  • Competition & Costs: Declining Li-ion prices aid BESS, but solar module commoditization and China supply chain dominance could pressure pricing.

Growth Prospects

  • Multi-Year Visibility: Order book + pipeline imply sustained revenue; FY26 guidance intact despite H1 strength (52% of target). Production ramp (cells 0.6 GW Q2) to accelerate H2.
  • Integrated Green Ecosystem: Shifting from modules (83% FY25 revenue) to full-stack (BESS, H2, inverters, transformers, meters). India BESS target 236 GWh by 2032; US hybrid/RTC demand.
  • US Expansion: 2.6 GW modules (post-Meyer); IRA tailwinds position for 500 GW US solar by 2030.
  • Capex Execution: PLI-funded 10-15 GW ramps (FY26-27); BESS Phase-1 (3.5 GWh FY27). Potential Enel Green Power India acquisition (₹792 Cr, 413 MW assets).
  • Sustainability Edge: EPD-certified low-carbon modules, EcoVadis Gold; CSR (8,200+ students, 18,300 trees).

Projected Trajectory: 3-5x revenue growth potential to FY30 via diversification (target 20-30% non-solar), assuming execution.

Overall Summary: Strong Buy Case with tailwinds dominating (demand, policy, execution). Growth prospects high (integrated platform, US/India exposure), but monitor US CBP (key overhang) and capex funding. FY26 on track; stock likely re-rates on H2 beat. Risks mitigated by balance sheet (equity ₹11,703 Cr) and 41% ROCE.
