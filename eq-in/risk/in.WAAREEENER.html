Summary Analysis for WAAREEENER (Waaree Energies Limited)

Waaree Energies Limited, India’s largest solar module manufacturer (18.7 GW capacity), demonstrates robust growth in Q2/H1 FY26 amid strong domestic/international solar demand. Key metrics: Q2 revenue ~Rs6,227 Cr (+70% YoY), EBITDA Rs1,567 Cr (25.2% margin, +155% YoY), PAT Rs878 Cr (+134% YoY); Order book ~24 GW (Rs47,000 Cr). Company reaffirmed FY26 EBITDA guidance of Rs5,500-6,000 Cr. Below is a structured analysis of headwinds, tailwinds, growth prospects, and key risks based on the provided filings.

Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts)

  • Explosive Financial Momentum: Record Q2/H1 performance with revenue diversification (47% domestic retail/EPC/enterprise, 53% overseas). Strong margins from operational leverage, favorable mix, and scale.
  • Robust Order Book & Pipeline: 24 GW executed (~Rs47k Cr value); 100+ GW pipeline. Recent 140 MW domestic order adds visibility.
  • Aggressive Capacity Expansion: Modules to 26.7 GW (FY26), cells/ingot-wafer to 15.4/10 GW (FY27). US capacity at 2.6 GW (post-Meyer Burger acquisition). PLI-backed (6 GW integrated).
  • Diversification into High-Growth Verticals: Rs8,175 Cr additional capex for BESS (20 GWh), electrolyser (1 GW), inverters (4 GW). Acquisitions: 64% Kotsons (transformers, Rs192 Cr), 76% Racemosa (meters, Rs53 Cr).
  • Policy Support: India (solar to 280 GW by 2030, PM Surya Ghar/Kusum, lower GST 3-4% savings, ALMM for cells/ingots); US (45X credits, 500 GW solar by 2030, data center boom).
  • Shareholder Returns & Governance: Rs2/share interim dividend. AGM resolutions passed (some institutional dissent on director appointments).
  • Operational Resilience: IT search closed without issues; production ramp (2.64 GW modules/Q2).

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Regulatory Scrutiny: GST penalty Rs85L (on rooftop subsidy; appealed, no financial impact claimed). US CBP EAPA investigation (Sep 2025) on module origins/exports since 2021 – potential duties/penalties (no adjustments made yet).
  • Institutional Pushback: AGM saw opposition from public institutions (e.g., 60% against director reappointment; 12% against consolidated FS/secretarial auditor).
  • High Working Capital Intensity: Inventory build-up (Rs4,709 Cr), receivables (Rs1,310 Cr) strained cash from ops (H1 FY26: Rs575 Cr vs. FY25: Rs1,493 Cr).
  • Capex Burden: Rs25k Cr planned (incl. Rs8k Cr new); debt up (current borrowings Rs2,368 Cr). Cash burn on expansions (investing outflow Rs2,378 Cr H1).
  • Margin Pressure Risks: Declining Li-ion/battery costs aid BESS but solar module prices volatile.

Growth Prospects

  • Near-Term (FY26): Order execution + capacity ramps to drive revenue >Rs20k Cr (implied from guidance). H2 stronger per mgmt. US ramp (tax credits), domestic DCR/PLI tailwinds.
  • Medium-Term (FY27+): Full vertical integration (poly/ingot to EPC/BESS/H2/power infra). BESS market boom (India 236 GWh by 2032; global 1,848 GWh cumulative by 2030). Overseas ~40-50% mix.
  • Long-Term: “One-stop green energy platform” – capture wallet share in modules (16% India mfg), storage, EPC/O&M, infra. Pipeline execution + acquisitions position for Rs50k+ Cr revenue by FY28.
  • Triggers: ALMM enforcement (Jun 2028), green H2 PLI (90kT awarded), state budgets (UP/Maharashtra solar push).
