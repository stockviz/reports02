Analysis of Persistent Systems Limited (PERSISTENT)

Persistent Systems Limited (BSE: 533179, NSE: PERSISTENT) is an AI-led digital engineering and enterprise modernization services provider, with a focus on software product engineering, BFSI, healthcare, hi-tech, and emerging industries. FY26 Q2 results (ended Sep 30, 2025) show robust performance amid an AI pivot. Below is a structured analysis based on the provided filings, investor presentation, audited results, and press releases.

Tailwinds (Positive Factors Supporting Momentum)

  • Strong Financial Momentum: Q2FY26 revenue at $406.2M (+17.6% YoY, +4.2% QoQ; 22nd straight QoQ growth). TTM revenue $1.53B (+19.2% YoY). H1FY26 revenue $795.9M (+18.1% YoY). EBIT $5,837M (16.3% margin, +43.7% YoY), PAT $4,715M (13.2% margin, +45.1% YoY). Rupee revenue +23.6% YoY. Healthy cash conversion (OCF/PAT 114.3%), DSO 75 days, ROCE 45.5%, ROE 26.2%.
  • Bookings Strength: TTM ACV $1,612M, TCV $2,242M. Key wins in BFSI (e.g., tax software transformation), hi-tech (AI infra with SASVA™), healthcare (digital modernization).
  • Balanced Growth: All segments growing (Hi-Tech 14.3% YoY to $162M, BFSI $144M, Healthcare $105M). Geography stable (NA 80%). 55 clients >$5M revenue (up from 39 in FY21). Client concentration managed (top 10: 43.2%).
  • AI & Innovation Push: “Re(AI)magining™” strategy with IPs (SASVA™, iAURA, SITHub), 4,630+ AI certifications, 75+ patents. Partnerships (Microsoft Solutions Partner, AWS/Google Premier). New XT Studio launch for AI-powered UX co-innovation.
  • Operational Excellence: Utilization 88.2%, attrition 13.8% TTM (contained). 26K+ employees. ESG leadership (carbon neutral Scope 1/2, 29.8% women workforce, 77/100 NSE sustainability score).
  • Awards/Recognition: Everest “Growth Honor of the Year”, ISG CX Star Performer (multiple), Leader in Everest Talent Readiness PEAK Matrix.

Headwinds (Challenges Pressuring Performance)

  • Key Executive Exit: SVP Business Finance Vikram Tulsyan resigned (Nov 21, 2025, US time) for personal reasons; no material issues stated, but signals potential churn in leadership.
  • Stock Underperformance: 1-year TSR -11.5% (vs. Nifty IT +0.8%). Lagged peers amid broader IT sector caution.
  • Margin Pressures: SG&A at 16.9% (stable but elevated). Employee costs 54% of revenue; subcontracting rising (YoY +19%).
  • Macro/External: Heavy NA reliance (80%), forex volatility (avg. ₹88.2/$ in Q2FY26, hedges at ₹87.8/$). Potential wage inflation/attrition in India (85% effort mix).
  • Reclassifications/Restatement: Minor prior-period adjustments in financials (e.g., trade receivables reclassification), no material impact but indicates housekeeping.

Growth Prospects (Medium-Term Catalysts)

  • AI-Led Expansion (High Confidence): Sixth “orbit” strategy (2024+): Agentic AI, outcome-based models, platforms. TTM ACV signals pipeline strength. XT Studio scales CX/AI (expansion planned to US/Europe/APAC). 20/50 Fortune 500 clients; deepening relationships (e.g., 7/10 top tech cos).
  • Revenue Trajectory: 17.4% CAGR since IPO; 25.6% 4-yr CAGR (FY21-25). FY26 annualized run-rate ~$1.6B. Sequential growth in 22/22 quarters. Targets sustained 18-20% USD growth via AI, deals >$50M.
  • Margin Upside: EBIT margin to 16-18% potential via hyper-productivity (AI engineering, automation). ROCE/ROE >40%/25%.
  • M&A/Organic: Recent subsidiary transfers (Aepona), Arrka/Starfish acquisitions. $8.5B market cap supports bolt-ons.
  • Sustainability Edge: ESG focus aids large-client wins (e.g., SBTi net-zero by 2050).

Projected Growth: 18-22% USD revenue CAGR over FY26-28, driven by AI (20-30% of revenue potential), assuming macro recovery.
