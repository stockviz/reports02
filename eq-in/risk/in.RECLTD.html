REC Limited (RECLTD) Analysis: Headwinds, Tailwinds, Growth Prospects, and Key Risks

REC Limited, a Maharatna PSU and leading NBFC in power sector financing, reported robust H1 FY26 results (ended Sep 30, 2025): record profits of ₹8,877 Cr (+19% YoY), loan book at ₹5.82 lakh Cr (+7% YoY), disbursements of ₹1.15 lakh Cr (+27% YoY), and net NPA at 0.24% (improved from 0.88% YoY). The company targets a ₹10 lakh Cr loan book by FY30 with strong govt backing and sector tailwinds.

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Robust Financials & Capital Strength: Highest-ever H1 profits, NIM at 3.64%, RoNW 22.14%, CAR 23.74% (vs. RBI’s 15%). Dividend yield strong at ₹9.20/share for H1. Net worth at ₹82,739 Cr (+14% YoY).
  • Disbursement Momentum: H1 sanctions ₹2.5 lakh Cr (+34% YoY), committed pipeline supports 11-12% FY26 loan growth despite prepayments. Distribution (69% of disbursals) and renewables (11%) lead.
  • Govt Alignment & Schemes: Nodal agency for RDSS (19 states), PM Surya Ghar (17L households covered), RDSS, Saubhagya. Diversified portfolio: power (90%), infra/logistics (10%).
  • Asset Quality Improvement: Gross NPA down to 1.06%, Stage-2 assets -52% QoQ post ₹11,400 Cr Kaleshwaram prepayment. PCR 77% on NPAs.
  • Power Sector Boom: ₹46 lakh Cr capex opportunity (next 4-5 yrs): RE 500 GW (₹21L Cr), thermal 80 GW (₹5L Cr), storage/transmission (₹13L Cr). REC eyes 20-25% share.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Prepayment Pressure: ₹49k Cr inflows in H1 (e.g., Kaleshwaram, RBPF scheme) capped loan growth at 7% (would be 16% otherwise). Potential continuation in H2 could temper FY26 guidance.
  • Margin Squeeze: Yield 10.06%, CoF 7.17% (up from 7.12% QoQ due to forex hedging). Renewables competition may compress spreads; NIM guidance 3.5-3.75%.
  • State Sector Exposure: 86% portfolio in state utilities; DISCOM debt restructuring discussions could impact (though seen as long-term positive for capex).
  • Borrowing Costs: 80-85% fixed-rate debt, forex volatility (99% hedged, but hedging costs up ~8-10 bps). Flat borrowings QoQ despite growth.

Growth Prospects

  • Loan Book Expansion: 11-12% FY26 growth; ₹10L Cr by FY30 (renewables ~30%/₹3L Cr). H1 disbursals already strong; order book ₹2.5L Cr.
  • Sector Diversification: Infra/logistics at 10% AUM; new MoU with Shipping Ministry; renewables sanctioned 61 GW (3x in 5 yrs).
  • ESG & Green Push: RE loan book grown 3x; ESG policy, second report published. Green Energy Corridor role.
  • Subsidiary Growth: New SPVs (e.g., WR-ER transmission) via RECPDCL for TBCB projects.
  • Demand Drivers: Data centers, tariff reforms, payment security for DISCOMs, net-zero 2070 alignment.

Key Risks

  • Asset Quality: 11 NPAs (₹6,146 Cr gross, 77% PCR) under NCLT (e.g., Sinnar, Hiranmaye; resolutions eyed FY26). Stage-2 at ₹16k Cr.
  • Regulatory/Policy: RBI project finance norms (effective Oct 2025) may impact new sanctions. DISCOM privatization (e.g., UP) could shift lending to private players/banks.
  • Macro Risks: Forex volatility (hedged to ₹100+), rate cuts increasing bank competition (card rates cut in May). Sovereign rating benefits limited.
  • Concentration: 40% AUM in distribution, 86% state sector; slow thermal/hydro in state utilities.
  • Execution: Rooftop solar/RDSS progress dependent on state subsidies/political will; competition from PFC/NaBFID in infra.

Overall Outlook: Strong tailwinds from power capex and govt schemes outweigh headwinds. Growth on track with improving quality, but monitor prepayments and NPAs. FY26 EPS ~₹33.71 (H1), dividend payout sustainable. Positive for long-term investors.
