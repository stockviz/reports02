Analysis of Unichem Laboratories Limited (UNICHEMLAB)

Unichem Laboratories, a pharma company focused on formulations (primarily international exports post-2017 divestment of domestic business), reported H1 FY26 (ended Sep 30, 2025) consolidated revenue growth of ~12% YoY to ₹1,106 Cr, but swung to a net loss of ₹22 Cr (vs. ₹27 Cr profit YoY) due to one-off exceptional items (₹58 Cr interest on settled EU fine). Balance sheet remains stable (equity ~₹2,435 Cr, cash up 99% YoY to ₹178 Cr), with positive operating cash flow (₹99 Cr). NPPA issued a ₹1.77 Cr penalty notice for historical overpricing (2013-18), deemed immaterial. Overall, short-term profitability pressured by one-offs, but underlying ops show resilience amid regulatory hurdles.

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Revenue Momentum: H1 consolidated revenue +12% YoY (Q2 +14%); standalone +12% in Q2 YoY, driven by international pharma exports (US subsidiary contributes ~₹1,051 Cr assets).
  • Strong Liquidity: Cash & equivalents doubled YoY to ₹178 Cr; net cash inflow ₹88 Cr in H1, supported by robust ops cash flow (₹99 Cr) and tax refunds (₹101 Cr).
  • One-Off Resolutions: EU fine (Euro 19.55 Mn incl. interest) fully settled (Oct 28, 2025); prior provisioning minimized impact. Ireland subsidiary divested (gain ₹2 Cr).
  • Operational Efficiency: Cost of materials down as % of revenue; single pharma segment focus aids scalability.
  • Capex Momentum: CWIP up to ₹79 Cr (consolidated), signaling expansion.

Headwinds (Negative Pressures)

  • Profitability Hit: H1 consolidated PBT loss ₹20 Cr (vs. ₹35 Cr profit YoY) due to ₹58 Cr exceptional EU interest; Q2 EBITDA margin ~8% but eroded post-exceptionals.
  • Margin Squeeze: Employee costs +2% YoY, finance costs +68%; inventory changes volatile (H1 +₹14 Cr drag).
  • Working Capital Strain: Trade payables down 24% YoY; borrowings up (working capital +₹55 Cr), receivables stable at ~₹778 Cr.
  • Historical Regulatory Drag: NPPA penalty ₹1.77 Cr (under appeal); overlaps divested domestic business period.

Growth Prospects

  • Export-Led Expansion: International subsidiaries (US, UK, SA, Brazil, China) drive ~60-70% revenue; US generics potential post-Bayshore deal restatement.
  • Pipeline & Investments: Recent ₹1 Cr in China sub; ongoing capex (₹59 Cr H1 outflow) for capacity. Pharma tailwinds (ANDAs, global demand) could sustain 10-15% revenue CAGR.
  • Balance Sheet Leverage: Low net debt/equity (~0.2x), ₹2,421 Cr reserves support M&A/divestments (e.g., recent Ireland sale).
  • Recovery Post-One-Offs: Normalized FY26 EBITDA could exceed FY25’s ₹155 Cr PBT if revenue holds; analysts may re-rate on cash generation.

Overall Summary: Moderate Buy/Neutral Outlook. Tailwinds from revenue growth and liquidity outweigh one-off headwinds (EU settlement done); growth prospects solid in exports, but regulatory overhang (NPPA) caps upside. Key monitor: Q3 results for normalized profitability; target multiple ~15-20x FY26E EPS (post-exceptionals ~₹15-18). Risks tilted regulatory/ops; maintain 5-10% portfolio weight for pharma exposure.
