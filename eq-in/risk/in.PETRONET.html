Petronet LNG Limited (PETRONET) Analysis

Context: Petronet LNG is India’s largest LNG importer/regasifier with key terminals at Dahej (22.5 MMTPA, expanding to 27.5 MMTPA) and Kochi (5 MMTPA). Q2 FY26 (Sep 2025) results show revenue decline due to lower volumes, but strong profitability (NPAT ₹806 cr, down 5% YoY) and robust balance sheet (net cash ~₹11,373 cr in liquid assets). Utilization: Dahej steady, Kochi at record 27%. Interim dividend ₹7/share declared.

Headwinds (Challenges Pressuring Performance)

  • Volume Pressure: Q2 revenue down 15% YoY to ₹11,009 cr due to lower term LNG volumes (shift to spot/regas services amid pricing/scheduling). Dahej processed 211 TBtu (stable but term contracts dipped).
  • UoP Dues Recovery: ₹1,393 cr gross receivables (net ₹657 cr post ₹736 cr provision), mainly CY22-24. Settlements with promoters (98% business), BGs in place, but provisions/impaired recognition hit margins (₹296 cr H1 impairment).
  • Project Delays: Dahej expansion (5 MMTPA) slipped to Mar 2026 (monsoon/Operation Sindoor); Gopalpur EC resubmitted (FSRU to land-based shift).
  • Cost/Volatility: Forex loss ₹84 cr (USD lease liabilities ~$300 mn, natural hedge via pass-through); competition from new terminals causing cargo shifts.
  • Macro: Soft spot LNG prices delaying offtake; monsoon/winter dependency for volumes.

Tailwinds (Supportive Factors)

  • Operational Momentum: Kochi utilization at historic 27% (BPCL cargoes); Dahej regas services up. H1 EBITDA ₹1,117 cr (10% margin ex-other income).
  • Strong Finances: Net worth ₹20,589 cr; capex FY26 guided ₹5,000 cr (petchem dominant, ₹600+ cr spent); high liquidity for expansions/dividends.
  • Customer Tie-ups: Indian firms (IOC, GAIL, Torrent, BPCL, GSPC) securing ~8 MMTPA long-term from FY26, mostly at Dahej; ExxonMobil from FY27.
  • Qatar Extension: Back-to-back pricing/volumes with offtakers (finalizing by Mar 2026).
  • Shareholder Returns: Consistent dividends (₹7 interim); AGM resolutions passed overwhelmingly.

Growth Prospects (High Potential)

  • Capacity Ramp: Dahej +5 MMTPA (Mar 2026); Kochi pipeline connectivity (FY26) to boost to sustainable levels; Gopalpur (5 MMTPA, 3-yr timeline post-EC).
  • LNG Demand Surge: India gas share 7% → 10-11% (govt aim 15%); sectors like CGD (leading), petchem/refining/power/fertilizer/LNG-as-fuel/CBG. Global supply glut (2027-28) to lower prices, fill capacities.
  • Diversification: Petchem project (full steam, all major packages awarded, civil started); jetty upgrades.
  • Volume Outlook: FY26 volumes steady (winter key); 8.25 MMTPA regas booked; Gorgon/Exxon ramps.
  • Long-term: Qatar to Apr 2028 (renewal seamless); east coast entry via Gopalpur.

Key Risks (Critical Watchpoints)

Risk Category Description Mitigants
Financial/Recovery UoP non-recovery (₹694 cr CY22 due FY26); forex volatility on leases. Promoter relations (98% biz), BGs, settlements (₹695 cr expected reversal Q4). Natural hedge.
Execution Project slippages (Dahej/Gopalpur/petchem); capex ramp H2 FY26 (₹4,500 cr left). On-track monitoring; land/EC progress.
Market/Competition Spot-term pricing arbitrage; new terminals (e.g., greenfield/brownfield) shifting cargoes. Expansions precede demand; customer loyalty.
Regulatory/External PNGRB tariff push (under challenge, non-material); Kochi tariff reset premature. Contractual protections; govt push for gas.
Macro LNG price spikes/gluts; weather (monsoon/winter); Ind AS impacts (e.g., ₹95 cr net H1). Diversified offtakers; 15%+ margins resilient.

Overall Outlook: Positive with cautious optimism. Near-term headwinds from volumes/UoP offset by balance sheet strength and expansions. Medium-term tailwinds from demand/capacity align with India’s gas transition (300 MMSCMD at 11% share). Stock likely supported by dividends/growth projects; monitor UoP reversals (Q4 FY26) and Dahej commissioning. Target utilization: Dahej 90%+, Kochi 50%+ post-pipeline.
