Apollo Tyres Ltd (APOLLOTYRE) Analysis: Headwinds, Tailwinds, Growth Prospects, and Key Risks

Summary Overview: Apollo Tyres reported a solid Q2 FY26 (Sep 2025) with consolidated revenue up 6% YoY to ₹68.3 bn (highest growth in 10 quarters), driven by India (6% YoY, 15.3% EBITDA margin) amid GST rationalization benefits. EBITDA margin expanded to 14.9% (vs. 13.6% YoY), but net profit was muted at ₹2.6 bn due to ₹1.8 bn exceptional restructuring costs in Europe. Net debt remains healthy at 0.8x EBITDA (consolidated). Management is optimistic for H2 FY26 growth, focusing on premiumization, brand investments (e.g., Indian Cricket Team sponsorship), and Europe restructuring (Enschede plant closure by Jun 2026). Overall, India tailwinds dominate, offsetting Europe headwinds, with ROCE trajectory improving toward 15% medium-term target.

Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts)

  • Robust India Demand: Mid-single digit volume growth (4% overall); replacement up 2%, OEM up 4%. Farm, 2/3-wheeler, and exports (double-digit YoY) led gains. GST cuts (7-8%) boosted post-monsoon infra/mining rebound; Oct demand accelerated. Vredestein hit record volumes (10k tyres/month, premium PCR mix rising).
  • Margin Expansion: Raw materials down 3% QoQ (stable in Q3); natural rubber ~₹210/kg. Operating leverage from higher replacement expected in H2.
  • Brand & Premiumization: Cricket sponsorship for mass reach (rural/car/2W/truck). Podium finishes in Europe tests; new OEM wins (German marques). UHP mix up to 49% in Europe.
  • Balance Sheet Strength: Net debt flat at ₹26 bn (0.8x EBITDA consolidated, 1.1x India). Capex on track; FY26 guidance unchanged. Proposed ₹10 bn NCD raise within limits.
  • Sustainability: ESG score up to 58 (S&P); ‘76’ from CFC; awards in CSR/safety/healthcare apps for drivers.
  • Exports Recovery: High single-digit FY26 target after Q2 double-digits.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Europe Weakness: 4% YoY revenue growth (EUR 177 mn) amid soft demand (PCR/TBR); below-target 12.7% EBITDA margin. Exceptional costs: €17 mn additional for Enschede closure (total ~€55 mn cash outflow, 2-yr payback).
  • Competitive Pressure in India: TBR replacement share ~29% (arrested losses); PCR ~20% (industry-leading). New entrants (e.g., OHT players into PCR/TBR) could intensify; no pricing aggression planned, focus on distribution/product.
  • Muted Segments: Truck/PCR OEM/replacement volumes low-single digit; GST transition caused Sep destocking (now restocked).
  • Minor Regulatory: Customs ADD demand + penalty (₹12.69 lacs, Oct 2025 receipt); appeal planned, no material financial/ops impact.

Growth Prospects

  • H2 FY26 Outlook: Maintain/accelerate India top-line (mid-high single-digit replacement); Q3 revenue ≥ Q2, margins stable/improving. Europe low-single digit growth post-capacity ramp (Hungary PCR) and restructuring benefits (similar to FY21 gains).
  • Strategic Pillars: | Pillar | Key Initiatives | Expected Impact | |—————–|——————————————|———————————-| | R&D/Premium | Model wins, Vredestein traction | Higher margins, 10-15%+ premium mix growth | | Branding | Cricket sponsorship, dealer expansion | Rural penetration, share gains in car/2W/agri/truck | | Capacity | Hungary/AP PCR expansions (FY27 start) | Utilization 80-90%; support volume push | | Exports | High single-digit FY26 | Offset domestic cyclicality |
  • Medium-Term: ROCE to 15% (from 12%+ cycle low); profitable growth via free cash flow/returns focus. No major M&A; selective OHT if accretive.
