Analysis of Prostarm Info Systems Limited (PROSTARM)

Prostarm Info Systems Limited (BSE: 544410, NSE: PROSTARM) is a listed company specializing in customized power electronic solutions, IT infrastructure (e.g., desktops, UPS, networking for govt projects like CCTNS), and related services. Incorporated in 2008, it recently went public via IPO, raising significant capital. The analysis below is derived solely from the provided documents (announcements, financial results for H1 FY26 ended Sep 30, 2025, AGM outcomes, order wins, and litigation updates). All figures in ₹ Lakhs unless stated.

Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts)

  • Strong Order Wins & Govt Backing: Secured Letter of Intent (LOI) on Sep 11, 2025, for ₹158.68 Cr (~₹1,587 Lakhs) CCTNS IT infrastructure project from Maharashtra DGP (supply/installation/maintenance of desktops, UPS, printers, etc., over ~5.5 years). This is a material domestic govt contract, signaling execution capabilities in public sector IT/police infra.

    • Post-IPO equity infusion (share capital up 37% to 5,887) boosted cash to ₹1,352 (standalone), reduced current borrowings 76% to 1,520.
    • EPS (basic, H1): ₹1.86 (standalone), strong profitability margins (~9% PAT/revenue).
  • IPO Momentum & Balance Sheet Strength: Net IPO proceeds ~₹14,963 fueled debt reduction, investments (e.g., subsidiaries), and capex (PPE up 5%). Total assets grew 41% YoY to 34,548.
  • Corporate Governance & Approvals: 18th AGM (Sep 26, 2025) passed all resolutions (7/7, >99% favor); ESOP 2024 ratified for employees/subsidiaries. CSR committee reconstituted post-resignation.
  • Investor Engagement: Analyst/investor meet scheduled Dec 2, 2025 (Nuvama Conference, Mumbai) – no UPSI discussion, but indicates growing institutional interest.
  • Litigation Resolution: GST SCN (₹1.46 Cr demand for FY19) dropped (Oct 28, 2025) after ₹28.19 Lakhs payment – removes overhang.

  • Revenue Volatility: Q2 FY26 revenue down 34% QoQ (₹6,461 vs ₹5,098 Q1) and 34% YoY (vs ₹9,851). Purchases of stock-in-trade dominate (47% of expenses), indicating trading-heavy model vulnerable to input costs/supply chains.
  • Weak Operating Cash Flows: Standalone CFO negative at -₹875 (consolidated -₹1,274), driven by working capital strain: | Key WC Items | Change (H1 FY26) | |————–|——————| | Inventories | +24% (₹6,441) | | Trade Receivables | +5% (₹11,299) | | Other Financial Assets | Sharp rise (deposits/investments) |
    • Offset by financing inflows (IPO).
  • Subsidiary Drag: 3 subs (Prostarm Energy Systems, Bihar/Karnataka BESS Pvt Ltd); some unreviewed results show losses (e.g., net loss ₹18.7 in Q2 for certain subs).
  • Margin Pressure: Employee costs up 40% YoY, finance costs persist (₹246). Other expenses volatile.

Growth Prospects

  • Order Book & Pipeline: CCTNS project (~10% of FY25 revenue of ₹34,589 standalone) provides multi-year visibility (5+ years incl. maintenance). Focus on govt IT infra/power electronics (single reportable segment) aligns with India’s digital police modernization (CCTNS expansion potential).
  • Strategic Expansion:
    • Post-IPO capex in PPE (₹1,565), investment property (₹872), CWIP.
    • Subsidiaries in BESS (Battery Energy Storage Systems) – emerging green energy tailwind.
    • ESOPs incentivize talent for scaling.
  • Projected Trajectory: H1 PAT ₹1,002 (33% of FY25 ₹3,051) suggests annualized ~₹2,000+ possible if Q3/Q4 execute on new orders. Cash hoard (₹1,352+) supports acquisitions/M&A.
  • Market Positioning: Domestic focus (no international exposure noted), stable govt clients reduce cyclicality.

Overall Summary: Moderate Buy/Accumulate outlook. Tailwinds from CCTNS order, IPO liquidity, and litigation win outweigh headwinds like revenue dip and negative CFO. Growth hinges on order execution (target ₹20,000+ FY26 revenue). Monitor Q3 results for sustained momentum; current mcap likely reflects post-listing premium. Risks are manageable but watch WC cycle.
