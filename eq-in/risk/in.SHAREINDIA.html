Executive Summary

Share India Securities Limited has reported a mixed performance in its September 2025 quarter, reflecting strong operational growth in certain segments but margin pressures and working capital volatility. The company is expanding strategically through investments, fund-raising plans, and subsidiary incorporations, while facing minor regulatory headwinds. Despite a net decrease in cash flow, the capital structure remains healthy, profitability trends are stabilizing, and EPS continues to grow.

1. Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts)

🔹 Revenue & Profit Stability in Core Broking Business

  • Total revenue (standalone): ₹27,071 Crore for Q2 (up from ₹27,314 Cr in Q1) — flat, but driven by high-risk components.
  • Net profit (Q2 FY26 standalone): ₹733.45 Crore, up from ₹687.29 Cr in Q1 — growth of 6.7% quarter-on-quarter.
  • EPS (Basic): ₹3.35 (Q2) vs ₹3.15 (Q1) — consistent growth in shareholder returns.
  • Net Profit Margin: 27.7% (Q2) vs ~25.2% (Q1), indicating margin improvement despite lower total income.

🔹 Strong Net Gains from Fair Value Changes

  • Net gain on fair value changes (Q2): ₹16,452 Cr — still a major contributor, though down 4.2% YoY (from ₹22,646 Cr).
  • Consolidated: ₹20,705 Cr gain — suggests continued trading/investment market activity is benefiting the firm.
  • Reflects strong market movement capture via proprietary or client-driven positions in volatile equities/derivatives.

🔹 Strategic Investments & Diversification

The company has made significant strategic equity investments in key financial infrastructure entities: - Metropolitan Stock Exchange (MSE): Up to ₹300 Cr - NCDEX: ₹280 Cr for 1.58% stake - SIFPL & SICSPL: ₹500 Cr total via rights issue - New subsidiary: ₹600 Cr in Share India Greyhill Pvt Ltd (Fintech/Wealth focus) - Initial ₹125 Cr in Wealth Multiplier Solutions — signals expansion into wealth tech

This reinforces vertical integration and long-term positioning in India’s growing financial technology and capital markets ecosystem.

🔹 Fund-Raising Strategy (FCCBs to USD 50M)

  • Approves raising USD 50M via Foreign Currency Convertible Bonds (FCCBs).
  • Indicates ambition to access international capital and strengthen balance sheet.
  • Likely used for technology upgrades, regulatory capital, or acquisitions.

🔹 Healthy Equity & Balance Sheet Growth (Standalone)

  • Net worth (Standalone): ₹2,10,009.95 Cr as of Sep 2025 (vs ₹1,96,064.73 Cr in Mar 2025)
  • Liabilities reduced: Financial liabilities down from ₹1,78,947 Cr (Mar) to ₹1,64,357 Cr (Sep)
  • Asset size up: Total assets → ₹3,77,831 Cr (from ₹3,22,494 Cr)

Solid balance sheet deepening supports expansion.

🔹 High Liquidity Coverage

  • Cash + Bank Balances (Standalone): ₹2.35L Cr
  • Current Ratio: 1.82x — healthy short-term solvency.
  • Despite net cash outflow, liquidity remains robust due to high bank balances (largely client funds and trading collateral).
2. Headwinds & Challenges

🔹 High Volatility in Revenue & Profit

  • Revenue heavily driven by mark-to-market gains (₹16.5K Cr in Q2), not recurring fees.
  • Interest income up sharply (₹5,481 Cr from ₹4,676 Cr) — may be tied to client margin usage, less stable than brokerage.
  • Net gain on fair value down 27.5% YoY — suggests difficult market conditions or reduced trading volumes.

🔹 Sharp Increase in Expenses

  • Total expenses: ₹17,198 Cr (Q2), down slightly from Q1 but still 63.5% of total income.
  • Employee benefits: ₹5,628 Cr — 32.7% of total expenses.
  • Operating expenses: ₹6,251 Cr — includes technology and distribution costs.

Margin pressure evident; though profit up, leverage on cost control is critical.

🔹 Negative Cash Flow from Operations

  • Net cash used in operating activities: ₹19,509 Cr (H1 FY26 vs ₹28,741 Cr in H1 FY25) — still high outflow, though improved YoY.
  • Key outflows due to:
    • Increase in loans/MTF funding: ₹12,110 Cr
    • Increase in securities for trade: ₹29,498 Cr
    • Reduction in borrowing base

Aggressive lending to clients (margin financing) is tying up capital — credit risk concern.

🔹 Regulatory Penalty from NSE

  • Penalty: ₹1.5 Lakh imposed by NSE for non-compliance with UID tagging for algorithmic orders.
  • Not material in monetary terms, but highlights growing regulatory scrutiny on tech compliance.
  • Could escalate if repeated; algorithmic trading is a core growth area — compliance systems need tightening.

🔹 Interest Rate Dependence

  • Finance costs: ₹2,692 Cr in Q2 (up 26.5% YoY)
  • Net interest income narrowing: despite higher interest income, rising borrowing costs (debt ↑ to ₹33,752 Cr) pressure margins.

Exposed to interest rate volatility as MTF and broking depend on funding costs.
