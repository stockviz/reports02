HEG Limited (NSE/BSE: HEG) - Strategic Analysis

HEG Limited is a leading graphite electrode manufacturer (primary revenue ~98% from Graphite segment, 70-75% UHP grade) with a 100k ton capacity (expanding to 115k tons by 2027). It operates a single-location plant in India, benefiting from cost advantages. The company is debt-free (treasury ~INR 1,167 cr as of Sep 2025), reported strong Q2/H1 FY26 results (standalone revenue +23% YoY to INR 1,310 cr, PAT +213% to INR 203 cr; consolidated PAT +135% to INR 248 cr), and is advancing a demerger into Graphite (core) and Greentech entities (anodes, hydro, BESS).

Headwinds

  • Weak Global Steel Demand: Global crude steel output -1.5% YoY (9M FY26); ex-China -0.1% YoY, Q2 -1.5% QoQ. China exports +9% YoY (58M tons Q2), pressuring prices/margins via oversupply.
  • Flat Electrode Prices: Stable QoQ (Q1-Q2 FY26) but at cyclical lows; industry utilization ~65-70% (HEG at 90%+). Historical upturn needs 80-85% utilization.
  • Geopolitical/Trade Barriers: US 50% reciprocal tariffs on electrodes (10-12% of HEG sales); customers resist absorption. Potential EU CBAM/quotas (2026) may boost EU steel but uncertain.
  • Chinese Competition: Dominant in HP-grade; low pricing hurts global basket prices (minimal Q3 uptick in HP/domestic).
  • Cost Pressures: Needle coke stable but spreads tight (~1/3 of electrode price historically); inventory comfortable but no pricing power yet.

Tailwinds

  • India Steel Resilience: +10.5% YoY output (122M tons 9M FY26); infra/auto driving demand.
  • Operational Excellence: 90%+ utilization (vs. peers ~65-80%); single-plant efficiency positions as low-cost producer.
  • Diversification: Sales to 35 countries (US ~10-12%, Europe <10%); balanced footprint mitigates single-market risks.
  • Strong Balance Sheet: Net cash INR 1,167 cr; no LT debt. Q2 EBITDA margins resilient (~32%).
  • EAF Transition: Global shift to low-emission EAF steelmaking; +20M tons new capacity CY26-27 (+30k tons electrode demand); long-term +200k tons ex-China by 2030.
  • ESG Momentum: Score 66.2/100; aligns with green steel trends.
