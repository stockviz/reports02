Saksoft Limited (SAKSOFT) - Q2/H1 FY26 Analysis

Saksoft, a mid-cap IT services player focused on digital transformation, AI-led products/platforms, and verticals like BFSI (31%), Emerging (47%), Logistics (14%), and Commerce (8%), delivered robust Q2/H1 FY26 results: H1 revenue +22% YoY to INR 508 Cr, EBITDA +34% to INR 96 Cr (19% margin, +175 bps), and PAT +32% to INR 68 Cr (13.5% margin). Guidance for FY26: INR 1,000-1,100 Cr revenue (~20-25% growth) at 17-18% EBITDA margins. US (44%) remains key market; 16 clients >$1M; utilization at 85%. Management eyes 25-30% CAGR organically + acquisitions for Vision 2030 ($500M revenue).

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • AI/Digital Momentum: New launches (SakMod for legacy modernization, SakCelerate for AIOps/FinOps, Conversational/Emergency AI Agents) driving wallet share in existing clients. 75% AI focus on productivity (efficiency gains); early small deals (e.g., AI testing from Indian bank).
  • Strong Execution: Sequential/YoY growth across metrics; margin expansion from scale, controlled costs, and higher billing. Offshore mix at 56%; diversified verticals (Emerging up to 47%).
  • Client Stickiness: Deep ties with 16 large clients (e.g., Honeywell, Broadcom; <5% current wallet share, runway to 20-25%). Proximity to CIOs/CTOs via Centers of Excellence (6 CoEs) aids relevance.
  • Balanced Profile: No H1B reliance; agile small-player advantage in vendor consolidations (flexible pricing/outcomes). Healthy cash (INR 167 Cr), low D/E (~0.05x), ROE ~27%.
  • Talent & Infra: 2,653 employees (2,430 billable); new US Chief Growth Officer to boost largest market.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Margin Normalization: Q2 19.6% EBITDA inflated (no sales costs yet); expect dip to 17-18% from hires (e.g., open sales roles, Growth Officer).
  • Demand Caution: Clients breaking big projects into smaller ones amid macro uncertainty; reduced predictability despite willingness to spend.
  • Concentration: Top 10 clients ~58% revenue (diluting slowly with scale).
  • SG&A Investments: Upfront spends for growth (sales, AI) may pressure near-term profitability; management pacing to match bandwidth.

Growth Prospects

  • Organic (75-80% of target): Massive runway in 16 key accounts (wallet expansion via AI/stickiness); aim 25-30% YoY. Emerging verticals/deal pipeline “decent”; H2 demand steady.
  • Inorganic: 1-2 tuck-in acquisitions ($10-15M revenue, >company EBITDA); potential larger deal (e.g., 50% of size by 2029) with war chest build-up. Recent mergers (e.g., Threesixty Logica) accretive.
  • AI Tailwind: Shift to Agentic AI/productivity; multi-year contracts with brands ensure visibility. FY26 implies H2 acceleration to hit guidance.
  • Long-Term: $500M by 2030 realistic (25% CAGR + M&A); small/mid-IT peers gaining from disruption (vs. large IT slowdowns).

Key Risks

  • Client Concentration/Consolidation (High): 58% from top 10; vendor rationalization could consolidate out (mitigated by proximity/agility, but no oral commitments).
  • Execution: Sales ramp-up, AI deal conversion (small now, no big wins); management bandwidth for hires.
  • Macro/External: Discretionary spend caution (US/Europe); forex (guidance at current rates); global uncertainty.
  • M&A: Integration, valuation/dilution risks; no timeline.
  • Competition/Talent: Person-dependent relationships (denied by mgmt.); AI disruption favors small players but requires sustained innovation.
  • Other: Tax rate 24-25%; dividend policy (10% payout) limits war chest slightly.

Overall Outlook: Positive. Strong fundamentals, AI alignment, and client depth support sustained 20-25% growth near-term. Risks are manageable via focus on incumbents/acquisitions, but monitor concentration and margin trajectory. Stock trades at premium (MCap ~INR 2,600 Cr); attractive for growth investors.
