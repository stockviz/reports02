DALBHARAT (Dalmia Bharat Limited) Analysis

Dalmia Bharat Limited (DALBHARAT), a leading Indian cement producer with ~49.5 MnT capacity (4th largest by capacity), reported strong Q2 FY26 results amid soft industry demand. Key metrics: Q2 revenue ₹3,417 Cr (+11% YoY), EBITDA ₹696 Cr (+60% YoY, ₹1,013/ton), volume 6.9 MnT (+3% YoY); H1 EBITDA ₹1,579 Cr (+43% YoY). Focus on profitable growth, cost leadership, and expansions. Below is a structured analysis based on filings, earnings call, financials, and disclosures (as of Oct/Nov 2025).

Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts)

  • Robust Profitability & Cost Discipline: EBITDA/ton at ₹1,013 (2nd straight 4-digit quarter) driven by +7.6% YoY realization, flat raw material/power costs (RE share 48%, 93 MW added; target 576 MW by FY26 end), and -4% YoY logistics (DD% 60%, lead distance 287 km). Blended fuel cost stable at ₹1.38/kCal.
  • GST Cut Benefits: 28%→18% passed fully to consumers, boosting channel liquidity/working capital, demand (esp. housing), and supply chain efficiency. Complements RBI cuts, tax rebates, good monsoon for H2 recovery.
  • Strong Balance Sheet: Net debt ₹1,602 Cr (0.56x EBITDA), gross debt ₹6,621 Cr, borrowing cost 6.9%. Interim dividend ₹4/share (200%). Cash from ops ₹735 Cr (H1).
  • Operational Momentum: Umrangso 3.6 MnT clinker trial run started (commercial Q3 FY26); Belgaum/Kadapa on track (+12 MnT in West/South). RE scaling and awards (energy, environment) enhance sustainability edge.
  • Incentive & Regulatory Wins: Coal cess removal (+₹20 Cr H2, ₹50-55 Cr FY27); GST SCN dropped (₹533 Mn saved); favorable credit terms cut FY26 CAPEX to ₹3,000 Cr.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Demand Softness: Industry/Q2 low-single-digit growth (rains/floods, GST transition slowed inventory). H1 volume -2% YoY (13.9 MnT); trade mix at 62% (4-yr low), premium products 22%.
  • Realization Pressure: QoQ -4% drop (mix/regional effects); Petcoke up to $116/ton (external pressures), potential H2 cost inflation.
  • Incentive Deferral: GST cut defers accruals (FY26 ₹240 Cr vs ₹300 Cr; ~₹60-65/ton). Outstanding ₹800 Cr.
  • High CAPEX: FY26 ₹3,000 Cr (H1 ₹1,189 Cr); FY27 ~₹4,000 Cr. Debt may rise with expansions.
  • Macro/Seasonal: Monsoon weakness, EPC cash crunches, volatile geopolitics on fuel.

Growth Prospects

  • Volume & Capacity: H2 pickup expected (pent-up demand, infra-led recovery); long-term industry CAGR 7-8% (1.2x GDP). Target 75 MnT by FY28 (organic: Belgaum/Kadapa/Umrangso/Northeast grinding/Jaisalmer; JP inorganic pending March ’26). Grow 1.5x industry selectively.
  • Strategic Focus: Profitable growth via micro-market strategies (share in low-utilization areas, margins elsewhere). Brand strengthening, variable pay (15-25%, tied to company/individual/safety).
  • Sustainability Edge: RE100/EP100/EV100 pioneer; low carbon footprint positions for green premiums.
  • Outlook: H2 volumes > H1; EBITDA/ton sustained via costs. FY26 volumes ~29-30 MnT implied.

Summary

Bull Case: DALBHARAT leverages cost leadership (lowest in peers), expansions, and H2 demand for 10-15% EBITDA CAGR to FY28, targeting 75 MnT with net debt <2x. Strong mgmt. (profitable growth ethos), low leverage, sustainability moat support 20%+ ROCE. Bear Case: Legal overhangs crystallize (₹500+ Cr hit), demand stays soft (4-5% industry), fuel spikes erode margins; debt rises to 1.5-2x. Overall: Positive (Buy/Hold). Q2 beat underscores resilience amid headwinds; tailwinds from GST/demand pickup dominant. Risks legal but non-ops impacting. Target FY26 EBITDA ₹3,500-4,000 Cr; watch H2 volumes, ED appeal, JP update (March ’26). Valuation: Attractive vs. peers on EV/EBITDA (~8-9x FY26E).
