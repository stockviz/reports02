Analysis of Dalmia Bharat Limited (DALBHARAT)

Tailwinds

  • GST Rate Cut (28% to 18%): Passed on fully to consumers, boosting demand, housing, and channel liquidity (eases working capital for partners). Removal of coal cess adds ~₹20 Cr H2FY26 benefit (₹50-55 Cr FY27).
  • Strong Cost Leadership: Q2FY26 EBITDA/ton at ₹1,013 (+56% YoY) driven by stable raw material/power costs (RE share 48%, 93 MW added), logistics down 4% YoY to ₹1,060/ton (DD% 60%). Blended fuel cost stable at ₹1.38/kcal.
  • Robust Financials: Q2 revenue +11% YoY to ₹3,417 Cr, EBITDA +60% to ₹696 Cr; H1 EBITDA/ton ₹1,138. Net debt/EBITDA 0.56x; interim dividend ₹4/share declared.
  • Operational Efficiencies: Premium products 22%, trade share 62%; variable pay linked to company/individual/safety performance to align incentives.

Headwinds

  • Demand Softness: H1 industry growth low single-digit (rains/floods); Q2 volumes +3% YoY but realization -4% QoQ due to regional/segment mix. September slowdown from GST transition.
  • Cost Pressures: Petcoke prices up (~$116/ton), potential fuel cost rise; incentive accrual down to ₹240 Cr FY26 (from ₹300 Cr) due to lower GST.
  • Capex Moderation: FY26 spend cut to ₹3,000 Cr (from higher est.) due to better terms/postponements, but still high at ₹1,189 Cr in H1.

Growth Prospects

  • Capacity Roadmap: 49.5 MnT current; Umrangso 3.6 MnT clinker trial started (commercial Q3FY26, enables 2-2.5 MnT grinding); Belgaum/Kadapa (12 MnT total) on track. Targeting 75 MnT FY28 (13.5 MnT gap via organic/JP/Jaisalmer greenfield ~₹5,000 Cr).
  • Demand Outlook: Industry 7-8% CAGR decade-long; H2 pickup from pent-up demand, good monsoons, RBI ECBs for realty, govt capex. Dalmia aims profitable growth (1.5x market historically).
  • Strategic Focus: Pan-India footprint (Northeast/East via Umrangso); hired Chief Strategy Officer; awards for energy/safety/environment.

Key Risks

  • Regulatory/Legal:
    • ED confirmed attachment on DCBL land (₹377 Cr value; challenging via Appellate Tribunal; no ops impact but sub-judice from 2011 CBI case).
    • Contingent liabilities: Mineral tax (₹257 Cr PV), Bawri dispute (DCNEL shares), mutual fund fraud (₹400 Cr guarantees), JP insolvency (provision reduced to ₹97 Cr post-review).
    • Incentives: ₹800 Cr outstanding; revocation risk in WB (₹250 Cr IPA challenged).
  • Execution: Expansion delays (e.g., Jaisalmer land/EC by Mar’26); rising net debt (target <2x EBITDA).
  • Market: Pricing volatility post-GST (industry passed full benefit); competition in micro-markets; geopolitical fuel volatility.
  • Media/Perception: Misreported GST relief (₹266 Cr demands dropped) caused short-term noise.

Summary

Dalmia Bharat delivered stellar Q2FY26 profitability (EBITDA +60% YoY) amid soft demand, leveraging cost discipline and GST tailwinds. Growth is anchored in aggressive expansions (75 MnT FY28) and H2 recovery, positioning it as a low-cost PAN-India player. However, legal overhangs (ED/JP disputes) and fuel costs pose near-term risks, offset by strong balance sheet (low leverage) and legal challenges underway. Overall Outlook: Positive for medium-term profitable growth (focus on EBITDA/ton >₹1,000), but monitor H2 demand/pricing and litigation outcomes. Stock likely supported by expansions and industry upcycle.
