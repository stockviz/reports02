Summary Analysis for Info Edge (India) Ltd (NAUKRI)

Info Edge (India) Ltd, operator of Naukri.com (recruitment), 99acres.com (real estate), Jeevansathi.com (matrimony), and Shiksha.com (education), reported solid Q2FY26 results (ended Sep 30, 2025). Standalone revenue grew 13.7% YoY to ₹746 Cr, driven by resilient core operations and strong cash generation (₹293 Cr pre-tax). An exceptional gain of ₹52,001 Cr from reclassifying a JV investment boosted profits, but core ops remain steady. The company holds ₹4,823 Cr cash (incl. subs) and declared ₹2.40/share interim dividend. Below is an analysis of headwinds, tailwinds, growth prospects, and key risks.

Tailwinds (Supportive Factors)

  • Resilient Core Business: Recruitment billings +11% YoY (₹545 Cr), with 13% higher cash generation. Dominant 75%+ traffic share, 111 Mn resume DB, and metrics like 26k daily resumes added remain strong.
  • Non-Recruitment Momentum: Combined +16.3% growth; 99acres (mid-teens, 49% traffic share, +23% new projects), Jeevansathi (+29%, breakeven), Shiksha (+13%, profitable).
  • Financial Strength: Operating margin 35.9% (stable), deferred revenue ₹1,250 Cr (visibility), net cash ops +12% YoY. Cash hoard supports investments/dividends.
  • Investment Upside: Massive OCI/P&L gains from fair-value investments (e.g., PB Fintech ~₹576 Cr stake). Portfolio diversification (21 unlisted, AIFs).
  • Market Leadership: Naukri’s scale (46k billed customers), 99acres gaining share vs. peers (Magicbricks/Housing).

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Hiring Slowdown: Uncertain environment moderating recruitment growth (tech/IT/BPM +7%, iimjobs/Naukri FastForward slowed). IT sector ~30-35% billings exposure.
  • Non-Core Losses: 99acres/Shiksha still cash-negative (though improving); combined non-recruitment cash losses reducing but persistent.
  • Leadership Transition: CFO Chintan Thakkar exited (Nov 19, 2025); interim appointee, potential short-term disruption.
  • Margin Pressure: Ad spends high (₹901 Cr Q2), employee costs up 15% YoY amid 6,238 headcount.
  • Macro Sensitivity: Recruitment tied to economy; real estate cyclical.

Growth Prospects

  • Short-Term (FY26): 12-15% revenue growth likely; recruitment stabilization, non-core scaling (99acres listings +37% YoY resale/rentals, Jeevansathi app-led efficiency). Cash-gen supports M&A (e.g., Zwayam/DoSelect integrations).
  • Medium-Term (2-3 Yrs): Tier-2/3/SMB penetration in recruitment; 99acres market share gains (broker-led); Jeevansathi/Shiksha profitability ramp-up. Investment exits (e.g., IPOs like NoPaperForms) could unlock value.
  • Long-Term: Digital classifieds dominance; venture portfolio (29 startups, ₹654 Cr unlisted) for 20-30%+ returns. FY26E revenue ~₹30,000 Cr+ possible with 12-14% CAGR.
  • Catalysts: Analyst meets (Dec 2025), merger schemes (subs), potential divestments.

Key Risks

Overall Outlook: Positive with tailwinds from ops stability and investments outweighing headwinds. Stock likely to benefit from recruitment recovery and portfolio value unlocks, but monitor macros/probe. Target FY26 core EPS ~₹20-22 (ex-exceptionals); buy on dips for long-term compounding.
