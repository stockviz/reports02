Exide Industries Limited (EXIDEIND) Analysis: Headwinds, Tailwinds, Growth Prospects, and Key Risks

Summary Overview (H1 FY26 Performance):
Standalone revenue grew 1.3% YoY to ₹8,688 Cr (Q2 degrowth of 2.1% due to GST destocking), EBITDA margin dipped to 10.9% (vs. 11.4% YoY) amid input cost pressures, PAT at ₹541 Cr (down 6%). Consolidated trends similar. Balance sheet strong (zero standalone debt, cash up ₹500 Cr via inventory cuts). Heavy capex in Li-ion subsidiary EESL (total ₹4,022 Cr invested).

Headwinds (Short-term Pressures)

  • GST 2.0 Transition Shock: Rate cuts (batteries 28%→18%, solar combos 12%→5%) triggered channel destocking/deferred buys in Q2, leading to production cuts, under-recovery of fixed costs, and modest H1 growth.
  • Input Cost Inflation: Lead prices 20-30% higher YoY, forex at ₹90 (vs. ₹80); partial pass-through halted post-GST to avoid mixed signals.
  • Segment Weakness: Exports (tariffs/geopolitics), telecom, e-rickshaw, home UPS (extended monsoon) declined; ~12% of revenue impacted.
  • Regulatory Costs: New EPR (battery waste mgmt.) accruals in Q2; ongoing circularity compliance adds to other expenses.
  • Macro Disruptions: Prolonged monsoon hit aspirational segments (solar/UPS); global trade uncertainties persist.

Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts)

  • GST Benefits Flowing Through: Full pass-through to consumers; early Q3 indicators (Oct retail +11% PV, +52% 2W) show rebound in auto OEM/trade.
  • Operational Efficiencies: Punched Grid tech (full 2W capacity by Dec’25), automation (robotics/continuous casting), inventory optimization (93 days vs. 110); generated ₹500 Cr+ cash.
  • Strong Core Segments: 88% revenue grew 7% (aftermarket/auto double-digit, solar/IUPS robust); domestic up 5%.
  • Liquidity & Balance Sheet: Zero debt (standalone), high cash flows; enables self-funding of growth.
  • Policy Support: PM Surya Ghar (rooftop solar subsidies), renewables push (500 GW by 2030), railways electrification.

Growth Prospects (Medium-Term Outlook)

  • Lead-Acid Core (88% Revenue): Double-digit aftermarket sustained; OEM rebound (SIAM Oct data positive); solar franchise to ₹1,500 Cr (from ₹800 Cr) via GST/PM schemes; infra (railways/power/data centers) order uptick.
  • Li-ion Gigafactory (EESL): 12 GWh plant (Phase 1: 6 GWh) nearing completion; Line 1 (cyl. NCM for 2W OEMs) trials Nov’25, production end-FY26; prismatic LFP for stationary. Early-mover edge (18-24 months yield learning); margins ~lead-acid OEM/aftermarket blend at 80% utilization. Engaged with 2W/3W/4W OEMs, stationary players.
  • White Spaces: Rural penetration, e-commerce, OEM tie-ups (Maruti/Tata 100% share), Invazen range; exports diversification to new geographies (Q4 uptick expected).
  • Overall: H2 FY26 rebound to double-digits; EV/storage tailwinds position for multi-year growth.
