Summary Analysis for Transformers and Rectifiers (India) Limited (TARIL)

TARIL, India’s second-largest transformer manufacturer by capacity (~40,000 MVA), reported Q2 FY26 results impacted by short-term headwinds but maintains strong fundamentals with a robust order pipeline, expansions, and domestic focus. FY25 revenue was INR 1,950 Cr (EBITDA margin 16.4%), with H1 FY26 revenue up 26% YoY to INR 989 Cr despite Q2 flatness. Management guides FY26 revenue at ~INR 2,600 Cr (+25% YoY) and 16% EBITDA margins, with H2 recovery.

Headwinds (Short-term Challenges)

  • Operational Disruptions: CTC/raw material shortages (customs delays on imports due to BIS norms) and heavy monsoons delayed site readiness/deliveries, leading to ~INR 100 Cr deferred revenue (high WIP/finished goods inventory at INR 310 Cr). Capacity utilization dipped to ~65%.
  • Margin Pressure: Q2 EBITDA margin fell to 13.8% (vs. 17.1% YoY) due to low-margin legacy orders, fixed costs on lower revenue (Q2: INR 460 Cr, flat YoY), and INR 3.76 Cr ESOP expense.
  • Capacity Delays: Changodar expansion lagged by 1Q (monsoon/equipment issues), denting ~25-30% production.
  • World Bank Debarment Perception: Old Nigeria project (USD 24.74 Mn, FY22-24, fully paid) led to temporary debarment; removed from list but response due Jan 2026. No current exposure, but market fear persists.

Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts)

  • Strong Order Momentum: Unexecuted book ~INR 5,472 Cr (Sep 30); Q2 inflow INR 592 Cr; recent INR 390 Cr GETCO order (total INR 493 Cr from GETCO in quarter). Pipeline INR 8,000+ Cr; inquiries INR 18,700 Cr (85-90% domestic from PGCIL, NTPC, GETCO).
  • Backward Integration & Expansions: CTC plant (1,500 tons/month) by Sep FY27; RIP bushings by Jun FY27; tank unit by Aug FY27. Moraiya on track; targeting 85-90% utilization and 22,000 MVA addition.
  • Financial Health: Current ratio 3.45; net debt reduction on track (debt-free in 18-24 months); subsidiaries profitable (boosting consolidated PAT).
  • Domestic Bias: 85-90% inquiries from Indian utilities; exports capped at 15% (FOB basis, no MDB-funded).

Growth Prospects

  • Near-term (FY26): H2 ramp-up from deferred execution, normalized supply, high-margin order book. Expect INR 1,600-1,700 Cr execution in H2; overall 25% revenue growth.
  • Medium-term (FY27-28): Capacity doubling via expansions; backward integration to lift margins >16%; niche demand (furnace/specialty transformers, e.g., INR 400-500 Cr pipeline). INR 1 Bn revenue aspiration by FY28 intact.
  • Sector Tailwinds: India power transmission/distribution capex (utilities pipeline); green energy (solar/hydrogen transformers); global niche leadership (e.g., 333 MVA/1200 kV auto transformer).
  • Strategic: ESOP alignment; governance (results in 8 days); NABL-accredited lab.
