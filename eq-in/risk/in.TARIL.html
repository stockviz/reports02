Analysis of Transformers and Rectifiers (India) Limited (TARIL)

Overview: TARIL, India’s second-largest transformer manufacturer by capacity (~40,000 MVA), reported solid H1 FY26 consolidated results with revenue of INR 989 Cr (+26% YoY) and PAT of INR 105 Cr (10.3% margin), despite a softer Q2 (revenue INR 460 Cr flat YoY, EBITDA margin 13.8% vs. 17.1%). Order book stands at ~INR 5,472 Cr (as of Sep 30, 2025), with INR 18,700+ Cr inquiries under negotiation. Recent INR 390 Cr order from GETCO boosts quarterly inflows to INR 493 Cr from them. Management guides for FY26 revenue of ~INR 2,600 Cr (+25-33% YoY) at 16% EBITDA margins, driven by H2 execution and expansions. Balance sheet remains strong (current ratio ~3.45), targeting net debt-free status in 18-24 months.

Headwinds (Short-term Challenges)

  • Execution Delays: Heavy monsoons, site unreadiness (e.g., PSU sites), and raw material shortages (CTC, copper bushings) led to deferred high-value deliveries, elevated inventory (INR 310-320 Cr WIP/finished goods vs. normal INR 15-20 Cr), and ~INR 100 Cr revenue slippage in Q2. Capacity utilization dipped below 65-70%.
  • Margin Pressure: Q2 EBITDA down 19% YoY due to low-margin legacy orders, fixed costs on lower volumes, and INR 3.76 Cr ESOP expense. Standalone EBITDA margin fell to 9.6%.
  • Expansion Delays: Changodar plant delayed by 1Q (monsoons/equipment); expected operational in Q3 FY26.
  • Order Intake Moderation: Deliberately slowed in H1 to align with capacity, risking pipeline conversion if not ramped up.

Tailwinds (Positive Supports)

  • Robust Demand Pipeline: Unexecuted order book INR 5,472 Cr; inquiries INR 18,700 Cr (85-90% domestic from utilities like GETCO, PGCIL, NTPC). Recent GETCO win validates strong state utility ties.
  • Subsidiary Strength: All subsidiaries delivered strong profits, aiding consolidated growth (H1 PAT contribution rising via backward integration).
  • Backward Integration: CTC plant (1,500 tons/month) by Sep 2026, RIP bushing (Jun 2026), tank unit (Aug 2026), and CRGO processing to mitigate raw material volatility and boost margins.
  • Financial Health: Healthy liquidity, low leverage, positive cash flows from ops (despite H1 working capital drag), and quick results declaration (8 days post-quarter) underscore governance.
  • World Bank Resolution Progress: Removed from debarred list; response deadline extended to Jan 2026. No current/inquiry exposure (focus shifted to domestic, FOB exports <15%).

Growth Prospects

  • Near-term (FY26): H2 rebound via deferred execution (INR 1,600-1,700 Cr), supply normalization, and 85-90% utilization. Target 25% revenue growth to INR 2,600 Cr at 16% margins; order book to close FY26 at ~INR 8,000 Cr.
  • Medium-term (FY27-28): 22,000 MVA Moraiya expansion (Q4 FY26) + backward integration for cost efficiencies. Niche products (furnace/specialty transformers, green H2/solar) with INR 400-500 Cr export pipeline. Aspirational INR 10 Bn revenue by FY28-29 via domestic capex cycle.
  • Long-term: Leadership in power/distribution/furnace transformers; global footprint (25+ countries); B2B model tied to India’s transmission infra boom (utilities/industrials).

Key Risks

Risk Category Description Mitigation
Execution/Supply Chain Recurring raw material shortages (CTC/copper), weather delays, or expansion slippages could miss H2 guidance. High inventory/receivables strain WC (H1 op cash flow negative). Backward integration; diversified suppliers; customer letters for imports.
Regulatory/Reputation World Bank sanctions probe (old Nigeria project, USD 24.7 Mn, fully paid) – potential MDB cross-debarment or image hit, though no active exposure. Legal response underway; 90%+ domestic focus; strong governance (NABL lab, quick disclosures).

Financial LDs from delays (provisioned); ESOP drag (~INR 3-4 Cr/Q); forex/volatility in metals. Conservative pricing; provisions; hedging.

Investment Summary: TARIL’s fundamentals remain strong amid temporary Q2 hiccups, with a massive domestic pipeline and expansions positioning it for 25%+ CAGR. World Bank noise is noise (resolved soon), but monitor H2 execution. Bullish on FY26 beat if supplies normalize; key watch: Q3 order inflows and margins. (Ratings neutral-positive; target upside to guidance multiples.)
