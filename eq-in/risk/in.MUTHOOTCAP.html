Analysis of MUTHOOTCAP (Muthoot Capital Services Limited) - Q2 FY26

Muthoot Capital Services Ltd. (MCSL), a vehicle financing NBFC focused on 2-wheelers (89% of portfolio), reported a mixed Q2 FY26 amid weak industry sales but showed operational improvements. AUM grew 38% YoY to ₹3,284 Cr (QoQ flat), disbursements fell 13% QoQ to ₹535 Cr, but turned profitable (PAT ₹3.31 Cr vs Q1 loss). GNPA rose to 6.46% (PCR 60%), yields improved to 20.32%, and borrowings cost dipped to 9.74%. Management targets FY26 AUM at ~₹4,000 Cr and long-term ₹10,000 Cr by FY28 at 4% ROA.

Headwinds (Challenges)
  • Weak Disbursements & Market Slowdown: 2W retail sales dropped 16% QoQ (40.16L units); MCSL’s own book down 12% QoQ to ₹442 Cr, exacerbated by floods/monsoon in key markets (North/East).
  • Asset Quality Pressure: GNPA up to 6.46% (₹212 Cr), NNPA 3.07%; slippages ₹42 Cr (0.67% of standard pool, down from Q1 but annualized ~6-7%). Credit cost 2.05% (target 1.65-1.85%).
  • Opex & Profitability Drag: Opex up 3% QoQ due to sourcing incentives/agency costs; ROA subdued at 0.42% (Q2), FY26 target 2% challenging post-Q1 loss.
  • Co-lending Reduction: Share fell 34% QoQ to ₹79 Cr (low yields); runoff expected to shrink book further.
  • Macro Headwinds: GST uncertainty muted Sep sales; festive season uncertain post-Diwali.
Tailwinds (Positive Factors)
  • Improved Collections & Efficiency: eNACH penetration at 92% (77% digital collections); NPA recoveries up 69% QoQ; fresh slippages down 6%.
  • Yield & Funding Improvements: Blended yield +68 bps QoQ to 20.32%; borrowing cost down 13 bps to 9.74% (more bank TLs, lower NCD/CP rates). Rating upgraded to A+ Positive (CRISIL).
  • Strong Capital Position: CRAR 22.02%, Debt/Equity 4.56x; promoter holding 62.62%.
  • Digital & Policy Wins: Risk-based pricing, dealer/location categorization (A-E); market share stable/improved in key areas.
  • Product Diversification: Non-2W (used car +131% YoY, CV +1511%) now 11% of AUM; lower GNPA (used car 1.73%, CV 0.31%).
Growth Prospects
  • Near-Term (Q3/FY26): Festive momentum (Oct up 26% YoY); Q3 disbursements ₹800-1,100 Cr; FY26 AUM ~₹4,000 Cr (close to guidance despite Q2 dip).
  • Medium-Term: Launch used 2W (Nov, 30-40% of new 2W), construction equipment (Oct); EV co-lending for green funding; cross-sell (insurance as corporate agent). Yield +100 bps in Q3, +50 bps Q4.
  • Long-Term Vision: ₹10,000 Cr AUM by FY28; portfolio shift to 50% larger-ticket (used car/CV) for better quality/yields; ROA 2-2.5% in 12 months, 4% at scale. Tech investments (LOS, data lake, bots) to drive efficiency.
  • Funding Access: Diversified (banks 27%, NCD 45%); green bonds ₹300 Cr issued; DA deals for top-line boost.

Overall Outlook: MCSL is navigating headwinds via execution (collections, yields), with tailwinds from diversification/digitalization supporting growth. Asset quality remains watch-item, but Q3 momentum and FY28 ambition signal resilience. Conservative FY26 AUM achievable; ROA ramp-up key for re-rating.
