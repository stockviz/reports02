FDC Limited (BSE: 531599, NSE: FDC) Analysis

Overview: FDC is a Mumbai-based pharma manufacturer/exporter focused on formulations (single segment: Pharmaceuticals). Key update: US FDA ANDA approval for Pilocarpine Hydrochloride Ophthalmic Solution (1%, 2%, 4%; 15mL) on Oct 4, 2025. H1FY26 (Apr-Sep 2025) results show revenue/PBT decline amid capex and working capital pressures. Strong balance sheet (debt-free, high equity). 85th AGM (Sep 25, 2025) passed all resolutions overwhelmingly.

Headwinds (Challenges Pressuring Performance)

  • Revenue & Profit Decline: Consolidated H1FY26 revenue ₹1,121 Cr (down 3% YoY from ₹1,152 Cr); PBT ₹198 Cr (down 22% YoY from ₹253 Cr). Standalone similar trend. Q2FY26 particularly weak (revenue ~₹463 Cr, PBT ₹35 Cr vs Q1 ₹646 Cr, PBT ₹161 Cr).
  • Margin Pressure: Higher material costs, employee expenses, other expenses (up YoY). Fair value loss in Q2 (₹14 Cr) offset by H1 gain (₹191 Cr), causing volatility.
  • Working Capital Deterioration: Inventories up ₹59 Cr YoY (to ₹435 Cr cons.); trade receivables up ₹54 Cr (to ₹164 Cr). Cash from ops negative ₹2 Cr (vs +₹225 Cr YoY), due to inventory build-up and slower collections.
  • Capex Drag: ₹81 Cr PPE acquisition in H1; CWIP ₹163 Cr signals ongoing investments straining cash (net investing outflow ₹68 Cr standalone).

Tailwinds (Supportive Factors)

  • Debt-Free Balance Sheet: Zero borrowings; equity ₹2,431 Cr cons. (84% of assets); liquid investments ₹387 Cr + cash ₹36 Cr. Total assets ₹2,886 Cr.
  • Regulatory Milestone: ANDA approval expands US generics portfolio (Pilocarpine eye drops for glaucoma). Complements existing US/UK/SA subsidiaries.
  • Shareholder Approval: 85th AGM resolutions (financials adoption, director re-appointments, auditors) passed with 96-100% favor (minor dissent <4%).
  • Auditor Comfort: Unmodified limited review by BSR & Co. on H1 results.

Growth Prospects

  • US/Export Expansion: ANDA win positions FDC for US market share in ophthalmics (high-margin generics). Subsidiaries (FDC Inc. US, FDC Intl. UK, Fair Deal SA) contribute ~2% revenue but growing.
  • Capex for Capacity: High PPE/CWIP (₹973 Cr non-current assets) supports formulations scale-up. Single pharma segment stable.
  • FY26 Potential: H1 dip may be cyclical; prior FY25 full-year revenue ₹2,108 Cr, PBT ₹357 Cr. New product launch + inventory normalization could drive 5-10% revenue growth if US ramps up.
  • Dividend History: ₹5 interim FY25 signals payout capacity (yield ~2-3% at current prices).
