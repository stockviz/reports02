Endurance Technologies Limited (ENDURANCE) Analysis

Company Overview: Endurance is a leading Indian automotive components manufacturer (aluminium castings, brakes, suspensions, alloy wheels, transmissions, BMS/battery packs) serving 2W/3W/4W OEMs in India and 4W in Europe (Italy/Germany). Q2 FY26 (ended Sep 2025) showed resilient growth amid strong Indian auto demand and Stöferle acquisition benefits. Standalone revenue +16.2% YoY to ₹2,692 Cr; Consolidated +22.6% to ₹3,604 Cr (72% from India ops).

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Robust Industry Volumes: India 2W sales +10.3% YoY (6.9 Mn units), 3W +21.4%, 4W +3.5%; Europe registrations +7.7%. Endurance outperformed (standalone +16.2% vs industry +9.5%).
  • Order Pipeline & Diversification: H1 FY26 wins: India ₹909 Cr (ex-Bajaj), Europe €12.7 Mn. Cumulative EV orders ₹4,671 Cr (peak ₹3,500 Cr by FY28), RFQs ₹4,209 Cr. 4W wins ₹356 Cr H1; non-auto (solar dampers ₹200 Cr, machined castings ₹388 Cr peak).
  • Capacity Expansions & Tech Edge: 5x ABS ramp-up (2.4 Mn units/annum, SOP Q1 FY27); new plants (Chennai brakes Q2 FY27, AURIC castings/alloy Jan 2026, battery packs Jan 2026). Backward integration (ECU, hoses); 91 patents.
  • Acquisitions & Incentives: Stöferle (Germany, €80 Mn turnover, consolidated from Apr 2025); Maxwell full ownership (H1 turnover ₹74 Cr record). PSI incentives ₹70 Cr H1.
  • Aftermarket & Sustainability: Aftermarket focus (₹243 Cr H1 sales); ESG ratings up (CRISIL 59, SES 74); renewable energy 28%.
  • Margin Resilience: Consolidated EBITDA 13.8% (stable YoY); Europe 17.8% despite 3x energy costs.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Margin Pressure (India/Standalone): EBITDA margin dip to 12.5% (-110 bps YoY) from aluminum (+₹10 Cr impact, 55% of RM), R&D/people investments (₹10 Cr each), special projects (aftermarket/sourcing consultants, ₹10-20 Cr), seasonality in discounts.
  • Segment-Specific Weakness: Scooters -4.4% (HMSI drag); Europe production down (de-stocking, Germany -4%).
  • Input Costs & Macro: Aluminum volatility; Europe energy/gas 3x pre-COVID; global uncertainties (US 50% tariffs on exports, slower FY27 growth forecast).
  • Working Capital Strain: H1 inventories +₹265 Cr, receivables +₹314 Cr YoY; cash from ops ₹657 Cr but net cash down ₹422 Cr (high capex).

Growth Prospects

  • High Visibility Ramp-Ups: ABS (regulatory tailwind, single/dual channel SOPs imminent); EV peak ₹3,500 Cr FY28 (castings ₹519 Cr, brakes ₹300 Cr); alloy wheels fully booked (9 Mn/annum); aluminum forging ₹140 Cr FY27.
  • New Avenues: Battery packs (₹300 Cr SOP Jan 2026, non-auto potential); solar dampers (multi-OEM); 4W proprietary (driveshafts, foundation brakes, suspensions); aftermarket to 2030 goals.
  • Capex-Led Expansion: FY26 capex ~₹750-800 Cr India + €30-32 Mn Europe (3 new plants, R&D doubling). Line of sight: India EV ₹1,500 Cr wins next 12-18 months; total order book peaks FY28.
  • Europe Stability: EV/hybrid share 62% (Q2: BEV 17%, PHEV 10%, hybrid 34%); Stöferle boosts machined castings.
  • Outlook: Profitable growth focus; FY26 guidance intact (strong H2 festive/rural demand, ABS/GST benefits). Medium-term: EV/4W/non-auto to drive 15-20% CAGR.

Key Risks

  • Commodity/Execution: Aluminum price spikes; capex delays (₹1,150 Cr H1 FY26, debt up); new plant SOP slippages (e.g., regulatory ABS timeline).
  • Customer Concentration: Heavy reliance on top OEMs (Bajaj/HMSI excluded in wins; Europe 50% Stellantis/VW).
  • Geopolitical/Macro: US tariffs hit exports; Europe Green Deal/chip shortages (no current impact); rupee depreciation aids exports but forex volatility.
  • Competition/EV Ramp: Late entrant in 4W suspension; EV order conversion from RFQs; tech validation for battery packs/high-voltage BMS.
  • Margin Dilution: Ongoing investments (R&D, consultants till Q4 FY26); working capital drag on cash (net debt ₹920 Cr cons.).
  • Regulatory/External: ABS guideline delays; OEM volume cuts (e.g., scooter de-growth spillover).

Overall Summary: Endurance is well-positioned for 15-20% growth (FY26-28) via EV/ABS/4W ramps and Europe synergies, outweighing near-term margin headwinds. Strong order book, capex execution, and diversification mitigate risks. Buy/Hold for long-term investors; monitor aluminum prices and Q3 festive volumes. (Valuation context: Trailing PE ~25-30x based on historicals; forward growth justifies premium.)
