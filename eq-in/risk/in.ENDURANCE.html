Endurance Technologies Limited (ENDURANCE) - Q2 & H1 FY26 Analysis

Overview: Endurance Technologies, a leading auto components manufacturer (focus: 2W/3W suspensions, brakes, castings, alloy wheels, EV BMS/battery packs), reported robust revenue growth in Q2 FY26 (ended Sep 2025), outperforming Indian 2W industry (+10.3% YoY). Standalone India business grew 16.2% YoY to ₹2,692 Cr (EBITDA margin dipped to 12.5%), driven by festive demand and capacity utilization. Consolidated growth was 22.6% to ₹3,604 Cr (13.8% margin), boosted by Stöferle acquisition in Europe. H1 consolidated revenue +20% YoY to ₹6,958 Cr, PAT +11.5% to ₹454 Cr. Order inflows remain strong (H1 India ₹909 Cr ex-Bajaj), with EV/non-auto diversification key themes. Capex remains aggressive (~₹750-800 Cr FY26 India est.).

Below is a structured analysis of headwinds, tailwinds, growth prospects, and key risks, derived from earnings call, financials, presentation, and filings.

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)
  • Volume Outperformance & Market Share Gains: Standalone revenue +16.2% vs. industry 2W +10.3% (motorcycles +9.6%, 3W +21.4%). Market leader in inverted forks (43% share), alloy wheels (full capacity booked: 9.1 Mn/annum). Scooter dip offset by MC/3W/4W ramps.
  • Order Momentum: H1 India wins ₹909 Cr (61% 4W, incl. Kia/Hyundai ₹146 Cr, US EV ₹103 Cr); cumulative EV ₹1,012 Cr (ex-Bajaj). Europe €12.7 Mn. RFQs ₹4,209 Cr; expect >₹1,500 Cr wins in 12-18 months. Peak realization: ₹3,500 Cr by FY28.
  • Strategic Expansions & Diversification:
    • EV/Battery: Maxwell H1 ₹74 Cr (+105% YoY), new wins ₹21 Cr (cumulative ₹209 Cr). Battery pack SOP Jan 2026 (₹300 Cr platform).
    • ABS: 5x capacity to 2.4 Mn/annum (Phase 1 Q1 FY27); dual-channel SOPs imminent.
    • New Plants: Alloy wheels (₹600 Cr peak Q2 FY27), Chennai brakes (7 Mn discs FY27+), AURIC castings (US/UK/Yazaki ₹388 Cr peak).
    • Non-Auto: Solar dampers (₹200 Cr won, more RFQs), aluminum forging (₹140 Cr FY27).
  • Europe Strength: +32.5% YoY (ex-Stöferle/tooling: +7.8% matching registrations). EV/Hybrid share 62% (H1); Stöferle (€80 Mn revenue) fully consolidated from Apr 2025.
  • Policy/Support: GST cuts, PSI incentives (₹70 Cr H1 booked), RBI growth forecast upgrade (6.8% FY26).
  • Operational Efficiency: Aftermarket push (₹243 Cr H1), backward integration (hoses/ECU), R&D upgrades.
Headwinds (Challenges)
  • Margin Pressure (Standalone India): EBITDA margin -110 bps YoY to 12.5% (H1 12.7% vs 13.6%).
    • Aluminum (55% RM) +40% of cost inflation (₹10 Cr impact).
    • Investments: People (strategy/R&D/sourcing), special projects (aftermarket/sourcing consulting: ₹20 Cr, till Q4 FY26).
    • Seasonality: Turnover discounts shifted to H2; R&D material for ABS/suspensions.
  • Segment-Specific Weakness: Scooters -4.4% (HMSI drag); Europe production down (de-stocking, holidays).
  • Input & Macro Costs: Europe energy 3x pre-COVID (electricity -8% QoQ but high base); raw material volatility.
  • Forex/Acquisition Drag: Rupee strength muted Europe INR growth; Stöferle integration (depreciation step-up).
Growth Prospects
  • Near-Term (FY26-27): Revenue CAGR 15-20% est. (India 13-15%, Europe stable+acquisitions). ABS/regulation tailwind (single-channel for <125cc 2W); festive spillover; new SOPs (battery packs, alloy wheels Oct 2025).
  • Medium-Term (FY27-28): Peak orders ₹3,500 Cr realization; EV ramp (₹1,195 Cr incl. Bajaj); 4W proprietary (driveshafts, foundation brakes, suspensions). Aftermarket to 2030 goals. Non-auto (solar, machined castings) 10-15% potential.
  • Long-Term: EV/Hybrid dominance (Europe ICE to 25% by FY28); Maxwell scaling (SMT lines to 88k/month); global diversification (US/UK exports). Target: Profitable growth, margin recovery to 13-14%+ via mix/cost controls.
  • Capex ROI: H1 ₹460 Cr (India), Europe €22 Mn; expansions create 2.4 Mn ABS, 7.6 Mn disc brakes capacity.
