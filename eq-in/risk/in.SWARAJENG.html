Analysis of Swaraj Engines Limited (SWARAJENG)

Swaraj Engines Limited (SEL), a key supplier of diesel engines to Mahindra & Mahindra’s Swaraj Division, reported record Q2/H1 FY26 results (ended Sep 2025), with revenue up 8.5%/12% YoY, PAT up 9.4%/12.5% YoY, and engine volumes hitting first-ever 100k+ units in H1 (+12.8% YoY). Strong balance sheet (net cash positive, low debt), healthy cash flows (₹113 Cr operating CF), and high ROE (~50% annualized) underscore operational strength. Recent board changes include Chairman transition and addition of industry-expert director.

Tailwinds

  • Robust Demand & Volumes: Record engine sales (Q2: 51k units +9% YoY; H1: 100k+ units), driven by sustained tractor demand amid favorable rural sentiment.
  • Operational Efficiency: Stable margins (Op. PBT margin ~13.5%), controlled costs (materials ~79% of revenue), inventory reduction (₹736 Cr vs ₹784 Cr FY25 end), and strong cash conversion.
  • Financial Strength: Zero debt (ex-lease), ₹13.6k Cr bank deposits/deposits, high dividend payout (₹127 Cr in H1), EPS ₹82 (H1, +12% YoY).
  • Strategic Ties: 100%+ reliance on M&M Swaraj Division benefits from promoter synergy; new Director (Gaganjot Singh, Swaraj Division CEO) enhances alignment/expertise.
  • Market Position: Single-segment focus (diesel engines/components) in growing Indian tractor market (~9-10% CAGR expected FY25-30).

Headwinds

  • Working Capital Strain: Trade receivables up to ₹19.9k Cr (+12% QoQ), payables ₹24.3k Cr (+10% YoY), signaling potential liquidity pressure despite strong CF.
  • Cash Burn: Net cash down ₹565 Cr (to ₹91 Cr) due to dividends/capex; deposits drawn down ₹3k Cr.
  • Cyclical Exposure: Agri/tractor sector vulnerability to monsoons, input costs, and slowing rural recovery.
  • Management Transition: Chairman resignation (Rajesh Jejurikar) could cause short-term uncertainty, though seamless handover.

Growth Prospects

  • Volume-Led Expansion: H1 momentum positions FY26 for 10-15% top-line growth; tractor industry tailwinds (govt. schemes, farm mechanization) could push annual volumes >200k.
  • Margin Upside: Pricing power from M&M, cost discipline, and scale could sustain 12-14% Op. margins; FY25 PAT ₹166 Cr suggests FY26 potential ₹220-240 Cr.
  • Capex Support: ₹1.4k Cr PPE addition in H1 enables capacity ramp-up.
  • Long-Term: Rural capex cycle, export potential via M&M, and diesel engine relevance in tractors (hybrids/EVs slower in segment) support 12-15% CAGR over 3-5 years.

Key Risks

  • Customer Concentration: Near-total dependence on M&M Swaraj (~95%+ revenue); any slowdown/relationship shift is existential.
  • Agri Volatility: Monsoon failures, subsidy cuts, or raw material inflation (metals/fuel) could hit volumes/margins (e.g., inventory changes volatile).
  • Regulatory/Transition: Diesel emission norms, shift to BS-VI/alt fuels, or EV adoption in tractors pose medium-term threats.
  • Execution: High receivables/payables stretch (DSO ~140 days), forex (minor), and competition from in-house M&M engines or rivals (e.g., Cummins).
  • Governance: Postal ballot for new Director approval (due Dec 2025); any dissent could signal issues (low likelihood).

Overall Summary: SEL’s pristine financials and volume records signal strong momentum (target multiples: 30-35x FY26 EPS), but agri-cyclicality and M&M reliance cap upside. Buy/Accumulate on dips for 15-20% FY26 returns; monitor Q3 volumes and rural indicators. Risks tilted cyclical, not structural.
