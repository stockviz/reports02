Bajaj Finserv Ltd (BAJAJFINSV) Analysis: Headwinds, Tailwinds, Growth Prospects, and Key Risks

Summary Overview: Bajaj Finserv delivered resilient Q2/H1 FY26 results with consolidated revenue +11-12% YoY and PAT +8-19% YoY (ex-MTM stronger at +12-23%). Lending (BFL/BHFL) drove growth (AUM +24%), insurance showed profitability gains (Life VNB +50%, General GWP ex-adj +18%), but offset by GST ITC losses (~₹112 Cr in Life Q2) and elevated credit costs. Emerging businesses scaling but loss-making. Acquisition of Allianz stake progressing (names changed to Bajaj Life/General Insurance). Overall, balanced portfolio positions for mid-teens growth amid macro tailwinds like GST reforms, but near-term pressures from costs/regulations.

Headwinds (Challenges Pressuring Performance)

  • Credit Costs & Asset Quality: BFL’s loan losses/provisions +19% YoY to ₹2,269 Cr (Q2), driven by 2/3-wheeler and unsecured MSME (AUM growth cut to 12-13% FY26). GNPA/NNPA up to 1.24%/0.60% (vs 1.06%/0.46% YoY).
  • GST ITC Loss: Life Insurance PAT down 91% YoY to ₹13 Cr (Q2) due to ₹112 Cr hit (140 bps NBM drag H1; annualized ~450 bps). Short-term pressure expected for 2 quarters.
  • Accounting/Regulatory Changes: General Insurance 1/n rule (from Oct’24) reduced GWP comparability (+9% reported vs +14% ex-1/n); COR stable but underwriting loss widened to ₹92 Cr.
  • Competitive Intensity: Housing finance AUM growth despite “heightened competition/portfolio attrition”; MSME volumes cut 25%.
  • MTM Volatility: Insurance MTM losses ₹161 Cr Q2, vitiating PAT growth.

Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts)

  • Lending Momentum: BFL AUM +24% to ₹462k Cr; new loans +26% (1.22 Cr). BHFL AUM +24%, NIM stable, lowest industry credit cost (0.18%). Deposits aiding CoF (7.52% Q2).
  • Insurance Profitability: Life VNB +50% (₹367 Cr Q2), NBM +630 bps to 17.1% (ex-GST 19.3%; H1 highest ever 14.8%). General outperforms industry (GWP ex-adj +18% vs industry +14%; COR ~101-102%, lowest in multiline). High solvency (Life 346%, General 339%).
  • GST Reforms: Exemption on retail life/health premiums boosts affordability/accessibility (full pass-through to customers); tailwind for industry/volume despite ITC hit.
  • Acquisition Progress: Allianz stake buyout (26%) nearing; 100% Bajaj ownership post-Oct’26 enhances control/branding.
  • Opex Discipline: BFL opex/NTI -60 bps to 32.6%; BHFL -90 bps to 19.6%.

Growth Prospects (Medium-Term Outlook)

  • Lending (Core Driver, ~55% Revenue): AUM targeting 20-25% FY26; cross-sell focus (7.13 Cr franchise). BFL festive momentum strong; BHFL distributed growth (home +19%, LAP +29%).
  • Insurance (~24% Revenue): Life “2.0” strategy (protection +71%, renewal +30%) eyes teens growth H2FY26; General +5-10% delta over industry via commercial/motor. Post-acquisition synergies.
  • Emerging (~5-10% Revenue): Health txns +158% YoY (6.2 Mn); AMC AUM +77% YoY (₹28.8k Cr, #25 rank); Markets partners +101. Path to breakeven (Health FY28).
  • Overall: 12-15% revenue CAGR feasible (diversified lifecycle offerings, 5.25L+ distributors, pan-India reach). Macro: GDP growth, rising incomes, fintech/ONDC tailwinds.

Key Risks

Risk Category Description Mitigants
Credit/Asset Quality Deterioration in consumer/MSME (elevated provisions); economic slowdown. Strong CRAR (21.23%), PCR 52%; granular portfolio; analytics/AI.
Regulatory GST ITC persistence, IndAS/RBC for insurance (FY27?), rate hikes. High solvency (300%+); agile pricing/underwriting.
Competition Margin pressure in lending/insurance from peers/banks. Differentiation (cross-sell, tech), low NPAs (BHFL 0.26%).
Execution Emerging biz losses (₹292 Cr H1); acquisition delays/integration. No capex since FY22 (Markets), scaling traction.
Macro Interest rates (CoF 7.55-7.60%), inflation impacting consumption. Diversified borrowings (52% money mkt), ALM focus.

Investment Thesis: Strong fundamentals with lending/insurance tailwinds offsetting near-term headwinds. Growth intact at 15%+ PAT CAGR; monitor credit costs/GST mitigation. Attractive for diversified BFSI exposure. (Ratings not provided; based on FY26 outlook.)
