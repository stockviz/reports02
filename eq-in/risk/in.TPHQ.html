TPHQ (Teamo Productions HQ Limited) Analysis

Overview: TPHQ (NSE: TPHQ, BSE: 533048), formerly GI Engineering Solutions, is a small-cap listed entity with a pivot toward media/production (name change) but dominated by Infrastructure Trading (main revenue driver), minor Shares/Securities dealing, and negligible Film ops. FY26 H1 (ended Sep 2025) showed explosive revenue growth (₹7,931L ops rev., +171% YoY) driven by Infra segment (₹7,023L), but margins compressed (PBT ₹174L, -56% YoY) amid rising costs. Balance sheet equity-heavy (₹13,719L total equity), but liquidity strained (cash ₹40L). No debt. Unaudited, limited review clean.

Tailwinds (Positive Momentum)

  • Revenue Surge: Infra trading boomed (Q2 rev. ₹5,105L, +126% QoQ/+136% YoY), signaling strong demand recovery. H1 total income +160% YoY.
  • Equity Strength: Massive ₹10,962L share capital (post-warrant forfeitures adding to reserves); Other Equity up to ₹2,757L.
  • Interest/Other Income: ₹126L H1 (stable contributor).
  • Cost Discipline: Employee expenses low (₹23L H1); no borrowings/finance costs.
  • Segment Diversification Potential: Intangibles under dev. (₹1,066L, +9%) hints at Film growth; unconsolidated Aus/UK subs could add scale.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Margin Erosion: Gross margins thin (Infra segment ~1-2%); PBT margins fell to 2.2% H1 from 12.8% prior (high Purchases 99% of rev.).
  • Liquidity Crunch: CFO -₹4,683L (worst-ever) due to WC ballooning; cash down 32% to ₹40L.
  • Receivables Explosion: Trade rec. +57% to ₹8,884L (>100% of H1 rev.), signaling delayed collections.
  • Segment Weakness: Shares dealing turned negative (H1 net gain ₹8L vs. ₹228L prior); Film dormant.

Growth Prospects

  • High (Short-Term): Infra trading poised for continuation if capex cycle sustains (top customers drove 60%+ Q2 rev.). H1 rev. already > FY25 full-year.
  • Medium-Term: Film/Productions ramp-up via intangibles/subs; net revenue presentation (Ind AS 109) improves transparency for trading.
  • Catalysts: No CSR threshold breach signals lean ops; warrant forfeitures boosted reserves for reinvestment. EPS stable at ₹0.01.
  • Target: 20-30%+ rev. CAGR if collections improve; mcap upside from ₹200-300Cr range (inferred from equity).
