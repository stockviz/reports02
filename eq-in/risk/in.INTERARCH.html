Analysis of Interarch Building Solutions Limited (INTERARCH)

Company Overview: Interarch is a leading integrated player in India’s Pre-Engineered Building (PEB) sector, specializing in turnkey steel construction solutions (PEB contracts, metal roofing/cladding via TRACDEK®, and non-industrial buildings via Interarch Life). FY25 revenue: ~₹1,454 Cr (top-2 integrated PEB player by capacity at 201k MT). Strong Q2/H1 FY26 results reflect robust execution amid industry tailwinds.

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Robust Financial Momentum: Q2 FY26 revenue hit record ₹491 Cr (+52% YoY), H1 ₹872 Cr (+39% YoY); EBITDA +65% YoY (Q2), margins stable at ~8.4-8.5%; PAT +56% YoY (Q2), net cash positive with ₹143 Cr cash equivalents. Order book at ₹1,634 Cr (Oct 31, 2025) signals visibility.
  • Capacity Expansion: Total capacity ~201k MT (utilizable ~168k MT); recent commissioning of AP Phase 2 (+40k MT) & Kichha line (+15k MT). Upcoming: Gujarat PEB (40k MT, capex ₹70 Cr), AP Heavy Steel Structures (25k MT Phase 1, ₹100 Cr). Enhances pan-India footprint (5 plants operational/planned).
  • Industry & Policy Support: PEB market ~₹195 Bn (FY24), growing 10-14% CAGR to ₹330-340 Bn by FY29 (industrial 64-66% share). Tailwinds from PLI scheme, NIP (₹111 Tn infra spend), PMAY-U, Atmanirbhar Bharat, Bharatmala/Sagarmala. Shift to organized players (40-45% share vs. unorganized 55-60%).
  • Strategic Initiatives: Partnerships (Mold-Tek for exports, JSPL for urban infra/heavy structures); certifications (IAS, ISO 45001/14001, ICC); sustainability (rooftop solar). Focus on EV/semicon/data centers; repeat orders ~73% revenue (FY25).
  • Balance Sheet Strength: Debt-free post-IPO (₹20 Bn raised Aug 2024); efficient working capital; ROE trajectory improving (PAT margins 7%).

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Tax & Regulatory Scrutiny: Multiple GST notices/orders (Sep 2025 disclosures): | Authority | Demand (₹) | Issue | |—|—|—| | Maharashtra (FY22) | 23.1L | ITC mismatch | | Jaipur CGST (Appeal rejection) | 97.5L | ITC reversal/interest | | AP (FY22) | 35.4L | Tax short-pay/blocked ITC | | Total | ~1.56 Cr | Company to reply/appeal; no material financial impact yet.
    • Income tax search (Aug 18-22, 2025) at offices/residences; no demands raised (auditor emphasis of matter).
  • Margin Pressure: Raw material costs (steel) volatile; erection/job work up YoY, offset by scale but EBITDA margins dipped slightly H1 FY26 (8.4% vs. 8.3% prior).
  • Execution Delays: Capex-intensive expansions (₹223 Cr invested FY25); working capital changes negative (-₹21 Cr H1 FY26 cash flow).
  • Competition: Organized sector growing faster (11.4% CAGR top-6 vs. 8.5% industry), but unorganized ~55% market share persists.

Growth Prospects

  • High Visibility: Order book supports H2 FY26 guidance; industrial/manufacturing ~73% revenue; entry into multi-storey/heavy structures via AP plant.
  • Market Expansion: PEB penetration low (~3-5% of construction); shift from RCC (faster 40-50% timelines, 25% less labor, sustainable). Target: Warehouses, data centers, renewables, logistics (e.g., orders from Rungta Mines, Havells, Jindal).
  • Geographic/Export Push: New Gujarat plant for EV/semicon hubs; Mold-Tek collab for exports (Middle East/Africa/SE Asia). Design centers in Noida/Chennai/Hyderabad (155+ engineers).
  • Capex Utilization: Post-expansion capacity to drive 20-25%+ revenue CAGR; IPO proceeds deployed (₹15 Cr utilized H1 FY26, ₹3.5 Cr unutilized).
  • Guidance Track: On path for FY26 targets; 40+ years track record (756 PEB contracts FY15-25).

Projected Outlook: 20-30% revenue CAGR FY26-28 feasible (industry 10-14%, company outpacing via capacity/debt-free status). PAT margins sustainable at 6.5-7.5%.
