Analysis of 360 ONE WAM Limited (NSE: 360ONE, BSE: 542772)

360 ONE WAM is a leading Indian wealth and asset manager with ~Rs 6.72 lakh Cr total AUM (Sep 2025), split into Wealth (~86%) and Asset Management (~14%). Q2 FY26 showed strong momentum: Revenue +31.5% YoY to Rs 813 Cr, PAT +27.7% YoY to Rs 316 Cr, ARR AUM +21.7% YoY to Rs 2.95 lakh Cr. Key drivers include UBS India WM acquisition (Sep 2025), B&K Securities integration, and robust net flows (H1 FY26: Rs 32k Cr ARR). Below is a structured summary of tailwinds, headwinds, growth prospects, and key risks.

Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts)

  • Strong Organic Growth & Momentum: ARR AUM up 21.7% YoY (Wealth +29.6%, Asset Mgmt +7.4%); H1 net flows Rs 32k Cr. ARR retention improved to 76 bps (Wealth 72 bps, AMC 83 bps). Transactional/brokerage revenue +9.3% YoY.
  • Strategic Wins: UBS deal adds scale/global reach (warrants for 4.95% stake issued); B&K integration complete, unlocking ECM/broking synergies + access to 700 corporate treasuries. ET Money entry into mass affluent.
  • Financial Health: Tangible RoE 20.6%; cost-income stable ~49%; dividend Rs 6/share (Q2). Market cap ~Rs 41k Cr; backed by Bain Capital, General Atlantic.
  • Recognition & ESG: 175+ awards (e.g., “India’s Best Wealth Manager” Euromoney 2025); ESG score 78.2/100 (top-tier).
  • Employee Alignment: New ESOP schemes (37.33 lakh options) for retention/performance, covering leadership and 100% eligible staff.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Regulatory Noise: Recent SEBI settlement (Rs 14.62 lakh for 360 ONE PE Fund violation on AIF investments; corrected Oct 2024, no material impact). Ongoing scrutiny in alternates/funds space.
  • Margin Pressure: Cost-income rose to 49.2% (employee costs +27% YoY due to B&K/ET Money ramp-up). Transactional revenue volatile (-13% H1 YoY).
  • Macro Benignity: Q2 commentary notes “benign macro environment and market sentiment,” implying slower inflows vs. peak cycles.
  • Competitive Intensity: Crowded UHNI/HNI wealth space (HDFC, ICICI, Kotak); alternates growth lags public markets.

Growth Prospects (High Potential)

  • Wealth (Core Driver, 86% AUM): ARR AUM Rs 2.03 lakh Cr; target mass affluent (INR 10-50 Cr via ET Money/360 ONE Plus). Lending book +52% YoY to Rs 10.3k Cr. UBS synergies for global/HNI expansion; 8,500+ families.
  • Asset Management: Rs 92k Cr ARR AUM; alternates (PE/credit/RE) +21% YoY (Private Equity +21%, Real Assets +40%). New launches (SIF, commodities).
  • ARR Transition: 73% of op. revenue recurring (up from 67% YoY); 24-33% CAGR historical. Target: Full-stack (advisory/distribution/broking/lending/ECM).
  • Outlook: 20-30% AUM CAGR feasible (FY21-25: 24%); liquidity events supportive. Q3/H2 conferences signal investor engagement.
  • Dilution-Adjusted Pool: ESOPs <1.5% equity; performance-linked vesting (48-108 months).

Overall Summary: Bullish outlook with strong tailwinds from AUM scale-up, deals, and recurring shift outweighing minor headwinds. Growth prospects pinned on wealth expansion (UBS/ET Money) and alternates; expect 20-25% revenue CAGR. Risks tilted macro/regulatory but mitigated by leadership (awards, RoE). Stock well-positioned for re-rating on execution; monitor Q3 AUM/net flows. (Data as of Oct/Nov 2025 filings.)
