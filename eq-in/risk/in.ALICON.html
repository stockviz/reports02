Alicon Castalloy Limited (ALICON) Analysis: Q2 & H1 FY26 Overview

Company Snapshot: Alicon Castalloy is a leading Indian aluminum casting manufacturer for automotive (96% revenue: 44% 2W, 41% PV, 10% CV) and non-auto sectors. Global presence in 18 countries, with plants in India (Pune, Haryana) and Europe (Slovakia). FY25 revenue: Rs 1,724 Cr; Order book: Rs 9,100 Cr (FY24-FY29, ~Rs 8,400 Cr remaining post-executed Rs 650-700 Cr).

Q2 FY26 Financials (Consolidated): Revenue Rs 429 Cr (+2% QoQ, -8% YoY); EBITDA Rs 55.5 Cr (12.9%, +100 bps QoQ); PAT Rs 13.9 Cr (+49% QoQ). H1: Revenue Rs 848 Cr (-6% YoY); EBITDA Rs 105 Cr (12.4%); PAT Rs 23 Cr (-35% YoY). Gross margins improved to 48.9% (+300 bps QoQ) on better PV mix and efficiencies. Capex H1: Rs 63 Cr (FY26 guide: Rs 125-130 Cr).

Headwinds (Challenges Pressuring Performance): - US Tariffs: 50% on Indian exports (Aug 2025) hit CV customers (7-8% North America revenue); PV parts at 25%. Moderated US export volumes; no clarity on Indo-US deal. - Supply Chain Disruptions: China rare earth restrictions + semiconductor shortages delaying OEM production. - Customer/Geography Issues: UK OEM cyber-attack (5-week supply pause); US CV demand down 25-26%; one-time YoY base projects ended. - YoY Degrowth: Revenue -8% amid global auto volatility (despite +2.6% industry growth); uneven demand skewed Alicon’s exposure. - Operational: New plants scaling (sub-optimal fixed cost absorption); employee costs up on hires/increments.

Tailwinds (Positive Momentum Drivers): - Domestic Boost: GST rationalization (end-Sep) revived sentiment; 2W +10.6% YoY, CV +10.9%, PV +4.2%. Alicon’s PV +16% YoY, 2W +8%. - Margin Expansion: Gross margins +130 bps YoY/+300 bps QoQ via PV/high-value mix, automation, German experts, digital controls, robotics, solar (50-55% energy). - Order Inflows: 7 new parts (Rs 257 Cr/5 yrs: 1 ICE, 1 carbon-neutral, 1 structural, 4 non-auto); Italian supercar follow-on, German OEM dev order, defense PSU. - Sequential Recovery: 3rd straight QoQ revenue growth (>Rs 400 Cr run-rate in 7/8 quarters post-Q3 FY25 dip). - Diversification: Non-auto at 5% (up from 4%); DAR vertical pilots (defense/aerospace/railways); 91 customers, 808 live parts.

Growth Prospects (Medium-Term Outlook): - Revenue Trajectory: Double-digit FY27 growth expected (Q4 FY26 guidance); domestic ramp-up (PV/2W), eAxle/structural SOPs stabilizing; order book execution Rs 8,400 Cr (FY26-FY29). - Margin Upside: EBITDA 13-14.5% potential on volumes, efficiencies, PV/non-auto mix; full-year capex Rs 125-130 Cr for capacity/tech (new site possible, e.g., Khed City land bank). - Strategic Levers: Europe for new tech dev (eAxle, motor housing, supercar), shift mass production to India; EV/hybrid/carbon-neutral focus; DAR/non-auto risk mitigation. - Leadership: Smooth CEO transition (Sumit Bhatnagar from Apr 2026) with auto ops expertise.

Key Risks: - Macro/Trade: Prolonged US tariffs (high CV exposure), rare earth/semi shortages, global auto slowdown. - Execution: New program ramps, capex absorption, competition in high-value PV/EV castings. - Concentration: 96% auto-dependent; US/Europe customer risks (e.g., cyber/volumes). - Financial: Rising depreciation (Rs 26.6 Cr Q2), raw material volatility, forex (unhedged exports ~19%). - Other: Geopolitical (China supply), policy delays (scrappage/EV incentives), leadership transition.

Overall Summary & Outlook: Alicon shows resilience with QoQ recovery (revenue +2%, PAT +49%) amid YoY headwinds from tariffs/supply issues, pivoting to domestic strength (81% revenue) and diversification. Strong order book, margins, and DAR/non-auto signal 10-15%+ CAGR potential FY26-27 (revenue Rs 1,900-2,000 Cr), with EBITDA margins 13-15% if tariffs ease/global chains stabilize. Buy/Hold for long-term (target multiples: 15-18x FY27 EPS on EV/auto localization tailwinds), but monitor US policy (key near-term risk). Balanced risk-reward; promoter voting intact (AGM passed all resolutions).
