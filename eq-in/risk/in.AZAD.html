Summary Analysis for Azad Engineering Limited (AZAD)

Azad Engineering Limited is a precision engineering company specializing in highly complex, mission-critical components for aerospace & defence, energy (gas/thermal/nuclear turbines), and oil & gas sectors. It supplies to global OEMs (e.g., Mitsubishi, GE Vernova, Safran, Pratt & Whitney, Siemens) with long-term contracts, strong entry barriers (NADCAP accreditation, zero PPM defects), and expanding capacity. FY25 standalone revenue: ₹4,529 Cr (+33% YoY), EBITDA margin ~36%. H1FY26 showed record growth (revenue +32% YoY to ₹2,772 Cr, PAT +65% to ₹630 Cr). Below is a structured analysis based on the provided documents.

Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts)

  • Robust Financial Momentum: H1FY26 standalone revenue ₹2,772 Cr (+32% YoY), EBITDA ₹999 Cr (+37% YoY, 36% margin), PAT ₹630 Cr (+65% YoY, 23% margin). Q2FY26 revenue +28% YoY. Consistent margin expansion (EBITDA 31-36% over FY21-26) due to better pricing, domestic sourcing, and scale.
  • Strong Order Inflows & Book: Mitsubishi Phase 2 (USD 73 Mn, total USD 156 Mn); Pratt & Whitney Canada MTA for aircraft engines; Safran MoU for defence platforms; others (GE Vernova USD 165 Mn, Arabelle USD 40 Mn, Honeywell USD 16 Mn). Energy/O&G +36% YoY, Aerospace/Defence +30% YoY in H1FY26.
  • Capacity Expansion: 3 new customer-dedicated lean facilities inaugurated (Siemens, GE Vernova, MHI; ~22,000 sqm). Total under construction: ~94,900 sqm (Phase 1) + 67,000 sqm (Phase 2). Fungible facilities enhance scalability.
  • Strategic Credentials: NADCAP accreditation for subsidiary Azad VTC (coatings for aerospace). 96% revenue from private players, 82-92% exports to 12+ countries. Long-term OEM relationships (avg. 10+ years), Make in India alignment.
  • Governance: Smooth AGM (all resolutions passed 99.9%+), new CS appointment.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Cash Flow Pressure: H1FY26 operating cash flow negative (₹-765 Cr) due to working capital outflow (₹-1,530 Cr from receivables/inventories). High capex (CWIP ₹1,549 Cr) and rising debt (total borrowings ₹2,874 Cr vs. ₹2,383 Cr in Mar-25).
  • Cost Inflation: Employee expenses +44% YoY (expansion/hiring), depreciation +61% (new assets), finance costs +48% (growth funding). Consumption % down but absolute up.
  • Execution Intensity: Rapid scaling (revenue CAGR 39% FY21-25) strains operations; forex/other income volatile.
  • Market Concentration: Heavy reliance on OEMs (e.g., Mitsubishi, GE); confidentiality limits order visibility.

Growth Prospects

  • Near-Term (FY26): 25-30% topline guidance backed by orderbook, H2 ramp-up from new facilities/products. Energy/O&G (74% H1 mix, +36% YoY), Aerospace (17%, +30% YoY) to drive; exports/domestic balanced.
  • Medium-Term: Huge TAM (Aerospace CY29: ₹2,190 Bn; Energy turbines 2029: ₹310 Bn). Wallet share expansion (new components/platforms), adjacencies (nuclear, landing gears), inorganic M&A for tech/full-stack capabilities. Geographical push (Saudi MoU with Baker Hughes).
  • Long-Term: Indigenization (GTRE/DRDO contract), replacement demand in turbines, defence spend rise. Capacity to 115,000+ sqm supports 2-3x revenue potential; EBITDA margins sustainable at 35%+ via automation/lean ops.
