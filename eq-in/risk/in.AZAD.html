Summary Analysis for Azad Engineering Limited (AZAD)

Azad Engineering Limited is a precision engineering company specializing in highly complex, mission-critical components for aerospace & defence (A&D), energy turbines, and oil & gas sectors. It supplies to global OEMs (e.g., GE Vernova, Siemens Energy, Mitsubishi, Honeywell, Rolls-Royce) with ~96% export revenue. FY25 revenue was Rs 4,529 Mn (standalone), with H1FY26 showing 32% YoY growth. Below is a structured analysis based on the provided documents (Q2/H1FY26 results, investor presentation, order wins, expansions, and announcements).

Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts)

  • Robust Financial Momentum: H1FY26 standalone revenue up 32% YoY to Rs 2,772 Mn; EBITDA margin stable at 36%; PAT up 65% to Rs 630 Mn. Q2FY26 revenue +28%, PAT +57%. Strong pricing power and cost efficiencies (e.g., lower consumption % due to domestic sourcing).
  • Order Book Strength: Mitsubishi Phase 2 (total USD 156 Mn), GE Vernova (USD 165.5 Mn combined), others (e.g., Arabelle USD 40 Mn, BHEL, Honeywell USD 16 Mn). Long-term contracts (5+ years) provide revenue visibility.
  • Strategic Wins: New Pratt & Whitney Canada agreement (aircraft engine components); Safran MoU for defence platforms. Analyst meets signal investor interest.
  • Capacity Expansion: 3 new customer-dedicated facilities (Siemens, GE, MHI; ~22,000 sqm total) operational; Phase 1 expansion ~95,000 sqm underway. Fungible facilities enhance scalability.
  • Credentials Boost: Subsidiary Azad VTC’s NADCAP accreditation (coatings) strengthens A&D positioning.
  • Segment Tailwinds: Energy/O&G +36% YoY; A&D +30% on new product commercialization. Exports to 12+ countries; diversified portfolio (1,700+ qualified parts).

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Capex Intensity: CWIP up to Rs 1,549 Mn (vs. Rs 798 Mn Mar’25); total assets +5% QoQ but cash burn from ops (Rs -765 Mn H1FY26) due to working capital (inventories +23%, receivables +28%).
  • Leverage Rise: Borrowings up ~15% QoQ to Rs 2,874 Mn; finance costs +48% YoY, pressuring margins if rates stay high.
  • Cash Flow Strain: Negative CFO (Rs -765 Mn) from WC changes; reliant on financing/investing inflows. QIP proceeds (Rs 7 Bn raised Mar’25) partially deployed (Rs 3.8 Bn utilized H1FY26).
  • Macro Dependencies: 96% exports expose to forex volatility (unrealized gains noted); cyclical end-markets (A&D backlog delays, energy transition risks).
  • Execution Delays: Schedule changes noted in investor meets; confidentiality on key order details limits visibility.

Growth Prospects

  • Near-Term (FY26): 25-30% topline growth guided; H2FY26 acceleration from new capacities/orders. H1 already at ~60% of FY25 revenue.
  • Medium-Term: Wallet share gains (adjacencies like landing gears, nuclear); new clients via proven portfolio. A&D TAM INR 2,190 Bn by CY29; energy replacement demand. Inorganic M&A for large components/full-stack capabilities.
  • Long-Term: Saudi Arabia expansion (Baker Hughes MoU); end-to-end services (e.g., GTRE turbo engine assembly). 3Mn+ parts delivered with zero PPM defects; 30-48 month qualifications create moat. EBITDA CAGR 45% (FY21-25); ROCE ~89% (FY25).
  • Catalysts: QIP-funded capex; multi-year OEM contracts; indigenization push (BHEL, DRDO).

Key Risks

Risk Category Description Mitigants
Execution Delays in capex ramp-up, qualification failures for new products. Dedicated facilities; experienced mgmt (20+ yrs); NADCAP/ISO certifications.
Financial WC deterioration, debt servicing (interest coverage ~7x but rising); forex (96% exports). Strong margins (36%); QIP liquidity; interest income from deposits.
Customer/Concentration Reliance on top OEMs (75% gas turbine market via key clients); order confidentiality. 12+ countries; diversified (Energy 65%, A&D 30%); long relationships (10+ yrs avg).
Market/External Geopolitical (A&D supply chain), commodity prices, competition (China/EU/US). High barriers (zero PPM, quals); “Make in India” alignment.
Regulatory SEBI compliance (e.g., LODR disclosures); AGM resolutions passed overwhelmingly. Strong governance; independent board.

Overall Outlook: Strong tailwinds from orders/expansions outweigh headwinds; positioned for 25-30%+ growth with defensive moat in high-TAM sectors. Monitor WC/debt quarterly. Buy/Hold for growth investors; stock likely to re-rate on execution. (Analysis as of Nov 2025 data.)
