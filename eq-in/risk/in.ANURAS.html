Anupam Rasayan India Ltd. (ANURAS) Analysis

Overview: ANURAS is a custom synthesis specialty chemicals player focused on life sciences (84% revenue: agro, pharma, personal care) and performance materials (16%: polymers, electronics, dyes). Q2/H1 FY26 showed exceptional recovery with revenue surpassing FY25 full-year in H1, driven by agro cycle upturn, new launches, and order execution. Strong order book (₹14,646 Cr, 5-7 years) and backward integration via Tanfac subsidiary position it well. Below is a structured analysis based on earnings transcript, investor presentation, BRSR, and recent disclosures.

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)
  • Industry Recovery & Demand Revival: Chemical sector rebounding post headwinds; agro volumes improving, pharma/polymer ramp-ups (pharma +201% YoY H1, polymers +306%). US exports (key growth driver) tariff-exempt.
  • Execution Strength: 25-30% Q2 revenue from old inventory liquidation; 1 new pharma molecule launched (total 81 products). Order book to contribute ₹450 Cr FY26.
  • Financial Momentum: H1 consolidated revenue ₹1,217 Cr (+122% YoY); EBITDA ₹273 Cr (+93%); PAT ₹106 Cr (+147%). Standalone even stronger (+152% revenue). WC days improved (409→247 consolidated).
  • Strategic Moves: Tanfac revenue +67% H1; fluorination expansion (₹220-260 Mn potential); global BD team (US/Europe/Japan). Debt reduced post ₹278 Cr warrants (net debt ₹730 Cr).
  • Sustainability Edge: ZLD, net-zero targets (Scope 1/2 by 2027), 87% waste diversion; strong ESG (BRSR assured by TÜV SÜD).
Headwinds (Challenges Faced)
  • Margin Pressure: EBITDA margins dipped to 19.5% Q2/22.2% H1 (vs. 26-28% guided earlier) due to old inventory; ~30% revenue from lower-margin areas. Expect Q3 normalization.
  • Balance Sheet Strain: Standalone inventory ~365 days (₹1,300 Cr), receivables +₹300 Cr (inter-co timing); gross block doubled (₹3,400 Cr FY25) with past EBITDA decline due to agro slowdown.
  • Past Cycles: Agro headwinds (2 years) led to deferred supplies, high WC (700+ inventory days peak), extended debtor terms.
  • Customer Dependency: Top-10 customers = 81% Q2 revenue; promoter pledging for warrants.
Growth Prospects
  • FY26 Guidance: 50%+ revenue growth; EBITDA ~25% (±1%). H1 already >FY25 full-year.

  • Medium-Term: Multi-decade MNC ties (31 MNCs); exports 58%; 50%+ FY26 growth sustainable via diversification.

Growth prospects robust (50%+ FY26), but monitor margins/WC. Risks tilted operational but mitigated by visibility. Positive outlook for 12-24 months; suitable for growth-oriented investors.
