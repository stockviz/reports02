Analysis of Campus Activewear Ltd. (CAMPUS) - Headwinds, Tailwinds, Growth Prospects, and Key Risks

Campus Activewear Ltd., India’s largest sports and athleisure footwear brand by value/volume (~17% market share), reported strong Q2 FY26 results (ended Sep 2025): Revenue +16% YoY to INR 387 Cr, PAT +40% to INR 20 Cr. H1 FY26 revenue +8.5% YoY. The company benefits from a robust omnichannel presence (28K+ retailers, 295+ EBOs, strong online), in-house manufacturing (30.7M pairs capacity), and premiumization focus. Below is a structured analysis based on the earnings transcript, financial results, press release, and AGM voting.

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)
  • Premiumization Momentum: Premium segment (>INR 1,500) saliency rose to 57.2% (from 45.2% YoY), driving +8% ASP to INR 672. Sneakers grew 100%+ YoY; women’s mix up to 16.2% (from 14.2%), boosted by Kriti Sanon ambassadorship and NPD focus.
  • Distribution Strength: Core channel +20% YoY (key growth driver); stable channel inventory (~100 days); Retailer Meet (150+ attendees) enhanced engagement/shelf space.
  • GST Reforms: Recent cuts expected to spur demand, narrow unorganized price gap, and favor organized players like Campus (BIS norms curbed Chinese imports).
  • Capacity Expansion: New Pantnagar factory for premium uppers (Phase 1: +3L pairs/month); total Capex ~INR 230 Cr over 3 years for self-sufficiency in uppers/soles/assembly.
  • Margin Expansion: Q2 EBITDA margin +140 bps to 14%; gross margin +110 bps to 53.9% on better mix. Normalized H1 EBITDA ~16%.
  • Omnichannel Resilience: D2C offline +35% (LFS doors +200); online festive sales (BBD) shifted to Q3 but strong traction expected.
  • Other: Accessories ~2-3% revenue; apparel pilot in 60 EBOs; export push (Sri Lanka, Morocco) under own brand leveraging India’s low-cost edge.
Headwinds (Challenges)
  • Online Channel Weakness: +5.7-6% YoY growth; impacted by festive sales timing shift (BBD sales to Q3), business model change (channel partners charge freight directly, reducing revenue/expenses by INR 8 Cr; ~2% revenue drag).
  • Seasonal/Phasing Effects: Elevated A&P spends (10.5% vs. 8.5% normalized); Q1 warehouse shift disrupted growth; Haridwar-2 ramp-up added 40 bps employee cost hit.
  • Working Capital Pressure: NWC days improved to 82 (from 92) but absolute levels up (inventory +INR 12 Cr YoY) due to seasonality (Q3 peak), MSME payment norms (45 days vs. 90), and pre-festive buildup. Borrowings +INR 165 Cr (expected to normalize by FY-end via FD arbitrage).
  • Volume Moderation: Q2 +7.5% (despite 15-day GST disruption); H1 -2.5% due to Q1 issues and low-end price corrections.
  • Macro/External: Peers noted destocking from GST/high-price inventory clearance; muted Tier-1 demand in some consumer categories.
Growth Prospects
  • FY26 Outlook: Double-digit revenue growth targeted (Q2 momentum + GST tailwind); H2 expected stronger on festive normalization, secondary sales traction, and October trending “well.”
  • Category Expansion: Sneakers/women/kids (total ~20-22% mix) as key levers; +2% market saliency via NPD/incentives.
  • Channel Scaling: EBOs to 500 in 3 years (+70-75/year from FY27); exports to scale sustainably (vision by Apr 2026); online CAGR 35%+ historically.
  • Capacity/Margins: Pantnagar ramps to +72L pairs over 3 years; premium mix to drive 16-17%+ EBITDA margins (17-18% aspiration).
  • Market Opportunity: Organized sports/athleisure TAM growth; Campus covers 85% via pan-India network (700+ districts).
  • Long-Term: Apparel/accessories diversification; IP-protected tech (China/Vietnam-level stitching) for faster premium launches.
Key Risks
Risk Category Description Mitigants
Execution/Operational Capex delays (INR 110-115 Cr FY26 Pantnagar); plant ramp-up costs; WC cycle (seasonal inventory spikes). Phased rollout; stable channel inventory; FD-backed borrowings.
Demand/Competition GST destocking spillover; unorganized rebound; intense rivalry (international brands dominate premium). Premium focus + GST edge; distribution depth (15.6K direct retailers).
Financial Margin compression from marketing/raw materials; receivables volatility (online/LFS credit). Normalized 16% EBITDA; cash discounts for early payments.
Macro/Regulatory Festive slowdown; inflation/GST volatility; MSME payment changes inflating WC. Diversified channels; strong balance sheet (ROE 18%, ROCE 16.6%).
Other Export nascent (small revenue); inventory valuation change (to weighted avg., immaterial). Own-brand GTM; retrospective Ind AS 9 compliance.

AGM Note: Overwhelming approval (>99%) for financials, dividend, director re-appointment, and secretarial auditor signals strong governance/shareholder confidence.

Summary

Bull Case (Tailwinds Dominate): Campus is well-positioned for 12-15%+ FY26 growth via premiumization (sneakers/women), distribution muscle, GST boost, and capacity (Pantnagar). Margins sustainable at 16%+; EBO/export/apparel upside. Stock attractive for organized footwear play (undervalued vs. peers on growth).

Bear Case (Headwinds Materialize): Online weakness persists; WC strains erode margins; macro destocking hits festive Q3. Growth <10% if premium traction fades.

Overall Outlook: Positive. Resilient Q2 (+16% revenue, +40% PAT) amid headwinds; H2 inflection likely on GST/festives. Target FY26 revenue ~INR 1,800-1,900 Cr (12-20% growth); monitor Q3 for online/distribution momentum. Risks manageable; buy on dips for long-term premium story. (Valuation context: Not provided in docs; compare to FY25 P/E ~25-30x normalized EPS).
