Campus Activewear Ltd. (CAMPUS) Analysis

Overview: Campus Activewear Ltd. (NSE: CAMPUS, BSE: 543523) is India’s largest sports & athleisure footwear brand by value/volume (~17% market share). Q2 FY26 (Jul-Sep 2025) delivered strong results: Revenue +16% YoY to INR 387 Cr (distribution +20%), EBITDA margin +160 bps to 14%, PAT +40% to INR 20 Cr. H1 FY26 revenue +8.5% YoY to INR 730 Cr. Key drivers: premiumization (premium saliency 45%→57%), ASP +8% YoY to INR 672, women’s mix +200 bps to 16%. Capacity expansion (Pantnagar factory) and GST cuts are key catalysts. Balance sheet robust (ROE 18%, NWC days 82), but WC borrowings up seasonally.

Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts)

  • Premiumization & Mix Shift: Premium (>INR 1,500) saliency up 12 pts YoY; sneakers +100% YoY volumes. Women’s (16%) & kids (~5%) growing via NPDs, incentives, Kriti Sanon endorsement. ASP stable/up despite selective low-end price hikes.
  • Distribution Strength: 20% YoY growth; 300+ distributors, 28K retailers, 85% TAM coverage. Retailer meets (150+ attendees) boosted engagement/shelf space.
  • Margin Expansion: Gross margin +110 bps to 53.9% (better mix); EBITDA +32% YoY. Normalized EBITDA ~16% (post ad phasing).
  • GST Cut Benefits: 15-day Sep disruption over; price parity with unorganized players. Channel inventory stable (~100 days); secondary sales strong (Oct trending well).
  • Capacity & Supply Chain Control: INR 230 Cr 3-year CAPEX (Phase 1: +3L pairs/month uppers at Pantnagar). In-house premium uppers/soles (China/Vietnam tech) protects IP, cuts costs, enables MNC-quality sneakers.
  • Omnichannel Scale: EBOs stable at ~295 (35% D2C offline growth via +200 LFS doors); online 2.9 Mn pairs H1 (despite shift/BBD timing). Accessories ~3% revenue; apparel pilot in 60 EBOs.
  • Exports Nascent Upside: Own-brand GTM in Sri Lanka/Morocco; India’s low-cost edge vs China.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Online Softness: +6% YoY (H1 modest); impacted by BBD shift (5 days sales to Q3), business model change (partners charge freight; revenue/expenses -INR 8 Cr, ~2% drag).
  • Seasonal/Execution Hiccups: Q1 warehouse shift; Q2 ad spends high (10.5% vs normalized 8.5%). Haridwar2 ramp-up added 40 bps employee cost hit.
  • WC Pressure: Inventory +INR 118 Cr H1 (seasonal Q3 pre-build); MSME law (45-day payments); borrowings +INR 165 Cr (to normalize by FY-end via FDs arbitrage).
  • Volume Dip H1: -2.5% YoY (Q2 +7.5%); low-end corrections offset premium gains.
  • Macro/Channel Dynamics: Festive postponement, destocking in peers (minimal here, but monitored).

Growth Prospects

  • FY26 Outlook: Double-digit revenue growth (Q2 momentum + GST tailwind). H2 acceleration via Q3 festive peak, normalized online/ad phasing.
  • Medium-Term (2-3 Years):
    • Capacity: +72L pairs (uppers + assembly); self-sufficient Pantnagar.
    • EBOs: 70-75/year to ~500 stores.
    • Categories: Premium/sneakers/women/kids to drive ASP/mix; apparel scaling post-pilot.
    • Exports: Structured GTM; revenue vision by Apr 2026 (higher margins).
    • Market: Organized share gain (BIS limits Chinese imports); GST boosts volumes.
  • Overall Potential: 15-20% CAGR feasible via premium (57%+ saliency), women/kids (20%+ mix), exports (5-10% revenue).

Key Risks

Risk Category Description Mitigation
Execution/Capex Pantnagar/Haridwar ramp-up delays/costs (0.4% margin hit seen). Phased rollout; in-house tech focus.
Channel/Inventory Post-GST destocking (15 days hit); distributor inventory skew (100 days norm). Secondary focus; no overstocking.
Competition Unorganized/internationals (Nike/Adidas); shelf space battles. Premium differentiation, distribution depth.

Summary: Bullish with strong tailwinds outweighing headwinds. Q2 momentum (16% growth, 40% PAT surge) signals recovery from Q1 softness. Premiumization, GST, capacity add firepower for double-digit FY26/H2 acceleration. Risks manageable (seasonal WC, execution), but monitor festive secondary/online traction. Target 16-18% EBITDA margins long-term; stock poised for re-rating on growth visibility. Investors: Buy on dips for 15-20% upside (volume/mix led). (Data as of Nov 12, 2025 filings).
