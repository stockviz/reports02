Exicom Tele-Systems Limited (EXICOM) Analysis

Company Overview: Listed in March 2024 (IPO ₹400 Cr), Exicom is a key player in Critical Power (telecom infrastructure power systems, batteries; ~70% standalone revenue) and EV Charging (AC/DC chargers; ~30%). Acquired Tritium (Aug 2024) for global EV scale. Q2 FY26 standalone revenue ₹228 Cr (+54% YoY), consolidated ₹282 Cr (+84% YoY), but consolidated EBITDA loss ₹33 Cr due to Tritium.

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Robust India Demand: Critical Power rebounded 60% QoQ on BharatNet (5k sites delivered; backlog >₹1,400 Cr), BSNL/UCV projects (60% share), Li-ion battery shift (₹60 Cr orders). EV: Record 20k+ AC chargers, bus/truck/defense wins (e.g., 43x 240kW defense chargers).
  • Policy Boost: PM E-Drive (14k e-buses; ₹4,391 Cr subsidy), EV public charging guidelines, new launches (MG, Mahindra, Tata), e-trucking (Tata, Sany).
  • Capacity Ramp: Hyderabad plant (550 workforce; trial Nov 2025, full Jan 2026) for chargers/batteries/telecom; improves margins/yield.
  • Exports/Geography: Africa/SEA 11% revenue; first African tower co. order ($0.7-1M/qtr repeat). Tritium pipelines in US/EU/UK (Tri-Flex RFPs, DC-Flex for largest fleet rollout H2 CY26).
  • Funding: Rights issue (₹260 Cr utilized: debt repayment ₹55 Cr, Tritium ₹59 Cr); Tritium $40M external raise approved (limits Exicom exposure).

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Tritium Drag: Consolidated EBITDA -11.6% (losses ₹33 Cr; 15% QoQ better but ongoing). Fixed costs high; scaling delays (EBITDA breakeven Q4 FY27).
  • Margin Pressure: Standalone gross margin 26.4% (down QoQ on Li-ion mix); forex losses impacted EBITDA.
  • Revenue Lull Recovery: H1 standalone flat YoY (Q1 weak); monsoon delayed projects.
  • Guidance Cut: FY26 standalone revenue/EBITDA +20% (vs. prior 50%/250%); consolidated revenue +35% (cautious on EV/telecom slowdown).
  • Regulatory Scrutiny: DRI search (Oct 29, 2025) on import classification; legal opinion says compliant, no ops impact, but potential duties/tax overhang.

Growth Prospects

Segment Near-Term (FY26) Long-Term (FY27+)
Critical Power BharatNet III/BSNL tenders (₹700 Cr opp.); utilities pilots; exports +Li-ion (150/200Ah). Rural broadband saturation; tower upgrades (Li-ion conversion); ₹1,500 Cr Li-ion/DC market.
EV Charging PM E-Drive buses; OEM networks; e-trucks; Gen2 DC migration (100% customers). Hyderabad output from Q4. High-power (>180kW) for fleets/highways; e-commerce/home growth; Tritium scale (US/EU fleets). Consolidated EVSE +141% YoY ex-Tritium.
Overall Standalone EBITDA sustained growth; consolidated revenue +35% FY26. New plant + exports = margin expansion. Global EV infra (1.3L+ chargers sold); Tritium upside (Grid-Flex solar inverter); diversified revenue (India 55%, US/EU/Aus/SEA 10-20% each).

Target: H1 momentum into H2; Q3/Q4 stronger on tenders/EV resurgence.

Key Risks

  1. Tritium Turnaround (High): Execution delays on Tri-Flex/DC-Flex; $40M funding failure → deeper losses/cash burn.
  2. Regulatory/Compliance (Medium): DRI probe escalation (customs disputes on imports); forex volatility.
  3. Execution/Competition (Medium): Tender delays (BSNL eval.); margin erosion from mix/competition in EV/telecom.
  4. Macro/Demand (Medium): EV sales slowdown; telecom tower growth at 3.8% YoY (vs. 8% 5-yr CAGR).
  5. Financial/Liquidity (Low-Medium): High debt/finance costs (₹24 Cr H1 standalone); capex (Hyderabad ₹17 Cr remaining).
  6. Other: Supply chain (batteries/chips); forex (Tritium USD exposure).

Overall Outlook: Strong standalone momentum (EBITDA inflection) offsets Tritium pain; India tailwinds + plant ramp = 20-35% FY26 growth. Long-term EV leader, but monitor Tritium funding/regulatory risks. Recommendation: Accumulate on dips for 2-3 yr growth. (Ratings: Tailwinds > Headwinds; Prospects High with managed Risks).
