Hyundai Motor India Limited (HYUNDAI) Analysis

Summary Overview: Hyundai Motor India reported robust Q2/H1 FY26 results with YoY revenue/PAT growth driven by exports, margin expansion (EBITDA 13.9% in Q2), and SUV dominance (70%+ sales mix). Domestic volumes dipped YoY but grew QoQ amid strong rural penetration (23.6%). Key positives include the Venue relaunch (Nov 2025) from the new Talegaon plant, capacity expansion, and leadership transition. However, domestic softness, regulatory overhangs, and high capex pose near-term challenges. Outlook remains positive with export tailwinds, product pipeline (26 launches by 2030), and INR 45,000 Cr investment commitment.

Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts)

  • Financial Strength: Q2 FY26 revenue ₹174.6 Bn (+1.2% YoY), EBITDA ₹24.3 Bn (13.9% margin, +110 bps YoY), PAT ₹15.7 Bn (8.9% margin). H1 PAT up 5.5% YoY to ₹29.4 Bn. Strong cash flows (₹23 Bn operating cash H1) support capex/dividends.
  • Export Resilience: Exports up 21.5% YoY to 51.4k units in Q2 (28% of sales), offsetting domestic decline. Sequential domestic growth (+5.5% QoQ).
  • SUV & Rural Momentum: SUVs 68% mix (up from 67% YoY), rural sales at record 23.6%. Diesel/CNG fuel mix rising (16%/12%).
  • Product Pipeline: 21 interventions in H1 FY26 (e.g., Exter CNG, Creta editions). New Venue/N Line (priced ~₹7.9-9.1L ex-showroom) debuts advanced tech (ccNC NVIDIA, ADAS L2, 65+ safety features). First export from Talegaon plant (170k capacity).
  • Strategic Moves: 26.49% stake in wind farm (ESG play). Talegaon plant operational (Oct 2025). Experienced leadership: Tarun Garg (ex-Maruti Suzuki) as MD/CEO from Jan 2026 (postal ballot ongoing).
  • Market Positioning: Post-GST SUV reforms boosting demand; analyst plant visits signal engagement.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Domestic Volume Pressure: Q2 domestic sales down 6.6% YoY to 139.5k units (total sales flat YoY at 190.9k). Discounts/incentives impacted margins (PBT analysis shows price adjustments as drag).
  • Regulatory Noise: Partly favorable GST order (Aug 2025): Dropped ₹0.15 Cr demand but upheld ₹1.38 Cr tax + ₹0.14 Cr penalty (appeal planned; “no material impact”).
  • Capex Intensity: CWIP ₹61 Bn (up from ₹47 Bn YoY); H1 capex ₹26 Bn. Leverage low but cash conversion strained (inventories up ₹4.7 Bn).
  • Transition Risks: Leadership change (Unsoo Kim to Tarun Garg) via postal ballot (voting Nov 12-Dec 11, results Dec 15).
  • Macro/Industry: Intense SUV competition (Maruti, Tata); softening urban demand.

Growth Prospects

  • Volume/Market Share: Venue relaunch to revitalize compact SUV segment (legacy: 7L+ units sold). SUVs to drive 70%+ mix; rural/CNG tailwinds. Exports + capacity (Talegaon) target global “Make in India” push.
  • Revenue/Margins: 2-5% YoY H1 growth; cost reductions/FX gains aided 90 bps EBITDA expansion. FY26 guidance implicit: sustained 13%+ margins via mix/opex leverage.
  • Long-Term: INR 45,000 Cr investment for 26 products by 2030. Talegaon ramps to full capacity; EV/ADAS focus. ROCE 10.7% (Q2), EPS ₹19.35 (up QoQ).
  • Catalysts: Venue bookings/sales ramp (Nov 2025), Q3 results, ballot outcome, export acceleration.
