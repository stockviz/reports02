Hyundai Motor India Ltd. (HYUNDAI) Analysis Summary

Based on Q2/H1 FY26 results (ended Sep 30, 2025), Nov 2025 sales, investor presentation, board outcomes, and other disclosures.

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Robust Sales Momentum: Q2 FY26 total sales at 190,921 units (flat YoY but +5.9% QoQ); H1 domestic SUV share ~70%+. Nov 2025 sales +9.1% YoY (66,840 units), driven by exports (+26.9% YoY to 16,500 units) and new Venue (32k+ bookings in 1 month).
  • Product Strength & Mix: 21 interventions in H1 FY26 (e.g., Venue with ADAS L2, ccNC infotainment, OTA updates); SUVs/CNG/diesel/rural penetration at record 23.6%; GST 2.0 reforms boosting SUV demand.
  • Financial Resilience: Q2 revenue ₹174.6 Bn (+1.2% YoY), EBITDA ₹24.3 Bn (13.9% margin, +1.1% YoY), PAT ₹15.7 Bn (8.9% margin). H1 revenue +2.1% YoY to ₹338.7 Bn, PAT +2.7% to ₹29.4 Bn; margins expanded via cost reductions/FX gains/better mix.
  • Operational Expansion: Talegaon plant (170k capacity) operational from Oct 1, 2025; capex supports volume growth (CWIP ₹61 Bn).
  • Strategic Moves: 26.49% stake in wind farm (₹38 Cr investment); leadership upgrade with Tarun Garg as MD/CEO from Jan 2026 (experienced in sales/IPO/export push).

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Modest Domestic Growth: Domestic sales +4.3% YoY in Nov (50,340 units); Q2 domestic down ~7% YoY amid competitive pressures.
  • Margin Pressures: Discounts/price adjustments offset some gains (PBT movement shows -₹975 Mn YoY drag); material costs at 70.1% of revenue.
  • High Capex & Liquidity: Cash from ops ₹23 Bn (H1), but heavy investing (₹26 Bn capex); inventories up (₹38.7 Bn), trade receivables +25% QoQ.
  • Export Reliance: 28% of sales; vulnerable to global slowdowns despite strong YoY growth.

Growth Prospects

  • High: SUV-led portfolio (Exter/Venue/Creta) + rural/digital push; exports positioning India as global hub; Talegaon ramps to boost capacity/utilization.
  • Volume/Margin Upside: Target sustained 10%+ EBITDA margins via cost discipline/product mix; H1 ROCE 10.7%; FY26 EPS trajectory strong (Q2 ₹19.35).
  • Catalysts: Venue momentum, ADAS/software-defined vehicles, CNG expansion; renewable investments align with ESG.

Key Risks

Risk Category Details Mitigation
Regulatory/Compliance No provision for End-of-Life Vehicles (EPR) rules (effective Apr 2025); potential future liability as pricing/portal pending. Postal ballot for leadership change (results by Dec 15). Monitor notifications; strong compliance track record.
Market/Competition Intense rivalry (Maruti/Tata); slowing domestic demand if macros weaken (e.g., high rates/inflation). SUV differentiation, exports (27% mix).
Operational/Financial Forex/commodity volatility; high debt (₹8 Bn borrowings); dividend payout (₹17 Bn in H1) strains cash. Cost reductions/FX hedges; ₹56 Bn cash.
Execution Talegaon ramp-up delays; inventory buildup (if demand softens). Proven execution (21 launches).

Overall Outlook: Strong tailwinds from exports/SUVs/new plant outweigh headwinds; growth prospects rated High with 10-15% FY26 revenue/PAT CAGR potential. Monitor EPR regs and domestic macros. Buy/Hold for long-term; target upside on Venue/export beats. (Data as of Dec 1, 2025 filings.)
