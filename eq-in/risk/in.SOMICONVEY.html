Analysis of SOMICONVEY (Somi Conveyor Beltings Ltd.)

Company Overview: SOMICONVEY is a manufacturer of conveyor belts (fabric, steel cord, bucket elevator) primarily serving industrial sectors like steel and mining. Listed on BSE/NSE, it reported standalone H1 FY26 results (ended Sept 30, 2025) showing revenue decline amid capacity expansion. Recent highlights include a Rs. 30.4 Cr order from SAIL-Durgapur Steel Plant and smooth AGM outcomes.

Tailwinds (Positive Factors)

  • Strong Order Pipeline: Secured Rs. 30.39 Cr (~3% of FY25 revenue) domestic order from SAIL (a PSU giant) for heavy-duty nylon conveyor belts (14,800 meters). Delivery by Feb 2026 with price variation clause protects margins. Demonstrates client trust and potential for repeat PSU business.
  • Capacity Expansion: Mining line expansion underway—machinery installed, trial runs progressing. To boost compounding capacity for all grades (incl. steel cord), aiding timely order execution and revenue growth in H2 FY26/FY27.
  • Robust Balance Sheet: Net worth Rs. 786 Cr (up YoY), low debt (debt-equity 0.19x), strong liquidity (current ratio 2.52x, cash ~Rs. 107 lakhs). Positive operating cash flow (Rs. 97 Cr in H1) supports capex without dilution.
  • Governance: Overwhelming AGM approval (>99.97%) for directors, auditors, etc., signaling shareholder confidence.
  • Sector Demand: Steady industrial demand from steel/mining; low promoter interest in orders ensures arm’s-length dealings.

Headwinds (Negative Factors)

  • Revenue & Profit Pressure: H1 FY26 revenue down 7% YoY to Rs. 46 Cr; Q2 FY26 plunged 42% YoY to Rs. 19.7 Cr. Net profit fell 34% to Rs. 20.6 Cr. Margins squeezed (net profit ratio 4.5% vs 6.3%).
  • Operational Inefficiencies: Inventory turnover slowed to 1.87x (vs 2.7x), signaling potential overstocking or weak sales. High trade receivables (Rs. 261 Cr, ~57% of revenue) indicate collection delays.
  • Cost Volatility: High material costs (78% of expenses); PVC clause mitigates but requires index submissions. Employee/finance costs stable but other expenses up.

Growth Prospects

  • High: Capacity addition could drive 20-30% revenue growth in FY26/27 by improving execution (e.g., steel cord belts for mining). SAIL order validates PSU penetration; total order book visibility positive.
  • Medium-Term Catalysts: Expansion completion by FY26-end; potential for export/international orders (though currently domestic-focused). ROCE at 8.9% with low leverage offers room for inorganic growth. FY25 full-year revenue Rs. 101 Cr sets base for scaling to Rs. 120-140 Cr.
  • EPS Trajectory: H1 EPS Rs. 1.75 (down from 2.64); expansion could restore 4-5x FY26 EPS.

Key Risks

Risk Category Description Mitigation
Execution Delays in mining line commissioning; stringent SAIL terms (third-party inspection, 90-day defect liability, 2.5% qty tolerance with penalties). Trial runs ongoing; experienced in PSUs.
Cyclical/Market Steel/mining slowdown (Q2 revenue drop); dependency on few clients (SAIL ~3% but symbolic). Diversified belts; PVC protects pricing.
Financial Margin erosion from raw material inflation (rubber/fabric); high receivables (DSO ~200 days). Low debt (interest coverage 6.4x); 100% payment in 45 days post-acceptance.
Regulatory/Compliance GST/e-Way bills, inspections (RITES/SGS); non-compliance penalties. High compliance track record; detailed clauses.
Operational Inventory buildup (Rs. 402 Cr); forex/raw material volatility. Positive inventory reduction in H1; domestic focus.

Overall Outlook: Moderately positive. Headwinds from weak Q2 are transitional (expansion phase), offset by tailwinds like SAIL order and capex. Growth hinges on expansion fruition; target price upside if FY26 revenue rebounds 15-20%. Monitor Q3 results for execution progress. Low debt and cash flow provide downside protection. Recommendation: Accumulate on dips for 12-18 month horizon.
