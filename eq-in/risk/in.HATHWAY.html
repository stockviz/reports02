Hathway Cable and Datacom Limited (HATHWAY) Analysis

Overview: Hathway, a key player in cable TV distribution, broadband, and securities trading, reported steady revenue growth in H1 FY26 (ended Sep 30, 2025), driven by cable TV, but faced profitability pressure from high costs/depreciation. Consolidated revenue up 5% YoY to ₹1,067 Cr; net profit up 12% YoY to ₹49 Cr. Strong balance sheet with ₹2,141 Cr in current investments and positive operating cash flow (₹120 Cr). AGM resolutions passed unanimously.

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Revenue Momentum: Cable TV revenue +10% YoY (₹741 Cr half-year), benefiting from subscriber base stability and tariff hikes. Total revenue +5% YoY.
  • Cash-Rich Balance Sheet: ₹28 Cr cash + ₹2,141 Cr liquid investments; equity base ~₹4,433 Cr. Operating cash flow robust (+ve ₹120 Cr), funding capex/investments.
  • Diversified Profits: Securities trading contributed ₹32 Cr profit (half-year); unallocable income (₹49 Cr) supports bottom-line.
  • Shareholder Confidence: 100% promoter voting; near-unanimous public approval at AGM for directors, auditors.
  • Low Debt: Negligible borrowings (₹1 Cr); finance costs minimal (₹0.5 Cr).

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Profit Squeeze: Q2 net profit -39% YoY to ₹18 Cr due to higher pay channel costs (+15% YoY to ₹549 Cr) and depreciation (₹161 Cr). Cable TV segment loss widened to ₹24 Cr.
  • Segment Weakness: Broadband revenue flat/slight dip (₹294 Cr); marginal profits only after JV share.
  • Working Capital Strain: Trade receivables +73% to ₹439 Cr; payables +56% to ₹711 Cr, signaling delayed collections/payments.
  • High Fixed Costs: Depreciation/amortization at 15% of revenue; operational expenses rising.

Growth Prospects

  • Core Business Expansion: Cable TV (70% revenue) poised for digitization tailwinds; broadband (27%) growth via fiber rollout amid India’s high-speed internet push (potential ARPU upside).
  • Securities Arm: High-margin (near 100% in Q2); scalable with market volatility, contributing 3% revenue but 22% of segment profits.
  • Investment Income: Large FVTPL portfolio (gains ₹44 Cr in cash flow adj.); potential for higher yields in bull markets.
  • Outlook: FY26 revenue could hit ₹2,100-2,200 Cr (+5-8% YoY) if subscriber adds continue; EPS ~₹0.55-0.60, supported by cost controls.

Growth in cable/broadband viable long-term, but monitor DOT case (Q3 update key). Target P/E 15-18x FY26 EPS (~₹30-35/share fair value). Risks skewed regulatory; diversify exposure.
