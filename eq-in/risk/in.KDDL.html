KDDL Limited (NSE/BSE: KDDL) - Analysis Summary (Q2 & H1 FY26)

KDDL is a diversified precision manufacturing player focused on watch components (Taratec), precision engineering (Eigen), luxury packaging (Ornapac), and luxury watch retail/branding via subsidiaries (Ethos ~50% stake, Favre-Leuba). Standalone revenue grew 32.5% YoY to ₹242 Cr (H1 FY26), driven by watch components (₹115 Cr) and Precision Eng (₹90 Cr, +44% YoY). Consolidated revenue hit ₹1,008 Cr (+29% YoY), boosted by Ethos retail. EBITDA margins held steady at ~22% (standalone), but PAT margins dipped slightly due to new biz ramp-up costs. Management guides 20-25% long-term growth.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Global Luxury Watch Slowdown: Swiss exports to China (-16% Jan-Sep’25) and HK (-27%) amid weak consumer sentiment/macro issues; US tariffs (now 15% post-39%) impacted demand. Watch components steady on volumes but nascent India market (<2% global Swiss exports).
  • Margin Pressure: EBITDA margins contracted (standalone 22.5% vs. 24.7% YoY) from new divisions (bracelets/packaging front-loaded costs, product mix shift). Consolidated PAT margin at 6.2%.
  • Cyclical Normalization: Post-COVID luxury boom fading; every 10-12 years cycle hits industry.
  • Macro/External: Geopolitical tensions, inflation, currency volatility (CHF/INR exposure).

Tailwinds (Supportive Factors)

  • Domestic Strength: India Swiss watch imports +10% YoY (+35% vs. 2023); healthy demand via organized retail.
  • Diversification Delivering: Precision Eng +44% H1 (export-heavy, ~2/3 revenue); Bracelets >80% utilization (profitable, expanding); Packaging +70% YoY. Non-Swiss Europe/Asia exploration reduces concentration.
  • Subsidiary Momentum: Favre-Leuba exceeding sales/profit targets (global POS expansion); Ethos natural forex hedge; Mahen dividend (₹30/share) passed through (KDDL ₹15/share interim).
  • Operational Leverage: Capex ₹9 Cr H1 (electroplating by Q1FY27); process optimization yielding better realizations/premiumization.

Growth Prospects

  • Core Manufacturing (20-25% CAGR Target):
    • Watch Components: Premiumization, bracelets/packaging scale-up (₹80-100 Cr each in 3-5 yrs).
    • Precision Eng: ₹750-1,000 Cr in 7-10 yrs (aerospace/defense/auto/alternate energy ~40%; EV exposure; exports to US/Europe).
  • Brand/Retail: Favre-Leuba ramp to 8,000+ units (’26); upped targets amid strong response. Ethos rights issue (₹410 Cr) funds expansion.
  • Strategic: Non-Swiss diversification, in-house capabilities (plating/tooling), sustainability focus. FY26 capex ₹15-18 Cr H2.
  • Overall: Consolidated revenue trajectory supports 25-30%+ near-term; long-term vertical integration (components → retail → brands).

Key Risks

Risk Category Details Mitigation
Market/Concentration Heavy Swiss watch reliance (China/HK/US weakness); cyclical luxury demand. Diversification (bracelets/packaging/Eigen); India/non-Swiss focus.
Forex/External Export-heavy (~2/3 standalone); CHF/EUR volatility, tariffs (US normalization expected short-term). Hedging (de-risking policy); natural hedge via Ethos imports.
Subsidiary-Specific Estima AG (Swiss sub): Over-indebted (CHF 11.3 Mn subordinated; additional CHF 0.3 Mn incoming); PPE impairment risk (₹49 Cr). Favre-Leuba early-stage losses (budgeted). Parent support (KIHL loans); strong Favre-Leuba traction.
Execution/Competition New capex ramps (bracelets/electroplating); China/Thailand in components. Customer ties (Amphenol/TE); certifications (AS9100D/IATF).
Financial Debt ₹110 Cr cons. (manageable); unreviewed sub-results (~₹771 Cr assets). Strong cash flows (H1 op. cash ₹99 Cr cons.); dividends.

Overall Outlook: Resilient growth amid headwinds; diversification de-risks core watch biz. Precision Eng/Favre-Leuba key upside levers. Maintain Buy for 20-25% CAGR potential, but monitor Estima/tariffs. Target FY26E cons. rev. ₹2,100-2,200 Cr (+25-30% YoY).
