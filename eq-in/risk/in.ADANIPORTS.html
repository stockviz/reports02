ADANIPORTS (Adani Ports and Special Economic Zone Ltd) Analysis

Overview: APSEZ is India’s largest private port operator with ~28% market share, handling 633 MMTpa capacity (target: 1 BMTpa by 2030). Q2/H1 FY26 results show robust growth: H1 revenue ₹18,294 Cr (+30% YoY), PAT ₹6,431 Cr (+16% YoY), driven by ports (15,707 Cr segment revenue, +18% YoY). Cargo volumes strong at 325 MMT YTD Nov’25 (+11% YoY). Balance sheet solid (net debt/equity 0.76x, liquidity ₹10k+ Cr). Recent moves: ESG upgrade (MSCI ‘B’), TNFD adopter, subsidiary merger, Abbot Point acquisition (AUD 3.975 Bn).

Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts)
  • Volume & Revenue Momentum: Nov’25 cargo 41 MMT (+14% YoY, containers +20%, dry +10%). H1 EBITDA margins ~60%, ports segment dominant (88% revenue). Integrated model (ports + logistics + rail) drives synergies.
  • Financial Strength: Record H1 PAT; op. cash flow ₹9,503 Cr (+23% YoY). Strong ratios: DSCR 6x, interest coverage 6.7x, asset cover >100% for NCDs. Dividend payout sustained.
  • ESG & Sustainability Edge: MSCI upgrade (CCC → B); largest private mangrove restorer (4,200 Ha afforested). TNFD adopter from FY26 signals proactive nature-risk disclosure, aiding global/investor appeal.
  • Strategic Wins: Amalgamation with Adani Harbour Services (₹14k Cr net worth sub) for efficiency. Abbot Point buy (14 Cr shares issued); past divestments (e.g., Ennore stake) unlocked ₹600+ Cr gains.
  • Macro Support: India’s port throughput growth (~8-10% CAGR); APSEZ’s 13 ports/terminals, rail (470k TEUs YTD +13%), GPWIS position it for infra boom.
Headwinds (Challenges)
  • Logistics Softness: Nov’25 rail -5% YoY (51k TEUs), GPWIS -4%; though YTD +13%/+1%, indicates volume volatility amid competition/execution issues.
  • Cost Pressures: Finance costs up (H1 ₹2,181 Cr, +116% YoY incl. derivatives ₹560 Cr loss, forex swings). Employee/opex steady but high capex (₹6.5k Cr H1) strains FCF.
  • Past Controversies: Short-seller report (2023) & SEBI probes closed (no penalties); US DOJ/SEC indictment on ex-director (no company impact claimed). Lingers as sentiment risk.
  • Margin Dilution: Operating margins 60% (stable), but NPM dipped to 35% H1 (vs. 39% prior) due to forex/derivs.
Growth Prospects
  • High Visibility: Cargo target 1 BMTpa by 2030 (from 633 MMTpa); containers/dry bulk tailwinds from trade/export push. Logistics parks (12), warehouses (3.1 Mn sq ft), 25k+ trucks scale “shore-to-door”.
  • Expansion Pipeline: Abbot Point (Aus) acquisition; intl. ports (Haifa, Colombo, E. Africa). Capex focus: CWIP ₹10k Cr.
  • EBITDA/EBIT CAGR: 15-20% potential (ports 90%+ EBITDA); H1 ports PBT ₹8,095 Cr (+16% YoY).
  • Rating Upside: MSCI ‘B’, S&P CSA top 5%; TNFD enhances ESG premium for funding/cost of capital.
  • Shareholder Returns: EPS ₹29.74 H1 (annualized ~₹60); tender offers reduced high-coupon debt.

Risk Category Details Mitigants
Execution/Operational Cargo volatility (e.g., logistics dip); high capex delays (₹10k Cr CWIP). Diversified portfolio (13 ports); 28% mkt share; strong YoY growth.

Regulatory/Geopolitical Intl. ops (Aus/Israel/SL); past probes; NCLT approval for merger. SEBI/SC clearances; no material impact noted.
ESG/Reputation Nature/marine risks; director indictment overhang. TNFD/MSCI progress; mangrove conservation leader.
Macro/Competition Trade slowdown, fuel prices, rival ports (e.g., JSW/Guj SEZ). Integrated logistics moat; capacity ramp.

Valuation Context: Trades at ~18-20x FY26E EPS (peer avg 15-25x); growth justifies premium. Target upside 15-20% if volumes hit guidance.

Summary: Strong Bull Case – Tailwinds dominate (volumes, finances, ESG), supporting 15%+ CAGR to 2030. Headwinds manageable (logistics fixable); risks mostly external/historical. Buy/Hold for infra/growth investors; monitor logistics recovery & Abbot integration. FY26 guidance intact post-H1 beat.
