ADANIPORTS (Adani Ports and Special Economic Zone Ltd.) Analysis

Overview: Adani Ports (APSEZL) is India’s largest private port operator with ~28% market share, handling 633 MMTpa capacity (target: 1 Bn MMTpa by 2030). Q2/H1 FY26 results (ended Sep 30, 2025) show robust growth amid strong volumes and margins. Key filings include ops updates, financials, ESG progress, sustainability commitments, and corporate actions.

Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts)

  • Volume & Revenue Momentum: Oct’25 cargo at 40.2 MMT (+6% YoY; containers +24%), YTD 284.4 MMT (+10%). H1 revenue ₹18,294 Cr (vs. ₹14,023 Cr H1 FY25; +30% implied), EBITDA margins ~60%. Ports/SEZ segment dominates (86% revenue).
  • Financial Strength: H1 PBT ₹7,538 Cr (+13% YoY), PAT ₹6,431 Cr (+16%). Net debt/equity at 0.76x (improved from 0.73x FY25). DSCR 6.04x, interest coverage 6.66x. Asset cover >100% for NCDs.
  • ESG & Sustainability: MSCI ESG upgrade (CCC to B). TNFD Adopter (first Indian integrated transport utility; disclosures from FY26). Mangrove afforestation (4,200 Ha restored + 3,000 Ha conserved).
  • Corporate Actions: Amalgamation with 100% subsidiary AHSL approved (synergies, efficiency). Postal ballot passed for new Independent Director (99.34% approval).
  • Visibility: Investor/analyst meets (Adani Conference, Singapore Nov 24-25). Top 5% global sustainability ranking (S&P CSA 2025).

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Margin Pressures: Finance costs up (H1 ₹2,181 Cr excl. FX), forex/derivative volatility (net ₹560 Cr loss). Employee/opex steady but scaling.
  • Past Controversies: H1 FY25 exceptional items (₹197 Cr settlements). SSR (2023) resolved (SC/SEBI closure, no penalties). US DOJ/SEC case vs. executive (not company; no financial impact).
  • Capex Intensity: H1 capex ₹6,462 Cr (ports/logistics expansion). CWIP ₹10,104 Cr.
  • External: Logistics GPWIS volumes down 6% YoY (Oct’25); YTD flat.

Growth Prospects

  • Organic/Volumes: Container/logistics rail +15-21% YoY. Integrated “shore-to-door” model (15 ports, 127 vessels, 25K+ trucks, 3.1 Mn sq ft warehouses).
  • Inorganic: Abbot Point acquisition (AUD 3.975 Bn; share swap). Astro Group acquired (Oct’24). Divestments (e.g., Ennore stake) unlock value.
  • Capacity: 633 MMTpa → 1 Bn by 2030. Logistics parks (12), rail (418K TEUs YTD).
  • Sustainability Edge: TNFD/ESG positions for global/institutional capital. Nature-positive infra (mangroves, biodiversity).
  • Projections: Consensus implies 15-20% CAGR revenue/PAT to FY28 (ports India growth ~8-10%; APSEZL outpacing via share gains).
