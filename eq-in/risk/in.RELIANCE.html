Reliance Industries Limited (RELIANCE) Analysis: Q2 FY26 (Sep 30, 2025)

Reliance Industries Limited (RIL) reported robust Q2 FY26 results with consolidated gross revenue up 9.9% YoY to ₹283,548 Cr, EBITDA up 14.6% to ₹50,367 Cr (margin +80 bps at 17.8%), and PAT up 14.3% to ₹22,092 Cr. Growth was driven by Jio Platforms (+15% revenue), Retail (+18%), and O2C (+3.2% revenue, +21% EBITDA). Net debt/EBITDA remains healthy at 0.58x, supporting ₹40,010 Cr capex. Below is a structured summary of headwinds, tailwinds, growth prospects, and key risks based on the filings.

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Diversified Portfolio Resilience: All major segments grew EBITDA YoY – Jio (+18%), Retail (+16.5%), O2C (+21%), Oil & Gas stable. Digital Services (Jio) and Retail led revenue growth.
  • Jio Platforms Leadership: 506 Mn subscribers (+5.8% YoY), 234 Mn 5G users (50% of wireless traffic), ARPU ₹211 (+8.4%). Fixed broadband at 23 Mn homes (+~3 Mn QoQ). EBITDA margin +140 bps to 51.6%.
  • Retail Momentum: Gross revenue ₹90,018 Cr (+18% YoY), 19,821 stores (+412 QoQ), 369 Mn registered customers (+13%). Quick commerce orders +200% YoY; Grocery/Fashion +23%/22%.
  • O2C Recovery: Fuel cracks up 22-37% (Gasoil +37%), Jio-bp volumes +34% (HSD), network at 2,057 outlets. Ethane cracking economics favorable.
  • Strong Balance Sheet: Cash ₹111 Bn, net debt low (0.58x EBITDA), supports capex/growth.
  • Media (JioStar) Turnaround: Record EBITDA ₹1,738 Cr (28.1% margin); 400 Mn MAUs; sports/digital viewership highs (e.g., India-England Tests: 170 Mn viewers).

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • O2C Volatility: Polyester chain margins weak (-9% deltas due to global overcapacity/China supply); global cracker rates down to 79.5%. EBITDA margin dipped QoQ due to higher OSPs/crude.
  • Oil & Gas Decline: KGD6 production -8.4% YoY (natural decline); EBITDA -5.4% due to maintenance/higher opex. CBM/KGD6 gas realizations mixed.
  • Rising Costs: Finance costs +13.5% (5G spectrum amortization); depreciation +11.9%.
  • Media Ad Pressure: TV entertainment ads challenged by FMCG cuts (offset by digital/sports growth).
  • Regulatory Penalty: ₹56.44 Cr GST penalty (input tax credit dispute; appeal planned, no ops impact).
  • Macro/Seasonal: Monsoon impacted PVC/PET demand; Q1 FY26 EBITDA inflated by ₹8,924 Cr one-off investment gain.

Overall Outlook: Bullish. RIL’s diversified model (Digital/Retail driving 40%+ growth) offsets O2C cyclicality. New Energy/AI position it for India’s $5 Tn economy.
