Analysis of GE Power India Limited (GVPIL): Headwinds, Tailwinds, Growth Prospects, and Key Risks

GE Power India Limited (GVPIL), a power equipment and services player focused on thermal power (boilers, spares, upgrades), reported solid Q2 FY26 results (ended Sep 2025) with revenue up 29% YoY to INR 281 Cr from continuing operations, driven by core services. PAT from continuing ops was INR 30 Cr (standalone), with EBITDA margins improving to ~18%. However, order backlog fell 14% QoQ to INR 1,825 Cr due to FGD contract terminations. The company is pivoting to high-margin services post-sale/demerger of non-core units (Hydro, Gas, Durgapur). Below is a structured summary based on the earnings transcript, financials, and disclosures.

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Core Services Momentum: 45% QoQ growth in core orders (INR 162 Cr in Q2 vs INR 112 Cr prior), up 46% H1 YoY to INR 343 Cr. Focus on spares, repairs, and overhauls for ~70 GW non-GEPIL thermal base; 55% of Q2 revenue from this high-margin (better gross margins), short-cycle (LTP ~12 months) segment.
  • Cash Unlocks from Settlements: BHEL settlement (INR 340 Cr phased receipt by Mar 2026; INR 50 Cr already received); JPVL amicable closure (post INR 775 Cr FGD termination, expect ~INR 25 Cr settlement post-shipments). Fire insurance claims fully settled (Solapur/NTPC Sipat: INR 66 Cr final).
  • Improved Financials: Revenue stability (H1: INR 567 Cr, +22% YoY); PBT up 4x YoY in Q2; net worth up to INR 298 Cr. Disciplined cost management post-demerger.
  • Strategic Portfolio Focus: Demerger of underutilized Durgapur (to JSW Energy; 5-year service access retained); sales of Hydro/Gas completed, unlocking value and sharpening services focus.
  • Export Traction: Penetration in 7 countries (Saudi, Turkey, etc.), intervening 8 GW base; INR 4 Cr boiler spares orders in H1; targeting deeper penetration without new segments.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • FGD Business Setback: Termination of 2 contracts (Jaypee Bina/Nigrie: INR 775 Cr); policy limits FGD to 30 GW (70 GW Category C plants exempted), capping tenders. Q2 charge of INR 27 Cr; backlog down 31% YoY.
  • Order Intake Volatility: Q2 intake down 46% YoY to INR 240 Cr (no repeat of INR 243 Cr Wanakbori turbine upgrade); overall H1 core+upgrades up 23% but base low.
  • High Receivables Burden: INR 10,838 Cr trade receivables (~57% of assets); INR 1,000 Cr gross (~35% BHEL). Aging risks despite ECL provisions; negative operating cash flow (H1: -INR 331 Cr).
  • Discontinued Ops Drag: Losses from Durgapur (H1: INR 252 Cr pre-exceptional); underutilization (2.5 lakh hrs capacity at 2/3rd load).
  • Macro Moderation: Power demand growth slowed to 3.2% YoY (Sep); coal stabilization at 2.5% growth; abundant rains curbed peaks.

Growth Prospects

  • Services Scalability: Core services as primary driver (double-digit YoY growth targeted); expand to non-GE thermal base via competencies/products. Upgrades (e.g., INR 47 Cr NOx from Adani Mahan) and exports (13 countries targeted) add levers. Post-demerger access to Sanand/Durgapur facilities ensures manufacturing flex.
  • Backlog Execution: INR 1,825 Cr provides visibility; 18 active FGD sites with 50+ milestones for retention releases.
  • Market Tailwinds: India power demand ~4% FY26 (IEA); industrial rebound, electrification sustain thermal services (~40 MT coal demand). Potential FGD tenders in select 30 GW.
  • EBITDA Path: Q2 margins at 17.7% (vs 15.2% YoY); normalized ops profitable (ex-settlements: ~INR 4 Cr Q2 charge netted). H1 continuing PAT INR 901 Cr signals turnaround.
  • Shareholder Value: Demerger (10 JSW shares per 139 GEPIL); focus on cash-accretive orders.

Key Risks

  • Collection/Receivables (High): INR 1,000 Cr exposure; BHEL/JPVL tied to fulfillments (tech support, shipments). ECL policy mitigates but delays could strain liquidity (cash down to INR 1,083 Cr).
  • FGD Execution/Policy (Medium-High): 18 sites at risk; revised MoEF notification caps market; terminations signal customer pushback.
  • Cash Flow (Medium): H1 net cash outflow INR 3,300 Cr (loans to group: INR 3,000 Cr); opex negative despite profits.
  • Transition Risks (Medium): Post-demerger reliance on outsourcing (JSW/Sanand); services manpower scalability amid renewables shift (thermal ~50% mix).
  • Macro/Competition (Low-Medium): Slower power growth; rivals in services/exports. No tax/refund volatility (zero current tax).
  • Regulatory: Demerger NCLT approval pending; ECL reversals not guided.

Overall Summary: GVPIL’s pivot to core services is yielding results (profitable H1 continuing ops, margin expansion), supported by settlements and backlog. Tailwinds from services/export growth outweigh FGD headwinds, positioning for sustainable profitability (9.2 EPS H1). However, receivables and cash flow remain Achilles’ heels—monitor Q3 collections/BHEL inflows. Valuation upside if services scale to 20-25% revenue CAGR; target normalized PAT ~INR 50-70 Cr/quarter. Risk-reward favors longs with 6-12 month horizon. (Analysis based solely on provided docs; FY26E revenue ~INR 1,300-1,500 Cr implied).
