Analysis of Bharat Forge Limited (BHARATFORG) - Q2/H1 FY26

Bharat Forge (BFL) reported Q2 FY26 results amid challenging global demand, particularly in North America (NA), but demonstrated resilience through strong domestic execution, defence growth, and strategic restructuring. Standalone revenue declined 7.5% QoQ to ₹19,469 Mn (EBITDA margin stable at 28%), while consolidated revenue grew 3.1% QoQ to ₹40,319 Mn (EBITDA ₹7,154 Mn, margin 17.7%). Balance sheet remains robust (net debt/EBITDA 1.4x consolidated, ROCE 15.5%). Board approved ₹20 Bn debt raise for growth and designated a new SMP.

Headwinds (Challenges Pressuring Performance)

  • NA Commercial Vehicle (CV) Weakness: Sharp 48% QoQ/63% YoY drop in CV exports due to truck production decline and inventory destocking. NA contributed ~16% revenue drop QoQ; expected to persist in H2 FY26 amid policy uncertainty (e.g., tariffs).
  • Overseas Operations Drag: Europe EBITDA margin low at 3.6% (review of steel footprint ongoing); US at 4.2%. Consolidated overseas PBT negative (₹-819 Mn in Q2).
  • Commodity/External Pressures: Weak crude prices hit Oil & Gas; forex losses (₹96 Mn H1 standalone); GST anticipation reduced domestic CV volumes.
  • E-Mobility Struggles: Ongoing impairments in Kalyani Powertrain Ltd. (KPTL) (₹493 Mn standalone); negative EBITDA.
  • Working Capital Strain: Inventory up ₹3.5 Bn consolidated H1; capex ₹5.7 Bn.

Tailwinds (Positive Supports)

  • Stable Margins & Cost Control: Standalone EBITDA margin resilient at 28% despite volume drop; Indian ops EBITDA ₹6,757 Mn (24.6% margin).
  • Robust Balance Sheet: Net cash ₹2,309 Cr consolidated; low net D/E 0.42x; QIP proceeds fully utilized for acquisitions.
  • Defence Momentum: H1 orders ₹559 Cr; orderbook ₹9,467 Cr. New ₹2,500 Mn MoD contract for underwater systems (delivery by Nov 2026).
  • Domestic Strength: India revenue ₹27,459 Mn (up QoQ via Defence ramp-up, Passenger Vehicle gains); Industrial steady (Construction, Aerospace).
  • Restructuring Wins: Defence assets transferred to KSSL (₹414 Mn gain standalone); K-Drive Mobility acquisition adds CV scale.

Growth Prospects

  • Defence as Key Driver: Orderbook positions for multi-year revenue; focus on unmanned/marine systems, naval guns. KSSL ramp-up expected to offset NA weakness.
  • India-Centric Expansion: Emphasis on Defence/Aerospace/Castings/Aggregates; BFISL EBITDA 13.7%. New orders H1 ₹1,582 Cr.
  • Capex & Debt Raise: ₹20 Bn approval for term loans/NCDs; H1 capex signals capacity addition. Acquisition synergies (e.g., K-Drive goodwill ₹1,124 Mn).
  • Diversification: Industrial (non-CV) resilient; exports to Europe/Asia-Pacific stable. Medium-term Passenger Car growth.
  • Outlook: Management expects offsets via India/Defence/non-US exports; ROCE target sustained at ~15%.

Key Risks

Risk Category Details Potential Impact
Market/Geopolitical Prolonged NA weakness/policy risks (tariffs); forex volatility. Revenue 20-25% export exposure hit; forex losses recurring.
Operational/Legal Customs litigation (₹1.53 Cr IGST + ₹1 Cr fine; appeal planned). VRS extension; insolvency in JV (BF-NTPC). Immaterial financials but reputation/execution delays.
Subsidiary/Impairment E-mobility weakness (KPTL); unreviewed subs/associates (29% assets). Further provisions; consolidated drag.
Liquidity/Capex High WC loans (₹46 Bn); debt raise dependency. Rising rates strain D/E (0.66x); execution delays.
Cyclical Auto/Industrial slowdown; crude volatility. Margin compression if unhedged.

Mitigants: De-risking via domestic/Defence shift; strong cash flows (₹10 Bn op. cash H1 standalone).

Summary

BFL faces near-term headwinds from NA CV destocking and overseas weakness (revenue -11.6% YoY standalone H1), but tailwinds in Defence (₹9.5 Bn book, ₹2.5 Bn new win) and India ops provide buffers, sustaining margins/ROCE. Growth prospects are strong in Defence/India manufacturing (H1 orders signal ramp-up), backed by ₹20 Bn debt and acquisitions. Key risks center on exports/NA policy and subsidiary impairments, but low leverage and cash buffers mitigate. Overall Outlook: Cautious positive; Defence pivot positions for FY27+ recovery. Target upside if NA stabilizes; monitor Q3 exports. (Valuation context: Trade at ~20x FY26E EPS post-results dip.)
