Analysis of CreditAccess Grameen Limited (CREDITACC) - Q2 & H1 FY26 Performance

CreditAccess Grameen Limited (CAGL), India’s largest NBFC-MFI by AUM (INR 25,904 Cr as of Sep-25), demonstrated resilient business momentum amid industry headwinds, with YoY disbursement growth of 33% (Q2) and 27% (H1), driven by rural micro-lending to women via JLG model. Asset quality showed gradual stabilization (PAR 0+ down to 4.7% QoQ), but elevated credit costs from accelerated write-offs (INR 683 Cr in Q2) pressured profitability (Q2 PAT: INR 126 Cr, RoA 1.8%). Strong balance sheet (CRAR 26.1%, liquidity 7.9% of assets) and deleveraging trends support recovery. Stock trades at ~1.5x FY26 BVPS; outlook hinges on H2 rural demand revival.

Headwinds (Challenges Impacting Performance)

  • Asset Quality Stress: PAR 0+ at 4.7% (ex-Karnataka: 3.8%), with Karnataka at 6.8% due to legacy issues, floods/rains, and over-leveraging. Sequential credit cost reduction (INR 526 Cr) but still elevated YoY; ECL coverage at 4.06% (156 bps over PAR90+).
  • Portfolio Contraction: GLP down 0.6% QoQ (ex-writeoffs: +2%), borrowers -10% YoY to 44.4L due to conservative write-offs (H1: INR 1,375 Cr).
  • Seasonal/External Factors: Q2 seasonally weak; heavy rains/floods capped PAR15+ accretion improvement. Employee attrition at 28.9% annualized.
  • Profitability Pressure: PAT down 32% YoY (Q2), RoE 7.1%; high opex/GLP at 5.2% from expansion (150 new branches H1).

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Business Momentum: Disbursements robust (33% YoY Q2), 2.2L new borrowers (39% NTC), unique borrowers at 41% GLP (up from 36% QoQ), >3 lender overlap down to 6.9%.
  • Asset Quality Stabilization: PAR15+ accretion range-bound but reversing (All-States: 0.47% Sep-25); state-wise trends improving (e.g., Karnataka 5.1% PAR90+). Collections at 94.9% (incl. arrears).
  • Financial Resilience: NIM steady at 13.3%, PPOP +3.4% YoY (INR 695 Cr); diversified liabilities (56.7% banks, 23.7% foreign), low CoB 9.6%, undrawn sanctions INR 3,455 Cr + pipeline INR 6,260 Cr.
  • Operational Scale: Branches +9% YoY (2,209), employees +11% (21,701); Retail Finance share up to 11% with better quality.

Growth Prospects

  • H2 FY26 Upside: Healthy monsoons, rural recovery to boost demand; stabilizing PAR to lower credit costs, enabling 20-25% AUM CAGR (management-implied via branch adds, NTC focus).
  • Strategic Expansion: Contiguous district model (447 districts, 16 states/1 UT), product diversification (Retail Finance >INR 2,500 Cr), tech enhancements (CBS, RPA, fintech ties).
  • Long-Term: Rural underpenetration, 87% retention, low district concentration (<3% GLP/district); promoter commitment (66% stake), strong IR (AA-/Stable ratings).
  • Projections: Consensus eyes FY26 AUM ~INR 32,000-34,000 Cr, RoA 2.5-3%; H2 profitability rebound to 10-12% RoE.

Key Risks

Risk Category Description Mitigation
Credit/Asset Quality High PAR in Karnataka (30% GLP), over-leveraging relapse, external shocks (floods). Deleveraging (unique borrowers 41%), ECL buffer (268 bps over IRAC), conservative provisioning/write-offs.
Liquidity/Funding Rising rates, refinancing term loans (D/E 2.8x). Strong liquidity (INR 2,176 Cr), sanctions pipeline, 74% long-term funding.
Operational Attrition (29%), execution in expansion. Rural hiring (90% local), rotation policies, incentives delinked from collections.
Regulatory RBI MFI norms (FOIR 50%, household income >INR 3L shifts to Retail). Compliant (e.g., underwriting tightened), diversified products.
Macro Rural slowdown, competition. Rural focus, NTC emphasis, low geographic concentration.

Overall Outlook: Positive; tailwinds from stabilization outweigh headwinds. H2 rural rebound key for re-rating. Target: Accumulate for 20-25% upside (FY26E P/B 1.8-2.0x). Monitor Karnataka PAR monthly.
