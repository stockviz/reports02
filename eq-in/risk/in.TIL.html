TIL Limited (NSE: TIL, BSE: 505196) Analysis

Company Overview: TIL Limited is a Kolkata-based manufacturer of material handling and infrastructure equipment (e.g., mobile cranes, port equipment, road construction gear). It operates in a single reportable segment focused on India, with global alliances (Manitowoc, Hyster-Yale). FY26 H1 results (ended Sep 30, 2025) show revenue growth amid losses, supported by a ₹200+ Cr order book. Recent corporate actions include warrant allotment (₹15 Cr raised), new director appointment, and positive AGM outcomes.

Headwinds (Challenges Pressuring Performance)

  • Persistent Losses & High Costs: Q2FY26 standalone net loss of ₹7.72 Cr (vs. ₹2.10 Cr YoY); H1 loss ₹13.94 Cr. Elevated finance costs (₹10.70 Cr in Q2, up significantly QoQ) due to high debt (total borrowings ~₹325 Cr). EBITDA margins contracted YoY (4.0% in Q2 vs. 10.1% prior).
  • Negative Cash Flows: Operating cash flow negative at ₹(36.78 Cr) in H1FY26; reliant on financing inflows (e.g., short-term borrowings up ₹32.81 Cr).
  • Liquidity & Debt Burden: Current liabilities exceed current assets; inventories up 23% YoY to ₹155.95 Cr, trade receivables steady at ~₹130 Cr. Balance sheet shows total equity ~₹64 Cr vs. liabilities ~₹482 Cr.
  • External Pressures: Forex losses from Rupee depreciation; SEBI penalty (₹100 lakhs, 50% deposited, appeal stayed but unresolved); minor subsidiary losses.
  • Macro Headwinds: Temporary infrastructure sector slowdown from global tariffs/uncertainties.

Tailwinds (Supportive Factors)

  • Revenue Momentum: Q2 standalone revenue +12% YoY (+15% QoQ) to ₹78.27 Cr; H1 +2% YoY. Total income ₹81.45 Cr, aided by “liabilities no longer required written back” (₹17 Cr in Q2).
  • Operational Improvements: EBITDA +211% QoQ to ₹3.27 Cr; order book >₹200 Cr signals execution visibility.
  • Capital Infusion: Allotted 37.5 lakh warrants (₹160 each, 25% paid = ₹15 Cr); potential ₹45 Cr more on conversion. Equity capital up to ₹68.80 Cr.
  • Governance & Approvals: All AGM resolutions passed overwhelmingly (99.99%+ favor); new Non-Exec Director (Mr. Anurag Srivastava, IAS background) appointed.
  • Related Party Support: Transactions worth ₹100.9 Cr (e.g., loans/purchases with associates like Gainwell/Indocrest at 0% interest), providing operational liquidity.

Growth Prospects

  • Order Book & Execution: ₹200+ Cr backlog positions for H2FY26 ramp-up; infrastructure capex tailwinds (ports, highways, defence) expected to drive volumes.
  • Product Innovation: Launching 3 safety-focused products at Excon 2025 to enter new categories; investments in indigenisation, R&D, and aftermarket to boost margins/recurring revenue.
  • Sector Outlook: India’s infra push (govt/private projects) favors TIL’s portfolio; single-segment focus enables scale. H1 forex hit temporary; H2 leverage from volumes/cost efficiencies projected.
  • Strategic Positioning: Pan-India presence, ISO-certified plants, 80+ years legacy; CSR/infra self-reliance alignment. Potential EPS improvement post-warrant conversion (anti-dilutive currently).
  • Outlook: Management targets “significantly improved” H2FY26 performance; revenue/EBITDA growth from order conversion/new products.

Key Risks

Overall Summary: TIL shows early recovery signals (revenue/order book growth) amid infra tailwinds, but headwinds from losses/debt dominate short-term. Growth hinges on H2 execution and new launches; risks center on liquidity/debt sustainability. Positive for long-term infra play, but high-risk turnaround—watch debt reduction and EBITDA trajectory. FY26 consensus potential: Revenue up ~20-30% if orders convert, but profitability lagged. Investors: Accumulate on dips for 12-18 month horizon if debt stabilizes.
