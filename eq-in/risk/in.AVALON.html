Avalon Technologies Limited (AVALON) Analysis

Overview: Avalon is a leading Indian EMS (Electronics Manufacturing Services) provider focused on high-value box-build solutions for sectors like clean energy, transportation, industrials, medical, aerospace, and semiconductors. It operates a global model with facilities in India (81% of Q2 revenue) and the US. FY26 H1 results (ended Sep 30, 2025) show robust top-line growth (Consolidated Revenue +48.7% YoY to ₹706 Cr), but margin pressures and working capital strain are evident. Q2 highlights: Revenue +39% YoY, PAT +43% YoY. Order book +25% YoY signals momentum.

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

Factor Details Impact
Revenue Momentum Consolidated Q2 revenue ₹383 Cr (+39% YoY, +18% QoQ); H1 ₹706 Cr (+49% YoY). India +31% YoY, US +44% YoY. Strong demand in EMS; global diversification.
Order Book & Wins +25% YoY; new supplier award from railway OEM; AS9100D aerospace certification; semi equipment progress with global major. Pipeline visibility; entry into high-margin sectors (semi, aero, rail).
Margin Resilience Q2 EBITDA 10.1% (India ops: 14.1%); PAT 6.4%. Standalone PBT +187% YoY H1. Operational leverage; cost optimization.
Strategic Positioning Global model (India scale + US proximity); one reportable EMS segment. Investor meet (Nov 26, 2025) signals engagement. Benefiting from India PLI schemes, China+1 shifts.

Headwinds (Challenges)

Factor Details Impact
Margin Compression EBITDA margin Q2 10.1% (-86 bps YoY); employee costs +50% YoY (₹699 Cr Q2 cons.); raw mat +61% YoY. Cost inflation (labor, materials); scale not fully offsetting.
Working Capital Drag Inventories +30% QoQ/H1 (Cons: ₹4,374 Cr vs ₹3,379 Cr FY25); Trade receivables +7% (₹3,369 Cr). Ops cash flow negative H1 cons. (-₹102 Cr vs +₹187 Cr YoY). Supply chain stocking for growth; ties up cash (capex +ve investing cash).
Leverage & Costs Borrowings stable but finance costs +5% YoY; lease liabilities up. Higher interest/depreciation amid expansion.
Governance Signal AGM (Sep 26): Director reappointment passed but 27% dissent (86% from institutions). Potential institutional concerns on board composition.

Growth Prospects

  • High: EMS market tailwinds (India EMS ~$20-25B by FY28, 20-25% CAGR). Avalon targeting complex, high-value (box-build, PCBA, cables).
  • Catalysts: Semi/rail/aero ramps; order book conversion; US ops scaling. H1 revenue mix supports 20-30% FY26 growth if margins stabilize at 10%.
  • Capex/Expansion: CWIP up (Standalone ₹153 Cr, Cons ₹160 Cr); PPE investments signal capacity addition.
  • Projections: Consensus-like view: Revenue FY26E ₹1,400-1,500 Cr (+25-30% YoY); EBITDA 10-12%; ROE ~15-20% on equity base ₹6,500 Cr.

Overall Summary: Bullish with Caution. Tailwinds from EMS boom and execution (revenue/order growth) outweigh headwinds, positioning Avalon for 25%+ FY26 growth. Key monitor: Working capital normalization (target <90-day cycle) and EBITDA >10%. Risks manageable but liquidity key. Valuation attractive if growth sustains (P/E ~30-40x FY26E). Positive AGM outcomes; upcoming investor meet for updates. Recommendation: Accumulate on dips.
