SOBHA Limited (Q2 FY26 Analysis)

Overview: Sobha Limited, a backward-integrated real estate developer focused on luxury residential, reported strong H1 FY26 operational performance with record collections (₹3,824 Cr, +31% YoY) and sales (₹3,981 Cr, +30% YoY). Q2 highlighted sustained demand (Bangalore 48%, NCR 38% of H1 sales), healthy cash flows (net operational ₹909 Cr, +79% YoY), and net cash position (₹751 Cr). However, reported margins remain subdued (H1 EBITDA 9.7%, PAT 3.6%) due to legacy provisions. Pipeline remains robust (forthcoming 16.69 Mn sft; developable land bank 403 acres), with FY26 sales guidance at ~₹8,500 Cr (potential upside).

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Robust Demand & Sales Momentum: H1 sales at 2.84 Mn sft (avg. realization ₹14,028/sft), driven by sustenance sales (e.g., Townpark 70% of Q2 Bangalore sales). Luxury segment resilient amid macro tailwinds (low inflation, rate cuts, S&P upgrade).
  • Record Liquidity: Q2 collections crossed ₹2,000 Cr milestone (+49% YoY); net cash flow H1 ₹120 Cr. Debt reduced (avg. cost 8.25%, down 60 bps QoQ); future inflows visibility ₹22,867 Cr (ongoing) + ₹7,100 Cr (forthcoming).
  • Backward Integration: In-house design/manufacturing aids cost control, timely delivery (H1 completions 2.25 Mn sft, FY target 5.5 Mn sft), and quality differentiation.
  • Strong Pipeline: 15.96 Mn sft residential + 0.74 Mn sft commercial forthcoming (next 4-6 quarters); 24 Mn sft subsequent. Existing inventory ₹13,000 Cr potential.
  • Geographic Diversification: Bangalore/NCR dominant; entry into Mumbai, steady Kerala NRI demand.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Subdued Margins: H1 EBITDA 9.7% (vs. project-level 30-35%); impacted by ₹27 Cr BBMP ground rent provision, legacy JDP inflation (2021-24), supply chain delays. Recognition from low-margin older sales.
  • Launch Delays: H1 launches slowed (external/internal issues); Magnus delayed to Oct’25 due to BBMP restructuring. Competitive BD raises land costs.
  • Market Dynamics: Gurgaon velocity slower (organizational tweaks underway); shift to mid-ticket (₹2-3 Cr) from super-luxury amid affordability pressures.
  • Execution Scale-Up: Balancing growth in competitive/price-sensitive markets while maintaining quality/timelines.

Growth Prospects

  • Sales Acceleration: H2 launches 8-9 Mn sft (7-8 projects: Magnus, NCR 3.5 Mn sft, Mumbai Phase 1). Potential to exceed ₹8,500 Cr FY26 if momentum sustains (vs. FY25 ₹6,276 Cr).
  • Margin Expansion: Completions ramp-up to unlock 33-34% EBITDA from ₹18,000 Cr unrecognized revenue (Sobha share). FY27+ higher from own projects (e.g., Neopolis).
  • Cash Flow Leverage: ₹9,800 Cr marginal CF from ongoing over 4-5 yrs; capex/land spend supported by inflows. Steady contractual/manufacturing (H1 revenue ₹371 Cr).
  • Strategic Expansion: Mumbai/GIFT City entry; North Bangalore (Hoskote Phase 1 >5 Mn sft by Q1 FY27); 40 Mn sft visibility from land bank.
  • Long-Term: 148 Mn sft track record; 7 Mn sft annual delivery capacity scaling.

Key Risks

Regulatory/Legal ED provisional attachment (₹2,016 Cr land, under appeal); IT search demands (₹672 Cr, appeals filed); fire NOC litigation (stay granted); project approvals (e.g., BBMP). Independent legal opinions; no material provisions needed per mgmt.
Execution/Operational Delays in launches/completions; cost overruns from inflation/supply chains. Backward integration; focus on timely delivery (5.5 Mn sft FY26 target).
Market/Competition Demand slowdown (volumes flat ~5-6 Mn sft); rising land costs; price sensitivity in mid-luxury. Premiumization + scale projects; patient BD in demand-supply gaps.
Financial Margin pressure from legacy; capex intensity (H1 land ₹632 Cr). Strong cash (₹751 Cr net); low debt cost (8.25%).
Macro Interest rates, geopolitical/trade issues; festive season demand. Diversified markets; NRI steady in Kerala.

Overall Summary: Bullish Outlook with Execution Focus. Tailwinds from liquidity, pipeline, and demand outweigh headwinds (margins, delays). Growth hinges on H2 launches (upside to ₹10,000 Cr FY26 possible per analysts). Risks are legal/regulatory heavy but mgmt.-mitigated (no provisions). Target higher-margin deliveries FY27+ for re-rating. Stock poised for 20-30% sales CAGR if macros hold. (Ratings: Tailwinds Strong; Growth High; Risks Moderate).
