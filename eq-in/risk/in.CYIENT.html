Cyient Limited (NSE: CYIENT) - Q2 FY26 Analysis

Overview: Cyient reported steady Q2 FY26 results amid macro headwinds, with DET (core engineering services, excluding Semicon) revenue flat YoY at $164.4M (+0.5% QoQ CC) and EBIT margins at 12.2% (+16 bps QoQ). Group revenue was $203.5M (-3.7% YoY INR). Strong cash generation (DET FCF-to-PAT: 114%) supported highest-ever interim dividend (Rs.16/share). New CEO Sukamal Banerjee highlighted transformation pillars (market impact, tech adoption, org effectiveness), targeting 15% EBIT by Q4 FY27. H2 FY26 expected stronger than H1. Semicon rebounded +12% QoQ but remains loss-making due to investments.

Headwinds (Challenges Pressuring Performance)

  • Macro/Geopolitical Pressures: Decision-making freezes in Q1 (e.g., US elections, tariffs/trade tensions) led to Semicon’s Q1 -35% QoQ drop; ongoing uncertainty in Europe (holidays) and aerospace (MRO-heavy, no new OEM designs).
  • Segment-Specific Weakness: DET Strategic Units -7.2% QoQ (ramp-down of large program, holidays); flat YoY DET revenue.
  • Cost Pressures: Wage hikes (2nd tranche), restructuring costs (-200 bps EBIT hit, employee severance).
  • Semicon Losses: Negative EBIT (-$2.7M) from revenue absorption, sales/R&D investments; Q1 strategic exits hurt growth.
  • PAT Volatility: -16% QoQ/YOY due to lower FX unrealized gains (vs. prior quarters).

Tailwinds (Positive Momentum)

  • Operational Recovery: DET +0.5% QoQ CC (Transportation +3.9%, Networks +3.6%); key accounts +3% QoQ/+11% YoY; 7 new logos.
  • Margin Discipline: +16 bps QoQ despite headwinds via cost optimization; gross margins resilient.
  • Cash & Dividend Strength: DET net cash ~Rs.14K Cr; FCF 117% of PAT (group); board confidence via record dividend.
  • Leadership & Initiatives: New CEO’s focus yielding results—pipeline +10% QoQ, new business 27% of intake (+6 pts QoQ), tech pipeline doubled; early wins in AI/digital (e.g., healthcare AI CoE, manufacturing digital twin).
  • DLM Momentum: +130% YoY H1 order intake; double-digit margins; new multi-year deals (eVTOL flight controls, EV charging).
  • Semicon Rebound: +12% QoQ revenue; order intake back to prior-year levels; ASIC 66% mix; partnerships (Global Foundries, Anora); top-3 govt bidder.
