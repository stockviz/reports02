Summary Analysis for Amanta Healthcare Limited (AMANTA)

Amanta Healthcare Limited, a pharma manufacturing and contract manufacturing company (single reportable segment), reported strong H1 FY26 (ended Sep 30, 2025) results post its successful IPO (listed Sep 9, 2025, raising ₹12,600 lakhs fresh capital). Revenue grew to ~₹13,419 lakhs (Q2: ₹7,089 lakhs), with PBT of ₹720 lakhs and robust operating cash flow of ₹2,765 lakhs. Balance sheet strengthened (total assets up 28% to ₹48,832 lakhs), driven by cash inflows, though capex is ramping up. Below is an analysis of headwinds, tailwinds, growth prospects, and key risks based on the provided filings.

Tailwinds (Positive Factors)

  • IPO Capital Infusion: Fresh ₹12,600 lakhs raised (utilized ₹3,030 lakhs so far), boosting equity to ₹21,068 lakhs (up >2x YoY) and cash to ₹9,196 lakhs. Reduced reliance on debt (total borrowings down to ₹17,750 lakhs from ₹19,500 lakhs).
  • Revenue Momentum: Q2 revenue up 6% QoQ and 19% YoY (to ₹7,089 lakhs), supported by operations in pharma products.
  • Operational Efficiency: Strong cash from operations (₹2,765 lakhs, stable YoY), healthy EBITDA margins implied by PBT growth, and unmodified limited review report from Price Waterhouse.
  • Debt Optimization: Early redemption of NCDs (historical finance costs included derecognition charges); ongoing repayments lowering leverage.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • High Expenses: Finance costs remain elevated (H1: ₹1,144 lakhs), with legacy debt (₹12,312 lakhs non-current). Exceptional IPO listing costs (₹263 lakhs) dented Q2 PBT.
  • Working Capital Strain: Inventories up 14% to ₹8,297 lakhs; trade receivables at ₹4,718 lakhs (provision for doubtful debts: ₹145 lakhs). Trade payables rose but indicate supplier dependency.
  • Capex Drag: Investing outflow of ₹1,661 lakhs (PPE/CWIP up), with only partial IPO utilization so far.
  • Profit Volatility: Q2 PAT ₹121 lakhs (EPS ₹0.35); impacted by exceptional items and forex gains/losses.

Growth Prospects

  • Capacity Expansion: ₹10,013 lakhs IPO allocation for new sterile manufacturing lines (SteriPort: ₹7,000 lakhs; SVP: ₹3,013 lakhs) at Hariyala, Khoda, Gujarat—poised to boost sterile injectables/contract mfg capacity amid India pharma export growth.
  • Unutilized Funds: ₹9,570 lakhs available for capex/general purposes, supporting scaling in high-margin segments.
  • Sector Tailwinds: Single-segment focus on pharma mfg/contract services aligns with rising domestic/export demand (e.g., USFDA-compliant facilities implied by certifications).
  • Financial Flexibility: Post-IPO balance sheet (equity >40% of assets) enables further investments; EPS potential from revenue scaling.

Key Risks

  • Execution/Regulatory Risk: Delays in capex (e.g., civil works, machinery) or regulatory approvals (e.g., for new lines) could idle ₹9,570 lakhs unutilized funds. Pharma sector prone to USFDA inspections/compliance issues.
  • Debt Servicing: ₹17,750 lakhs borrowings (high interest: H1 ₹1,144 lakhs) vulnerable to rate hikes; lease liabilities add pressure.
  • Liquidity/Working Capital: Heavy reliance on operating cash (despite strength); rising inventories/receivables could strain if sales slow.
  • Market/Concentration Risk: Single segment exposes to pharma cyclicality, competition, forex volatility (noted in cash flows), and raw material inflation.
  • Post-IPO Adjustments: High share issue expenses (₹2,265 lakhs) and promoter dilution; stock volatility as new listing (scrip: 544502).

Overall Outlook: Strong tailwinds from IPO-funded expansion position Amanta for multi-year growth in sterile pharma, but execution of capex and debt reduction are critical. Monitor Q3 results for capex progress and margins. Positive setup for investors focused on mid-cap pharma growth.
