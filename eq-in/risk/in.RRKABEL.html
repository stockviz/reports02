RR Kabel Limited (RRKABEL) - Q2 FY26 Analysis

Overview: RR Kabel delivered a stellar Q2 FY26 with record H1 revenue (₹4,222 Cr, +16.7% YoY) and PAT (₹206 Cr, +80.9% YoY), driven by robust Wires & Cables (W&C) performance (91% of revenue, +22% YoY, 16% volume growth). EBITDA margins expanded sharply to 8.1% (+341 bps YoY) due to operating leverage and product mix. FMEG remains challenged but stabilizing. Management targets 18% volume CAGR (Project Rise), H2 > H1 growth, and W&C EBIT margins of 10.5-11% by FY28. Interim dividend of ₹4/share declared.

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Strong W&C Momentum: Backbone of growth (₹1,971 Cr Q2 revenue, +22% YoY). Benefiting from infrastructure push, housing/real estate boom, renewables, electrification, and data centers. 16% volume + better realizations; institutional/retail demand resilient.
  • Margin Expansion: Operating leverage, cost efficiencies, premium product mix (safety-compliant, energy-efficient). Q2 gross margins +300 bps YoY; sustainable per management (no major inventory gains).
  • Export Strength: Healthy growth (~20% YoY), led by Europe/Middle East (75-80% mix). Diversified geographies; certifications aiding global shift to Indian cables.
  • Capacity Ramp-Up: ₹1,200 Cr capex (80% in cables, up to 220 kV incl. HV/LV/special cables). Utilization: Wires ~70%, Cables ~90%. Boosts B2B/institutional share.
  • Macro Support: Govt. infra spending (PLI, smart cities, power reforms), formalization of electrical sector, urbanisation. Economy’s strength sustains demand.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • FMEG Weakness: Revenue flat/declining (₹193 Cr Q2, -3% YoY); fans/appliances hit by soft demand, competition, seasonal factors. Losses stable but ongoing (~Q4 FY26 breakeven targeted).
  • Input Cost Pressure: Rising copper/aluminum prices; volatility inherent but managed via pass-through pricing (3-4 lists/quarter). Potential near-term demand impact if unabsorbed.
  • Working Capital Strain: Inventory up for demand; net WC ~57 days. H1 cash flow negative (-₹130 Cr ops), normalizing by FY26-end.
  • Regional/Market Caution: Rural recovery slow; uneven weather/industrial capex. South/East market share <5%, growth from core North/West.

Growth Prospects

  • Near-Term (H2 FY26): Sequential outperformance vs. H1; 18% volume target on track (H1 ~12%). Domestic (infra/housing) + exports to drive; cables/institutional mix up.
  • Medium-Term (FY27-28): W&C to lead (1.5x industry growth; sector ~13-14% CAGR). FMEG profitability via rationalization/expansion. Exports > domestic focus; new products (special cables for OEMs/utilities).
  • Long-Term: ROE >20%; debt/equity low (~0.1x). Capex execution to scale cables (currently small player), deepen distribution (Tier 2/3), R&D for premium FMEG. Highest-ever H1 sets base for FY26 record.
