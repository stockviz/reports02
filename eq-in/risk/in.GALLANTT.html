Analysis of Gallantt Ispat Limited (GALLANTT)

Gallantt Ispat Limited is a steel manufacturer (pellets, sponge iron, ingots, TMT bars, power generation) with operations in Uttar Pradesh and Gujarat. The provided documents cover key disclosures from Oct-Nov 2025, including board outcomes, Q2/H1 FY26 financials (ended Sep 30, 2025), and AGM results. The company shows robust H1 performance amid steel sector headwinds, with strategic diversification into solar power.

Tailwinds (Positive Factors)

  • Strong Financial Momentum: H1 FY26 standalone revenue at ₹214 Cr (up ~2% YoY), net profit ₹26 Cr (up ~53% YoY). Q2 profit ₹8.7 Cr on ₹101 Cr revenue. Net interest income of ₹1.66 Cr (due to high FD/loan interest vs. low borrowing costs). EPS ₹10.82 (H1). Balance sheet expansion: Total assets up 16% YoY to ₹410 Cr; equity up 9% to ₹310 Cr; RoE implied ~17% annualized.
  • Capex and Asset Growth: PPE surged ~11% to ₹189 Cr (Gorakhpur/Gujarat units); CWIP at ₹17 Cr. Cash from ops ₹12.6 Cr (H1), supporting investments.
  • Strategic Diversification: Entry into renewables via WOS (Suryalaxmi Tech) for 62.5-80 MW solar plant in UP (UPPCL tender); new 18 MW Gujarat plant (₹48 Cr capex, 33.75 Mn KWH/year, operational May 2026). Reduces steel cyclicality; power sales to govt/exchanges for steady revenue.
  • Corporate Actions: Sold non-core associates (Medicity ₹7.8 Cr, Lifespace ₹82 Cr) to streamline focus (nil revenue contribution). High promoter/public support at AGM (99%+ approval for key resolutions like MD re-appointment).
  • Sector Support: India’s infra push (roads, housing) boosts steel demand; renewables aligned with govt targets.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Cyclical Steel Exposure: Core business (steel/allied) remains vulnerable to raw material volatility (e.g., iron ore costs at ₹73 Cr in Q2). Inventory up 8% YoY; receivables up 47% signals working capital strain.
  • Cash Constraints: Cash equivalents dropped to ₹0.4 Cr (from ₹5.3 Cr); heavy investing outflows (₹40 Cr H1) funded by short-term borrowings (up 101% to ₹55 Cr).
  • Profit Dip in Q2: Q2 profit ₹8.7 Cr vs. Q1 ₹17.4 Cr (seasonal? higher raw mat costs, inventory changes).
  • Promoter Dominance: ~69% holding; related-party sales to promoter group (Gallantt Fincares) may raise governance flags despite “arm’s length” claim.

Growth Prospects

  • High: Solar pivot offers 100+ MW pipeline (UP + Gujarat), with ~₹50 Cr initial capex scaling via equity infusions in WOS. Potential annuity-like revenue from power offtake (state govt/exchanges). Steel capex (PPE/CWIP growth) eyes capacity addition amid India’s 7-8% steel demand CAGR (infra/housing).
  • Revenue Potential: Solar could add ₹50-100 Cr+ annual topline (at ₹3-4/kWh); H1 EBITDA margin ~20% sustainable if steel prices hold.
  • Valuation Upside: Market cap implied growth stock if diversification succeeds; dividend yield 1.25% (₹1.25/share declared FY25).
  • Timeline: Gujarat plant by May 2026; UP bidding underway.
