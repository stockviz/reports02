Gallantt Ispat Limited (GALLANTT) Analysis

Company Overview: Gallantt Ispat is a steel manufacturer (pellets, sponge iron, ingots, TMT bars) with captive power generation. Listed on BSE/NSE (Scrip: 532726/GALLANTT). FY25 revenue ~₹429 Cr; H1 FY26 revenue ₹214 Cr (↑2%), net profit ₹26 Cr (↑53%). Market cap ~₹1,500-2,000 Cr (indicative). Expanding into solar power.

Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts)

  • Robust Financial Momentum: H1 FY26 standalone revenue +2% YoY, PBT +31% (₹319 Cr), net profit +53% (₹261 Cr). EPS ₹10.82 (↑53%). Net interest income of ₹1.66 Cr (interest earned > paid). Balance sheet strong: Equity ₹310 Cr, total assets +16% to ₹4,101 Cr.
  • Dividend Policy: Declared 12.5% final dividend (₹1.25/share) for FY25, signaling confidence.
  • Shareholder Approval: 21st AGM (Sep 2025) passed all resolutions overwhelmingly (>99% favor), including reappointment of CMD C.P. Agrawal and directors.
  • Government Support for Steel/Renewables: Steel capacity expansion aligns with India’s infra push; solar entry taps PLI scheme, green energy targets.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Cyclical Steel Sector: Q2 FY26 revenue dipped 10% QoQ (₹1,013 Cr vs ₹1,128 Cr Q1); raw material costs ~72% of revenue. Vulnerable to steel price volatility and import dumping.
  • Rising Borrowings: Short-term debt doubled to ₹554 Cr (funds capex/working capital). Finance costs up (₹14.7 Cr H1).
  • Cash Flow Pressure: Operating cash +₹126 Cr but investing cash outflow ₹396 Cr (PPE/capex). Cash equivalents fell to ₹0.4 Cr.
  • Associate Divestment: Sold stakes in Gallantt Medicity (₹7.8 Cr) and Lifespace Developers (₹82 Cr) to promoter group entity (Gallantt Fincares) – streamlines but raises RPT concerns.

Growth Prospects

  • Diversification into Renewables (High Potential):
    • New WOS (Suryalaxmi Tech) for 62.5-80 MW solar plant in UP (bidding via UPPCL; power sale to state/exchange).
    • 18 MW Gujarat solar plant (₹48 Cr capex; operational by May 2026; 33.75 Mn KWH/year).
    • Total solar pipeline ~100 MW; positions for 20-30% revenue mix from power in 2-3 yrs, aided by subsidies (e.g., SECI tariffs).
  • Steel Capacity Ramp: Ongoing capex (PPE +11% to ₹1,890 Cr; CWIP ₹172 Cr). Targets higher utilization amid infra demand (roads, housing).
  • Capex Visibility: H1 investments ₹107 Cr (PPE) + ₹74 Cr (shares/loans). Projected FY26 revenue growth 15-20% on steel recovery + solar.
  • Long-term: Steel + green power hybrid model; potential EBITDA CAGR 20-25% if solar executes on time.

Key Risks

Risk Category Details Mitigation
Execution Solar delays (land, bids, funding); Gujarat plant ₹48 Cr cost overrun risk. WOS structure; technical analysis done.
Commodity/Market Steel prices down 10-15% CY25; China dumping. Demand slowdown if infra spend cuts. Diversification (solar 20% buffer).
Financial/Leverage Debt/EBITDA ~1x but rising ST borrowings; forex if imports rise. Positive net interest; op. cash covers.
Regulatory/Governance RPTs (associate sales to promoter group); SEBI scrutiny on disclosures. Arm’s length pricing affirmed; compliant filings.
Operational Raw material volatility (73% costs); power costs/depreciation up 8% YoY. Captive power; cost pass-through.
Macro Interest rate hikes; monsoon/infra delays. Low debt base; dividend payout.

Overall Outlook: Positive (Buy/Hold). Tailwinds from profits/solar diversification outweigh steel cyclicality. Growth via 100 MW solar (~₹100-150 Cr revenue potential) could re-rate stock 20-30%. Monitor Q3 results/debt for steel recovery. Target: 15-20% upside in 12M (steel + green premium). Risks tilted toward execution/commodities (medium).
