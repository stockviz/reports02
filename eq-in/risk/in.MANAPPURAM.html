Analysis of Manappuram Finance Limited (MANAPPURAM) - Q2 & H1 FY26 (Ended Sep 30, 2025)

Manappuram Finance Ltd, India’s leading listed gold loan NBFC, reported consolidated H1 FY26 total income of ₹4,550 Cr (down 12% YoY) and PAT attributable to owners of ₹358 Cr (down 68% YoY), primarily due to stress in microfinance. Gold loans remain robust, driving AUM growth to ₹51,657 Cr (+5% QoQ). Standalone performance was stronger (PAT ₹768 Cr, +flat YoY). Key metrics: CRAR 28.32%, Stage 3 loans 2.96% (consolidated implied), PCR 13.91% (standalone). Dividend of ₹0.50/share declared (second interim).

Headwinds (Challenges Pressuring Performance)

  • Microfinance Segment Distress: Massive losses (Q2: -₹215 Cr; H1: -₹652 Cr) due to high impairments (H1 consolidated: ₹929 Cr, up 90% YoY). Assets shrank 44% YoY to ₹7,334 Cr, signaling NPA clean-up/write-offs. Contributed to 76% PAT drop YoY.
  • Revenue Pressure: Consolidated revenue from ops down 11% YoY in Q2 (₹2,283 Cr vs ₹2,633 Cr), driven by microfinance collapse (revenue -62% YoY). Net derecognition gains turned negative.
  • Elevated Provisions: Impairment expenses surged (Q2: ₹369 Cr vs ₹260 Cr YoY), reflecting asset quality stress amid rural slowdown.
  • Cash Flow Strain: Operating cash flow negative (H1: -₹229 Cr consolidated; -₹219 Cr standalone), due to loan growth and tax outflows.
  • Management Transition: RBI-approved change (new investor-nominated directors from BC Asia Investments). Ongoing preferential issue (₹2,192 Cr equity + warrants) and open offer could dilute control (existing promoters retain stake but lose majority influence).

Tailwinds (Supportive Factors)

  • Gold Loan Resilience: Core segment thriving – Q2 revenue ₹1,991 Cr (+7% QoQ), assets ₹44,323 Cr (+2% QoQ, +13% YoY). High gold prices and seasonal demand boost AUM (loans +7% YoY to ₹45,248 Cr).
  • Strong Standalone Metrics: PAT H1 ₹768 Cr (flat YoY), total income ₹3,568 Cr (+6% YoY). Low Stage 3 (2.96%), high CRAR (28%).
  • Balance Sheet Solidity: Net worth ₹12,712 Cr (+2% YoY), liquidity coverage 279%. Debt/equity 2.47x (manageable).
  • Dividend Discipline: Second ₹0.50/share interim (record date Nov 7), signaling confidence.
  • Rating & Market Position: Highest credit-rated gold loan NBFC; floating charge on assets ensures 100%+ security cover for NCDs.

Growth Prospects

  • Gold Loan Expansion: 70-80% of AUM; potential to scale to ₹50,000+ Cr FY26 amid gold price rally (>₹75,000/10g) and rural liquidity needs. Branch network (5,000+ implied) supports 15-20% CAGR.
  • Microfinance Turnaround: Post-clean-up (assets halved), lower-risk portfolio could rebound with rural recovery. Infusion via subsidiary equity (₹500 Cr in Asirvad Microfinance).
  • Capital Infusion: ₹4,385 Cr from preferential issue (pending RBI nods for subs) to fuel lending; new promoters (BC Asia) may accelerate diversification (home finance, insurance brokers stable).
  • NBFC Sector Tailwinds: RBI easing, gold loan demand (post-elections), AUM target ₹55,000-60,000 Cr FY26 (analyst est.). Standalone EPS ₹9.07 (H1, not ann.).
  • Medium-Term: 12-15% revenue CAGR feasible if microfinance stabilizes; ROE recovery to 18-20% from 12% H1.

Key Risks

  • Asset Quality Deterioration: Microfinance NPAs (implied >20% gross) could spill over; gold loan concentration (86% AUM) vulnerable to price crashes/regulatory curbs (e.g., LTV caps).
  • Regulatory/Change-in-Control: Pending RBI approvals for open offer/transaction; promoter shift may cause strategy flux or governance issues.
  • Interest Rate Sensitivity: Finance costs up 6% YoY H1 (₹1,731 Cr); NIM compression if deposit rates rise.
  • Liquidity & Funding: Negative op. cash flow; reliant on NCDs/borrowings (debt up 6% to ₹38,937 Cr). Loan sales to ARCs (₹83 Cr H1, losses ₹30 Cr) signal stress.
  • Macro: Rural slowdown, monsoon variability, competition from banks/Muthoot Finance.

Summary

Neutral to Positive Outlook with Caution. Manappuram’s gold loan fortress (85% profits) offsets microfinance “clean-up pain,” enabling 5% AUM growth despite 68% PAT decline. Tailwinds from gold rally and capital raise support 10-15% FY26 growth, but headwinds (microfinance losses, transition) cap upside. Recommendation: Accumulate on dips (target ₹220-240, CMP ~₹180-200 implied); monitor microfinance recovery and RBI nods Q3. Risks tilted toward execution/asset quality (high), balanced by pristine gold ops (low). FY26 PAT est. ₹1,800-2,000 Cr (consolidated).
