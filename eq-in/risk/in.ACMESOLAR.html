ACME Solar Holdings Limited (ACMESOLAR) Analysis

ACME Solar Holdings Ltd. is a leading Indian renewable energy developer with a focus on solar, wind, hybrid, and battery energy storage systems (BESS). It has ~2.93 GW operational capacity, 4.46 GW under construction (incl. 13.5 GWh BESS), and total PPA-signed capacity of 5.38 GW as of Nov 2025. The company benefits from in-house EPC/O&M, strong tender wins, and a diversified project pipeline. Below is an analysis based on the provided disclosures (Q2/H1 FY25 results, PPAs, tenders, AGM outcomes).

Tailwinds

  • Robust Project Pipeline & Tender Wins: Recent PPA for 200 MW solar+ESS (SECI, Rs 3.42/unit, SCOD Jun 2027; adds 800 MW/2.32 GWh BESS signed in FY25). Won 130 MW RTC tender (REMCL/Railways, Rs 4.35/unit; LOA expected soon). Demonstrates execution strength and competitiveness in RTC/hybrid segments.
  • Strong Financial Performance: H1 FY25 consolidated revenue ₹9.79 bn (+72% YoY), EBITDA ₹10.65 bn (high margins ~108% of revenue), PAT ₹2.46 bn. Q2 revenue ₹4.68 bn, PAT ₹1.15 bn. Standalone revenue from EPC/O&M grew to ₹6.15 bn.
  • Investor & Market Momentum: Scheduled physical meets in Mumbai/Hong Kong. AGM approvals for fundraising (equity/debt), director re-appointments, RPTs, and auditors signal governance stability.
  • Policy Support: Benefits from ISTS connectivity, SECI/REMCL tenders, and India’s RE push (75% min. availability mandates favor hybrids/BESS).
  • Operational Efficiency: Industry-leading CUF (25-27% solar hours, 70-85% peak), in-house capabilities reduce costs.

Headwinds

  • High Debt Burden: Consolidated LT borrowings ₹97.4 bn (up from ₹98.6 bn YoY), finance costs ₹4.97 bn in H1 (46% of revenue). Short-term borrowings up to ₹25.6 bn. Negative cash flow from financing (₹24.6 bn outflow in H1 due to NCD repayments).
  • Cash Flow Pressure: Net operating cash positive (₹2.94 bn H1), but investing outflows (₹6.85 bn) and capex (CWIP ₹13.7 bn) strain liquidity. Cash equivalents down to ₹4 bn from ₹12.8 bn.
  • Margin Volatility: Exceptional items (₹-117 mn H1), forex gains/losses, and ancillary borrowing costs impact profitability. Employee/other expenses up 25-40% YoY.
  • RPT Scrutiny: Multiple material RPT approvals (e.g., with promoter group VRS Infotech, subsidiaries like ACME Alpha/Renewtech). Public institutions voted ~87-100% in favor, but non-institutions showed 30-46% against in some cases.
  • Execution Delays: Changes in investor meets possible; project timelines sensitive to exigencies.

Growth Prospects

  • Capacity Ramp-Up: 5.38 GW PPA-signed; operational ramp from 2.93 GW could drive revenue to ₹20-25 bn+ in FY26. Focus on high-margin hybrids/BESS (e.g., 13.5 GWh pipeline).
  • Diversification: RTC/peak power tenders (Railways), FDRE/hybrids position it for 24x7 RE demand. Cumulative wins (350 MW SECI tranche fully PPA’d).
  • Fundraising Flexibility: AGM approval for securities issuance (equity/debt) supports capex (₹13.7 bn CWIP).
  • EBITDA/PAT Trajectory: H1 EBITDA margins >100% (other income boost); scaling to 7-8 GW operational could yield 20-25% CAGR revenue/PAT.
  • Market Opportunity: India’s 500 GW RE target by 2030; ACME’s portfolio (solar/wind/storage) aligns with hybrid mandates.

Overall Summary

Bullish Outlook with Managed Risks: ACMESOLAR is well-positioned for 20-30% growth via tender wins and execution, leveraging India’s RE boom. Tailwinds from PPAs (5.38 GW), financial strength (PAT +47% YoY H1), and AGM approvals outweigh headwinds like debt (mitigated by fundraising). Stock catalysts: LOA for 130 MW RTC, Q3 results, debt refinancing. Target operational 7 GW by FY27 could drive re-rating. Valuation Upside: Trade at ~8-10x FY26 EV/EBITDA (peers 12-15x); risks tilted financial but execution track record strong. Recommendation: Accumulate on dips; monitor debt metrics.
