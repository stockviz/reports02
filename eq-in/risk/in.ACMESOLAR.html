ACME Solar Holdings Limited (ACMESOLAR) Analysis

ACME Solar is a leading Indian renewable energy developer with a focus on solar, wind, hybrid, and battery energy storage systems (BESS). It has ~2.9 GW operational capacity, 4.5 GW under construction (including 13.5 GWh BESS), and total PPA-signed capacity of 5.38 GW as of Nov 2025. Q2/H1 FY26 results show robust revenue growth, but high leverage and execution risks persist. Below is a structured analysis based on the provided filings.

Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts)

  • Capacity Expansion & PPA Wins: Signed 200 MW Solar+ESS PPA with SECI (tariff ₹3.42/unit, SCOD Jun 2027; adds to 800 MW/2.3 GWh BESS contracted in FY26). Won 130 MW RTC tender for Indian Railways (₹4.35/unit, direct PPA, commissioning in 30 months). Total PPA capacity up to 5.38 GW.
  • Strong Financial Momentum: H1 FY26 consolidated revenue ₹9.8 Bn (+72% YoY), PBT ₹3.3 Bn (vs ₹0.7 Bn), PAT ₹2.5 Bn. EBITDA margins ~65-70%. Operational CUF leadership and in-house EPC/O&M drive efficiency.
  • Policy & Market Support: RTC/firm power tenders (e.g., Railways) favor hybrids/BESS. ISTS connectivity and land bank accelerate execution.
  • Shareholder Approval: AGM (Sep 2025) passed fundraising (equity/debt up to ₹ unspecified via securities), director re-appointments, and material RPTs (99%+ approval on most).
  • Investor Engagement: Upcoming 1x1/group meetings (Mumbai/Hong Kong).

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • High Debt Burden: Consolidated long-term borrowings ₹97 Bn (+/- minor), finance costs H1 ₹5 Bn (up 33% YoY). NCD repayments (₹14 Bn in H1) strained cash; net debt likely elevated post capex.
  • Cash Flow Pressure: Operating cash positive (₹2.9 Bn H1), but investing outflows (₹6.8 Bn net) and financing (₹24.6 Bn outflow) led to cash drop from ₹12.8 Bn to ₹4 Bn. Bank balances up to ₹30 Bn, but liquidity tight.
  • Subsidiary-Heavy Model: 70+ SPVs; holding co. revenue (₹6.2 Bn H1) from EPC/O&M, but consolidated growth reliant on project ramps.
  • Institutional Pushback: ~29% institutions voted against CEO remuneration (Resolution 5); some against auditors/fundraise.
