HINDOILEXP – Investor Summary: Headwinds, Tailwinds, Growth Prospects & Key Risks

(Based on Earnings Call Transcript: Q2 FY2025-26 – November 21, 2025)

1. Tailwinds (Growth Catalysts & Positive Factors)

① Strategic Focus on Natural Gas – Aligned with Energy Transition

  • 50% of HOEC’s resource base is gas-based, aligning with India’s push for cleaner fuel and energy transition.

  • Key focus on monetizing Kharsang and Dirok gas fields in the Northeast, where commercialization will accelerate with grid connectivity.
  • The company benefits from rising domestic gas demand — India imports >1 TCF of LNG annually — creating a long-term offtake opportunity.

② Near-term Production & Revenue Inflection

  • Northeast Gas Grid integration (Guwahati–Numaligarh completed; Duliajan–Numaligarh pending) is a key revenue trigger.
  • Once fully online, offtake from Dirok is expected to 3x (~14 → ~45 MMSCFD), lifting revenue and utilization of existing wells.
  • Q4 FY26 and FY27 expected to see a step-up in production and realization, especially from Dirok and Kharsang.

③ Reserves & Development Pipeline Unlocking Value

  • 1P Reserves in B-80: ~15 million barrels of oil + 40–45 BCF gas.
  • Dirok/Kharsang Potential: Estimated 200–400 BCF gas resources; long plateau life (~20 years).
  • Offshore Potential:
    • B-15 (Mumbai High): High-potential field (332 sq km), ~2-year timeline to production, 100% owned.
    • PY-1 (Cauvery): First well planned in FY27; infrastructure-ready; low CAPEX via existing platform.
  • Drilling Plans: 18 shallow + 3 deep wells in Kharsang; 10 offshore wells across B-80, B-15, and PY-1.

④ Cost Advantage in Production

  • Low operating cost structure:
    • Dirok: < ₹0.75/MMBTU gas production cost.
    • B-80: $30/barrel of oil equivalent (includes FSO & MOPU costs), competitive for Indian offshore.
  • Fully owns key offshore assets (FSO, MOPU), enabling cost control and margin protection.

⑤ Strong Funding for CAPEX

  • Raised ₹250 crores term loan exclusively for capital expenditure.
  • Committed to no additional borrowing beyond ₹250 crores, signaling financial prudence.
  • Cash flow + internal accruals to fund growth without balance sheet stress.

⑥ Supportive Partnerships

  • Collaboration with Oil India (OIL), ONGC, and IOC adds credibility and de-risks operations.
  • IOC already confirmed crude intake from HOEC (no issues reported), reinforcing trust.
2. Headwinds (Short-term Challenges & Constraints)

① Delayed Gas Grid Connectivity (Dirok/Northeast)

  • Despite progress, Duliajan–Numaligarh link (DNPL) remains uncompleted, limiting gas offtake.
  • Current Dirok gas sales: 14 MMSCFD (vs. 20 MMSCFD prior) due to demand constraints.
  • Full monetization delayed until late FY26 or Q1 FY27, impacting current cash flows.

② Monsoon-Related Disruptions (Offshore)

  • B-80 production was shut down for ~37 days (June–August 2025) due to monsoon.
  • Led to sharp sequential drop in EBITDA (₹35cr → ₹25cr consolidated) and revenue volatility.
  • Management aims to minimize future disruptions with improved planning.

③ HPCL Payment Delay – ₹259 Crores Outstanding

  • Crude delivery completed (417,000 barrels) and revenue booked, but payment not yet received.
  • HPCL alleges chloride contamination; however, HOEC asserts:
    • Crude quality is sweet & light (no reservoir issues).
    • COSA contract transfers risk/title at offshore flange → liability lies with buyer.
  • Risk: Temporary cash flow strain if resolution is delayed; reputational risk if dispute escalates.

④ Drilling Delays & RIG Availability

  • Workover on D1 well (B-80) delayed to Q4 FY26 due to availability of tree-running tools and rig delays from mergers.
  • Drill rig availability concerns highlighted, though management confident via support from Oil India.

⑤ Accounting & Consolidated Earnings Volatility

  • Standalone PAT (₹19cr) vs Consolidated PAT (₹2.83cr) due to:
    • Depreciation, finance costs, and costs at subsidiaries Hindage & Geopetrol (FSO/MOPU owners).
    • No material loss, but investors may misread consolidated performance.

4. Key Risks
Risk Category Details
Pipeline & Infrastructure Risk DNPL-IGGL connection, PESO license, PNGRB approval** pending. Any delay extends monetization timeline.
Counterparty Risk (HPCL) Although legally strong position, delayed resolution impacts liquidity; potential for reputational damage if claims escalate.
Commodity Price Volatility HOEC does not hedge – exposed to oil price swings. However, low costs and natural storage provide some flexibility.
Gas Field Challenges Well control issue at Kharsang well #6 (resolved in days, insured). Demonstrated gas presence but adds execution risk.
Regulatory & Sub-judice Matters PY-3 dispute: 21% stake. Legal process underway; outcome uncertain.
Market Perception Confusion between standalone vs consolidated earnings may deter less-informed investors.
5. Management Strategy & Confidence Indicators
  • “We will continue to drill” – persistent focus on unlocking value.
  • “We are a marginal operator” – cost discipline embedded in operations.
  • Strong confidence in gas monetization, offshore development, and long-standing relationships.
  • No debt fear: “Balance sheet risk is not there.”
  • Natural hedge strategy: Use of storage to time sales, though no formal hedging policy.
Conclusion & Investment Thesis

✅ Positive Case (Bullish)

HINDOILEXP is poised for transformational growth from FY26–FY27 as: - Gas evacuation bottlenecks are resolved. - Offshore and onshore drilling bears fruit. - Debt-free operations and self-owned infrastructure support margin resilience.

It could emerge as a high-growth Indian E&P company with long-cycle, low-decline assets.

⚠️ Cautious View (Risks to Monitor)

  • DNPL Pipeline Delays: Any slip into FY27 delays revenue growth.
  • HPCL Payment: Resolution timeline is critical — watch for collections in Q4 FY26.
  • Execution Risk: Relying on timely rig access and partner coordination.

Summary Verdict: Constructive Buy (Hold for Growth Inflection)
