Analysis of Clean Science and Technology Limited (CLEAN)

Clean Science and Technology Limited (CLEAN) is a specialty chemicals manufacturer with proprietary catalytic processes, global leadership in products like MEHQ, BHA, AP, TBHQ, and HALS. It operates debt-free with high margins (44% EBITDA standalone in Q2 FY26). Below is a structured analysis based on Q2 FY26 earnings transcript, investor presentation, promoter disclosures, and postal ballot notice (all dated Nov 2025). FY26 refers to Apr-Sep 2025 (H1) and Q2 (Jul-Sep).

Headwinds (Short-term Pressures)
  • Revenue Moderation: Standalone revenue declined 5% QoQ (INR 206 Cr) and 8% YoY; consolidated flat QoQ (INR 240 Cr). Driven by 2-6.5% volume drop and 3% realization fall in established products (top-4 contribute 80%). H1 standalone down 3-4% YoY.
  • Market/Geo Challenges: China (25-30% revenue) hit by customer slowdowns, possible backward integration in FMCG product, and oversupply. US/Americas impacted by 55% tariffs (e.g., BHT) and customer-specific deferrals. End-market uncertainty (e.g., pigments, polymers).
  • Cost Optics: Subsidiary (CFCL/HALS) gross margins dipped to 26% QoQ due to inventory unwind (higher-cost opening stock); forex losses widened PAT dip (15% QoQ standalone).
  • Capex Phase: INR 150 Cr H1 spend; near-term ramp-up risks as new plants stabilize.
Tailwinds (Supportive Factors)
  • Margin Resilience: Standalone EBITDA 44% (flat QoQ, +2% YoY) on favorable mix; HALS material margins up to 35% (from 31%) via better RM costs/higher grades. H1 PAT flat YoY at INR 141 Cr standalone.
  • HALS Momentum: Volumes ~260 tons/month (+25% QoQ); commercialized HALS 2020; exports to US/Europe/Vietnam ramping (domestic share ~50%, shifting to 75-25 domestic-export mix). Blended realizations improving.
  • Strong Fundamentals: Debt-free (CRISIL AA-/A1+); ROE ~44%; 63% export revenue; tech moat (global #1/2 in key products); ZLD/ISO-certified plants.
  • Promoter Stability: Inter-se gifts (1.78 Cr shares, 16.8%) consolidate holdings (no aggregate change); leadership transition (Ashok Boob to Exec Vice Chairman; Siddhartha Sikchi to MD from Apr 2026).

Summary

CLEAN faces near-term headwinds from China/US slowdowns and tariffs, leading to revenue softness (Q2 down 5-8%), but demonstrates resilience with 44% EBITDA margins and debt-free status. Tailwinds include HALS ramp (+25% volumes, margin gains) and promoter consolidation. Growth prospects are robust via PC1 commercialization (INR 300 Cr potential), HALS exports, and capex execution—targeting sequential H2 recovery and FY28 scale-up. Key risks center on China competition, tariffs, and execution, but tech moat/ESG focus provide buffers. Overall, positive long-term outlook (16% 5-yr revenue CAGR historically) with cautious FY26 (flat H1, Q4 uptick). Stock suits patient investors; monitor Q3 results (Jan 2026) for ramps. Valuation context: Trade at ~35-40x FY26 EPS (est.); peers at 30-50x.
