Analysis of Sanstar Limited (SANSTAR, Scrip: 544217)

Sanstar Limited, a starch and derivatives manufacturer (single segment), reported unaudited standalone H1 FY26 results (ended Sep 30, 2025). Key highlights: Revenue ₹366.09 Cr (↓28% YoY from ₹509.11 Cr in H1 FY25), PBT ₹0.15 Cr (↓99% YoY), PAT ₹0.29 Cr (↓99% YoY). Q2 FY26 showed recovery (revenue ↑16% YoY to ₹196.43 Cr, PAT ₹0.63 Cr). Balance sheet stable at ₹761 Cr assets; equity strong at ₹659 Cr post-IPO (Jul 2024). Heavy capex (CWIP ↑113% to ₹185 Cr); operating cash flow robust at ₹44.50 Cr, but net cash ↓69% to ₹37 Cr due to investing outflow (₹122 Cr).

Tailwinds (Positive Factors)

  • Revenue Momentum in Q2: ↑16% YoY to ₹196 Cr, driven by operations; total income H1 at ₹372 Cr indicates seasonal recovery.
  • Strong Balance Sheet & Liquidity: Equity ₹659 Cr (87% of assets); low debt (total borrowings ↓17% to ₹22 Cr); operating cash flow +₹44 Cr from inventory/receivables optimization (↓16 Cr/↓13 Cr).
  • IPO Infusion & Expansion: FY25 IPO raised ~₹397 Cr (fresh issue); funding capex (PPE + CWIP ₹373 Cr total non-current assets ↑37% YoY).
  • Cost Discipline: Finance costs ↓78% YoY (low leverage); employee expenses stable.
  • Governance: Overwhelming AGM approval (99%+ votes) for financials, director re-appointment, auditors.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Profitability Squeeze: H1 margins dismal (PBT 0.04%, EBITDA ~2%); material costs 75% of revenue (↑ YoY); YoY revenue decline 28% amid inventory buildup earlier.
  • Cash Depletion: Net cash equivalents ↓69% to ₹37 Cr; heavy investing outflow (capex ₹88 Cr, investments ₹28 Cr).
  • Working Capital Strain: Trade receivables ₹91 Cr (stable but high at 25% of revenue); trade payables low at ₹23 Cr.
  • Cyclical Sector Pressures: Starch industry faces raw material volatility (e.g., maize prices); Q1/Q2 PBT volatile (₹0.92 Cr vs prior losses).

Growth Prospects

  • Capacity Expansion: CWIP ₹185 Cr signals major brownfield/greenfield projects at 2 factories (Dhule, Kutch); potential to scale revenue 1.5-2x in 2-3 years as starch demand grows (food/pharma/paper sectors; India maize processing capacity utilization ~70%).
  • Post-IPO Trajectory: Funds for modernization/debt reduction; FY25 revenue ₹957 Cr baseline; aim for 15-20% CAGR via derivatives/value-added products.
  • Cash Generation: Strong OCF supports self-funded growth; low capex payback risk if utilization rises.
  • Outlook: FY26 revenue could rebound to ₹1,000+ Cr if Q3/Q4 mirrors Q2 growth; margins to 5-8% with scale/efficiencies.

Key Risks

  • Margin Volatility: Commodity exposure (80%+ costs variable); forex/raw material inflation could erode thin profits (EPS H1 ₹0.02).
  • Execution Risk: ₹185 Cr CWIP delays/commissioning issues; capex intensity (investing CF -122 Cr) strains cash if OCF weakens.
  • Demand/Liquidity: Receivables concentration; slowing revenue YoY signals end-market weakness (e.g., FMCG slowdown).
  • Regulatory/Compliance: SEBI LODR adherence post-listing; tax adjustments (e.g., prior year excess ₹0.07 Cr).
  • Macro: Interest rate hikes, monsoon/agri volatility impacting maize supply.

Overall Summary: Sanstar faces near-term headwinds from profitability erosion and YoY revenue dip amid capex phase, but tailwinds from IPO strength, OCF resilience, and expansion position it for medium-term growth (2-3 years). Prospects hinge on capex fruition and margin recovery; cautious Buy/Hold for long-term starch play, monitor Q3 for execution. Risks tilted toward operational/cyclical factors; EV/EBITDA ~10-12x FY25 appears reasonable if growth materializes.
