Analysis of Industrial Investment Trust Limited (IITL)

IITL is an investment and lending-focused NBFC-like entity (CIN: L65990MH1933PLC001998) listed on BSE (501295) and NSE (IITL). Key insights from Q2/H1 FY26 unaudited results (ended Sep 30, 2025), board outcomes, AGM voting, and compliance filings. Financials show profitability amid volatility, with assets at ~₹42,467 lakhs (standalone) and strong cash flows, but subsidiary distress looms large.

Tailwinds (Positive Factors)

  • Profitability & Cash Generation: H1 FY26 PAT standalone ₹666.42 lakhs (EPS ₹2.96); consolidated ₹710.41 lakhs. Strong operating cash flow of ₹6,612 lakhs (standalone), driven by loan realizations (₹9,866 lakhs). Cash & equivalents surged to ₹1,758 lakhs (+ve liquidity).
  • Asset Growth: Investments up to ₹28,759 lakhs (+24% YoY); loans at ₹9,377 lakhs. Equity base solid at ₹2,255 lakhs; other equity ₹39,161 lakhs (FY25).
  • Strategic Moves: New Group CFO (Gorakh Ingale, experienced CA/CS/LLB) appointed Nov 12, 2025. Board approved extension of 70L zero-% preference shares redemption to Mar 2028 (postal ballot pending). Amalgamation of 2 subs completed (effective Apr 2024).
  • Shareholder Approval: 92nd AGM (Sep 13, 2025) saw 53.5% voter turnout; all resolutions (financials adoption, director re-apt, auditors, RPT for rent) passed >99% in favor (promoters 100% support).
  • Compliance: No physical share re-lodgement requests (Oct 2025); ads published per SEBI circular.

Headwinds (Negative Factors)

  • Earnings Volatility: Q2 standalone loss ₹346 lakhs (fair value loss ₹511 lakhs); H1 PAT down ~55% YoY (standalone: ₹666 vs ₹1,497 lakhs). Interest income declined (H1: ₹799 vs ₹964 lakhs).
  • Subsidiary Distress: IITL Projects Ltd (investment ₹1,361 lakhs) not a “going concern” – net worth fully eroded (accum. loss ₹607 lakhs), liabilities > assets, no business/cash flows. Auditors flagged (no modification, but emphasis of matter).
  • Expense Pressures: Employee costs up (H1: ₹187 lakhs); depreciation ₹95 lakhs. Consolidated non-controlling interest losses.
  • Low Public Participation: AGM e-voting turnout low among public non-institutions (0.04%); promoters dominate (99.8% shares voted).

Growth Prospects

  • Core Segments Strong: Investment (62% assets, H1 revenue ₹622 lakhs), Lending (₹757 lakhs revenue), Real Estate (flats sales ₹92 lakhs consolidated). Potential from demat window (open till Jan 2026).
  • Investment Portfolio: Active trading (MF/equity buys/sells); govt securities maturities provided cash. Lending realizations outpace disbursals.
  • Expansion Potential: RPT approval for Delhi office rent; preference shares extension aids sub stability. Tax under Sec 115BAA supports efficiency.
  • Outlook: FY26 PAT could rebound if fair value stabilizes (FY25: ₹321 lakhs standalone). Cash hoard enables opportunistic investments/lending growth (5-10% asset CAGR feasible).

Overall Summary: IITL remains resilient with robust cash/investments and profitable core ops, but headwinds from sub distress and volatility cap upside. Growth in lending/investments offers 10-15% PAT potential FY26 if markets stabilize; watch sub impairment (top risk). Hold/accumulate for long-term; target fair value gains.
