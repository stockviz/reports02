Godrej Agrovet Limited (GODREJAGRO) Analysis

Overall Snapshot (Q2 & H1 FY26, ended Sep 30, 2025):
Consolidated revenues grew 5% YoY to INR 2,567 cr (Q2) and 8% to INR 5,182 cr (H1), driven by Vegetable Oil (+41% Q2) and Animal Feed volumes (+11% Q2). PBT flat at INR 125 cr (Q2) but +14% ex-non-recurring to INR 313 cr (H1). Crop Protection dragged (-30% Q2 revenue due to excess rainfall). EBITDA margins resilient at 8.6-9.7%. Debt stable YoY expected; Net Worth INR 1,949 cr. New MD & CEO Sunil Kataria outlined FY30 strategy refresh focusing on portfolio simplification, diversification, value-add, and cost efficiencies (Project PI). Recent MoU with Andhra Pradesh govt for INR 70 cr investment in dairy/oil palm.

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Strong Segment Performers:
    • Animal Feed (44% revenue): Record volumes (+11% Q2, +10% H1), cattle feed +18% YoY on market share gains, geographic expansion, and quality upgrades. Underlying margins +70 bps to 6.6%.
    • Vegetable Oil/Oil Palm (21% revenue): Stellar +41% Q2 revenue on high CPO/PKO realizations (+20-68%) and record OER (19% H1). FFB +9% Q2, +24% H1; mid-teens FY26 growth guided.
    • Dairy (15%): VAP +10%, salience to 36%; resilient EBITDA margins despite higher procurement/marketing spends.
    • Foods (Poultry): Branded salience to 86% (+3% YoY growth), EBITDA +28% Q2.
  • Astec Lifesciences Improving: Enterprise +15% YoY; EBITDA loss narrowed (H1 -INR 16 cr vs -63 cr). CDMO phasing to H2; targeting EBITDA breakeven FY26, 55% salience.
  • Strategic/M&A Momentum: Full control of Creamline Dairy (99.78%) and Godrej Foods; new MoU for dairy expansion + 5 oil palm Samadhan centers.
  • Macro Support: Rising milk prices aiding branded feed adoption; good monsoons aiding oil palm.
  • ESG Edge: Water positive (13x sequestration), 78% renewable energy, SBTi-aligned emissions targets; strong CDP ratings.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Crop Protection (11% revenue): Sharp -30% Q2/-9% H1 decline; margins compressed to 23% from 43% due to uneven/excess rainfall narrowing spray windows, lower chilli acreages. H2 “moderate to soft”; unseasonal Oct rains add uncertainty.
  • Realization Pressures: Animal Feed revenue flat despite volumes (soft commodities); pass-through nature limits upside.
  • Cost Headwinds: Unallocated expenses + (one-time base reversal last year + variable pay); employee costs up on profitability.
  • Astec CDMO Volatility: -25% Q2 revenue on cautious demand/phasing; ramp-up slower than expected.
  • Working Capital/Debt: H1 debt up (seasonal); unwinding expected H2, but tight management emphasized.

Growth Prospects

  • Near-Term (FY26): Healthy revenue growth (original 16-18% tempered by Crop Protection); strong bottom-line (+14% H1 pace). Oil Palm/Animal Feed to lead; H2 CDMO recovery; Crop Protection diversification via new launches (Ashitaka-maize success; Q4 pan-crop entry; 3 more FY27).
  • Medium-Term (FY27-30): Strategy refresh to build multi-pillar portfolios (e.g., Crop Protection beyond cotton/chilli); value-add shift (branded salience rising); R&D/CDMO ramp at Astec (world-class center, innovator tie-ups, 12 molecules in pipeline). Oil palm planting to yield post-4-5 years. AP MoU scales dairy/VAP + farmer support.
  • Long-Term Catalysts: FY30 “unleash” model (end-Mar’26 disclosure); INR 15,000 cr revenue aspiration per investor query. Pan-India footprint, JV expansions (ACI Bangladesh), ESG-driven sustainability.
