Analysis of Narmada Agrobase Ltd. (NSE: NARMADA, BSE: 543643)

Narmada Agrobase Ltd., a Gujarat-based manufacturer of cattle feed and cottonseed-based agro by-products (ISO 9001:2015 certified), reported robust H1 FY26 results (ended Sep 30, 2025): Revenue ₹2,304 lakhs (+~2% YoY), EBITDA ₹262 lakhs (11% margin), PAT ₹205 lakhs (8.6% margin). FY25 was a breakout year with revenue ₹6,634 lakhs (+31% YoY) and PAT ₹409 lakhs (6.2% margin). The company operates at 50% of 40,000 TPA capacity, follows a high-volume B2B model (150+ wholesale, 1,000+ retail points), and raised ₹3,658 lakhs via rights issue (partially deployed for capex/working capital). Promoter holding ~50%. Stock trades at ~₹24 (MCAP ₹91 Cr as of Nov 2025), with 52-week range ₹14.6-28.4.

Below is a structured summary of headwinds, tailwinds, growth prospects, and key risks, derived from the Q2/H1 FY26 investor presentation, financial results, balance sheet/cash flow, press release, and AGM disclosures.

Tailwinds (Supportive Factors)
  • Strong Demand in Core Markets: India’s cattle feed market (₹1,110 Bn in 2024) projected to grow at 6.9% CAGR to ₹2,025 Bn by 2033, driven by India’s 25% global milk production share and livestock GVA rising to 30% (FY23). Cottonseed market to grow ~6% CAGR to USD 1.16 Bn by 2030.
  • Revenue Momentum & Margin Expansion: H1 FY26 revenue up 2% YoY; cattle feed (52% mix) grew on dairy/poultry demand. EBITDA margins improved to 11% (H1 FY26) from 4-5% in FY23-24, aided by operational efficiencies and value-added products.
  • Healthy Balance Sheet: Net worth ₹5,587 lakhs (FY25), low debt/equity (0.17), ROE 6.9%, ROCE 8.6%. Rights issue funds provide liquidity (₹770 lakhs unutilized for capex).
  • Brand & Distribution Strength: 99% retention, no client concentration; diversified revenue (52% cattle feed, 48% cottonseed); 12+ years experience.
  • Procurement Edge: Pre-booking/buffer stocks mitigate volatility; local sourcing in Gujarat.
Headwinds (Challenges)
  • Raw Material Cost Pressure: Elevated cottonseed/molasses prices squeezed Q2 FY26 EBITDA (despite 19% YoY revenue growth); raw materials ~84% of costs (H1 FY26).
  • Sub-Optimal Capacity Utilization: 50% of 40,000 TPA; needs volume ramp-up amid seasonal demand.
  • Negative Operating Cash Flows: H1 FY26 CFO -₹258 lakhs (working capital drag: inventories up ₹654 lakhs); FY25 CFO -₹1,593 lakhs, reliant on financing inflows.
  • Margin Volatility: Low-margin B2B model (historically 4-6% PAT); FY24 dip due to costs.
  • Geographic Concentration: Gujarat-dominant (strong but limits scale vs. national players).
Growth Prospects
  • Capacity & Product Expansion: ₹770 lakhs capex for automation/new plant (10 Cr fixed deposits held); launch pelletized/molasses-enriched feeds for margin upside.
  • Market Expansion: Enter Maharashtra/Punjab-Haryana; exports to SE Asia/Middle East/Africa (FY25 exports ₹1,248 lakhs, 19% of revenue; up from nil in FY23).
  • Volume Leverage: 50% spare capacity; target higher utilization via 1,000+ retail/150+ wholesale network.
  • Rights Issue Deployment: ₹1,787 lakhs for working capital (fully used), ₹901 lakhs general corporate (used); supports scaling.
  • Industry Tailwinds: Govt. initiatives (National Livestock Mission); shift to scientific feeds. FY26 revenue potential: 20-30%+ growth if margins hold.

Projected Trajectory: Consensus implies 25-35% revenue CAGR over 2-3 years, with EBITDA margins stabilizing at 10-12% on efficiencies/exports.

Overall Outlook: Positive with moderate risks. Tailwinds from industry growth and rights-funded expansion outweigh headwinds; focus on execution. Stock appears undervalued vs. growth (FY26E P/E ~11x at ₹24), but monitor raw material costs and cash flows. Suitable for long-term agri exposure; near-term catalysts: Q3 results, capex progress.
