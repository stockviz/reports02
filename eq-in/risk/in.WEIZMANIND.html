Analysis of Weizmann Limited (WEIZMANIND)

Weizmann Limited is a textile processing and manufacturing company (single reportable segment per Ind AS 108). The provided documents cover the FY25 Annual Report intimation, Q2/H1 FY26 unaudited standalone/consolidated results (ended Sep 30, 2025), balance sheet/cash flows, and AGM outcomes (Sep 25, 2025). Key highlights: H1 FY26 standalone revenue up 25.7% YoY to ₹657 Cr, PAT up 10.7% to ₹36.8 Cr (EPS ₹2.37); consolidated PAT ₹31.9 Cr impacted by associate losses. Strong operating cash flow (₹177 Cr); capex heavy (₹110 Cr). All AGM resolutions passed with ~99.99% approval.

Tailwinds (Positive Factors)

  • Robust Revenue & Profit Momentum: H1 revenue +26% YoY (₹659 Cr); Q2 sequential growth (₹359 Cr vs. ₹300 Cr in Q1). PAT margins stable ~5.6%; total comprehensive income up.
  • Operational Efficiency: Gross margins improving (inventory drawdown aided); employee costs up but controlled. Power/fuel and job work expenses scaled with revenue.
  • Strong Liquidity & Balance Sheet: Cash from operations surged to ₹177 Cr (vs. ₹53 Cr YoY); cash equivalents up to ₹28 Cr. Borrowings down (short-term -32% to ₹60 Cr); equity grew 4% to ₹676 Cr. Low debt (net debt/equity <0.1).
  • Shareholder-Friendly: Dividend declared (AGM Item 2); buyback in prior year signals confidence.
  • Strategic Investments: Heavy capex (₹110 Cr in H1, PPE up to ₹301 Cr) + CWIP (₹9 Cr) indicates capacity expansion in textiles.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Cost Pressures: Power & fuel (17% of expenses, ₹110 Cr H1), materials (31%), and job work (15%) remain high; up YoY with volume but squeezed margins vs. FY25 full-year.
  • Associate Drag: Consolidated PAT down 3% YoY to ₹32 Cr due to ₹49L associate losses (e.g., Windia Infra); OCI volatility from equity investments.
  • Working Capital Intensity: Trade receivables steady at ₹114 Cr (despite sales growth); trade payables up 17% to ₹156 Cr for support.
  • Sequential Slowdown in Q1: Q1 revenue ₹300 Cr (flat YoY); profit ₹21 Cr, reflecting possible seasonal/weak demand start.

Growth Prospects

  • High (Medium-Term): Textile sector tailwinds (export recovery, domestic demand via PLI schemes). 26% H1 growth + capex (PPE +2%) positions for 15-20% FY26 revenue CAGR. Standalone focus shields from associates; inventory optimization (down 17% to ₹71 Cr) signals efficiency.
  • Expansion Levers: New investment property (₹104 Cr vs. ₹23 Cr); potential from VC/OAVM AGM (digital shift). Dividend payout supports investor base growth.
  • Projections: If Q3/Q4 mirrors H1, FY26 standalone PAT could hit ₹140-160 Cr (vs. FY25 ₹80 Cr), EPS ₹9-10. Market cap upside if textile cycle sustains.
