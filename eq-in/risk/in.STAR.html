Analysis of STAR (Strides Pharma Science Limited)

Stock Codes: BSE: 532531 | NSE: STAR
Report Date: November 17, 2025

Executive Summary

Strides Pharma Science Limited (STAR) delivered a robust performance in Q2FY26, marked by strong year-on-year (YoY) growth across key profitability metrics, including a record quarterly EPS of ₹15.2 and an 84.3% YoY growth in operational PAT. This performance underscores its improving operating discipline, margin expansion, and strategic focus on profitable markets.

However, growth is uneven across segments, and external headwinds—particularly in currency, donor funding, and intense generic market competition—pose near-term challenges. The company’s long-term prospects hinge on its “Beyond Generics” pipeline, pan-European B2B partnerships, and expansion into emerging geographies.

This analysis details the tailwinds, headwinds, growth drivers, and risks in the context of STAR’s financial performance, strategic direction, and macro-industry environment.

1. Tailwinds: Key Growth Drivers & Strengths

1.1 Strong Profitability Momentum

  • Operational PAT: ₹1,403m (Q2FY26) → up 84.3% YoY, outpacing revenue growth.
  • Gross margin expansion: Margin improved to 57.8% (+500 bps YoY), driven by cost optimization, product mix, and operational efficiency.
  • EBITDA growth: Up 25.4% YoY to ₹2,316m with margin at 19.0% (up 320 bps YoY).
  • Record EPS: ₹15.2 (vs. ₹8.3 in Q2FY25), highest ever quarterly EPS, showing strong capital efficiency.

Indicator: Focus on profitability over volume is yielding results and improving return metrics.

1.2 Growth in Other Regulated Markets (ORM) – Key Engine of Growth

  • ORM revenue: $44m (₹3.842B) → +16% YoY.
  • Growth driven by B2B partnerships in Europe, especially with large pan-EU partners.
  • Strong customer advocacy and dependable supply enabling new customer acquisition and portfolio expansion.
  • Robust regulatory filing pipeline supports medium-term growth.

Strategy: ORM is positioned as a strategic growth pillar, leveraging reliable supply chains and strategic alliances.

1.3 Financial Discipline and Balance Sheet Strengthening

  • Net debt reduced by ₹733m in H1FY26 despite:
    • ₹1,492m capex (including intangibles),
    • ₹710m adverse currency impact.
  • Net debt down ₹469m in Q2 alone.
  • Operating cash flow: ₹3,915m in H1FY26 → strong internal accrual generation.
  • Net debt to EBITDA (TTM): 1.7x (down from 3.5x in FY24).
  • RoCE (TTM): Improved to 16.0% (from 9.7% in FY24).

Governance win: Balance sheet reset post-demerger is bearing fruit, with improved leverage, cash flow, and investor confidence.

1.4 Strategic Push Beyond Generics

  • First “Beyond Generics” product (Nasal Spray) filed with USFDA.
  • Entry into Controlled Substances and 505(b)(2) pathways:
    • Higher margins and lower competition.
    • Long-term differentiation from commoditized generics space.
  • Platform expansion into higher-value therapeutic niches.

Long-term story: Moves to reduce dependency on traditional generics and build sustainable IP-adjacent revenue.

1.5 US Generics Business Stability and Revival Plan

  • Q2FY26 US revenue: $73m → +2% YoY, despite intense competition in recent launches.
  • 70 commercialized products, with 37 in top 3 market share (75% of US revenue).
  • Strategy to relaunch 60 dormant ANDAs over next 3 years to target $400m generics revenue by FY27–28.
  • 230+ ANDAs filed, 215+ approved as of July 2025.

Resilience: Core US business is stable and rebuilding momentum despite challenges.

1.6 Expanding Footprint in Growth Markets (Africa, LATAM, MENA, APAC)

  • Growth Markets revenue: $17m (₹1,468m) in Q2FY26, up 7% YoY.
  • Growth driven by Africa operations and new product launches.
  • “In Africa for Africa” strategy enhancing localization and market penetration.
  • Pipeline of regulatory filings in new geographies, though timelines are long.

Sustainable tailwind: Addressing underserved markets with customized product portfolios.

2. Headwinds & Challenges

2.1 Subdued Access Markets Due to Donor Funding Challenges

  • Access Markets revenue: $7m (₹609m) → -25% YoY.
  • Driven by tightening donor funding (e.g., Global Fund, PEPFAR).
  • Historically significant for institutional supply, especially in public health programs.
  • Company acknowledges “challenging donor funding environment”.

Risk: Donor dependency makes this segment vulnerable to geopolitical and budgetary shifts.

2.2 Intense Competition in US Generics

  • US market flat (+2% YoY) despite new launches due to rapid entry of new competitors.
  • Pricing pressure persists in highly competitive generics space.
  • Revival of dormant ANDAs required to achieve $400m target but execution risk remains.

Threat: Without differentiation (via 505(b)(2) or new formulations), revenue growth may stall.

2.3 Currency Volatility

  • Adverse forex impact of ₹710m in H1FY26.
  • Company earning in USD/EUR but costs in INR → FX volatility erodes margins.
  • Net debt reduction achieved despite headwinds, but risk persists.

Macroeconomic risk: INR appreciation or dollar volatility can pressure margins and cash flows.

2.4 Lumpy Growth in Emerging Markets

  • Growth Markets to have “lumpy quarters” for next 2 years due to:
    • Long regulatory timelines.
    • Gradual market stabilization.
  • Unpredictable revenue patterns may affect investor sentiment.

Volatility: Early-stage expansion introduces uncertainty in quarterly performance.

2.5 High Leverage of Exceptional Items in Prior Periods

  • H1FY25 reported PAT was ₹34.2B, primarily due to ₹31.9B profit from discontinued operations (demerger of Softgel business to OneSource).
  • Future comparability distorted by base effects.
  • Current “operational PAT” strips this out for clarity, but investors must remain cautious.

6. Valuation Implications
  • Strong earnings momentum and improving RoCE suggest potential re-rating catalyst.
  • Operational de-risking post-demerger and debt reduction make STAR more attractive.
  • Shift from generics to 505(b)(2)/niches could improve valuation multiples over time.
  • Near-term P/E looks reasonable given profitability surge, but dependent on sustaining growth.
7. Conclusion: Buy Case vs. Caution

✅ Bull Case (Buy/Hold)

  • Profitability is accelerating with best-ever EPS.
  • Balance sheet has significantly improved with debt reduction and strong cash flow.
  • Strategic pivot into higher-margin, less competitive “Beyond Generics” adds long-term optionality.
  • ORM and Growth Markets provide diversified revenue base.
  • Management demonstrates strong execution and capital discipline.

⚠️ Bear Case (Caution)

  • Growth lags profitability: Revenue up only 4.6% YoY.
  • Access Markets in decline due to donor dependency.
  • ORM and new geographies face lumpy performance.
  • US generics growth is muted and faces stiff competition.
  • Success depends on execution of long-term strategies (ANDA relaunch, 505(b)(2)), which carry risk.

STRIDES (STAR) is transitioning from a transactional generics company to a profitable, diversified global pharma player. While revenue growth is modest, profitability, cash flow, and strategic diversification are strengthening.

Ideal for:
- Investors seeking improving balance sheets and rising returns. - Those willing to wait 2–3 years for “Beyond Generics” and pan-EU/pan-Africa strategies to mature.

Monitor:
- Progress on ANDA relaunches in US. - Commercialization of 505(b)(2)/nasal spray. - Donor funding stability. - Quarterly trends in Growth/Access Markets.

Final Note:
With strong governance, clear strategy, and execution on track, Strides is on a credible path to sustainable value creation. Risk-tolerant investors may find it an attractive turnaround story with above-average return potential.
