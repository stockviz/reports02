Analysis of Om Infra Limited (OMINFRAL): Headwinds, Tailwinds, Growth Prospects, and Key Risks

Om Infra Limited is an infrastructure player specializing in hydro-mechanical equipment (gates, cranes, penstocks), water supply (Jal Jeevan Mission - JJM), irrigation, pumped storage projects (PSPs), and minor real estate. It has 55+ years of experience, 70+ projects executed (~Rs 5,000 Cr), and a strong order book of Rs 2,541 Cr (Sep 2025; ~3.3x FY25 TTM revenue). H1FY26 consolidated financials show revenue of Rs 228 Cr (-44% YoY), EBITDA Rs 6 Cr (3% margin; -83% YoY), and PAT Rs 6 Cr (EPS Rs 0.55). FY26 guidance: Revenue Rs 600-700 Cr, EBITDA 6-8%, order inflows Rs 1,500 Cr.

Tailwinds

  • ** Govt. Policy Support**: JJM allocation Rs 67,000 Cr (extended to 2028; 79.8% complete, 16 Cr households pending). Hydro/PSP push (42 GW to 67 GW by 2032; 74 GW PSPs targeted). ERCP, Namami Gange, river linking (Rs 2,400 Cr budget). Indus Water Treaty suspension to boost border hydro projects.
  • Order Momentum: H1FY26 inflows Rs 328 Cr (Rs 129 Cr UP Jal Nigam; Rs 199 Cr Dibang 2,880 MW NHPC; Rs 48 Cr Tapovan NTPC). JJM ~56% of order book (Rs 1,410 Cr). Bid pipeline in water, hydro (NHPC/NEEPCO), smart cities.
  • Asset Monetization: Non-core real estate (Rs 600+ Cr realizable; MHADA issues resolved with Valor Estate). Arbitration awards (Rs 640 Cr expected; Rs 587 Cr Bhilwara toll road partially received).
  • Low Leverage: Net D/E 0.05x; in-house manufacturing/turnkey expertise reduces costs.
  • Milestones: Isarda Dam water impounding started (Rs 615 Cr project benefiting 3 Cr people).

Headwinds

  • Revenue & Margin Pressure: H1FY26 revenue down 44% YoY (Rs 228 Cr vs Rs 408 Cr); EBITDA margins compressed to 3% (vs 8%). Q2FY26 revenue Rs 124 Cr (-48% YoY). Delayed JJM payments (>9 months) led to early expense booking despite progress.
  • Execution Delays: State fund constraints; funds releasing progressively post-H1FY26.
  • Segment Weakness: Engineering dominant (90%+ revenue); real estate subdued (Rs 306 Cr remaining value).
  • Cash Flow Strain: Negative operating cash flow (standalone Rs -42 Cr H1FY26); inventories/debtors up.

Growth Prospects

  • FY26 Outlook: Revenue Rs 600-700 Cr (2-3x H1); order inflows Rs 1,500 Cr (focus: hydro/PSPs, JJM Rs 1,000 Cr bids, ERCP Rs 2,000 Cr). Order book execution: 40% FY27, 60% beyond.
  • Sector Tailwinds: Hydro (Rs 28,352 Cr budget; 12,340 MW by 2026); PSPs (Kundah 1 GW execution); water infra US$270 Bn investment over 15 years.
  • Diversification: Expand in AMRUT 2.0, NIP roads, Sagarmala. Strong clients (NHPC, NTPC, World Bank, govt. depts.).
  • Balance Sheet Boost: Rs 700+ Cr from assets/arbitration over 2-3 years for CAPEX/WC.
  • Long-Term: Revenue CAGR 31% FY20-25; book-to-bill 3.3x supports 20-30% bid-hit ratio.
