Analysis of Sangam (India) Limited (SANGAMIND)

Sangam (India) Limited, a fully integrated textile player (yarn, fabrics, denim, garments), reported robust Q2/H1 FY26 results amid a completed capex cycle and improving operational efficiencies. Revenue grew 16%/15.4% YoY to INR 785 Cr / 1,587 Cr, driven by volumes and better product mix. EBITDA rose 32%/13% YoY to INR 76 Cr / 146 Cr (margins +120 bps / -22 bps YoY to 9.6%/9.2%). PAT surged 317%/27% YoY to INR 23 Cr / 25 Cr, aided by a one-time ~INR 19 Cr PAT boost from revised asset useful lives (depreciation down ~INR 25 Cr, no cash impact). Capacity utilizations are rising (yarn 91%, garments 35%→60-65% expected). Exports stable at ~37% (low US exposure <1%). Guidance: 12-15% revenue growth FY26 (to ~INR 3,200-3,300 Cr), EBITDA margins to 11-12%, debt reduction ~INR 350 Cr over 3 years.

Tailwinds (Supportive Factors)

  • Operational Leverage & Efficiency: Post-capex, utilizations improving (yarn +14% QoQ, fabrics +10-15% scope). Better product mix (higher-margin fabrics/denim/garments ~45% revenue), cost controls, and RM realizations stabilizing (yarn prices bottomed after 8-10Q decline).
  • Cost Savings Initiatives: 12 MW captive renewables (Dec’25 start, INR 10 Cr annual savings); PET recycling (45 TPD, INR 15 Cr EBIT savings); power costs moderating.
  • Demand Resilience: Strong domestic demand; exports pivot to Bangladesh/EU (no US tariff hit); athleisure/sustainable fashion tailwinds (C9 brand, GOTS/OEKO-TEX certs).
  • Policy Support: Textile PLI scheme, FTAs (e.g., India-UK), import duty extensions.
  • Financial Discipline: WC cycle improved to 61 days (H1FY26); interest subsidies; debt cost <7%.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Margin Volatility: Textile margins historically volatile; gross margins dipped to 40% (vs. 43.5% YoY) due to RM costs/power; QnQ revenue flat (-2%).
  • Raw Material Pressure: Cotton/yarn prices ticking up (+2-3% QoQ); 30-35% import reliance (dynamic).
  • Debt Burden: Net D/E ~1.1x; annual repayments INR 100-120 Cr; ROE low (< cost of debt) but improving.
  • Garment Ramp-Up: Utilization low at 35% (prior decline); trading income masked true growth in Q1.
  • External: US tariffs (50% on apparel, indirect via clients); competition (peers expanding, but MD sees no pricing pressure).

Growth Prospects

  • Near-Term (FY26): 12-15% revenue growth to INR 3,200-3,300 Cr at 90% utilization; EBITDA +1-2% expansion (to 11-12%); PAT sustainable post one-off.
  • Medium-Term (FY27-30): INR 4,000 Cr top-line by FY28-29 (14% CAGR); ROCE 12-14% in 12-18 months; debt halving possible.
  • Strategic Drivers: Value-add shift (fabrics/garments up); exports 35-40%; JVs (Goldenseams denim garments); brands (C9, DaMensch); sustainability (70-75% renewables by FY27).
  • Industry Tailwinds: India textiles to USD 350 Bn by FY30; exports USD 100 Bn; athleisure 15-20% CAGR.
