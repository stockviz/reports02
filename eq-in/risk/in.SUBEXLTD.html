Subex Limited (SUBEXLTD) – Comprehensive Analysis

Stock: BSE 532348 | NSE: SUBEXLTD
Reporting Period: Q2 FY26 (September 30, 2025)

Based on the provided set of financial statements, board meeting outcomes, press releases, investor presentations, and regulatory filings, this analysis evaluates the headwinds, tailwinds, growth prospects, and key risks for Subex Limited, offering a holistic view of the company’s current business and financial trajectory.

🔷 Executive Summary

Subex has undergone a strong operational turnaround in Q2 FY26, with normalized EBITDA improving by 70% QoQ (₹730L → ₹428L) and achieving sustained profitability without reliance on one-off gains. This reflects improved project execution, margin optimization, and strategic focus on its core AI-powered telecom software offerings.

Despite revenue stagnation YoY (₹6,891L vs ₹7,416L), the company is winning high-value renewals and greenfield contracts globally (>$15M in recent wins) and is capitalizing on the growing AI-driven digital transformation trend in the telecom sector.

Subex is a lean, focused pure-play on telecom AI solutions with a 95% customer retention rate, ~70% recurring revenue, and increasing ESG-aligned maturity in governance (e.g., successful liquidation of non-core entity).

🌬️ Tailwinds: Growth Enablers
  1. Strong Tailwinds in Telecom AI Adoption
    • AI and GenAI are central to telecom vendors’ RFPs, especially in fraud management and assurance.
    • Subex’s HyperSense and FraudZap platforms are directly aligned with this rising demand.
    • Management notes that Fraud Management is one of the top GenAI use cases in telecom.
  2. Robust Recurring Revenue Model
    • ~70% of revenue is annuity or recurring, providing predictability and resilience to macro fluctuations.
    • Customer retention: ~95%, indicating sticky client relationships and high switching costs.
  3. Global Installed Base & Data Advantage
    • Operates in 100+ countries, with 300+ installations, processing >500 billion records/day.
    • This data access and domain expertise create a unique moat for developing accurate AI/ML models.
  4. Recent Major Contract Wins
    • Won and renewed multi-year, multi-million-dollar contracts with Tier-1 operators in APAC, Europe, and Middle East.
    • Notable upgrade: $1.2M deal with a major Middle East operator to transition their fraud system to HyperSense FM.
    • Total > $15M in recent order book, signaling strong sales momentum.
  5. Operational Turnaround & Margin Improvement
    • Normalized PAT reached ₹388L in Q2 FY26 (from ₹1,281L of non-recurring items in Q1) – marks sustained profitability.
    • Normalised EBITDA up 70% QoQ (₹730L) due to improved contribution margin.
    • Cash balance stable at ~₹10.5B across standalone and consolidated.
  6. Strategic Focus on Efficiency & Talent
    • Onboarded a new Sales Head based in Europe, boosting geographic go-to-market.
    • Investing in AI-native talent development, future-proofing innovation.
🌀 Headwinds: Near-Term Challenges
  1. Revenue Decline YoY – Market Pressures
    • Q2 FY26 revenue: ₹6,891L, down from ₹7,416L in Q2 FY25 (YoY drop of ~7%).
    • While QoQ growth of 3.8% is positive, the year-on-year decline indicates pricing pressure or project delays in some markets.
  2. Earnings Volatility in Recent Quarters
    • PAT swung from ₹1,281L (Q1) to ₹286L (Q2) due to absence of one-time gains (lease termination, tax refunds).
    • While normalized profits are improving, investors may perceive volatility due to reliance on exceptional items in past quarters.
  3. High Days Sales Outstanding (DSO)
    • DSO at ~100 days, up from ~96 in Q1 FY26.
    • Though down 13 days YoY, working capital pressure remains – delay in collections may impact cash flow.
  4. Negative Net Income in Standalone Books
    • Standalone PAT: ₹89L loss (Q2) due to loss in Subex Assurance LLP and impairment charges.
    • Consolidated profitability is carried by subsidiaries, creating a disconnect between standalone and group performance.
  5. Geographic Concentration Risk
    • Despite a global footprint, revenue growth appears biased toward emerging markets where competition is high and contract monetization may be slower.

✅ Key Growth Drivers:

  • AI-Native Product Strategy: Subex is transitioning to a cloud-first, AI-model-driven platform that allows faster deployment and customization.
  • Landed & Expand Strategy: High retention enables cross-selling (e.g., FM + Partner Settlement).
  • Global Renewals Pipeline: Large base of long-term clients = predictable renewal revenue.
  • Talent & Sales Push: Hiring aggressive sales leadership in strategic geography (Europe).
⚠️ Key Risks
  1. Execution & Integration Risk in Renewals
    • Greenfield upgrades and consulting-led mandates require precise execution. Any delays impact margin and reputation.
  2. Dependence on a Few Large Customers
    • While serving 150+ clients, revenue concentration risk may exist with Tier-1 operators (not disclosed, but a typical risk in this sector).
  3. Subsidiary Performance Volatility
    • Subex Assurance LLP reported losses (₹384L in impairment losses), dragging standalone results.
    • Despite investment in Subex Middle East (FZE), early-stage entities may face prolonged gestation.
  4. Foreign Exchange (Forex) Exposure
    • Subsidiaries in UAE, UK, US, Asia, Middle East expose Subex to currency risk, although not currently significant based on financials.
  5. Governance & Succession Risks
    • Recent board changes: Three directors exited (Sep 29, 2025) – though likely planned, highlights governance transition risk.
      • Non-executive and independent director roles need to be filled promptly.
  6. Technology & Competitive Pressure
    • Risks from large competitors (e.g., Amdocs, Ericsson, Huawei) and niche fraud vendors (e.g., Nokia Subex, Tata Elxsi).
    • Subex must continue high R&D spend to stay ahead in AI/GenAI applications.
  7. Regulatory & Tax Scrutiny (Global)
    • With a global footprint, Subex is exposed to complex international tax frameworks (provision for foreign taxes: ₹301L in H1 FY26).

🏁 Conclusion: Investment Thesis

🟢 Why Invest in Subex Now?

  • Operational turnaround confirmed with normalized profits, margin expansion, and solid cash flow.
  • Leading AI adoption in telecom assurance/fraud – positioned to capture high-growth digital spend.
  • Recurring revenue + high retention = predictable earnings and lower churn risk.
  • Global footprint + Tier-1 clients = scalable global product platform.
  • Lean cost base and improving governance post-SLPS liquidation.

🔴 Risks to Monitor

  • Revenue growth remains flat YoY; need for new logo acquisition in developed markets.
  • Volatility in standalone results due to LLP subsidiaries.
  • Execution on large upgrade projects and consulting mandates.

🔮 Outlook

Subex is transitioning from a restructuring phase to a growth-phase software vendor, leveraging AI to build long-term defensibility. While not showing explosive revenue growth, its profitability foundation is being rebuilt sustainably.

With strong tailwinds in telecom AI, data analytics, and fraud prevention, and a solid order book, Subex appears well-positioned to grow earnings at a double-digit CAGR over the next 3–5 years, assuming execution discipline.

📌 Final Verdict:

Subex Limited is a ‘Buy’ for long-term investors seeking exposure to AI-driven digitization in global telecom, with improving fundamentals, strong tailwinds, and a resilient business model, despite near-term revenue headwinds and execution risks.
