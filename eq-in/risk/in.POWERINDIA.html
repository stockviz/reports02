Summary Analysis for POWERINDIA (Hitachi Energy India Ltd.)

Hitachi Energy India Ltd. (POWERINDIA) reported robust Q2 FY26 results (ended Sep 30, 2025): Orders +13.6% YoY to ₹2,217 Cr, Revenue +23.3% YoY to ₹1,915 Cr, PAT +406% YoY to ₹264 Cr (13.8% margin), Op. EBITDA 15.2%. H1 FY26 PAT +531% YoY. Record order backlog of ₹29,413 Cr provides multi-quarter visibility. Company benefits from India’s energy transition (500+ GW capacity), but faces execution and macro challenges.

Tailwinds

  • Energy Transition Boom: India’s RE investments (₹1L Cr in H1 2025), 500 GW transmission plan (₹2.4L Cr), HVDC pipeline (2-3 projects/year; tariff-based bids ongoing). Renewables orders +40% YoY, rail/metro +61%.
  • Order Backlog & Visibility: ₹29,413 Cr backlog (base ex-HVDC ~₹10k Cr); products/utilities dominant mix.
  • Exports & Services: Exports +59% YoY (25-30% of orders; Europe, ME, NA); services high single-digit contribution, +35% YoY growth (e.g., EconiQ SF6-free tech).
  • Margin Expansion: 550 bps YoY gross margin rise (better mix, execution, exports); double-digit Op. EBITDA sustained ahead of FY26 target.
  • CAPEX & Capacity: ₹750 Cr FY26 plan (on track post-slow H1); expansions in transformers, HVDC (Chennai/Bangalore/Mysore); QIP funds (₹2,521 Cr raised) deployed.
  • ESG Momentum: 76% CO2 cut (vs. 50% target), 100% RE electricity; awards for safety/sustainability.

Headwinds

  • Segment Volatility: Transmission -43% YoY, data centers -50% (timing/base effects; e.g., prior Marinus HVDC order).
  • Slow CAPEX Start: Only ₹67 Cr in H1 FY26 vs. ₹750 Cr plan; interest income (₹120 Cr H1) to decline as deployed.
  • Macro Pressures: US tariffs (50% on exports), trade deficit, geopolitics; pricing affordability limits despite localization (75-80% local content).
  • Royalty/Expenses: Other expenses down 7% YoY (royalty lag), but ~20% normalized.

Growth Prospects

  • High Visibility: Backlog execution + new orders (HVDC, RE, data centers, industries) to drive 20-25%+ revenue growth; asset turns to support scaling.
  • Emerging Segments: Data centers (15-20% of CAPEX; Google ₹90k Cr hub), BESS, rail (Vande Bharat), green industries/steel/alumina.
  • Strategic Levers: Services/export/digital ramp; one Hitachi synergies; Mysuru pressboard expansion (₹300 Cr, fossil-free by 2027).
  • Pipeline: 6 GW LCC HVDC NIT soon; 2-3 projects/year for 3-5 years; global transformer demand gap.
  • Long-Term: Double-digit EBITDA sustained/improved; FY27+ CAPEX traction; India GDP +6.5% (World Bank).

Overall Outlook: Strong tailwinds from India’s grid/RE super-cycle outweigh headwinds. Multi-year growth (20%+ revenue CAGR feasible) with margin resilience; monitor HVDC awards and CAPEX execution. Stock poised for re-rating on backlog conversion.
