Analysis of Data Patterns (India) Limited (DATAPATTNS)

Data Patterns (India) Ltd., a defense electronics player specializing in radars, EW, avionics, and subsystems/systems integration, reported stellar H1 FY26 results: Revenue +109% YoY to INR 407 Cr, EBITDA +41% to INR 101 Cr (25% margin), PAT +18% to INR 75 Cr. Q2 saw explosive growth (Revenue +238% YoY to INR 308 Cr) driven by a INR 180 Cr strategic low-margin project, diluting margins (EBITDA 22%). Order book stands at ~INR 1,290 Cr (INR 737 Cr on-hand + INR 552 Cr negotiated), up from INR 730 Cr in Apr 2025. Debt-free balance sheet (Equity INR 1,538 Cr), but high receivables (INR 668 Cr) inflate working capital days to 343. Management confident on FY26 revenue (~INR 1,400 Cr implied) and margin recovery in H2.

Tailwinds

  • Robust Demand & Indigenization: India’s defense push (Atmanirbhar Bharat) favors private players like Data Patterns. H1 inflows (INR 351 Cr) from BrahMos, ECIL, EW; pipeline includes AMCA consortium (with Bharat Forge/BEML), Super Sukhoi jammer pod, MiG-29/Su-30 radars, BrahMos seeker. Proactive product dev (using INR 122 Cr QIP funds) positions it ahead of RFIs/RFPs.
  • Order Visibility: Strong book (1.3x FY25 revenue) + INR 552 Cr negotiated (expected in 2-3 months). H2 inflows eyed at INR 900 Cr+ (production-heavy).
  • Export Momentum: TPAR radar acceptance in Europe (INR 80 Cr book); traction in South America, UK; co-dev with global OEMs. Shift to full systems (vs. subsystems) unlocks larger global opps.
  • Execution Capability: Scaled to complex electromechanical systems (140-ton payload in strategic project). Debt-free, cash generative (post-advances CCC ~310 days).
  • Strategic Shift: From subsystems to systems integrator (radars/EW full suites), differentiating vs. DPSUs/imports (India imports 70-80%).

Headwinds

  • Margin Pressure: Q2 EBITDA margin dipped to 22% (vs. 35-40% guided) due to competitive pricing on strategic project. Mix skewed toward dev/low-margin work; recovery needs balanced production orders.
  • Working Capital Strain: Receivables ballooned to INR 668 Cr (H1 cash from ops: -INR 50 Cr). Long dev cycles (testing/integration) delay collections; advances help but CCC remains high (343 days reported).
  • Lumpy Revenue: Defense orders volatile—H1 growth front-loaded; H2 execution key. Negotiated orders delayed (MOD tenders).
  • Capex/People Intensity: Rising staff costs (INR 74 Cr H1), capex (INR 37 Cr H1), intangibles under dev (INR 51 Cr) for scaling.

Growth Prospects

  • Near-Term (FY26): H2 revenue ~INR 1,000 Cr (management guidance), full-year top-line doubling+ YoY. Production ramps (BrahMos, EW), margin normalization to 35%+ EBITDA. Total inflows FY26: INR 1,500 Cr+.
  • Medium-Term (2-3 Yrs): TAM INR 15,000-20,000 Cr across radars/EW/avionics/drone jammers/comms. Big platforms (AMCA prototypes in 6-12 months, seekers, glass cockpits). Exports to scale faster (private clients, quicker quals: 1-2 yrs vs. India’s 3-5 yrs).
  • Long-Term: Global co-dev, EMI-EMC facility, land acquisition (QIP utilization near-complete). Ambition: IP-driven model, faster growth (>30% CAGR) via systems/export. Order book to propel to “higher heights.”

Key Risks

  • Order Delays/Competition: Govt tenders unpredictable (e.g., AMCA RFP pending; L&T/BEL consortium rival). Foreign tie-ups erode indigenization edge.
  • Execution/Tech Risks: Complex systems (power/mech/electronics integration) unproven at scale; dev failures (e.g., trials) could hit credibility.
  • Financial: Receivables concentration (DRDO ~INR 200 Cr Q2), forex/supply chain volatility (imports). High CCC strains cash amid dividend/capex.
  • Macro/Geopolitical: Defense budget cuts, election cycles, global tensions affecting exports. No subsidiaries/JVs limits diversification.
  • Regulatory: SEBI disclosure limits on strategic contracts; QIP unutilized INR 50 Cr must deploy efficiently.

Summary: Data Patterns is a high-conviction play on India’s defense indigenization, with tailwinds from order pipeline, exports, and tech moat outweighing near-term margin/WC headwinds. Growth prospects strong (FY26 revenue 2x FY25, 30%+ CAGR medium-term), but lumpy execution and delays pose risks. Maintain overweight; target FY26 EBITDA margins 30%+ for sustained re-rating. Monitor H2 inflows and collections.
