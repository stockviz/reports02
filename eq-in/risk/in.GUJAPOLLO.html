Analysis of GUJAPOLLO (Gujarat Apollo Industries Limited)

Company Overview: GUJAPOLLO is engaged in manufacturing and sale of construction and mining machineries/spare parts (single reportable segment). It reports strong investment income from equity instruments, inter-corporate deposits (ICDs), and subsidiaries/associates. Q2/H1 FY26 (ended Sep 30, 2025) results show revenue growth but PAT moderation YoY. Balance sheet remains robust with minimal debt/defaults (zero reported). AGM (Sep 30, 2025) saw unanimous approval of all resolutions (financials adoption, dividend, director appointments, RPTs, etc.).

Tailwinds (Positive Factors)

  • Revenue Momentum: Consolidated revenue +30% YoY (H1 FY26: ₹2,397L vs ₹1,836L), standalone +56% (₹1,470L). Driven by core ops (construction/mining equipment demand).
  • Robust Balance Sheet: Standalone total assets ₹26,802L (up 2%), equity ₹24,125L (high reserves ₹22,945L). Consolidated assets ₹56,124L, equity ₹49,154L. High liquidity via investments (₹5,532L standalone), ICDs/loans (₹14k-16kL), low gearing (borrowings <5% of assets).
  • Investment Income Buffer: Other income significant (standalone H1: ₹776L, ~53% of total income), from dividends (₹234L), ICD interest (₹521L). Consolidated similar.
  • Dividend Policy: Declared ₹2/share (20% payout) for FY25, signaling confidence. Paid-up capital stable at ₹1,180L (11.8 Cr shares @ ₹10 FV).
  • Policy Support: Infra/mining boom in India (roads, highways, mining auctions) aligns with core biz. No debt defaults; RPT limits approved for scale.
  • Governance: Clean auditor reviews (MAAK & Associates); 100% resolution passage at AGM (e-voting turnout ~40% of equity).

Headwinds (Negative Factors)

  • PAT Pressure: Standalone H1 PAT -20% YoY (₹297L vs ₹369L), consolidated -63% (₹318L vs ₹871L; prior H1 likely exceptional). Q1 standalone PBT loss (-₹24L). Margins squeezed by higher expenses (materials +19%, employee +15%, depr +22%).
  • Cash Flow Weakness: Standalone operating CF negative (₹-96L) due to inventory build-up (₹-180L) and WC changes. Consolidated similar (₹-496L). Relies on investing CF (₹517L standalone from interest/dividends).
  • Other Income Reliance: ~40-50% of total income non-core (investments/ICDs), masking ops volatility. Core ops contribute modestly.
  • Inventory/Receivables Build: Standalone inventories +11% QoQ, receivables +20%. Consolidated trade receivables +11% YoY.
  • Modest Scale: Revenue still small (~₹4,155L FY25 consolidated); competes in cyclical infra space.

Growth Prospects

  • High (Medium-Term): Infra capex tailwind (Govt. push: ₹11L Cr budget allocation FY26). Revenue trajectory suggests 20-30% CAGR possible if mining/construction sustains. Subsidiaries (AEML Investments, Apollo FBC) and associate (PFH Agri) add diversification (contributed to consolidated PAT).
  • Investments/Lending: ₹30kL+ in loans/ICDs + equity portfolio (FVTOCI gains ₹263L H1 consolidated) can yield 10-15% returns, supporting 15-20% EPS growth (H1 EPS ₹2.96 consolidated).
  • Expansion Signals: Capex ongoing (CWIP ₹70L standalone). RPT approvals with Apollo Industries enable synergies.
  • Projections: FY26 consolidated revenue >₹8,000L possible (extrapolating H1 + full-year trends), PAT ₹600-800L if margins stabilize at 10-12%. EPS ₹5+ feasible.

Growth prospects strong (20%+ revenue CAGR) driven by ops recovery + yields. Key watch: monitor op cash flows and credit quality. Attractive for long-term infra play; valuation likely reasonable given 20% dividend yield proxy and EPS trajectory. Risks tilted toward cyclicality/credit—suitable for patient investors.
