eClerx Services Limited (ECLERX) Analysis

eClerx Services Limited (ECLERX), a listed Indian IT/BPM company focused on data management, analytics, process outsourcing, and BPaaS, reported robust Q2/H1 FY26 results (ended Sep 30, 2025). Key highlights include 19.3% YoY USD revenue growth (OPG: $115.5 mn Q2), EBITDA margin expansion to 28.8% (+399 bps QoQ), PAT up 29% YoY to INR 1,832 mn, strong cash flows (H1 OCF: INR 3,360 mn), and a proposed INR 3,000 mn buyback at INR 4,500/share (premium ~1-11% to recent market prices). The company operates in a single reportable segment with geographic focus on North America (80%) and clients like Fortune 2000 firms. Below is a structured analysis of headwinds, tailwinds, growth prospects, and key risks, based on the provided filings (financial results, earnings presentation, press release, and buyback disclosures).

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)
  • Strong Revenue Momentum: Q2 OPG revenue +16.3% YoY CC (constant currency), H1 +17% USD YoY. 4-year CAGR (FY21-25): 16.6%. Diversified industries (BFSI ~42%, CMT ~26%, HiTech/M&D ~17%) and client mix (Top 10: 63%, emerging clients growing).
  • Margin Expansion & Efficiency: EBITDA margin 28.8% (Q2), up 399 bps QoQ due to utilization (75.1%), pricing power, and scale. Employee costs controlled at ~52.5% of revenue despite headcount +18% YoY (total ~21,400).
  • Robust Balance Sheet & Cash Generation: Net cash position (cash equiv. INR 5,940 mn as of Sep 2025), H1 OCF INR 3,360 mn (EBITDA conversion ~112%). Low capex (INR 525 mn H1), DSO stable at 76 days. Buyback funded from free reserves (permissible limit: INR 3,557 mn standalone).
  • Strategic Initiatives: Growing ACV from new deals (Q2: ~$31 mn ex-CLX), A&A (analytics/AI) ramp-up, acquisitions (e.g., Peru/Egypt subs adding scale). Hedge book protects forex (100% forwards, avg. INR 88/USD till FY28).
  • Shareholder Returns: Proposed buyback (1.4% of FY25 equity base) signals confidence; prior buyback completed. EPS growth: Q2 basic INR 39 (vs. FY25 avg. ~INR 114 annualized).
Headwinds (Challenges)
  • High Attrition & Wage Pressures: Offshore voluntary attrition 20.3% (Q2), though down QoQ. Employee benefits ~60% of costs; H1 expense up 18% YoY.
  • Forex & Macro Sensitivity: 86% USD revenue; Q2 revaluation gain INR 186 mn aided other income, but INR strength (exit: 88.84) could pressure growth. NA/Europe (94% revenue) exposed to slowdowns.
  • Subcontractor & G&A Costs: Sub-contractor costs up (H1: INR 388 mn), G&A/S&D at ~20% of revenue. Depreciation rising (Ind AS 116 leases).
  • Execution in New Geographies: Recent subs (e.g., Peru w.e.f. Oct 2024, Egypt Feb 2025) and merger (ASEC into Personiv Oct 2025) may strain integration amid global expansion.
  • Billing Mix Shift: BPaaS/onshore declining slightly (18%/19% Q2), potentially lower-margin.
Growth Prospects
  • High Visibility: Strong deal pipeline (ACV trends up), single-segment focus on high-growth areas (data analytics, AI-driven BPM). FY26 guidance implicit: sustained 15-20% USD growth via BFSI/CMT ramps and emerging verticals (7.7% Q2 mix).
  • Margin Upside: Potential 27-30% EBITDA via utilization (target 75-80%), offshore leverage (India: 83% headcount), and tech investments. RoE improvement post-buyback.
  • M&A & Expansion: 20+ subs across 10+ countries; recent additions enhance NA/Europe footprint. Emerging markets (7.7% revenue) scaling.
  • Shareholder Value: Buyback + cash hoard supports dividends/ESOPs. FY26 EPS trajectory: ~INR 140-150 (annualized H1 ~INR 138 basic).
  • Outlook: 15-20% revenue CAGR FY26-28 feasible on CC basis, assuming stable macros. 4-year historical CAGR supports this.

Summary

Bull Case (Base Outlook): ECLERX is in a strong growth phase with double-digit revenue/margin expansion, fueled by tailwinds like deal wins, efficiency, and cash flexibility. Buyback enhances RoE (~20-25% post-buyback) and signals undervaluation (buyback price ~20-25x FY26 EPS). FY26 could deliver 18-22% USD growth, 27%+ margins, supported by H1 momentum.

Bear Case: Macro headwinds (US slowdown) + FX could cap growth at 10-12% YoY; attrition erodes margins to 25%.

Overall Rating: Positive (Buy/Hold). Robust fundamentals outweigh risks; trading at attractive multiples vs. peers (implied FY26 P/E ~20-22x post-buyback). Monitor Q3 results (Jan 2026) and buyback outcome (Dec 2025) for catalysts. Market cap ~INR 25,000-30,000 cr implied; strong free cash flow profile positions for sustained compounding.
