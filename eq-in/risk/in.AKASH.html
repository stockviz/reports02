Analysis of Akash Infra-Projects Limited (AKASH)

Akash Infra-Projects Limited (CIN: L45209GJ1999PLC036003), a NSE-listed infra company focused on road construction, operates in construction (core) and fuel/grocery segments. Below is a structured analysis based on the provided documents (work order, Q2/H1 FY26 financials, and AGM results) as of Sept/Oct/Nov 2025. All figures in ₹ Lakhs unless stated.

Tailwinds (Positive Momentum)

  • Order Book Expansion: Secured a ₹37.99 Cr (₹379.94 Mn) work order (30 Sep 2025) from Gandhinagar District Panchayat for road construction/widening/resurfacing (annual/emergency basis). Awarded at +29.2% premium over ₹29.4 Cr estimate; 12-month tenure (Aug 2025–Aug 2026). Boosts visibility in core Gujarat roads segment.
  • Revenue Growth: Consolidated H1 FY26 revenue at ₹3,327 (↑37% YoY from ₹2,431); Q2 at ₹1,206. Construction segment H1 revenue ₹1,747 (↑131% YoY). Fuel/Grocery stable at ₹1,619 H1.
  • Cash Flow Strength: Consolidated operating cash flow +₹179 H1 (standalone +₹81); supported by receivables reduction (↓₹206 standalone) and inventory adjustments.
  • Governance: 26th AGM (30 Sep 2025) saw 100% approval on all 10 resolutions (e-voting turnout ~73% of shares); re-appointments of key directors/MDs strengthen leadership continuity.
  • Diversification: Two segments (Construction ~52% H1 revenue; Fuel/Grocery ~48%); subsidiaries (e.g., Akash Infra Inc., International LLC) and associates add global exposure.

Headwinds (Challenges/Pressures)

  • Profitability Erosion: Consolidated H1 pre-tax loss ₹64 (standalone ₹83); Q2 losses ₹90/₹100. High costs: Materials ₹2,635 H1 (79% of revenue), finance ₹208 (6% of revenue), other expenses ₹410. Construction segment H1 profit before interest/depr. ₹104 but dragged by interest.
  • Revenue Volatility: Q2 consolidated revenue halved QoQ (₹1,206 vs ₹2,144 Q1); standalone Q2 ₹467 (↓63% QoQ). Inventory buildup (₹3,594 consolidated, ↑6% YoY).
  • Liquidity Strain: Trade receivables elevated at ₹14,732 (71% of assets); current borrowings ₹8,399 (41% of liabilities). Other equity dipped to ₹6,686 (↓1% YoY).
  • Segment Weakness: Construction results volatile (Q2 loss before tax ₹90); Fuel/Grocery margins thin (H1 profit before interest/tax ₹67).

Growth Prospects

  • High (Medium-Term): Infra tailwinds from govt schemes (e.g., Bharatmala, Gujarat roads); new order signals repeat business (annual basis). Order execution could drive FY26 revenue >₹7,000–8,000 (H1 at 40–50% pace). Fuel/Grocery steady (H1 ₹1,619, potential 10–15% growth via volumes).
  • Order Pipeline: Core competency in roads; +29% bid premium indicates competitive edge. Subsidiaries/associates (e.g., Petroleum, Residency) could contribute via forex/niche ops.
  • EPS Recovery: H1 consolidated EPS -₹0.54 (vs +₹0.50 FY25); FY26 turnaround possible if receivables convert and costs controlled (target EPS +₹0.50–1.00).
  • Market Cap Potential: Current assets ₹20,662 support 2–3x revenue multiple if profitability improves; NSE symbol AKASH could benefit from infra rally.
