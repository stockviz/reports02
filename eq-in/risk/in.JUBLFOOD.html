Analysis of Jubilant FoodWorks Limited (JUBLFOOD) – Q2FY26 Overview

Context: Jubilant FoodWorks (JFL) reported robust Q2FY26 results (ended Sep 30, 2025) with consolidated revenue up 19.7% YoY to ₹23.4B, driven by 9.1% LFL growth in India Domino’s, network expansion (93 stores added QoQ, total ~3,480), and strong Turkey performance. Pre-IndAS EBITDA margin improved 37bps YoY to 13.9%. PAT from continuing ops up 53.7% YoY to 4.7% margin. Management guided for sustained 5-7% LFL + 7-10% store growth in India Domino’s, with 200bps EBITDA expansion over 3 years.

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Robust Demand & Execution: India Domino’s LFL +9.1% (delivery +16.5%), orders +14.8% YoY; mature ADS ₹83K. Delivery mix ~73%, up 21.6% YoY due to free delivery & speed focus.
  • Network Scale: ~1 store/day addition; India at 2,450 stores (500 cities), Popeyes at 68 (entered West India). Total Domino’s: 3,179 stores.
  • Margin Leverage: Pre-IndAS EBITDA +37bps YoY via productivity, supply chain automation, G&A control. Gross margins expanding; GST input benefits (~50bps) partially passed on.
  • Innovation & Tech: Sourdough pizzas, Flavor Burst (Popeyes), app MAU +28% (15.6Mn), loyalty 40Mn members. New ad monetization (e.g., Apple, Flipkart) targeting 50bps revenue.
  • Intl Strength: Turkey revenue +28.7% (LFL +5.6% inflation-adj.), PAT margin 10.4%; SL/BD +86%/54% revenue growth.
  • Macro Support: Festive demand strong (Oct sales beat plans); govt measures (GST cuts, rate cuts) boosting QSR consumption.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Channel Shift: Dine-in flat YoY, takeaway -19% due to delivery preference; lower dine-in base reset could pressure LFL.
  • Input Costs: Cheese/milk inflation post-Maharashtra elections; RM costs up 23.6% YoY.
  • High Base: Prior double-digit LFL quarters; Q3 has Diwali/Chhath in base.
  • Promo Intensity: Free delivery, value offers (e.g., ₹99 dine-in) aided volume but capped ticket sizes (delivery bill size -2% QoQ).
  • Emerging Brands Drag: Popeyes/Dunkin/Hong’s improving but still margin dilutive (~200bps drag in FY24).

Growth Prospects

  • Core India Domino’s: Target 12-17% revenue growth (5-7% LFL incl. 1-2% pricing/mix + 7-10% stores); aim 900 stores/3yrs. Pizza market share ~65-70%.
  • Multi-Brand Expansion: Popeyes to 100 stores (double-digit SSG, ADS nearing industry avg); COFFY (172 cafes) ramping.
  • Intl Acceleration: Turkey pipeline strong; SL/BD high growth.
  • Margin Uplift: 100bps from gross margins (mix/procurement), leverage from 5-7% LFL; 200bps EBITDA expansion by FY28 vs. FY24.
  • New Levers: Ad revenue, combos on app, premium innovations (e.g., Big Big Pizza at ₹799 post-GST).
  • H2 Momentum: Oct sales ahead; promising outlook per mgmt.

Key Risks

  • Competition: Unorganized/premium players (e.g., La Pino’z) gaining; need to defend 65-70% pizza share.
  • Macro/Consumption: IT sector weakness (Bangalore/Hyderabad); festive base in Q3/Q4.
  • Cost/Inflation: Commodity volatility (cheese); Turkey hyperinflation (IAS29 adjustments).
  • Execution: Store ops shift to Q1/Q2 openings; scaling Popeyes to industry ADS/gross margins.
  • Debt & Capex: ₹16B borrowings; high store Capex (~₹4.8B H1FY26).
  • Regulatory: GST tweaks, ESOP dilution (10.23M options granted Dec 2025).

Overall Outlook: Strong momentum with balanced growth (topline + margins). Tailwinds from scale/tech outweigh headwinds; 15-20% revenue CAGR feasible if LFL sustains 5-7%. Risks tilted macro/competitive but mitigated by leadership (India #1 QSR pizza). Positive for H2FY26.
