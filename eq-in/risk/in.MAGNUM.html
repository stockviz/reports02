Summary Analysis for Magnum Ventures Limited (MAGNUM)

Company Overview: Magnum Ventures Limited (paper manufacturing and hotel operations) reported unaudited H1 FY26 results (Apr-Sep 2025) with revenue growth but net loss of ₹17 Cr. Key actions include NCD issuance/redemption, preferential equity allotment approval (EGM Oct 2025), and in-principle ₹150 Cr term loan for debt refinancing. Total assets ₹1,199 Cr; equity ₹678 Cr; debt ~₹231 Cr (excluding leases). Segments: Paper (80% revenue), Hotel (20%).

Tailwinds (Positive Factors)

  • Strong Revenue Momentum: H1 revenue +35% YoY to ₹234 Cr (Q2 +42% YoY); Paper segment +42% YoY, Hotel +9%. Indicates demand recovery post-industry cycles.
  • Debt Restructuring Progress: Issued ₹50 Cr 18% NCDs (Aug 2025, partially redeemed ₹4.84 Cr); in-principle ₹150 Cr term loan @~13% from TFCI for NCD redemption (cheaper than existing 18% NCDs). Authorized MDs to execute.
  • Equity Infusion: EGM (Oct 14, 2025) approved 20L equity shares @₹30 (~₹6 Cr) to Neo Fund (99.19% votes in favor).
  • Asset Security & Utilization: Strong 5.53x security cover; no fund deviation for NCD proceeds (fully utilized for capex ₹20 Cr, WC ₹29.5 Cr).
  • Segment Resilience: Hotel profitable (₹3.5 Cr H1 PBT); Paper scaling with capex (CWIP ₹43 Cr).

Headwinds (Negative Factors)

  • Profitability Pressure: H1 net loss ₹17 Cr (vs ₹4.3 Cr YoY loss); Q1 loss ₹18 Cr offset by Q2 ₹1.2 Cr profit. High finance costs (₹19 Cr, +18% YoY), depreciation (₹25 Cr), and other expenses drag margins (OPM -7.5%).
  • High Leverage & Costs: Debt/Equity 0.34 (up); finance costs ~8% of revenue. DSCR weak at 0.29 (H1).
  • Auditor Qualifications: No comment on physical verification of inventory (₹67 Cr)/PPE (₹891 Cr); debtors ₹66 Cr (10% >6 months, some litigated); unconfirmed balances.
  • Litigation Overhang: SEBI penalty ₹12L (deposited, appeal pending Jan 2026); BoB ₹3 Cr deposit dispute (court Nov 2025).

Growth Prospects

  • Medium-Term Expansion: Paper revenue poised for 30-40% FY26 growth (H1 trend); hotel steady at ₹42 Cr H1. New WOS (Magnum Paperz Ltd., investment Oct 2025) for diversification.
  • Balance Sheet Strengthening: ₹150 Cr loan + ₹6 Cr equity to reduce high-cost debt; capex focus (PPE +CWIP up 6% QoQ).
  • Operational Leverage: Inventory/Receivables turnover improving (3.6x/3.8x H1); current ratio 2.46 (healthy WC ₹136 Cr).
  • Upside Catalysts: Debt refinancing lowers interest (~₹5-7 Cr annual savings); preferential allotment closes promoter gap; strong security cover aids future borrowings.
