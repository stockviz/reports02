Minda Corporation Limited (MINDACORP) Analysis

Current Context (as of Q2FY26, ended Sep 30, 2025):
Minda Corp, a leading Indian auto components player (flagship of Spark Minda group), reported record Q2 consolidated revenue of ₹1,535 Cr (+19% YoY) and H1 revenue of ₹2,921 Cr (+17.7% YoY), driven by diversified portfolio (mechatronics, EV systems, plastics), EV ramp-up, and order wins (>₹3,600 Cr lifetime orders in H1). EBITDA margins stable at ~11.4-11.6%. FY25 group revenue: ₹7,472 Cr (consolidated ₹5,056 Cr). Vision 2030 targets 3.5x revenue growth to ₹17,500 Cr.

Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts)

  • Robust Industry Backdrop: Indian auto production up 9% in FY25 (2W +11%, PV +3%); components market to hit $200 Bn by 2030 (18% CAGR). EV penetration rising (global 10% CAGR to $1,614 Bn by 2032; India 22% CAGR). Govt. support (PLI, GST cuts to 18%, PM e-Bus) boosts demand.
  • Strong Execution & Financials: Outperformed industry (revenue +21% 5-yr CAGR); EBITDA +28% CAGR; PAT +2.5x over 5 yrs. H1FY26 PAT ₹150 Cr (+8% YoY). Order book strength from marquee OEMs (Maruti, Tata, Hero, global like Bosch).
  • EV & Premiumization Push: Flash Electronics (49% stake) EV revenue 23% of FY25; products like traction motors, controllers in production. New JVs (sunroof w/ HCMF Taiwan: ₹500 Cr by 2030; switches w/ Toyodenso: ₹650 Cr by 2030). EV portfolio expanding (3W/2W parts SOP FY26).
  • Strategic Expansions: Capex ₹2,000 Cr (next 5 yrs); new greenfield plants (die-casting, clusters). Exports targeted (FY25: ₹220 Cr → ₹1,500 Cr by FY30). R&D: 4% spend, 900+ engineers, 310 patents.
  • Balance Sheet Improving: Deleveraging (net debt: ₹1,247 Cr, D/E 0.6x → 0.3x target); WC days down to 31.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Margin Pressure: PAT margins dipped (Q2: 5.5% vs 5.8% YoY; H1: 5.1% vs 5.6%) due to higher finance costs (₹3,099 lakhs Q2) and investments. PBT down 3% YoY in Q2.
  • Leadership Transition: Group CFO Vinod Raheja resigning Nov 30, 2025 (personal reasons) – potential short-term disruption in finance/strategy.
  • Cyclical Exposure: 74% revenue from 2W/3W/PV; vulnerable to auto slowdowns (e.g., festive demand volatility).
  • Input Costs & Macro: Rising material costs, forex volatility could squeeze margins if not passed on.

Growth Prospects (High Confidence)

  • Short-Term (FY26-27): 15-20% revenue growth via EV wins, premium products (ADAS, sunroofs), exports (7-8% → 9% of revenue). H1 order inflows signal momentum.
  • Medium/Long-Term (to FY30): 3.5x revenue to ₹17,500 Cr (20%+ CAGR); EBITDA >3x (12%+ margins); ROCE >25%. Key drivers: EV content/vehicle up 20-30%; JVs adding ₹1,150 Cr revenue; global footprint (ASEAN, Europe). SDV/ADAS focus positions for tech shift.
  • Upside Levers: Festive recovery, GST benefits, PLI inflows; potential M&A in EV/battery.

Key Risks (Moderate-High Impact)

Risk Category Description Mitigation
Execution/Operational Capex delays, JV integration (e.g., Flash, HCMF); supply chain disruptions. Strong track record (120x growth at Flash); 32 plants, 18K workforce.

Overall Outlook: Positive (Buy/Hold for Long-Term). Tailwinds from EV/auto boom outweigh headwinds; CFO exit is transitory. Target FY30 delivery hinges on execution. Stock up >500% in 5 yrs – momentum intact. Monitor Q3 festive results & CFO successor. (Ratings based on FY25-26 data; future performance not guaranteed.)
