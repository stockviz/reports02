Minda Corporation Limited (MINDACORP) Analysis

Summary Overview: Minda Corp, a leading Indian auto components player (focus: mechatronics, EV electronics, plastics), reported robust Q2/H1 FY26 results (revenue +19%/17.7% YoY to Rs 1,535 Cr / Rs 2,921 Cr; EBITDA margin stable at ~11.6%/11.4%). FY25 group revenue Rs 7,472 Cr (consol Rs 5,056 Cr). Strong tailwinds from auto/EV growth, order wins (Rs 3,600 Cr lifetime), and expansions outweigh near-term headwinds like CFO exit. Growth trajectory targets 3.5x revenue to Rs 17,500 Cr by FY30 via premiumization, EVs, exports.

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Industry Boom: Indian auto production +9% FY25 (2W +11%, PV +3%); components market to $200 Bn by 2030 (18% CAGR); EV market 22% CAGR to $118 Bn by 2032.
  • Financial Momentum: Revenue 2x in 5 yrs (21% CAGR); EBITDA 11.4% (FY25); PAT 2.5x; ROCE improving. H1 FY26 order wins signal sustained demand.
  • Diversification & Positioning: 6 verticals (e.g., EV via 49% Flash Electronics stake – 23% EV revenue FY25); 900+ engineers, 310 patents, 4% R&D spend; marquee clients (Maruti, Tata, Hero); JVs (Toyodenso, HCMF).
  • Operational Efficiency: WC days down to 31 (FY25); 37% renewable energy; Great Place to Work certified.
  • Policy Support: GST cuts (28%→18%), PLI schemes, Make in India boosting affordability/manufacturing.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Leadership Transition: Group CFO Vinod Raheja resigns Nov 30, 2025 (personal reasons) – potential short-term disruption in finance/strategy.
  • Margin Pressure: PAT margin dipped to 5.5% Q2FY26 (-25 bps YoY) due to higher finance costs (Rs 3,099 lakhs Q2).
  • High Capex Intensity: FY25 Rs 342 Cr (+161% YoY); next 5 yrs Rs 2,000 Cr – strains cash flows amid rising debt (net debt Rs 1,247 Cr).
  • Input Costs/Inventory: Rising inventories (Rs 64,921 lakhs consol Sep’25); material costs ~62% of revenue.
  • Cyclical Exposure: Dependent on OEM volumes (2W/PV ~70% revenue); festive slowdown risks.

Growth Prospects

  • High Visibility: Lifetime orders Rs 3,600 Cr H1 FY26; EV ramp-up (Flash: Rs 1,537 Cr FY25 revenue, 14.5% margin); new wins in ICE/EV.
  • EV/Tech Focus: 23% EV revenue (Flash); products like traction motors, inverters, VCU; 3W EV SOP FY26. Target: content/vehicle up 20-30%.
  • Premiumization/Exports: JVs for sunroofs (Rs 500 Cr by FY30), switches (Rs 650 Cr); exports from Rs 220 Cr FY25 to Rs 1,500 Cr FY30 (7-28% CAGR).
  • Capacity Expansion: 2 new greenfield plants (die-casting, clusters); 40 plants globally; Vision 2030: Rs 17,500 Cr revenue (21% CAGR), 12%+ EBITDA, debt/equity 0.3x.
  • M&A/JVs: Recent Flash (49%), HCMF (50%), Toyodenso (60%); global footprint (ASEAN, Europe).

Key Risks

Risk Category Description Mitigation
Cyclical/Volume OEM slowdowns (e.g., 2W/PV volatility); festive demand risks. Diversified segments (aftermarket 16%); order book buffer.
EV Transition Tech obsolescence, battery costs, competition (e.g., from China). R&D focus (900 engineers); JVs for tech transfer.
Debt/Liquidity Debt/equity 0.6x; interest coverage ~4x; capex funding. Deleveraging plan (to 0.3x); internal accruals + equity.
Execution New plants/JVs delays; CFO transition. Strong leadership (Ashok/Aakash Minda); track record.
Macro/Regulatory Commodity inflation, forex volatility, emission/safety norms (Bharat NCAP, cyber security). Hedging; ESG compliance (42% carbon cut target).
Competition/Geopolitics Global shifts (China+1); export risks. Cost leadership (1st quartile wiring harness); premium products.

Investment Thesis: Buy/Hold for Long-Term. Tailwinds from EV/auto secular growth and execution strength support 20%+ CAGR. Monitor CFO succession and Q3 volumes. Target FY30 upside aligns with industry multiples (EV focus premium). Risks manageable with balance sheet flexibility.
