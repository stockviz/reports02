Hindustan Zinc Limited (HINDZINC) Analysis

Summary Overview:
Hindustan Zinc (HZL), India’s largest integrated zinc-lead-silver producer (world’s #1 zinc producer by integrated capacity), delivered a stellar Q2FY26 (ended Sep 2025) with record mined metal production (258kt, +1% YoY), 5-year low zinc C1 cash cost ($994/t, -7% YoY), and highest-ever Q2 EBITDA (₹4,467 Cr, +16% QoQ at 52% margin). Silver rally (to $50/oz, ~40% of profits) and cost discipline drove PAT up 19% QoQ to ₹2,649 Cr. H1FY26 was second-best ever (EBITDA ₹8,328 Cr, +3% YoY). FY26 guidance: refined metal 1,075kt (±10kt), silver 680t (±10t), COP ~$1,000/t. Strong ESG (#1 global metals sustainability ranking for 3rd year) and growth capex ($350-400M) position HZL well amid resilient commodity markets and India’s steel/infra boom. Market cap ~₹204k Cr; included in Nifty 100/Next 50.

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)
  • Commodity Super-Cycle Leverage: Silver at all-time highs ($50/oz, +45% in 9M FY26) drives ~40% profits; zinc/lead resilient ($3,010/t & $1,957/t LME). HZL uniquely positioned as India’s only primary silver producer (top-5 globally).
  • Operational Excellence: Lowest 5-yr COP ($994/t Q2, H1 $1,002/t); 19% renewable energy (target 25% FY26-end, 70% FY28); higher by-product credits, grades (7.4%), domestic coal (52-58%).
  • Robust Domestic Demand: India GDP +6.5-6.8% FY26; steel output to 300Mt by 2030 (zinc demand tailwind); 77% domestic zinc market share.
  • Sustainability Edge: #1 S&P Global CSA score (90/100); ICMM member (1st Indian); fatality-free; 3.32x water-positive; CSR impacts 2.3M lives.
  • Financial Strength: Net cash trajectory (flat FY26-end post-capex); ROCE 65% TTM; contributed ₹8,400 Cr to exchequer H1FY26.
Headwinds (Challenges)
  • Volume Pressures: Refined metal -6% YoY Q2 (plant availability, roaster delay); silver output -22% YoY (lower grades ~90ppm at SK mine).
  • Input/Regulatory Costs: Royalty-sensitive COP; minor GST penalty (₹1.08 Cr + tax/interest, 2011 issue; appeal planned, immaterial).
  • Macro Softness: Global GDP 2.5-3% FY26 (trade tensions); tepid non-ferrous demand offset by tight inventories.
  • Capex Ramp-Up: Growth capex $350-400M FY26 (total ~$750-800M incl. maintenance); large projects strain near-term FCF.
Growth Prospects
  • Near-Term (FY26-27): Revised FY26 volumes (1,075kt refined zinc-lead, 680t silver); debottlenecking adds 25ktpa zinc; H2 silver ramp via SK mine focus. COP < $1,000/t exit FY26; EBITDA upside from silver/zinc firmness.
  • Medium-Term (FY27-29): 250ktpa integrated expansion (₹12k Cr, Q2FY29; total capacity 1.51Mtpa); 10Mtpa tailings reprocessing (₹3.8k Cr, Q4FY28; +100kt zinc, 25t silver); fertilizer (510ktpa DAP by Q1FY27); silver to 750-800t FY27, 1,500t long-term.
  • Long-Term Vision: 2x growth (2Mtpa UG mining/smelting); VAPs to 50% sales; critical minerals (potash, tungsten, REEs); 25+ yr mine life (40Mt R&R).
  • Capex Phasing: 20% FY26, 50% FY27, 30% FY28; high-ROI projects (e.g., tailings IRR attractive).
Key Risks
Risk Category Description Mitigation
Commodity Price Volatility Zinc/lead surplus risks; silver dependent on industrial/green demand (solar/EVs). Sensitivity: +$100/t zinc = +₹660-690 Cr EBITDA. Hedging (87kt zinc @ $2,872; 131t silver @ $37); diversified portfolio (silver 40%).
Execution/Operational Capex delays (e.g., past roaster); grade variability; plant outages. On-track projects; contractor incentives; debottlenecking.
Regulatory/Legal Royalty hikes; GST/Excise disputes (e.g., ₹1.08 Cr penalty); mining auctions. Appeals; strong compliance (ICMM-aligned).
Cost Inflation/FX Power/coal volatility; INR/USD (+₹1 = +₹190-210 Cr EBITDA). Renewables (19%→70%); domestic coal.
ESG/External Climate policy; biodiversity; geopolitical (China supply). Net Zero 2050; water-positive; #1 CSA ranking.
Liquidity/Governance High promoter holding (Vedanta 61.84%, Govt 29.54%); director approvals pending postal ballot. Strong balance sheet (₹8,155 Cr cash); robust governance.

Overall Outlook: Bullish. Tailwinds from silver/zinc resilience, cost leadership, and India demand outweigh headwinds. Growth capex unlocks 2x capacity; ESG leadership attracts capital. Target FY26 EBITDA ~₹17-18k Cr (assuming stable prices). Risks manageable via hedging/diversification. Buy/Accumulate on dips; monitor silver prices and capex execution.
