Hitech Corporation Limited (HITECHCORP) Analysis

Overview: Hitech Corporation Limited (BSE: 526217), a plastic products manufacturer (single reportable segment), reported audited Q2 & H1 FY26 results (ended Sep 30, 2025). Consolidated revenue grew 14% YoY to ₹329 Cr, with PAT up 34% to ₹89 Cr (EPS ₹5.19). Standalone revenue +4% to ₹301 Cr, but PAT down 30% to ₹49 Cr (EPS ₹2.86). Strong capex (₹20-22 Cr) signals expansion, but debt and working capital pressures persist. Subsidiaries (Thriarr Polymers Pvt Ltd ~₹23 Cr assets, Hitech Global Inc minor) boost consolidated growth.

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Revenue Momentum: Consolidated top-line +14% YoY H1 (₹329 Cr), driven by subsidiaries (Q2 contribution ~₹16 Cr revenue). Standalone steady +4%, with Q2 edging prior year.
  • Profitability Leverage: Consolidated PAT margins improved (2.7% H1), aided by scale and other income. Operating cash flow robust at ₹26 Cr (+ve YoY).
  • Expansion Investments: Heavy capex (standalone ₹20 Cr, cons. ₹22 Cr) in PPE/CWIP (total assets +5-8% YoY), signaling capacity build-out in plastics/moulding.
  • Subsidiary Synergies: Thriarr Polymers adds ₹28 Cr H1 revenue/₹45 Cr PAT (pre-adjust); enhances group scale post-FY25 formation.
  • Cash Generation: OCF covers capex; equity stable at ₹172 Cr.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Margin Squeeze (Standalone): PAT -30% YoY due to higher finance costs (+15% to ₹9 Cr), depreciation (+5%), and employee expenses (+4%). EBITDA margins ~3-4% strained.
  • Working Capital Drag: Trade receivables +28% to ₹57-66 Cr; inventories +11-13%. OCF dip YoY standalone (₹20 Cr vs ₹29 Cr).
  • Debt Burden: Borrowings +25-30% to ₹125-132 Cr (current up sharply); finance costs now 3% of revenue, eroding profits.
  • Cost Pressures: Raw materials ~60% of expenses; forex unrealized losses and ECL provisions add volatility.

Growth Prospects

  • High (Medium-Term): Plastics demand (auto/medical/metro via expertise in moulding tech) supports 10-15% revenue CAGR. Capex/CWIP (₹13 Cr) points to new capacity; subsidiaries enable diversification (e.g., NPD for OEMs like Tata/Mahindra).
  • Catalysts: Management extensions (HR/IR/Tech VP/WTD) ensure continuity. Single-segment focus but tech edge (GAIM/2K moulding). FY26E revenue potential ₹110-120 Cr Q/Q extrapolation + sub growth.
  • Outlook: Consolidated EPS trajectory >₹10 FY26; ROE ~12-14% sustainable if debt controlled.
