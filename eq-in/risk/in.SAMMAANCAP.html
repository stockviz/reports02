Analysis of Sammaan Capital Limited (SAMMAANCAP)

Sammaan Capital Limited (formerly Indiabulls Housing Finance Limited) is a mid-sized NBFC focused on mortgage lending to low/mid-income segments (home loans, small business loans). It operates ~220 branches, with AUM ~INR 42,000 Cr (growth book stable, legacy declining). Recent Q2/H1 FY26 results show profitability turnaround (Q2 PAT INR 308 Cr vs prior loss). Key catalyst: Proposed INR 8,850 Cr (~USD 1 Bn) investment by IHC (UAE-based, $240 Bn mcap) for ~41% stake (diluted), plus open offer.

Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts)

  • Strategic Investment & Promoter Change: IHC (via Avenir) preferential issue at INR 139/share approved by shareholders (94% yes on Oct 29, 2025). Potential 63% stake post-open offer. Brings patient capital, tech/AI expertise, governance. Immediate impact: $450 Mn bond at 150 bps lower yield; Moody’s upgrade review. CoF to drop ~200 bps (to 7.5%), boosting PAT ~50%.
  • Funding & Liquidity Improvement: Debt availability up 1.5x YoY. Strong secondary market demand. Asset quality stable (Gross NPA benign, AA ratings).
  • Regulatory Support: New co-lending norms (no priority sector limit, 10% retention, broader partners incl. AIFIs). Enables asset-light model (100% originations for co-lend/assign/sec).
  • Operational Momentum: Q2 PAT INR 308 Cr (vs -INR 2,761 Cr YoY); H1 PAT INR 643 Cr. NIM 5.5%; gearing 2x (headroom for growth). Retail AUM mix rising; collections robust (INR 9-10k Cr annualized).

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Legacy Portfolio Drag: Legacy AUM down INR 9k Cr YoY; target INR 15k Cr by FY26-end. Back-ended rundown; ongoing provisions (H1 impairment INR 695 Cr).
  • Near-Term Margin Squeeze: Retail shift lowers yields; CoF still 9-9.5%. NII down QoQ.
  • Transition Uncertainty: Strategy revision with IHC; no FY27 guidance till Q4 FY26. Disbursals currently annualized INR 15k Cr (target INR 35k Cr FY27).
  • High OpEx: Employee/branch expansion planned (4x people, 3-4x branches to 500).

Growth Prospects

  • High-Conviction Scaling: Post-approvals (expected by FY26-end/FY27-start), disbursals to INR 35k Cr FY27 (from INR 15k Cr), AUM >INR 1.1 lakh Cr, then 20-23% CAGR. Mortgages 80% (ticket INR 10L-3Cr); diversify to unsecured/small biz loans. ROA 1.5% → 2.5%; leverage to 4.5x.
  • Asset-Light Expansion: Co-lend gains ~4% of disbursals; partnerships with 2-3 more banks. Tech from IHC for app-based disbursals, lower costs.
  • Market Tailwinds: Robust housing demand (underserved self-employed mid-market); real estate cycle strong. Target: FY27/28 catch-up growth.
  • Long-Term: IHC backing for profitability (current gearing low); potential ROE compounding.
