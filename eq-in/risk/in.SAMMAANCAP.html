Analysis of Sammaan Capital Limited (SAMMAANCAP) – Headwinds, Tailwinds, Growth Prospects, and Key Risks

Sammaan Capital (formerly Indiabulls Housing Finance) is an NBFC focused on mortgage lending to low-mid income segments. Q2/H1 FY26 marked a turnaround with profitability resumption amid a transformative ~INR 8,850 Cr preferential investment by IHC (Abu Dhabi-based, $240 Bn mcap). Below is a structured summary based on Q2 FY26 earnings call, financials, investor presentation, EGM outcomes, and disclosures (as of Sep 30, 2025).

Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts)

  • Strategic Investment & Promoter Backing: IHC’s ~41% stake (diluted; potential 63% post-open offer at INR 139/share). Shareholder approval secured (94.4% in favor). Provides patient capital, tech/AI expertise, governance upgrade. Immediate impact: $450 Mn bond raise at 150 bps lower yield; Moody’s upgrade review.
  • Financial Recovery: Q2 PAT INR 308 Cr (vs. -INR 2,761 Cr YoY); H1 PAT INR 643 Cr. AUM stable ~INR 42k Cr (growth AUM +ve); Networth INR 22.4k Cr. Cost of funds expected to drop ~200 bps (to 7.5% blended), boosting PAT ~50%.
  • Regulatory Support: Favorable co-lending norms (retention 10%, broader partners). Asset quality stable (Gross NPA benign, AA ratings).
  • Operational Momentum: Retail focus (mortgages 80%+ disbursals); INR 468 Cr Q2 gain on derecognition (4% run-rate). Robust real estate demand.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Legacy Portfolio Drag: Legacy AUM down INR 9k Cr YoY (target INR 15k Cr by FY26E); back-ended collections (~INR 9-10k Cr FY26). High past impairments (Q2 INR 229 Cr).
  • Margin Squeeze: Retail shift compresses yields/NIM (~5.5%); NII down YoY despite revenue stability.
  • Transition Uncertainty: Strategy pivot from asset-light (100% retail originations co-lend/assign) to on-BS growth. Revised FY27 guidance pending IHC integration (next 2Q).
  • Historical Pain: 7-year turnaround; past promoter exit eroded trust (though management retention emphasized).

Growth Prospects

  • High-Conviction Ramp-Up: Disbursals from ~INR 15k Cr annualized to INR 35k Cr FY27 (catch-up mode); AUM >INR 1.1 lakh Cr, then 20-23% CAGR. Mortgages core (80%+ next 2-3Y); expand to non-mortgage (personal/small biz loans, ticket INR 10L-3Cr).
  • Leverage Capital: Gearing 2x → industry norm 4.5x. ROA target 2.5% (from 1.5%); ROE accretion via lower CoF.
  • Scale Enablers: Branches to 500 (3-4x), headcount 4x; IHC tech (AI-driven disbursals/app). Sammaan Finserv sub AUM INR 6.2k Cr → INR 10k Cr FY26E.
  • Market Opportunity: Underserved mid-income housing/SME; robust industry demand.

Key Risks

Regulatory RBI/CCI approval delays/denials for IHC deal (4-month timeline). NBFC-UL norms add scrutiny. Filed applications; strong track record.

Macro Rate hikes, realty slowdown, economic softening. Robust demand; diversified liabilities.

Overall Outlook: Strong tailwinds from IHC infusion position Sammaan for re-rating (book value discount addressed). FY26 stabilization → FY27+ growth inflection (~2-3x disbursals). Risks execution-heavy but manageable; monitor approvals. Target: Profitable compounding NBFC post-transformation.
