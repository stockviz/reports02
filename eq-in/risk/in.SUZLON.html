Suzlon Energy Ltd. (SUZLON) Analysis

Overall Summary: Suzlon is experiencing a strong turnaround in FY26, driven by record executions (565 MW in Q2, 1 GW H1), a 6.2 GW order book (up from 2.9 GW YoY), and policy tailwinds like GST cut to 5% on wind turbines and ALMM SOPs favoring domestic manufacturing. Q2 revenue surged 85% YoY to ₹3,866 Cr, EBITDA 145% to ₹721 Cr (18.6% margin), with PAT at ₹1,279 Cr aided by ₹718 Cr DTA recognition. Net cash at ₹1,480 Cr supports growth. A reserves reorganization scheme (NCLT hearings Dec 2025) aims to clean up accumulated losses (~₹14,000 Cr carry-forward) by offsetting against reserves and reclassifying General Reserve to Retained Earnings, reflecting true financial health without impacting capital/shareholding. FY26 guidance: 60% YoY growth across parameters. Long-term prospects bright with India’s wind targets (122 GW by FY32, 400 GW by 2047), but execution risks and competition persist.

Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts)

  • Robust Demand & Order Book: 6.2 GW firm orders (65% C&I/non-bidding, 35% PSU/bidding); H1 wins >2 GW (incl. 838 MW from Tata Power). Pipeline strong; industry installations doubled H1 FY26 (3 GW vs. 1.5 GW YoY).
  • Policy Boost: GST cut (12%→5%) lowers LCOE by ₹0.15-0.17/kWh; MNRE ALMM/REEIMS enforces domestic sourcing (Suzlon 100% compliant, lowest carbon footprint S144 turbine). FDRE/hybrid focus favors wind (blended LCOE ₹4.65/kWh vs. solar+BESS ₹6.5/kWh).
  • Execution Momentum: 4.5 GW manufacturing fully operational; 1,865 MW pipeline (568 MW erection-complete); land bank for 23+ GW (7.5 GW development underway) to boost EPC mix (20:80 →50:50 by FY28).
  • Financial Strength: Net cash ₹1,480 Cr; ₹7,000 Cr banking limits; DTA ₹1,229 Cr (shield on ₹5,000 Cr future profits); OMS annuity (15.4 GW, 37-40% margins); SE Forge 53% YoY H1 growth.
  • Market Leadership: 29% cumulative India share; 21+ GW global installs; new CFO (Rahul Jain) from Dec 2025.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Historical Losses: Accumulated losses drag Retained Earnings (negative despite recent profits); receivables up due to PSU orders (longer cycles, 70% on site receipt vs. 90-95% standard).
  • Margin Mix Pressure: WTG margins (26.9%) lower than OMS (37%); Q2 OMS EBITDA dip to 34.5% (one-off, H1 at 37%).
  • Volume Dependency: Push-mode manufacturing (not pull); H1 deliveries low at 270 MW vs. FY26 target ~1.5 GW (25% market share).
  • Regulatory Scrutiny: Ongoing customs penalty (reduced to ₹216 Cr, appeal planned); SEBI SCN on past disclosures (no material impact per mgmt).
