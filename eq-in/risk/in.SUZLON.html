Suzlon Energy Ltd. (SUZLON) Analysis

Suzlon Energy Ltd., India’s leading wind turbine manufacturer and solutions provider, has demonstrated robust recovery and growth in FY26 (post strong FY25 turnaround). The Q2 FY26 results (ended Sep 30, 2025) reflect record deliveries, surging revenues, and a cleaned-up balance sheet trajectory via a proposed NCLT-approved reserve reorganization scheme. Below is a structured analysis of headwinds, tailwinds, growth prospects, and key risks, based on the provided filings (financials, orders, announcements, and scheme details).

Tailwinds (Positive Catalysts)
  • Explosive Financial Momentum: Q2 FY26 revenue ₹3,866 Cr (+85% YoY), EBITDA ₹721 Cr (+145% YoY, 18.6% margin), PAT ₹1,279 Cr (+538% YoY, aided by ₹717 Cr deferred tax gain). H1 FY26: Revenue ₹6,983 Cr (+70% YoY), PAT ₹1,604 Cr (+219% YoY). Net cash ₹1,480 Cr (up from prior periods), enabling execution flexibility.
  • Record Order Book: 6.2 GW (highest ever; +2 GW added in H1 FY26), diversified (54% WTG models like S144; 80% non-EPC scope; mix of central/state/C&I). Latest: 838 MW from Tata Power (FY26’s largest; across 3 states; 3rd order from them).
  • Favorable Industry/Policy Environment: India’s 500 GW non-fossil target by 2030 (wind: 122 GW by FY32); GST cut to 5% on turbines; ALMM SOPs boosting localization; VGF for offshore; C&I RE demand (100 GW by 2030). Suzlon’s 4.5 GW domestic mfg. capacity (largest in India) positions it as export hub.
  • OMS Stability: Annuity-like revenue from 15.4 GW installed base (99 sites, 1,900+ customers); Renom adds multi-brand O&M (3.3 GW AUM).
  • Balance Sheet Cleanup: NCLT-directed scheme (meetings Dec 12, 2025) to set off ₹~17,000+ Cr accumulated losses against reserves (Capital Reserve, Securities Premium, etc.) and reclassify General Reserve to Retained Earnings. No share capital reduction; enhances dividend flexibility without cash outflow.
  • Operational Excellence: Highest Q2 India deliveries (565 MW); S144 turbine (lowest carbon footprint; 2.3 GW delivered, 5+ GW orders); 21+ GW global installs.
Headwinds (Challenges)
  • Legacy Losses & Cleanup Needs: Accumulated losses (~₹17,000 Cr debit in Retained Earnings) distort financial health despite recent profits; scheme addresses but requires NCLT approval.
  • Regulatory Overhang: Customs penalty reduced to ₹215.86 Lacs (from ₹428.50 Lacs; appeal planned); SEBI SCN (Sep 26, 2025) on past disclosures/transactions (management: no material impact).
  • WC Strain: H1 FY26 trade receivables up to ₹4,855 Cr (+25% QoQ); inventories ₹4,226 Cr; cash conversion slower despite net cash position.
  • Margin Pressure: EBITDA margin dipped QoQ (19.2% to 18.6%) due to forex losses, employee costs; exceptional items (₹40 Cr impairment).
Growth Prospects
  • Execution Visibility: 1,865 MW FY26 pipeline; potential 6 GW industry installs FY26 (8 GW FY27). WTG contribution margin ~26-35%; scalable mfg. for exports/repowering (25 GW potential).
  • Market Expansion: Wind complementary to solar (RTC/FDRE/hybrid); C&I (100 GW RE need); project dev. (23+ GW identified, 7 GW land bank). Order book implies multi-year revenue (₹20,000+ Cr potential at current pricing).
  • FY26 Guidance: 90%+ YoY delivery growth; sustained 18-20% EBITDA margins; OMS/F&F steady (₹1,200+ Cr H1 revenue).
  • Long-Term: 400 GW wind by 2047; Suzlon’s 29% India mkt. share, tech leadership (S144), ESG ratings (S&P upgrade), awards reinforce positioning.
