Jindal Saw Ltd. (NSE: JINDALSAW, BSE: 500378) - Analysis Summary (Q2 FY26: Ended Sep 30, 2025)

Jindal Saw Ltd., a diversified iron & steel pipes and pellets manufacturer, reported a challenging Q2 FY26 with sharp YoY declines in performance due to sector-specific headwinds, but offset by a robust order book and positive macro tailwinds. Standalone revenue fell 29% YoY to ₹34,091 Mn (EBITDA margin: 9.8% vs 18.3%; PAT: ₹793 Mn, down 83%). Consolidated revenue: ₹42,640 Mn (down 24%); PAT: ₹1,386 Mn (down 71%). H1 FY26 trends similar, though tax refunds (₹1,335 Mn) aided cash flows. Balance sheet stable with net debt reduction (long-term down), but working capital debt rose amid inventory/receivables buildup. Upcoming investor meet (Nov 28, 2025) signals engagement.

Headwinds (Current Pressures)

  • Revenue & Profitability Slump: Q2 standalone revenue/EBITDA/PAT down 29%/62%/83% YoY; pipes production/sales halved (293k MT vs 425k/432k MT) due to domestic water sector liquidity crunch (delayed payments, high inventories/receivables).
  • Margin Compression: EBITDA margin at 9.8% (standalone) from 18.3%; higher finance costs/depreciation amid capex.
  • Working Capital Strain: Net WC debt up sharply (standalone: ₹27,568 Mn; consol: ₹31,177 Mn); cash burn in ops/investments led to ₹447 Mn (standalone)/₹507 Mn (consol) cash outflow.
  • Domestic Execution Delays: Water infra clients’ issues persisted, impacting ~half of pipes order book.

Tailwinds (Supportive Factors)

  • Record Order Book: ~US$1,452 Mn (~1.9 Mn MT volumes, all-time high); pipes: US$1,412 Mn (43% export volumes); pellets: US$40 Mn. Execution: 9-12 months (plus 15-18 months for 0.622 Mn MT job-work export water order). UAE add-on: US$240 Mn (~215k MT).
  • Debt Profile Improving: Long-term debt down (standalone: ₹5,536 Mn; consol: ₹7,417 Mn); ratings stable (CARE AA/A1+; BWR AA).
  • Operational Resilience: Pellets stable (407k/356k MT prod/sales); UAE sales up (58k MT DI pipes); tax refunds boosted H1 tax credit.
  • Capex Momentum: Ongoing efficiency upgrades; seamless piercing mill trial started (Oct 2025 stabilization).

Growth Prospects (Medium-Term Outlook)

  • Infra Boom: Govt push (Jal Jeevan Mission to 2028, AMRUT 2.0 ₹10k Cr FY26, Namami Gange, oil/gas pipelines worth ₹50k+ Cr e.g., Mumbai-Nagpur 1,700 km). Water/wastewater: 675 Mn urban pop by 2035; MENA projects (Saudi/UAE: 10k+ km pipelines, US$2Bn+ LNG).
  • Export Diversification: 31-43% order book value/volumes; MENA/LatAm focus; rust-free DI pipes strength.
  • Capacity/Tech Expansion: JV with Hunting (premium OCTG threads, commercial since FY24); pellet/pipes ancillaries; total solutions (coatings, fittings).
  • Balanced Portfolio: Pipes (O&G/water dominant), pellets hedge; FY26 revenue potential from 1.9 Mn MT backlog + UAE visibility.

Key Risks

Risk Category Details Mitigation
Litigation Jindal ITF (51% sub) appeal vs NTPC (₹1,891 Cr award set aside Jan 2025; next hearing Oct 27, 2025). Investment: ₹1,633 Cr; mgmt confident but potential impairment/going concern hit. Legal counsel views “strong case”; no FY26 adjustment.
Cyclical/Execution Infra liquidity (water sector), forex volatility (natural hedging noted). Strong OB, exports (43%); policy tailwinds.
Financial High WC debt (0.28 D/E standalone); capex outflow (₹460 Mn H1). Cash equivalents down 87% QoQ. LT debt reduction; ratings intact; refunds aided.
Macro/Geo Commodity prices, O&G volatility, competition; geopolitics (exports). Diversified (water 50%+ rev; O&G ~25%); global projects.
Operational Mill stabilization delays; sub-perf (e.g., negative NCI PAT). Capex focus; JV growth.

Overall: Short-term pain from domestic water woes, but strong OB and infra tailwinds position for H2 FY26 recovery. Monitor ITF hearing and water liquidity. Trading at ~10x FY26E EPS (est.); attractive if execution delivers. Positive forward-looking statements with caveats on risks.
