Analysis of Arman Financial Services Limited (ARMANFIN)

Arman Financial Services Ltd. (ARMANFIN) is a diversified NBFC with ~66% exposure to microfinance (via subsidiary Namra Finance), ~22% MSME loans, ~7% two-wheeler/LAP/individual business loans. H1FY26 results reflect sector headwinds in MFI but resilience in non-MFI segments. Consolidated AUM: ₹2,130 Cr (-14% YoY); disbursements flat at ₹865 Cr; PAT loss of ₹6.6 Cr (vs. ₹47 Cr profit YoY) due to provisions/write-offs (₹105 Cr). Asset quality improving (GNPA 3.69%, NNPA 0.53%); capital adequacy strong (38.7% standalone, 57.8% Namra); liquidity ₹238 Cr + ₹160 Cr undrawn.

Headwinds (Challenges Impacting Performance)

  • MFI Sector Stress: Overleveraging, high attrition, rural macro slowdown led to AUM decline (Namra: -24% YoY to ₹1,507 Cr). Collections dipped to 95.6% (Sep’25); impairment costs high (₹38 Cr in Q2, down from ₹89 Cr in Q4FY25 but still elevated).
  • Growth Moderation: Cautious disbursements (login-to-disbursement ~20%) prioritized quality over volume; flat YoY growth vs. historical 30%+ CAGR.
  • Margin/Cost Pressures: Yield ~22-34% across segments, but NIM compressed (13% MFI); cost-to-income up (due to fixed OpEx amid AUM shrinkage); borrowing cost ~12%.
  • Regulatory/External: Tighter guardrails, pricing sensitivity to regulators; spillover from MFI to rural MSME (GNPA 3.86%).

Tailwinds (Positive Factors Supporting Recovery)

  • Improving Metrics: Collections stabilized at 95%+; sequential disbursement growth (Q2FY26: +27% YoY, +21% QoQ); impairment trending down 3 quarters; 67% MFI under CGFMU guarantee.
  • Non-MFI Strength: Standalone AUM +29% YoY (₹623 Cr); PAT +4% (₹21 Cr); disbursements +26% (₹260 Cr H1); stable asset quality (MSME GNPA 3.86%, LAP 0.16%).
  • Structural Reforms: BCM model (credit-recovery separation) in 50% branches cut delinquencies 40%; digital collections ~12-20%; 100% cashless disbursements.
  • Balance Sheet Resilience: High CRAR (well above RBI norms); diversified borrowings (banks 30%, NCDs 18%); positive ALM (15-16 months).

Growth Prospects (Medium-Term Outlook)

  • MFI Recovery: Stabilization evident (post-Sep’24 book: 99.4% zero DPD); target ₹180 Cr/month disbursements; expect H2FY26 momentum from rural sentiment/policy support.
  • Diversification: Scale MSME/LAP (LAP ATS ₹7.5L, yields 21.7%); aim SME share to 35% (from 22%), MFI to 60%; new geographies (UP North).
  • Overall Targets: AUM growth resumption (historical 33% CAGR FY16-25); ROA aspiration 3-4.5%; leverage 40+ bank/FI ties; tech (LOS/LMS, AI for underwriting).
  • Catalysts: Festive/Q3 demand (Oct disbursements high); CGFMU/MSME insurance; healthy liquidity for expansion (509 branches → more).

Key Risks

  • Credit/Asset Quality: Prolonged MFI stress (PAR 1-30 ~1.2%); rural overleverage spillover; high write-offs if collections slip.
  • Operational: Attrition, high OpEx (cost-income 50%+ MFI); execution risks in BCM rollout.
  • Macro/Regulatory: Rural slowdown, interest rate hikes, SRO guardrails/pricing caps; competition in MSME/LAP.
  • Liquidity/Funding: Incremental cost sensitivity; refinancing ~₹1,274 Cr liabilities.
  • Concentration: 66% MFI exposure; execution on diversification.

Overall Outlook: Cautiously Positive. Near-term stabilization (Q3 YoY easier base); H2FY26 growth likely from non-MFI ramp-up and MFI revival. Reforms yielding results; strong capital buffers mitigate downside. Target normalized ROA 3-4% sustainable; stock re-rating on disbursement traction/asset quality. Monitor rural macros, collections. Valuation: Attractive if growth resumes (trading ~0.8x FY26E BVPS).
